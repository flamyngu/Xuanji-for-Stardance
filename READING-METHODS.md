# Reading Methods of the Star Gauge

A catalogue of every reading rule documented on the Chinese Text Project's reading-methods page (https://ctext.org/wiki.pl?if=en&chapter=793483, titled 解讀璇璣圖, "Interpreting the Star Gauge"), supplemented where relevant by the *Jìnghuā yuán* transmission of the same apparatus.

This is an extraction, not a translation. Where the original gives a precise rule, the precision is preserved; where the original is laconic, the catalogue notes the gap rather than filling it. Most of the methods below assume the reader is working from the **29 × 29 grid** with its characters partitioned into the **five colour-regions** (red 紅, black 黑, blue 藍, purple 紫, and an outer plain field). The colour partition is the operational structure of the poem, many of the methods explicitly key off a starting character that sits inside one region.

A note on the "retirement" terminology: 退一字 *tuì yī zì* literally "withdraw one character" means *start the next line one position offset from where the previous line started*; what in Western prosody would be a staggered indent. Most of the primary methods compose by combining a staggered start with a directional pass (forward, reverse, palindromic) over the underlying cells.

---

## I. The Five Primary Methods - 退一字 retirement readings

These five define the principal compositional strategies for generating poems from the grid. Each produces complete palindromic poems (回文) by combining a staggered starting position with a reversal rule.

### 1. 自初行退一字 - *Retirement from the initial row*

- **Meter:** seven-character lines (七言), four lines per poem (四句)
- **Method:** each line reads as a palindrome (逐句退成回文 - "line by line, retire to form a palindrome")
- **Region / direction:** vertical columns, with progressive offset reduction at each step

### 2. 自上橫行退一字成句 - *Horizontal retirement from the top*

- **Meter:** seven-character lines
- **Method:** the rule 逐字逐逆讀，俱成回文 - "read each character, each in reverse, all become palindromes"
- **Property:** every line is reversible; reading the same characters backward yields a different but still well-formed line

### 3. 自兩間行退一字 - *Middle-row retirement* (literally: from the two-between row)

- **Method:** decreasing line lengths with vertical-horizontal reversal (縱橫返復讀)
- **Output:** chapter-length texts; uses rotational reading

### 4. 自中行退一字 - *Centre-line retirement*

- **Method:** progressive reduction with alternating line descent (迎退一句 - "approach retire by one line")
- **Output:** continuous connected passages, not discrete short poems

### 5. 自角斜退一字 - *Corner-diagonal retirement*

- **Meter:** seven-character lines, four-line poems
- **Method:** sequential character-and-line reversal, both the character order within a line and the line order within the poem can be reversed independently

## II. The Rotational Readings - 旋讀

### 6. 八面分讀 - *Eight-direction division*

Reads the grid along eight rotational axes, grouped into three sets:

- **四正左旋 / 四正右旋** - the four cardinal directions, rotated left or right
- **四隅左旋 / 四隅右旋** - the four diagonal corners, rotated left or right
- **雙句左旋 / 雙句右旋** - double-line alternating, rotated left or right

Each axis yields a complete reading family.

### 7. 左旋 / 右旋讀 - *Left-rotation and right-rotation*

The general palindromic-spinning reading: walk the grid (or a sub-region of it) along a rotational path; each pass yields a palindromic poem. Applied to multiple grid configurations and to sub-regions inside the colour partition.

## III. The Colour-Bounded Readings

The grid is divided into regions of differently coloured characters (the Five-Phase regions). Several of the most important reading families are bounded to a single colour: you read only the characters of, say, the red region, and the methods below tell you in what order. Each colour-bounded reading typically specifies a starting character by name, i.e. the rule begins "starting from the character X."

### 8. 紅書讀法 - *Red-character method*

- **Starting character:** 仁 *rén* (humaneness, kindness)
- **Meter:** seven-character lines, four-line poems
- **Method:** 逐字逐句逆讀，俱成回文 - "character by character, line by line, read in reverse, all become palindromes"

### 9. 紅書讀法 井欄式 - *Red, the "well-frame" configuration*

A second method over the same red region, named after the square-frame structure of an old well-mouth (井欄). Two starting points and two reversal rules are given:

- **From 欽** *qīn* (reverent), reading line by line in reverse (自沉字起，逐句逆讀 - note: ctext gives 沉, likely a typographic slip for 欽 or a second start-point in another edition; both are attested)
- **From 沙** *shā* (sand), reading character by character in reverse (自沙字起，逐字逆讀)
- **Meter:** seven-character lines

### 10. 黑書讀法 - *Black-character method*

- **Starting character:** 嗟 *jiē* (an exclamation: alas, ah)
- **Meter:** three-character lines, twelve-line poems (三言十二句)
- **Methods:** the black region supports several distinct passes:
  - **反復讀** - back-and-forth reading
  - **左右分讀** - left-right divided reading (read the left half independently from the right half)
  - **半段回環讀** - half-segment circular reading (the half-section forms a closed circle)

### 11. 藍書讀法 - *Blue-character method*

- **Starting character:** specified as 中行各借一字 - "borrow one character from each line of the center row," i.e. the start point is constructed from the center rather than from a single fixed character
- **Meter:** both four-character (四言) and six-character (六言) lines are produced from this region
- **Methods:**
    - **分讀** - divided reading (sub-regions read independently)
    - **左右遞退** - left-right progressive retirement (the "退一字" pattern applied left-to-right and right-to-left in alternation)

### 12. 紫書讀法 - *Purple-character method*

- **Starting character:** 歲寒 *suì hán* (the cold of the year - by classical convention, "the bitter end of winter", and by extension steadfastness in adversity)
- **Meter:** five-character lines, four-line poems (五言四句)
- **Methods:** the richest set of any single region
  - **反覆讀** - back-and-forth reading (palendromic)
  - **蛇行讀** - serpentine / snake-like reading (boustrophedon turns at each line-end)
  - **從外讀入** - read from the outside inward (spiral toward the centre)
  - **從內讀出** - read from the inside outward (spiral outward from the centre)

## IV. Specialised Techniques

These are general operators applied across multiple regions, they don't define any region by themselves but are used in combination with the methods above.

### 13. 間一句 / 間二句順讀 - *Interval readings*

Skip-one-line or skip-two-line reading: read line 1, skip line 2, read line 3 (間一句); or read line 1, skip lines 2 and 3, read line 4 (間二句). Both forward and reverse senses are admitted.

Combined with **兩邊分讀** (read from both edges in toward the centre) and **上下分讀** (read top and bottom halves separately).

### 14. 羅文分讀 - *Lattice-pattern divided reading*

Applied chiefly to the black region, reads characters in a lattice / open-weave pattern, with left-right interval variation. The "羅" is the loose-weave silk; the metaphor is that the reading skips through the grid the way light passes through gauze.

### 15. 蛇行讀 - *Serpentine / boustrophedon reading*

The general snake-reading: each line reverses direction relative to the previous, producing a continuous walk that turns at every edge. Used both as a primary method (in the purple region, above) and as a modifier on other methods.

--- 

## V. Structural Notes

The source page uses the formula 以下X段讀俱同前 - *"the following X sections are read by the same method as above"* - repeatedly, indicating that many of the named methods are applied iteratively across multiple grid sub-regions or sub-blocks. The page contains at least **fifteen to sixteen** such repeated-application sequences, but the precise sub-region partition for each iteration is not given on this page.

The ~7,000-readings figure that recurs in scholarship on the Star Gauge derives from totaling the products of these iterated applications - i.e. each named method, multiplied by the number of sub-regions it iterates over, multiplied by the number of distinct passes (forward, reverse, palindromic, etc.) admitted within the method.
