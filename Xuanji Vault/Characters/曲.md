**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; "Say" radical 曰; p.502 #02)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 丘玉. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 區玉 — sounding like 䱡, i.e. **qū**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "It images the shape of a vessel that receives things." Yijing, *Xici*: "it shapes all things in their windings (曲) and omits none"; subcommentary: "bent and minutely detailed." Shu, *Hong fan*: "Wood [means] 'crooked or straight (曲直)'"; tradition: "wood can be bent crooked or straight."

**Senses.**
- *The inmost windings (of the heart).* Shijing, *Qin feng*: "it troubles the windings of my heart (心曲)"; tradition: "心曲 means the heart's intricacies." Liji, *Qu li*, *Shiwen*: "*Qu li* means the detailed, point-by-point exposition of ritual."
- *Small matters.* Liji, *Zhongyong*: "next he attends to the particular (曲)"; commentary: "曲 is as it were the small and minor"; Zhu Xi's note: "one-sided, partial."
- *A frame for rearing silkworms (蠶簿).* Shuowen, alternate explanation. Liji, *Yue ling*: "they prepare the *qu*, the stands, the round and square baskets"; commentary: "the implements for rearing silkworms; 曲 is the *bo* (frame)." Former Han, *Biography of Zhou Bo*: "he made his living weaving silkworm-frames (簿曲)"; commentary: "reed frames are the *qu*."
- *A tune, a melody.* Song Yu, *Reply to the King of Chu*: "the higher the tune (曲), the fewer can join the harmony."
- *Surname.* Shiji, *Biography of Meng Tian*: the Imperial Censor Qu Gong.
- *Read 踽 (jǔ).* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 顆羽 — a place name. Shiji, *House of Chancellor Cao*: "he encamped at Quyu (曲遇)." Shiji, *House of Chancellor Chen*: "Chen Ping was further made Marquis of Quni (曲逆)."
- *Rhymes,* fanqie 區聿 (*Yunbu*). Liu Zhi, *Rhapsody on the Lu Capital*: "crags and chasms wind round and apart, the steep peaks hidden and bent (曲); fierce beasts lie deep in hiding, scaly fowl crouch concealed.
