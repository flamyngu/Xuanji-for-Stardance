## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈未集上〉【米字部】頁907第16【廣韻】方吻切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】府吻切，𠀤分上聲。【篇海】米粉。【釋名】分也。硏米使分散也。【周禮·天官】修籩之實，糗餌粉餈。【註】豆屑也。　又【說文】傅面者也。【韻會】古傅面亦用米粉。又染之爲紅粉，後乃燒鉛爲粉。【博物志】燒鉛成胡粉。【釋名】胡粉，胡餬也，脂和以塗面也。　又【正韻】設采潤色謂之粉澤。　又煆石爲白灰，塗牆壁曰粉。【白居易詩】文昌新入有光輝，紫界金牆白粉闈。【註】尙書省皆以粉圖壁，畫古賢列士，曰畫省，亦曰粉省。　又【事文類聚】海粉母如墨魚形，大三四寸，冬畜家中，春種海濵田內，色綠如荷包，海粉卽所溲也。或曰插竹枝田中，母緣枝吐出成粉。　又【正字通】凡物磑之如屑者，皆爲粉。粉通稱，非獨米也。　又竹名。【閩部疏】粉竹舂絲，爲佳紙料者，美于江南之白苧。　又地名。【水經】堵水出自上粉縣。【注】孟達爲太守，治房陵，故縣有粉水，縣居其上，故曰上粉。取此水漬粉，皓耀鮮潔，縣水皆取名焉。　又【集韻】方問切，音奮。傳也。飾也。【字彙補】粉，白飾也，从上聲。所以傳物曰粉，从去聲。《字彙》皆作府刎切，非。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, upper; Rice radical 米; p.907 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 方吻. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 府吻 — the rising tone of 分 (fěn).

**Etymology and senses.** Pianhai: "rice-flour (米粉)." *Shiming*: "粉 means to part (分): grinding rice to make it disperse." Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the contents of the dried-food basket: parched grain, cakes, and flour-cakes (粉餈)"; note: "bean-flour (豆屑)."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "what one applies to the face." *Yun Hui*: "of old, face-powder too used rice-flour; it was dyed into rouge (紅粉), and later lead was burnt to make powder." Bowu zhi: "burning lead makes white lead (胡粉)." *Shiming*: "胡粉 is hu-paste (胡餬): mixed with grease to coat the face."
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: laying on color to enrich the hue is called 粉澤 (embellishment).
- *Also,* calcining stone into white lime to coat walls is called 粉. Bai Juyi's poem: "newly entered at Wenchang, all is radiant — purple bounds, golden walls, the white-washed (白粉) gates"; note: "the Department of State Affairs all had its walls whitewashed (粉), figured with ancient worthies and famous men, and was called the Painted Department, or the Powder Department (粉省)."
- *Also* Shiwen leiju: "the sea-powder mother (海粉母) is shaped like a cuttlefish, three or four inches large; in winter it is kept at home, in spring sown in shore-fields; green as a lotus-pouch in color, the sea-powder (海粉) is what it excretes. Some say one sticks bamboo twigs in the field, and the mother spits it out along the twigs to form powder."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "whatever thing is ground to crumbs is 粉; 粉 is a general term, not for rice alone."
- *Also a bamboo-name.* Min bu shu: "the powder-bamboo (粉竹), pounded into fiber, makes a fine paper-stock, finer than the white ramie of Jiangnan."
- *Also a place-name.* Shuijing: "the Du River issues from Shangfen county (上粉縣)"; note: "Meng Da was prefect, governing at Fangling; the old county had the Fen River (粉水), and the county lay above it, hence Shangfen (上粉). Steeping powder in this water made it dazzling white and clean; county and river both took their name from it."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 方問, sounding like 奮 (fèn): to apply (傳); to adorn (飾). Zihui bu: "粉 as 'white adornment' is in the rising tone; 粉 as 'what is applied to a thing' is in the departing tone; the Zihui makes both fanqie 府刎, which is wrong."
