**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, middle; Silk radical зіё; p.925 #24)*

**Ancient graphs.** рӨ”•, еқ•.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*: fanqie еҸӨйқҲ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie е …йқҲ вҖ” sounding like ж¶Ү (jД«ng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the warp [of weaving] (з№”)." *Yupian*: "warp and weft, making up silk cloth." Yijing, *Zhun*: "the gentleman accordingly orders and weaves (з¶“з¶ё)"; subcommentary: "з¶“ means warp and weft."
- *Rightness, the norm (зҫ©).* Yijing, *Yi*: "going against the norm (з¶“) on the hill"; note: "з¶“ is like 'rightness.'"
- *Constant (еёё).* Shu, *Jiugao*: "constant in virtue (з¶“еҫ·), holding to wisdom"; tradition: "able to make virtue constant and to keep wisdom." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 25: "rite is the constant (з¶“) of Heaven"; note: "з¶“ is the constancy of the Way."
- *To measure, plan (еәҰ).* Shijing, *Daya*: "he planned it and laid it out (з¶“д№ӢзҮҹд№Ӣ)"; tradition: "з¶“ means to measure it."
- *To lay out (a north-south line).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhongzai*: "to mark out the capital and lay out (з¶“) the countryside"; note: "з¶“ means to make its measures"; subcommentary: "a north-south road is the з¶“ (warp), an east-west road the з·Ҝ (weft)."
- *A law, rule (жі•).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Da zai*: "to govern (з¶“) the states"; note: "з¶“ means a law."
- *The [celestial] order.* Liji, *Yueling*: "lose not the order (з¶“зҙҖ)"; note: "the advancing and retreating measures of the heavens." Liji, *Jingjie* subcommentary: "the *Jingjie* ('Explanation of the Classics') is so named because it records the merits and faults of the Six Arts in governance and teaching."
- *To order one's sphere.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 25: "to make husband and wife, outer and inner, and so order (з¶“) the two spheres"; note: "the husband governs without, the wife within."
- *To hang oneself (иҮӘз¶“).* Lunyu: "to hang oneself in a ditch with none to know"; He Yan's note: "з¶“ is to die by hanging in a ditch." The Jinyu: "[Shensheng] hanged himself (йӣүз¶“) at the Xincheng shrine"; Shiming: "to bend the neck and stop the breath is йӣүз¶“, as the snared pheasant does."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie еҸӨе®ҡ, sounding like еҫ‘ (jГ¬ng) вҖ” "warp and weft"; also "to weave." Lisao, Wang Yi's note: "з¶“ is еҫ‘ (a path)." *Shiming*: "з¶“ is a path: like a footway it goes everywhere and may be ever used."
