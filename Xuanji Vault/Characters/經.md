**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Silk radical 糸; p.925 #24)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𤔕, 坕.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*: fanqie 古靈. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 堅靈 — sounding like 涇 (jīng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the warp [of weaving] (織)." *Yupian*: "warp and weft, making up silk cloth." Yijing, *Zhun*: "the gentleman accordingly orders and weaves (經綸)"; subcommentary: "經 means warp and weft."
- *Rightness, the norm (義).* Yijing, *Yi*: "going against the norm (經) on the hill"; note: "經 is like 'rightness.'"
- *Constant (常).* Shu, *Jiugao*: "constant in virtue (經德), holding to wisdom"; tradition: "able to make virtue constant and to keep wisdom." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 25: "rite is the constant (經) of Heaven"; note: "經 is the constancy of the Way."
- *To measure, plan (度).* Shijing, *Daya*: "he planned it and laid it out (經之營之)"; tradition: "經 means to measure it."
- *To lay out (a north-south line).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhongzai*: "to mark out the capital and lay out (經) the countryside"; note: "經 means to make its measures"; subcommentary: "a north-south road is the 經 (warp), an east-west road the 緯 (weft)."
- *A law, rule (法).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Da zai*: "to govern (經) the states"; note: "經 means a law."
- *The [celestial] order.* Liji, *Yueling*: "lose not the order (經紀)"; note: "the advancing and retreating measures of the heavens." Liji, *Jingjie* subcommentary: "the *Jingjie* ('Explanation of the Classics') is so named because it records the merits and faults of the Six Arts in governance and teaching."
- *To order one's sphere.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 25: "to make husband and wife, outer and inner, and so order (經) the two spheres"; note: "the husband governs without, the wife within."
- *To hang oneself (自經).* Lunyu: "to hang oneself in a ditch with none to know"; He Yan's note: "經 is to die by hanging in a ditch." The Jinyu: "[Shensheng] hanged himself (雉經) at the Xincheng shrine"; Shiming: "to bend the neck and stop the breath is 雉經, as the snared pheasant does."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 古定, sounding like 徑 (jìng) — "warp and weft"; also "to weave." Lisao, Wang Yi's note: "經 is 徑 (a path)." *Shiming*: "經 is a path: like a footway it goes everywhere and may be ever used."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 居良.* Han Yu, *"This day is much to be regretted"*: "...the parting has not been long, but much hardship have I passed through (經)" (情 sounds 翔).

**On the graph.** Wujing wenzi: "to write it 𦀰 is a corruption.
