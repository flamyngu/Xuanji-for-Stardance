**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҜ…, lower; Shelter radical е№ҝ; p.351 #03)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie еҸӨжҷғ вҖ” the rising tone of е…ү (guЗҺng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the great roof of a hall." Also *Yupian*: "е»Ј means great (еӨ§)." *Guangyun*: "е»Ј means broad (й—Ҡ)." Yijing, *Xici*: "broad and great (е»ЈеӨ§), it matches Heaven and Earth"; subcommentary: "the great matches Heaven, the broad matches Earth."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Geography*: "the Lingnan circuit has Guangzhou (е»Је·һ)."
- *Also a surname.* Xingpu: "from Danyang, descendants of Guangchengzi; the Song had Guang Han (е»Јжјў)."
- *Also* *Yupian*: fanqie еҸӨжӣ  вҖ” the departing tone of е…ү (guГ ng). Zhouli, *Diguan, Da situ*: "to know fully the figures of the breadth and length (е»ЈијӘ) of the lands of the Nine Provinces"; subcommentary: "Ma Rong says: east-west is е»Ј (breadth), north-south is ијӘ (length)"; Shiwen: "е»Ј, fanqie еҸӨжӣ ."
- *Also a chariot-name.* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Chepu*: "the reserve of the broad-chariots (е»Ји»Ҡ)"; note: "the е»Ји»Ҡ is the chariot for ranging across in line"; Shiwen: "е»Ј, fanqie еҸӨжӣ ." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "the Western е»Ј and the Eastern Palace guards"; subcommentary: "Chu had a Left and Right е»Ј вҖ” names of war-chariot divisions." Xuan yr 2: "divided into two е»Ј"; note: "fifteen chariots make one е»Ј."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: pronounced жӣ  (kuГ ng). Xunzi, *Wangba*: "why does the ruler not take his ease (е»Ј)?"; note: "е»Ј is the look of being open and at ease."
- *Also interchangeable with жӣ  (to leave idle).* Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "when an army goes out past its season, this is called е»Ј (idling)."
- *Also* fanqie е§‘й»ғ, sounding like е…ү (guДҒng). Erya, *Shishou*: "a whorl of hair on the back вҖ” the quГЁguДҒng (й—Ӣе»Ј)"; subcommentary: "in Bo Le's method of judging horses, one with a whorl of hair on the back is named quГЁguДҒng"; the Yinyi: "е»Ј is pronounced е…ү."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie жһңдә”, sounding like еҸӨ (gЗ”). Liji, *Yue ji*: "now the ancient music вҖ” advancing in rank and retiring in rank, harmonious, upright, and broad (е»Ј); the strings, the gourd, the mouth-organ, and the reed wait together upon the hand-drum; it begins its performance with the civil and concludes its movement with the martial."
