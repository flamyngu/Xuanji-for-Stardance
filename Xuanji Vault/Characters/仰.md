**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еӯҗ, middle; Person radical дәә; p.94 #08)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йӯҡе…©. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иӘһе…© вҖ” sounding like рҰҜ’ (yЗҺng).

**Etymology and senses.** To lift the head and gaze (иҲүйҰ–жңӣ). Yijing, *Xi ci*: "looking up (д»°), one observes the patterns of the sky." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "now resting, now lying back (еҒғд»°)."

**Senses.**
- *To admire in the heart is called дјҒд»° (to look up to).*
- *Also* to charge an inferior by a superior's command is called д»°. Today, in official dispatches, where a superior addresses an inferior, the graph д»° is used. Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wen*: "he decreed that the rites and forms of the Three Honoured Guests be fixed, this too being referred (д»°иӯ°) for deliberation"; note: "д»°иӯ° is like saying to deliberate at court."
- *Surname.*
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йӯҡеҗ‘, sounding like рЁӢ• (yГ ng). "To rely on (жҒғ); to wait on (дҝҹ); to draw supply from (иіҮ)." *Zhanguo ce*: "the people of Eastern Zhou may be made wholly to depend (д»°) on Western Zhou"; note: "having an expectation of those above, one looks up (д»°)." Shiji, *Treatise on the Balanced Standard*: "for clothing and food they relied (д»°) on the district office for supply."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie з–‘еүӣ, sounding like жҳӮ (ГЎng). Zhouli, *Di guan, Baoshi*, note on the bearing for the host: "the bearing in the host is fierce and high-headed (д»°д»°)"; *Shiwen*: "fanqie дә”еүӣ, also written еҚ¬." A note: by the *Ji Yun*, еҚ¬ is the abbreviated form of д»°. Also Chuci, *Bu ju*: "had I better be high and proud (жҳӮжҳӮ) like a thousand-li colt?" Shijing, *Daya*: "grave and lofty (йЎ’йЎ’еҚ¬еҚ¬)"; the *Han shi waizhuan* writes зӣҺзӣҺ вҖ” so it runs together with жҳӮ and зӣҺ. The ancients used many graphs by loan; one need not follow them in all.
- *Also* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Xiaozongbo*: "he marks the altars of the Five Emperors at the four suburbs." Of the Five Emperors, the Azure is called Lingweiyang (йқҲеЁҒд»°), the title of the Green Emperor.
