## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈子集中〉【人字部】頁94第08【唐韻】【正韻】魚兩切【集韻】【韻會】語兩切，𠀤音𦯒。舉首望也。【易·繫辭】仰以觀于天文。【詩·小雅】或棲遲偃仰。　又心慕曰企仰。　又以尊命𤰞曰仰。今公家文移，上行下，用仰字。【前漢·孝文帝紀】詔定三恪禮儀體式，亦仰議之。【註】仰議，猶言議於朝也。　又姓。　又【集韻】【正韻】𠀤魚向切，音𨋕。恃也。俟也，資也。【戰國策】東周之民，可令一仰西周。【註】有望於上則仰。【史記·平準書】衣食仰給縣官。　又【韻會】疑剛切，音昂。【周禮·地官·保氏軍旅之容註】軍旅之容，闞闞仰仰。【釋文】五剛反，亦作卬。◎按《集韻》卬本仰字省文。又《楚辭·卜居》寧昂昂若千里之駒乎。《詩·大雅》顒顒卬卬。《韓詩外傳》作盎盎，則與昂、盎𠀤通矣。古人字多假借，未可盡从。　又【周禮·春官·小宗伯】兆五帝于四郊。五帝蒼曰：靈威仰，靑帝號也。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.94 #08)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 魚兩. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 語兩 — sounding like 𦯒 (yǎng).

**Etymology and senses.** To lift the head and gaze (舉首望). Yijing, *Xi ci*: "looking up (仰), one observes the patterns of the sky." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "now resting, now lying back (偃仰)."

**Senses.**
- *To admire in the heart is called 企仰 (to look up to).*
- *Also* to charge an inferior by a superior's command is called 仰. Today, in official dispatches, where a superior addresses an inferior, the graph 仰 is used. Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wen*: "he decreed that the rites and forms of the Three Honoured Guests be fixed, this too being referred (仰議) for deliberation"; note: "仰議 is like saying to deliberate at court."
- *Surname.*
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 魚向, sounding like 𨋕 (yàng). "To rely on (恃); to wait on (俟); to draw supply from (資)." *Zhanguo ce*: "the people of Eastern Zhou may be made wholly to depend (仰) on Western Zhou"; note: "having an expectation of those above, one looks up (仰)." Shiji, *Treatise on the Balanced Standard*: "for clothing and food they relied (仰) on the district office for supply."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie 疑剛, sounding like 昂 (áng). Zhouli, *Di guan, Baoshi*, note on the bearing for the host: "the bearing in the host is fierce and high-headed (仰仰)"; *Shiwen*: "fanqie 五剛, also written 卬." A note: by the *Ji Yun*, 卬 is the abbreviated form of 仰. Also Chuci, *Bu ju*: "had I better be high and proud (昂昂) like a thousand-li colt?" Shijing, *Daya*: "grave and lofty (顒顒卬卬)"; the *Han shi waizhuan* writes 盎盎 — so it runs together with 昂 and 盎. The ancients used many graphs by loan; one need not follow them in all.
- *Also* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Xiaozongbo*: "he marks the altars of the Five Emperors at the four suburbs." Of the Five Emperors, the Azure is called Lingweiyang (靈威仰), the title of the Green Emperor.
