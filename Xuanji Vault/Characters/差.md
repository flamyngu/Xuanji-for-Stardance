**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, middle; Work radical 工; p.326 #02)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢀩.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 初牙. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 初加 — sounding like 杈 (chā).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A second, a discrepancy (貳); not tallying (不相値)." Xu Kai says: "to err in a matter (左于事) is not to tally rightly." *Guangyun*: "awry (舛)." *Yun Hui*: "the sense of error (差錯)." Shu, *Lü xing*: "examine the testimony for discrepancy (差)." Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "miss by a hair's breadth, and you err (差) by a thousand li."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Erya, *Shi gu*: "to choose (擇)"; *Shiwen*: "差 sounded 㐅." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "I have chosen (差) my horses."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 楚宜. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 叉宜. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 叉茲 — sounding like 縒 (cī). *Guangyun*: "a grading (次); not even (不齊等)." Later Han, *Biography of Xun Shuang*: "the Son of Heaven takes twelve wives, and from the feudal lords down each has his grade (等差)."
- *Also* *Yupian*: 參差 (cenci), uneven. *Yun Hui*: "參差, the look of tangled silk; three threads crossing is 參, two threads crossing is 差." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "uneven (參差) the floating-heart waterweed." *Fengsu tong*: "Shun made the Xiaoshao in nine movements, and the phoenix came in homage; its form was uneven (參差), like the phoenix's wings."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: 參差 is the pan-pipe (洞簫). Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "blowing the pan-pipe (參差) — for whom do I yearn?"
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: 差池 (chichi), the flight of swallows. Shijing, *Bei feng*: "the swallows in their flight, uneven (差池) their wings."
- *Also* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 楚佳. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 初佳 — sounding like 釵 (chāi). *Yun Hui*: "to dispatch on duty (差使)." Emperor Xuanzong of Tang's decree: "for all corvée matters, let the dispatching (差) be done by rotation."
- *Also* to choose (擇). *Guangyun*: "to select (簡)." Shijing, *Chen feng*: "a fair morning is chosen (差)"; *Shiwen*: "差, Zheng's fanqie 初佳; Wang sounds it 嗟; Xu's fanqie 七何."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: 差殊 (chashu), also uneven. Liji, *Wang zhi*: "for commoners holding office, their stipend is graded (差) by this"; *Shiwen*: "差, fanqie 初佳; Xu's fanqie 初宜."
- *To overstep, transgress (僭差).* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Da zongbo*: "by military rite he unites the states"; note: "to unite means to overawe those discordant and overstepping (僭差)"; *Shiwen*: "差, fanqie 初佳."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: Fuchai (夫差), the name of a king of Wu.
- *Chafen (差分), a method of reckoning.* Zhouli, *Di guan, Jiushu* note: "there is chafen, what is now called chongcha (重差)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 倉何, sounding like 磋 (cuō). *Ji Yun*: "to rinse rice (淅)." Liji, *Sang da ji*: "the attendant rinses (差) the hair-wash above the hall"; note: "差 means to rinse; one rinses the rice and takes the water (潘) for the wash."
- *Also* *Yunbu*: "差 means to overpass (過)." Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "Tang and Yu, grave and reverent, and Zhou weighing the Way without erring (莫差)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: Jingcha (景差), the name of a man of Chu.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 楚懈 — the departing tone of 釵 (chài). *Guangyun*: "sickness ending (病除)." *Ji Yun*: "to recover (瘉)." Weizhi, *Biography of Zhang Liao*: "the sickness somewhat mended (小差)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "差 means to compare (較)." Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 12 note: "to pull down the banner and throw it on the yoke, so that it does not catch the wind, makes it somewhat (差) the lighter"; *Shiwen*: "差, fanqie 初賣."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 楚嫁 — the departing tone of 杈 (chà). To differ (差異). Han Yu, *Poem of the rapids-officer*: "the hurricane at times arises, tossing and heaving — truly a strange affair (差事)."
- *Also* interchangeable with 磋. *Yun Hui*: "磋 is sometimes written 差."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: anciently interchangeable with 嗟; the note is given in full under the Mouth radical, ten strokes.

**Textual notes.** Liji, *Sang da ji*, "the attendant rinses the hair-wash above the hall; note: 差 means to rinse; one rinses the rice and takes its 瀋 for the wash" — checked against the original, 瀋 is corrected to 潘. Also Qu Yuan, *Li sao*, "Tang and Yu, grave and reverent, and 同 weighing the Way without erring" — checked against the original, 同 is corrected to 周 (Zhou).
