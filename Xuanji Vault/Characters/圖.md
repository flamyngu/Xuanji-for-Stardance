**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Enclosure radical 囗; p.220 #18)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𡈖, 𡇴.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 同都, sounding like 徒 (tú).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To plan and scheme at what is hard (計畫難). It follows 囗 (enclosure) and 啚; 啚 is the sense of difficulty." Xu Kai: "to draw a chart (圖畫), one must first lay it out by rule, hence it follows 囗; 啚 is the sense of stinting and difficulty."

**Senses.**
- *To plan, scheme (謀).* Erya, *Shi gu*. Shu, *Tai jia*: "be careful of your frugal virtue, cherishing only the long-term plan (永圖)." Also *Jun ya*: "think on its hardships, to plan (圖) its ease, and the people will be at peace." Also Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Da xing ren*: "in spring he gives audience to the feudal lords and deliberates (圖) the Son of Heaven's affairs"; note: "the king sees the lords in spring, and so weighs whether their affairs may be done or not."
- *To gauge, weigh (度).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "this we probe and this we weigh (圖) — is it truly so?" Lunyu: "I never imagined (圖) that music could reach such a height!"
- *To put in order, root out (除治).* Zuozhuan, Yin yr 1: "do not let it spread; spreading, it is hard to deal with (圖)."
- *To reckon, count (計).* Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Xiao sikou*: "in early winter, sacrificing to the people's registrar, he presented the population-count to the king; the king bowed and received it, to reckon (圖) the state's expenditure, and to advance or reduce it."
- *河圖 (the River Chart).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the He brought forth the Chart (圖), the Luo brought forth the Writing, and the sages took them as pattern." Kong Anguo: "the River Chart — when Fuxi reigned, a dragon-horse came forth from the He, and he then took its markings as the pattern to draw the eight trigrams." *Tongjian*: "Emperor Guangwu of Han read the River Chart's *Huichang fu*: 'the ninth of the Red Liu shall be charged at Mount Dai.'" *Chunqiu wei*: "the River Chart has nine chapters."
- *版圖 (the registers and maps).* Zhouli, *Tian guan, Gong zheng*: "he made the registers (版) for it, in readiness"; *Shiwen*: "版 is the name-registers, 圖 is the land-maps." Also *Di guan, Da situ*: "by the maps (圖) of the realm's lands, to know fully the breadth and length of the Nine Provinces' territories." Also *Xia guan, Zhifang shi*: "he managed the realm's maps (圖), to govern the realm's lands"; note: "the 圖 is like today's Minister of Works' commandery-and-kingdom and land maps." Shiji, *Hereditary House of the Marquis of Zan*: "when the Lord of Pei reached Xianyang, Xiao He alone entered first and seized the Qin chancellor's and censor's statutes, ordinances, maps and books (圖書), so coming to know fully the realm's strongholds and passes, the size of its populations, and where it was strong or weak."
- *圖讖 (the prophetic charts), books of divination and portent.* Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "Li Tong persuaded the emperor by the prophetic charts (圖讖)." Also: "in Zhongyuan year 1, he proclaimed the prophetic charts (圖讖) throughout the realm."
- *圖象 (images, figures).* Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Si yue*: "lesser bonds and contracts are written on the red figure (丹圖)"; note: "lesser bonds are the covenants of the myriad people; the red figure is the carved vessels — the fu and gui and the like — that bear figures (圖象)." He Yan, *Jingfu dian fu*: "figures (圖象), in olden days, served as admonition and rule." Wang Yanshou, *Lu Lingguang dian fu*: "they figured (圖畫) Heaven and Earth, and the kinds of the living throng."
- *浮圖 (futu), the Buddhist teaching.* Also a monastery-pagoda is called 浮圖. Du Fu has the poem *Matching Gao Shi: Climbing the Pagoda of Ci'en Temple*. Also *Wang Junyu, Guolao tanyuan*: "Li Yunze governed Xiongzhou and drew on the treasury to build a pagoda (浮圖); the supervising office impeached him, and Zhenzong had him quietly questioned. Yunze said: 'it is not that I attend to Buddhism — it is truly to raise a watchtower for the border country.'" Han Yu, *Tomb-inscription for Wang Zhongshu*: "he forbade monks and Daoists to set up pagodas (浮圖) within his bounds, because they swindle and beg for profit, robbing the registered commoners of their property."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 吐 (tǔ), fanqie 他魯.* Shijing, *Daya*: "we weigh and plan (圖) it, and only Zhongshan Fu can lift it." *Yilin*: "schemed against (圖) by the bond-servants, ranked among the common herd."
