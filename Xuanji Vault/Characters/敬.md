**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Rap radical 攴; p.473 #35)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣀖, 𢿩.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 居慶, sounding like 竟 (jìng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Reverent (肅)." *Shiming*: "敬 means to be on guard (警); ever keeping oneself grave and watchful." *Yupian*: "respectful (恭); heedful (愼)." Yijing, *Kun* hexagram: "the gentleman by reverence (敬) makes the inner upright, and by right makes the outer square." Shu, *Hong fan*: "reverently (敬) use the five matters." Zhouli, *Tian guan, Xiaozai*: "the third is uprightness and reverence (廉敬)"; note: "敬 is not to be slack in one's post." Liji, *Qu li*: "let there be nothing unreverent (毋不敬)"; note: "ritual centres on reverence." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 33: "reverence (敬) is the gathering of virtue." Lunyu: "to cultivate oneself with reverence (敬)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Later Han, *Biography of Zhou Xie*: "Emperor An invited Xie with black-and-crimson silk and lamb-gifts; Xie thereupon went of himself to Yangcheng in Yingchuan, sent a student to bear his thanks (送敬), and so pleaded illness and went home"; note: "送敬 is like to tender thanks."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "descendants of Jing Zhong of Chen; the Han had Jing Xin, Inspector of Yangzhou."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 精 (jīng), fanqie 居卿.* Cao Zhi, *Cheju wan fu*: "how mighty and strange this marvel — truly at one look it wins our awe (敬); though one had the keen sight of Li Zhu, still he would be dazzled and lose his sharpness (精)."
