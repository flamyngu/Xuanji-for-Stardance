**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.98 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 胡歌. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 寒歌 — the level tone of 賀, i.e. **hé**.

**Senses.** An interrogative word: "曷 (what), 奚 (what), 孰 (which)." Shu, *Gaoyao mo*: "Yu said, 'What (何)?'" Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "how goes the night (夜如何其)?"
- *誰何, to challenge ("who goes there").* Like saying "none dares do anything." Jia Yi, *Faulting Qin*: "he set out sharp weapons and challenged all comers (誰何)."
- *無何 / 無幾何, in no long while.* Shiji, *Biography of Cao Can*: "after no long while (居無何), the envoy did summon Can." Former Han, *Biography of Yuan Ang*: "the south is low and damp; if you can drink daily and do nothing else (無何), you may avoid disaster"; note: "無何 means there being no other business."
- *突何, a hat (a loanword).* Nanshi, *Western Regions*: "in the Western Regions they call a hat 突何."
- *Melody-syllables.* *Gujin yuelu*: "羊無夷伊那何, all surviving sounds of tunes."
- *A state.* Suishu: "in the Western Regions there is the state of He."
- *Surname.*
- *娙何, a Han female-official title,* ranked equivalent to 2,000 bushels.

**Rising tone (*hè*).** *Ji Yun, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 下可, the rising tone of 賀 — same as 荷: "to bear (儋), to carry (負)." Yijing, *Shi he*: "he bears the cangue (何校), his ears destroyed." Shijing, *Cao feng*: "bearing dagger-axe and staff (何戈與祋)." *Xiaoya*: "bearing straw-cape and hat (何簑何笠)." *Shang song*: "the hundred blessings these he bears (何)."
- *Same as 訶 (hē, to scold).* Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "great rebuke, great reproof (大何)"; note: "譴, to reprove; 何, to interrogate (詰問)."
