**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Dry radical 干; p.339 #03)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𤔁, 𠀭.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 符兵. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 蒲兵. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蒲明 — sounding like 苹 (píng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "平 is the even, easy flow of speech (語平舒)." *Guangyun*: "平 means upright, correct (正)." *Zengyun*: "平 means level, flat (坦)." Yijing, *Tai* hexagram: "there is no level (平) without a slope."

**Senses.**
- *Harmonious (和).* *Guangyun*. Shu, *Yao dian*: "he harmonized and made bright (平章) the hundred clans"; tradition: "平和章明, harmonized and brightly displayed"; subcommentary: "concorded and made manifest among the clans and surnames of the hundred officers."
- *To settle, conclude (成) — to dissolve resentment and make peace.* Erya, *Shi gu*: "平 means to complete (成)." Chunqiu, Xuan yr 15: "the men of Song and the men of Chu made peace (平)." Guliang zhuan: "平 means to complete — approving that they measured their strength and returned to right"; note: "each knew his own strength and could not master the other, and so returned to the principle of harmony." Zuozhuan subcommentary: "平 means harmony — meaning they were formerly not at peace, and now first became at peace."
- *To govern, bring order (治).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "the earth was ordered (平) and Heaven complete"; tradition: "when water and land are well-governed it is called 平." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the uplands and lowlands are now ordered (平)."
- *To put in order (治之).* Shijing, *Daya*: "they trimmed it and levelled it (平之), the thickets and the saplings"; subcommentary: "what they dressed and levelled were the places of brushwood and of sapling-trees."
- *Pacified, subdued (平服).* Shijing, *Daya*: "the four quarters being now subdued (平), the royal domain is nearly settled"; subcommentary: "the four quarters being already pacified, within the royal domain there may happily be settlement."
- *Equal, even (齊等, 均).* *Yupian*; *Zengyun*. Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "the clouds move and the rain falls, and the world is at peace (平)"; subcommentary: "the world universally gains its benefit, evenly and level, with no partiality or slant." Shu, *Jun shi*: "Heaven grants long life, even and exact (平格)"; subcommentary, *Correct Meaning*: "平 means that governance and teaching are even-handed."
- *To equalize taxation (均賦).* *Guangya*. Shiji, *Treatise on the Balanced Standard*, note: Suoyin, "among the subordinate officers of the Grand Minister of Agriculture there were the Director and Aide of the Balanced Standard (平準), who equalized the levies and transports of the realm's commanderies and kingdoms — selling when dear, buying up when cheap. They balanced (平) the levies against one another and forwarded them to the capital, hence the name Balanced Standard."
- *Of musical notes, not overstepping one another.* *Discourses of Zhou*: "music follows harmony, and harmony follows evenness (平)."
- *Easy (易).* Erya, *Shi gu*: "平 means easy"; subcommentary: "易 means not difficult." Later Han, *Biography of Ban Chao*: "Ren Shang said: 'I supposed Lord Ban would have some remarkable strategy, but what he has said is just commonplace (平平).'"
- *A good harvest (歲稔).* Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "a second ripening is called 平, a third ripening 太平 (great peace)."
- *In posthumous titles (諡法):* "to manage affairs with regulation is called 平; to govern without fault is called 平; to set out the bonds and order the rules is called 平."
- *A great plain.* Erya, *Shi di*: "a great wild is called 平"; subcommentary: "the marsh of the great wild is also named 平." Han Yu, *Linked verses south of the city*: "the sand-seal stamps the winding flat (平)"; note: Hong Qingshan, "Mount Hua has the Green-Bough Flat (靑柯平) and the Herb-Planting Flat (種藥平), named from the level places of the land."
- *The twelfth month is called 嘉平 (Jiaping).* Shiji, *Annals of the First Emperor of Qin*: "he renamed the La-month 嘉平"; note: the *Inner Record of Mao Ying* says: "Ying's great-grandfather Meng ascended to heaven in broad daylight at Mount Hua. His town's ballad ran: 'The immortal who attained it was Mao Chucheng; he rode a dragon up into the Great Pure, now descending to the Dark Isle to sport at Red Wall; the succeeding age that goes is in my Ying; if the Emperor would learn it, let the La be Jiaping.' The First Emperor heard the ballad and asked its reason; the elders told him in full, and so he renamed the La-month 嘉平." Suoyin: "the *Guangya* says: under the Xia it was called the Clear Sacrifice (淸祀), under the Yin 嘉平, under the Zhou the Great Zha (大蜡), also La (臘); Qin renamed it 嘉平 — taking the Yin title because of the ballad's words."
- *華平 (Huaping), an auspicious tree.* Songshu, *Treatise on Auspicious Signs*: "the 華平 — its branches are perfectly level. When the ruler has virtue it grows; if his virtue is rigid the branches turn up, if his virtue is weak they bend down." Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "planting the 華平 in the spring garden"; note: "when the world is at peace, its blossoms are level."
- *廷尉平 (Tingwei-ping), an official title.* Shiji, *Biography of Zhang Shizhi*: "the Commandant of Justice is the balance (平) of the realm." Former Han, *Table of Officials*: "in Dijie year 3 of Emperor Xuan, the Left and Right Ping of the Commandant of Justice (廷尉左右平) were set up, ranked at six hundred bushels."
- *平原, 太平, 平陸 — all place-names.* Erya, *Shi di*: "a broad level is called 原, a high level is called 陸"; subcommentary: "a broad level is called 原 — the broad and level part of a marsh is also called 原; the Han made Pingyuan (平原) a commandery-name. A high level is called 陸 — land rich and abundant, properly named 陸." Also: "east to where the sun rises is 太平 (Great Peace); the people of Taiping are benevolent." Mengzi: "Mencius went to Pinglu (平陸)."
- *A prefecture-name.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Geography*: "Pingzhou, the Beiping commandery." *Guangyun*: "anciently the land of the two states of the mountain-Rong Guzhu and the White Di Bizi; under Qin it was Liaoxi commandery, under Sui Beiping commandery, under Tang Pingzhou (平州)."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: descendants of Yan Pingzhong, minister of Qi. Former Han, *Biography of Ping Dang*: "Dang was chancellor under Emperor Ai, and his son Yan rose to Grand Minister over the Masses." Also compound surnames: *He's Garden of Surnames* has the two clans Pingling (平陵) and Pingning (平寧); the *Register of Surnames*: "the Pingling clan are descendants of Pingling Lao of the Shiji."

**Further readings.**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 房連. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 毗連. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蒲眠 — sounding like 㛹 (pián). *Guangyun*: "平平 means to discern and govern (辨治)." Shu, *Hong fan*: "the kingly way is even and ordered (平平)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "even-handed (平平) on left and right." *Yun Hui*: "even (均)."
- *Yun Hui*: fanqie 皮命, sounding like 病 (bìng). "To fix the price of goods (平物賈)." Zhouli, *Di guan, Zhi ji*, note: "the standard price in the market (平賈) — what is now called the monthly fixing." Also *Di guan, Zhi ren*, note: "質 means to balance (平) — it governs the fixing of prices." Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "in a single bustling market there must be set a balance (平)." Later Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "early in Emperor Huan's reign, a children's ballad of the capital ran: 'Roaming-Ping (游平) buys office — there is a price of his own (平); he spares neither the mighty and worthy nor the great clans.'"

**On the graph.** Anciently interchangeable with 便 and 辯. Shiji, *Basic Annals of the Five Emperors*: "便章 the hundred clans"; note: Suoyin, "the old-script Shangshu writes 平; 平 being glossed as 便 (bian), it was made 便章; the modern-script writes 辯章. The old-script graph 平 is also written 便, and 便 is glossed as 辯, so it became 辯章."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 龎 (páng), fanqie 皮陽.* Zhang Ji, *Sacrificial verses for Han Yu*: "the north terrace looks over the paddy-fields, the lush wilds with much shade and cool; in the plank pavilion I sit dropping my line, and the vexing toil grows somewhat eased (平)."

**Editorial note.** *Ji Yun*: 拼 anciently was written 平 — see in full under the Hand radical, eight strokes.
