> 《康熙字典》〈酉集下〉【辛字部】頁1250第25【唐韻】息鄰切【集韻】【韻會】斯人切【正韻】斯鄰切，𠀤音新。【說文】秋時萬物成而熟，金剛味辛，辛痛卽泣出。【徐曰】言萬物初見斷制，故辛痛也。【書·洪範】金曰從革，從革作辛。【白虎通】金味所以辛者，西方煞傷成物，辛所以煞傷之也，猶五味得辛乃委煞也。　又歲、月、日之名。【爾雅·釋天】太歲在辛曰重光，月在辛曰塞。【禮·月令】其日庚辛。【註】辛之言新也。【前漢·律歷志】悉新于辛。【史記·律書】言萬物之辛生也。　又【正韻】葷味也。【風土記】元旦，以蔥、蒜、韭、蓼、蒿芥，雜和而食之，名五辛盤，取迎新之意。　又股象也。【說文】辛承庚，象人股。【徐曰】辛漸揫斂，故象人股，漸焦殺也。　又養筋之味也。【周禮·天官】以辛養筋。【註】辛，金味，金之纏合異物似筋，人之筋亦纏合諸骨，故以辛養之也。　又【正韻】苦辛，取辛酸之意。【李白詩】英豪未豹變，自古多艱辛。【杜甫詩】生離與死別，自古鼻酸辛。　又高辛，古帝號。　又姓。【史記·夏本紀贊】夏啟封支子于莘，因聲近攺爲辛。　又少辛，藥名。【本草】卽細辛也。　又叶宵前切，音先。【焦仲卿詩】奉事循公姥，進退敢自專。晝夜勤作息，伶俜縈苦辛。【正字通】按《說文》徐註，泣出象股之說，與𨐌義反。壬癸繼辛，天道剝中有復，秋德義中寓仁，非偏屬斷制焦殺。徐曲附《說文》，非。又《爾雅》重光𨐌，《史記》攺昭陽。昭陽癸，《史記》攺尚章。上章庚，《史記》攺商橫。當有譌誤。舊註：重光一曰昭陽，使不知者疑。《爾雅》歲陽在𨐌有二名，亦非。本作𨐌，《字彙》譌省作辛，與辛音愆字形相溷，尤非。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Bitter radical 辛; p.1250 #25)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 息鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 斯人. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 斯鄰 — sounding like 新 (xīn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "In autumn the myriad things are formed and ripened; metal, being hard, has the pungent (辛) taste; the pungent pains, and tears come forth." Xu (Kai): "it means the myriad things first meeting their cutting-off, hence the pungent pain." Shu, *Hong fan*: "metal is said to yield and change; yielding and changing makes the pungent (辛)." *Baihu tong*: "metal's taste is pungent because the west slays and wounds to complete things, and the pungent is what slays and wounds them — just as the five tastes, gaining the pungent, are then brought to an end."

**Senses.**
- *A name for years, months, and days.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year in 辛 is called Chongguang (重光); the month in 辛 is called Se (塞)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "its days are 庚 and 辛"; note: "辛 speaks of the new (新)." Former Han, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes and Calendar*: "all made new in 辛." Shiji, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes*: "it speaks of the myriad things' pungent birth (辛生)."
- *A pungent flavour (葷味).* *Zheng Yun*. *Fengtu ji*: "on New Year's Day, scallion, garlic, leek, knotweed, and wormwood-mustard are mixed and eaten together, named the five-pungent platter (五辛盤), taking the sense of welcoming the new."
- *The image of the thighs.* *Shuowen*: "辛 follows upon 庚, imaging a man's thighs." Xu (Kai): "辛 is the gradual gathering-in, hence it images a man's thighs, the gradual scorching and slaying."
- *A flavour that nourishes the sinews.* Zhouli, *Tian guan*: "with the pungent (辛) one nourishes the sinews"; note: "辛 is the taste of metal; metal binds together unlike things as the sinews do, and man's sinews too bind the bones together, hence the pungent nourishes them."
- *苦辛, taking the sense of bitter and sour.* *Zheng Yun*. Li Bai's poem: "the hero not yet transformed like the leopard — from of old, much hardship and toil (艱辛)." Du Fu's poem: "parting in life and parting in death — from of old, the nose stings with grief (酸辛)."
- *高辛 (Gaoxin), an ancient imperial style.*
- *Surname.* Shiji, *Eulogy in the Basic Annals of Xia*: "Xia Qi enfeoffed a cadet son at Shen (莘), and by the closeness of sound it was changed to 辛."
- *少辛 (shaoxin), a drug-name.* *Bencao*: "it is xixin (細辛)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 先 (xiān), fanqie 宵前.* *Poem of Jiao Zhongqing*: "serving and obeying the husband's mother, in coming and going I dared take nothing on myself; by day and night I toiled at work and rest, forlorn and bound in bitter toil (苦辛)." *Zhengzitong*: a note disputing Xu's *Shuowen* gloss that "tears come forth, imaging the thighs," holds it contrary to the sense of 𨐌; for 壬 and 癸 follow upon 辛, and in the Way of Heaven there is recovery within decay, autumn's virtue holding benevolence within righteousness — it is not solely cutting-off and scorching-slaying; Xu's forced gloss is wrong. It further notes the discrepancies between the Erya's and Shiji's year-yang names, and that the *Zihui* corruptly abbreviates 𨐌 to 辛, confusing it with the graph 辛 (sounded 愆, xián) — most wrong.
