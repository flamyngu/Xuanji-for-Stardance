**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, middle; Tiger radical 虍; p.1073 #25)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 昌與. *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 敞呂 — sounding like 杵 (chǔ).

**Etymology (Yupian).** *Yupian*: "to dwell (居)." Shijing, *Wang feng*: "none has any leisure to rest (處)."

**Senses.**
- *To halt, stay (止).* Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "and afterward she settles (處)." *Guangyun*: "to linger (留); to rest (息); to settle (定)."
- *A dwelling-room (居室).* Shijing, *Daya*: "then they dwelt at ease (處處)."
- *To return home (歸).* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 4: "the people have their sleeping-shrines, the beasts their rich grass, each with its own resort (處)."
- *To distinguish, settle (分別).* Jinshu, *Biography of Du Yu*: "the dispositions (處分) being fixed, he then memorialized to ask the date for striking Wu."
- *To control, deal with (制).* Jinshu, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "among men cunning and fraud grew the more, and though one dealt with it (處) by stern punishment, it could not be checked."
- *Surname.* Former Han, *Treatise on Arts and Letters*: "the *Chuzi (處子)* in nine chapters"; Yan Shigu's note: "the *Shiji* says Zhao had a Chuzi (處子)." *Guangyun*: "the *Fengsu tong* says: the Han had Chu Xing, Grand Administrator of Beihai."
- *A prefecture-name.* The *Yitong zhi*: "under Jin it belonged to Yongjia commandery; the Sui set up Chuzhou (處州)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: read in the departing tone, fanqie 昌據. "A place (所)." Shijing, *Bei feng*: "here he dwells, there he stays (爰居爰處), and here he loses his horse." *Discourses of Lu*: "the five punishments and the three stations (三次)"; note: "次 means a place (處); the three stations are the wilds, the court, and the market."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 居御. Interchangeable with 據. A personal name: Qi had Liangqiu Chu (梁丘處).
- *Also* interchangeable with 杵. Gongyang zhuan, Xi yr 12: "Chujiu, Marquis of Chen, died (處臼)"; note: "the *Zuozhuan* writes 杵臼."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: written 処. *Guangyun*: commonly written 䖏.

**Textual note.** Shijing, *Wang feng*, "and afterward she settles (其後也處)" — checked against the original book, *Wang feng* (王風) is corrected to *Zhao nan* (召南).
