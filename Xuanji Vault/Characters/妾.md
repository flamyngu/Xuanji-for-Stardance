## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集下〉【女字部】頁257第39【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤七接切，音踥。接也。得接于君子者也。【禮·曲禮】買妾，不知其姓，則卜之。【前漢·五行志】處妾遇之而孕。【註】處妾，童女也。　又國名。【山海經】雨師妾在其北。【楊愼曰】雨師妾如姮娥織女之類，非。下文元股國在雨師妾北，可證。　又姓。漢妾胥，妾志，見【印藪】。【說文】从䇂从女。䇂音愆。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Woman radical 女; p.257 #39)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 七接, sounding like 踥 (qiè).

**Etymology and senses.** To attend on (接) — she who gets to attend on a gentleman. Liji, *Qu li*: "in buying a concubine (妾), if one does not know her surname, one divines on it." Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "a virgin maid (處妾) met him and conceived"; note: "處妾 is a girl-child (童女)."

**Senses.**
- *A state-name.* Shanhai jing: "the Rain-master's Handmaid (雨師妾) is to the north of them"; Yang Shen says: "to take the Rain-master's Handmaid as a being like Chang'e or the Weaving-girl is wrong; the next text — 'the Yuangu state is north of the Rain-master's Handmaid' — proves it a place."
- *Surname.* The Han had Qie Xu and Qie Zhi; seen in the *Yin sou*.

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: it follows 䇂 and 女; 䇂 is sounded 愆 (qiān).
