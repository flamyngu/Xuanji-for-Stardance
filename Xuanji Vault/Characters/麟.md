## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈亥集下〉【鹿字部】頁1511第20【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤力珍切，音鄰。【說文】大麚也。麕身牛尾，狼額馬蹄，五彩腹下黃，高丈二。【玉篇】仁獸也。【詩·周南】麟之趾。【傳】麟信而應禮，以足至者也。【箋】麟角末有肉，示有武不用。【大戴禮】毛蟲三百六十，麟爲之長。【禮·禮運】麟以爲畜，故獸不狘。【註】狘，驚走也。　又州名。漢五原，河西二地，唐置麟州。又【十洲記】鳳麟州有集弦膠。　又與燐通。光明也。【揚雄·劇秦美新文】炳炳麟麟。　又叶陵延切，音連。【韓愈·雜詩】指摘相告語，雖還今誰親。翩然下大荒，被髮騎麒麟。親叶音千。此詩終篇皆先韻。○按經傳皆作麟字。《爾雅》《公羊》《京房易傳》皆作麐。《說文》麐，牝麒也。麟，大牝鹿也。據此，則麐與麟有分。《爾雅註疏》幷州界有麟，大如鹿，非瑞麟也。故司馬相如賦曰：射糜脚麟，謂此麟也。《爾雅》麠大麃牛尾一角，註云：謂之麟者，此是也。然麟麐二字，今俱通用。互詳麐字註。    考證：〔【禮·禮運】麟以爲畜，故獸不狨。【註】狨，驚走也。〕　謹照原文兩狨字𠀤改狘。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, lower; Deer radical 鹿; p.1511 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 力珍, sounding like 鄰 (lín).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A great stag (大麚); deer-bodied, ox-tailed, wolf-browed, horse-hoofed; five-coloured, yellow beneath the belly, twelve feet tall." *Yupian*: "a benevolent beast (仁獸)." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "the unicorn's hooves (麟之趾)"; tradition: "the unicorn is trusty and answers to the rites, coming on foot"; Jian: "the unicorn's horn has flesh at the tip, to show it has might but does not use it." Da Dai li: "of the three hundred and sixty hairy creatures, the unicorn (麟) is chief." Liji, *Li yun*: "the unicorn is made a domestic beast, hence the beasts do not bolt"; note: "狘 means to start and run."

**Senses.**
- *A prefecture-name.* The Han Wuyuan and Hexi lands; the Tang set up Linzhou (麟州). Also *Shizhou ji*: "Fenglin-zhou has the lyre-string-gathering glue."
- *Interchangeable with 燐 (phosphor-light), brightness (光明).* Yang Xiong, *Ju Qin mei Xin*: "blazing and gleaming (麟麟)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 連 (lián), fanqie 陵延.* Han Yu, *Miscellaneous poem*: "pointing and telling one another, though I return, who now is dear? lightly he descends to the great waste, hair loose, riding the qilin (麒麟)" — 親 sounded 千 (qiān); this whole poem is in the 先-rhyme. A note: in the classics it is everywhere written 麟. The Erya, the Gongyang, and Jing Fang's *Yi zhuan* all write 麐. The *Shuowen*: "麐 is the female qi (牝麒); 麟 is a great female deer." By this, 麐 and 麟 are distinct. The *Erya* note and subcommentary: "within the bounds of Bing province there is a 麟, large as a deer, not the auspicious unicorn; hence Sima Xiangru's rhapsody says 'shooting the elk and tripping the 麟,' meaning this 麟." The Erya: "the 麠 is a great pao with ox-tail and one horn"; note: "what is called the 麟 is this." Yet the two graphs 麟 and 麐 are now both used interchangeably. See in full under the graph 麐.

**Textual note.** Liji, *Li yun*, "the unicorn is made a domestic beast, hence the beasts do not bolt (狨); note: 狨 means to start and run" — checked against the original, both 狨 are corrected to 狘.
