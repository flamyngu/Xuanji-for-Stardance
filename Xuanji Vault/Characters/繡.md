**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, middle; Silk radical зіё; p.939 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жҒҜж•‘ вҖ” sounding like з§Җ (xiГ№).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the five colors complete." *Shiming*: "з№Ў means well-wrought (дҝ®): its patterning is finely finished." Shu, *Yi Ji*: "the axe-and-bow figures, the fine cloth and embroidery (зөәз№Ў)." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "in the work of painting and broidery, when the five colors are complete it is called embroidery (з№Ў)."
- *Also a surname.* Xingshi jijiu pian: "the Han had Xiu Junshi (з№ЎеҗӣеҜҰ)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie е…ҲеҪ«, sounding like и•ӯ (xiДҒo). *Leipian*: "of the figured-silk kind (з¶әеұ¬)." Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "white robe with red broidery (жңұз№Ў)"; Jian: "з№Ў should be з¶ғ (raw silk)"; *Yunbu*: "it should be read like иӮ– (xiГ o)."

**Textual notes.** In the Kaogong ji citation, зҲІд№Ӣ should read и¬Ӯд№Ӣ.
