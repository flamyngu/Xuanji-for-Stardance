## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈未集中〉【糸字部】頁939第18【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤息救切，音秀。【說文】五采備也。【釋名】繡，修也，文修修然也。【書·益稷】黼黻絺繡。【周禮·冬官考工記】畫繢之事，五采備，謂之繡。　又姓。【姓氏急就篇】漢有繡君實。　又【集韻】先彫切，音蕭。【類篇】綺屬。【詩·唐風】素衣朱繡。【箋】繡，當爲綃。【韻補】當讀如肖。    考證：〔【周禮·冬官考工記】畫繢之事，五采備，爲之繡。〕　謹照原文爲之改謂之。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Silk radical 糸; p.939 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息救 — sounding like 秀 (xiù).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the five colors complete." *Shiming*: "繡 means well-wrought (修): its patterning is finely finished." Shu, *Yi Ji*: "the axe-and-bow figures, the fine cloth and embroidery (絺繡)." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "in the work of painting and broidery, when the five colors are complete it is called embroidery (繡)."
- *Also a surname.* Xingshi jijiu pian: "the Han had Xiu Junshi (繡君實)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 先彫, sounding like 蕭 (xiāo). *Leipian*: "of the figured-silk kind (綺屬)." Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "white robe with red broidery (朱繡)"; Jian: "繡 should be 綃 (raw silk)"; *Yunbu*: "it should be read like 肖 (xiào)."

**Textual notes.** In the Kaogong ji citation, 爲之 should read 謂之.
