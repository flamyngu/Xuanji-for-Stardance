**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, middle; Face radical йқў; p.1383 #07)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie еҪҢз®ӯ вҖ” sounding like еҒӯ (miГ n).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "properly written р ҡ‘, the front of the face (йЎҸеүҚ). It follows рҰЈ» (head) and pictures a man's face." Shu, *Yi Ji*: "do not assent to my face (йқў) and then, retiring, have words behind my back."
- *Also, to see, to present oneself (иҰӢ).* Liji, *Quli*: "as for one who is a son, going out he must announce it, returning he must present himself (йқў)"; note: "to say 'present himself' on returning вҖ” coming from outside, he should know whether his parents' looks are well or not." Yili, *Pin li*: "the usher comes out to ask the business; the guest presents himself (йқў) as for the offering of gifts"; note: "йқў too means to present oneself." Zhouli, *Qiuguan, Siyi*: "the private presentation (з§Ғйқў)"; note: "a private visit (з§ҒиҰҝ)."
- *Also, to face (еҗ‘).* Shu, *Zhou guan*: "not to learn is to face a wall (зүҶйқў)"; subcommentary: "a man who does not learn is like one facing a wall." Liji, *Quli*: "the Son of Heaven stands by the screen, and the feudal lords face north (еҢ—йқў) to see him вҖ” this is called the audience (иҰІ)." Also *Yuzao*: "only for the ruler does the wine-jar face (йқў) him"; note: "йқў is like й„ү (to face toward)." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji, Jiangren*: "the court in front (йқўжңқ), the market behind."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "the front (еүҚ)." Yili, *Shi guan li*: "covered, with the leaf to the front (йқўи‘ү)"; note: "йқў is the front."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: ж–№йқў (a quarter), one side of the four directions. Shu, *Gu ming*: "the Great Carriage at the front of the guest-steps (иі”йҡҺйқў), the Adorned Carriage at the front of the eastern steps (йҳјйҡҺйқў)." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "some examine the curve and aspect (йқўеӢў)"; note: "examining the fitness of the bends and straights, the squares and aspects, of the five materials." Shiji, *Hereditary House of the Marquis of Liu*: "only Han Xin can be entrusted with great affairs and hold a whole sector (дёҖйқў)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: turning the back is called йқў. Shiji, *Annals of Xiang Yu*: "LГј Matong turned away from (йқў) him"; note: Ru Chun says: "йқў means not looking straight at." Former Han, *Biography of Xiang Yu*, note: Yan Shigu says: "Ru Chun's view is wrong; йқў means to turn one's back to him, not to face him; йқўзёӣ (binding with face turned) likewise means to bind with the back reversed. Du Yu held it to mean merely showing the face вҖ” this is wrong."
- *Also* Former Han, *Biography of Zhang Chang*: "he would screen his face (дҫҝйқў) and pat his horse"; note: Yan Shigu says: "дҫҝйқў is of the fan kind; also called a face-screen (еұӣйқў)."

**Textual notes.** In the Shuowen citation, д»ҺиҮӘ should read д»ҺрҰЈ» (per the original).
