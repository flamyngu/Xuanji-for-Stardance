**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls Тѕї, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical жџ╣; p.1367 #58)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жЃйтЃџ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie СИЂУЂі Рђћ sounding like У▓ѓ (di─Ђo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the tuan-eagle (жи╗). It follows жџ╣ (bird) with тЉе as phonetic." *Yupian*: "an eagle (жи▓); it can eat grass." Qinjing: "the eagle (жЏЋ) hunts by encircling (тЉе)." Piya: "the жЏЋ is like a hawk but larger, black, commonly called the black-eagle (уџЂжЏЋ); also named the ­фєЃ; in flight it nears the Milky Way. Now the great eagle wheels over the water, fanning the fish to surface, then snatches them from the seething waves; one calls it 'the river-boiler' (Т▓ИТ▓│)." Shiji, *Biography of Li Guang*: "they took one man alive, and he proved to be a Xiongnu eagle-shooter (т░ёжЏЋУђЁ)."
- *Also* Xunzi, *Yibing*: "conspicuously (жЏЋжЏЋ) it hangs high rank and rich reward before them"; note: "жЏЋжЏЋ is the look of being bright and manifest."
- *Also* Erya, *Shiqi*: "working jade is called жЏЋ." Also: "жЏЋ is called уљб (to chisel)." Mengzi: "he must have the jade-worker carve and polish (жЏЋуљб) it." Piya: "the sense of жЏЋ (to carve) arises from the жЏЋ of жЏЋжХџ (the eagle), because the eagle's nature is to cut and shape."
- *Also an office-name.* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: the carvers (жЏЋС║║).
- *Also жЏЋжАї (Diaoti), a state-name.* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "Diaoti (жЏЋжАї) and Jiaozhi"; note: "жЏЋТќЄ (tattooing) means cutting the skin and dyeing it with cinnabar and indigo."
- *Also a personal name.* Former Han, *Table of Meritorious Officials*: "Marquis Changle, Chou Diao (уеажЏЋ)."
- *Also a surname.* Former Han, *Table of Emperor Wu's Meritorious Officials*: "Zangma the Stable-Marquis, Diao Yannian (жЏЋт╗Хт╣┤)." Also a compound surname. Lunyu: "the Master had Qidiao Kai (Т╝єжЏЋжќІ) take office." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written жх░.
