**Original entry (verbatim):**

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical 隹; p.1367 #58)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 都僚. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 丁聊 — sounding like 貂 (diāo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the tuan-eagle (鷻). It follows 隹 (bird) with 周 as phonetic." *Yupian*: "an eagle (鷲); it can eat grass." Qinjing: "the eagle (雕) hunts by encircling (周)." Piya: "the 雕 is like a hawk but larger, black, commonly called the black-eagle (皁雕); also named the 𪆃; in flight it nears the Milky Way. Now the great eagle wheels over the water, fanning the fish to surface, then snatches them from the seething waves; one calls it 'the river-boiler' (沸河)." Shiji, *Biography of Li Guang*: "they took one man alive, and he proved to be a Xiongnu eagle-shooter (射雕者)."
- *Also* Xunzi, *Yibing*: "conspicuously (雕雕) it hangs high rank and rich reward before them"; note: "雕雕 is the look of being bright and manifest."
- *Also* Erya, *Shiqi*: "working jade is called 雕." Also: "雕 is called 琢 (to chisel)." Mengzi: "he must have the jade-worker carve and polish (雕琢) it." Piya: "the sense of 雕 (to carve) arises from the 雕 of 雕鶚 (the eagle), because the eagle's nature is to cut and shape."
- *Also an office-name.* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: the carvers (雕人).
- *Also 雕題 (Diaoti), a state-name.* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "Diaoti (雕題) and Jiaozhi"; note: "雕文 (tattooing) means cutting the skin and dyeing it with cinnabar and indigo."
- *Also a personal name.* Former Han, *Table of Meritorious Officials*: "Marquis Changle, Chou Diao (稠雕)."
- *Also a surname.* Former Han, *Table of Emperor Wu's Meritorious Officials*: "Zangma the Stable-Marquis, Diao Yannian (雕延年)." Also a compound surname. Lunyu: "the Master had Qidiao Kai (漆雕開) take office." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 鵰.
