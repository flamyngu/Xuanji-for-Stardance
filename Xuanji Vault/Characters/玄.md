**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, upper; Dark radical 玄; p.725 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 瑚涓, sounding like 懸 (xuán).

**Etymology and senses.** Yijing, *Kun* hexagram: "Heaven is dark (𤣥) and Earth yellow"; subcommentary: "𤣥 is the colour of the sky." Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "about to sacrifice in the suburb to the high dark (上𤣥) and fix the Tai altar"; note: "the high dark is Heaven." Suishu, *Annals of Emperor Gao*: "receiving the mandate from the high dark (上𤣥), he swept clean the realm; the holy spirits hung down their aid, and civil and martial were of one heart." Liangshu, *Biography of Zhu Yi*: "the sage-bright rules the cosmos, answering above to the azure dark (蒼𤣥)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Huangting jing*: "the heart is the high dark (上𤣥)."
- *Also* *Shuowen changjian*: "black with a red cast is 纁; black with a yellow cast is 𤣥." Liji, *Wang zhi*, subcommentary: "the dark robe (𤣥衣) models Heaven." *Li yun*, subcommentary on the five colours and six emblems: "the five colours with the dark of Heaven (天𤣥) make the six emblems."
- *A stem-and-branch name.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the stem 壬 is 𤣥黓 (Xuanyi)."
- *The month-mark: the ninth month is the 𤣥 month.*
- *What is subtle and profound in principle is called 𤣥.* *Cantong qi*: "of old the sages and worthies cherished the dark (𤣥) and embraced the true." Yan Yanzhi's poem: "probing the Way, I love its deep mystery (淵𤣥); reading books, I disdain mere chapter-and-verse." Yao Song, *Memorial expounding the meaning of Buddhism*: "what is dark (𤣥) in principle cannot be named in words; what is subtle in matter is surely not what common speech can praise."
- *Calm and still (淸靜).* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "the ruler makes dark silence (𤣥默) his spirit, and placid detachment his virtue." Also *Account of the Western Regions*: "meeting the dark silence (𤣥默) of Wen and Jing, the people were nurtured five generations, the realm rich and abounding, with wealth and strength to spare, and soldiers and horses strong."
- *Deep (深).* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "splendid the divine sage, dwelling in the dark hall (𤣥宮)."
- *The god of the north.* Liji, *Qu li*: "the Vermilion Bird in front and the Dark Warrior (𤣥武) behind."
- *Titles of immortals.* *Shenxian zhuan*: "Laozi, in the time of the Upper Three Sovereigns, was the Dark-Centre Master (𤣥中法師); in the time of the Lower Three Sovereigns, the Lord-Emperor of the Golden Portal." Also: "the immortal Ge, named Xuan (𤣥), received from Zuo Yuanfang the *Immortal Canon of the Nine Elixirs and the Golden Liquor*." *Xu xianzhuan*: "Zhang Zhihe styled himself the Dark-True Master (𤣥眞子)." *Xianzhuan shiyi*: "Xue Xuanzhen (薛𤣥眞) roamed the cloud-springs and gained the Way of long life." Old Tang History, *Annals of Minghuang*: "he summoned Zhang Guo of Hengzhou and granted him the title Master Penetrating-the-Dark (通𤣥先生)." *Taiping guangji*: "the recluse Yiqi Xuanjie (伊祁𤣥解), with thick hair and a child's face, his breath fragrant and pure, would speak with men of affairs of a thousand years before, all as if witnessed."
- *Surname.* *Shiben*: "Xuanshou (𤣥壽), a minister of the Yellow Emperor." *Liexian zhuan*: "Xuansu (𤣥宿), a man of Hejian."
- *A mountain-name.* *Shenxian zhuan*: "the Heaven-Gate Master, aged two hundred and eighty, still had a youth's complexion; taking pearl-nectar he gained immortality and went off into the Xuanzhou Mountains (𤣥洲山)."
- *𤣥圃 (Xuanpu), the capital of the Heaven-Emperor.* Huainanzi, *Lan ming*: "Kunlun is eighteen thousand li from the earth; above it is the layered city of nine tiers; double that height is what is called Langfeng; double that again is what is called Xuanpu (𤣥圃)."
- *𤣥都 (Xuandu).* *Shizhou ji*: "Xuanzhou lies in the Northern Sea, three hundred and sixty thousand li from shore; on it is the Great Xuandu (太𤣥都), governed by the immortal-elder Lord Zhen." *Yujing jing*: "Xuandu — on Mount Yujing there is a seven-jewel city, governed by the Most High Limitless Great-Way Void-August Lord."
- *Also* Liji, *Li yun*: "the dark wine (𤣥酒) is in the chamber"; note: "the dark wine is water."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi qin*: "a great-grandson's son is the 𤣥孫 (great-great-grandson)." Wei Dan, *Ode on the Royal Sericulture*: "she enfolds rich blessing on the myriad states, and rolls up bounty in her turning; fair the good fortune for myriad years, opening a hundred generations of descendants (曾𤣥)."
- *A book-title.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "Xiong held that of classics none is greater than the Yijing, and so composed the *Tai xuan* (太𤣥)."
- *Same as 炫 (to dazzle).* Sima Xiangru, *Feng shan shu*: "its hues dazzling and bright (𤣥耀)"; note: "𤣥 is read as 炫."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 雲 (yún), fanqie 五分.* Ban Gu, *Dongdu fu*: "the women ply weaving, the men toil at plough and hoe; their vessels are pottery and gourd, their dress prizes plain and dark (素𤣥)."
