**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; White radical 白; p.786 #14)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𦤍, 𦤐, 𤽙, 𤽚, 𤽧.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 胡光 — sounding like 黃 (huáng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "great (大)." Fengsu tong: "Heaven." Erya, *Shitian* subcommentary: "honoured and made sovereign, it is called August Heaven (皇天)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "August Heaven (皇天) looked on him with favour and gave the mandate." Also *Tang gao*: "the august (皇) High God"; tradition: "皇 is great; the High God is Heaven."
- *The Three Augusts (三皇):* Fuxi, Shennong, and the Yellow Emperor. Shu, *Preface* subcommentary: "they are called 皇 because 皇 is a name of beauty and greatness, meaning greater than 'Di' (帝)." Fengsu tong: "the Three Augusts, in virtue primordial and placid, resembled August Heaven, hence called 皇; 皇 means the central (中), the bright (光), the vast (弘)."
- *A general term for the holder of the realm.* Erya, *Shigu*: "a sovereign (君)." Baihu tong: "a title; he is titled 皇 because, in his blazing splendour (煌煌), none dare disobey." Shu, *Lü xing*: "the August Emperor (皇帝) clearly questioned the people below." Shijing, *Daya*: "the August King (皇王) is sovereign." Chunqiu fanlu: "he whose virtue equals Heaven and Earth is called 皇帝." Cai Yong, *Duduan*: "皇帝 is the title of the most exalted. In high antiquity the Sons of Heaven Fuxi and Shennong were styled 皇; Yao and Shun styled 帝; Xia, Yin, and Zhou styled 王 (king). Qin combined them as a title, and the Han followed it unchanged." Former Han, *Annals of Gaozu*: "the King of Han took the imperial throne (皇帝位); he honoured the queen as Empress (皇后), the heir as Crown Prince (皇太子), and honoured the Grand Duke as Grand Supreme August (太上皇)"; note: "Taishang is a title of utmost honour; being the Son of Heaven's father he was titled 皇; not sharing in governing the state, he was not called 帝." The Ming statute: "the heir is styled 皇; the princes and below are not; the imperial kin and relatives are managed by the Court of the Imperial Clan."
- *In sacrificial titles.* Liji, *Quli*: "in sacrifice, the grandfather is called 皇祖考, the grandmother 皇祖妣, the father 皇考, the mother 皇妣, the husband 皇辟"; note: "fresh titles are set up, honouring the spirits as distinct from the living."
- *Divine titles.* Song shi, *Annals of Zhenzong*: "in the fifth year of Xiangfu, he sacrificed in person to the Jade August (玉皇) in the Zhaoyuan Hall"; note: "Yuhuang is the holy title of the Heavenly Emperor." Also the Hongbao bosou: "the Buddha is in one place called the August of Awakening (覺皇)."
- *Fine, beautiful (美).* Boya. Shijing, *Daya*: "ah, the splendid (皇) many officers."
- *To set right (正).* Erya, *Shiyan*: "皇 is to set right." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "the four states he set right (皇)."
- *Grave and stately (莊盛).* Yili, *Pin li*: "the guest enters the gate, stately (皇)."
- *The look of rich splendour.* Shijing, *Daya*: "majestic and resplendent (穆穆皇皇)."
- *Like 煌煌, blazing.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "splendid (皇皇) are the flowers." Erya, *Shiyan*: "皇 is a flower (華)"; subcommentary: "the flower of plants is also called 皇."
- *Like 熒熒, glittering.* Yang Xiong, *Taixuan jing*: "things ascend the Bright Hall, abundant and resplendent (矞矞皇皇)."
- *Like 栖栖, restless.* Liji, *Tan Gong*: "restless (皇皇) as one who has a hope, looking out but it does not come."
- *於皇, an exclamation of praise.* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "oh splendid (於皇) the wheat and barley"; note: 於 sounds like 烏.
- *聿皇, the look of swiftness.* Former Han, Yang Xiong, *Jiaolie fu*: "the war-horsemen sweeping swift (聿皇)."
- *遹皇, the look of going to and fro.* Zhang Heng, *Sixuan fu*: "observing the entangled coursing to and fro (遹皇) of the two cords and five planets."
- *A cap-name,* painted on top with feather ornament. Liji, *Wangzhi*: "the house of Yu wore the 皇 cap and sacrificed."
- *A screen-name,* set where the king sits. Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Master of Awnings sets up the 皇 screen-backing."
- *A dance-name;* one splits five-coloured feathers and holds them to dance. Zhouli, *Diguan*: "the Dance-master has charge of teaching the 皇 dance, and leads it in matters of drought"; note: "皇 is sometimes 䍿."
- *The fenghuang (鳳皇), the numinous bird.* Shu, *Yi Ji*: "the phoenix (鳳皇) came and danced in measure"; tradition: "the male is 鳳, the female 皇"; 皇 is used interchangeably with 凰. Ji Yun: also written 䳨.
- *The oriole.* Erya, *Shiniao* subcommentary: "皇 is also called the yellow bird (黃鳥), commonly the oriole (黃離留)."
- *A field-grass.* Erya, *Shicao* subcommentary: "皇 is also called *shoutian* (守田); it resembles wild-oat seed, like *diaohu* rice, and grows in waste fields."
- *A dappled horse.* Piya: "a black horse marked yellow-and-white is called 皇." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "dappled and piebald (皇駁) their horses."
- *Star-names.* Former Han, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "Jupiter, rising in the tenth month, is named Tianhuang (天皇)." Also the Guohuang (國皇) star, large and red.
- *Yuhuang (餘皇), a boat-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 17: "Chu defeated the Wu host and took their flagship Yuhuang (餘皇)"; vulgarly written 艅艎.
- *Yuhuang (矞皇), a spirit-name.* Former Han, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "Changli before and Yuhuang (矞皇) behind"; the Shiji writes it 潏湟.
- *The tower before a tomb.* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 19: "buried at the mourning-tower (絰皇)."
- *The tower of the inner-chamber gate.* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 13: "in such haste that his shoes reached only the chamber-court (窒皇)."
- *A hall without four walls.* Former Han, *Biography of Hu Jian*: "they sat in rank on the open hall (堂皇)." Boya writes it 堭, which is wrong.
- *A stream-name.* Shijing, *Daya*: "flanking the Huang stream (皇㵎)."
- *Mountain and water names.* Shanhaijing: "Mount Huangren (皇人之山), whence the Huang water (皇水) issues."
- *A place-name.* Chunqiu, Zhao yr 22: "the Liu and Shan viscounts settled Wang Meng at Huang (皇)."
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan subcommentary: "Huangfu, son of Duke Dai of Song, whose descendants took 皇 as their clan-name." Also Huangfu (皇甫), a compound surname.
- *Posthumous-title rule:* "to bring the people peace and be a model is called 皇."
- *Interchangeable with 遑, 偟, 徨.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 32: "no leisure (不皇) to settle down." Shijing, *Xiaoya* writes it 遑. Erya, *Shixun* writes it 偟, "leisure." Former Han, Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "floating idly to and fro (方皇) in the western purity"; note: "like 仿偟; one text has 彷徨."
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 戸廣, the rising tone of 黃 (huǎng). Liji, *Shaoyi*: "the beauty of the sacrifice — grave and reverent (齊齊皇皇)"; so read by Lu Deming.
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 于放, the departing tone of 黃 (huàng); same sense, so read by Xu Miao.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 羽兩, sounding like 往 (wǎng). 皇皇, the demeanour of sacrifice. Liji, *Shaoyi* note: "皇 is read like the 往 of 歸往 (turning toward)"; subcommentary: "that to which the heart is bound and turns; the filial son in sacrifice, dignified and grave, must have continuity, hence 'grave and turning-toward' (齊齊皇皇)." Shijing, *Lu song*: "abundant and earnest (烝烝皇皇)"; Jian: "皇皇 should be 暀暀, like 往往."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "it originally followed 自 (self/from), meaning 'beginning.'" Xu [Kai]: "自 means 'from'; hence it signifies 'beginning.' Now it is abbreviated to 白."

**Textual notes.** In Erya, *Shitian* subcommentary, "尊而君子" — corrected to "尊而君之." In Erya, *Shiyan*, "匡正也" — by the sense, 匡 should read 皇. In Former Han, *Treatise on Astronomy*, "太歲，十月出" — 太歲 should read 歲星.
