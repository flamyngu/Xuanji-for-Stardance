**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.540 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 羊朱. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 容朱. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 雲俱 — sounding like 兪 (yú).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the elm — the white *fen* elm (白枌)." Lu Ji, *Cao mu shu*: "the elm has ten kinds, all alike in leaf but differing in bark and grain. The Erya glosses three elms: one is *ouchi* (藲荎)" — Guo Pu's note: "the thorny elm of today"; subcommentary: "the *shu* of Shijing, *Tangfeng*, 'on the hill is the elm.'" — "one is *wugu* (無姑), whose fruit is the *yi*" — Guo Pu: "*wugu* is the *gu* elm, growing in the hills, its leaf round and thick, what is called *wuyi*." — "one is the elm, the white *fen*" — subcommentary: "the *fen* of Shijing, *Chenfeng*, 'the elm at the eastern gate.'"
- *also.* Liji, *Neize*: "violet and water-mallow, *fen*-elm and elm, fresh and dried, [steeped in] starch-water to smooth them."
- *also.* Liji, *Tan Gong*: "the feudal lords use the elm-soak (楡沈) and set up the drag-ropes"; note: "with water they wash out the juice of the elm's white bark, and in haste sprinkle it on the ground to make the hearse-cart slide."
- *also.* Ji Kang, *On Nourishing Life*: "the elm (楡) makes a man drowsy"; the Bowu zhi: "eat elm and you sleep without wishing to wake."
- *also.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Siguan* note: "Master Zou in spring took fire from the elm and willow (楡柳)."
- *also.* The Bencao jihe: "the great elm puts out pods in the second month, the *lang* elm in the eighth."
- *桑楡, the evening light.* Later Han, *Biography of Feng Yi*: "lost at the eastern corner [dawn], it is regained at the mulberry-and-elm (桑楡) [dusk]."
- *地楡 (sanguisorba), a plant-name.* Boya: "it is *chouchu* (菗蒢)."
- *白楡, a star-name.* An old yuefu: "what is there in heaven? Row on row, the planted white elms (白楡)."
- *枌楡, a district-name;* Han Gaozu set up an altar there. Former Han, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "Gaozu prayed at the Fenyu altar (枌楡社)."
- *楡林, a frontier-name.* The Shuijing zhu: "the Cizhi water runs east past the Yulin fort (楡林塞), which the world calls Yulin Mountain."
- *A surname.* Later Han: General Yu Ji (楡棘).
