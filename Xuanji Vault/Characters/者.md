**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Old radical 老; p.961 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 章也. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 止野 — sounding like 赭 (zhě).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a word that distinguishes things (別事詞). From 白, with 𣥐 as phonetic; 𣥐 is the archaic form of 旅." *Yun Hui*: "now written 者." *Yupian*: "an auxiliary particle (語助)."
- *A word pointing to a thing.* *Zengyun*: "as in 彼者 (that one), 此者 (this one)." Yijing, *Qian*: "the originating (元者) is the foremost of goods."
- *This (此).* *Zengyun.* To call 'this one' 者箇 is this usage; today colloquially 這 (zhè) is mostly used — 這 is fanqie 魚戰, "to meet."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 渚 (zhǔ), fanqie 掌與.* Shiji, *Annals of Qin Shihuang*: "wherever human tracks reach, there are none who are not subjects (者)"; note (Suoyin): "者, rhyme-sound 渚." Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "I pluck fragrant pollia on the isle, to send to the far-off one (者)…"; *Yun Hui*: "者 is the archaic form of 渚 (islet), hence the 旅 phonetic; later people added 'water' to make 渚, distinguishing it from the particle 者 — so 者 serves only as a particle."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 阻可.* Miao Xi, *Dirge*: "…who can depart from this (者)?"
- *Rhymes, fanqie 之戈.* Han Yu, *Tomb inscription for the Lu lady*: "…to inform the beholder (者)."

**On the graph.** *Yun Hui*, per *Shuowen*: it is from 白; the *Zhengzitong* merges the variant forms (者 also from 白 with 𣏔 phonetic, 𣏔 being the archaic form of 困).
