> 《康熙字典》〈巳集上〉【水字部】頁613第09【唐韻】乎哥切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】寒歌切，𠀤音何。水名。【說文】水出敦煌塞外崑崙山，發源注海。【春秋·說題辭】河之爲言荷也。荷精分佈，懷隂引度也。【釋名】河，下也。隨地下處而通流也。【前漢·西域傳】河有兩源，一出蔥嶺，一出于闐。于闐在南山下，其河北流，與蔥嶺河合，東注蒲昌海，潛行地下。南出於積石，爲中國河云。【書·禹貢】導河積石，至于龍門。【爾雅·釋水】河出崑崙，色白，所渠幷千七百一川，色黃，百里一小曲，千里一曲一直。　又九河。【書·禹貢】九河旣道。【傳】九河，徒駭一，太史二，馬頰三，覆釜四，胡蘇五，𥳑六，絜七，鉤盤八，鬲津九。　又三河，謂河南，河北，河東也。【後漢·光武紀】三河未澄，四關重擾。又【小學紺珠】以黃河，析支河，湟中河爲三河。　又兩河，謂東河，西河也。【爾雅·釋地】兩河閒曰冀州。　又州名。【廣輿記】古西羌地，秦漢屬隴西，唐曰河州，明置河州衞。　又梗河。星名。【甘氏星經】梗河三星，在大角帝座北。　又銀河，天河也。　又【趙崇絢雞肋】道家以目爲銀河。　又酒器也。【乾𦠆子】裴鈞大宴有銀河，受一斗。　又淘河，鳥名。見【爾雅·釋鳥註】。　又姓，明河淸，長沙人。𦠆字从月从㢲作。    考證：〔兩河，謂河南，河北也。【爾雅·釋地】兩河閒曰冀州。〕　謹按爾雅註云自東河至西河，非謂河南河北。謹改河南河北爲東河西河。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.613 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 乎哥. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 寒歌 — sounding like 何 (hé).

**Etymology and senses.** A river-name. *Shuowen*: "the water rises in the Kunlun mountains beyond the Dunhuang frontier, springs forth and pours into the sea." *Chunqiu Shuotici*: "河 means 荷 (to bear): it bears the essence and spreads it abroad, holding the yin and drawing it across." *Shiming*: "河 is 'low' (下): it follows the land's low places and flows on through." Former Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "the River has two sources, one rising at the Onion Range, one at Khotan; Khotan lies below the Southern Mountains, and its river flows north and joins the Onion-Range river, then pours east into Lake Puchang and runs hidden underground; it comes out south at Jishi, and becomes the River of the Central States." Shu, *Yugong*: "he led the River from Jishi to Longmen." Erya, *Shishui*: "the River issues from Kunlun, white in colour; the channels it gathers number one thousand seven hundred and one streams, turning it yellow; every hundred *li* a small bend, every thousand *li* one bend and one straight run."
- *The Nine Rivers (九河).* Shu, *Yugong*: "the Nine Rivers were given their courses"; tradition: "the Nine Rivers are Tuhai first, Taishi second, Majia third, Fufu fourth, Husu fifth, Jian sixth, Jie seventh, Goupan eighth, Gejin ninth."
- *The Three Rivers (三河): Henan, Hebei, Hedong.* Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "the Three Rivers were not yet settled, the Four Passes again in turmoil." Also Xiaoxue ganzhu: "the Yellow River, the Xizhi River, and the Huangzhong River are taken as the Three Rivers."
- *The Two Rivers (兩河): the East River and the West River.* Erya, *Shidi*: "between the Two Rivers is Jizhou."
- *A prefecture-name.* Guangyu ji: "anciently land of the Western Qiang; under Qin and Han it belonged to Longxi; the Tang called it Hezhou; the Ming set up the Hezhou garrison."
- *Genghe (梗河), a star-name.* Gan's Star Classic: "the three stars of Genghe lie north of Arcturus and the Emperor's Seat."
- *The Silver River (銀河), the River of Heaven.* Also Zhao Chongxuan's Jilei: "the Daoists call the eye the Silver River."
- *A wine-vessel.* Qianzhuanzi: "at Pei Jun's great feast there was a 'Silver River' [cup] holding one *dou*."
- *The taohe (淘河), a bird-name.* See the note to Erya, *Shiniao*.
- *A surname.* The Ming had He Qing, a man of Changsha. (The graph 𦠆 is written with 月 and 㢲.)

**Textual note.** On the Two Rivers, the Erya note says "from the East River to the West River," not "Henan and Hebei"; so the gloss is corrected to East River and West River.
