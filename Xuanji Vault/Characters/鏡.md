## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集上〉【金字部】頁1320第10【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤居慶切，音竟。【說文】取景之器也。【玉篇】鑑也。【釋名】鏡，景也。言有光景也。【前漢·韓安國傳】淸水明鏡，不可以形逃。　又明也。【前漢·衞姬傳】深說經義，明鏡聖法。　又石鏡，山名。【潯陽記】石鏡山之東，一圓石縣厓，明淨照人見形。　又石名。【述異記】饒州舊傳，軒轅氏鑄鏡於湖邊，今有軒轅磨鏡石。　又破鏡，獸名。【前漢·郊祀志】人有言，古天子常以春解祠，祠黃帝用一梟破鏡。【註】祠祭以解罪求福，梟鳥食母，破鏡似貙食父，黃帝欲絕其類，故使百吏祠皆用之。　又姓。【姓苑】漢河內令鏡斂，後唐同光中媵臣鏡新磨。　又【韻補】叶居亮切，姜去聲。【道藏歌】風雲隱宛微，講論五岳匠。硨磲精琉璃，琥珀金剛鏡。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, upper; Metal radical 金; p.1320 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居慶 — sounding like 竟 (jìng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the implement for catching a reflection (景)." *Yupian*: "a mirror (鑑)." *Shiming*: "鏡 means reflection (景): it speaks of having a bright image." Former Han, *Biography of Han Anguo*: "from clear water and a bright mirror (明鏡), no form can hide."
- *Also, to illumine, to make clear (明).* Former Han, *Biography of Consort Wei*: "deeply expounding the meaning of the classics, clearly mirroring (明鏡) the sage statutes."
- *Also 石鏡 (Shijing), a mountain-name.* Xunyang ji: "east of Mount Shijing (石鏡), a round stone hangs from the cliff, bright and clear, reflecting a man so he sees his form."
- *Also a stone-name.* Shuyi ji: "an old tradition of Raozhou says Xuanyuan cast mirrors by the lake; today there is the Xuanyuan mirror-grinding stone (磨鏡石)."
- *Also 破鏡 (pojing), a beast-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Suburban Sacrifices*: "someone said that the ancient Sons of Heaven would in spring hold the expiation-sacrifice, sacrificing to the Yellow Emperor with one owl (梟) and one pojing (破鏡)"; note: "the sacrifice was to expiate guilt and seek blessing; the owl eats its mother, the pojing — like the chu-beast — eats its father; the Yellow Emperor wished to extirpate their kind, so had all the officials use them in sacrifice."
- *Also a surname.* Xingyuan: "the Han Henei magistrate Jing Lian (鏡斂); in the Tongguang era of the Later Tang, the attendant minister Jing Xinmo (鏡新磨)."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 居亮 — the departing tone of 姜 (jiàng). A Daozang hymn: "wind and cloud lie hidden, faint and dim; discoursing, the masters of the Five Marchmounts. Of tridacna-shell, of fine lapis lazuli, of amber, the adamant mirror (金剛鏡)."
