**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Yawn radical 欠; p.571 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** 可, 哥.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 古俄. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居何 — sounding like 柯 (gē).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to chant (詠)." Xu [Kai]: "to draw out the voice in chant." *Shiming*: "the human voice is 歌 (song); 歌 is 柯 (a bough): the voice intones up and down, as plants have boughs and leaves." Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "in Yan and Ji they say 歌, sounding like 柯." Shu, *Shundian*: "poetry voices the intent, song prolongs the words (永言)"; Correct Meaning: "plain speech is not enough to convey the meaning, so one chants the poem's sense in song to prolong the words." Liji, *Yueji*: "poetry voices the intent, song chants the sound"; and: "song as utterance is the prolonging of words; speech being insufficient, one prolongs it."
- *A tune set to music.* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "I sing (歌) and I chant (謠)"; tradition: "a tune set to music is 歌, unaccompanied song is 謠"; subcommentary: "loosely, 歌 is the general name, not necessarily set to music." Han Shi zhangju: "with stanza and tune it is 歌, without, 謠."
- *Regional songs.* Note to the old Yuefu: "a Qi song is 謳, a Wu song 歈, a Chu song 豔; to perform [to the hall] is 登歌 or 升歌."
- *A bell-name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 11: "the men of Zheng bribed the Marquis of Jin with two racks of song-bells (歌鐘); the Marquis gave half the music to Wei Jiang."
- *A mountain-name.* Guangyu ji: "Ge Mountain (歌山), in Fuchuan county of Pingle prefecture, Guangxi."
- *朝歌, a place-name, the capital of [the tyrant] Zhou.* Under Han a county, in Henei; see Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 古賀, sounding like 過 (guò).* Zuo Guipin, *Dirge for the Jin Empress Yuan*: "...kindness soared with the wind, bounty spread with the rain; within and without were blessed, far and near sang her praise (詠歌)." So explained in the Yan [Shigu] Kanmiu zhengsu.
- *Rhymes, fanqie 居之, sounding like 姬 (jī).* Qu Yuan, *Yuanyou*: "...the two maidens attend the Nine Shao; song (歌)! — I bid the Xiang sprite play the lute, and make Hairuo dance with Pingyi."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 斤於, sounding like 居 (jū).* Liu Zongyuan, *Stele-verse for Rao E*: "the Po folk cried in grief, and some in praise-song (歌); the maid of Qi grieved in her looks, mourning the locust-tree, and the punishment ceased."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "sometimes written 謌"; *Ji Yun*: "sometimes 𡃭".
