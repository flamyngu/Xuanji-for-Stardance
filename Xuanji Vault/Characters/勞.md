**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Strength radical 力; p.148 #35)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠣁, 𢥒.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 魯刀. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 郞刀 — sounding like 牢 (láo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "toil, hardship (劇); it follows 力 (strength) with 熒 contracted; he who uses strength toils (勞)." *Erya, Shigu*: "勞 means to labour diligently." Yijing, *Dui*: "delighting [the people] and going before them, the people forget their toil (勞)." Shiji, *Annals of Emperor Wen*: "I will sacrifice in person to God on high and the spirits; let the ritual officers deliberate without scruple, though it tire (勞) me."
- *Achievement, service.* Liji, *Ruxing*: "service (勞) first and pay after"; note: "勞 is like 'service.'"
- *Anxiety (憂).* Cao Pi, *Letter to Wu Zhi*: "not enough to loosen the knot of care (勞結)"; note: "the knot of an anxious heart."
- *A surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Later Han had Lao Bing of Langya."
- *Departing tone (lào).* *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 郞到. *Guangyun*: "勞 means to comfort, console." Liji, *Quli*: "when the lord consoles (勞) him, he bows." *Yun Hui*: "to 勞 someone is to recount his diligence by way of reward.
