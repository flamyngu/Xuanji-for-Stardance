**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.401 #26)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠪍, 𨗫.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun, Ji Yun, Yun Hui*: fanqie 於求. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於尤. Originally written 𢝊; now written 憂.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "sorrow (愁)." Erya, *Shi gu*: "thought (思)"; subcommentary: "憂 is sorrowful thought." Shu, *Hong fan*: "of the Six Extremes, the third is grief (憂)."
- *Illness (疾).* Liji, *Qu li*: "so-and-so has the affliction of carrying firewood (負薪之憂)"; note: "憂 sometimes means illness."
- *Hidden, obscure (幽).* Yijing, *Qian*: "in grief (憂) he withdraws from it"; note: "the time calls for retiring into obscurity."
- *Disgrace (辱).* Yijing, *Xici*: "the way of the small man is grief (憂)."
- *Mourning.* Shu, *Yue ming*: "the king dwelt in mourning (宅憂)."
- *The travail of pregnancy.* Jinyu: "King Wen in the womb gave [his mother] no trouble (不憂)."
- *Drooping in grief.* Liji, *Qu li*: "below the sash, then grief (憂)"; note: "in grief one droops."
- *癙憂, deep hidden grief.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "deep grief unto sickness." Zhouli, *Da zongbo*: "by rites of ill-omen to mourn the griefs (憂) of the states."
- *Surname.* From the *Xingyuan*.
- *Yun Hui*: fanqie 烏侯, sounding like 謳 (ōu); same sense (*Ji Yun* writes 㥑).
- *Ji Yun, Yun Hui*: fanqie 於救 — *Ji Yun*: "to be anxious (慮)"; Preface to Shijing, "the people are anxious (見憂)," Xu Miao's reading.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 衣 (yī), fanqie 於希.* Yilin: "not seeing Shu-ji, [she] makes Bo's heart grieve (憂)."
- *Rhymes as 於 (yū), fanqie 衣虛.* Yilin: "going out and in, resting at home, settled and still, without grief (憂)." *Huangting jing*: "…in a flash roaming free, with no lingering grief (憂)."
- *Rhymes as 妖 (yāo), fanqie 伊姚.* *Three Strategies*: "the officer may be humbled but not slighted; the general may be gladdened but not grieved (憂)."
- *Rhymes as 要 (yāo), fanqie 一笑.* Shijing, *Wang feng*: "…I meet these hundred griefs (憂)." Also *Tang feng*: "…having seen the gentleman, how then this grief (憂)?" (鵠 fanqie 居號).
- *Rhymes as 黝 (yǒu), fanqie 於糾.* Jinyu: "in Shang's decline, a maxim ran: 'a meagre virtue suffices not for advancement… and only takes grief (憂); a meagre fare suffices not to cling to… and only courts blame.'"

**On the graph.** *Ji Yun*: originally 𢝊, also 懮. *Shuowen*: from 心 and 頁 (頁 = the head); when the heart grieves, the hair whitens. — *Zihui bu*: also the same as 𢖒; *Shuowen*: "the gait of harmony (憂和之行)," citing Shijing, "spreading governance in harmony (憂憂)." Later people used 𢝊 for the "grief" sense, so the "harmony" sense came to use 𢖒 — gradually losing the origin of the Six Scripts.
