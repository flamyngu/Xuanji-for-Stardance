**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Moon radical 月; p.504 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 云久. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 云九 — sounding like 友 (yǒu).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "that which ought not to be there (i.e. an anomaly)." The Chunqiu tradition says: "the sun and moon have (有) an eclipse." It follows 月 (moon) with 又 as phonetic. The Jiujing ziyang: "有 follows 月 (moon); to follow 冃 is corrupt."
- *To have, to possess.* *Yupian*: "the not-without." Yijing, *Da You* subcommentary: "able to make great what one has (有)." Also *Xici*: "rich possession (富有) is called the great enterprise."
- *A region (a province).* Shijing, *Shang song*: "wholly he possessed the Nine Regions (九有)"; tradition: "the Nine 有 are the Nine Provinces."
- *A year of plenty (有年).* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 3: "a year of plenty (有年)"; note: "when the five grains all ripen, it is recorded as 'a year of plenty.'"
- *Other glosses.* *Yupian*: "indeed (果); to obtain (得); to take (取); substance (質); to examine (寀)."
- *A surname.* Lunyu: "You Zi (有子)"; note: "Confucius' disciple You Ruo."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 尤救; interchangeable with 又 ("and besides"). Shu, *Yao dian*: "the year is three hundred and (有) sixty and (有) six days." Shijing, *Beifeng*: "no day but it grows yet (有) more overcast"; note: "有 means 'and again.'"
- *Yunbu*: rhymes, fanqie 羽軌. Former Han, *Postface*: "the diction gorgeous, the use slight — 'Master Vacuous' and 'Sir Nonexistent' (烏有); lodging his words in lavish beauty, he set remonstrance from first to last."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 演女.* Xu Gan, *Fu on the Qi Capital*: "the host spreads a rich feast, meaning to exhaust all he has (有); the three wines already mellow, the five brews thick and strong."
