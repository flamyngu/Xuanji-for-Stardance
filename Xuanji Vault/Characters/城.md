**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.229 #32)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 是征. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 時征 — sounding like 成 (chéng).

**Etymology and senses.** The inner wall is the 城, the outer the 郭. *Shiming*: "城 means to complete (成): once completed, it cannot be destroyed." Gujin zhu: "to hold (盛): it holds and contains the state capital." Huainanzi, *Yuandao*: "Gun of Xia built walls three ren high; one says, the Yellow Emperor first set up walled towns (城邑) to dwell in." Baihu tong: "the Son of Heaven's wall is called the lofty wall (崇城)." Shiji, *Annals of the First Emperor*: "the emperor built the Ten-Thousand-Li Great Wall (長城)." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Yuan*: "the emperor first built the Chang'an wall; the south of the wall was shaped like the Southern Dipper, the north like the Northern Dipper, hence named the Dipper City (斗城)."
- *Also,* when feudal lords presumed beyond measure and built walls exceeding the regulation, it was called a "grown wall" (產城), as in the sense of a child growing up. Sima fa says: "to attack a wall is to attack what it has bred."
- *Also* Tang, Li Zhao, *Guoshi bu*: "on New Year's Day and the winter solstice, at the grand audience, when the hundred officials had assembled and the chancellor arrived last, the ranged candles numbered up to several hundred torches — called the fire-wall (火城)." Wang Yucheng, *Dailou yuan ji*: "the northern towers toward dawn, the east not yet light; the minister sets forth, the fire-wall (火城) ablaze."
- *Also 層城 (the tiered city).* Huainanzi, *Dixing*: "dig down below the Ruins of Kunlun, and within is the Tiered City of nine layers (層城九重)." Sun Chuo, *Tiantai fu*: "if the ridges of Tiantai may be climbed, why envy the Tiered City (層城)?"
- *Also an office-name.* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 16: "Prince Dang was made Sicheng (司城)"; note: "Duke Huan of Song, owing to the taboo on Duke Wu's name, changed Sikong (司空) to Sicheng (司城)."
- *Also a palace-name.* Former Han, *Biography of Favorite Beauty Ban*: "the Favorite Beauty dwelt in the Zengcheng lodge (增城舍)."
- *Also a mountain-name.* Xicheng (析城), west of Huo county in Hedong. Shu, *Yu gong*: "Dizhu and Xicheng (析城)." Also Mount Chicheng (赤城), southeast of Kuaiji. Sun Chuo, *Tiantai fu*: "Red City (赤城), where the rosy clouds rise to set a marker."
- *Also a grave-site is called a jiacheng (佳城, "the fair city").* Bowu zhi: "when Xiahou Ying died, the cortège reached outside the East Capital gate, where the horse stamped the ground and cried mournfully; digging, they found a stone coffin inscribed: 'the fair city (佳城), dense and dim — in three thousand years it sees the daylight. Alas, Duke Teng, you shall dwell in this chamber.'"
- *Also a surname:* Cheng Hun (城渾). Also Sicheng (司城), a compound surname.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 辰羊, sounding like 常 (cháng). Han Yu, *poem to Zhang Ji*: "my friend, come from the east, told me my family had escaped disaster; boarding a boat down the Bian waters, eastward I hasten to Pengcheng (彭城)."

**Textual notes.** In the Bowu zhi citation, 二 should read 三 (three thousand years), and 夏 should read 滕 (Duke Teng), per the original.
