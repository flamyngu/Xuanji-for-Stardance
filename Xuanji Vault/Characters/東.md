> 《東部》東：動也。从木。官溥說：从日在木中。凡東之屬皆从東。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Tree radical 木; p.513 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 德紅. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 都籠 — sounding like 蝀 (dōng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to move (動). The yang energy stirs; in the seasons it is spring." Shu, *Yao dian*: "regulate in due order the work of the east (東作)"; Kong's tradition: "the year begins in the east, and there ploughing first sets in." Huainanzi, *Tianwen*: "the east is Wood; its god is Taihao."
- *Also* Shiji, *Treatise on the Calendar*: "the sun rises in the east, the moon in the west." Zheng Qiao, *Tongzhi*: "the sun amid the tree is 東 (east); above the tree, 杲 (bright); below the tree, 杳 (dim). The tree is the Ruo tree, by which the sun ascends and descends."
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "in the east is the Opening-Brightness (啓明)" [the morning star].
- *Also* Erya, *Shidi*: "to the east as far as Taiyuan (泰遠)."
- *Also a surname.* Shengxian qunfu lu: "Shun's friend Dong Buzi (東不訾)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 當經, sounding like 丁 (dīng). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "I think of my land and dwelling; I was born in no good hour. Meeting Heaven's mighty wrath, from west I go to east (東)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 都郞, sounding like 當 (dāng). Yang Quan, *Can fu*: "so I summon my servants to build a house alongside. Alongside where? On the east (東) of the courtyard."

**On the graph.** A note: in the *Shuowen*, 東 forms its own radical-section; here it is merged in under 木 (wood).
