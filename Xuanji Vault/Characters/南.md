**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Ten radical 十; p.157 #05)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𡴖, 𡴟.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 那含 — sounding like 男 (nán).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "when plants reach the southern quarter, they put forth branches and bear (任)." Xu [Kai] says: "the south governs transformation and nurture, hence it is said to govern the bearing of branches." Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "the Great Yang is the south. 南 means to bear (任): the yang energy bears and nourishes things; in the seasons it is summer." Baihu tong: "the pitch-pipe of the eighth month is called Nanlü (南呂) — why? 南 means to bear: it says the yang energy still has the task of bringing forth shepherd's-purse and wheat."
- *Also a name of music.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "with the Ya, with the Nan (南)"; *Yun Hui*: "南 too is a name of refined music (雅樂), like the Nine Xia (九夏); both Nan and Xia belong to the quarter of culture and brightness, hence named 南. The Zhounan (周南) and Shaonan (召南) are likewise names of music."
- *Also a surname.* *Yun Hui*: "the Lu grandee Nan Yi (南遺)." Song shu, *Treatise on Pitch*: "Ban and Zuo gallop side by side, Dong and Nan rein abreast."
- *Also 雙南 (twin-south) means gold.* Fan Zhongyan, *Jin zai rong fu*: "its splendor once issued, the price of twin-south gold (雙南) climbs the higher; the casting not yet stayed, the merit of a hundred temperings may be awaited."
- *Also* Fanyi mingyi: "to join the palms in salutation is called 和南 (vandana)"; in the Chunhua tie, Lady Wei's calligraphy closes: "Wei makes salutation (和南)."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 尼心, sounding like 寧 (níng). Shijing, *Beifeng*: "I escort you far to the south (南)" — so read by Shen Zhong. Lu Yun, *Xiji fu*: "the Vermilion Brightness opens the season, the genial wind comes from the south (南); the old office of the Fire-Regent is restored, and the Sovereign Earth is put down amid the doubled shade."
