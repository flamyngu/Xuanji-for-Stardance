**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Village radical 里; p.1291 #03)*

**Ancient graph.** 𡍴.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 柱用. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 儲用 — sounding like 緟 (zhòng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "thick, weighty (厚)." *Zengyun*: "the opposite of light." Yijing, *Xici*: "the rush as a thing is slight, yet its use may be weighty (重)." Liji, *Wangzhi*: "light loads are combined, heavy (重) loads divided."
- *To do again (更為).* *Guangyun.* *Boya*: "重 means 'again, twice.'"
- *To be reluctant (難).* Zhanguo ce: "what I am loath (重) to do is to stay, and loath to linger"; note: "重 is like 'to find hard.'" Former Han, *Biography of the King of Huainan*: "Emperor Wen was reluctant (重) to upbraid him in person"; note: Ru Chun: "重 means 'to be loath.'"
- *To prize, value (貴).* Zhanguo ce: "Zhang Yi, in undoing Chuli Ji, honoured (重) him and yet used him"; note: "重 is like 'to esteem.'"
- *To honour (尊).* Liji, *Jitong*: "...to make manifest the Duke of Zhou's virtue, and also to honour (重) his state"; note: "重 is like 'to revere.'"
- *To set store by (尚).* Liji, *Ziyi*: "the minister models his conduct and sets no store (重) by empty words"; note: "重 is like 'to value'"; subcommentary: "the rule for a minister is not to prize hollow, showy words."
- *Frequent, repeated (數).* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 4: "arms may not be used over and over (重), else their use does not enlarge the house of Xia"; note: "重 is like 'frequent.'"
- *Greatly, severely (甚).* Liji, *Tangong*: "your weeping is just like one with sore (重) grief." Zhanguo ce: "...and you, sir, are deeply (重) on bad terms"; note: "重 is like 'severely.'"
- *Grave, chaste.* Huainanzi, *Fanlun*: "anciently men were honest, artisans solid, merchants plain, women grave (重)"; note: "女重, chaste, upright, without depravity."
- *The baggage-train (輺重).* Zhanguo ce: "the army's baggage (重) followed close at Gaowan"; note: "重 is the baggage-train." Former Han, *Biography of Zhang Er and Chen Yu*: "by a byway they cut off its baggage-train (輺重)."
- *A star-name.* *Boya*: "Jupiter is called the Chong-star (重星)."
- *A county-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Chongping county, in Bohai commandery."
- *A mountain-name.* Shanhaijing: "there is the Mountain of Doubled Shade (重隂)."
- *A tree-name.* *Boya*: "重皮 (double-bark) is the *houpo* magnolia."

**Rising tone (zhòng).** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 柱勇; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 直隴 — sounding like 𢝆 (zhòng). *Ji Yun*: "weighty; good; careful." *Wuyin jiyun*: "much." *Yun Hui*: Mao says, "of a thing not light but heavy, [read] the rising tone; to weight what is weighty, as in 再重 and 鄭重, [read] the departing." A note: rising and departing are two sounds but not two senses; the ancients used the three tones interchangeably, and to insist that they differ in gloss is the over-niceness of the Song men.

**Level tone (chóng).** *Guangyun*: fanqie 直容; *Yun Hui*: fanqie 傳容 — sounding like 褈 (chóng). *Guangyun*: "doubled, layered."
- *Doubled.* Yijing, *Qian*: "the third nine, doubled-hard (重剛) and off-centre"; subcommentary: "above and below both yang, hence 'doubled-hard.'" Shu, *Shundian*: "[Shun's] doubled brilliance (重華) accorded with the Emperor." Liji, *Liqi*: "the Son of Heaven's mat is five-fold (重), a lord's three-fold, a grandee's two-fold." Chuci, *Jiubian*: "...my lord's gate is ninefold (九重)."
- *A grain-name.* Shijing, *Binfeng*: "millet and grain, late and early (重穋)"; tradition: "the late-ripening is 重"; *Shiwen*: fanqie 直容; also written 穜.
- *Much (多).* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 2: "with abundant (重) vessels and gear"; note: "重 is like 'many.'"
- *To add to, burden (累).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "no thought but is anxious, it only adds to (重) my burden"; gloss: "重 is like 'to burden.'"
- *重光, the year of 辛.* Erya, *Shitian.*
- *A place-name.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 31: "Zang Wenzhong went and lodged at the Chong post-house (重館)"; note: "northwest of Fangyu county in Gaoping is the walled town of Chong." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 17: "...watered the horses at Chongqiu (重丘)"; note: "Chongqiu, a Cao town."
- *A carriage-name.* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "graceful the double-railed [car] (重較)"; tradition: "重較, a minister's carriage."
- *The eaves-gutter.* Liji, *Tangong*: "the coffin-channel matches the eaves-gutter (重霤)"; subcommentary: "重霤 is the eaves-gutter of a house, made of wood."
- *A personal name.* Shu, *Lüxing*: "he charged Chong and Li (重黎)"; tradition: "Chong is Xi, Li is He."
- *A surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "descendants of Chong-Li; the Ming had Chong Sheng."
- *That on which the spirit rests.* Liji, *Tangong*: "the *chong* is the way of the spirit-tablet (重主道也)"; note: "at first death, before the tablet is made, the *chong* hosts the spirit; once the *yu* sacrifice is done the *chong* is buried, and then the tablet is made."
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒紅, sounding like 同 (tóng) — same as 穜, a grain-name.
- *Same as 童.* Liji, *Tangong*: "with his neighbour the youth (重) Wang Qi he went, and both died there"; note: "重 should be 童, a term for one not yet capped."
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 之仲, sounding like 衆 (zhòng). Former Han, *Account of the Xiongnu*: "not so handy and good as the milk-curd (重酪)"; note: "重 is milk; properly written 潼."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 直良, sounding like 長 (cháng).* The Daozang hymn: "...in silent thought piercing the ninefold (九重); the spirit-song orders the dark course, a hundred harmonies bound in the cinnabar writ."

**Textual note.** "臣儀𠛬不重辭" should read "臣儀行不重辭".
