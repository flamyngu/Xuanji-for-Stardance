**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, middle; Self radical 己; p.326 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 羊己. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 養里 — sounding like 以 (yǐ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to stop (止); to finish (畢); to be done (訖)." *Guangyun*: "to complete (成)." *Ji Yun*: "a word for finishing a matter." Yijing, *Sun* hexagram: "the affair being done (已事), one hastens on."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to withdraw (退)." *Guangyun*: "to remove (去); to cast off (棄)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "try him, and if able, then employ; if not, then desist (已)." Lunyu: "thrice he dismissed (已) him."
- *Too, excessively (太).* *Guangyun*: "已 means very (甚)." Mengzi: "Confucius did nothing in excess (已甚)"; note: "he would not do anything to excess, going too far."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: a particle for a concluded matter. Shiji, *Biography of Guan Fu*: "having pledged his word (已然諾)"; note: "the *Suoyin* says: it means, having promised, he must make good his former word."
- *Also* *Leipian*: "the closing of speech." *Zengyun*: "a word ending speech." Former Han, *Biography of Mei Fu*: "and there is no overtaking it now (已)."
- *Past the time is called 已而 (presently).* Shiji, *Annals of Emperor Gao*: "presently (已而) she was with child."
- *Also* interchangeable with 以. Xunzi, *Fei xiang*: "that whereby (已) man is man — what is it? It is that he has the power to discriminate." Former Han, *Biography of Zhang Liang*: "the affairs of Yin being thereby (以) finished."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 羊吏, sounding like 異 (yì). The sense the same.
