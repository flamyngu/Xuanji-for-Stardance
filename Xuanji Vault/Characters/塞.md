## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集中〉【土字部】頁236第09【廣韻】蘇則切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】悉則切，𠀤音㥶。塡也，隔也。【禮·月令】孟冬，天地不通，閉塞成冬。【又】謹關梁，塞徯徑，季春開通道路，無有障塞。　又充也，滿也。【書·舜典】溫恭允塞。【詩·鄘風】秉心塞淵。　又國之阨險曰塞。【史記·蘇秦傳】秦四塞之國，披山帶渭。【後漢·杜篤傳】城池百尺，扼塞要害。　又月在辛曰塞，見【爾雅·釋天】。　又塞塞，不安貌，見【博雅】。　又伊蒲塞，卽優婆塞。【後漢·楚王英傳】以助伊蒲塞桑門之饌。　又【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤先代切，音賽。邊界也。【禮·月令】孟冬備邊境，完要塞。　又九塞。【淮南子·地形訓】九塞，太汾，澠阨荆阮方城，殽阪，井陘，令疵，句注，居庸。【註】太汾在晉，澠阨殽阪皆在弘農郡，荆阮方城皆在楚，井陘在常山，令疵在遼西，句注在鴈門隂館，居庸在上谷阻陽之東。　又紫塞。【古今注】秦築長城，土色皆紫，因名。　又博塞，戲具也。【莊子·騈拇篇】問穀何事，則博塞以遊。　又姓。　又與賽同。【前漢·郊祀志】冬塞禱祈。本作𡫟。亦作𡫼。    考證：〔【史記·蘇秦傳】秦四塞之固。〕　謹照原文固改國。〔【莊子·騈拇篇】問縠何事，則博塞以遊。〕　謹照原文縠改穀。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.236 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 蘇則. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 悉則 — sounding like 㥶 (sè).

**Etymology and senses.** To stop up (塡); to bar (隔). Liji, *Yue ling*: "in early winter, Heaven and Earth do not communicate, and shut and barred (閉塞) winter is formed." Also: "watch the barriers and bridges, block the by-paths (塞徯徑)"; "in late spring open the roads, and let there be no obstruction (障塞)."

**Senses.**
- *To fill; to be full (充, 滿).* Shu, *Shun dian*: "warm, reverent, and truly substantial (允塞)." Shijing, *Yong feng*: "holding her heart full and deep (塞淵)."
- *A state's narrow strongholds are called 塞 (passes).* Shiji, *Biography of Su Qin*: "Qin, a land of passes on four sides (四塞之國), cloaked in mountains and girt by the Wei." Later Han, *Biography of Du Du*: "ramparts and moats a hundred 尺, gripping the passes at the vital points (扼塞)."
- *The moon standing in 辛 is called 塞; seen in Erya, Shi tian.*
- *塞塞 (sese), an uneasy look; seen in the Boya.*
- *Yiputai (伊蒲塞), that is, the upāsaka (優婆塞).* Later Han, *Biography of Prince Ying of Chu*: "to aid the fare of the upāsaka (伊蒲塞) and the śramaṇa."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 先代, sounding like 賽 (sài). A border (邊界). Liji, *Yue ling*: "in early winter provide for the frontiers and make whole the vital passes (要塞)."
- *The Nine Passes (九塞).* Huainanzi, *Dixing xun*: "the Nine Passes are Taifen, Ming'e, Jingruan, Fangcheng, Xiaoban, Jingxing, Lingci, Gouzhu, and Juyong"; note: "Taifen is in Jin; Ming'e and Xiaoban are both in Hongnong commandery; Jingruan and Fangcheng are both in Chu; Jingxing is in Changshan; Lingci is in Liaoxi; Gouzhu is at Yinguan in Yanmen; Juyong is east of Zuyang in Shanggu."
- *The Purple Pass (紫塞).* The *Gujin zhu*: "when Qin built the Long Wall, the soil's colour was all purple, whence the name."
- *Bosai (博塞), a gaming-apparatus.* Zhuangzi, *Pian mu*: "ask what Gu was about, and it was at dice and draughts (博塞) that he idled."
- *Surname.*
- *Also* same as 賽. Former Han, *Treatise on the Sacrifices*: "in winter the thanksgiving sacrifice and the prayers (塞禱)."

**On the graph.** Originally written 𡫟; also written 𡫼.

**Textual notes.** Shiji, *Biography of Su Qin*, "Qin, a stronghold of passes on four sides (四塞之固)" — checked against the original, 固 is corrected to 國 (land). Also Zhuangzi, *Pian mu*, "ask what Gu (縠) was about, and it was at dice and draughts that he idled" — checked against the original, 縠 is corrected to 穀 (Gu, the name).
