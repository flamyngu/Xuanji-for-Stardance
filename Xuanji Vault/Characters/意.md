## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈卯集上〉【心字部】頁394第22【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】於記切【正韻】於戲切，𠀤音䔬。志之發也。【禮·大學疏】總包萬慮謂之心，爲情所意念謂之意。【禮運】非意之也。【註】意，心所無慮也。【疏】謂於無形之處，用心思慮也。無慮，卽慮無也。　又與抑通。【徐鍇曰】見之於外曰意。意，猶抑也。舍其言，欲出而抑之。【大戴禮】武王問黃帝，顓頊之道存乎，意亦忽不可得見歟。意猶抑。【論語】抑與之歟。【漢石經】作意，抑猶意，古通用也。　又【轉注古音】於宜切，音醫。【前漢·韓信傳】意嗚猝嗟。　又叶乙力切，音億。【秦之罘刻石文】大矣哉。宇縣之中，承順聖意。羣臣頌功，請刻於石，表垂乎常式。又與臆通。【賈誼·服賦】請對以意。【史記】作臆。【師古曰】叶韻音億。【魏校曰】从心从音。意不可見而象，因言以會意也。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.394 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於記. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於戲 — sounding like 䔬 (yì).

**Etymology and senses.** "The issuing-forth of the will (志之發)." Liji, *Daxue* subcommentary: "what embraces all thoughts is the heart (心); what the feelings dwell on in thought is the intent (意)." Liyun: "it is not [a thing] imagined (意)"; note: "意 is what the heart muses on the unseen"; subcommentary: "to use the heart in thought upon the formless; 無慮 means 'to muse on the void.'"
- *Interchangeable with 抑 (yet, or).* Xu Kai: "shown without, it is 意; 意 is like 抑 — setting aside one's words, about to speak and checking it." Da Dai Li: "King Wu asked whether the way of the Yellow Emperor and Zhuanxu survived, or rather (意) had it vanished beyond seeing?" Lunyu: "or (抑) was it given him?"; the Han Stone Classics write 意 — 抑 is like 意, anciently interchangeable.
- *Zhuanzhu guyin*: fanqie 於宜, sounding like 醫 (yī). Former Han, *Biography of Han Xin*: "[with] sighing and sudden cries (意嗚猝嗟)."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 乙力, sounding like 億 (yì).* The Qin Zhifu stele inscription: "...within the realm, all comply with the sage's will (意); the assembled ministers extol his merit, and beg to engrave it on stone, to hang it forth as a lasting model."

**On the graph.** Interchangeable with 臆 (the breast; to surmise): Jia Yi, *Owl fu*: "let me answer with my surmise (意)"; the Shiji writes 臆; Yan Shigu: "in rhyme it sounds 億." Wei Jiao: "it follows 心 (heart) and 音 (sound); the intent cannot be seen but is imaged — through words one grasps the intent.
