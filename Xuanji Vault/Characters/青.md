**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Blue radical 青; p.1381 #19)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𡴑, 𡴏, 𡴐, 𡗡, 寈.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 倉經, sounding like 鶄 (qīng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The colour of the east (東方色)." *Shiming*: "靑 means birth, growth (生); it images the colour of things at their growing time." Shu, *Yu gong*: "its soil is blue-black and friable (靑黎)." Xunzi, *Quan xue*: "blue (靑) comes from the indigo-plant, yet is bluer than the indigo."

**Senses.**
- *A god's name.* Shiji, *Feng shan shu*: "Duke Xuan of Qin made the Mi altar south of the Wei, sacrificing to the Blue Thearch (靑帝)."
- *A prefecture-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "between the sea and Mount Dai is Qingzhou (靑州)."
- *A bird-name.* Liji, *Qu li*: "if water is ahead, raise the blue pennant (靑旌)"; note: "靑 is the blue-sparrow, a water-bird."
- *A tree-name.* Yu Xin, *Buxu ci*: "the empty-blue (空靑) makes a whole grove"; note: "the *Yunji qiqian* says: in the Yuqing heaven there is a tree like a pine, named the grove of empty-blue (空靑)." Also *Guangyun*: "the male-blue (男靑) and female-blue (女靑), both tree-names, from the *Record of Mount Luofu*."
- *A fruit-name.* 靑子 (the blue fruit) is the olive (橄欖). Su Shi's poem: "the blue fruit (靑子) falling thick on the red salt."
- *A drug-name.* *Bencao gangmu*: "the empty-blue (空靑) is hollow within; broken, it has a juice; it cures eye-ailments — also called bayberry-blue. The white-blue (白靑) cures eye-ailments; the deep-coloured is stone-blue (石靑), the pale is jasper-blue (碧靑)." The Huainanzi, *Biwan shu*, says: "the white-blue, getting iron, turns to copper." Also the layered-blue (曾靑), green-blue (綠靑), flat-blue (扁靑), green-skin-blue (綠膚靑) — all detailed in the *Bencao gangmu*.
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "bamboo-skin is called 靑." Later Han, *Biography of Wu You*: "they 'killed the green' (殺靑簡) of the slips to write the canonical texts"; note: "by roasting the slips over fire to make them sweat and take out the green, the writing comes easier and is also not worm-eaten — called 'killing the green' (殺靑)."
- *Also* Tang, Li Zhao, *Hanlin zhi*: "for all the Great Pure-Palace and the temple-shrines' presented prayer-texts, blue vine-paper and vermilion characters were used, called blue-words (靑詞)."
- *Also* Li Chuo, *Suishi ji*: "on the Shangsi day, the feast by the Qujiang waters is called 'treading the green' (踏靑)."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "from the *He's Garden of Surnames*." Also three compound surnames: the Han had Qingwuzi (靑烏子); also the Qingniu (靑牛) clan and the Qingyang (靑陽) clan.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 子丁 — same as 菁. Shijing, *Weifeng*: "the green bamboo lush and thick (靑靑)"; tradition: "靑靑 is a flourishing look"; *Shiwen*: "靑, fanqie 子丁; also written 菁." Also *Xiaoya*: "its leaves green and thick (靑靑)"; *Shiwen*: "靑, fanqie 子零."

**Textual note.** Xunzi, *Quan xue*, "blue comes from the indigo, yet outdoes the indigo (靑出於藍，而勝於藍)" — checked against the original, it is corrected to "靑出之藍而靑於藍."
