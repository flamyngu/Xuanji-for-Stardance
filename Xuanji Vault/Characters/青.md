**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, middle; Blue radical йқ’; p.1381 #19)*

**Ancient graphs.** рЎҙ‘, рЎҙҸ, рЎҙҗ, рЎ—Ў, еҜҲ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie еҖүз¶“, sounding like й¶„ (qД«ng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The colour of the east (жқұж–№иүІ)." *Shiming*: "йқ‘ means birth, growth (з”ҹ); it images the colour of things at their growing time." Shu, *Yu gong*: "its soil is blue-black and friable (йқ‘й»Һ)." Xunzi, *Quan xue*: "blue (йқ‘) comes from the indigo-plant, yet is bluer than the indigo."

**Senses.**
- *A god's name.* Shiji, *Feng shan shu*: "Duke Xuan of Qin made the Mi altar south of the Wei, sacrificing to the Blue Thearch (йқ‘еёқ)."
- *A prefecture-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "between the sea and Mount Dai is Qingzhou (йқ‘е·һ)."
- *A bird-name.* Liji, *Qu li*: "if water is ahead, raise the blue pennant (йқ‘ж—Ң)"; note: "йқ‘ is the blue-sparrow, a water-bird."
- *A tree-name.* Yu Xin, *Buxu ci*: "the empty-blue (з©әйқ‘) makes a whole grove"; note: "the *Yunji qiqian* says: in the Yuqing heaven there is a tree like a pine, named the grove of empty-blue (з©әйқ‘)." Also *Guangyun*: "the male-blue (з”·йқ‘) and female-blue (еҘійқ‘), both tree-names, from the *Record of Mount Luofu*."
- *A fruit-name.* йқ‘еӯҗ (the blue fruit) is the olive (ж©„ж¬–). Su Shi's poem: "the blue fruit (йқ‘еӯҗ) falling thick on the red salt."
- *A drug-name.* *Bencao gangmu*: "the empty-blue (з©әйқ‘) is hollow within; broken, it has a juice; it cures eye-ailments вҖ” also called bayberry-blue. The white-blue (зҷҪйқ‘) cures eye-ailments; the deep-coloured is stone-blue (зҹійқ‘), the pale is jasper-blue (зў§йқ‘)." The Huainanzi, *Biwan shu*, says: "the white-blue, getting iron, turns to copper." Also the layered-blue (жӣҫйқ‘), green-blue (з¶ йқ‘), flat-blue (жүҒйқ‘), green-skin-blue (з¶ иҶҡйқ‘) вҖ” all detailed in the *Bencao gangmu*.
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "bamboo-skin is called йқ‘." Later Han, *Biography of Wu You*: "they 'killed the green' (ж®әйқ‘з°Ў) of the slips to write the canonical texts"; note: "by roasting the slips over fire to make them sweat and take out the green, the writing comes easier and is also not worm-eaten вҖ” called 'killing the green' (ж®әйқ‘)."
- *Also* Tang, Li Zhao, *Hanlin zhi*: "for all the Great Pure-Palace and the temple-shrines' presented prayer-texts, blue vine-paper and vermilion characters were used, called blue-words (йқ‘и©һ)."
- *Also* Li Chuo, *Suishi ji*: "on the Shangsi day, the feast by the Qujiang waters is called 'treading the green' (иёҸйқ‘)."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "from the *He's Garden of Surnames*." Also three compound surnames: the Han had Qingwuzi (йқ‘зғҸеӯҗ); also the Qingniu (йқ‘зүӣ) clan and the Qingyang (йқ‘йҷҪ) clan.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie еӯҗдёҒ вҖ” same as иҸҒ. Shijing, *Weifeng*: "the green bamboo lush and thick (йқ‘йқ‘)"; tradition: "йқ‘йқ‘ is a flourishing look"; *Shiwen*: "йқ‘, fanqie еӯҗдёҒ; also written иҸҒ." Also *Xiaoya*: "its leaves green and thick (йқ‘йқ‘)"; *Shiwen*: "йқ‘, fanqie еӯҗйӣ¶."

**Textual note.** Xunzi, *Quan xue*, "blue comes from the indigo, yet outdoes the indigo (йқ‘еҮәж–ји—ҚпјҢиҖҢеӢқж–ји—Қ)" вҖ” checked against the original, it is corrected to "йқ‘еҮәд№Ӣи—ҚиҖҢйқ‘ж–ји—Қ."
