**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.230 #17)*

**Ancient graphs.** 㽣, 䧕, 𢨊.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 雨逼. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 越逼 — sounding like 棫 (yù).

**Etymology and senses.** "A state (邦); a region (區域); a bounded space (界局)." Gu Sanfen: "returning, they settle and dwell in their domain (域)." Shijing, *Shangsong*: "he first bounded (域) those four seas." Zhouli, *Diguan, Da situ*: "by the map of the world's lands he knows fully the territories (地域) of the Nine Provinces, their breadth and length."
- *Also, within the cosmos is called 域中.* Zhuangzi, *Qiushui*: "drifting, drifting, like the four quarters without end, with no bound or border (畛域)." Huainanzi, *Chuzhen*: "in the age of utmost virtue, men slept content in the realm (域) of murky vastness."
- *Also, a foreign land is called a 絕域 (far realm).* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he decreed the provinces and commanderies to seek out among officials and people those fit to be generals or ministers, or to be sent to far realms (絕域)."
- *Also, a grave-plot is called a 域.* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Zhongren*: "he has charge of the ducal burial-ground, and distinguishes its grave-bounds (兆域)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 乙六, sounding like 郁 (yù). Xie Huilian, *Xue fu*: "the Snow Palace built in the eastern realm, the Snow Mountain towering in the western region (西域)" — rhyming with 竹 below.

**On the graph.** Separately written 𡌳; see the Peixi. 𡌳 differs from 𢧄 (𢧄, pronounced 耊, follows 戈, 口, and 𡈼; this graph follows 或 and 土). 𢧌 is the same as 𡌳; 𢎆 is the same as 𢧄.
