**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls з”і, upper; Grass radical иүё; p.1023 #12)*

**Ancient graphs.** рҰұЎ, рҰұ¶, р§ҒҮ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie иҖҢзҒј. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie ж—ҘзҒј вҖ” sounding like ејұ (ruГІ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "иӢҘ means to pick vegetables (ж“ҮиҸң)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Yupian*: "жқңиӢҘ (duruo), a fragrant herb." Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "I pluck duruo (жқңиӢҘ) on the fragrant islet." *Mengxi bitan*: "duruo is today's galangal (й«ҳиүҜи–‘)."
- *To comply, obey (й Ҷ).* Shu, *Yao dian*: "reverently complying with (ж¬ҪиӢҘ) vast Heaven"; tradition: "respectfully obeying." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the descendant complies (жҳҜиӢҘ)."
- *You, thou (жұқ).* Yili, *Shi hun li*: "exhort and lead her in reverence to carry on the late mother's place; let you (иӢҘ) be constant." *Discourses of Jin*: "Duke Wen of Jin said to Bo Di: 'you served Duke Hui in following me to the Wei shore; the order was for three days, and you (иӢҘ) came after a night.'"
- *Like, as (еҰӮ).* Shu, *Pan geng*: "like (иӢҘ) the net on its cord."
- *Then, and so (д№ғ).* *Discourses of Zhou*: "there must be forbearance, and then (иӢҘ) there can be success."
- *A particle.* Yili, *Shi xiang jian li*: "if (иӢҘ) the lord comes down to see him off, he dares not look back"; subcommentary: "иӢҘ is a word of uncertainty."
- *Also* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "for the people ninety years and over, exempt their sons or (иӢҘ) grandsons"; note: "иӢҘ is a word reaching forward to include."
- *иӢҘиӢҘ, a dangling look.* Former Han, *Biography of Shi Xian*: "what trailing seals, what dangling cords (з¶¬иӢҘиӢҘ)!"
- *Also* Zhuangzi, *Qiu shui*: "he sighed facing Ruo (иӢҘ)"; note: "Ruo is the sea-god."
- *A year-name.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year in дё‘ is called Chifenruo (иөӨеҘ®иӢҘ)."
- *иӢҘжңЁ (the Ruo tree).* Huainanzi, *Di xing*: "the Ruo tree (иӢҘжңЁ) is west of the Jian tree." Chuci, *Tian wen*: "the Ruo's bloom (иӢҘиҸҜ), what light?"; note: "how could the Ruo tree have bright red light and bloom?"
- *A water-name.* Shuijing zhu: "the Ruo water (иӢҘж°ҙ) flows southeast, and the Xian water joins it."
- *Surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "Ruo Zhang, Chancellor of Xiapi under the Han."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie дәәиҖ…, sounding like жғ№ (rДӣ). Dry grass (д№ҫиҚү).
- *иҲ¬иӢҘ (prajГұДҒ), Sanskrit for wisdom (жҷәж…§).* Jinshu, *Biography of Tan Huo*: "Huo held a tin staff and bade a man kneel, saying: 'this is the eye of prajГұДҒ (жіўиӢҘ).'"
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "a Buddhist dwelling, in the Western Regions called a иҳӯиӢҘ (ДҒraб№Үya)." Liu Zongyuan's prose: "Lanruo Zhengong (иҳӯиӢҘзңһе…¬)"; note: "those granted a name-board by the state are temples (еҜә); those privately built are zhaoti or lanruo (иҳӯиӢҘ)."
- *Also* fanqie дәәиі’, sounding like е©ј (ruГІ). A Shu place-name. Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Ruo (иӢҘ) belongs to Nan commandery"; the *Chunqiu zhuan* writes й„Җ. *Tang yun guyin*: read жұқ (rЗ”). The *San lГјe*: "high and low ranking each other (зӣёиӢҘ), strong and weak enslaving each other"; the ancients read the graph иӢҘ as жұқ, hence in records иӢҘ is often used for жұқ. Shiji, *Annals of Xiang Yu*: "my father is your (иӢҘ) father"; the Hanshu: "my father is your (жұқ) father" вҖ” this is the proof.
