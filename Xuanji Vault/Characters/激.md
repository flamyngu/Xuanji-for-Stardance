**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.654 #17)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 吉歷. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 訖逆 — sounding like 擊 (jī), i.e. **jī**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "An obstructed, slanting, racing wave"; one sense: "to half-block." Former Han, *Treatise on Ditches and Canals*: "stone dikes were built to *deflect* (激) [the water] so it poured eastward." To "*ji*" is to heap stones at a strategic point beside a dike, so as to drive the water away.

**Senses.**
- *To dash against.* Pan Yue's poem: "the startled rapids dash (激) on the cliff-bends."
- *A landmark.* Shuijingzhu: some *li* north of the Mian River there is a great rock named the "Five-Maidens' Dash (五女激)."
- *Blunt and over-direct speech (激切).* Hou Han, *Biography of Chen Chong*: "those who speak on affairs are surely much given to blunt directness (激切)."
- *To be stirred, moved (感激).* Hou Han, *Biography of Cai Yong*: "moved to the depths, he forgot himself."
- *To flout custom and strike a pose (激詭).* Hou Han, *Biography of Fan Ran*: "Ran loved to defy the times and cut himself off from the common, in acts of contrived eccentricity (激詭)" — 冉 is also written 丹.
- *Jichu, a clear/high musical tone.* Chuci, *Summons of the Soul*: "the palace courts shook with awe as the *jichu* strains rang out."
- *Surname.* Former Han, *Biography of the Prince of Huainan*: there was one Ji Zhang.

**Further readings.**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 古弔. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 吉弔 — sounding like 叫 (jiào): the look of a rushing current; one sense, the sound of wind.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 堅堯, sounding like 驍 (xiāo) — same as 憿, "good fortune"; interchangeable with 僥/徼.

**Textual note.** The quotation assigned to *Chuci, "Nine Songs"* should be from *"Summons of the Soul."*
