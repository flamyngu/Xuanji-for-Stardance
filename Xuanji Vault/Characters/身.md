**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, middle; Body radical 身; p.1237 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 失人. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 升人 — sounding like 申 (shēn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the trunk/person (躬); it images a person's body." Erya, *Shi gu*: "I, oneself (我)"; subcommentary: "身, referring to oneself." *Shiming*: "身 is 'to stretch (伸)' — it can bend and stretch." *Guangyun*: "near, kin (親)." *Jiujing yunlan*: "the frame (軀); the sum of all the bones is 身." Yijing, *Gen*: "still his body (身)." *Xici*: "near at hand, take it from the body (身)." Shu, *Yi xun*: "examine the self (身) as if falling short." *Xiaojing*: "body, limbs, hair, and skin are received from one's parents."
- *Pregnancy.* Shijing, *Daya*: "Tai Ren was with child (有身)"; tradition: "身, doubled (重)"; *Jian*: "重 means carrying a child"; subcommentary: "within the body there is another body, hence 'doubled.'"
- *告身, a certificate of appointment.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Selection*: "drafts and grants were all issued with a tally, called 告身."
- *身毒, India.* Shiji, *Account of the Southwestern Yi*; note (Suoyin): "身 sounds 捐 (juān)."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 尸連.* Yang Fang, *Hehuan shi*: "my feeling joins with yours, as the shadow follows the body (身); we sleep beneath one woven coverlet, its floss of single-spun silk."
