## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈申集下〉【衣字部】頁1111第01【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤於希切，音依。【說文】上曰衣，下曰裳。【世本】胡曹作衣。黃帝時人。【白虎通】衣者，隱也。【釋名】衣，依也。人所以依以庇寒暑也。【玉篇】所以形軀依也。【類篇】象覆二人之形。【易·繫辭】黃帝、堯、舜垂衣裳，而天下治，蓋取諸乾坤。【禮·玉藻】衣，正色。裳，閒色。【傅𤣥·衣銘】衣服從其儀，君子德也。衣以飾外，德以飾內。　又絲衣，祭服也。【詩·周額】絲衣其紑。　又【博雅】寢衣，衾，𩏩服也。　又【釋名】中衣，言在外小衣之外，大衣之中也。　又心衣，抱腹而施鉤肩，鉤肩之閒施一襠，以養心也。　又面衣。【晉書·惠帝紀】尚書高光進面衣。　又耳衣。【唐·邊塞曲】金縫耳衣寒。　又綴衣，掌衣服官名。【書·立政】王左右常伯、常任、準人、綴衣、虎賁。　又白衣，未仕之稱。【後漢·崔駰傳】憲諫以爲不宜與白衣會。　又牛衣，編亂麻爲之。卽今俗呼爲龍具者。【前漢·王章傳】章疾病，無被，臥牛衣中。　又垣衣，苔也。【王融詩】垣衣不可裳。　又姓。【通志·氏族略】見《姓苑》。【正字通】明有衣勉仁、衣祐。　又人名。【高士傳】被衣，堯時人。蒲衣，舜時人。　又靑衣，地名。【史記·彭越傳】處蜀靑衣。【註】今爲臨卭。　又借服膺意。【書·康誥】紹聞衣德言。　又【唐韻】於旣切，讀去聲。服之也。【玉篇】以衣被人也。【增韻】著衣也。【晏子·雜下篇】衣十升之布。【前漢·東方朔傳】身衣弋綈。　又【韻補】於斤切。齊人言衣聲如殷，今姓有衣者，殷之謂歟。一作㐆。【通志·六書略】卽衣字，从向、身。    考證：〔【正字通】明有衣勉仁，衣𧙗。〕　謹照原文𧙗改祐。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, lower; Clothes radical 衣; p.1111 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於希 — sounding like 依 (yī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the upper [garment] is 衣, the lower is 裳 (skirt)." Shiben: "Hu Cao made clothes; a man of the Yellow Emperor's time." Baihu tong: "衣 is 隱 (to cover)." *Shiming*: "衣 is 依 (to lean on): what man relies on to ward off cold and heat." *Yupian*: "that on which the body relies for its form." *Leipian*: "it images the shape of covering two persons." Yijing, *Xici*: "the Yellow Emperor, Yao, and Shun let their robes (衣裳) hang down and the world was governed — they took this from Qian and Kun." Liji, *Yuzao*: "the upper robe is of a pure colour, the skirt of a mixed colour." Fu Xuan, *Inscription on Clothes*: "dress should match one's station — that is the gentleman's virtue; clothes adorn without, virtue adorns within."
- *絲衣, a silk sacrificial vestment.* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "the silk robe (絲衣), how clean."
- *寢衣, the sleeping-robe (coverlet).* *Boya.*
- *中衣, the middle robe.* *Shiming*: "outside the small inner clothes and within the great outer robe."
- *心衣, the heart-robe.* A belly-wrap with shoulder-hooks, a pad set between to warm the heart.
- *面衣, the face-cloth.* Jinshu, *Annals of Emperor Hui*: "the minister Gao Guang presented a face-cloth (面衣)."
- *耳衣, the ear-muff.* Tang frontier song: "gold-stitched, the ear-muffs (耳衣) cold."
- *綴衣, an officer of clothing.* Shu, *Lizheng*: "the Changbo, Changren, Zhunren, Zhuiyi (綴衣), and Huben."
- *白衣, plain-robe (one not yet in office).* Later Han, *Biography of Cui Yin*: "Xian remonstrated that he ought not to consort with commoners (白衣)."
- *牛衣, the ox-blanket (woven of coarse hemp).* Former Han, *Biography of Wang Zhang*: "Zhang fell ill and, having no quilt, lay in an ox-blanket (牛衣)."
- *垣衣, wall-moss.* Wang Rong's poem: "the wall-moss (垣衣) cannot be made a skirt."
- *A surname.* Tongzhi, *Monograph on Clans*: "found in the Xingyuan." *Zhengzitong*: "the Ming had Yi Mianren and Yi You."
- *Personal names.* Gaoshi zhuan: "Beiyi, a man of Yao's time; Puyi, a man of Shun's time."
- *靑衣, a place-name.* Shiji, *Biography of Peng Yue*: "banished to Qingyi in Shu"; note: "now Linqiong."
- *To take to heart.* Shu, *Kanggao*: "carry on what you have heard and take to heart (衣) words of virtue."
- *To wear (departing tone).* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 於旣. *Yupian*: "to clothe another." *Zengyun*: "to put on clothes." Yanzi, *Zaxia*: "he wore (衣) cloth of ten *sheng*." Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "his body clad (衣) in dark coarse silk."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 於斤.* "The men of Qi pronounce 衣 like 殷; the present surname Yi (衣) is perhaps from 殷." Also written 㐆. Tongzhi, *Liushu lüe*: "it is the 衣 graph, from 向 and 身."

**Textual note.** "衣𧙗" should read "衣祐".
