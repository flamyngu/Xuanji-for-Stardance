## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈午集上〉【玉字部】頁730第29【唐韻】章俱切【集韻】鍾輸切【正韻】專于切，𠀤音朱。【說文】蚌之隂精。春秋國語曰：珠以禦火災，是也。【通雅】古有辟塵珠，辟寒珠，夜光照乗，大者徑寸，或出於龍魚異物腹中，非獨出於蚌也。陸佃云：龍珠在頷，蛇珠在口，魚珠在眼，鮫珠在皮，𪔀珠在足，蛛珠在腹，皆不及蚌珠。【沈懷遠·南越志】珠有九品，寸五分以上至寸八九分者爲大品，有光彩，一邊小平似覆釜者名璫珠，璫珠之次名走珠，走珠之次爲滑珠，滑珠之次爲磊砢珠，磊砢珠之次爲官珠雨珠，官雨珠之次爲稅珠，稅珠之次爲蔥珠。見續博物志。【書·禹貢】淮夷𧓍珠曁魚。【周禮·天官·玉府】若合諸侯，則共珠槃玉敦。　又【博物志】江珠，琥珀別名。　又【正字通】山海經濫水注：漢水多𩶯𩶆之魚，狀如覆銚，是生珠玉數，歷山楚水多白珠，蜀郡平澤出靑珠。左思云：靑珠黃環，西國琅玕碧珠，皆寶石名之以珠者也。　又地名。【前漢·武帝紀】珠厓儋耳。【註】二郡在大海中，崖岸之邊出眞珠，故曰珠崖。【穆天子傳】天子舍于珠澤。【註】珠澤在越巂。【水經注】若水旁有光珠穴。　又木名。【山海經】開明北有視玉珠樹。【註】論衡云：珠樹似珠，非眞珠也。　又連珠，文家篇名。【傅𤣥序】連珠興於漢章帝之世，班固，賈逵，傅毅三子受詔作之，不指說事情，假喻達旨，合古詩勸興之義，欲使歷歷如貫珠，易睹而可悅也。【沈約·註連珠表】竊尋連珠之作，始於子雲，謂辭句連續，互相發明，若珠之結排也。　又【韻補】叶音周。【易林】老猾大偸，東行盜珠。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, upper; Jade radical 玉; p.730 #29)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 章俱. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 鍾輸. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 專于 — sounding like 朱 (zhū).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the yin-essence of the oyster." The Chunqiu Guoyu says: "pearls (珠) ward off fire-disaster" — this is it. Tongya: "of old there were the dust-repelling pearl, the cold-repelling pearl, and the night-shining one that lit a carriage; the large ones an inch across, sometimes from the bellies of dragons, fish, and strange creatures, not from oysters alone." Lu Dian says: "the dragon's pearl is in its jaw, the snake's in its mouth, the fish's in its eye, the shark's in its skin, the turtle's at its feet, the spider's in its belly — none equals the oyster's pearl." Shen Huaiyuan, *Nanyue zhi*: "pearls have nine grades. Those from an inch and five-tenths up to an inch eight or nine-tenths are the great grade, having luster; one slightly flat on one side like an inverted cauldron is the dang-pearl (璫珠); next the zou-pearl (走珠); next the hua-pearl (滑珠); next the leiluo-pearl (磊砢珠); next the official-pearl and rain-pearl (官珠雨珠); next the shui-pearl (稅珠); next the cong-pearl (蔥珠)." Seen in the Xu Bowu zhi. Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Huai-Yi sent oyster-pearls (𧓍珠) and fish." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Yufu*: "when the feudal lords assemble, the Jade Treasury supplies the pearl basin and jade bowl (珠槃玉敦)."
- *Also* Bowu zhi: 江珠 (jiangzhu), another name for amber.
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "the Shanhai jing, note to the Lan River, says the Han waters abound in a fish shaped like an inverted pan, which yields pearls and jade; the waters of Lishan and Chu abound in white pearls; the level marshes of Shu commandery yield green pearls. Zuo Si says, 'green pearls and yellow rings'; the western lands' langgan and emerald pearls — all are gem-stones named with 'pearl' (珠)."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "Zhuya (珠厓) and Dan'er"; note: "the two commanderies lie in the great sea; true pearls came from the edge of their shores, hence Zhuya (珠崖, Pearl-Shore)." Mu Tianzi zhuan: "the Son of Heaven lodged at the Pearl Mere (珠澤)"; note: "the Pearl Mere is in Yuexi." Shuijing zhu: "beside the Ruo River is the Bright-Pearl Cave (光珠穴)."
- *Also a tree-name.* Shanhai jing: "north of Kaiming is the jade-pearl tree (珠樹)"; note: "the Lunheng says: the pearl-tree resembles pearls but is not true pearl."
- *Also 連珠 (linked pearls), a name for a literary genre.* Fu Xuan's preface: "the linked-pearls (連珠) arose in the age of Emperor Zhang of Han; the three masters Ban Gu, Jia Kui, and Fu Yi composed them by decree — not pointing to facts directly, but conveying the import by metaphor, in keeping with the exhortatory sense of the old odes, wishing them to be distinct, like pearls on a string, easy to behold and pleasing." Shen Yue, *Memorial annotating the Linked-Pearls*: "I find, on inquiry, that the making of linked-pearls began with Ziyun [Yang Xiong]: it means phrases linked in succession, mutually illuminating, like pearls strung in a row."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as 周 (zhōu). Yilin: "the old rogue, the great thief, goes east to steal pearls (珠)."
