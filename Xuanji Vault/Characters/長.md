**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, upper; Long radical 長; p.1328 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** 镸, 𨱗, 𠤐, 𠑷, 𠔊, 𠑻, 𠑿, 𡕣.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 直良. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 仲良 — sounding like 場 (cháng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Zengyun*: "the opposite of short." Mengzi: "now I, Jiao, am nine *chi* four *cun* in height (長)." Former Han, *Biography of Tian Heng*: "a foot has its shortcomings, an inch its length (長)."
- *Long-lasting (久).* Shijing, *Shangsong*: "deep-wise was Shang; long (長) did it send forth its omens"; gloss: "長 is like 'long-enduring.'" Laozi, *Daodejing*: "the reason Heaven and Earth can be lasting and enduring is that they do not live for themselves — hence they can live long (長生)."
- *Far (遠).* Shijing, *Lusong*: "following that long (長) road, he subdued these hordes"; gloss: "長 means far." Old poem: "the road is hard and long (長)."
- *Ever, constantly (常).* Tao Qian, *Gui qu lai ci*: "though there is a gate, it is ever (長) barred." Li Shangyin's poem: "wind and cloud forever (長) guard the palisade."
- *Great (大).* Shishuo xinyu (supplement): "I would ride the great (長) wind and break the waves of ten thousand *li*."
- *Good, excellent (善).* Jinshu, *Biography of Yue Guang*: "in appraising a man, first praise his strong points (所長)." Tangshu, *Biography of Han Yuan*: "excelling and surpassing (優長) in letters and arts."
- *To hold under the arm (挾).* *Boya.*
- *長弟, sooner or later.* The Wuyu: "how should I dare not obey your command? — sooner or later (長弟) I assent"; note: "長弟 is like saying 'first and last.'"
- *A star-name.* *Boya*: "Venus is called Changgeng (長庚)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in the east is Qiming, in the west Changgeng"; tradition: "at daybreak the bright star is called Qiming; after sunset, Changgeng."
- *Palace-names.* Ban Gu, *Xidu fu*: "northward it stretches to Mingguang and spans Changle (長樂)." Xie Tiao, *Yuanqing shi*: "from the side-courts one is betrothed to far lands; at Changmen (長門) the glad feasts are lost"; note: "Changmen, where the Han Empress Chen dwelt."
- *A place-name.* Yuhai: "Chang'an (長安), originally land within the Passes; the Western Han set its capital here, and thereafter the Son of Heaven's capital was called Chang'an."
- *Mountain-names.* Shuolin: "the duke saw the Long Mountain (長山) of Dongyang and said, 'how level and far-stretching!'" Jinshi, *Treatise on Rites*: "Changbai Mountain (長白山) lies in the land of the dynasty's rise, and by rite ought to be venerated."
- *A state-name.* Shanhaijing: "there is the country of Yanchang (鹽長)."
- *A beast-name.* Shanhaijing: "there is a beast shaped like a monkey but with four ears, named Changyou (長右)."
- *A plant-name.* Erya, *Shicao*: "長楚 (*changchu*) is the *yaoyi*"; subcommentary: "長楚, also called *yaoyi*."
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "Master Ning sent Chang Zang ahead to guard the gate"; note: "Chang Zang, a grandee of Wey." Also Zhongchang, a compound surname. Also Changcheng, a god's name: Shanhaijing, Guo Pu's encomium: "the *qi* of the Nine Virtues gave birth to Changcheng; man-shaped with a leopard tail, his spirit congealed; a wondrous being self-hidden, the age could not name him."

**Rising tone (zhǎng).** *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 展兩, sounding like 掌 (zhǎng). "The eldest, the chief."
- *Chief, foremost.* Yijing, *Qian*: "the Originating is the chief (長) of goods"; subcommentary: "the Originating is the source of life-giving, hence 'the chief of goods.'" Zhanguo ce: "if you, my lord, head (長) Qi, what need of Xue?"; note: "長 is the 長 of 'to be chief.'"
- *Senior in years (齒高).* Shu, *Yixun*: "establish love beginning with kin, establish reverence beginning with elders (長)." Liji, *Quli*: "one twice one's years in age (長) one serves as a father; ten years one's senior, as an elder brother; five years one's senior, one walks shoulder-behind."
- *High in rank (位高).* Shu, *Yiji*: "to the borders of the four seas, everywhere are set the Five Chiefs (五長)"; tradition: "out to the sea, among every five feudal states one worthy is set up as regional earl, called the Five Chiefs"; *Shiwen*: "五長, the heads of the many offices." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Da zai*: "he applies the rules to the towns and lands, and sets up their heads (長)"; note: "長 means the dukes, ministers, and grandees, and the king's sons and brothers who hold fiefs."
- *To advance, grow (進).* Yijing, *Tai*: "the gentleman's Way grows (長), the petty man's Way wanes."
- *To grow, be born (生長).* Mengzi: "given its nurture, nothing fails to grow (長)."
- *To rear, foster.* Former Han, *Biography of Dong Zhongshu*: "the yang ever abides in high summer, and makes its work the begetting and rearing (養長) [of things]."
- *To be elder, to instruct.* Shijing, *Daya*: "able to discern, able to judge kinds, able to be elder (長), able to be lord"; gloss: "to teach untiringly is called 長."
- *An office-name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 11: "the Qin Shuzhang Bao and Shuzhang Wu led troops to attack Jin and relieve Zheng"; note: "庶長 (Shuzhang), a Qin rank."
- *A county-name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 18: "in summer the men of Jin seized the Wey envoy Shi Mai at Zhangzi (長子)"; note: "Zhangzi county, in Shangdang commandery."

**Departing tone (zhàng).** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 直亮, sounding like 仗 (zhàng).
- *To measure length.* *Ji Yun*: "to measure length is 長 (zhàng)."
- *Surplus, excess.* *Ji Yun*: "surplus (餘)." *Zheng Yun*: "much, superfluous, left over." Lunyu: "[its length] was his height and a half over (長)." Shishuo xinyu: "all his life he had no surplus things (長物)." Lu Ji, *Wen fu*: "so take nothing of the redundant and excessive (宂長)."
- *To wax, flourish.* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 知亮, sounding like 障 (zhàng) — "to increase and flourish." Han Yu's poem: "gaining its season, it just then waxes lush (長王)."

**Textual notes.** The "風雲長爲護儲胥" line (the Choubi Station poem) is by Li Shangyin, not Du Fu. The Zhouli source reads 大宰, not 大冢宰.
