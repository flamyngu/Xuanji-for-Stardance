**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.223 #10 — the entry is filed under the orthodox head 𡉈)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 昨宰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 盡亥 — the rising tone of 裁, i.e. **zài**.

**Senses.** Erya, *Shi xun*: "to dwell (居)." Yijing, *Qian zhuan*: "dwelling (在) in a low place yet not grieved."
- *To examine (察).* Shu, *Shun dian*: "he examined (在) the jade sighting-tube and gauge, to set in order the Seven Governors." Liji, *Wenwang shizi*: "before the meal he would always check (在) the measure of cold and warm."
- *To exist, to be living (存).* Lunyu: "while father and mother are alive (在)."
- *所在, where one is.* Former Han, *Annals of Wudi*: "summoned to the imperial sojourn (行在)." Cai Yong, *Du duan*: "the Son of Heaven takes the Four Seas as home; where he dwells is called 行在所."
- *Surname.* The Jin Grand Administrator of Runan, Zai Yu.
- *The spleen-spirit is called 常在* (see the *Huangting jing*).
- *fanqie 昨代, sounding like 載 (zài)* — same sense.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 此 (cǐ), fanqie 雌氏* — "good (善)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "belonging not to the hair, cleaving not to the lining; Heaven gave me birth — where is my [lucky] hour (在)?"
- *Rhymes as 示 (shì) rising, fanqie 才里.* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "I bade Fenglong ride the clouds, to seek out where Fufei dwells (在)…"

**On the graph.** From 土 and 才; originally written 𡉈, now 在. Zhengzitong: "在 is fundamentally fanqie 昨代; old editions, over-rigidly following the rhyme-books, first placed it in the rising tone of 才 glossed 'to dwell,' then shifted it to the departing tone glossed 'place' — not realising that 'place' just *is* 'dwell,' and that it is not the case that the rising tone means 'dwell' and the departing 'place.'" — A note: 在 has both rising and departing tones, as dictionaries and rhyme-books agree; the *Liyun* originally allowed cross-rhyming, so the *Zihui*'s split-glossing seems over-rigid, while the *Zhengzitong*'s refusal to allow the rising-to-departing shift is also wrong.
