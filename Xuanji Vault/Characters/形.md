**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҜ…, lower; Bristle radical еҪЎ; p.363 #06)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жҲёз¶“. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie д№Һз¶“. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еҘҡз¶“ вҖ” sounding like йӮў (xГӯng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the image of a shape (иұЎеҪў)." *Shiming*: "еҪў [denotes] the differences of shape and image." Yijing, *Qian*: "the kinds of things flow into shape (еҪў)." Xici: "on earth they take shape (еҪў)."
- *The look, appearance (е®№).* *Yupian.* Shu, *Yueming*: "he examined the image and had its likeness (еҪў) sought everywhere in the realm"; tradition: "examining the man he had dreamed of, he carved his likeness." Guliang zhuan, Huan yr 14: "looking afar, one marks the figure (иІҢ) but not the features (еҪў)"; note: "иІҢ is the bodily form, еҪў the features and colour."
- *The body (й«”).* *Yun Hui.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Wangsun*: "the bodily frame (еҪўйӘё) belongs to the earth."
- *To appear, show forth (зҸҫ).* *Zheng Yun.* The Daxue: "this is called: sincere within, it shows (еҪў) without."
- *For the bones to show.* Liji, *Quli*: "in the rites of mourning, one wastes but not so as to show the bones (дёҚеҪў)"; note: "еҪў means the bones showing."
- *The lie of the land (ең°еӢў).* Shiji, *Annals of Gaozu*: "Qin, a land of strong terrain (еҪўеӢқ)"; note: "having the advantage of the lie of the ground." Former Han, *Biography of Chao Cuo*: "...the first is to gain the terrain (ең°еҪў)."
- *A vessel.* Shiji, *Annals of Qin*: "he ate from an earthen bowl and supped from an earthen vessel (еңҹеҪў)"; note: Ru Chun: "еңҹеҪў is a kind of food-vessel, an earthenware ware." (A note: same as йү¶, a soup-vessel.)
