**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Bristle radical 彡; p.363 #06)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 戸經. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 乎經. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 奚經 — sounding like 邢 (xíng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the image of a shape (象形)." *Shiming*: "形 [denotes] the differences of shape and image." Yijing, *Qian*: "the kinds of things flow into shape (形)." Xici: "on earth they take shape (形)."
- *The look, appearance (容).* *Yupian.* Shu, *Yueming*: "he examined the image and had its likeness (形) sought everywhere in the realm"; tradition: "examining the man he had dreamed of, he carved his likeness." Guliang zhuan, Huan yr 14: "looking afar, one marks the figure (貌) but not the features (形)"; note: "貌 is the bodily form, 形 the features and colour."
- *The body (體).* *Yun Hui.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Wangsun*: "the bodily frame (形骸) belongs to the earth."
- *To appear, show forth (現).* *Zheng Yun.* The Daxue: "this is called: sincere within, it shows (形) without."
- *For the bones to show.* Liji, *Quli*: "in the rites of mourning, one wastes but not so as to show the bones (不形)"; note: "形 means the bones showing."
- *The lie of the land (地勢).* Shiji, *Annals of Gaozu*: "Qin, a land of strong terrain (形勝)"; note: "having the advantage of the lie of the ground." Former Han, *Biography of Chao Cuo*: "...the first is to gain the terrain (地形)."
- *A vessel.* Shiji, *Annals of Qin*: "he ate from an earthen bowl and supped from an earthen vessel (土形)"; note: Ru Chun: "土形 is a kind of food-vessel, an earthenware ware." (A note: same as 鉶, a soup-vessel.)
