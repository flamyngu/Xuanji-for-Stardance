**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1024 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於驚 — sounding like 瑛 (yīng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Erya, Shimu*: "what flowers but bears no fruit is called 英 (blossom)." Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "there is a girl who shares my carriage, her face like the hibiscus bloom (舜英)"; note: "英 is like 'flower.'"
- *A tree-name.* *Erya, Shimu*: "the *quan* is the yellow-blossom (黃英)."
- *A leaf.* Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "at dusk I eat the fallen blossoms (落英) of autumn chrysanthemum." *Xixi congyu*: "in the Songshu, *Treatise on Auspicious Omens*, Shen Yue says 英 means leaf; the Lisao's 'eat the fallen 英' means eating the leaves of autumn chrysanthemum. By the *Yuhan fang*, sweet chrysanthemum leaves picked on the first *yin* day of the third month are called 'jade blossom (玉英)' — so 英 is also called 'leaf.'"
- *A paragon, the flower of men.* Liji, *Liyun*: "when the Great Way prevailed, [I lived] with the flower (英) of the Three Ages"; note: "twice a select man is a paragon (俊); one in a thousand is a flower (英)." Mengzi: "to gain the finest talents (英才) of the world and teach them."
- *Fine, splendid (美).* *Boya.* Jinshu, *Biography of Xun Kai*: "a saying in the capital ran, 'in Luoyang, the brilliant (英英) Xun Daoming.'"
- *A two-tiered mountain.* *Erya, Shishan*: "a twofold [tier] is 英"; subcommentary: "a mountain of two tiers is named 英; the present Yingshan county in Nan commandery likely takes this name."
- *瓊英, a fair jade-like stone.* Shijing, *Qifeng*: "adorned moreover with fine jade-stone (瓊英)." *Shuijing zhu*: "Sun Sheng's *Wei chunqiu* says: Emperor Wen ever more enlarged the palaces, taking white quartz (白石英) and purple quartz (紫石英)."
- *英英, the look of clouds.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "billowing (英英) white clouds shed dew on those rushes and reeds."
- *A spear-plume.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the two spears with doubled plumes (重英)"; note: "decking the spears with feathers." *Lusong*: "red tassels (朱英) and green bindings"; subcommentary: "silk wound about and dyed red."
- *A music-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "Emperor Ku made the *Wuying* (Five Blossoms); 英 means flowering abundance."
- *A state-name.* Shiji, *House of Chen and Qi*: "the descendants of Gao Yao — some were enfeoffed at Ying (英) and Liu"; note to the *House of Chu*: "the state of Ying lay south of the Huai; it was likely the state of Liao."
- *A surname.* *Tongzhi, Monograph on Clans*: "the Ying clan took the state as surname; the Han had Ying Bu, King of Jiujiang."
- *Fanqie 於虔, sounding like 閼 (yān).* Ban Gu, *Xijing fu*: "kingfisher-jade and fire-crystal, streaming radiance holding the gleam (英); the Xuanli and Chuiji [gems] — the night-shining pearl is there."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Read 央 (yāng), fanqie 于良 (Tang Yun guyin).* Qu Yuan, *Jiuge*: "I bathe in orchid water and wash in fragrance, my flowered robe bright as a blossom (英)." Mao Shi, *Benyin*: "舜英, 重英, 瓊英, 美如英 — all rhyme, read 央." Guanzi, *Diyuan*: "the five colours blend their blooms (英), each with its own pattern." *Ji Yun*: "英, rice newly sprung but not yet transplanted, also read 央."
- *Fanqie 於慶, sounding like 映 (yìng).* "To ornament.
