**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls з”і, upper; Grass radical иүё; p.1024 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie ж–јй©ҡ вҖ” sounding like з‘ӣ (yД«ng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Erya, Shimu*: "what flowers but bears no fruit is called иӢұ (blossom)." Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "there is a girl who shares my carriage, her face like the hibiscus bloom (иҲңиӢұ)"; note: "иӢұ is like 'flower.'"
- *A tree-name.* *Erya, Shimu*: "the *quan* is the yellow-blossom (й»ғиӢұ)."
- *A leaf.* Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "at dusk I eat the fallen blossoms (иҗҪиӢұ) of autumn chrysanthemum." *Xixi congyu*: "in the Songshu, *Treatise on Auspicious Omens*, Shen Yue says иӢұ means leaf; the Lisao's 'eat the fallen иӢұ' means eating the leaves of autumn chrysanthemum. By the *Yuhan fang*, sweet chrysanthemum leaves picked on the first *yin* day of the third month are called 'jade blossom (зҺүиӢұ)' вҖ” so иӢұ is also called 'leaf.'"
- *A paragon, the flower of men.* Liji, *Liyun*: "when the Great Way prevailed, [I lived] with the flower (иӢұ) of the Three Ages"; note: "twice a select man is a paragon (дҝҠ); one in a thousand is a flower (иӢұ)." Mengzi: "to gain the finest talents (иӢұжүҚ) of the world and teach them."
- *Fine, splendid (зҫҺ).* *Boya.* Jinshu, *Biography of Xun Kai*: "a saying in the capital ran, 'in Luoyang, the brilliant (иӢұиӢұ) Xun Daoming.'"
- *A two-tiered mountain.* *Erya, Shishan*: "a twofold [tier] is иӢұ"; subcommentary: "a mountain of two tiers is named иӢұ; the present Yingshan county in Nan commandery likely takes this name."
- *з“ҠиӢұ, a fair jade-like stone.* Shijing, *Qifeng*: "adorned moreover with fine jade-stone (з“ҠиӢұ)." *Shuijing zhu*: "Sun Sheng's *Wei chunqiu* says: Emperor Wen ever more enlarged the palaces, taking white quartz (зҷҪзҹіиӢұ) and purple quartz (зҙ«зҹіиӢұ)."
- *иӢұиӢұ, the look of clouds.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "billowing (иӢұиӢұ) white clouds shed dew on those rushes and reeds."
- *A spear-plume.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the two spears with doubled plumes (йҮҚиӢұ)"; note: "decking the spears with feathers." *Lusong*: "red tassels (жңұиӢұ) and green bindings"; subcommentary: "silk wound about and dyed red."
- *A music-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "Emperor Ku made the *Wuying* (Five Blossoms); иӢұ means flowering abundance."
- *A state-name.* Shiji, *House of Chen and Qi*: "the descendants of Gao Yao вҖ” some were enfeoffed at Ying (иӢұ) and Liu"; note to the *House of Chu*: "the state of Ying lay south of the Huai; it was likely the state of Liao."
- *A surname.* *Tongzhi, Monograph on Clans*: "the Ying clan took the state as surname; the Han had Ying Bu, King of Jiujiang."
- *Fanqie ж–јиҷ”, sounding like й–ј (yДҒn).* Ban Gu, *Xijing fu*: "kingfisher-jade and fire-crystal, streaming radiance holding the gleam (иӢұ); the Xuanli and Chuiji [gems] вҖ” the night-shining pearl is there."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Read еӨ® (yДҒng), fanqie дәҺиүҜ (Tang Yun guyin).* Qu Yuan, *Jiuge*: "I bathe in orchid water and wash in fragrance, my flowered robe bright as a blossom (иӢұ)." Mao Shi, *Benyin*: "иҲңиӢұ, йҮҚиӢұ, з“ҠиӢұ, зҫҺеҰӮиӢұ вҖ” all rhyme, read еӨ®." Guanzi, *Diyuan*: "the five colours blend their blooms (иӢұ), each with its own pattern." *Ji Yun*: "иӢұ, rice newly sprung but not yet transplanted, also read еӨ®."
- *Fanqie ж–јж…¶, sounding like жҳ  (yГ¬ng).* "To ornament.
