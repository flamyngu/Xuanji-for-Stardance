**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҜ, lower; Rap radical ж”ҙ; p.472 #34)*

**Ancient graph.** жһҡ.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иҳҮж—ұ. *Ji Yun*: fanqie йЎҷж—ұ вҖ” sounding like еӮҳ (sЗҺn).

**Etymology and senses.** гӘ” is used interchangeably with ж•Ј. Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "the wind to scatter (ж•Ј) them." Liji, *Qu li*: "to amass, yet be able to disperse (ж•Ј)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Gongyang zhuan, Zhuang yr 12: "he let them loose (ж•ЈиҲҚ) within the palace"; note: "ж•Ј means to set free (ж”ҫ)."
- *Also* *Boya*: "to spread (еёғ)." *Guangyun*: "ж•Ј means unbound (иӘ•)." *Zengyun*: also е®Ӯж•Ј (idle and loose), й–’ж•Ј (at leisure). *Yun Hui*: "not keeping oneself in check is ж•Ј." Zhuangzi, *Yangsheng zhu*: "what should an idle man (ж•Јдәә) know of a useless tree (ж•ЈжңЁ)?"; note: "not within the count of the usable."
- *Surname.* Shu, *Jun shi*: "there was one like San Yisheng (ж•Је®ңз”ҹ)"; tradition: "the San (ж•Ј) clan; Yisheng was his name."
- *A wine-vessel name.* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Chang ren*: "for all split-victim rites, the san (ж•Ј) is used"; note: "a lacquered vessel; one without ornament is called ж•Ј." Yili, *Yan li*: "they pour the san (ж•Ј) at the top of the western steps"; note: "to pour the san is to pour the square-jar wine." Liji, *Li qi*: "the lowly present with the san (ж•Ј)"; note: "a five-sheng (еҚҮ) vessel is called ж•Ј." A note: the *Zhouli*, *Yili*, *Liji*, and *Shiwen* all sound it fanqie зҙ ж—ұ; the *Yun Hui* alone draws this passage into the departing tone, which is wrong, for the rising and departing tones may be read interchangeably.
- *Also* powdered medicinal mineral is called ж•Ј (a powder). Later Han, *Biography of Hua Tuo*: "the lacquer-leaf and green-nian powder (жјҶи‘үйқ‘дҙҙж•Ј)."
- *A zither-tune name.* Jinshu, *Biography of Ji Kang*: "there was the Guangling san (е»Јйҷөж•Ј)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie иҳҮж—°. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie е…Ҳж—° вҖ” sounding like йҸҫ (sГ n). The sense the same.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie зӣёе№І вҖ” same as и·ҡ (to limp, shДҒn). Shiji, *Biography of the Lord of Pingyuan*: "limping along (ж§ғж•Ј) to draw and carry water"; note: "the *Suoyin* says: fanqie е…ҲеҜ’, also written зҸҠ; the sound is the same."
