## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈寅集上〉【宀字部】頁287第03    〔古文〕：𠕺【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤餘封切，音融。【說文】盛也。从宀从谷。【徐鉉曰】屋與谷，皆所以盛受也。【增韻】受也，包函也。【易·師卦】君子以容民畜衆。【書·君𨻰】必有忍其乃有濟，有容德乃大。【註】謂包之也。又【唐書·狄仁傑傳】婁公盛德，爲所包容久矣。　又儀容也。【禮·冠義】禮義之始，在於正容體。【射義】試之於射宮，其容比於禮，其節比於樂。又【玉藻】君子之容舒遲，見所尊者齊遫，足容重，手容恭，目容端，口容止，聲容靜，頭容直，氣容肅，立容德，色容莊。　又從容，安也。【書·君𨻰】從容以和。【中庸】從容中道，聖人也。　又【爾雅·釋器】容謂之防。【郭璞註】形如今牀頭小曲屛風，唱射者所以自防隱也，所以容身防矢也。　又禮官曰容。【禮·樂記】使之行商容，而復其位。【註】容謂禮樂之官。使之檢視殷家禮樂之官，而復其位。【前漢·儒林傳】徐生善爲容。是善禮樂者謂之容也。　又飛揚貌。【楚辭·九章】紛容容之無經兮。　又【禮·內則】佩容臭。【註】香物也。助爲形容之飾，猶後世香囊也。　又紗之輕者曰輕容。【唐苑類】輕容，無花薄紗也。　又州名。古象郡地，唐置容州。　又姓。八凱仲容之後。【禮記】有徐大夫容居。又慕容，複姓。　又【集韻】尹竦切。與慫慂之慂同，勸也。　又【正字通】余壟切，音涌。【前漢·郊祀歌】神之行，旌容容。　又與頌通。【說文】貌也。从頁，公聲。【徐曰】此儀容字。歌頌者，美盛德之形容，故通作頌。後人因以爲歌頌字。【前漢·惠帝紀】有罪當盜械者，皆頌繫。【顏師古註】古頌與容同。【𠛬法志註】頌謂寬容之，不桎梏也。　又叶與章切，音陽。【韓愈·獨孤申叔哀辭】如聞其聲，如見其容。嗚呼遠矣，何日而忘。　又【參同契】天道甚浩廣，太𤣥無形容。虛寂不可覩，匡郭以消忘。    考證：〔【禮·冠義】禮文之始，在於正容體。〕　謹照原文禮文改禮義。〔【射義】試之於澤宮。〕　謹照原文澤宮改射宮。〔【禮記】有周大夫容居。〕　謹照檀弓周改徐。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.287 #03)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠕺.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 餘封, sounding like 融 (róng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To be full, to hold (盛)." It follows 宀 (roof) and 谷 (valley). **Xu Xuan**: "house and valley both serve to hold and receive." *Zengyun*: "to receive (受); to enfold and contain (包函)."

**Senses.**
- *To contain, tolerate.* Yijing, *Shi*: "the gentleman contains (容) the people and nurtures the multitude." Shu, *Jun chen*: "one must have forbearance, then there is success; have capacity (容) for virtue, then it is great"; note: "to enfold." Tang shu, *Biography of Di Renjie*: "Lord Lou's ample virtue — long have I been embraced (包容) by it."
- *Bearing, demeanour (儀容).* Liji, *Guan yi*: "the start of rite and right lies in correcting one's bearing (容體)." *She yi*: "tested in the archery hall, his demeanour matches the rites, his rhythm the music." *Yu zao*: "the gentleman's bearing is unhurried… the feet weighty, the hands reverent, the eyes upright, the mouth still, the voice quiet, the head straight, the breath solemn, the stance virtuous, the expression grave."
- *從容, calm, at ease (安).* Shu, *Jun chen*: "calm and harmonious (從容以和)." Zhongyong: "to hit the Way with ease (從容中道) — that is the sage."
- *A screen (防).* Erya, *Shi qi*: "容 is called 防"; Guo Pu: "shaped like today's small curved bedside screen; what the archery-caller uses to screen himself, to shelter the body and ward off arrows."
- *A ritual officer.* Liji, *Yue ji*: "send him to act as the *shang-rong* and restore his post"; note: "容 means the officer of rites and music." Former Han, *Forest of Scholars*: "Mr. Xu was skilled at 容" — one expert in rites and music is called 容.
- *A soaring, fluttering look.* Chuci, *Jiu zhang*: "tossing and swirling (容容) without bound."
- *容臭, a scent-ornament.* Liji, *Nei ze*: note, "a fragrant thing, worn to adorn the appearance — like the scent-bag of later ages."
- *輕容, a flowerless light gauze.* *Tang yuan lei*.
- *A prefecture.* The old Xiang commandery land; the Tang set up Rongzhou.
- *Surname.* Descendants of Zhongrong, one of the Eight Worthies. Liji: the Xu grandee Rong Ju. Also 慕容 (Murong), a compound surname.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 尹竦 — same as the 慂 of 慫慂, "to urge (勸)."
- *Zhengzitong*: fanqie 余壟, sounding like 涌 (yǒng). Former Han, *Jiao si ge*: "the spirit moves, its banners streaming (容容)."
- *Interchangeable with 頌.* Shuowen: "appearance (貌)"; follows 頁 with 公 phonetic; Xu [Kai]: "this is the 'demeanour' graph; eulogists praise the form (形容) of ample virtue, hence it is borrowed for 頌, and later used as the 'eulogy' graph." Former Han, *Annals of Huidi*: "the guilty due for fettering were all 頌-bound (held leniently)"; Yan Shigu: "anciently 頌 was the same as 容"; note to the *Treatise on Punishments*: "頌 means to treat them leniently (容), not to shackle."
- *Rhymes as 陽 (yáng), fanqie 與章.* Han Yu, *Lament for Dugu Shenshu*: "as if I heard his voice, as if I saw his face (容); alas, how far — and what day shall I forget?"
- *Cantong qi*: "the Way of Heaven is vast indeed; the Great Mystery has no form or face (容)…"
