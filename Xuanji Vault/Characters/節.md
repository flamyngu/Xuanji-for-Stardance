## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈未集上〉【竹字部】頁891第24    〔古文〕：卩【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤子結切，音接。【說文】竹節也。又操也。【左傳·成十五年】諸侯將見子臧于王而立之，子臧辭曰：前志有之曰：聖達節，次守節，下失節。爲君非吾節也。　又止也，檢也，制也。【易·頤象】君子以愼言語，節飮食。【疏】節，裁節。又【節卦】節亨。苦節不可貞。【疏】節者，制度之名，節止之義，制事有節，其道乃亨。又【說卦傳】節，止也。【書·康誥】節性惟日其邁。【禮·檀弓】品節斯。【註】制斷也。【爾雅·釋樂】和樂謂之節。【疏】八音克諧，無相奪倫，謂之和樂，樂和則應節。【左傳·莊二十三年】公如齊觀社，曹劌諫曰：會以訓上下之則，制財用之節。【史記·樂書】大禮與天地同節。【正義曰】天有日月，地有山川，高𤰞如形，生用各別。大禮辯尊𤰞貴賤等差異別，是大禮與天地同節。　又符節，所以示信。【書·康誥】惟厥正人越小臣諸節。【註】諸有符節之臣，若爲官行文書而有符，今之印者也。【周禮·地官】掌節，掌守邦節而辨其用，以輔王命。凡通達于天下者，必有節以傳輔之。無節者，有幾則不達。【註】以王命往來，必有節以爲信。又【秋官】小行人掌達天下之六節：山國用虎節，土國用人節，澤國用龍節，皆以金爲之。道路用旌節，門關用符節，都鄙用管節，皆以竹爲之。【禮·玉藻】凡君召以三節，二節以走，一節以趨。【註】隨事緩急，急則二節，故走。緩則一節，故趨也。【左傳·文十二年】秦西乞術曰：不腆先君之敝器，使下臣致諸執事，以爲瑞節。【註】節，信也。【史記·秦始皇紀】衣服、旄旌、節旗，皆上黑。【正義曰】旄節者，編旄爲之，以象竹節。　又時節。【左傳疏】凡春、秋分，冬、夏至，立春、立夏爲啟。立秋、立冬爲閉。用此八節之日登觀臺，書其所見雲物氣色。　又人君壽日皆名節，有萬壽節、天中節等名。　又地名。【左思·魏都賦】掘鯉之淀，蓋節之淵。【註】蓋節淵在平原鬲縣北。　又貫節，藥草名。　又樂器，卽拊也，所以節樂。　又諡法，好廉自克曰節。　又漢宣帝改元地節。【應劭曰】欲令地得其節。又姓。明正德中，守禦指揮僉事節鐸。　又官名。【眞𦡳風土記】村中人家稍密，有鎭守之官，名曰買節。　又【集韻】昨結切，音截。【詩·小雅】節彼南山。　又叶子悉切。【陸機·答賈長淵詩】大辰匿暉，金虎習質。雄臣馳騖，義夫赴節。　又叶子計切。【曹植·王霸贊】壯氣凌雲，挺身奮節。所征必拔，謀顯垂惠。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, upper; Bamboo radical 竹; p.891 #24)*

**Ancient graph.** 卩.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子結 — sounding like 接 (jiē).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a bamboo joint (竹節)." Also "moral integrity (操)." Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 15: "the lords were about to present Zizang to the king and set him up, but Zizang declined, saying, 'an old record has it: the sage rises above the bounds (達節), the next keeps the bounds (守節), the lowest loses the bounds (失節). To be ruler is not within my bounds (節).'"
- *To stop; to restrain; to regulate.* Yijing, *Yi* image: "the gentleman accordingly is careful in speech and temperate (節) in eating and drinking"; subcommentary: "節 is to moderate." Also the *Jie* (Limitation) hexagram: "Jie prospers; bitter limitation (苦節) cannot be persevered in"; subcommentary: "節 is a name for measure and rule, the sense of limiting and stopping; when affairs are ordered with measure, the Way then prospers." The Shuogua zhuan: "節 is 'to stop.'" Shu, *Kanggao*: "to curb (節) their nature, that day by day it may advance." Liji, *Tangong*: "graded and regulated (品節) thus"; note: "to determine and cut off." Erya, *Shiyue*: "harmonious music is called 節"; subcommentary: "when the eight timbres accord and none usurps another's order, it is called harmonious music; music in harmony answers to measure (節)." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 23: "assemblies are to instruct in the norms of high and low and to fix the measure (節) of wealth and use." Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "the Great Rites share measure (節) with Heaven and Earth"; Correct Meaning: "Heaven has sun and moon, Earth has hills and streams, high and low have their forms, their natures and uses distinct; the Great Rites distinguish honoured and humble, noble and base, by grades and differences — this is sharing measure with Heaven and Earth."
- *A tally (符節), used to show good faith.* Shu, *Kanggao*: "his head men and lesser officers, the tally-holders (諸節)"; note: "the ministers who bear tallies — like an official who carries a tally in dispatching documents, the seal of today." Zhouli, *Diguan*: "the Keeper of Tallies keeps the state's tallies and distinguishes their uses, to support the king's commands; whoever would pass through the realm must have a tally to convey and warrant him; without a tally, where there is a checkpoint he does not pass"; note: "going to and fro on the king's command, one must have a tally for good faith." Also the Qiuguan: "the Junior Envoy keeps the realm's six tallies — hill-states use the tiger-tally (虎節), plain-states the man-tally (人節), marsh-states the dragon-tally (龍節), all of metal; roads use the banner-tally (旌節), gates and passes the tally-token (符節), towns and lands the tube-tally (管節), all of bamboo." Liji, *Yuzao*: "the ruler summons with three [degrees of] tally: at two tallies one runs, at one tally one hastens"; note: "according to the urgency — urgent, two tallies, so one runs; less urgent, one tally, so one hastens." Zuozhuan, Wen yr 12: "the Qin envoy Xiqi Shu said, 'a worn vessel of my late lord's, which he sends his servant to present to your officers as a token of faith (瑞節)'"; note: "節 means good faith." Shiji, *Annals of Qin Shihuang*: "robes, yak-tail banners, and tally-flags (節旗) all favoured black"; Correct Meaning: "the yak-tail tally (旄節) is plaited of yak-tails, in the likeness of bamboo joints."
- *A season (時節).* Zuozhuan subcommentary: "the spring and autumn equinoxes, the winter and summer solstices, and the beginnings of spring and summer are 'openings'; the beginnings of autumn and winter are 'closings.' On the days of these eight nodes (八節) one ascends the watchtower and records the clouds, things, and colours of the air one sees."
- *A ruler's birthday festival.* Such names as the Myriad-Longevity Festival (萬壽節) and the Tianzhong Festival (天中節).
- *A place-name.* Zuo Si, *Weidu fu*: "the Juli marsh, the Gaijie pool (蓋節之淵)"; note: "the Gaijie pool is north of Ge county in Pingyuan."
- *貫節, a medicinal-herb name.*
- *A musical instrument — the* fu, *used to beat the measure (節) of the music.*
- *A posthumous title.* "One who loves integrity and masters himself" is titled 節 (the Steadfast).
- *A reign-title.* The Han Emperor Xuan changed the reign to Dijie (地節); Ying Shao says, "wishing the earth to attain its measure (節)."
- *A surname.* In the Ming Zhengde era there was Jie Duo, Assistant Commander of the Guard.
- *An office-name.* Zhenla fengtu ji: "where village households are somewhat dense, there is a garrison officer called the *maijie* (買節)."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 昨結, sounding like 截 (jié). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "lofty (節) is that southern mountain."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 子悉.* Lu Ji, *Reply to Jia Changyuan*: "...bold ministers gallop forth, righteous men rush to their duty (節)."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 子計.* Cao Zhi, *Encomium on Wang Ba*: "his stout spirit topped the clouds; he braced himself and roused his integrity (節).
