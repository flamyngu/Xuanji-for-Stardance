**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; See radical 見; p.1136 #12)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢈥, 寴, 𡩁.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 七人. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 雌人 — the level tone of 七, i.e. **qīn**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Guangyun*: "to love." Preface to the Xiaojing: "love and praise grew daily clearer"; note: "a heart of tender love is 親." Xunzi, *Bugou*: "intimate (親) in friendship yet not partial"; note: "親 means kindness and grace." The Zhouyu: "kind and gracious in protecting the people — that is 親."
- *Near (近).* Yijing, *Qian*: "what is rooted in Heaven draws near (親) the high, what is rooted in Earth draws near the low."
- *In person, oneself (躬).* *Zengyun.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "neither in person nor in his own hand (親)"; gloss: "he does not do it himself." Liji, *Wen wang shizi*: "the heir himself (親) fasts in dark robes and tends his parent"; note: "親 is like 'oneself.'"
- *Kin (姻, 屬).* *Shiming*: "親 is 襯 (to be next to): to be close against one another." Liji, *Dazhuan*: "kin (親) are connections (屬)"; subcommentary: "those who are kin each wear mourning for one another by their connection." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 14: "reward merit and unite the kin (親)"; Du's note: "親, the nine degrees of kindred." Zhouli, *Diguan, Da situ*: "by the marriage rites (隂禮) he teaches kinship (親), and the people do not resent"; note: "marriage in due season, so men are not without wives nor women resentful."
- *The Six Kin (六親).* Father, mother, brothers, wife, children. Guanzi, *Mumin*: "when the ruler keeps to measure, the Six Kin are secure." Note to Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: Ru Chun: "father, son, father's-brother's sons, grandfather's-brother's sons, great-grandfather's-brother's sons, and clan-cousins are the Six Kin."
- *A surname.* Shiji, *Biography of Lord Mengchang*: "the King of Qi drove out Zhou Zui and heeded Qin Fu (親弗)"; note: "a person's surname and name"; the Zhanguo ce writes 祝弗.
- *Interchangeable with 新.* The Daxue: "[the Way lies] in renewing (親) the people"; Cheng's note: "親 should be 新."
- *Departing tone (qìn).* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 七遴; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 七刃; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 寸遴. Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "commoners, artisans, and merchants each have their degrees of kinship (分親)"; note: "distinguished by nearness and distance of kin"; *Shiwen*: "it has both level and departing sounds." *Guangyun*: 親家 (in-laws); *Ji Yun*: "marriage-connections address each other as 親."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 蒼先, sounding like 千 (qiān).* Yang Fang, *Hehuan poem*: "...gong and shang tones in concord, hearts as one, naturally drawn close (親)." (*Zihui*: "anciently from 𣐽; now contracted to 亲.")

**Textual note.** "勿躬勿親" should read "弗躬弗親".
