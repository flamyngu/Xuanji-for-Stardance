## Shuowen Jiezi (說文解字) — original
> 《舁部》興：起也。从舁从同。同力也。

## Guangyun (廣韻) — original
> 《廣韻·下平聲·蒸·興》興：盛也，舉也，善也。《說文》曰：起也，从舁从同同力也，亦州名戰國時爲白馬玄之地漢置武都郡魏立東益州梁爲興州因武興山而名。虛陵切，又許應切，三。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Mortar radical 臼; p.1005 #05)*

**Pronunciation (level tone, *xīng*).** *Tang Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 虛陵, sounding like 𨞾.

**Senses.** Erya, *Shi yan*: "興, to rise (起)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "rise early, sleep late (夙興夜寐)." Liji, *Zhongyong*: "when the state has the Way, his words suffice to raise him up (興)"; note: "興 means to rise into office."
- *Flourishing (盛).* *Guangyun.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "Heaven secures you, so that nothing does not thrive (興)"; *Jian*: "興, flourishing."
- *To raise, lift up (舉).* *Wuyin jiyun.* Zhouli, *Da sima*: "advance the worthy and raise (興) the meritorious, to build the state"; note: "興 is like 舉."
- *To move (動).* Zhouli, *Kaogong ji*, bow-maker; note: "興 is like 動."
- *To esteem, exalt (尊尚).* Shijing, *Daya*: "興迷亂于政"; *Jian*: "興 is like 尊尚."
- *A levy.* Zhouli, *Lü shi*: "頒其興積"; note: "what the county officials levy and gather is called 興 — today's 'military levy (軍興)' is this." Also *Si jia*: "平其興"; note: "what is levied as tax."
- *A prefecture.* The Han set up Wudu commandery; the Wei established eastern and western prefectures; the Liang made it Xingzhou, named for Mount Wuxing.
- *A county.* Li Yong, *Crossing the Lake*: "I passed round the bounds of Yixing (義興)."
- *A palace.* Zhang Heng, *Western Capital Rhapsody*: "Longxing, Hanzhang"; note: "Longxing, a palace name."
- *Surname.* *Xingpu*: under the Han, the usher Xing Qu of the Prince of Jiyin.

**Departing tone (*xìng*).** *Guangyun, Ji Yun, Yun Hui, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許應, sounding like 嬹. *Ji Yun*: "image, likeness (象)."
- *比興 (the poetic 'stimulus').* *Zengyun*: "興 means the drift of meaning." Zhouli, *Da shi*: "taught the six modes of poetry — feng, fu, bi, xing, ya, song." *Shi gu*: "興 (stimulus) is to be moved by a thing and so give forth, as in 'the oriole in flight, gleaming its wings' or 'long ago I went, the willows lush.'"
- *To delight (悅).* Liji, *Xue ji*: "not delighting (興) in the arts, one cannot enjoy learning"; note: "興 means joy." Yin Zhongwen's poem: "…it can make high delight (高興) run its course."
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許刃, sounding like 釁 (xìn). Liji, *Wenwang shizi*: "having consecrated (興) the vessels, they used the silk"; *Shiwen*: "sound same as 釁."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 凶 (xiōng), fanqie 火宮.* Ma Rong, *Long-Flute Rhapsody*: "the tune ends, all spent, yet the remaining strings rise again (興)…"
- *Rhymes as 香 (xiāng), fanqie 虛良.* Xu Gan, *Miscellaneous Poems*: "deep shadow swells my sorrow — for whom does the sorrow rise (興)?" Pan Qian stele: "…there shall a Han general arise (興)."
- *Rhymes as 欽 (qīn), fanqie 丘侵.* Shijing, *Daya*: "the hosts of Yin-Shang, their gathering like a forest; sworn at Muye, it is I who shall arise (興)."

**Textual note.** 掌均 should read 司稼 (Zhouli, *Diguan*); there is no office named 掌均.
