**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls й…ү, middle; Foot radical и¶і; p.1225 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жҙӣж•…. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йӯҜж•… вҖ” sounding like иіӮ (lГ№).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a road, way (йҒ“)." Note: Xu Xuan says, "a road is where each man goes his own way." *Shiming*: "и·Ҝ is йңІ (exposed): it is what men tread, lying open to view." Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Hefang shi*: "he is in charge of opening the roads of the world." Erya, *Shigong*: "и·Ҝ is the travelling-road (ж—…йҖ”)"; note: "йҖ” is just йҒ“." Also: "a single thoroughfare is called йҒ“и·Ҝ"; note: "a long road."
- *Great (еӨ§).* Erya, *Shigu.* Shijing, *Daya*: "their fame filled the great way (ијүи·Ҝ)"; gloss: "by that time the fame was already great."
- *A carriage.* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "how different from the ducal carriage (е…¬и·Ҝ)"; tradition: "и·Ҝ means carriage." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Jinche*: "the king's five carriages (дә”и·Ҝ)"; note: "where the king rides is called и·Ҝ." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "the Great Carriage with rush-mats (еӨ§и·Ҝ)"; note: "the Great Carriage is the Jade Carriage, used to sacrifice to Heaven"; subcommentary: "и·Ҝ is glossed 'great'; where the ruler is, all takes 'great' as its name вҖ” the gate is the Lu Gate (и·Ҝй–Җ), the chamber the Lu Chamber (и·ҜеҜў), the carriage the Lu Carriage (и·Ҝи»Ҡ)."
- *A drum.* Zhouli, *Diguan, Guren*: "with the lu-drum (и·Ҝйј“) he drums for the offerings to the ghosts"; note: "the lu-drum is a four-faced drum."
- *A bow.* Shiji, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "a lu-bow (и·Ҝеј“) and a set of arrows."
- *The look of a ruined place.* Xunzi, *Fuguo*: "the fields run to weeds, the towns lie ruined (и·Ҝ)"; note: "и·Ҝ means without walls or ramparts."
- *Same as иј… (a carriage).* Liji, *Yueling*: "he rides the phoenix-carriage (йёһи·Ҝ)"; *Shiwen*: "и·Ҝ is in some texts written иј…."
- *A surname.* Note to the Jijiu pian: "Lu is a river-name; it was also made a county, in Zhuo commandery, and dwellers there took it as surname; the Han had the grandee Lu Wenshu."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie жӯ·еҗ„, sounding like жҙӣ (luГІ). Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "then he fenced (иҷҺи·Ҝ) the three peaks of Zong to make a hunting-park"; note: Jin Zhuo says, "и·Ҝ sounds иҗҪ"; Fu Qian says, "to pen this mountain with a bamboo tiger-fence (иҷҺиҗҪ)"; Yan Shigu says, "иҗҪ means to bind вҖ” to encircle it all round with rope.
