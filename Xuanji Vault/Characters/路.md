**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, middle; Foot radical 足; p.1225 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 洛故. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 魯故 — sounding like 賂 (lù).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a road, way (道)." Note: Xu Xuan says, "a road is where each man goes his own way." *Shiming*: "路 is 露 (exposed): it is what men tread, lying open to view." Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Hefang shi*: "he is in charge of opening the roads of the world." Erya, *Shigong*: "路 is the travelling-road (旅途)"; note: "途 is just 道." Also: "a single thoroughfare is called 道路"; note: "a long road."
- *Great (大).* Erya, *Shigu.* Shijing, *Daya*: "their fame filled the great way (載路)"; gloss: "by that time the fame was already great."
- *A carriage.* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "how different from the ducal carriage (公路)"; tradition: "路 means carriage." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Jinche*: "the king's five carriages (五路)"; note: "where the king rides is called 路." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "the Great Carriage with rush-mats (大路)"; note: "the Great Carriage is the Jade Carriage, used to sacrifice to Heaven"; subcommentary: "路 is glossed 'great'; where the ruler is, all takes 'great' as its name — the gate is the Lu Gate (路門), the chamber the Lu Chamber (路寢), the carriage the Lu Carriage (路車)."
- *A drum.* Zhouli, *Diguan, Guren*: "with the lu-drum (路鼓) he drums for the offerings to the ghosts"; note: "the lu-drum is a four-faced drum."
- *A bow.* Shiji, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "a lu-bow (路弓) and a set of arrows."
- *The look of a ruined place.* Xunzi, *Fuguo*: "the fields run to weeds, the towns lie ruined (路)"; note: "路 means without walls or ramparts."
- *Same as 輅 (a carriage).* Liji, *Yueling*: "he rides the phoenix-carriage (鸞路)"; *Shiwen*: "路 is in some texts written 輅."
- *A surname.* Note to the Jijiu pian: "Lu is a river-name; it was also made a county, in Zhuo commandery, and dwellers there took it as surname; the Han had the grandee Lu Wenshu."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 歷各, sounding like 洛 (luò). Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "then he fenced (虎路) the three peaks of Zong to make a hunting-park"; note: Jin Zhuo says, "路 sounds 落"; Fu Qian says, "to pen this mountain with a bamboo tiger-fence (虎落)"; Yan Shigu says, "落 means to bind — to encircle it all round with rope.
