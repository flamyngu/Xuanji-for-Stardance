**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.397 #25)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 疾之. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 牆之. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 才資 — sounding like 磁 (cí).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To love (愛)." It follows 心 (heart) with 兹 as phonetic. *Jingyun*: "the love of parents for their child." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 26: "the father loving (慈) and the son filial, the mother-in-law kind (慈) and the wife heeding."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Yili, *Sang fu*: "the foster-mother (慈母) is as a mother" — it means the nursing mother (養母).
- *Also* Liji, *Nei ze*: "tenderly (慈) with sweet delicacies"; note: "慈 means to offer them with love and respect."
- *Kindly and harmonious, winning over things (慈和, 服物).* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 18: "diffusively kind and gracious (宣慈惠和)"; note: "the kind (慈) one — love issues from the heart, and grace covers things."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "gentle (柔); good (善); benevolent (仁)."
- *A stone-name.* *Jingyun*: "where a mountain bears iron on its south, the loadstone (慈石) is born on its north." Guo Pu, *Eulogy on the loadstone*: "the loadstone (慈石) draws iron, as mother and child long for each other." Commonly written 磁, which is wrong.
- *A bamboo-name.* *Zhu pu*: "bamboo that grows in clumps, child and mother leaning together, is called loving-bamboo (慈竹)."
- *A fruit-name.* *Qunfang pu*: "the arrowhead (慈姑) — one root yearly bears twelve children, and in an intercalary year thirteen, like a loving mother (慈母) suckling her children."
- *A prefecture-name.* The Tang set up Cizhou (慈州).
- *Surname.* The Han had Ci Ren; the Ming, Ci Zhi.

**On the graph.** It follows 心 (heart) and 兹. Wei Jiao says: "兹 is to rear one's child and make it grow, with the heart set on loving it." Commonly miswritten 㤵.
