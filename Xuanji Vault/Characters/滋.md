## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.642 #15)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣳯, 𦱳.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 子之. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 津之. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 津私 — sounding like 兹 (zī).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** A water-name. *Shuowen*: "the water issues from the White-xing valley of Mount Niuyin (牛飮山白陘谷)."

**Senses.**
- *Another name for the Ba river.* Shuijing zhu: "the Ba water of Baling county was anciently called the Zi river (滋水)."
- *To plant; to grow; to increase (蒔, 長, 益).* Shu, *Tai shi*: "in planting virtue, strive to increase it (滋)."
- *Sap, moisture (液).* Liji, *Tan gong*: "there must be the sap (滋) of grass and trees."
- *Many; teeming (多, 蕃).* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 15: "things are born and then have form, have form and then multiply (滋)."
- *Flavour (滋味).* Liji, *Yue ling*: "make the flavours (滋味) light, do not aim at the blending."
- *Turbid (濁).* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 8: "the men of Wucheng seized the men of Zeng who were soaking sedge, saying: why do you foul (滋) our water?"
- *Interchangeable with 孳 and 孜.*
- *Used for 兹.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "the levies and taxes were thereby (兹) the heavier."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 疾之, sounding like 慈 (cí). A water-name, issuing from Mount Gaoli (高麗山). Shanhai jing: "Mount Gaoshi — the Zi water (滋水) issues there."
