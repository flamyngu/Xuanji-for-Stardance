**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Woman radical 女; p.258 #16)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠃭, 乨, 兘, 𠙉.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 詩止. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 首止 — sounding like 𦰯 (shǐ).

**Etymology and senses.** "The beginning (初)." Yijing, *Qian*: "great indeed is the Originating of Qian; the myriad things take from it their beginning (始)." Mao Shi preface: "these are the Four Beginnings (四始)"; note: "the Feng, the two Ya, and the Song." Former Han, *Biography of Bao Xuan*: "an eclipse at the three beginnings (三始)"; note: "New Year's day is the morning of the year, of the month, of the day; 始 is like 'morning.'" Wang Bao, *Eulogy on the Sage Ruler*: "the Chunqiu takes for its rule the essential of the Five Beginnings (五始)"; note: "the Originating is the beginning of *qi*; spring of the four seasons; the king of the mandate; the first month of governance; the duke's accession of a state."
- *七始, 華始, music-names.* Meng Kang: "the Seven Beginnings are the beginnings of Heaven, Earth, Man, and the four seasons; the Flower Beginning is the beginning of the flowering of all things." Han, *Anshi fangzhong songs*: "Seven Beginnings, Flower Beginning, solemnly leading the concordant sound."
- *旬始, a star-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "Xunshi appears beside the Northern Dipper." Sima Xiangru, *Daren fu*: "trailing Xunshi as a streamer."
- *A mountain-name.* Shanhaijing: "Mount Dongshi (東始) abounds in green jade."
- *A prefecture-name.* In Guanghan; Wei renamed it Shizhou (始州); the Song changed Longqing prefecture to Shizhou.
- *Fanqie 式吏, sounding like 試 (shì).* Mao Huang: "the 始 of 'origin' is rising tone — as in the Yi's 資始, 大始; the 始 of 'just now' is departing tone — as in the Yueling's 'the peach first (始) blooms,' 'the cicada first (始) sings.'"

**Textual notes.** "肅侶和聲" should read "肅倡和聲"; "垂旬始以爲輽" should read "幓".
