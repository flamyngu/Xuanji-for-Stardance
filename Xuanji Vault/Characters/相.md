**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; Eye radical 目; p.800 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息良. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 思將 — sounding like 襄 (xiāng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to inspect, to examine (省視)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "together (共)." *Zheng Yun*: "mutually (交相)." Yijing, *Xian*: "the two energies stir and respond, and join with each other (相與)." Gongyang zhuan, Huan yr 3: "what is 胥命? — it is mutual command (相命)"; note: "胥 means 相 (mutually); 相 and 胥 differ in sound but their sense is one."
- *Also, substance (質).* Shijing, *Daya*: "chiseled and polished its pattern, gold and jade its substance (相)."
- *Also 相思, a tree-name.* Zuo Si, *Wudu fu*: "the tree of longing (相思)"; note: "a great tree; it grows at Dongye."
- *Also* *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息亮 — the departing tone of 襄 (xiàng). Erya, *Shigu*: "to look at (視)." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 11: "observe (相) the moment and act."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: "to help (助)." Yijing, *Tai*: "to aid and assist (輔相) the fitness of Heaven and Earth." Shu, *Lizheng*: "thereby strenuously to assist (相) our state."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "to guide (導); also to urge on (勴)"; note: "it means to assist and encourage (贊勉)"; subcommentary: "the Xiangyinjiu says the 相 is one man who instructs and leads — that is, assisting and encouraging."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "an usher (𢷤)." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Da zongbo*: "at court audiences and assemblies he acts as the chief usher (上相)"; note: "the 相 instructs the king in ritual; going out to receive guests is 𢷤, coming in to instruct in ritual is 相; of the 相 there are five, the minister being the chief usher."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to support (扶)." Liji, *Liqi*: "the music has the helper-steps (相步)"; note: "supporting the musicians."
- *Also* Xiao Erya: "to govern, to manage (治)." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 9: "Chen belongs to Water; Fire is Water's mate, and what Chu governs (相)"; note: "Chu's ancestor Zhurong had charge of fire-matters."
- *Also, to select (選擇).* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Shiren*: "in early spring he chooses (相) the divining-stalks"; note: "it means to renew and select the milfoil."
- *Also the sound that accompanies the pestle.* Liji, *Quli*: "if a neighbor has a death, one pounds grain without the work-chant (相)"; note: "the 相 is a sound that helps along, a song to aid the pounding, like haulers of heavy loads crying 'heave-ho.'"
- *Also physiognomy (相術).* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 1: "the Interior Scribe Shu Fu could read men's faces (相人)."
- *Also a month-name.* Erya, *Shitian*: "the seventh month is 相."
- *Also an office-name.* Lülan: "the 相 (chancellor) is the chief of the hundred officials." Gu Sanfen: "Fuxi's upper minister (上相) was Gonggong, his lower minister Huanghuan." Former Han, *Table of Officials*: "the Chancellor of State (相國) and the Prime Minister (丞相) were both Qin offices."
- *Also 計相 (the accounts-minister).* Shiji, *Biography of Chancellor Zhang*: "Zhang Cang was promoted to accounts-minister (計相)"; note: "in sole charge of the account-registers."
- *Also 內相 (the inner chancellor).* Tang shu, *Biography of Lu Zhi*: "while Zhi was a Hanlin academician, he was styled the Inner Chancellor (內相)."
- *Also 家相 (the household steward).* Liji, *Quli*: "an officer does not call his household steward (家相) by name"; note: "the one in charge of household affairs."
- *Also,* Zhouli, *Chunguan*, has the Pingxiang masters (馮相氏); the *Xiaguan* has the Fangxiang masters (方相氏).
- *Also a musical instrument.* Liji, *Yue ji*: "ordering the closing tumult with the xiang (相)"; note: "the 相 is the fu drum; it too marks the music's measure, covered with leather and stuffed with chaff (穅); chaff is also called 相, and so it is named."
- *Also a star-name.* Shi Shen's *Xingjing*: "the Minister-star (相星) is south of the Northern Dipper."
- *Also a river-god*, named Qixiang (奇相); see the Boya.
- *Also* the Grand Astrologer's observatory had the wind-watching pole (相風竿). Fu Xiuyi, *Xiangfeng fu*: "topped with a sacred bird, weighted with a golden tiger, to watch the auspicious wind and divine fortune and ill." Gujin zhu: "made as a wind-watching bird (伺風鳥), invented by Yu of Xia."
- *Also a medicine-name.* Bencao gangmu: "beixiang (𤰞相) is another name for ephedra (麻黃); xiangwu (相鳥) is another name for the malan aster (馬蘭)."
- *Also a place-name.* Preface to the *Shang shu*: "Hedanjia (河稟甲) dwelt at Xiang (相)"; note: "in Hebei; now Wei commandery has Xiang county (相縣)."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* The Later Wei set up Xiangzhou (相州); the Tang called it the Ye Capital (鄴都).
- *Also a surname.* Later Han, *Account of the Southern Man*: "Mount Wuluo-Zhongli produced four surnames, the first being Xiang (相氏)." The Later Qin had Xiang Yun (相雲); the Northern Qi, Xiang Yuan (相願). Also Xiangli (相里), Wuxiang (務相), Kongxiang (空相), Xiongxiang (熊相), Yixiang (倚相), Jingxiang (京相), Yixiang (沂相), all compound surnames.
- *Also* Zihui bu: pronounced 禳 (ráng). Liji, *Jifa*: "the xiangjin (相近) at the pit and altar, sacrificing to cold and heat"; note: "相近 should be 禳祈 (the expiation-prayer); Wang Su also writes it 祖迎."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 思必, sounding like 悉 (xī). Du Fu, *Manxing* quatrain: "just as the spring wind bullies (相欺) me — overnight it snapped several sprays of blossom." Lu You's *Shihua*: "Bai Letian often used the graph 相 as fanqie 思必, as in 'I would ask the moon of Chang'an, how can it not part (相離)' — this is it; in this poem 相欺 too should be read in the entering tone."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "the Yi-apocrypha says: of what may be observed on earth, nothing is more observable than trees, hence 相 follows 目 (eye) and 木 (wood)." *Zhengzitong*: "相 is vulgarly written 𥄢, a derivative graph. 相 (fanqie 思將) means to inspect; 𥄢 (fanqie 莫卜) means dim-eyed. To split 相 and 𥄢 into two characters is wrong."
