**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Mound radical 阜; p.1347 #05)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 扶方, sounding like 房 (fáng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A dyke (隄)." *Yupian*: "a barrier (鄣)." *Guangyun*: "to ward off, defend (防禦)." Erya, *Shi di*: "墳 is a great dyke (大防)"; subcommentary: "墳 means a cliff-bank, shaped like a grave-mound, named a great dyke." Zhouli, *Kao gong ji, Jiang ren*: "in all, a ditch must follow the water's set, and a dyke (防) must follow the land's set." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 25: "they marked off the lowland dykes (原防)"; note: "防 is a dyke." Guliang zhuan, Zhao yr 8: "mowing the thoroughwort to make a bound (防)"; note: "防 is the great boundary of a field." Mengzi: "make no crooked dykes (曲防)." Zhanguo ce: "it has the Long Wall and the Great Dyke (鉅防), enough to make a frontier."

**Senses.**
- *To guard against, prepare (備).* *Yupian*. Yijing, *Ji ji*: "the gentleman thereby thinks on troubles and guards against (防) them beforehand."
- *To prohibit (禁).* *Yupian*. Liji, *Tan gong*: "Kui is the cook; it was not his to supply knife and spoon, yet he dared also to share in the warding (防)"; note: "防 means to check excess and overflow."
- *A screen (容).* Erya, *Shi gong*: "a 容 is called a 防"; note: "shaped like today's small curved bedside screen, by which the archery-caller screens himself." Yan Yanzhi, *Reply to Director Zheng*: "lingering, the pure screen (淸防) is close"; note: "the pure screen is a folding-screen (屏風)."
- *A town-name in Chen.* Shijing, *Chen feng*: "on the Dyke (防) is a magpie's nest"; tradition: "防 is a town."
- *A Lu place-name.* Chunqiu, Yin yr 9: "in winter, the duke met the Marquis of Qi at Fang (防)"; note: "防 is a Lu place, southeast of Langya county." Also Yin yr 10: "they took Fang (防)"; note: "southwest of Changyi county in Gaoping there is the Xifang town (西防城)."
- *防風氏 (Fangfengshi), a state-name.* *Discourses of Lu*: "Yu summoned the host of spirits to Mount Kuaiji, and Fangfengshi arrived last."
- *Surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "the Han had the filial son Fang Guang; the Ming, Fang Sheng."
- *Same as 房.* Shiji, *Annals of Xiang Yu*: "he enfeoffed Yang Wu as Marquis of Wufang (吳防)"; Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*, writes 吳房, belonging to Runan commandery; note: Meng Kang, "originally the state of Fangzi." Also *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "a magic-fungus grew within the chamber-walls (殿防) of the hall." Also *Treatise on Ditches and Canals*: "the Xuanfang (宣防) is dammed, and myriad blessings come."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: sometimes written 𨹛. *Yupian*: sometimes written 坊. Liji, *Fang ji*: "though one make a great barrier (坊), the people still overstep it"; note: "坊 is the same as 防."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 分房, sounding like 方 (fāng). Shijing, *Qin feng*: "this Zhongxing, a match for a hundred men (百夫之防)"; tradition: "防 means a match (比)"; Jian: "like to befit (當)"; *Shiwen*: "Mao's reading 方, Zheng's reading 房."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: interchangeable with 方. Former Han, *Table of Meritorious Officials*: "Zhifang, the resolute Marquis, Yong Chi (汁防肅侯雍齒)"; the Shiji writes 什方.
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 符况. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 符訪 — the departing tone of 坊 (fàng). *Guangyun*: "to guard and ward (守禦)." Preface to the Chunqiu: "the sage takes a wholeness that guards (防) the whole person"; *Shiwen*: "防, fanqie 扶放, also sounded 房." Also *Ji Yun*: "a dyke (隄), also the same as 坊."
