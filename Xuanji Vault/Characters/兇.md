**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Open-box radical 凵; p.124 #04)*

**Ancient graph.** 𣧑.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許容. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 虛容 — sounding like 胷 (xiōng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen* [of 凶]: "it depicts the ground pierced, something crossed and fallen into the midst." Xu [Kai]: "an evil place, not to be dwelt in; it depicts a pit in the ground, an evil that can swallow a man." *Erya, Shigu*: "a misfortune (咎)"; subcommentary: "it means calamity and ill."
- *To die young (短折).* *Yupian.*
- *Interchangeable with 忷.* *Ji Yun*: "anxious dread." The Jinyu: "the enemy entered and there was alarm (凶)"; note: "凶 is like 凶凶, fearful dread"; also written 兇. *Shuowen*: "agitated fear."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 虛王, the level tone of 貺.* Dongfang Shuo, *Seven Remonstrances*: "the enterprise lost and not retrieved — what use to speak of disaster and ill (禍凶)? They turn renegade and form their cliques; the man who walks alone — what has he to hope?"

**On the graph (兇).** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許容. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 虛容 — sounding like 胷 (xiōng). *Shuowen*: "agitated fear"; it follows 人 (man) beneath 凶. Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "the men of Cao were in fearful dread (兇懼)." *Yupian*: "the sound of fear." *Zengyun*: "凶 is used interchangeably with 兇." A Tang poem: "to remove the wicked (兇) is to requite a thousand ages." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 恟 or 忷. *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許拱. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 詡拱 — the rising tone of 胷 (xiǒng); same sense.
