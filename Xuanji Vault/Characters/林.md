**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.516 #05)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 力尋. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 犂針. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 犂沈 — sounding like 臨 (lín).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "level ground with clustered trees is a 林 (grove)." Xu [Kai]: "clustered trees, hence it follows two 木 (trees); level ground, hence the two 木 are even." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "lush is that level grove (平林)."
- *The open country.* Shijing, *Zhounan*: "spread out in the midst of the wood (中林)."
- *Trees on a hill.* Guliang zhuan, Xi yr 14: "a grove joined to a hill is the foothill-forest (麓)."
- *Bamboo and trees [under the Forester].* Note to Zhouli, *Diguan, Linheng*: "bamboo and trees are 林, waters under the Weir-keeper (衡)."
- *Lord (君).* *Erya, Shigu.*
- *A look of abundance.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "stately and abundant (有壬有林)."
- *林鐘, a pitch-pipe name.* Liji, *Yueling*: "in the last month of summer the pitch matches Linzhong"; the Zhouli writes Hanzhong (函鐘).
- *羽林, a star-name.* Ying Shao: "Heaven has the Yulin, the star of the Grand General; 林 is likened to a forest of trees, with the sense of [feathered] wings and a bird-of-prey's strike, hence it names a military officer." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Xuan*: "he took the sons of those who died on campaign and reared them as the Yulin army, called the Yulin Orphans."
- *綠林, a mountain-name in Jingzhou.* Later Han, *Biography of Liu Yuan*: "the fugitives gathered at Lülin."
- *A surname.* Xingpu: "descendants of Bigan of Yin, who fled to Mount Changlin and took it as surname." Also descendants of Lin Kai, son of King Ping, whose seat was Nan'an.

**On the graph.** A note: in the Shuowen 林 forms its own radical, and 棼, 楚, etc. follow it; here it is merged in under 木.

**Textual note.** The "歌林鐘，舞大夏" citation is from a Zuozhuan subcommentary quoting the *Da siyue*, not the Zuozhuan proper; and the Zhouli writes 函鐘, not 林鐘.
