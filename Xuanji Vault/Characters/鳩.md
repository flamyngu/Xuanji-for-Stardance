## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈亥集中〉【鳥字部】頁1481第10【唐韻】居求切【集韻】【正韻】居尤切，𠀤九平聲。【說文】鶻鵃。似山雀而小，短尾，靑黑色。【禽經】拙者莫如鳩，不能爲巢。【詩·召南】維鵲有巢，維鳩居之。【禮·月令】仲春，鷹化爲鳩。又【王制】鳩化爲鷹，然後設罻羅。【註】仲秋也。又官名。【左傳·昭十七年】郯子曰：少皞氏以鳥名官，祝鳩氏，司徒也。鴡鳩氏，司馬也。鳲鳩氏，司空也。爽鳩氏，司𡨥也。鶻鳩氏，司事也。五鳩，鳩民者也。【嚴粲詩緝】左傳五鳩備見詩經。祝鳩，鵓鳩也，四牡嘉魚之鵻是也。鴡鳩，關關鴡鳩之鳩是也。鳲鳩，布穀也，曹風之鳲鳩是也。鷞鳩，大明之鷹是也。鶻鳩，鱟鳩，非《班鳩》《小宛》之鳴鳩，《氓》食桑葚之鳩是也。又蒙鳩，鷦鷯也。【荀子·勸學篇】南方有鳥，名曰蒙鳩。又【爾雅·釋詁】鳩，聚也。【正字通】鳩能聚陽氣，故取義於聚。【書·堯典】共工方鳩僝功。【左傳·襄二十五年】鳩藪澤。又安也，集也。【左傳·隱八年】君釋三國之圖，以鳩其民。又【襄十六年】敢使魯無鳩乎。又國名。【山海經】始鳩在海中轅厲南。又舒鳩，楚屬國。見【左傳·襄二十四年】。又地名。鳩兹。【左傳·襄三年】楚子重伐吳，克鳩兹。【註】吳邑。又山名。【山海經】北二百里曰發鳩之山。又人名。汝鳩，湯賢臣。又小腹下曰鳩口。【巢氏病源】斜踵至鳩口內。又【韻會】渠尤切，音裘。同馗。土菌，可食。又叶斤於切，音居。【道藏歌】重萌鬱以赫，朱風引鳴鳩。旣忘榮曜契，何不寶僊居。【說文】鳩，从鳥九聲。【禽經】九鳥曰鳩。或曰鳩字从九以此。【列子·天瑞篇】一變而爲七，七變而爲九。九者，究也。乃變而爲一，故名鳩，此鳩所以从九也。【玉篇】或作勼。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, middle; Bird radical 鳥; p.1481 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 居求. *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居尤 — the level tone of 九 (jiū).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the guzhou (鶻鵃); like a mountain sparrow but smaller, short-tailed, blue-black." Qinjing: "none so clumsy as the dove (鳩), which cannot build a nest." Shijing, *Shaonan*: "the magpie has a nest, the dove (鳩) dwells in it." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-spring, the hawk transforms into the dove (鳩)." Also *Wang zhi*: "the dove (鳩) transforms into the hawk, and only then are the fowling-nets set"; note: "this is mid-autumn."
- *Also an office-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 17: "the Viscount of Tan said, 'the house of Shaohao named its offices after birds — the Zhujiu (祝鳩) clan were the Ministers of Education (司徒); the Juju (鴡鳩) clan the Ministers of War (司馬); the Shijiu (鳲鳩) clan the Ministers of Works (司空); the Shuangjiu (爽鳩) clan the Ministers of Justice (司寇); the Gujiu (鶻鳩) clan the Ministers of Affairs (司事). The Five Doves (五鳩) were they who gathered (鳩) the people.'" Yan Can's *Shiji*: "the Five Doves of the Zuozhuan are all to be seen in the Shijing. The Zhujiu (祝鳩) is the bojiu (鵓鳩) — the zhui (鵻) of 'Si mu' and 'Jia yu.' The Juju (鴡鳩) is the dove of 'guan-guan cry the ospreys.' The Shijiu (鳲鳩) is the cuckoo (布穀) — the shijiu of the Cao airs. The Shuangjiu (鷞鳩) is the hawk of 'Da ming.' The Gujiu (鶻鳩), the houjiu (鱟鳩), is not the calling-dove of 'Ban jiu' and 'Xiao wan' but the dove that eats mulberries in 'Mang.'"
- *Also 蒙鳩, the wren (鷦鷯).* Xunzi, *Quanxue*: "in the south there is a bird named the mengjiu (蒙鳩)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "鳩 means to gather (聚)." *Zhengzitong*: "the dove can gather the yang energy, hence the sense of gathering." Shu, *Yao dian*: "Gonggong has gathered (鳩) and displayed his merits." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 25: "they gathered (鳩) the marshes and meres."
- *Also, to settle (安); to bring together (集).* Zuozhuan, Yin yr 8: "the lord set aside his designs on the three states, to settle (鳩) their people." Also Xiang yr 16: "dare we leave Lu without peace (鳩)?"
- *Also a state-name.* Shanhai jing: "Shijiu (始鳩) lies amid the sea, south of Yuanli."
- *Also Shujiu (舒鳩), a state subordinate to Chu*; see Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 24.
- *Also a place-name, Jiuzi (鳩兹).* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 3: "Zizhong of Chu attacked Wu and took Jiuzi (鳩兹)"; note: "a town of Wu."
- *Also a mountain-name.* Shanhai jing: "two hundred li north is Mount Fajiu (發鳩)."
- *Also a personal name:* Rujiu (汝鳩), a worthy minister of Tang.
- *Also, below the lower belly is called the jiukou (鳩口).* Chao's *Bingyuan*: "slanting from the heel to within the jiukou (鳩口)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie 渠尤, sounding like 裘 (qiú); the same as 馗: an earth-fungus, edible.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 斤於, sounding like 居 (jū). A Daozang hymn: "the doubled sprouts swell luxuriant and bright, the vermilion wind draws on the calling dove (鳴鳩); having forgotten the bond of glory and splendor, why not treasure the immortal's dwelling?"

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "鳩 follows 鳥 (bird) with 九 (nine) as phonetic." Qinjing: "the nine-bird is called 鳩; some say the graph 鳩 follows 九 for this reason." Liezi, *Tianrui*: "one transforms into seven, seven into nine; nine is the limit (究), and then it turns into one — hence the name 鳩, and this is why 鳩 follows 九." *Yupian*: sometimes written 勼.
