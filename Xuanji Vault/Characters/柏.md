**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.518 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 博陌, sounding like 百 (bǎi).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The ju-cypress (椈)." *Liushu jingyun*: "柏 is a yin tree. Trees all belong to the yang, but the cypress faces the yin and points west — being a tree of steadfast virtue (貞德), hence the graph follows 白; 白 is the true colour of the west." Also *Chunqiu wei*: "the feudal lords plant cypress (柏) at their tombs." Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "the cypress (柏) is the court of ghosts."

**Senses.**
- *Great (大); interchangeable with 伯.* *Shiming*: "the bo-cart (柏車) is a great cart."
- *To press close (逼); same as 廹.* Zhouli, *Chun guan*: "their mats pressed to the ground (柏席), with reeds and an axe-patterned border"; Zheng's note: "the 柏 mat is the mat pressed to the ground (廹地之席)." Shiji, *Annals of Han Gaozu*: "Boren (柏人) means 'pressed by men (廹于人).'" The Han, *Huzi ge* of Emperor Wu: "the fish are pent and gloomy, pressed (柏) by the winter days."
- *A state-name*, in Xiping county, Henan.
- *A mountain-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "he led the Huai from Tongbai (桐柏)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he raised the Boliang Terrace (柏梁臺)."
- *Surname.* *Xingpu*: "Bo Zhi of the Warring States, Bo Ying of the Han."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 搏 (bó), fanqie 卜各.* Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "the one in the mountains, fragrant with duruo, drinks the stone-spring and shades beneath pine and cypress (松柏)."
- *Rhymes as 必 (bì), fanqie 壁益.* Tao Qian, *Passing Qianxi*: "of garden and field I daily dream — how can I bear long to be parted? My longing in the end is in the homebound boat; truly, fit as the frost-cypress (霜柏)." It follows 木 with 白 as phonetic. Commonly written 栢 — wrongly.
