**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls дё‘, upper; Enclosure radical еӣ—; p.217 #02)*

**Ancient graph.** рӨҮҖ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie ж–јзңһ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie дјҠзңһ вҖ” sounding like е§» (yД«n).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "it follows еӣ— (enclosure) and еӨ§ (great) вҖ” a meaning-compound." Xu Kai: "what is able to be great, the multitude encircle and gather to it."
- *To continue, follow on (д»Қ, иҘІ).* Lunyu: "the Yin followed on (еӣ ) the rites of Xia." Mengzi: "to make a height one must build on (еӣ ) a hill, to make a depth one must build on a watercourse." Shu, *Yao dian*: "when the day is longest and the star is Fire, thereby fix mid-summer; the people then disperse (еӣ )"; note: "they spread apart and spread again."
- *To rely on (дҫқ).* Lunyu: "if in relying (еӣ ) one does not lose those near to him, he too may be honoured."
- *To entrust by way of (жүҳ).* Mengzi: "Master Shi, through (еӣ ) Master Chen, told this to Mengzi."
- *By way of, owing to (з”ұ).* Zou Yang, *Memorial to the King of Liang*: "the night-shining jade disc, if thrown to a man in the dark on the road, none but would grip his sword and glare askance вҖ” for there was no occasion (еӣ ) for it to come before him."
- *Cause, karmic occasion (з·Ј).* Chuandeng lu: "would you know the cause (еӣ ) of your former life? What you receive in this life is it. Would you know the cause (еӣ ) of your life to come? What you do in this life is it."
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan, Ding yr 2: "the four clans of the men of Sui include the Yin (еӣ ) clan." Zhengzitong: "in the Ming there were Yin Li and Yin Jiong."
- *The Liushu zhengwei*: "it is borrowed for the е§» of 'marriage' (жҳҸе§»), meaning the woman has one she relies on (еӣ )."
- *Rhymes as зғҹ (yДҒn), fanqie зғҸеүҚ.* Lu Yun, *Poem Presented to Director Gu*: "diverse notes joined in concert, different tunes their sounds linked; you cut off my strands of joy, yet gather up my strands of thought and occasion (жҖқеӣ )." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written г§ў.

**Textual notes.** In Zou Yang's *Memorial to the King of Liang*, "жҢүеҠ’зӣёзӣј" вҖ” checked against the original biographies in the Shiji and Han shu, зӣј should both read зң„; corrected to зң„.
