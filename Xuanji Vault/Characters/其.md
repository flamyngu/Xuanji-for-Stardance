**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Eight radical 八; p.127 #18)*

**Ancient graphs.** 丌, 亓.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 渠之. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 渠宜 — sounding like 碁 (qí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yun Hui*: "a word pointing to a thing." Yijing, *Xici*: "its (其) import is far, its diction patterned." Shijing, *Daya*: "as it (其) is now."
- *An auxiliary particle.* Shu, *Xibo kan Li*: "now the king — what (其) of it for us?" Shijing, *Zhounan*: "brilliant its (其) blossom." *Yupian*: "a particle."
- *A surname.* *Yun Hui*: "the Han Marquis of Yang'e, Qi Shi."
- *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居之, sounding like 姬 (jī). *Yun Hui*: "a particle." Shu, *Weizi*: "what is to be done about it (其)?" Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "how goes the night (其)?"
- *In personal names.* Shiji, *Biography of Master Li*: "Master Li Yiji (食其) was a man of Gaoyang"; note: the Correct Meaning: "酈食其, three graphs of three sounds, read *li*, *yi*, *ji*." Former Han, *Biography of King Yuan of Chu*: "Gaozu had Shen Yiji (食其) attend the Grand Supreme Emperor"; note: Yan Shigu: "食 sounds *yi*, 其 sounds *ji*."
- *A mountain-name.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he visited Buji (不其)"; note: "其 sounds *ji*, a mountain-name"; *Guangyun*: "in Langya."
- *A place-name.* *Yun Hui*: "Zhuji (祝其) is Jiagu."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居吏; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 吉器 — sounding like 寄 (jì). *Yun Hui*: "a sentence-final particle." Shijing, *Kuaifeng*: "that (其) one." Interchangeable with 記: Liji, *Biaoji* cites the Shi, "that (記) one." Also with 己: Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 27 cites the Shi, "that (己) one." Also written 忌: Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "Shu is good at archery (忌).
