**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Sun radical 日; p.494 #22)*

**Ancient graph.** 旹.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 市之. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 辰之 — sounding like 蒔 (shí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the four seasons (四時)." *Shiming*: "the four seasons — each direction has one season; 時 is 'period (期)': things' birth and death each arrive according to their term." Shu, *Yao dian*: "reverently he bestowed on the people the seasons (時)"; tradition: "reverently record Heaven's times to give to men." Also: "the round-year of 366 days, by the intercalary month fixes the four seasons and completes the year." Huainanzi, *Tianwen xun*: "the concentrated essence of yin and yang makes the four seasons; three months make one season."
- *A [double-]hour.* *Yun Hui*: "a *chen* (辰); the twelve hours."
- *This, right (是).* *Guangyun.* Shu, *Yao dian*: "the black-haired people are thereby (時) harmonized"; tradition: "時, this." Shijing, *Daya*: "he said 'stop,' he said 'now (時)' — and built a house here"; Zhu: "one may stop here and build."
- *To watch for (伺).* *Boya.* Lunyu: "Confucius watched for the time (時) when [Yang Huo] was out, and went to pay his respects."
- *Good (善); apt, centred (中).* *Boya*, *Guangyun.*
- *Place name.* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 9: "fought at Qianshi (乾時)"; note: "乾時, a Qi place; the Shi waters lie in the Le'an district — a branching flow that dries up in drought, hence 'Dry Shi.'"
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: the *Lianglizhuan* has Shi Miao; He's *Xingyuan*: "now a man of Julu."
- *Same as 塒 (a wall-roost).* Shijing, *Wang feng*: "the fowl roost in the wall-niche (塒)"; *Shiwen*: "塒 also written 時."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 上紙.* Wang Can, *Seven Expositions*: "he changes not the Way for ambition, nor falls behind the times (時) for himself."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 側吏.* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "choked with grief, forlorn and lost, I alone am wretched in this time (時)" (rhyming with 態, which sounds 替).
