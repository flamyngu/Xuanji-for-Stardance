**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.646 #32)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 莫半 — sounding like 縵 (màn).

**Etymology and senses.** "A great flood." Some say: water soaking and spoiling things. Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "soaked and ruined is 漫."
- *A river-name.* Later Han, *Treatise on Commanderies*: "Chenggao has the Man River (漫水)."
- *A torrent-name.* Shuijing zhu: "the Tuo water rises in Mount Tuo, flows north out of the valley, and is called the Man torrent (漫㵎)."
- *漫漫, the look of length and distance.* Zuo Si, *Wudu fu*: "the broad court stretching boundless (漫漫)."
- *The colour of clouds.* Shangshu dazhuan: "the auspicious clouds glow bright, the rites spread vast (漫漫)."
- *All over, throughout (徧).* Gongyang zhuan, Xuan yr 3: "the suburban ox died; it does not say where [the rats] gnawed — because it was all over (漫)"; note: "they gnawed all over the ox's body."
- *Loose, unrestrained (放).* Former Han, *Treatise on Letters*: "diffuse and rambling (漫羨), with no settled aim."
- *汗漫, the vast and dim.* Guanyinzi, *Jiuyao*: "do not, on account of the boundless (汗漫), call the Way wide."
- *澶漫, unbridled.* Zhuangzi, *Mati*: "unbridled (澶漫) in making music."
- *The form of plains and rivers.* Zhang Heng, *Xijing fu*: "spreading wide and trailing far (澶漫靡迤)."
- *爛漫, the look of scattering.* Wang Yanshou, *Lu Lingguang Hall fu*: "glittering and scattered (流離爛漫)."
- *漢漫, vexation.* Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "between Chaoxian and the Lie River, to be vexed is called 漢漫."
- *Same as 墁 (to plaster): to daub.* Zhuangzi, *Xu Wugui*: "a man of Ying daubed (漫) white clay on the tip of his nose."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 謨官, sounding like 瞞 (mán) — the look of great waters.
- *Boya*: "漫漫 means level"; also "漫漫, the look of a long road," interchangeable with 曼.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 莫晏, sounding like 謾 (màn) — same as 慢 (idle): "slothful"; also written 僈.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 民堅, sounding like 眠 (mián).* Emperor Wen of Wei, *Widow fu*: "...passing the autumn night, long and long (漫漫); the thin frost falls and gathers in the court, the swallows and sparrows fly before me."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 眼見, sounding like 面 (miàn).* Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "...vast and clear, broad and dim, pointing east and west to the boundless (漫漫)."

**Textual note.** "體漫漫兮" should read "禮漫漫兮.
