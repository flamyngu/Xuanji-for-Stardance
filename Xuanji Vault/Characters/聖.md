**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Ear radical 耳; p.967 #22)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𦕡, 𠄵.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 式正 — the departing tone of 聲 (shēng), i.e. **shèng**.

**Etymology and senses.** Yijing, *Qian*: "the sage (聖人) arises and the myriad things behold him." Shu, *Hongfan*: "penetration makes the sage (聖)"; tradition: "to be unobstructed in all affairs is called sage." Liji, *Liyun*, subcommentary on "the flower of the Three Ages": "ten thousand men [in one] is a hero (傑); twice a hero is a sage (聖)." Mengzi: "great, and transforming [others] — this is called sage." *Fengsu tong*: "聖 is 聲 (sound): hearing the sound he knows the inner truth, hence 'sage.'"
- *A posthumous title.* In title-law: "one who proclaims the good and levies lightly" is titled 聖; "one who honours guests with rich rites" is titled 聖.
- *A tree-name.* Shanhaijing: "north of Kaiming is the sage-tree (聖木)"; note: "eating of it makes a man wise and sagely."
- *A river-name.* *Shuijing zhu*: "the Sheng River (聖水) rises east of Shanggu, passes north of Changxiang county, runs east past Anci county, and flows east into the sea."
- *A surname.*

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 裳聲, sounding like 成 (chéng).* Chang Qu, *Huayang guozhi* encomium: "...Ziyun [Yang Xiong], dark and far-reaching, brilliant in his grand sageliness (聖).
