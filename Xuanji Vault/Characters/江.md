**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.606 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 古雙, sounding like 杠 (jiāng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** A water-name. *Fengsu tong, Shanze*: "江 means tribute (貢); it yields rare things that may be sent up as tribute." *Shiming*: "江 means in common (共); the lesser rivers flow into it, and it is held in common." *Shuowen*: "the water issues from Mount Min beyond the Jiandi marches in Shu, and enters the sea." Shu, *Yu gong*: "Mount Min channels the Jiang." *Kongzi jiayu*: "the Jiang begins at Mount Min, and its source one may overbrim a goblet with; but when it comes to the Jiang-ford, one cannot, without boats and braving no wind or rain, get across."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Three Jiang now entered (三江旣入)"; Wei Zhao's note on the *Discourses of States*: "the Wusong Jiang, the Qiantang Jiang, the Puyang Jiang." Guo Pu's note on the Shanhai jing: "from Mount Min the Great Jiang issues; from Mount Lai the South Jiang issues; from Mount Ju the North Jiang issues; the South and North Jiang both flow east into the Great Jiang." Yu Chan's note on the *Yang du fu*: "the Song Jiang, going seventy li to the northeast, reaches the Three-Jiang mouth: to the northeast it enters the sea as the Lou Jiang, to the southeast it enters the sea as the East Jiang, and with the Song Jiang these make three." Former Han, *Treatise on Geography* note: "the Three Jiang are the North Jiang, the Middle Jiang, and the South Jiang."
- *Also* the Nine Jiang (九江), in Jingzhou. Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Nine Jiang are truly mighty (孔殷)"; the *Correct Meaning*: "south of the Jiang, no water great or small but the common folk all call it a 江."
- *A prefecture-name.* In the Spring-and-Autumn it was the land of Wu and Chu; under Qin it was Jiujiang commandery, under Jin Xunyang commandery, that is, the old Jiangzhou. The *Dili tongshi*: "the Han Jiujiang commandery was originally north of the Jiang; but what is now called Jiangzhou is really the Chaisang county of Wuchang commandery. Later the Xunyang north of the Jiang was joined with Chaisang to set up a commandery, and the seat was moved from north of the Jiang to south of it, so that the south of the Jiang came to bear the name Xunyang; and later, after Xunyang, it was renamed Jiangzhou — truly not the land of the old Jiangzhou."
- *Surname.* Descendants of Boyi; enfeoffed at Jiang, they later took the state for their clan-name.
- *A star-name.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*: "beside the Tianhuang is the Jiang star (江星); when the Jiang star stirs, men ford the waters."
- *Dijiang (帝江), a divine bird.* It comes from Mount Tian and knows song and dance; seen in the Shanhai jing.
- *Also* *Yunbu*: rhymes, fanqie 古紅, sounding like 公 (gōng). Chuci, *Jiu zhang*: "I shall steer my boat and drift downstream, up the Dongting and down the Jiang; leaving the dwelling of all my days, I now roam free and come eastward." Today the Dian (Yunnan) dialect calls the Jiang 公, and calls the river-fish (江魚) the gong-fish (公魚).
