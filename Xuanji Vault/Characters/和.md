**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls дё‘, upper; Mouth radical еҸЈ; p.185 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** е’Ҡ, йҫў.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жҲёжҲҲ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иғЎжҲҲ вҖ” sounding like зҰҫ (hГ©).

**Etymology and senses.** *Guangyun*: "concordant; harmonious; neither hard nor soft." Shu, *Yaodian*: "he harmonized (е’Ң) the myriad states." Also *Shundian*: "the pitch-pipes harmonize (е’Ң) the sounds." Yijing, *Qian*: "preserving the union of the Grand Harmony (еӨӘе’Ң)." Zhongyong: "when [the feelings] arise and all hit the due measure, that is called harmony (е’Ң)."
- *The Xi and He officers.* Shu, *Yaodian*: "he charged Xi and He (зҫІе’Ң)"; note: "the Xi clan and the He clan, the officers of the calendar and the giving of the seasons."
- *A carriage-bell.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the *he* and *luan* bells chime (е’Ңйёһйӣқйӣқ)"; tradition: "on the carriage-bar it is the *he* (е’Ң), on the bit the *luan*"; subcommentary: "е’Ң is also a bell, because it answers in concord with the *luan*." *Guangyun*: "originally written йүҢ."
- *A small mouth-organ.* Erya, *Shiyue*: "the big mouth-organ is the *chao*, the small the *he* (е’Ң)"; note: "the *he* has thirteen reeds."
- *A camp-gate.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Da sima*: "with banners he makes the gates of the left and right *he* (е’Ң)"; note: "the camp-gate is called *he*, now the rampart-gate, set up with two banners." Zhanguo ce: "they pitched camp gate-to-gate (дәӨе’Ң) with Qin."
- *A posthumous title.* "Neither hard nor soft" is titled е’Ң (the Harmonious).
- *Heyi (е’ҢеӨ·), a place-name.* Shu, *Yugong*: "at Heyi the work was achieved."
- *Yunhe (йӣІе’Ң), a place-name.* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Da siyue*: "the zithers and lutes of Yunhe (йӣІе’Ң)."
- *A prefecture-name.* *Guangyun*: south of the Huai; under Han part of Jiujiang commandery, under Qi it became Hezhou (е’Ңе·һ). *Yun Hui*: Xihezhou (иҘҝе’Ңе·һ), where Meng Tian of Qin began the Great Wall; under Tang it was Minzhou, the Song changed it to Xihezhou.
- *A surname.* *Guangyun*: descended from Xihe, or, some say, from Bian He; the Jin had He Qiao.
- *йӨҠе’Ң, a back-rest.* *Zhengzitong*: Li Mi took a strange tree's coiled branch to lean his back on, and named it the *yanghe*. The Songling ji: Pi Rixiu sent Wei Buzhuo five things, among them the Wulong *yanghe* and the Tonglu *yanghe*.

**Departing tone (hГЁ).** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie иғЎиҮҘ вҖ” the departing tone of зҰҫ, i.e. **hГЁ**. *Guangyun*: "sounds answering one another."
- *To answer in concord.* Yijing, *Zhongfu*: "the crane calls in the shade, and its young answer (е’Ң) it." Erya, *Shiyue*: "to blow [the pipe] alone is called е’Ң."
- *To blend, season (иӘҝ).* *Ji Yun.* Liji, *Tangong*: "the reed-organs were ready but not tuned (е’Ң)." Liyun: "the five flavours, the six blendings (е…ӯе’Ң), the twelve foods, in turn the staple"; note: "in spring more sour, in summer more bitter, in autumn more pungent, in winter more salty, with the smooth and the sweet added вҖ” these are the six blendings." Liqi: "the sweet receives the blending (е’Ң)"; *Shiwen*: е’Ң fanqie жҲёиҮҘ. Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Inner Cook is in charge of the cutting, boiling, frying, and seasoning (е’Ң) of the food for the king, queen, and heir.
