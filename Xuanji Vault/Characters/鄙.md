**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; City radical 邑; p.1276 #18)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠴿.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 方美. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 補美. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 補委 — sounding like 比 (bǐ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shiming*: "鄙 means denial (否); a small town that cannot reach far." Zhouli, *Tian guan, Da zai*: "by the eight rules he governs the capital-and-domains (都鄙)"; note: "where the capital dwells is the 鄙 — the fief-towns of the dukes, ministers, and grandees, and the towns within the royal domain that feed the king's kinsmen." Also *Di guan, Sui ren*: "he managed the law of laying out the districts and hamlets (縣鄙): five zan make a 鄙, five 鄙 a district."

**Senses.**
- *A border-town (邊鄙).* *Guangyun*. Zuozhuan, Yin yr 1: "Taishu ordered the west border (西鄙) and the north border (北鄙) to be of double allegiance to himself"; note: "鄙 is a Zheng border-town."
- *Mean, base; to despise (陋, 厭薄).* *Zheng Yun*. Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 14: "to pass through us and not ask the road is to despise (鄙) us." Laozi, *Dao de jing*: "the multitude all have their use, and I alone am dull as the base (鄙)."
- *鄙 contrasted with 都 — rustic, boorish (朴野).* Huainanzi, *Quan yan*: "what begins in the city (都) is ever greater than what is rustic (鄙)."
- *鄙 contrasted with 仁 — meaning not penetrating.* Former Han, *Biography of Dong Zhongshu*: "some benevolent, some boorish (鄙)."
- *One stingy with wealth is called 鄙吝 (mean and grasping).* Commonly written 鄙 or 𨝣.

**Textual note.** Laozi, *Dao de jing*, "the multitude all have their use, and I alone am dull and base (且鄙)" — checked against the original, "且" should read "似" (like).
