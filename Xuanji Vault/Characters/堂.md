**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.231 #23)*

**Ancient graphs.** 坣, 㙶.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 徒郞 — sounding like 唐 (táng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a great hall (殿). The main chamber is called 堂." *Shiming*: "having a high, conspicuous look." Yanyi: "堂 means facing (當): the building that faces squarely toward the sun." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "ascend that ducal hall (公堂)."
- *Also 明堂 (the Bright Hall), the palace where the king receives the feudal lords.* Liji, *Mingtang wei*: "the Bright Hall is that which makes plain the high and low rank of the feudal lords." Former Han, *Treatise on the Suburban Sacrifices*: "in the first year of Yuanfeng, Gongyu Dai of Jinan presented a plan of the Bright Hall from the time of the Yellow Emperor."
- *Also 堂室 (hall and inner chamber).* Erya, *Shigong*: "in antiquity there was the 堂: from the midpoint forward it was left open — this is the hall (堂); from the midpoint back it was walled in — this is the chamber (室)."
- *Also a government office.* Han guan yi: "the Yellow Gate had the Painted Hall (畫堂) office; the Secretariat had the Jade Hall (玉堂)." Yang Xiong, *Jie chao*: "passing the Golden Gate, ascending the Jade Hall (玉堂)" — today the Hanlin Academy is also called the Jade Hall. Tang shu, *Treatise on Officials*: "at first the Three Departments deliberated affairs in the Hall of Government Affairs (政事堂) under the Chancellery; later Pei Yan moved it to the Secretariat."
- *Also,* the Ministry of Revenue had the Audit Hall (考堂), where the realm's yearly accounts were reckoned.
- *Also* Wu junguo zhi: "the prefect's hall of the commandery was the former residence of Lord Chunshen's son Jiajun. It repeatedly caught fire; coating it with orpiment (雌黃) stopped this, so the prefectural seat is called the Yellow Hall (黃堂)." Lüshi chunqiu: "Fuzi Jian played the zither and Shanfu was well governed, so a county seat is called the Zither Hall (琴堂)."
- *Also 堂堂: imposing (盛); upright (正).* Lunyu: "imposing (堂堂), this Zhang!" Huainanzi, *Bing lüe*: "a grand and imposing (堂堂) battle-array."
- *Also a crowd's laughter is called 哄堂.* Yinhua lu: "the Censorate had the Terrace Court, the Hall Court, and the Investigation Court; one man oversaw miscellaneous affairs, titled the Miscellany-Head (雜端). At the official communal meal all laughter was forbidden, and the attendants could scarce restrain themselves; when the Miscellany-Head laughed, all three courts (三堂) laughed — hence the term 哄堂 (the hall-roar)."
- *Also a Buddhist hall is called 鴈堂 (the Wild-Goose Hall).* Shishi yaolan: "Vaiśālī built a hall for the Buddha shaped like the graph 鴈 (wild goose), and so it was named."
- *Also a shaman's rite (巫祭).* Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "the male shaman in winter performs the 堂, the sending-off without fixed direction or number"; note: "at the year's winter end a sacrifice is set in the hall (堂) to dispatch the myriad ghosts."
- *Also place-names.* Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "gazing toward Chu and Tang (堂)"; note: "Chu is Chuqiu; Tang is a town beside the mound."
- *Also a broad, level place on a mountain is called 堂.* Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "what has Zhongnan? It has its ridges (紀) and its terraces (堂)."
- *Also a surname.* Han shi waizhuan: "Tang Yiruo knocked at Confucius's gate." Also a compound surname: the Han scholar Gaotang Sheng (高堂生), whom a Zhenguan-era decree of the Tang enrolled among those sacrificed to.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 徒紅, sounding like 同 (tóng). Qu Yuan, *Jiu ge*: "a roof of fish-scales, a dragon hall (堂); gates of purple cowries, a palace of pearls."
