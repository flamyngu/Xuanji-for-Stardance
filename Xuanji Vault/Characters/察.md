## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈寅集上〉【宀字部】頁290第16【唐韻】初八切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】初戛切，𠀤音刹。【說文】覆審也。从宀祭聲。【徐鉉曰】祭祀必質明。明，察也。故从祭。【廣韻】諦也，知也。【李陵·答蘇武書】功大罪小，不蒙明察。　又【增韻】考也，廉視也。【周禮·秋官·士師註】士，察也，義取察理獄訟之事也。又【唐書·百官志】監察御史，掌司六察。一察官人善惡，二察賦役不均，三察農桑不勤，倉廩耗減，四察妖猾盜賊，五察茂才異等，六察黠吏豪宗，兼幷縱暴。　又昭著也。【中庸】言其上下察也。　又察察，潔淸貌。【史記·屈原傳】安能以身之察察，受物之汶汶。　又苛察也。【老子·道德經】其政察察，其民𦈫𦈫。又【晉書·謝安傳】弘以大綱，不存小察。　又【正字通】偏見曰察。【莊子·天下篇】道德不一，天下多得一，察焉以自好。　又姓。吳將軍察戰，明正德中朝城知縣察童。　又讀作祭。【尙書大傳】祭之爲言察也。察者，至也，人事至然後祭。　又叶直列切，音徹。【前漢·郊祀歌】景星顯見，信星彪列。象載昭庭，日親以察。【古詩】置我懷袖中，三歲字不滅。一心抱區區，懼君不識察。　又叶子例切，音穧。【班固·幽通賦】攬葛藟而授余兮，眷峻谷曰勿墜。昒昕寤而仰思兮，心蒙蒙猶未察。【集韻】或作詧。    考證：〔【班固·幽通賦】攬葛藟而授子兮。〕　謹照原文授子改授余。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.290 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 初八. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 初戛 — sounding like 刹 (chà).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To examine thoroughly (覆審). It follows 宀 (roof) with 祭 as phonetic." Xu Xuan: "in sacrifice (祭祀) one must wait for daybreak; daybreak is brightness, brightness is to discern (察), hence it follows 祭." *Guangyun*: "to scrutinize (諦); to know (知)." Li Ling, *Reply to Su Wu*: "great in merit, small in fault, yet I gained no clear examination (明察)."

**Senses.**
- *To examine, to inspect closely (考, 廉視).* Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Shi shi*, note: "士 means to examine (察) — the sense is taken from examining and ordering the affairs of suits and litigation." Also Tang shu, *Treatise on Officials*: "the Investigating Censor managed the Six Inspections (六察): first, inspecting the good and ill of officials; second, inspecting unequal levies and corvée; third, inspecting neglect of farming and sericulture, and the wasting of granaries; fourth, inspecting the monstrous, the sly, and brigands; fifth, inspecting the talented and the extraordinary; sixth, inspecting crafty clerks and powerful clans, with their engrossing and violence."
- *Manifest, conspicuous (昭著).* Zhongyong: "it speaks of its being manifest (察) above and below."
- *察察, a pure, clean look.* Shiji, *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "how could I, with a body so spotless (察察), take on the grime of things?"
- *Harshly exacting (苛察).* Laozi, *Dao de jing*: "when its governance is meddling and exacting (察察), its people are split and crumbling (𦈫𦈫)." Also Jinshu, *Biography of Xie An*: "he was liberal with the great outlines and did not dwell on petty scrutiny (小察)."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "a partial view is called 察." Zhuangzi, *Tian xia*: "the Way and its power being not one, the world mostly grasps a single point and scrutinizes (察) it to please itself."
- *Surname.* General Chazhan (察戰) of Wu; Chatong (察童), magistrate of Chaocheng in the Zhengde era of the Ming.
- *Read as 祭 (jì).* *Shangshu dazhuan*: "祭 (sacrifice), as a word, means 察 — 察 means to arrive; human affairs reach their fullness, and then one sacrifices."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 徹 (chè), fanqie 直列.* Former Han, *Suburban Sacrifice Songs*: "the Bright Star appears, the Trusty Star shines in array; its image set forth lights the court, the sun drawing near and clear (察)." Old poem: "I place it in the fold of my sleeve; in three years the writing does not fade; with single heart I hold this small devotion, fearing my lord does not know and see (察)."
- *Rhymes as 穧 (jì), fanqie 子例.* Ban Gu, *You tong fu*: "grasping the kudzu vine he hands it to me, gazing on the steep valley, saying 'do not fall'; at dawn-twilight I wake and look up in thought, my heart dim and dull, still not perceiving (察)." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 詧.

**Textual note.** Ban Gu, *You tong fu*, "grasping the kudzu vine he hands it to you (授子)" — checked against the original, "授子" should read "授余" (hands it to me).
