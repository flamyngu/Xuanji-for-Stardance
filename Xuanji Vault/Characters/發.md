**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; Bow-legs radical 癶; p.784 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 方伐, sounding like 髮.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to shoot, discharge (䠶發)." Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "with one shot (發), five boars"; tradition: "發, an arrow." Former Han, *Account of the Xiongnu*: "arrows, four 發"; note: "by archery ritual one stops after three rounds, each round four arrows, so twelve arrows make one 發 (volley)"; Shigu: "發 is like saying today an arrow 'one release, two releases.'"
- *To rise (起).* *Guangyun.* Mencius: "Shun rose (發) from the midst of the fields."
- *To spread, raise up (舒, 揚).* Yijing, *Qian*: "the six lines spread their play (發揮)"; subcommentary: "發, to surpass." *Kun*: "made manifest (發) in deeds." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "with fame to set it forth (發)"; note: "to make this virtue manifest."
- *To open (開).* *Boya.* Shu, *Wu cheng*: "he opened (發) the granaries of Juqiao." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "from daybreak (明發) sleepless"; note: "as dawn nears, light opens forth."
- *To advance, to go (進, 行, 去).* *Yupian*, *Boya.* Shijing, *Qi feng*: "treading where I go (發)"; subcommentary: "to go, one must set the foot forth (發足) and depart, hence 發 means 'to go.'" Liji, *Yu zao*: "hurrying fast one would lift the feet (發), yet hands and feet not shifting"; note: "means to raise the shoes."
- *To push open (撥).* *Shiming.* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "if there is a call-up (發), then the Grand Situ is ordered to drill the men in chariot and armour"; subcommentary: "means a military expedition, dispatching (發) the soldiers."
- *To make clear (明).* *Guangyun.* Lunyu: "enough, too, to draw [it] out (發)"; note: "to make clear the great substance."
- *To raise, rouse (舉, 興).* Former Han, *Biography of Wang Ji*: "take care to set nothing afoot (發)"; note: "means raising up the many affairs."
- *To disturb (亂).* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "do not disturb (發) my fish-trap."
- *To strike, plough (伐).* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "vigorously break open (發) your private fields"; subcommentary: "with the plough strike the private fields, making them break open."
- *To send (遣).* Liji, *Tan gong*: "when Xianwenzi of Jin completed his hall, the Jin grandees sent gifts (發)"; note: "sent presents in congratulation."
- *To show (見).* Liji, *Li qi*: "the gentleman delights in its showing (發)"; note: "delights that much of it appears outwardly."
- *To move (動).* Laozi: "had Earth nothing to make it still, it might split (發)."
- *To release, vent (洩).* Chuci, *Da zhao*: "the spring air bursts forth (奮發)." Calendrically: "spring and summer are 發, autumn and winter are 斂 (gathering)."
- *發發, swiftness.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the whirlwind rushing (發發)"; *Jian*: "cold and swift."
- *長發, the great Di sacrifice.* Shi tradition; subcommentary: "the music-song of the great Di." Tang shu, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "Yizu's [dance] is called the 長發 dance."
- *發齊, a wedding rite.* Xunzi, *Li lun*: "before the great wedding has its 發齊"; note: "before ceremonial form exists — in the image of high antiquity." Shiji, *Lishu* writes 廢齊.
- *To unhitch a carriage (駕車).* *Yupian.* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "發, 稅 — to halt a carriage; in eastern Qi and the Hai-Dai region 發, in Song-Zhao-Chen-Wei 稅"; note: "舍 read as 寫; today generally 發 means 寫 (unload); 稅 is like 脫."
- Hou Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "the eastern divine bird is 發明." *Boya*: "the phoenix's dawn-cry is 發明."
- *Jizhong Zhoushu*: "the 發 people are 'deer-deer,' like deer running swift"; note: "發, an Eastern Yi people."
- *淸發, a water name* (Zuozhuan).
- *Counties.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: 餘發 county in Jiuzhen; 發干 county in Dong commandery.
- *Surname.* Shiji, *Treatise on Feng and Shan*: 游水發根; note: "游水, a county; 發根, a personal surname-name."

**Further readings.**
- *Ji Yun, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 北末, sounding like 撥 (bō). Shijing, *Wei feng*: "sturgeon and mandarin-fish teeming (發發)"; tradition: "an abundant look"; Ma Rong: "the fish-tails caught in the net, 發發-ly." The *Hanshi* writes 鱍; the *Shuowen* writes 鮁.
- *Rhymes as 廢 (fèi), fanqie 方吠.* Shijing, *Bin feng*: "on the first days the cold blasts (觱發)"; tradition: "wind-cold" (rhyming with 烈烈, sound 例).
- *Rhymes, fanqie 非律, the entering tone of 廢.* Yang Xiong, *Changyang fu*: "…clouds massing, lightning bursting (發)…" (a rhyme discussion with 軼).

**Textual notes.** 桓元年 should read 桓二年; 軍甲 should read 車甲.
