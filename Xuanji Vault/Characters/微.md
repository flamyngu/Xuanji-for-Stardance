## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈寅集下〉【彳字部】頁370第12【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤無非切，音薇。【爾雅·釋詁】幽微也。【易·繫辭】知微知彰。【書·大禹謨】道心惟微。　又【廣韻】微，妙也。【禮·禮運】德產之致也精微。　又【說文】隱行也。【史記·秦始皇紀】微行咸陽。　又【廣韻】細也。【孟子】乃孔子，則欲以微罪行。　又【玉篇】不明也。【詩·小雅】彼月而微，此日而微。　又【韻會】衰也。【詩·小雅】式微式微。【箋】微乎微者也。【史記·杞世家】杞小微。　又【韻會】賤也。【尚書序】虞舜側微。　又【爾雅·釋詁】匿微也。【註】微謂逃藏也。【左傳·哀十六年】白公奔山而縊，其徒微之。【註】微，匿也。　又【爾雅·釋詁】蔽，微也。【晉語】公子重耳過曹，曹共公聞其駢脅，諜其將浴，設微薄而觀之。【註】微，蔽也。　又殺也。【禮·檀弓】禮有微情者。【疏】微，殺也，言賢者喪親，必致滅性，故制使三日而食，哭踊有數，以殺其內情。　又伺察也。【前漢·郭解傳】使人微知賊處。【註】微，伺問之也。　又【爾雅·釋訓】骭瘍爲微。【註】骭，脚脛。瘍，瘡也。【詩·小雅】旣微且尰。　又【韻會】非也。【詩·邶風】微我無酒。　又【韻會】無也。【禮·檀弓】齊餓者，不食嗟來之食。曾子曰：微與。【註】微，猶無也。　又國名。【書·牧誓】微盧彭濮。【傳】微在巴蜀。　又【爾雅·釋山】未及上翠微。【疏】未及頂上，在旁陂陀之處，山氣靑縹色，故曰翠微也。　又紫微，太微，少微，𠀤星名。【晉書·天文志】紫微垣十五星在北斗。北一曰紫微，天帝之座也，天子之常居也。太微，天子庭也。五帝之座也，十二諸侯府也。少微，在太微西，士大夫之位也，明大而黃，則賢士舉也。　又三微。【後漢·章帝紀】春秋於春每月書王者，重三正，愼三微也。【註】三微者，三正之始，萬物皆微，物色不同，故王者取法焉。十一月，時陽氣始施於黃泉之下，色皆赤，赤者陽氣，故周爲天正，色尚赤。十二月，萬物始牙而色白，白者隂氣，故殷爲地正，色尚白。十三月，萬物莩甲而出，其色皆黑，人得加功展業，故夏爲人正，色尚黑。　又姓。【左傳·哀八年】微虎。【註】魯大夫。又微生，複姓。【論語】微生高。    考證：〔【書·舜典】虞舜側微。〕　謹按舜典無此文，係出書序。謹將書舜典改爲尚書序。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Step radical 彳; p.370 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 無非 — sounding like 薇 (wēi).

**Etymology and senses.** *Erya, Shigu*: "hidden, minute (幽微)." Yijing, *Xici*: "to know the minute (微) and to know the manifest." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "the heart of the Way is subtle (微)."
- *Subtle, wondrous (妙).* *Guangyun.* Liji, *Liyun*: "what virtue brings forth at its utmost is fine and subtle (精微)."
- *To go in secret (隱行).* *Shuowen.* Shiji, *Annals of Qin Shihuang*: "he went incognito (微行) about Xianyang."
- *Small, slight (細).* *Guangyun.* Mengzi: "as for Confucius, he wished to leave on a slight (微) pretext of fault."
- *Dim, not bright.* *Yupian.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "that moon grows dim (微), this sun grows dim."
- *To decline (衰).* *Yun Hui.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "decayed (式微), decayed!"; gloss: "minute among the minute." Shiji, *House of Qi*: "Qi was small and weak (微)."
- *Lowly, humble (賤).* *Yun Hui.* Preface to the Shu: "Shun of Yu was of low and humble (側微) station."
- *To flee and hide (匿微).* *Erya, Shigu*; note: "微 means to flee and hide." Zuozhuan, Ai yr 16: "the Duke of Bai fled to the hills and hanged himself; his followers hid (微) the body"; note: "微 means to hide."
- *To screen (蔽).* *Erya, Shigu*: "to screen is 微." The Jinyu: "Prince Chong'er passed through Cao; Duke Gong, hearing his ribs were [joined like] one bone, spied on him as he bathed, setting up a thin screen (微薄) to watch"; note: "微 means to screen."
- *To diminish (殺).* Liji, *Tangong*: "the rites have a 'diminishing of feeling (微情)'"; subcommentary: "微 means to reduce — when a worthy mourns a parent he is bound to ruin his health, so the rule has him eat after three days, with set numbers for wailing and leaping, to temper his inner grief."
- *To spy out covertly (伺察).* Former Han, *Biography of Guo Jie*: "he had men covertly learn (微知) the brigands' whereabouts"; note: "微 is to spy and inquire."
- *A shin-sore.* *Erya, Shixun*: "a sore on the shin is a 微"; note: "骭 is the shin, 瘍 a sore." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "with both shin-sores (微) and swollen legs."
- *Were it not for (非).* *Yun Hui.* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "it is not (微) that I have no wine."
- *There being no (無).* *Yun Hui.* Liji, *Tangong*: "the famished man of Qi would not eat food offered with a contemptuous 'hey, come eat'; Zengzi said, 'surely not (微與)!'"; note: "微 is like 'there is no.'"
- *A state-name.* Shu, *Mushi*: "the Wei (微), Lu, Peng, and Pu"; tradition: "Wei lay in Ba and Shu."
- *翠微, the kingfisher-haze of a mountain.* *Erya, Shishan*: "not yet reaching the top is the 'kingfisher-haze' (翠微)"; subcommentary: "before the very summit, on the sloping flanks, the mountain air is a pale blue-green, hence 'kingfisher-haze.'"
- *Star-enclosure names: Ziwei, Taiwei, Shaowei.* Jinshu, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "the Purple Tenuity enclosure (紫微垣), fifteen stars, lies by the Dipper; the northern one is Ziwei, the seat of the Heavenly Emperor, the constant abode of the Son of Heaven; Taiwei (太微) is the Son of Heaven's court, the seat of the Five Emperors and the offices of the Twelve Lords; Shaowei (少微), west of Taiwei, is the station of the gentry — when bright, large, and yellow, then worthy gentlemen are raised up."
- *The Three Subtleties (三微).* Later Han, *Annals of Emperor Zhang*: "the Chunqiu, writing 'the king's [month]' for each month of spring, honours the Three Beginnings (三正) and heeds the Three Subtleties (三微)"; note: "the Three Subtleties are the start of the Three Beginnings, when the myriad things are all faint (微) and their colours differ, so kings take their model from them: in the eleventh month the yang *qi* first works below the Yellow Springs and all is red — red being yang, so Zhou made it the Heaven-beginning and favoured red; in the twelfth month things first bud and are white — white being yin, so Yin made it the Earth-beginning and favoured white; in the thirteenth month things sprout and break forth, all black, and man can add his labour, so Xia made it the Man-beginning and favoured black."
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 8: "Wei Hu"; note: "a grandee of Lu." Also Weisheng, a compound surname. The Lunyu: "Weisheng Gao."

**Textual note.** "虞舜側微" is not in the *Shundian* but comes from the Preface to the Shu.
