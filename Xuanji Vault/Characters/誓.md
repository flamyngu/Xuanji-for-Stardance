## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)

> 《康熙字典》〈酉集上〉【言字部】頁1162第01    〔古文〕：𣂶、𣂯【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤時制切，音逝。【說文】約束也。【六書統】从言从折。以言折其罪也。【爾雅·釋言】謹也。【註】所以約勤謹戒衆。【疏】謹，敕也。集將士而戒之曰誓。【釋名】制也，以拘制之也。【正韻】約信也。【書·大禹謨】禹乃會羣后，誓于師。【傳】誓，戒也。軍旅曰誓。【左傳·閔二年】誓軍旅。【註】宣號令也。【禮·曲禮】約信曰誓。【疏】用言辭共相約束，以爲信也。又【文王世子】曲藝皆誓之。【註】戒謹也。　又【玉篇】命也。【周禮·春官·典命】凡諸侯之適子，誓于天子攝其君。【註】誓，猶命也。言誓者，明天子旣命之爲嗣，樹子不易也。　又禮，將祭而號令齊百官，亦謂之誓。【周禮·天官·大宰】祀五帝則掌百官之誓戒。【註】誓戒，要之以𠛬，重失禮也。　又男女私約亦曰誓。【詩·國風】信誓旦旦。　又【尚書】有甘誓、湯誓、泰誓、牧誓、費誓、秦誓，皆篇名。【傳】書有六體，誓其一也。　又人名。【史記·秦始皇紀】高誓，古仙人名。　又【集韻】以制切，音曳。相約以言也。　又食列切，音舌。義同。    考證：〔【周禮·天官·大宰】祀五帝，則掌百官之誓戒。【鄭箋】誓戒，要之𠛬，重失禮也。〕　謹按周禮註不名爲箋，鄭箋謹改註。要之下照原文增以字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1162 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣂶, 𣂯.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 時制 — sounding like 逝 (shì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to bind by covenant (約束)." Liushu tong: "it follows 言 (speech) and 折 (to break): by words to cut off one's guilt." Erya, *Shiyan*: "to be careful, solemn (謹)"; note: "that by which one binds the diligent and solemnly warns the multitude"; subcommentary: "謹 is to admonish; gathering the officers and soldiers and warning them is called 誓 (the oath-charge)." *Shiming*: "to restrain (制): to bind and hold in check." *Zheng Yun*: "a binding pledge (約信)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "Yu then assembled the princes and gave a solemn charge (誓) to the host"; tradition: "誓 is to warn; the charge before an army is called 誓." Zuozhuan, Min yr 2: "he charged (誓) the troops"; note: "proclaiming the orders." Liji, *Quli*: "a binding pledge is called 誓"; subcommentary: "to bind one another by words and make it a pledge." Also *Wenwang shizi*: "in the minor arts too they are charged (誓)"; note: "to warn and admonish."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to command (命)." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Dianming*: "in all cases, a feudal lord's heir-son is charged (誓) by the Son of Heaven and so acts for his ruler"; note: "誓 is like 命; to speak of 誓 makes clear that the Son of Heaven, having appointed him heir, sets up the son and does not change it."
- *Also,* in ritual, the commands marshalling the hundred officials before a sacrifice are likewise called 誓. Zhouli, *Tianguan, Da zai*: "for the sacrifice to the Five Emperors he has charge of the oath-warning (誓戒) of the hundred officials"; note: "the oath-warning binds them under threat of punishment, holding lapses in ritual a grave matter."
- *Also a private pledge between man and woman is called 誓.* Shijing, *Guofeng*: "the faithful vows (信誓), how earnest!"
- *Also,* the Shang shu has the Gan shi, Tang shi, Tai shi, Mu shi, Fei shi, and Qin shi (誓) — all chapter-names; tradition: "the Shu has six genres, the 誓 being one."
- *Also a personal name.* Shiji, *Annals of the First Emperor of Qin*: "Gao Shi (高誓), the name of an ancient immortal."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 以制, sounding like 曳 (yì): to bind one another by words.
- *Also* fanqie 食列, sounding like 舌 (shé); same sense.

**Textual notes.** The Zhouli annotation is not styled "Jian"; 鄭箋 corrected to 註 (note). And after 要之, the graph 以 is added (per the original).
