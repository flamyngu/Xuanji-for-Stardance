## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈未集中〉【糸字部】頁917第06    〔古文〕：𠄤、㝄【廣韻】常倫切【集韻】殊倫切，𠀤音淳。【說文】絲也。【論語】今也純儉，吾從衆。【何晏註】純，絲也。絲易成，故從儉。【前漢·王褒傳】難與道純緜之麗密。　又【易·乾卦】純粹，精也。【疏】純粹，不雜。　又【書·酒誥】嗣爾股肱純。【傳】繼汝股肱之敎，爲純一之行。　又【詩·大雅】純嘏爾常矣。【箋】純，大也。　又【詩·周頌】文王之德之純。【箋】純，亦不已也。　又【周禮·冬官考工記】諸侯純九，大夫純五。【註】純，猶皆也。　又【禮·郊特牲】貴純之道也。【註】純，謂中外皆善。　又【左傳·隱元年】潁考叔，純孝也。【註】純，猶篤也。　又【前漢·地理志】織作冰紈綺繡純麗之物。【註】純，精好也。　又【淮南子·地形訓】里閒九純，純丈五尺。【註】純，量名。　又【集韻】主尹切【韻會】之尹切，𠀤音準。【書·顧命】篾席黼純。【註】白黑雜繒緣之。【儀禮·士冠禮】服纁裳純衣。【註】純衣，緣衣也。又【旣夕】緇純。【註】飾衣曰純。【禮·曲禮】冠衣不純素。【註】純，緣也。　又【集韻】規倫切，音鈞。又朱閏切，音稕。義𠀤同。　又【集韻】徒溫切，音屯。【詩·召南】白茅純束。【傳】純束，猶包之也。【箋】純讀曰屯。【戰國策】錦繡千純。【註】純音屯，束也。　又縣名。【左傳·襄十八年】執孫蒯于純留。【註】純留，縣名。【釋文】純，《地理志》作屯。　又【集韻】杜本切，音盾。義同。　又【集韻】從緣切，音全。【儀禮·鄕射禮】二算爲純。【註】猶全也。【禮·投壷】二算一純。【疏】二算合爲一全。　又【集韻】莊持切。緇或作純。【周禮·地官·媒氏】純帛無過五兩。【註】純實緇字也。古緇以才爲聲。【禮·祭統】以共純服。【疏】凡言純者，其義有二：一糸旁才是古之緇字，二是糸旁屯是純字。但書文相亂，雖是緇字，𠀤皆作純。鄭氏所註，於絲理可知，於色不明者，卽讀爲緇。【史記·五帝本紀】黃收純衣。【註】索隱曰：純讀曰緇。又【集韻】朱倫切，音諄。諄或作純。　又【集韻】船倫切，音脣。門名。《春秋傳》有純門。    考證：〔又朱聞切，音分。〕　謹按朱聞切不得音分，今据集韻改朱閏切，音稕。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Silk radical 糸; p.917 #06)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠄤, 㝄.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 常倫. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 殊倫 — sounding like 淳 (chún).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Silk (絲)." Lunyu: "now plain silk (純) is thrifty, and I follow the crowd"; He Yan's note: "純 is silk; silk is easily made, hence thrifty." Former Han, *Biography of Wang Bao*: "hard to compare with the close-woven fineness of pure floss (純緜)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "純粹 (pure and unmixed) is its essence"; subcommentary: "純粹 means unmixed."
- *Also* Shu, *Jiu gao*: "to carry on your limbs' pure work (純)"; tradition: "to carry on the teaching of your limbs, in conduct pure and single (純一)."
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "great blessing (純嘏) be ever yours"; Jian: "純 means great."
- *Also* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "the purity (純) of King Wen's virtue"; Jian: "純 too means unceasing."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Kao gong ji*: "the feudal lords all nine (純九), the grandees all five (純五)"; note: "純 is like all (皆)."
- *Also* Liji, *Jiao te sheng*: "the way of prizing the wholly good (純)"; note: "純 means good both within and without."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Yin yr 1: "Ying Kaoshu was purely filial (純孝)"; note: "純 is like earnest (篤)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "weaving ice-white silk and damask and embroidery, of pure and fine (純麗) things"; note: "純 means choice and fine."
- *Also* Huainanzi, *Di xing*: "in the hamlets, nine bolts (九純), a 純 being one zhang five feet"; note: "純 is a measure-name."

**Further readings.**
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 主尹. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 之尹 — sounding like 準 (zhǔn). Shu, *Gu ming*: "the bamboo mat with axe-patterned border (黼純)"; note: "edged with black-and-white mixed silk." Yili, *Shi guan li*: "wearing the pink skirt with bordered robe (純衣)"; note: "純衣 is a bordered robe (緣衣)." Also *Ji xi*: "a black border (緇純)"; note: "to trim a robe is 純." Liji, *Qu li*: "the cap and robe are not bordered (純) with plain"; note: "純 means a border (緣)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 規倫, sounding like 鈞 (jūn). Also fanqie 朱閏, sounding like 稕 (zhùn). The sense in both the same.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 徒溫, sounding like 屯 (tún). Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "the white-thatch bound up (純束)"; tradition: "純束 is like to wrap it"; Jian: "純 read as 屯." Zhanguo ce: "a thousand bolts (千純) of brocade and embroidery"; note: "純 sounded 屯, a bundle." Also a county-name. Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 18: "they seized Sun Kuai at Chunliu (純留)"; note: "Chunliu is a county-name"; *Shiwen*: "純, in the *Treatise on Geography* written 屯."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 杜本, sounding like 盾 (dùn). Same sense.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 從緣, sounding like 全 (quán). Yili, *Xiang she li*: "two counters are a 純"; note: "like a whole (全)." Liji, *Tou hu*: "two counters are one 純"; subcommentary: "two counters joined make one whole."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 莊持. 緇 is sometimes written 純. Zhouli, *Di guan, Mei shi*: "the black silk (純帛) not exceeding five bolts"; note: "純 is really the graph 緇; anciently 緇 took 才 as phonetic." Liji, *Ji tong*: "to supply the black robe (純服)"; subcommentary: "in all uses of 純 there are two senses: one, with 糸 beside 才, is the old graph 緇; two, with 糸 beside 屯, is the graph 純. But the texts are confused, so even where it is 緇, it is everywhere written 純. Where Zheng's note can tell from the silk-sense but not the colour-sense, it is read as 緇." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "the yellow cap and black robe (純衣)"; note: Suoyin, "純 read as 緇." Also *Ji Yun*: fanqie 朱倫, sounding like 諄 (zhūn). 諄 is sometimes written 純.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 船倫, sounding like 脣 (chún). A gate-name. The *Chunqiu zhuan* has the Chun Gate (純門).

**Textual note.** "Also fanqie 朱聞, sounding like 分" — a note: fanqie 朱聞 cannot be sounded 分; following the *Ji Yun* it is corrected to fanqie 朱閏, sounding like 稕 (zhùn).
