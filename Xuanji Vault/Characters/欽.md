**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【欠字部】頁568第21    〔古文〕：𣣽【廣韻】去金切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】祛音切，𠀤音衾。【說文】欠貌。一曰敬也。【書·堯典】欽明文思安安。又【太甲】欽厥止。　又【正字通】今御音曰欽敕，御使曰欽命，俗曰欽差，皆取敬意。　又欽欽，思望意。【詩·秦風】憂心欽欽。【毛傳】思望之，心中欽欽然也。　又鐘聲有節。【詩·小雅】鼓鐘欽欽。　又諡法。【汲冢周書】威儀悉備曰欽。　又州名。亦江名。【韻會】梁安州，隋改欽州，取欽江名。【廣輿記】屬廣東廉州府。州有欽江，有龍門江。　又山名。【山海經】欽山多金玉而無石，師水出焉。又欽口。【前漢·地理志】魏郡武安縣欽口山，白渠水所出。　又欽䲹、欽原，俱鳥名。見【山海經】。　又姓。【何氏姓苑】吳人也。宋有欽德載，自號壽巖老人。　又去聲。按也。【李翊·俗呼小錄】按謂之欽。欽，去聲。【王琚·射經】欽身微曲，註目視的。又：開弓發矢，要欽身弝外，分明認帖。　又【集韻】魚音切，音唫。與吟同。呻也。【山海經】剛山多神𩳁，其音如欽。【郭璞註】欽亦吟字假音。○按《漢志》內曰恭，外曰欽。又鄭康成云：敬事節用謂之欽。欽本兼內外言，不得專屬外也。卽敬事節用，亦於欽義未全，故不入正訓，附記於此。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Yawn radical 欠; p.568 #21)*

**Ancient graph.** 𣣽.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 去金. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 祛音 — sounding like 衾 (qīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the look of a yawn; one says, reverence (敬)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "reverent (欽), enlightened, accomplished, and thoughtful, calm and gentle." Also *Tai jia*: "be reverent (欽) of where you rest."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "today an imperial pronouncement is called 欽敕 (the august edict), an imperial envoy 欽命 (the august command), commonly 欽差 (the imperially commissioned) — all taking the sense of reverence."
- *Also 欽欽, the sense of yearning hope.* Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "the grieving heart, full of longing (欽欽)"; Mao's tradition: "longing for it, the heart within is 欽欽."
- *Also the bells' measured ring.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the bells are struck, ringing in measure (欽欽)."
- *Also a rule for posthumous titles.* Jizhong Zhou shu: "majesty and decorum complete in all is called 欽."
- *Also a prefecture-name, also a river-name.* *Yun Hui*: "the Liang's Anzhou; the Sui changed it to Qinzhou (欽州), taking the name of the Qin River (欽江)." Guangyu ji: "it belongs to Lianzhou prefecture in Guangdong; the prefecture has the Qin River and the Longmen River."
- *Also a mountain-name.* Shanhai jing: "Mount Qin (欽山) has much gold and jade but no stone; the Shi waters issue from it." Also 欽口 (Qinkou). Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Mount Qinkou (欽口山) in Wu'an county of Wei commandery, whence the Baiqu waters issue."
- *Also Qinpi (欽䲹) and Qinyuan (欽原), both bird-names*; see the Shanhai jing.
- *Also a surname.* He's *Xingyuan*: "men of Wu." The Song had Qin Dezai (欽德載), who styled himself the Old Man of Shouyan.
- *Also in the departing tone: to press down (按).* Li Yi, *Suhu xiaolu*: "to press down is called 欽; 欽 is in the departing tone." Wang Ju, *Shejing*: "press down (欽) the body, slightly bent, and fix the eye on the target." Also: "in drawing the bow and loosing the arrow, one must press the body (欽身) outside the grip, and clearly note the mark."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 魚音, sounding like 唫 (yín); the same as 吟: to groan (呻). Shanhai jing: "Mount Gang has many spirit-chi (神𩳁), whose sound is like 欽"; Guo Pu's note: "欽 is also a borrowed sound for the graph 吟."

**Editorial notes.** A note: the Han *Treatise* says "within is 恭 (respect), without is 欽 (reverence)." And Zheng Kangcheng says, "to be reverent in affairs and sparing in use is called 欽." But 欽 originally embraces both inner and outer, and cannot be assigned to the outer alone; nor does "reverent in affairs, sparing in use" fully cover its sense — so it is not entered as the proper gloss, but appended here.
