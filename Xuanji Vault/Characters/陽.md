**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Mound radical 阜; p.1355 #15)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𨼘, 𨽐, 𣆄.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 與章. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 余章. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 移章 — sounding like 羊 (yáng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "that which works Heaven's task and lights the myriad things is called the yang (陽)." *Shuowen*: "high and bright (高明)."
- *The sun.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "heavy the dew — but for the sun (陽) it dries not"; tradition: "陽 is the sun." Zuozhuan, Wen yr 4: "the Son of Heaven faces the sun (陽)." Liji, *Jiyi*: "the Yin people sacrificed at the yang (陽)"; note: "陽 means midday." Mengzi: "bleached in the autumn sun (秋陽)."
- *重陽, the ninefold heaven.* Chuci, *Yuanyou*: "I gather at the ninth heaven (重陽) and enter the Emperor's palace"; note: "massed yang is Heaven; Heaven has nine layers, hence 'ninefold yang.'"
- *A pair.* *Yupian.*
- *A month-name.* Erya, *Shitian*: "the tenth month is 陽." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the year too is at 陽."
- *靑陽, spring.* Erya, *Shitian*; note: "the air clear, warm, and yang."
- *昭陽, the year of 癸.* Erya, *Shitian*: "when the Grand Year is in 癸, the year is Zhaoyang (昭陽)."
- *Festivals.* The fifth of the fifth month is the Duanyang (端陽), the ninth of the ninth the Chongyang (重陽); see the Yueling guangyi.
- *夕陽 / 朝陽, the western and eastern slopes.* Erya, *Shishan*: "west of a hill is the 'evening-sun' (夕陽), east of it the 'morning-sun' (朝陽)." Shijing, *Daya*: "he measured its western slope (夕陽)"; and: "the *wutong* grows, on that eastern slope (朝陽)."
- *The north bank of a river.* Shijing, *Daya*: "on the north bank (陽) of the Qia." Guliang zhuan, Xi yr 28: "the north of a river is 陽."
- *陽丘, a high mound.* *Shiming*: "its bulk being high and near the sun."
- *Bright (明).* Shijing, *Binfeng*: "my red is very bright (陽)"; tradition: "陽 means bright." *Yupian*: "clear."
- *陽陽, figured / carefree.* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "the dragon-banners gleaming (陽陽)"; tradition: "having figured pattern." Shijing, *Wangfeng*: "the gentleman, carefree (陽陽)"; tradition: "with no care on his mind."
- *陽禮, the rites of archery and feasting.* Zhouli, *Diguan, Da situ*: "by the yang-rites (陽禮) he teaches deference, and the people do not contend"; note: "the rites of the district archery and the drinking-feast."
- *A state-name.* Chunqiu, Min yr 2: "the men of Qi removed [the people of] Yang (陽)"; note: "Yang, a state-name." Zhanguo ce: "to caulk a leaking boat yet make light of the waves of Lord Yang (陽侯)"; note: the Bowu zhi: "the Marquis of the state of Yang of Jin drowned, and became the god of the great sea." Chunqiu, Zhao yr 12: "Gao Yan of Qi installed the Earl of Northern Yan at Yang (陽)"; note: "Yang is Tang, a Yan dependency."
- *A county-name.* Shiji, *Annals of Gaozu*: "westward he passed Gaoyang (高陽)"; note: "in Chenliu."
- *A pass-name.* Former Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "seven thousand eight hundred and two *li* from the Yang Pass (陽關)."
- *陽州, a place-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 24: "the duke fled to Qi and halted at Yangzhou (陽州)."
- *陽城, a mountain-name (under Han a county, in Yingchuan).*
- *An ancient emperor's title.* Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "Emperor Zhuanxu of the Gaoyang clan (高陽氏)."
- *The 'standing-man' radical.* *Shiming*: "[立人] images a man standing; some call it the 'yang-gate' (陽門) — in front it is 陽, the two sides like a gate."
- *To wound.* *Yupian.*
- *泆陽, a demon.* Zhuangzi, *Dasheng*: "in the lower northwest dwells the Yiyang (泆陽)"; note: "a demon's name."
- *雲陽, a speaking-tree sprite.* Baopuzi, *Dengshe*: "a tree in the hills that can speak like a man — it is not the tree but its sprite, named Yunyang (雲陽)."
- *昌陽, another name for the sweet-flag.* Han Yu, *Jinxue jie*: "sweet-flag (昌陽) prolongs the years."
- *A surname.* Shiji, Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "Master Yang (陽子) rode as outrider"; note: "the ancient immortal Yang Ling." *Guangyun*: "King Jing of Zhou fiefed his youngest son at Yangfan, whose descendants took the place as surname." The Han had twenty-two compound surnames: Ouyang, Gaoyang, Qingyang, Sunyang, Ziyang, Zhouyang, Jingyang, Biyang, Gengyang, Xiyang, Guiyang, Yeyang, Lingyang, Xianyang, Liyang, Puyang, Taiyang, Laoyang, Anyang, Chengyang, Zhuyang, Suoyang.
- *Interchangeable with 揚.* Liji, *Yuzao*: "fill the breath, swell and breathe forth warmth (揚休)"; note: "顚 is read 闐; 揚 is read 陽 — to fill the body's *qi*, making the breath swell, like the yang *qi* giving body to things." *Shiming*: "陽 is 揚 (to raise): the *qi* is without, sent forth and rising."
- *Same as 佯 (to feign).* Liji, *Tangong*: "he feigned (陽) to approve it." Former Han, *Annals of Gaozu*: "he feignedly (陽) honoured King Huai as Righteous Emperor, but in truth heeded not his commands."
- *Sounding like 腸 (cháng).* Erya, *Shigu*: "陽 means 'I, me' (予)"; note: "the Lu Shi has 'me (陽), what of it?'; the men of Ba and Pu today call themselves 'A-yang'"; subcommentary: "Master Shen took 陽 as 'me,' hence he cited it"; *Shiwen*: "陽 sounds 腸." (*Wuyin jiyun*: "vulgarly 𨹈"; *Zihui*: "vulgarly 阦.")
