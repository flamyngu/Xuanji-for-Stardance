**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Feather radical 羽; p.959 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於計. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 壹計 — sounding like 殪 (yì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a flowery canopy (華蓋)." *Guangyun*: "a feather-plume canopy (羽葆)." Note to the Jijiu pian: "翳 means any bird-feather that can screen one; the feather-fan (羽翿) the dancer holds to screen himself was thence named 翳; some say it is the flowery canopy — the pheasant-tail fan of today is its surviving likeness."
- *A screen.* The Zhouyu: "this strips away their store and screens off (翳) their people"; note: "翳 is like 'to screen'; some say 'to destroy.'"
- *To cover, bar, shade.* Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "翳 means to cover"; note: "to cover over." *Guangya*: "翳 means to bar." *Guangyun*: "to hide; to screen." *Leipian*: "to shade."
- *Dead and fallen [of a tree].* Shijing, *Daya*: "the standing-dead and the fallen-dead (其菑其翳)"; tradition: "a tree dead but standing is 菑; one dead of itself [and fallen] is 翳."
- *The Rain-Master (荓翳 / 屏翳).* *Guangyun.* Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "he summoned Pingyi (屏翳)"; note: "the Thunder-Master."
- *A bird like the phoenix.* *Yupian.* Shanhaijing: "within the North Sea is a five-coloured bird whose flight darkens a whole district, named the *yi*-bird (翳鳥)"; note: "of the phoenix kind." Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "I yoke jade hornless-dragons and ride the *yi* [plumed canopy] (翳)."
- *媒翳, the shooting-blind.* Pan Yue, preface to the *Pheasant-Shooting fu*: "he practised the art of the decoy and blind (媒翳)"; note: "the *meiyi* is the blind one hides behind to shoot."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 烏奚; *Ji Yun*: fanqie 煙奚 — sounding like 鷖 (yī); same sense.

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 一結 / 於歇, sounding like 謁 (yè).* Zuo Si, *Weidu fu*: "peach and plum cast their shade (䕃翳)" — rhyming, sounding like 咽.

**Textual note.** The Shanhaijing line "名曰翳焉" should read "名曰翳鳥.
