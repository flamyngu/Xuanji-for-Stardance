**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, middle; Fire radical 火; p.675 #12)*

**Ancient graph.** 𤓉.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 如延, sounding like 䔳 (rán).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To burn (燒)." Note: Xu Xuan, "the common writing 燃 is a later addition." Mengzi: "like fire just beginning to burn (然)." Guanzi, *Dizi zhi*: "leave space between the brands, and let the burning ones (然者) lie below"; Liu Ji: "anciently bundled firewood-brands made torches; the brand (蒸) is fine wood; loosen the bundle a little so each can hold one brand, to let the fire's breath through; and let the already-lit lie below and the unlit above, so the fire burns easily." *Ji Yun*: interchangeable with 䕼.

**Senses.**
- *To assent; just so; a word of response (許, 如是, 譍言).* *Yupian*. Shiji, *Biographies of Zhang Er and Chen Yu*: "this is indeed how Zhao set up its name and righteousness, not violating, as men of their word (然諾)."
- *A particle (語助).* *Guangyun*. Liji, *Tan gong*: "in a drought, Duke Mu summoned Xianzi and asked thus (然)"; note: "然 is the word 焉."
- *Like, as (如).* *Guangyun*. Shijing, *Bei feng*: "kindly (惠然) willing to come." Liji, *Tan gong*: "blindly, blunderingly (貿貿然) he came."
- *A word carrying on above and joining below.* Liji, *Qu li*: "then (然後) the guest sits." Also *Tan gong*: "then (然則) why not go?" *Wen wang shizi*: "and so (然而) the multitude knew the way of father and son."
- *Also* Liji, *Ji yi*: "the people of the state praised and wished, saying thus (然): 'happy he, to have a son like this!'"; note: "然 is like 而 (and)."
- *果然 (guoran), a beast-name.* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Jin che*: "然, lacquered and adorned"; note: "然 is the guoran (果然)." A note: the *Piya* writes 𤡮; see in full under the graph 𤡮 in the Dog radical.
- *率然 (shuairan), a snake-name.* Sunzi, *Jiu di*: "the shuairan (率然) is the snake of Mount Chang; strike its head, the tail comes; strike its tail, the head comes; strike its middle, both head and tail come."
- *連然 (Lianran), a county-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Lianran (連然) in Yizhou commandery has a salt-office."
- *燕然 (Yanran), a mountain-name.* Former Han, *Account of the Xiongnu*: "reaching the Suyewu and the Yanran Mountains (燕然山)."
- *Surname.* The Zuozhuan: the Chu Randan (然丹), the Zheng Ranming (然明).

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 人 (rén), fanqie 而鄰.* Liu Xiang, *Lienü zan*: "the Qi maiden's matron warded the maiden before all came to pass (未然); Lady Jiang too was apt, and could in the end mend herself." Ma Rong, *Dongxun song*: "the scattered fast being done, on the morrow at the good hour, the oak and the brushwood heaped high, the fierce fire blazes and burns (燔然)."

**Textual note.** Guanzi, *Dizi zhi*, the note ascribed to "Zhao Yongxian" — a note: in the Guanzi, the head-marked notes are all the words of Liu Ji, not Zhao Yongxian; following the editorial rule, "Zhao Yongxian said" is corrected to "the text of Liu Ji." Also "let the already-lit lie above and the unlit below, so the fire burns easily" — by the sense, since fire flames upward one lets the lit lie below and the unlit above; the words are corrected accordingly.
