**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Child radical 子; p.278 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 呼敎. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許敎 — the departing tone of 嘐 (xiào).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "one who serves his parents well." It follows an abbreviated 老 (aged) and 子 (child) — the child supporting the aged.
- *Filial piety.* Xiaojing: "filial piety (孝) is the constancy of Heaven, the right of Earth, the conduct of the people." Shu, *Yao dian*: "able to bring harmony through filial piety (孝)." Liji, *Jitong*: "filial piety (孝) is nurture; to follow the Way and not go against the bonds of kinship is called nurture."
- *also.* Liji, *Jiyi*: "Zengzi said: to live without gravity is unfilial (非孝); to serve one's lord without loyalty is unfilial; to hold office without reverence is unfilial; to be faithless with friends is unfilial; to lack courage in battle is unfilial. If these five are unfulfilled, the harm reaches one's parents — dare one not be reverent?"
- *Posthumous-title rules.* "To bring peace to the five lineages; to be kind, loving, and devoted to kin; to hold to virtue unswerving; to harmonize the seasons and inaugurate the sacrifices; to be great in counsel and measured in conduct — all are called 孝."
- *A surname.* Fengsu tong: "descendants of Duke Xiao of Qin (秦孝公)."
- *Rhymes as 臭 (xiù), fanqie 許救.* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "ah, my august father, through endless ages able to be filial (孝)" — rhyming with 疚 and 造 above.
- *Rhymes as 旭 (xù), fanqie 許六.* Shijing, *Daya*: "not hastening his own desires, but following on to render filial duty (孝)" — rhyming with 淢 above, 淢 sounding like 侐.
