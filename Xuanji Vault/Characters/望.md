**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Moon radical 月; p.505 #29)*

**Ancient graph.** 𦣠.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 巫放. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 無放 — sounding like 𧧄 (wàng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Gone abroad, away outside, one looks for (望) his return; it follows 亡 (gone) with an abbreviated 朢 as phonetic." *Shiming*: "望 means at a loss (惘); gazing afar, dim and lost." Shijing, *Bei feng*: "I gaze (望) but cannot reach."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "fair fame and fair regard (令望)"; subcommentary: "one whom others look up to (觀望)."
- *Also* Mengzi: "in disappointment (望望然) he went off"; Zhao Qi's note: "a look of shame"; Zhu Xi's tradition: "a look of going off without looking back."
- *Also* Boya: "to watch (覗)." *Yun Hui*: "one whom others look up to is called 望." Also to expect of (責望); also to resent (怨望).
- *A sacrifice-name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "the 望-sacrifice to mountains and rivers"; tradition: "all at one time given the distant-sacrifice (望祭)." Gongyang zhuan, Xi yr 31: "what is 望? the distant-sacrifice (望祭)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 武方. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 無方 — sounding like 亡 (wáng). Same sense. Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "what ten thousand men look to (萬夫所望)"; *Shiwen*: "by rhyme-harmony sounded 亡."
- *Also* *Shiming*: "a name for the moon at the full. The great month on the sixteenth, the small on the fifteenth: the sun in the east, the moon in the west, far off, gazing across (望)." Yijing, *Xiao xu*: "the moon near full (幾望)." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 3, subcommentary: "the moon's body has no light; it waits on the sun's shining to give light; half-lit it is the crescent (弦), fully lit it becomes the full (望)." *Yun Hui*: "it follows 壬, corruptly written with 王. The *Shuowen* writes the 'full of sun and moon' as 朢, and the 'gazing' 望 as 望; now both are written 望, and the old graph's sense is lost."
