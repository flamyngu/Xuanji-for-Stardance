**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Cliff radical 厂; p.163 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 力制. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 力霽 — sounding like 例 (lì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a hard whetstone (旱石). It follows 厂, with 蠆 abbreviated as phonetic." Xu [Kai] says: "旱石 is a coarse, hard stone." *Yupian*: "a grinding-stone (磨石)." Shijing, *Daya*: "take the whetstone (厲), take the forge-hammer."
- *Also, to grind, whet (磨).* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 16: "they foddered the horses and whetted the weapons (厲兵)." Xunzi, *Xing'e*: "a dull blade must await grinding and whetting (礱厲) before it is sharp."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "stern (嚴)." Lunyu: "to hear his speech, it is stern (厲)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "fierce (烈); severe (猛)." Liji, *Biao ji*: "without being harsh (厲), he is awe-inspiring."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "perilous (危)." Yijing, *Qian*: "perilous (厲), yet without blame."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "厲 means to stir up, set going (作)"; note: "the Guliang zhuan says, 'they first struck up (厲) the music'"; subcommentary: "to raise up and set going." Fangyan: "厲 and 𠨐 mean to do (爲): in Ou and Yue it is 𠨐, in Wu it is 厲."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to oppress (虐)." Mengzi: "he oppresses (厲) the people to nourish himself."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "the upper part (上)." Shijing, *Weifeng*: "on that bank of the Qi (淇厲)." *Yun Hui*: "a steep, dangerous place on a bank is called 厲."
- *Also* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "if it is deep, wade in your clothes (厲)"; note: "to ford water clothed, the water reaching from the girdle up, is called 厲."
- *Also* note to Zhouli, *Qiuguan, Sili*: "to transgress the government and do evil is called 厲."
- *Also* Erya, *Shitian*: "when the moon stands in xu (戌) it is called 厲."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "ugly, ill-favored (醜惡)." Zhuangzi, *Tiandi*: "an ugly (厲) man, when at midnight a son is born to him, fears the child may resemble himself."
- *Also* Shiji, *Biography of Yan An*: "the people did not die young of pestilence (夭厲)"; note: "厲 means sickness (病)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Biographies of the Confucians*: "thereby to spur on (厲) the worthy and talented"; note: Yan Shigu says: "厲 means to exhort and urge on."
- *Also* the *Biography of Xifu Gong*: "the hawk and falcon dart fiercely across (橫厲)"; note: Yan Shigu says: "厲 means to fly swiftly."
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: 厲鬼 (a malignant ghost). Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 7: "Zichan said, 'when a ghost has somewhere to return to, it does not become a malignant spirit (厲).'"
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: 鞶厲, the doubled hanging of a belt. Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "the belt-ends (鞶厲), the pennon-fringes, and the cap-strings."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Han had Li Wen (厲溫), prefect of Wei commandery."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 落蓋, sounding like 賴 (lài). Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Lai-town (厲鄕) was the old state of Lai (厲國)"; note: Yan Shigu says: "厲 is read 賴." Also Shiji, *Biography of Fan Ju*: "he lacquered his body to feign leprosy (厲)"; note: "厲 is pronounced 賴; it means coating the body with lacquer so that sores break out as the disease leprosy (癩)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie 力孽; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 良薛 — sounding like 列 (liè). *Yun Hui*: "stern (嚴); one says, the hanging ornament of a pouch." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the grief of my heart is as if it were knotted; the governance of this present time — how harsh (厲) it is!" Zuo Si, *Shudu fu*: "the Ba maidens pluck the strings, the Han girls beat the measure; raising the western air on the tightened frets, they sing the clear-soaring (𩙂厲) song of the river"; note: "𩙂厲 is a song-sound clear and far-carrying."

**Textual notes.** In the Yijing, *Qian* citation, 旡 should read 无 (per the original).
