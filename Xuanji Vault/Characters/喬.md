**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.200 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 巨嬌. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 渠嬌 — sounding like 僑 (qiáo).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "High and curved (高而曲). It follows 夭 (bent) and an abbreviated 高 (high)." Erya, *Shi mu*: "hooked like feathers is 喬"; note: "the tree's branches curl curved, like a bird's plumage." Also: "curving downward is 朻, curving upward is 喬, as the catalpa-tree is called 喬." Also: "small branches twining upward is 喬." Shu, *Yu gong*: "its trees are tall (喬)"; tradition: "喬 means high." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "in the south is a tall tree (喬木)"; tradition: "喬 means rising up."

**Senses.**
- *The upcurved end of a spear is called 喬.* Shijing, *Zheng feng*: "two spears with double curves (重喬)"; tradition: "重喬 is a layered burden"; Jian: "喬 is the spear-shaft, near the top and the head-band, where the plumes are hung."
- *Surname.* Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "Qiao Hu, Grand Administrator of Yunzhong."
- *A personal name.* Zhanguo ce: "generation upon generation styled themselves orphan-princes, yet had the longevity of Qiao and Song (喬松)"; note: "喬 is Prince Qiao (王子喬); 松 is Red-Pine (赤松)."
- *Interchangeable with 橋.* Shijing, *Zhou nan*, *Shiwen* on "喬木": "also written 橋喬."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 舉橋. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 居妖 — sounding like 驕 (jiāo). Shu, *Yu gong*, *Shiwen* on "惟喬": "喬, Xu's reading 驕." Also Shijing, *Zheng feng*, *Shiwen* on "重喬": "喬, fanqie 居橋."
- *Interchangeable with 驕 (proud).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the music of Qi is arrogant, perverse, and proud in spirit (喬志)"; *Shiwen*: "喬 sounded 驕; one text writes 驕." Also *Biao ji*: "proud (喬) and boorish."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 丘祅, sounding like 蹺 (qiāo). 喬詰, a mind ill at ease. Zhuangzi, *Zai you*: "the world first grew fretful and fierce (喬詰卓鷙)"; *Shiwen*: "喬, fanqie 欽消; some say fanqie 去夭; Guo's reading 矯; Li's reading 驕."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 舉夭, sounding like 矯 (jiǎo). Also fanqie 祛矯, sounding like 槁 (gǎo). The sense in both the same.
- *Also* fanqie 渠廟, sounding like 轎 (jiào). A tree-branch curving upward.

**Textual note.** Zhanguo ce, "the age styled themselves (世之) orphan-princes" — checked against the original, "世之" should read "世世" (generation upon generation).
