**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Inch radical 寸; p.295 #12)*

**Ancient graph.** 𡬻.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 徐林. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徐心 — sounding like 潯 (xún).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To draw out and order (繹理); properly written 𢒫. It follows 工 and 口, and 又 (hand) and 寸 (inch); 工 and 口 mean disorder, and 又 and 寸 are to part and order it; with 彡 as phonetic." *Zengyun*: "to seek (求)." Former Han, *Biography of Huang Ba*: "in the course of talk he drew it out (尋繹)"; note: "to draw out and bring forth."

**Senses.**
- *A measure-name.* Zhouli, *Di guan, Mei shi*, note: "eight feet are a 尋, twice a 尋 a 常." *Xiao Erya*: "four feet are a 仞, twice a 仞 a 尋." Mengzi: "to bend a foot and straighten a 尋 — it might seem worth doing."
- *To continue, follow on (仍, 繼).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 1: "daily plying shield and spear (尋干戈) in mutual war." An old saying: "if the tip is not nipped, one will in time need the axe-haft (尋斧柯)."
- *Presently, soon (俄).* Jin, Yang Hu, *Memorial declining the Open Office*: "to err and so disgrace Your Majesty's high station — its overthrow too will presently (尋) come."
- *To use (用).* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 5: "they were about to make use of (尋) the army"; note: "尋 means to use."
- *侵尋, like 浸淫 (creeping on by degrees).* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he toured the commanderies and counties, creeping on (侵尋) to Mount Tai."
- *Borrowed for 溫燖 (to warm over).* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 12: "Wu sent men to ask for a renewal (尋) of the covenant; Zigong said: 'if it can be warmed over (尋), it can also be let go cold'"; note: "尋 means to redouble, to warm; the former covenant being gone cold, to warm it again and make it hot — the same sense as 燖."
- *Long (長).* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "from the passes westward, between Qin, Jin, Liang, and Yi, whatever is long is called 尋."
- *Commonly the ordinary is called 尋常.*
- *Surname.* The Jin Xun Hui; the Tang general Xun Xiang under Liu Heita. *Yun Hui*: "Mr. Mao says: it follows 口; the common writing 𡬶 with 几 is wrong."
