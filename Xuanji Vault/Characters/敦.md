**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Rap radical 攴; p.473 #08)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢼪, 𠧈.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 都昆, sounding like 墩 (dūn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** Written 𣀦. "To be angry (怒); to revile (詆). One account: to challenge, 'who goes there?' (誰何)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Wujing wenzi*: "敦 means thick, generous (厚)." Yijing, *Lin* hexagram: "generous oversight, auspicious (敦臨吉)"; subcommentary: "generous (厚)."
- *Also* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "the king's affairs press hard on me (敦我)"; *Shiwen*: "the *Han shi* says: 敦 means to press (迫); Zheng reads fanqie 都回, like to hurl (投擲)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi gu*: "敦 means to encourage (勉)"; subcommentary: "敦 is to urge one another in earnest." Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "he urged on (敦) the host of spirits to be his road-guides"; note: "Yan Shigu says: 敦 means to encourage."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year standing in 午 is called Dunzang (敦牂)"; the *Shiji, Calendar* note: "敦 means abundant (盛)."
- *Also* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "敦 means great (大)."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "Dunqia (敦洽), an ugly man of Wei."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 都回, sounding like 堆 (duī). Shijing, *Bin feng*: "lonely he sleeps apart (敦彼獨宿)"; tradition: "敦敦, lonely and apart in sleep."
- *Also* Shijing, *Lu song*: "he set in order (敦) the host of Shang"; Jian: "敦 means to put in order (治)."
- *Also* Zhuangzi, *Shuo jian*: "today let me try the men at sword-play (敦劒)"; note: "敦 means to cut (斷)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 度官. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒官 — sounding like 團 (tuán). Shijing, *Bin feng*: "there hangs the bitter gourd, clustered (有敦瓜苦)"; tradition: "敦 is like clustered close (專專)"; subcommentary: "敦 is the look of a gourd clinging to its vine — the gourd clinging to the vine, clustered together"; *Shiwen*: "fanqie 徒丹." Zhu Xi's tradition: "sounded 堆."
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "clustered are those wayside reeds (敦彼行葦)"; tradition: "a look of gathering (聚貌)"; *Shiwen*: "fanqie 徒端."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 徒渾, sounding like 屯 (tún). Shijing, *Daya*: "he spread his array on the Huai's banks (鋪敦淮濆)"; Jian: "it should be written 屯." Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "he massed ten thousand horse in the central camp (敦萬騎)"; note: "敦 is the same as 屯, to array (𨻰)."
- *Also* *Leipian*: Dunhuang (敦煌), a commandery-name.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 丁聊, sounding like 雕 (diāo). Shijing, *Daya*: "the painted bow is firm (敦弓旣堅)"; tradition: "敦弓 is a painted bow; the Son of Heaven's painted bow, 敦 sounded 彫"; subcommentary: "敦 and 彫 are the old and modern forms."
- *Also* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "carved and polished is his host (敦琢其旅)"; subcommentary: "敦 and 雕 are the old and modern graphs." Zhu Xi's tradition: "sounded 堆."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 都內, sounding like 對 (duì). Yili, *Shi hun li*: "the four millet-vessels (四敦) all covered." Liji, *Ming tang wei*: "the two dui (敦) of the Yu clan"; note: "敦 is sounded 對, a millet-vessel"; subcommentary: "敦 ranks with the hu, lian, fu, and gui as square vessels, hence called a millet-vessel."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Tian guan, Yu fu*: "if the feudal lords be assembled, then he furnishes the pearl-basin and the jade-dui (玉敦)"; note: "敦 is a kind of basin; anciently they held blood in a basin and food in a dui."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 大到, sounding like 道 (dào). 幬 is sometimes written 敦; to cover (覆). Zhouli, *Chun guan, Si jiyan*: "to each dui (敦), one stool"; note: "敦 is read as 燾, to cover."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 杜皓, sounding like 稻 (dào). Also fanqie 𨻰留, sounding like 儔 (chóu). The sense in both the same.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 杜本, sounding like 盾 (dùn). Zuozhuan, Wen yr 18: "the people of the world called him Hundun (渾敦)"; note: "it means Huandou, Hundun, a look of being not open and clear"; subcommentary: "混沌 and 渾敦 differ only in graph."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 都困. Used for 頓. Erya, *Shi qiu*: "a hill of one tier is a dun-hill (敦丘)"; subcommentary: "the Shijing, *Wei feng*, 'Meng,' 'as far as Dunqiu (頓丘),' is this."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year standing in 子 is called Kundun (困敦)"; note: "敦 is sounded 頓"; the *Shiji, Calendar* note: "困敦 is Hundun (chaos)."
- *Also* Zhuangzi, *Lie Yukou*: "he planted his staff and pressed it (敦杖)"; the *Yinyi*: "敦 is sounded 頓; Sima says: to stand upright (豎)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 主尹, sounding like 準 (zhǔn). 淳 is sometimes written 敦. Zhouli, *Tian guan, Nei zai*: "he issues their measures and standards of bolt-width (淳制)"; note: "the old text writes 淳 as 敦; Du Zichun reads 敦 as 純; 純 means the breadth of the cloth."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 他昆. 憞 is sometimes abbreviated to 敦. 憞 means a resentful heart, unenlightened.
- *Also* *Yunbu*: rhymes, fanqie 都鈞. Cui Yuan, *Eulogy on the Nanyang school*: "our state being pure, our customs being generous (敦)."
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie 亭年. Su Shi, *Sacrificial text for the Lady of Tong'an*: "to be brothers in succession, none so worthy as you; her wifely duties complete, her motherly bearing most generous (敦)."

**Textual note.** Zhouli, *Tian guan, Wang fu* — checked against the original, 王府 is corrected to 玉府 (Yu fu).
