**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Rain radical 雨; p.1372 #05)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 王分. *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 于分 — sounding like 云 (yún).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The breath of mountains and streams (山川气)." It follows 雨 (rain) and 云, picturing the curling, turning shape of cloud. *Guangyun*: "the *Hetu* says: cloud is the root of Heaven and Earth." *Yuanmingbao*: "yin and yang gathered make cloud (雲)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "the clouds move and the rain falls (雲行雨施)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "lofty is that Cloud-river (雲漢)"; tradition: "the Cloud-river (雲漢) is the Sky-river (the Milky Way)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi qin*: "the son of a great-great-grandson's grandson is a cloud-descendant (雲孫)"; note: "it means light and far, like a floating cloud."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Da siyue*: "they dance the Cloud-gate Great-roll (雲門大卷)"; note: "of the music of the Six Ages that Zhou preserved, the Yellow Emperor's was called Cloud-gate Great-roll."
- *Also* Shiji, *Annals of the Yellow Emperor*: "for office-names, all were named by 'cloud,' the offices being called cloud-masters (雲師)."
- *A marsh-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Cloud-土 and the Meng were made fit for tillage."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Ding yr 4: "the Viscount of Chu forded the Sui, crossed the Jiang, and entered Yunzhong (雲中)." Erya, *Shi di*: "Chu has the Yunmeng (雲夢)"; subcommentary: "this marsh straddles north and south of the Jiang, and may be called simply Yun, or simply Meng." Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "the Yunmeng (雲夢) is nine hundred li square."
- *Also* *Shiyi ji*: "Mount Penglai is also called Yunlai (雲來)."
- *A commandery and county name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yun county (雲縣) in Langya commandery." Also Yunzhong commandery (雲中郡).
- *Surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "descendants of the Jinyun (縉雲) clan. The Tang had Yun Hongsi; the Ming, Yun Qu and Yun Xiu."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 于員.* Chen Lin, *Manao le fu*: "first wounded by 'do not use,' it waits for the auspicious cloud (慶雲兮); whether the gentleman is straitened or thriving is likewise a matter of the times (時然兮)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: 雲 is used interchangeably with 云.
