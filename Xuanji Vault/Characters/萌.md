**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1041 #05)*

**Ancient graph.** 鋂.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 莫耕. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 眉庚 — sounding like 氓 (méng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A grass-sprout (草芽)." Also *Yun Hui*: "the first springing of plants." Boya: "the beginning (始)."

**Senses.**
- *A motionless look.* Zhuangzi, *Ying di wang*: "just now I showed him the patterns of earth, sprouting-still (萌乎), neither quaking nor upright."
- *To plough is also called 萌.* Zhouli, *Qiu guan*: "the Tishi managed the killing of weeds: in spring, when they first grow, he sprouts them (萌)"; note: "it means ploughing to turn over their sprouts."
- *Surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "Meng Lü, a subordinate general of the Five Dynasties Shu."
- *Jiameng (葭萌), a place-name.* Shiji, *Biography of the Money-makers*, note: "belonging to Guanghan, now a county of Lizhou."
- *Interchangeable with 氓 (the common people).* Guanzi, *Shan guo gui*: "he said to the people (萌) of the high fields: 'the money I lodged with you is so much'"; note: "萌 means the people." Zhanguo ce: "extending even to the common folk and bondservants (萌隸)."
- *Same as 蕄.* Erya, *Shi xun*: "存存, 萌萌, meaning to be present (在)."
- *Also* *Tang yun guyin*: read 芒 (máng). Yang Xiong, *Admonition for the Governor of Youzhou*: "the righteous troops cross the desert, and our border-folk (邊萌) are laid to rest" — rhyming with 康 below.
- *Also* the sense interchangeable with 芒. Liji, *Yue ling*: "the hooked sprouts all come out, the budding (萌) all break through." Guanzi, *Wu xing*: "grass and trees in tiny sprout (區萌)" — 區萌 is the hook-sprout (句芒).
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 彌登, sounding like 瞢 (méng). Same sense.
- *Also* fanqie 眉兵, sounding like 明 (míng). 蕨萌, a plant-name.
