**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Small radical 小; p.296 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 書沼. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 始紹 — the rising tone of 燒 (shǎo).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Not many, few (不多)." It follows 小 (small) with 丿 as phonetic; Xu (Kai): "丿 is sounded 夭 (yāo)." Liji, *Li qi*: "in the rites there are cases where fewness (少) is honoured — that is, the Son of Heaven eats once, the feudal lords twice, the grandees and officers thrice; those who live by their labour, without limit. Those who live by their labour are the commoners."

**Senses.**
- *少頃, a short while, a moment (有閒).* Mengzi: "in a little while (少) it brimmed up."
- *Also, to belittle (短).* To disparage a man is called 少之 ("to make him out little"), just as to praise a man is 多之 ("to make him out much"). Shiji, *Biography of Su Qin*: "those who had long known Su Qin all made light of him (少之)."

**Departing tone (shào).**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 式照. *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 失照 — the departing tone of 燒 (shào). *Yupian*: "young (幼)." *Zengyun*: "the opposite of old (老)." Yijing, *Original Meaning*: "the Two Modes come first, as a single stroke, dividing yin and yang; the Four Images come next, as a double stroke, dividing greater and lesser (少)."
- *少儀 (Shao yi), the name of a chapter in the Liji; 少室 (Shaoshi), a mountain-name.*
- *Also, a deputy, second-in-rank (副貳).* Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "thereupon the Three Junior officers (三少) were established, all senior grandees — namely the Junior Guardian (少保), the Junior Mentor (少傅), the Junior Preceptor (少師)"; note: "deputies to the Three Dukes."
- *Surname.* Shao Niannwei, magistrate of Xiagui under the Han.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 守 (shǒu), fanqie 書久.* Ouyang Xiu, *Tomb-inscription for Lord Du Qi*: "the gentleman, kindly and mild, was father and mother to the people; though the lord lived a hundred years, men held it too few (少)." (Here 母 is sounded 畝, mǔ.)
- *Zhengzitong*: it enters the 宥-rhyme. Ouyang Xiu, *Tomb-inscription for Cai Junshan*: "the father did not weep for the son, the old did not weep for the young (少); alas, Junshan, that you could not have length of days."

**Editorial note.** A note: in modern script 少 is written 幼 ("young"). *Zhengzitong*: anciently 小 and 少 were the same; adding 丿 makes it a derivative graph (轉注).
