**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Rain radical 雨; p.1373 #05)*

**Ancient graph.** 𩃿.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 堂練. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蕩練 — sounding like 殿 (diàn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The flashing brightness of yin and yang (隂陽激燿)." It follows 雨 (rain) and 申. *Piya*: "lightning (電) and thunder are of one breath. Thunder follows 回, lightning follows 申: yin and yang, pressing in a circling (回) way, make thunder; venting in an extending (申) way, make lightning. Some say thunder issues from the breath of Heaven, lightning from the breath of Earth, hence 電 follows an abbreviated 坤. The *Shuo gua*: 'Li is lightning (電)'; lightning is of the fire-kind. When yin and yang clash violently, dividing and striking, fire is born in it: its light is the lightning (電), its sound the thunder (雷). Today when iron and stone are struck together fire is born, and when heated stone is dropped in a well thunder rises — how much more in the great furnace of Heaven and Earth, where they press and stir and the true fire flashes! Master Dong (Dong Zhongshu) said: in an age of great peace, the thunder does not frighten men but only proclaims commands; the lightning does not dazzle the eye but only displays its radiance." *Shiming*: "電 means to vanish (殄); seen of a sudden, it then is snuffed out."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Yijing, *Shi he*: "thunder and lightning join and make brightness (雷電合而章)"; note: "thunder and lightning joining without confusion, then there is brightness."
- *Also* the *Feng* hexagram: "thunder and lightning both come (雷電皆至)"; subcommentary: "thunder is Heaven's awesome stirring; lightning is Heaven's shining glory; when thunder and lightning come together, then awe and brightness are complete enough to make Abundance."
- *Also* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "flashing, flashing the quaking lightning (㷸㷸震電)."
- *Also* Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-spring, the thunder then gives voice, and lightning begins (始電)"; subcommentary: "lightning is the yang's light; when the yang is faint the light is unseen; in this month the yang-breath gradually waxes and strikes against the yin, so its light appears, hence 'lightning begins.'"
- *Also* Chunqiu, Yin yr 9: "great rain, with quaking lightning (震電)"; subcommentary: "the *Hetu* says: yin striking yang makes lightning; lightning is the thunder's light."
- *Also* the *Guliang zhuan*: "震 is thunder; 電 is the thunderbolt (霆)."
- *Also* Huainanzi, *Yuandao xun*: "lightning (電) for a whip and goad."
