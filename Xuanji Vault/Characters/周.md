**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.181 #36)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠱬, 𠄗.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 職流. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 之由 — sounding like 州 (zhōu).

**Etymology and senses.** *Guangyun*: "all around, encompassing (徧)." Yijing, *Xici*: "his knowing encompasses (周) the myriad things."
- *Also, utmost, closest (至).* Shu, *Tai shi*: "though one have the closest (周) kin, they are not like the man of benevolence"; tradition: "周 means closest."
- *Also, loyal and faithful (忠信).* Shu, *Tai jia*: "out of faithfulness (周) comes a good end"; tradition: "by loyalty and good faith he reaches a good end." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "conduct returns to faithfulness (周)." Lunyu: "the gentleman is faithful (周) and not partisan (比)"; note: "loyalty and good faith is 周, factional favoritism is 比."
- *Also, to complete, finish (終).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 20: "to serve you to the end (周)"; note: "周 is like finishing through."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "close, secret (密)." Guanzi, *Shuyan*: "the former kings prized aptness and prized discretion (周); what is discreet does not issue from the mouth nor show in the face — now a dragon, now a snake, five transformations in a day — this is called 周"; note: "deep and secret, beyond fathoming, hence 周."
- *Also, a bend, curve (曲).* Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "growing at the bend (周) of the road"; tradition: "周 means a bend."
- *Also* subcommentary to Shijing, *Zhounan*: "Zhou (周) is a dynasty-name; its land lay south of Mount Qi, in Han times belonging to Meiyang county of Fufeng."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "complete, thorough (備)." Former Han, *Biography of Lu Wenshu*: "they forged the case and brought it fully (周) within the law"; note: Jin Zhuo says: "skilled and exhaustive (周悉), they fitted it into the law."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "a full circuit (周帀)." Former Han, *Biography of Liu Xiang*: "the circuit (周回) was over five li." *Yun Hui*: "vulgarly written 週, which is wrong."
- *Also a bird-name.* Erya, *Shiniao*: "the guizhou (巂周)"; subcommentary: "now called the cuckoo (子規)." Also Hanfeizi, *Shuolin*: "there is a bird called zhouzhou (周周), heavy of head and bent of tail; wishing to drink from the river it would surely topple, so it holds another's feathers in its beak and drinks." Also written 翢.
- *Also 不周 (Buzhou), a mountain-name, in the Kunlun.* Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "by the road of Buzhou (不周) I turn leftward."
- *Also a wind-name.* Baihu tong: "the northwest wind is the Buzhou wind (不周風). 不周 means 'not joining': it says the yin energy has not yet combined and transformed."
- *Also Yangzhou (陽周), Pingzhou (平周), and Dingzhou (定周) are all county-names.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yangzhou county in Shang commandery, Pingzhou county in Xihe commandery, Dingzhou county in Yulin commandery."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "originally from a son of King Ping of Zhou, separately enfeoffed at Ruchuan; people called them the Zhou family (周家), and so it became their clan-name. Also Emperor Xian of Wei's second elder brother was of the Pu (普) clan, later changed to the Zhou clan." Also a compound surname: in the early Wei, the summoned scholar Zhousheng Lie (周生烈) of Dunhuang.
- *Also interchangeable with 賙 (to relieve).* Lunyu: "the gentleman relieves (周) urgent need, but does not add to wealth." Mengzi: "the lord, toward the common folk, should surely relieve (周) them."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 市朱, sounding like 殊 (shū). Ji Li, *Aimu ge*: "the wutong trees, lush and dense, grow at the road's bend (周); the palace pavilions wind about, the terraces and towers now cleared away."
