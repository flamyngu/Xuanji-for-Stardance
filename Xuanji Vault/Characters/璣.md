**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, upper; Jade radical 玉; p.742 #17)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 居衣. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居希 — sounding like 機 (jī).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A pearl that is not round (珠不圓者)." Shu, *Yu gong*: "their baskets held dark and pink silks and irregular pearls on cords (璣組)"; tradition: "璣 is a kind of pearl, born in water"; *Shiwen*: "璣 is a pearl not round; the character-books say: a small pearl." Former Han, *Biography of the Thirteen Kings of Jing*: "he sent Jian fragrant herbs and irregular pearls (珠璣)"; note: Yan Shigu, "璣 is a pearl that is not round."

**Senses.**
- *A mirror-name.* *Shuowen*, Xu's note: "the *Auspicious-Omens Chart* has the ji-mirror (璣鏡)"; note: "a great pearl, finely set, with a glowing light, that can serve as a mirror."
- *An instrument-name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "he examined the jade armillary sphere (璿璣玉衡), to set in order the Seven Regulators"; note: "在 means to examine. 璣 is the instrument for setting the heavens right: the 璣 is the turning part, the 衡 the cross-tube; one turns the 璣 to make it move. The 璣 is eight feet across, its circumference two feet five inches and more; the 衡 is eight feet long, its bore one inch across; sighting from its lower end, one divines from it the auspicious and ill aspects of the stars. The Seven Regulators are the sun, moon, and five planets; gain and loss follow upon governance, hence they are called the Regulators (政)."
- *A star-name.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*, note on the seven stars of the Northern Dipper: "the *Chunqiu yundouji* says: the third star of the Dipper is the Ji (璣)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 渠希, sounding like 祈 (qí). Also *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 其旣. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 吉器 — sounding like 禨 (jì). Also *Ji Yun*: fanqie 巨至, sounding like 𦤌 (zhì). The sense in all is the same.
