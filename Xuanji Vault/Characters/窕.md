**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Cave radical 穴; p.865 #14)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒了 — the rising tone of 掉 (tiǎo).

**Etymology and senses.** 窈窕 (retiring); utterly deep (深極); leisured (閒). Shijing, *Zhounan*: "the modest, retiring (窈窕) virtuous maiden"; tradition: "窈窕 means secluded and serene." Erya, *Shiyan*: "窕 means leisured." Yangzi, *Fangyan*: "窕 means fair and lovely." *Guangyun*: "a fair heart is 窈, a fair complexion is 窕."
- *Also a man too may be called 窈窕.* An old yuefu: "there was, they say, a third young master, comely (窈窕) beyond compare in the world."
- *Also the depth of hills and waters is called 窈窕.* Cao Shu's poem: "deep and winding (窈窕), the mountain road runs far." Du Fu's poem: "mist rises on the deep secluded (窈窕) brook."
- *Also the depth of palace chambers is called 窈窕.* Qiao Zhizhi's poem: "deep-secluded (窈窕), the ninefold inner chamber."
- *Also, fine, slight (細).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 21: "the small notes are not thin (窕)"; subcommentary: "窕 has the sense of fine and slender."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 他彫; the same as 佻 (tiāo): the look of being light and slight. Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 16: "the army of Chu was rash and flighty (輕窕)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 他弔, sounding like 糶 (tiào); also *Yun Hui*: fanqie 吐了, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 土了 — the rising tone of 眺 (tiǎo); the senses throughout are the same.
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 餘招, sounding like 遙 (yáo). Xunzi, *Lilun*: "in setting up its ornament, it does not reach to the seductive (窕冶)"; note: "窕 is pronounced 姚 (yáo)."
- *Also the same as 篠 (xiǎo).* Xunzi, *Fu*: "it fills the great vault and yet is not slack (窕)"; note: "窕 is pronounced 篠."
- *Also the same as 條 (tiáo).* Huainanzi, *Yuandao*: "the field of xiaotiao (霄窕)"; note: "the same as 蕭條 (desolate)."
