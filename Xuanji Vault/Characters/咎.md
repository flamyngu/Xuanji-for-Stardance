**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.185 #06)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 其九. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 巨九 — sounding like 舅 (jiù).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Calamity (災). It follows 人 (man) and 各; 各 means to be at variance." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "Heaven sent down calamity (咎) on him."

**Senses.**
- *A flaw, ill (病).* Erya, *Shi gu*: "咎 means an ill"; subcommentary: "a guilt-ill." *Guangyun*: "a transgression (愆); a fault (過)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "in the nine-three, the gentleman is diligent and unwearied all day, at evening wary as if in peril — no fault (无咎)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "may it be that they simply do not come, and no blame (咎) attach to me."
- *Also* Shu, preface: "Yin first found fault with (咎) Zhou"; tradition: "咎 means to think ill of (惡)."
- *Interchangeable with 臼.* *Discourses of Jin*: "Yijiu (宜咎)"; note: "咎 is also written 臼; anciently the graphs were used interchangeably."
- *Interchangeable with 舅.* Yili, *Shi hun li*, note: "the old text writes 舅 as 咎." Xunzi, *Chen dao*: "Jiufan (咎犯) of Jin"; the Zuozhuan writes 舅.
- *Also* *Tang yun zheng*: "in the latter half of the forty-fourth 有-rhyme, anciently it ran together with 篠, 小, 巧, and 皓 into one rhyme. Shu, *Da Yu mo*: 'the people abandon those they do not protect; Heaven sent down calamity (咎) upon him.' Yijing, *Xiao xu*: 'the first nine returns by its own way — what fault (咎) in that?' In the classics, commentaries, and the masters such usage is very common; it is no rhyme-borrowing."

**Further readings (gāo).**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 古勞. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居勞. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 姑勞 — sounding like 高 (gāo). *Guangyun*: "Gao Yao (臯陶), the name of Shun's minister, anciently written 咎繇." Former Han, *Table of Officials*: "Gao Yao (咎繇) was made the Justice." Shu, preface: "Jiu Dan (咎單) composed the *Mingju*"; tradition: "Jiu Dan was a minister's name." (A note: the *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*, and *Zheng Yun* all say it is a surname — wrongly.)
- *A state-name.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 23: "the Di attacked Qiang-Jiuru (廧咎如)"; note: "a separate kind of the Red Di"; Lu Deming, *Yinyi*: "咎, fanqie 古刀."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 技 (jì), fanqie 曁几.* Shiji, *Biography of the Tortoise and Milfoil*: "if the king sends him off, Song will surely have calamity (咎); though he repent it after, it will be of no avail."
- *Rhymes as 巨 (jù), fanqie 跽許.* *San lüe*: "strong and weak enslaving one another, with none to check or curb; reaching even to lord and minister, the state suffers its calamity (咎)."
- *Rhymes as 舊 (jiù), fanqie 巨又.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the planners are very many, and so nothing is achieved; words fill the court, but who dares shoulder the blame (咎)?" (集 rhymes fanqie 疾救.) A note: the *Zihui* and *Zhengzitong* both take the departing tone as the proper sound of 咎; but examining the *Yupian*, *Guangyun*, and *Ji Yun*, the graph 咎 has no departing-tone reading at all — the *Zihui* and *Zhengzitong* are both wrong.
