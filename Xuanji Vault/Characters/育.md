**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Flesh radical 肉; p.976 #14)*

**Pronunciation.** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 余六 — sounding like 昱 (yù).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to rear a child so that he becomes good." *Guangyun*: "to nourish (養)." Yijing, *Meng*: "the gentleman, by resolute conduct, fosters (育) his virtue"; note: "to foster virtue is the merit of nurturing what is upright"; subcommentary: "to nurture his virtue."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "育 means to grow up (長)." Shijing, *Weifeng*: "born and grown (育)"; Jian: "育 means to grow to age." Shu, *Pangeng*: "I will then cut them off and destroy them, leaving none to grow on (遺育)"; tradition: "育 means to grow." Jin yu: "to rectify names and foster the kinds (育類)"; note: "育 means to grow."
- *Also, to give birth, to engender (生).* Liji, *Zhongyong*: "putting forth and engendering (育) the myriad things"; note: "育 means to engender."
- *Also, to shelter and nurture (覆育).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "he raised me and nurtured (育) me"; Jian: "育 means to shelter and nurture."
- *Also, young, infant (幼稚).* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "in my youth (昔育) I dreaded being driven to want"; Jian: "昔育 is the time when one was young."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yuyang (育陽)"; note: "belonging to Nanyang commandery."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: also a surname.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 直祐, sounding like 胄 (zhòu): offspring (胤).
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: interchangeable with 鬻. Shijing, *Binfeng*: "the pity of rearing (鬻) a child." Liji, *Yue ji*: "the furred ones conceive and bear (孕鬻)"; note: "鬻 means to give birth."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 毓. Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "conceiving and breeding (孕毓) root and stalk."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as 亦 (yì). Shijing, *Daya*: "now bearing, now rearing (育)" — rhyming with 夙 above and 稷 below, 夙 pronounced 夕.
- *Rhymes as* 乙 (yǐ). Huang Boren, *Longma song*: "endowed with the transforming numen of the spirits, in Dayuan it was bred (育) anew; drawing its image from the dark hornless-dragon, like the divine qiu-dragon poured into a mold."
