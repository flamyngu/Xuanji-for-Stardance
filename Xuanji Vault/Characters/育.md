**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, lower; Flesh radical иӮү; p.976 #14)*

**Pronunciation.** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie дҪҷе…ӯ вҖ” sounding like жҳұ (yГ№).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to rear a child so that he becomes good." *Guangyun*: "to nourish (йӨҠ)." Yijing, *Meng*: "the gentleman, by resolute conduct, fosters (иӮІ) his virtue"; note: "to foster virtue is the merit of nurturing what is upright"; subcommentary: "to nurture his virtue."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "иӮІ means to grow up (й•·)." Shijing, *Weifeng*: "born and grown (иӮІ)"; Jian: "иӮІ means to grow to age." Shu, *Pangeng*: "I will then cut them off and destroy them, leaving none to grow on (йҒәиӮІ)"; tradition: "иӮІ means to grow." Jin yu: "to rectify names and foster the kinds (иӮІйЎһ)"; note: "иӮІ means to grow."
- *Also, to give birth, to engender (з”ҹ).* Liji, *Zhongyong*: "putting forth and engendering (иӮІ) the myriad things"; note: "иӮІ means to engender."
- *Also, to shelter and nurture (иҰҶиӮІ).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "he raised me and nurtured (иӮІ) me"; Jian: "иӮІ means to shelter and nurture."
- *Also, young, infant (е№јзЁҡ).* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "in my youth (жҳ”иӮІ) I dreaded being driven to want"; Jian: "жҳ”иӮІ is the time when one was young."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yuyang (иӮІйҷҪ)"; note: "belonging to Nanyang commandery."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: also a surname.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie зӣҙзҘҗ, sounding like иғ„ (zhГІu): offspring (иғӨ).
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: interchangeable with й¬». Shijing, *Binfeng*: "the pity of rearing (й¬») a child." Liji, *Yue ji*: "the furred ones conceive and bear (еӯ•й¬»)"; note: "й¬» means to give birth."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: sometimes written жҜ“. Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "conceiving and breeding (еӯ•жҜ“) root and stalk."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as дәҰ (yГ¬). Shijing, *Daya*: "now bearing, now rearing (иӮІ)" вҖ” rhyming with еӨҷ above and зЁ· below, еӨҷ pronounced еӨ•.
- *Rhymes as* д№ҷ (yЗҗ). Huang Boren, *Longma song*: "endowed with the transforming numen of the spirits, in Dayuan it was bred (иӮІ) anew; drawing its image from the dark hornless-dragon, like the divine qiu-dragon poured into a mold."
