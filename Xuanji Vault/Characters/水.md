**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.603 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 式軌. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 數軌 — the rising tone of 稅 (shuǐ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the level (準). The phase of the north; it pictures the many waters flowing together, with a faint yang energy within." Xu Xuan says: "many bends make water; utterly soft, yet able to attack the hard, hence the single stroke within." *Shiming*: "水 means level (準): it levels things flat." Baihu tong: "the place of water is the north. The north is the yin energy, beneath the Yellow Springs, bearing and nourishing the myriad things. 水 as a word means to moisten (濡)." Shu, *Hongfan*: "of the Five Phases, the first is Water"; also: "Water means moistening and descending (潤下)"; the Correct Meaning: "Heaven's One gives birth to Water, Earth's Six completes it. Of the bodies of the Five Phases, Water is the most subtle, hence One; Fire grows more manifest, hence Two; Wood takes solid form, hence Three; Metal is firm in body, hence Four; Earth is great in substance, hence Five." Yijing, *Qian*: "water flows to the damp." *Shuogua*: "Kan is water." Guanzi, *Shuidi*: "water is the blood and breath of the earth, like the sinews and veins through which it flows." Huainanzi, *Tianwen*: "the accumulated cold breath of yin becomes water."
- *Also one of the six drinks.* Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Beverage-master has charge of supplying the king's six drinks: water, rice-water, sweet-wine, cooling-drink, medicinal-drink, and thin-gruel." Also Liji, *Yuzao*: "the five drinks: foremost water, then rice-water, ale, sweet-wine, and thin-gruel"; note: "'foremost water' — water is highest, the rest follow."
- *Also* Liji, *Quli*: "in all the rites of sacrifice at the ancestral temple, water is called the 'clear cleanser' (淸滌)."
- *Also 明水 (bright water), for use in sacrifice.* Zhouli, *Qiuguan*: "the Sixuan masters take bright fire from the sun with the burning-mirror, and bright water (明水) from the moon with the mirror, to supply the sacrificial bright-grain, bright-candle, and bright-water"; note: "the 鑒 is of the mirror kind, used to take water; the age calls it the fangzhu (方諸)."
- *Also an office-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 17: "the house of Gonggong ordered itself by water, hence had Water Masters (水師) and named its offices after water."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "of the five notes, yu corresponds to Water."
- *Also 天水 (Tianshui), a commandery-name, established by Emperor Wu of Han.* Also 中水 (Zhongshui), a county-name, belonging to Zhuo commandery; Ying Shao says: "between the two waters Yi and Lao." Both are seen in Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*.
- *Also 黑水 (Heishui), a state-name; that is, the Mohe (𩎟韐).*
- *Also dew is the "upper-pool water" (上池水).* Shiji, *Biography of Bian Que*: "drink this with upper-pool water (上池之水)"; note: "upper-pool water means water that has not reached the ground — collecting dew and the water on bamboo and trees, to compound medicine."
- *Also a surname.*
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 式類, sounding like 墜 (zhuì). Liu Zhen, *Ludu fu*: "the duckweed drifts on Yanghou's waves, the lotus rises at the islet's edge; it flaunts its red blossoms gleaming bright, and the fleeting light shines on the cliff-side waters (水)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 呼委, sounding like 毀 (huǐ). Li Bai, *poem on roaming Danyang Lake at Gaochun*: "turtles swim on the lotus leaves, birds roost among the reed-flowers; a young girl poles her light boat, her song chasing the flowing water (水)."

**Editorial notes.** The *Yunbu* also gives the sound 準 (zhǔn), citing the Baihu tong: "水 as a word means level (準)." A note: 準 is the sense of 水, not the sound of 水; this follows Zheng [Xuan]'s note to the Zhouli, *Kaogong ji*, where 準 is read as 水, and is mistaken — here it is not adopted.
