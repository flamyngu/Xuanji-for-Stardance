## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈卯集上〉【心字部】頁389第31    〔古文〕：𢚏、啨【唐韻】疾盈切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】慈盈切，𠀤音晴。性之動也。从心靑聲。【董仲舒曰】人欲之謂情。【詩序】六情靜于中，百物盪于外。【白虎通】喜，怒，哀，樂，愛，惡，謂六情。【禮·禮運】何謂人情。喜，怒，哀，懼，愛，惡，欲。七者弗學而能。　又情，實也。【論語】上好信，則民莫敢不用情。　又叶慈良切，音牆。【韓愈·贈張籍詩】閉門讀書史，淸風𥦬戸凉。日念子來游，子豈知我情。【朱子曰】古人制字，先制得心字，性與情皆从心。性卽心之理，情卽心之用。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.389 #31)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢚏, 啨.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 疾盈. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 慈盈 — sounding like 晴 (qíng).

**Etymology and senses.** "The movement of [human] nature (性之動)." It follows 心 with 靑 as phonetic. **Dong Zhongshu**: "what man desires is called 情." Preface to the *Shijing*: "the six feelings (六情) lie still within, while the myriad things stir without." *Baihu Tong*: "joy, anger, grief, delight, love, and hate are called the six feelings." Liji, *Li yun*: "What are the feelings of man? Joy, anger, grief, fear, love, hate, desire — these seven, unlearned yet within our power."
- *The real, the actual (實).* Lunyu: "if the ruler loves good faith, then none of the people dare withhold their true feeling (情)."
- *Rhymes as 牆 (qiáng), fanqie 慈良.* Han Yu, poem to Zhang Ji: "behind shut doors I read the histories, a clear breeze cool at the window… day by day I long for your visit — how could you not know my heart (情)?"

**Note.** Zhu Xi: "When the ancients formed characters, they first made 心 (heart); both 性 (nature) and 情 (feeling) follow 心. 性 is the *principle* of the heart; 情 is the *function* of the heart."
