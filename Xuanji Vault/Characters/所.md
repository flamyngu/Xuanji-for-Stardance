**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, middle; Door radical 戶; p.415 #16)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢨷.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 疏舉. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 爽阻 — the rising tone of 數, i.e. **suǒ**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The sound of chopping wood (伐木聲)." It follows 斤 (axe) with 戶 as phonetic.

**Senses.**
- *A place (處所).* Shijing, *Zheng feng*: "presented at the duke's place (公所)"; *Shang song*: "and you in this place (斯所)."
- *行在所, the imperial sojourn.* Under the Han, wherever the imperial carriage was. Cai Yong, *Du duan*: "the Son of Heaven takes the Four Seas as his home, so wherever he is is called 行在所."
- *To devote effort to one thing.* In the Guanxi dialect, to concentrate on one task is 所 — what is meant by "cutting off [other] gains and [keeping to] a single source." Shu, *Wu yi*: "the gentleman keeps to his [task] (所其無逸)." *Shao gao*: "the king reverently makes [his] place (作所)."
- *A particle.* Lunyu: "observe what he does (所以), watch whence he comes (所由)."
- *A word in oaths.* Lunyu: "if I (予所) have done wrong…" Zuozhuan, Xi yr 24: "if I do not (所不) share heart with my uncle…"
- *A word of approximation (about, some).* Liji, *Tan gong*: "its height could screen one"; note: "means about four *chi* (四尺所)." Former Han, *Biography of Shu Guang*: "he asked how much (幾所) gold was left"; note: "幾所 is like 幾許 (how much)." *Biography of Zhang Liang*: "the old man went off about a *li* (里所) and came back"; note: "里所 is like 里許 (about a *li*)."
- *Surname.* In Emperor Wu of Han's time, the Counsellor Suo Zhong.
- *Rhymes as 徙 (xǐ), fanqie 襄里.* Ban Gu, *Western Capital Rhapsody*: "ringed by palace walls, four hundred *li* and more; detached palaces and lodges, six-and-thirty places (所)."
