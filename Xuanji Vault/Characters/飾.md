**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, lower; Food radical йЈҹ; p.1418 #42)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie иіһиҒ·. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иЁӯиҒ· вҖ” sounding like иӯҳ (shГ¬).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to adorn, dress up (дҝ®йЈҫ)." The *Yiya* [Shiming]: "йЈҫ is жӢӯ (to wipe): a soiled thing is wiped on its surface to make it bright вҖ” bright by another thing, as adding pattern upon plain substance."
- *The adornment of sound.* Liji, *Yueji*: "sound is the image of music; pattern and rhythm are the adornment (йЈҫ) of sound; so the gentleman stirs its root, delights in its image, and then orders its adornment"; note: "to take the sound and clothe it upon the instruments."
- *Cuff-edging, trim.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "a lamb's-fur coat with leopard trim (иұ№йЈҫ)"; note: "йЈҫ means the cuff-edging; by rite the ruler uses whole pelts, the subject below him, so the coat is trimmed (йЈҫ) with leopard skin."
- *To groom.* Zhouli, *Diguan, Fengren*: "he grooms (йЈҫ) the sacrificial ox"; note: "to brush and cleanse it; some say to deck it beforehand."
- *To cover, drape.* Liji, *Quli*: "for the gift-lamb and gift-goose, drape (йЈҫ) them with figured cloth"; subcommentary: "йЈҫ means to cover вҖ” cloth painted with cloud-forms, draped over them at the audience."
- *еҺІйЈҫ, stern (martial) array.* Liji, *Yueling*: "the Son of Heaven then dons stern array (еҺІйЈҫ)"; note: "еҺІйЈҫ means martial dress, favouring might and valour."
- *To gloss over, deck out falsely.* Shiji, *Biography of Gongsun Hong*: "skilled in clerkly affairs, he glossed them over (з·ЈйЈҫ) with Confucian arts." Later Han, *Annals of Emperor Zhang*: "the edict said, 'common clerks falsely deck (зҹҜйЈҫ) their outward looks, seeming right but wrong вҖ” this I much detest.'"
- *Furnishings.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Zhanggu*: "he sets out its furnishings (йЈҫеҷЁ)"; note: "weapons and armour and the like."
- *Manshi (ж»ҝйЈҫ), a state-name.* *Zhengzitong.*

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "a brush (г•һ); it follows дәә (man) and е·ҫ (cloth), with йЈҹ as phonetic; read like ејҸ; one says it means polishing-decoration (ж©ЎйЈҫ)."

**Textual notes.** еүқжІ» should read еҲ·жІ»; "д»Ҙзёҫ" should read "д»Ҙз№ў" and the иЁ» should read з–Ҹ; "еҮЎеҗҸдәӢ" should read "зҝ’еҗҸдәӢ"; the Annals of Emperor Zhang are in the Later Han (еҫҢжјў), not the Former Han (еүҚжјў).
