## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集下〉【食字部】頁1418第42【廣韻】賞職切【集韻】【韻會】設職切，𠀤音識。【玉篇】修飾也。【逸雅】飾，拭也，物穢者拭其上使明，由他物而後明，猶加文于質上也。　又【禮·樂記】聲者，樂之象也。文采節奏，聲之飾也，故君子動其本，樂其象，然後治其飾。【註】以聲而被之器也。　又【詩·鄭風】羔裘豹飾。【註】飾，緣袖也。禮，君用純物，臣下之，故裘以豹皮爲飾也。　又【周禮·地官·封人】飾其牛牲。【註】刷治潔淸之也。一曰豫飾也。　又【禮·曲禮】飾羔鴈者以繢。【疏】飾，覆也。畫布爲雲氣，覆之以相見也。　又【禮·月令】天子乃厲飾。【註】厲飾謂戎服，尚威武也。　又【史記·公孫弘傳】習吏事，緣飾以儒術。【後漢·章帝紀】詔曰：俗吏矯飾外貌，似是而非，朕甚厭之。　又【周禮·夏官·掌固】設其飾器。【註】兵甲之屬。　又【正字通】滿飾，國名。【說文】㕞也。从人从巾，食聲。讀若式，一曰橡飾。    考證：〔【周禮·地官·封人】飾其牛牲。【註】剝治潔淸之也。〕　謹照原文剝治改刷治。〔【曲禮】飾羔鴈者以績。【註】飾，復也。〕　謹照原文以績改以繢。註改疏。〔【史記·公孫弘傳】凡吏事，緣飾以儒術。〕　謹照原文凡吏事改習吏事。〔【前漢·章帝紀】詔曰，俗吏矯飾外貌，似是而非。〕　謹按章帝紀係後漢書，前漢改後漢。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, lower; Food radical 食; p.1418 #42)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 賞職. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 設職 — sounding like 識 (shì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to adorn, dress up (修飾)." The *Yiya* [Shiming]: "飾 is 拭 (to wipe): a soiled thing is wiped on its surface to make it bright — bright by another thing, as adding pattern upon plain substance."
- *The adornment of sound.* Liji, *Yueji*: "sound is the image of music; pattern and rhythm are the adornment (飾) of sound; so the gentleman stirs its root, delights in its image, and then orders its adornment"; note: "to take the sound and clothe it upon the instruments."
- *Cuff-edging, trim.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "a lamb's-fur coat with leopard trim (豹飾)"; note: "飾 means the cuff-edging; by rite the ruler uses whole pelts, the subject below him, so the coat is trimmed (飾) with leopard skin."
- *To groom.* Zhouli, *Diguan, Fengren*: "he grooms (飾) the sacrificial ox"; note: "to brush and cleanse it; some say to deck it beforehand."
- *To cover, drape.* Liji, *Quli*: "for the gift-lamb and gift-goose, drape (飾) them with figured cloth"; subcommentary: "飾 means to cover — cloth painted with cloud-forms, draped over them at the audience."
- *厲飾, stern (martial) array.* Liji, *Yueling*: "the Son of Heaven then dons stern array (厲飾)"; note: "厲飾 means martial dress, favouring might and valour."
- *To gloss over, deck out falsely.* Shiji, *Biography of Gongsun Hong*: "skilled in clerkly affairs, he glossed them over (緣飾) with Confucian arts." Later Han, *Annals of Emperor Zhang*: "the edict said, 'common clerks falsely deck (矯飾) their outward looks, seeming right but wrong — this I much detest.'"
- *Furnishings.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Zhanggu*: "he sets out its furnishings (飾器)"; note: "weapons and armour and the like."
- *Manshi (滿飾), a state-name.* *Zhengzitong.*

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "a brush (㕞); it follows 人 (man) and 巾 (cloth), with 食 as phonetic; read like 式; one says it means polishing-decoration (橡飾)."

**Textual notes.** 剝治 should read 刷治; "以績" should read "以繢" and the 註 should read 疏; "凡吏事" should read "習吏事"; the Annals of Emperor Zhang are in the Later Han (後漢), not the Former Han (前漢).
