**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; Dish radical 皿; p.793 #07)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 以成. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 怡成. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 餘輕 — sounding like 嬴 (yíng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a filled vessel." *Boya*: "full (滿); to fill (充)."
- *Full, brimming, at the full.* Yijing, *Feng* tuan: "Heaven and Earth wax (盈) and wane, ebbing and flowing with the seasons." Shijing, *Qifeng*: "the cock has crowed, the court is already full (盈)." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 10: "their ardour spent and ours at the full (盈), so we beat them." Liji, *Liyun*: "the moon waxes in three fives of days to the full (盈), and wanes in three fives."
- *also.* Liji, *Jiyi*: "music takes overflowing fullness (盈) for its principle"; note: "like brimming over."
- *weiying (魏盈), to be angry.* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "in the outer marches of Yan, between Korea and the Lie River, all scolding and rebuking is called *weiying* (魏盈)."
- *A state-name.* Shanhaijing: "in the south of the Great Wilds lies the land of the Ying people (盈民之國)."
- *A prefecture-name.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Geography*: "among the Man tribes is Yingzhou (盈州)."
- *A surname.* Descendants of Luan Ying of Jin.
- *In antiquity interchangeable with 嬴.* *Zheng Yun*: "盈縮 (waxing and shrinking): excess is 盈, falling short is 縮." Shiji, *Biography of Cai Ze*: "advancing and retreating, waxing and waning (盈縮)"; the *Treatise on the Heavenly Offices* writes it 嬴縮. An old poem: "fair and full (盈盈), the girl up in the tower"; note: "盈 is the same as 嬴, 'comely.'"
- *Rhymes as 陽 (yáng), fanqie 與章.* Zhang Ji, *Sacrificial Poem for Han Yu*: "the new pavilion built but never climbed, shut away in the western chamber of the villa; your letters and your poems and prose lie heaped, filling (盈) my book-chest."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*, in Xu's note: "夃, fanqie 古乎, has the sense of 'increasing in number.' Anciently, getting much in a purchase was 夃, hence 盈 follows 夃; 'getting much in a purchase' should properly be the graph 𡕔."
