## English meanings
_All senses recorded for this character (Unicode Unihan / CC-CEDICT-class definitions):_
- clan, family
- mister
- a lineage; a hereditary house; a branch of a clan
- a surname
- (suffix) appended to a surname or an eminent name (honorific; 'Mr.', 'Ms.')
- (historical) née; a married woman's maiden surname (e.g. 王氏)
- (suffix) marking attribution (of a work, discovery, or invention)
- (as zhī) in proper nouns: 月氏 the Yuezhi; 閼氏 (yānzhī) the consort of a Xiongnu chief

## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【氏字部】頁597第18【唐韻】承旨切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】上紙切，𠀤音是。氏族也。【白虎通】有氏者何，貴功德，下伎力，所以勉人爲善也。【左傳·隱八年】天子建德，因生以賜姓，胙之土而命之氏。諸侯以字爲諡，因以爲族。官有世功，則有官族，邑亦如之。【疏】釋例曰：別而稱之謂之氏，合而言之則爲族。【趙彥衞·雲麓漫抄】姓、氏後世不復別，但曰姓某氏，雖史筆亦然。按姓者，所以統系百世，使不別也。氏者，所以別子孫所自出。如周姓姬氏，所以別子孫，如魯衞毛耼邘晉應韓之分，若夫《易》云：黃帝堯舜氏作。堯舜雖非姓氏，旣是天子當一代，稱曰堯舜氏，義亦通。此又不拘姓氏之例。【柳芳·論氏族】氏於國則齊魯秦吳，氏於諡則文武成宣，氏於官則司馬司徒，氏於爵則王孫公孫，氏於字則孟孫叔孫，氏於居則東門北郭，氏於志則三馬五鹿，氏於事則巫乙匠陶。　又古者貴有氏，賤無氏，故其詛辭有曰：墜命亡氏，言奪爵失國也。詛辭見【左傳·襄十一年】。　又婦人例稱氏。【儀禮·士昏禮】祝告婦之姓，曰某氏來歸。　又樂氏，津名，在鄭。【左傳·襄二十六年】涉於樂氏。　又元氏、猗氏、盧氏、尉氏，皆縣名。【廣輿記】元氏屬常山，今屬眞定府。猗氏屬河東，今屬平陽府。盧氏本漢縣，今屬河南府。尉氏本秦縣，今屬眞定府。【師古·漢書註】凡地名稱某氏者，皆謂因之而立名。如尉氏、左氏、緱氏、禺氏之類。　又以氏名其物。【大戴記】蘭氏之根，櫰氏之苞。　又姓。【吳志】有氏儀，後改姓是。　又猛氏，獸名。【司馬相如·上林賦】鋋猛氏。【郭璞曰】今蜀中有獸，狀似熊而小，毛淺有光澤，名猛氏。　又【說文】巴蜀山名岸脅之旁著欲落墮者曰氏。氏崩，聞數百里。《揚雄·解嘲》響若氏隤。○按今《揚雄傳》作𨸝。【玉篇】亦云：巴蜀謂山岸欲墮曰氏，崩聲也。承紙切。又《元包經》剝屵氏。傳曰：山崩於地也。註：屵音蔡，氏音支。與《說文》《玉篇》義同而音異。　又【集韻】掌是切，音紙。姓也。義同𣱄。　又【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】章移切【正韻】旨而切，𠀤音支。月氏，西域國名，在大宛西。【史記·大宛傳】有大月氏，小月氏。亦作月支。　又閼氏，單于后名。【史記·韓王信傳】上乃使人厚遺閼氏。【註】閼音燕，氏音支。　又烏氏，縣名。【史記·酈商傳】破雍將軍烏氏。【註】烏音於然反，氏音支，縣名。屬安定。【前漢·地理志】作閼氏。又【史記·貨殖傳】烏氏倮。【註】韋昭曰：烏氏，縣名，倮，名也。索隱以烏氏爲姓，非是。　又【廣韻】子盈切【集韻】咨盈切，𠀤音精。狋氏，縣名。【前漢·地理志】代郡有狋氏縣。【註】孟康曰：狋音拳，氏音精。亦作𠂩。【古今印史】𠂩，承旨切，族下所分也。古者姓統族，族統𠂩。適出繼位之餘，凡側出者皆曰𠂩。故爲文從側出以見意。    考證：〔【趙彥衞·雲麓漫抄】如魯衞毛耼邗晉應韓之分。〕　謹照原文邗改邘。〔【史記·貨殖傳】烏氏倮。〕　謹照原文鳥改烏。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Clan radical 氏; p.597 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 承旨. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 上紙 — sounding like 是 (shì).

**Etymology and senses.** A clan, a lineage (氏族). Baihu tong: "what is the having of a clan-name (氏)? It honours merit and virtue and ranks mere skill and strength below — the means of urging men to do good." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 8: "the Son of Heaven raises up the virtuous, and by their birth bestows the surname (姓); granting them land, he names them a clan (氏). The feudal lords take a style as a posthumous name, and thence make a lineage. Where office has hereditary merit, there is an office-lineage, and likewise with towns"; subcommentary: "the Shili says: distinguished severally, it is called 氏; spoken of together, it is 族 (lineage)." Zhao Yanwei, *Yunlu manchao*: "in later ages surname (姓) and clan (氏) are no longer distinguished; one says only 'surnamed so-and-so 氏,' and even the historians' brush does so. Now the 姓 binds a hundred generations in one line, that they be not sundered; the 氏 distinguishes the stock from which the descendants spring. Thus Zhou's surname was Ji, clan-name Ji (姬氏), to distinguish the descendants, as in the branches of Lu, Wei, Mao, Dan, Yu, Jin, Ying, and Han. As for the Yijing's saying, 'the Yellow Emperor, Yao, and Shun (氏) arose' — though Yao and Shun are not surnames or clan-names, since each was Son of Heaven over an age, to call them 'the Yao and Shun 氏' makes sense too; this does not keep to the rule of surname and clan." Liu Fang, *On Clans and Lineages*: "named by a state, then Qi, Lu, Qin, Wu; by a posthumous title, then Wen, Wu, Cheng, Xuan; by an office, then Sima, Situ; by a rank, then Wangsun, Gongsun; by a style, then Mengsun, Shusun; by a dwelling, then Dongmen, Beiguo; by an aspiration, then Sanma, Wulu; by an occupation, then Wu (shaman), Yi, Jiang (artisan), Tao (potter)."
- *The noble's mark.* Anciently the noble had a clan-name (氏), the base had none; hence the imprecation runs: "may he lose his mandate and lose his clan (亡氏)," meaning to be stripped of rank and lose his state. The imprecation is in Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 11.
- *Of women.* A woman is regularly styled by 氏. Yili, *Shi hun li*: "the invocator announces the bride's surname, saying, 'so-and-so 氏 has come to wed.'"
- *Yueshi (樂氏), a ford-name,* in Zheng. Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 26: "he forded at Yueshi (樂氏)."
- *County-names.* Yuanshi, Yishi, Lushi, and Weishi are all county-names. The Guangyu ji: "Yuanshi belonged to Changshan, now to Zhending prefecture; Yishi to Hedong, now to Pingyang; Lushi was a Han county, now in Henan; Weishi was a Qin county, now in Zhending." Yan Shigu's note to the Han shu: "wherever a place-name is called 'such-and-such 氏,' the name was set up after [a person] — Weishi, Zuoshi, Goushi, Yushi, and the like."
- *In thing-names.* A thing may be named with 氏. The Da Dai ji: "the root of the *lan*-clan (蘭氏), the bud of the *huai*-clan (櫰氏)."
- *A surname.* The Wuzhi: "there was Shi Yi (氏儀), who later changed his surname to Shi (是)."
- *mengshi (猛氏), a beast-name.* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "they spear the *mengshi* (猛氏)"; Guo Pu: "in Shu today there is a beast shaped like a bear but smaller, with thin glossy fur, named *mengshi*."
- *A collapsing cliff.* *Shuowen*: "in Ba and Shu, a mountain feature — where a cliff-side juts out and seems about to fall — is called 氏; when the 氏 collapses, it is heard for hundreds of *li*." Yang Xiong, *Jie chao*: "echoing like a collapsing cliff (氏隤)." A note: the present Yang Xiong biography writes it 𨸝. Yupian likewise: "in Ba and Shu a cliff-side about to fall is called 氏, the sound of its collapse; fanqie 承紙." Also the Yuanbao jing: "剝屵氏"; commentary: "the mountain crumbling to the ground"; note: "屵 sounds like 蔡, 氏 sounds like 支" — the sense agrees with the Shuowen and Yupian, the sound differs.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 掌是, sounding like 紙 (zhǐ). A surname; same sense as 𣱄.
- *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 章移. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 旨而 — sounding like 支 (zhī). Yuezhi (月氏), a Western-Region state, west of Dayuan. Shiji, *Account of Dayuan*: "there is the Greater Yuezhi and the Lesser Yuezhi"; also written 月支.
- *yanzhi (閼氏), the name for the Chanyu's consort.* Shiji, *Biography of Han Xin the king*: "the emperor then sent men to bestow rich gifts on the *yanzhi* (閼氏)"; note: "閼 sounds like 燕, 氏 sounds like 支."
- *Wuzhi (烏氏), a county-name.* Shiji, *Biography of Li Shang*: "he broke Yong's general at Wuzhi (烏氏)"; note: "烏, fanqie 於然; 氏 sounds like 支; a county, belonging to Anding." The Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*, writes it 閼氏. Also Shiji, *Biographies of the Money-makers*: "Luo of Wuzhi (烏氏倮)"; note: "Wei Zhao says Wuzhi is a county-name and Luo a personal name; the Suoyin takes Wuzhi to be a surname, which is wrong."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 子盈. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 咨盈 — sounding like 精 (jīng). Quanjing (狋氏), a county-name. Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Dai commandery has Quanjing (狋氏) county"; note: "Meng Kang says 狋 sounds like 拳, 氏 sounds like 精." Also written 𠂩. The Gujin yinshi: "𠂩, fanqie 承旨, is what is branched off below the lineage. Anciently the surname governed the lineage, the lineage governed the 𠂩 (branch). Apart from the direct heir who succeeded to the position, all the collateral issue were called 𠂩; hence the graph is formed from a 'side-issue' to convey the sense."

**Textual notes.** In Zhao Yanwei's *Yunlu manchao*, "如魯衞毛耼邗晉應韓之分" — 邗 should read 邘. In Shiji, *Biographies of the Money-makers*, "烏氏倮" — 鳥 should read 烏.
