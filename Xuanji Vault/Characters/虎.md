**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, middle; Tiger radical 虍; p.1073 #03)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𧇂, 虝, 𪊖.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 火古. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 火五 — sounding like 滸 (hǔ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "a fierce beast." *Shuowen*: "the lord of the mountain beasts; it follows 虍 and 儿 (legs), the tiger's feet resembling a man's." Xu Xuan's note: "pictographic." Yijing, *Qian*: "the wind follows the tiger (虎)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "neither wild oxen nor tigers (虎), they roam that wilderness." Da Dai Li: "thrice nine is twenty-seven, ruling the stars; the star rules the tiger, hence the tiger is born in the seventh month." Shuyi ji: "when a tiger reaches a thousand years, its teeth molt and horns grow."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Han had the Hepu prefect Hu Qi (虎旗), whose forebear was Bo Hu (伯虎) of the Eight Yuan."
- *Also a prefecture-name*; the Tang had Huzhou (虎州), later renamed Wuzhou to avoid the taboo on the Founder's name.
- *Also a rapids-name.* Shuijing zhu: "the Yi waters flow further east past Tiger Rapids (虎灘)."
- *Also a mountain-name.* Wu-Yue chunqiu: "the King of Wu was buried outside the Chang Gate; the essence of his gold and jade floated up as a white tiger, hence named Tiger Hill (虎丘)."
- *Also the name of a chamber-vessel.* Xijing zaji: "the Han made the huzi (虎子) of jade as a urinal."
- *Also interchangeable with 琥.* Wu zhi, Pei Songzhi's note: "Yu Fan said, 'I have heard that amber (虎魄) does not pick up rotten mustard-seed.'"

**On the graph.** Liushu zhengwei: "it pictures a tiger crouching and looking back." Pianhai: "儿 (rén) is the old graph for 'man'; the tiger's feet resemble a man's, hence it follows 人; to make it follow 几 is wrong." Ganlu zishu: interchangeably written 𧆞.
