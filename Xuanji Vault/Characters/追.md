**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Walk radical 辵; p.1256 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 陟隹. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 中葵 — sounding like 䨨 (zhuī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Guangyun*: "to follow (隨)." *Zengyun*: "to overtake (逮)." *Yupian*: "to escort, to send off (送)." Shijing, *Zhousong*: "then we escort (追) him"; note: "it means he had set out on the road and they pursued to see him off." Former Han, *Biography of Han Xin*: "you have no one to pursue (追)" — the report of pursuing Xin was a ruse.
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "to chase (逐)." Zhouli, *Qiuguan, Shishi*: "he has charge of the district musters, ordering the matter of pursuit and patrol (追胥)"; note: "追 is pursuing bandits (追寇)." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 18: "he pursued (追) the Rong west of the Ji."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to reach, to catch up (及); to retrieve (救)." Shu, *Wuzi zhi ge*: "though one repent, can it be retrieved (追)?"; note: "it says, though one would repent and reform, can it be reached?" Lunyu: "what is past cannot be remonstrated against, what is to come may yet be pursued (追)." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 9: "they besieged Pengcheng of Song — but it was not Song's territory; this is a retroactive entry (追書)"; note: "a retroactive entry is one where the place was no longer Song's, but is retroactively assigned to Song."
- *Also, any tracing-back of the past is called 追.* Shijing, *Daya*: "and so he traced back (追) his filial duty." Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 13: "recalling (追念) the former merit."
- *Also, persisting in error is called 追非.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "imputing guilt and not ceasing — this is called 追非"; note: "it means casting the blame on the people and not blaming oneself. 解 is to stop; 追非 is persisting in wrong."
- *Also a state-name.* Shijing, *Daya*: "the Zhui (追) and the Mo — he received in full the northern lands."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 都雷, sounding like 堆 (duī). *Yupian*: "a term for working jade." Note to Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhuishi*: "追 is a term for working jade and stone." Shijing, *Daya*: "chiseled (追) and polished its pattern."
- *Also 毋追 (moudui), a cap-name.* Liji, *Jiao te sheng*: "the moudui (毋追) was the way of the house of Xia"; Shiwen: "the first graph is pronounced 牟 (móu), the second fanqie 多雷."
- *Also the knob of a bell.* Mengzi: "the knob (追) worn thin as a wood-borer's leavings."
- *Also* Zihui bu: fanqie 旬爲; interchangeable with 隨. Chuci, *Lisao*: "turning my back on the marking-line to follow (追) the crooked"; note: "追 anciently interchanged with 隨."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 馳僞, sounding like 墜 (zhuì). Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "chariots and horsemen rise like thunder, shaking the sky and stirring the earth; fore and aft in dazzling array, scattering apart and breaking off in pursuit (追)."

**Textual notes.** In the Zhouli, *Shishi* citation, 卿 should read 鄕. In the Jiao te sheng citation, the two graphs 母 should read 毋 and 冠 should read 道, and the text from "註" on is corrected to "Shiwen: the first graph pronounced 牟, the second fanqie 多雷." And the source of the Lisao citation, *Jiu tan*, should read *Lisao*.
