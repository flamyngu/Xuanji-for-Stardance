**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.194 #32)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𡅟, 𠾃, 𡂦, 𠿧, 𡃬, 𡄚, 𠹧, 𧶜.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 式陽. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 尸羊 — sounding like 觴 (shāng), i.e. **shāng**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To know the inner from the outer." It follows 㕯, with 章 abridged as phonetic. *Guangyun*: "to gauge/measure (度)." Yijing, *Dui* hexagram, line 4: "he weighs (商) his joining, not yet at rest"; commentary: "商 means to deliberate, to weigh and decide." Liji, *Qu li*: "dried fish [in sacrifice] is called *shang-ji*"; commentary: "商 is like 'to gauge' — one judges the dryness of the dried fish and uses it at the right point."
- *A musical note.* *Yupian*: one of the five notes, the note of Metal. Liji, *Yue ling*: "its note is *shang*"; commentary: "*shang* numbers 72; belonging to Metal, being turbid it ranks next after *gong*; it is the image of the minister. When autumn's *qi* is harmonious, the *shang* note is in tune." Former Han, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes*: "*shang* means 'manifest' (章): things ripen and may be openly measured." *Baihu Tong*: "*shang* means 'strong'." Liang Yuandi, *Zuanyao*: "autumn is 'plain *shang*,' also 'high *shang*.'"
- *A merchant.* Shuowen: "a travelling trader." Yijing, *Fu* hexagram: "merchants and travellers do not move." Zhouli, *Da zai*: "of the nine occupations, the sixth is merchants and traders, who make goods and wares circulate"; commentary: "the travelling [trader] is *shang*, the settled one *gu*." Guangyun: "properly written 𧶜; the vulgar form 啇 is wrong."
- *A unit of the water-clock (刻).* Subcommentary to Shijing, *Qi feng, "Dongfang weiming"*: the *Shangshu* apocrypha call a clock-mark a *shang*. Yili, *Shi hun li*, commentary citing Zheng Xuan's catalogue: "three *shang* after sunset is dusk"; subcommentary (Ma Rong): before sunrise and after sunset are each two-and-a-half marks; "three *shang*" speaks in round numbers. Zhengzitong: a *shang* is the graduation cut on the clock's float-rod; anciently, to engrave was "*shang*," whence "*shang* gold," "*shang* silver" — the water-clock's marks were cut to gauge the water.
- *Other glosses (Guangyun):* to stretch/extend; to descend; the constant/ordinary.
- *The Shang dynasty / its territory.* Shijing, *Shang song* commentary: "Shang was the land enfeoffed to Xie." Subcommentary: Zheng [Xuan] held that Tang took Xie's fief as the dynastic name; Fu Qian, Wang Su differ. Zuozhuan (Xiang yr 9): "Ebo dwelt at Shang-qiu, and Xiangtu followed him there." (A long scholastic discussion follows on whether the name derives from Shang or Shang-qiu.)
- *Place names.* Zuozhuan (Xi yr 25): garrisoning Shang-mi with the troops of Shen and Xi; Chunqiu (Xiang yr 21): "a meeting at Shang-ren"; Zhanguo Ce: "the battle of Gao-shang."
- *A prefecture.* Guangyun: the old state of Shang; the Northern Wei set up Luozhou, the Zhou made it Shangzhou, taking the name from the land of Shang-yu. Zhanguo Ce: Wei Yang fled Wei into Qin; Duke Xiao made him chancellor and enfeoffed him at Shang, titled Lord Shang. Shiji, *Biography of Zhang Yi*: "I beg to present the land of Shang-yu, six hundred *li*"; commentary: in Shangzhou is the old Shang city, and some two hundred *li* west of it the old Yu city.
- *Shangling*, a Han marquisate, in Linhuai (Shiji, *Table of Marquises under Hui and Jing*).
- *Surname.* Shiji, *Disciples of Confucius*: Shang Qu, Shang Ze.
- *Posthumous title.* "To make merit shine and bring peace to the people is *shang*."
- *Interchangeable with 謫 (zhé, to censure).* Xunzi, *Ru xiao*: "to censure (謫) virtue and fix the ranks"; commentary: "謫 is the same as 商."
- *Read like 章 (zhāng).* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 諸良 — "to gauge." Shu, *Bi shi*: "I shall apportion (商) rewards to you"; *Shiwen*: "商 read in its usual sound; Xu reads it as 章."

**Textual note.** In the Zhouli citation, 九賦 ("nine levies") should read 九職 ("nine occupations").
