**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Hiding-enclosure radical 匸; p.154 #36)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 譬吉. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 僻吉 — the entering tone of 品 (pǐ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Four 丈 (a bolt of cloth)." *Zhengwei*: "four 丈 make eight 端, hence it follows 八 (eight); it follows 匸, picturing the shape of a bound roll of silk." Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "cloth and silk two 尺 two 寸 wide make a breadth, four 丈 long make a bolt (匹)." *Xiao Erya*: "twice two 兩 is called a bolt (匹)." *Guangyun*: "commonly written 疋."

**Senses.**
- *A mate, a pair (偶).* Shijing, *Daya*: "following his band of mates (羣匹)"; note: "King Cheng followed and used the worthy among his ministers, whose conduct and ability matched (匹偶) his own heart."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to pair; to join; two (配, 合, 二)." Erya, *Shi gu*: "匹 means to join (合)"; note: "it means to pair together"; subcommentary: "匹 is to pair and join." Shijing, *Daya*: "he made Feng a fit match (匹)."
- *Also* Liji, *Zi yi*: "only the gentleman can love his fellows (匹)"; note: "匹 means acquaintances and friends."
- *A spouse-mate (妃匹).* Kuang Heng's *Memorial on governance* (Han): "the bond of mate and consort (匹妃) is the beginning of the people's life."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 10: "the common man (匹夫) is without crime"; note: "the commoner and his wife match (匹) each other; the term once fixed, even a single one is called 匹, hence the common pair are called 匹夫 and 匹婦."
- *Of horses one says 匹.* Zhouli, *Xia guan*, note: "four horses (匹) make a team."
- *Also* *Yiwen leiju*: "a horse's gleam-and-shadow is one bolt (疋) long, hence one says 疋." *Zhengwei*: "a horse's shadow is four 丈, so 匹 too is borrowed; otherwise 疋 is used."
- *Also* used for 騖, sounding like 木 (mù). Liji, *Qu li*: "the commoner's gift is a duck (匹)."
