## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈巳集上〉【水字部】頁603第04【唐韻】【集韻】【正韻】𠀤于憬切，音栐。【說文】水長也。象水巠理之長。【詩·周南】江之永矣。　又【爾雅·釋詁】遠也，遐也。【揚子·方言】凡施于衆長謂之永。【書·大禹謨】萬世永賴。【詩·周頌】永觀厥成。　又州名。【韻會】唐置以二水名。　又姓。【列仙傳】有永石公。　又【集韻】【正韻】𠀤與詠同。【書·舜典】詩言志，歌永言。【傳】謂歌詠其義，以長其言也。音詠。又如字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.603 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 于憬, sounding like 栐 (yǒng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Long-flowing water (水長); it images the length of a watercourse's channel." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "long (永) is the Jiang."

**Senses.**
- *Far, distant (遠, 遐).* Erya, *Shi gu*. Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "in general, to extend to the multitude and at length is called 永." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "for myriad ages they shall ever rely (永賴) on it." Shijing, *Zhou song*: "long (永) shall they behold its completion."
- *A prefecture-name.* *Yun Hui*: "the Tang set it up, named from two rivers."
- *Surname.* *Liexian zhuan*: "there was a Yong Shigong (永石公)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: same as 詠 (to chant). Shu, *Shun dian*: "the poem speaks the intent, the song chants (永) the words"; tradition: "it means to sing and chant (歌詠) its meaning, drawing out the words." Sounded 詠 (yǒng); also read as written.
