**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, lower; Leaf radical 頁; p.1407 #17)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 五姦. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 牛姦 — the level tone of 眼 (yán).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the space between the brows and eyes."
- *The brow, the forehead.* Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "your clear bright brow, the brow and the forehead (顏) full"; Mao tradition: "顏 means the brow-corners full and ample." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 9: "Heaven's awe is not a foot's breadth from one's face (顏)." Huangdi Suwen: "when the heart is feverish, the forehead (顏) reddens first." Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "𩕇, 頟, and 顏 all mean the forehead (顙); between the Ru, Ying, Huai and Si rivers they call it 顏."
- *The front of a headband (幘顏).* The Yufu zashi: "in the Yanxi years of Han Emperor Huan, the capital made headcloths all with short fronts (顏) and long ears — held to be an ill omen in dress."
- *Noyan (那顏).* A foreign word, like the Chinese "great man."
- *Shangyan (商顏), a mountain-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Rivers and Canals*: "they led the Luo waters down below Shangyan (商顏)"; note: "the 'face' (顏) of Mount Shang, like the brow of the mountain."
- *孱顏, the look of a mountain's height.* Ouyang Xiu's poem: "the empty azure, and towering yet (孱顏)."
- *A surname.* Xingyuan: "it comes from Langya. A cadet branch of Bo Qin of Lu held the fief of Yan (顏) town, and took it as their clan-name." Also Duke Wu of Zhu was styled Yan; the Gongyang calls him Duke Yan (顏公), and afterwards it became a clan-name.
- *In antiquity it joined the 眞 rhyme, sounding like 銀 (yín).* Su Shi, *Sacrificial Text for Han Zhongxian*: "...with no design of the great craftsman, he laughs at that shame-sweated face (汗顏); reaching out a hand to save the drowning, he set his hope of me in benevolence."
