> 《康熙字典》〈寅集上〉【宀字部】頁285第16【唐韻】居戎切【集韻】【韻會】居雄切【正韻】居中切，𠀤音弓。【說文】室也。从宀，躳省聲。【白虎通】黃帝作宮室，以避寒暑。宮之言中也。【釋名】宮，穹也。屋見垣上穹隆然也。【詩·大雅】雝雝在宮。【周禮·內宰六宮註】婦人稱寢曰宮。宮者，隱蔽之言，天子謂之六寢。　又【禮·儒行】儒有一畝之宮。【註】宮，牆垣也。【儀禮·士昏禮】母戒女曰：夙夜無違宮事，古者貴賤所居，皆得稱宮，至秦始定爲至尊所居之稱。　又宗廟亦曰宮。【詩·召南】于以用之，公侯之宮。【孔氏曰】可以奉祭祀曰事，祭必於宗廟曰宮，互見其義也。　又學名。【禮·曲禮】諸侯曰頖宮。【註】謂半於天子之宮也。　又官名。【周禮·天官】宮正掌王宮之戒令糾禁。　又五音中聲曰宮。【前漢·律歷志】宮，中也。居中央，暢四方，倡始施生，爲四聲綱。【史記·樂書】宮，土音，聲出於𦜉，合口而通之，其性圓而居中。五聲六律十二管，還相爲宮也。【註】宮爲君主之義，當其爲宮，五聲皆備。　又腐𠛬曰宮。【書·呂𠛬宮辟註】宮，淫𠛬，次死之𠛬也。【禮·文王世子】公族無宮𠛬，不翦其類也。　又環也。【爾雅·釋山】大山宮，小山霍。【註】宮謂圍繞之。禮記曰：君爲廬宮之是也。　又【周禮·春官·小胥】正樂縣之位，天子宮縣。【註】宮縣，四面縣也。　又姓。左傳虞宮之奇，戰國宮佗。又北宮，南宮，俱複姓。　又守宮，木名。【爾雅·釋木】守宮，槐。　又守宮，蜥蜴名，別作𧎡。　又叶古黃切，音光。【班固·張敖銘】功成德立，襲封南宮。垂號萬期，永保無疆。　又叶古元切，音涓。【黃庭經】自高自下皆眞人，玉堂絳宇盡元宮。人音然。    考證：〔【釋名】宮，宆也。屋見垣上宆隆然也。〕　謹照原文兩宆字𠀤改穹。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.285 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 居戎. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居雄. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居中 — sounding like 弓 (gōng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a dwelling (室). It follows 宀 (roof), with 躳 abbreviated as phonetic." Baihu tong: "the Yellow Emperor made palaces (宮室) to escape cold and heat. 宮 as a word means 'within' (中)." *Shiming*: "宮 means vaulted (穹): the roof seen above the walls, arching high." Shijing, *Daya*: "harmonious within the palace (宮)." Zhouli, note on the Neizai's six palaces: "a woman calls her chamber 宮; 宮 is a word for the secluded; the Son of Heaven calls them the Six Chambers (六寢)."
- *Also* Liji, *Ruxing*: "the scholar has a dwelling (宮) of one mu"; note: "宮 is the surrounding wall." Yili, *Shi hun li*: "the mother warns her daughter, 'early and late do not neglect the household affairs (宮事)'" — anciently the dwellings of high and low could all be called 宮; only under the Qin was it fixed as the term for the residence of the most honored.
- *Also the ancestral temple is called 宮.* Shijing, *Shaonan*: "wherewith to use it? — in the temple (宮) of lord and prince." Mr. Kong says: "what can serve sacrifice is called 'affair' (事); sacrifice is always at the ancestral temple, called 宮 — the two reveal each other's sense."
- *Also a name for a school.* Liji, *Quli*: "the feudal lords' college is called the Pan Palace (頖宮)"; note: "it means half the size of the Son of Heaven's college (宮)."
- *Also an office-name.* Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Palace Warden (宮正) has charge of the watch-orders and prohibitions of the royal palace."
- *Also the central note among the five tones is called 宮.* Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "宮 means central: it dwells at the center, spreads to the four quarters, leads off and gives life, and is the bond of the four other notes." Shiji, *Yueshu*: "宮 is the earth-note; the sound issues from the spleen, the mouth closed and the breath passing through; its nature is round and central. Of the five notes, six pitches, and twelve pipes, each in turn becomes the 宮 [tonic]"; note: "宮 carries the sense of sovereign; when it is the 宮, all five notes are complete."
- *Also castration is called the 宮 punishment.* Shu, *Lü xing*, note to "the 宮 capital crime": "宮 is the punishment for lewdness, next to death." Liji, *Wenwang shizi*: "the ducal kin suffer no castration (宮刑), that their stock be not cut off."
- *Also, to encircle (環).* Erya, *Shishan*: "a great hill encircling (宮) a small hill is a huo (霍)"; note: "宮 means to surround it. The Liji says, 'the lord made a hut and encircled it (廬宮)' — this is it."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Xiaoxu*: "he sets right the positions of the hung bells and chimes; the Son of Heaven has the palace-hanging (宮縣)"; note: "the palace-hanging is hung on all four sides."
- *Also a surname.* In the Zuozhuan, Gong Zhiqi (宮之奇) of Yu; in the Warring States, Gong Tuo (宮佗). Also Beigong (北宮) and Nangong (南宮), both compound surnames.
- *Also 守宮 (shougong), a tree-name.* Erya, *Shimu*: "守宮 is the pagoda-tree (槐)."
- *Also 守宮 is the name of the gecko*; separately written 𧎡.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 古黃, sounding like 光 (guāng). Ban Gu, *Zhang Ao ming*: "his merit done and virtue set, he inherited the fief of Nangong (南宮); handing down his name ten thousand ages, forever to keep it without bound."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 古元, sounding like 涓 (juān). Huangting jing: "from high to low all are true men, the Jade Hall and the crimson eaves all the Primal Palace (元宮)" — 人 here is pronounced 然 (rán).

**Textual notes.** In the Shiming citation, the two graphs 宆 should both read 穹 (per the original).
