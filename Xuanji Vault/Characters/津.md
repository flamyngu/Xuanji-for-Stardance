**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.621 #09)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𦪉, 𦩏, 𣸁, 𦩦, 𦩨, 𨔥.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 將鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 資辛 — sounding like 蓁 (jīn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A ford (渡)." Shu, *Tai shi*: "the great assembly at Mengjin (孟津)"; the *Correct Meaning*: "Meng is a place-name, 津 is the crossing-place."

**Senses.**
- *To moisten (潤).* Zhouli, *Di guan*: "he distinguishes the growth of things in the five soils; the second is the rivers and marshes, whose folk are dark and moist (津)."
- *A stellar-station name.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "Ximu (析木) is called the Ford (津)." Also a star-name: the *Gan shi xing jing*: "the Tianjin (天津), nine stars, lie in the River north of the Xu lodge, governing fords, channels, and ford-bridges (津梁)."
- *Hejin (河津).* The *Xin shi Sanqin ji*: "the He-ford (河津), also called the Dragon Gate (龍門) — fish cannot mount it, and those that mount it become dragons."
- *A Chu place-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 19: "the men of Ba attacked Chu, and the Viscount of Chu was heavily defeated at Jin (津)."
- *Guanjin (觀津), a county-name.* The *Yudi guangji*: "originally the land of Zhao; under the Han a county, belonging to Xindu commandery."
- *The sense of ferrying later students across.* Liu Xie, *Xin lun, Chong xue*: "the marvel of the Way's images, without speech is not forded (津); the marvel of forded speech, without learning is not handed on."
- *津津 (jinjin), to brim over.* Zhuangzi, *Gengsang Chu*: "within it brims over (津津), there is yet some ill."
- *Also* *Yunbu*: rhymes, fanqie 將先, sounding like 牋 (jiān). The *Huangting jing*: "within is a lad, dark above and dim, governing the sources of the Six Bowels and the Nine Fluids, and answering without in the brimming (津) of the hundred fluids of the two ears."
