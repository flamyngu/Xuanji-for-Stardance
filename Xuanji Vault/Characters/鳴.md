**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, middle; Bird radical 鳥; p.1482 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 武兵. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 眉兵 — sounding like 明 (míng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A bird's cry (鳥聲)." *Yupian*: "calling to one another by voice (聲相命); to cry out (嘷)." Shijing, *Daya*: "the phoenix cries (鳴), there on that high ridge." Also a beast too is said to 鳴. Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "among horses, it is the good cryer (善鳴)." Also *Zengyun*: "whatever gives out a sound is said to 鳴." Liji, *Xue ji*: "strike it with a small blow and it cries small (小鳴), strike it with a great blow and it cries great (大鳴)." Zhuangzi, *De chong fu*: "you make your name (鳴) by 'hard and white.'"

**Senses.**
- *A bird-name.* Shanhai jing: "Mount Yanzhou has a five-coloured bird that cries facing the sky, named the Crying-bird (鳴鳥)." Shu, *Shao gao*: "then we shall not hear the Crying-bird (鳴鳥)"; *Yinyi*: "Ma says: the Crying-bird means the phoenix."
- *Surname.* From the *Xingyuan*.

**Departing tone (mìng).**
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 眉病, sounding like 命 (mìng). "Birds calling to one another." Ma Rong, *Changdi fu*: "the mountain-fowl flock at dawn, the wild pheasants call in the morning; seeking mates and calling their young (鳴子), they wail in grief and whistle long"; note: "鳴 means to call (命)." Cao Zhi's poem: "calling their fellows and whistling for their mates (鳴儔嘯匹)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 芒 (máng), fanqie 謨郞.* Former Han, *Suburban Sacrifice Songs*: "cold and heat unerring, splendid the royal emblem; the songs set forth to match the pitch, the jade chimes ring (玉鳴); the box-note sends the jue, rouses the clear zhi, lifts the beam and raises the yu, drawn out with the shang." Zhang Hua, *Xia qu*: "Mengchang got east of the pass, saving himself by the cock's crow (雞鳴); Xinling turned back west to Wei, and the men of Qin dreaded his might."

**Textual note.** "Liji, *Yue ji*: 'strike it small and it cries small, strike it great and it cries great'" — checked against the original work, "*Yue ji*" should read "*Xue ji*."
