**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҲ, upper; Jade radical зҺү; p.731 #19)*

**Ancient graph.** рӨј¬.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie еёғйӮ„. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йҖӢйӮ„ вҖ” sounding like й ’ (bДҒn). Shu, *Yao dian*: "he distributed (зҸӯ) the auspicious tokens among the host of lords."

**Etymology and senses.** Erya, *Shi yan*: "зҸӯ means to bestow (иіҰ)"; note: "to hand out, to give (еёғиҲҮ)." Shu, *Hong fan*: "when King Wu had conquered Yin, he dealt out (зҸӯ) the ancestral vessels among the feudal lords"; tradition: "he bestowed the ancestral-temple ritual vessels and wine-jars on the feudal lords." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 26: "they spread brushwood (зҸӯиҚҶ) and ate together"; note: "зҸӯ means to spread out (еёғ)." Gongyang zhuan, Xi yr 31: "the Marquis of Jin seized the Earl of Cao and parcelled out (зҸӯ) the encroached lands he had taken among the feudal lords."

**Senses.**
- *Rank, order (з§©еәҸ).* Boya. Zuozhuan, Wen yr 6: "Zhao Meng said: 'Chen Ying's low rank (зҸӯ) is among the nine'"; note: "зҸӯ means position."
- *Order, sequence (ж¬Ў).* *Ji Yun*. Zuozhuan, Huan yr 6: "the grandees of the feudal lords garrisoned Qi, and the men of Qi sent them provisions, making Lu set their order (зҸӯ)"; note: "зҸӯ means sequence."
- *To part, separate (еҲҘ).* *Ji Yun*. Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 18: "there was the sound of parting horses (зҸӯйҰ¬)"; note: "зҸӯ means to part; fleeing by night, the horses could not see one another and so made the sound of separation."
- *Everywhere, all round (еҫ§).* *Discourses of Jin*: "the carriages went all round (зҸӯ), within and without, and so he instructed them"; note: "зҸӯ means everywhere."
- *Also* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "зҸӯ means to draw up in rank (еҫ№еҲ—). In Northern Yan it is зҸӯ, in Eastern Qi еҫ№." Yijing, *Zhun* hexagram: "mounting the horse, he draws back (зҸӯеҰӮ)"; subcommentary: "the fourth six responds to the first, hence mounting the horse; fearing that the second blocks his path, at first he draws back and wheels round." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "he withdrew the army (зҸӯеё«) and mustered the troops."
- *Mottled, of mixed colour (йӣңиүІ).* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "the grey-streaked (зҸӯзҷҪ) do not carry burdens"; note: "mixed colour is called зҸӯ."
- *зҸӯзҸӯ, the sound of carriages.* Later Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "the carriages rumbling (зҸӯзҸӯ) enter Hejian."
- *Surname.* *Fengsu tong*: "descendants of Dou Ban, chief minister of Chu."
- *A county-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Banshi (зҸӯж°Ҹ)"; note: "it belongs to Dai commandery."
- *зҸӯиҢ… (banmao), the name of an insect.* *Gujin zhu*: "among the five medicinal kinds, the fifth is the banmao (зҸӯиҢ…); rock-salt neutralizes it."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: sometimes written иҫЁ. Former Han, *Biography of Wang Mang*: "he arrayed (иҫЁ) the altars of the feudal lords."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "interchangeable with иҲ¬." Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 13: "Prince Ban (зҸӯ) of Zheng, from Zi, sought to enter the great temple"; *Shiwen*: "зҸӯ is also written иҲ¬." Former Han, *Suburban Sacrifice Songs*: "first with rain, spreading and streaming (иҲ¬иЈ”иЈ”)"; note: "'first with rain' means the spirit is about to move, and bids the rain run before it; иҲ¬ is read the same as зҸӯ, 'to spread'; иЈ”иЈ” is the look of flying and flowing."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as йһӯ (biДҒn), fanqie рӨ°һйҖЈ.* He Yan, *Jingfu dian fu*: "the light gleaming and glittering, the patterns shimmering and mottled (з’ҳзҸӯ); the clear wind gathers and forms an echo, the morning sun shines and adds freshness." *Guangyun*: commonly written рӨҰҰ. *Zheng Yun*: also written й ’ or жңҢ.

**Textual note.** "Surname. *Fengsu tong*: descendants of Que Ban (й—•зҸӯ), chief minister of Chu" вҖ” checked against the *Fengsu tong* as cited in the *Guangyun*, "Que Ban (й—•зҸӯ)" should read "Dou Ban (й¬ӘзҸӯ)."
