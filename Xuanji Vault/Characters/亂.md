**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; Second radical 乙; p.84 #33)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠧎, 𠧏, 𠮗.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 郞段. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 盧玩 — the departing tone of 鑾 (luàn).

**Etymology and senses.** "Tangled, disordered (紊)." Erya, *Shigu*: "亂 means to govern, to set in order (治)." *Shuowen*: "it follows 乙; 乙 is the ordering of it." *Yupian*: "to order (理)." Shu, *Gao Yao mo*: "orderly (亂) yet reverent"; Kong's tradition: "having order yet able to be reverent and careful." Also *Pangeng*: "to order (亂) and extend to my house." *Zicai*: "their ordering (亂) is for the people." *Luo gao*: "the four quarters are guided and ordered (迪亂), the ordering being the four supports." *Lizheng*: "greatly then to make them govern (俾亂)" — all gloss 亂 as "to govern."
- *Also, not governed; whatever is in disorder is called 亂.* Erya, *Shixun*: "夢夢 (muddled) and 訰訰 (confused) mean disorder (亂)." Shu, *Zhou guan*: "to fashion order before disorder (亂) arises." Zhouli, *Diguan*: "the Sibao oversees the proclaimed prohibitions of the market, forbidding brawls and clamor and violent disorder (虣亂)."
- *Also, armed rebels (兵寇).*
- *Also, a time when affairs are unsettled.* Liji, *Tan'gong*: "Zhongliangzi said, 'husband and wife are then in confusion (亂)'"; note: "at the mourning-stations the men's and women's wailing-positions are not yet formed into ranks."
- *Also the final movement of a piece of music is called the 亂.* Lunyu: "the finale (亂) of the Guanju." Also, the ends of ancient rhapsodies all have a 亂, summing up the whole rhapsody's close and bringing out its main import.
- *Also* Erya, *Shishui*: "to cut straight across a current is called 亂"; note: "to cross by going athwart the flow." Shu, *Yu gong*: "crossing (亂) the Yellow River." Shijing, *Daya*: "they forded the Wei, cutting across (亂)."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 力眷, sounding like 戀 (liàn). Yang Xiong, *Jiaozhou mu zhen*: "the Duke of Zhou took up the regency, and white pheasants offered themselves; King Zhao declined, and the house of Zhou fell into disorder (亂)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 力敬 — the departing tone of 夌 (lìng). Yang Xi, *Li Zhengfang zan*: "not in concord, not in harmony, he forgot restraint and spoke in disorder (亂); his end ill, his beginning to be regretted — truly it was his nature."

**Textual notes.** The source 秋官 should read 地官, and 憲布 should read 憲巿.
