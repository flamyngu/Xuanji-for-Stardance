**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1166 #08)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 徒遼. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 田聊 — sounding like 迢 (tiáo).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To harmonize (和)." *Yupian*: "to blend in concord (和合)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "bow and arrows being well-matched (調)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-summer, tune (調) the yu-organ, the sheng-organ, the chi-flute, and the reeds."

**Senses.**
- *To tame, subdue (揉伏).* *Yun Hui*. Shiji, *Basic Annals of Qin*: "Dafei aided Shun in taming (調馴) the birds and beasts."
- *To mock, tease (嘲笑).* *Zhengzitong*. *Shishuo*: "Chancellor Wang was always teasing (調) him."
- *Also* *Guangya*: "to cheat (欺); to taunt (啁); to boast falsely (譀); to set a price (出買)."
- *Also* Zhuangzi, *Qi wu lun*: "and do you alone not see the swaying and the trembling (調調...刁刁)?"; note: "the form of branches and leaves stirring atop a tree."
- *An official title.* Zhouli, *Di guan*: "the Reconciler (調人) managed the quarrels of the myriad people and brought them to concord."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Zhouli has the Reconciler (調人); his descendants took it as their clan."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 張流, sounding like 輈 (zhōu). "Morning (朝)." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "not having seen my lord, I grieve as with morning hunger (調飢)"; Mao's tradition: "調 means morning." Also written 輈.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 徒弔, the departing tone of 迢 (diào). *Yupian*: "to select and post (選調)." Shiji, *Annals of the First Emperor*: "he levied (調) on the commanderies and counties, transporting beans, grain, fodder and straw"; note: "an order to levy and gather (調斂)." Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "the horses numbered tens of thousands, and the keepers were too few, so they conscripted (調) from the nearby counties"; Yan Shigu's note: "it means to select and dispatch them." Also *Biography of Zhang Shizhi*: "for ten years he got no posting (調)"; Yan Shigu's note: "to select." Also *Biography of Yuan Ang*: "because Ang remonstrated bluntly so often, he could not stay long at court, and was transferred (調) to be Commandant of Longxi"; Yan Shigu's note: "to move, promote."
- *To gauge, reckon (度, 算).* *Yupian*; *Zengyun*. Former Han, *Biography of Chao Cuo*: "in planning the founding of walled towns (調立城邑), none of fewer than a thousand households"; Yan Shigu's note: "it means to reckon and gauge them."
- *To seek (求).* *Yupian*.
- *A levy, tax (賦).* *Leipian*. *Zhengzitong*: "the people's levy is called 調; the Jin, on pacifying Wu, instituted the household-levy (戸調) — see the Jinshu. Also the Tang levy: of the impositions on persons there were four, the second called 調 — see the Old Tang History."
- *Tune, modulation (韻調).* *Guangyun*. *Zengyun*: "音調 is the pitch and key of music; 才調 is talent and elegance (韻致)."
- *A poem (詩).* *Yun Hui*.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 同 (tóng), fanqie 從紅.* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "I strive to rise and fall, up and down, seeking where rule and measure agree (同); Tang and Yu were grave and sought concord (合), Zhi and Gao Yao could keep harmony (調)."
