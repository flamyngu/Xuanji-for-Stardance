> 《康熙字典》〈子集上〉【一字部】頁76第10    〔古文〕：丅、𠄟【廣韻】胡雅切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】亥雅切，𡘋遐上聲。在下之下，對上之稱。【易·乾·文言】本乎地者親下。　又【說文】底也。【玉篇】後也。又賤也。　又【儀禮·士相見禮】始見于君，執摯至下。【鄭註】下謂君所。【賈疏】不言所而言下者。凡臣視袷已下，故言下也。　又【集韻】【韻會】𡘋亥駕切，遐去聲。【正韻】降也，自上而下也。【易·屯卦】以貴下賤。【詩序】君能下下。　又【爾雅·釋詁】下，落也。【郉疏】下者，自上而落也。草曰零，木曰落。　又去也。【周禮·夏官·司士】歲登，下其損益之數。　又【韻補】叶後五切，音戶。【詩·召南】于以奠之，宗室牖下。與女叶。【吳棫曰】毛詩下字一十有七，陸德明皆此讀。【𨻰第·古音考】與吳同。　又叶胡佐切，音賀。【曹丕·寡婦賦】風至兮淸厲，隂雲曀兮雨未下。伏枕兮忘寐，逮乎朝兮起坐。    考證：〔【爾雅·釋訓】下，落也。〕　謹照原書釋訓改釋詁。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; One radical 一; p.76 #10)*

**Ancient graphs.** 丅, 𠄟.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 胡雅. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 亥雅 — the rising tone of 遐 (xià).

**Etymology and senses.** The 下 of "below," the counterpart-term to "above." Yijing, *Wen yan*: "what is rooted in Earth draws near to the below (下)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "the bottom (底)." *Yupian*: "behind (後)." Also "lowly (賤)."
- *Also* Yili, *Shi xiangjian li*: "on first seeing the lord, he holds the gift and comes below (下)"; Zheng's note: "下 means the lord's place"; Jia's subcommentary: "that he says not 'place' but 'below' is because a subject looks no higher than the collar; hence he says 'below.'"
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 亥駕 — the departing tone of 遐 (xià). *Zheng Yun*: "to come down (降); to go from above downward." Yijing, *Tun* hexagram: "the noble puts himself below (下) the lowly." The *Shi* Preface: "the lord can lower himself to inferiors (下下)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi gu*: "下 means to fall (落)"; Xing's subcommentary: "下 is to drop from above; of grass one says 零, of trees one says 落."
- *To remove, send down (去).* Zhouli, *Xia guan, Si shi*: "at the year's reckoning, he sends down (下) the figures of decrease and increase."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 戶 (hù), fanqie 後五.* Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "and where do they set it out? below the window of the ancestral chamber (牖下)" — rhyming with 女. Wu Yu says: "in the *Mao shi* the graph 下 occurs seventeen times, and Lu Deming reads them all thus." Chen Di, *Gu yin kao*: "the same as Wu."
- *Rhymes as 賀 (hè), fanqie 胡佐.* Cao Pi, *Gua fu fu*: "the wind comes keen and chill, the dark clouds lower, but the rain not yet fallen (下); I lie on my pillow forgetful of sleep, and at the morning's coming rise and sit."

**Textual note.** Erya, *Shi xun*, "下 means to fall" — checked against the original book, *Shi xun* (釋訓) is corrected to *Shi gu* (釋詁).
