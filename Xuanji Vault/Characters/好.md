**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Woman radical 女; p.255 #11)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𡥆, 𡚽, 𩐔.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 呼皓. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許皓 — the rising tone of 蒿 (hǎo).

**Etymology and senses.** "Fine (美); good (善)." Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "the zithers are at hand, and all is calm and good (靜好)."
- *Also, to be on good terms (相善).* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "forever to keep this friendship (好)."
- *Also a friendly meeting (好會).* Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "with the wan-tablet to seal amity (好)." Zuozhuan, Wen yr 12: "by my ruler's command, to seal the amity (好) of the two states."
- *Also a personal name.* Zhang Haohao (張好好), aged thirteen, fair and lovely and a fine singer, whom Du Mu set on the register of musicians; see Tang shu, *Biography of Du Mu*.
- *Also a tune-name.* The Lord of Wuyi gathered his kindred on the mountaintop, where immortal music vied in playing, and they sang "Renjian hao" (人閒好, the good things of the world of men); see the Wuyi shan zhi.
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 呼到; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 虛到 — sounding like 耗 (hào). *Shuowen*: "to love and not let go." The woman's nature is soft and clinging; when she loves a thing she cherishes it and will not let go, hence in the graph a woman (女) with a child (子) makes 好. Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "in my inmost heart I love (好) him."
- *Also, a hole (孔).* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the bi-disc's diameter is a foot, its bore (好) three inches, taken as the measure"; note: "羨 is the diameter; the bi's diameter sets the standard; 好 is the hole in the bi-disc."
- *Also a surname*; see the Zuanwen.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 呼厚, sounding like 吼 (hǒu). Wang Bao, *Jiangde lun*: "of Mao Qiang and Xi Shi, even the skilled slanderer cannot conceal their beauty (好); of Mo Mu and Wo Gui, even the skilled praiser cannot cover their ugliness."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 滂佩, sounding like 配 (pèi). Chuci, *Jiu zhang*: "from former ages the worthy have been envied: they say the basil and angelica may not be worn; envying the fragrance of the fair and alluring, they make out that even Mo Mu was comely and well-pleased with herself (自好)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 呼𠋫, sounding like 詬 (hòu). Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "are there no other men? — but you are my dear (好)" — rhyming with 究 above.

**Textual notes.** The source of the wan-tablet citation, *Diguan*, should read *Chunguan*. And in the Zuozhuan citation, 先君 should read 寡君 (per the original).
