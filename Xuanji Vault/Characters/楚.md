**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.539 #23)*

**Ancient graph.** 𣕪.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 創舉. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 創阻 — the rising tone of 粗 (cū), i.e. **chǔ**. Also written 䠂.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A thicket of trees"; one sense: the chaste-tree (荆 *jīng*, vitex). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "thick stand the thorn-thickets (楚楚)"; commentary: "楚楚, the look of thorny brush."

**Senses.**
- *The chaste-tree / brushwood.* Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "tall and tangled the mixed brush — cut its chaste-tree (楚)"; subcommentary: "of the vitex kind; though all the firewood is high, the 楚 stands tallest." Liji, *Xue ji*: "the cane and the rod (夏楚), two things, gather in their awe"; commentary: "楚 is vitex, used to flog those who breach the rites."
- *Changchu, a plant — the carambola (yang-tao).* Shijing, *Kuai feng*: "in the marsh is the *changchu*, supple are its boughs."
- *Bright, fresh (楚楚).* Shijing, *Kuai feng*: "the mayfly's wings — its robes so fresh and trim (楚楚)."
- *Bitter pain (辛楚)*, also written 憷. Lu Ji's poem to his brother Shi-heng: "stirred and grieving, full of bitter pain."
- *A state.* Shu, *Yu gong*: the region of Jingzhou; in Zhou, first enfeoffed to Xiong Yi.
- *The Three Chu, a regional division.* Shiji, *Huozhi liezhuan*: Huaibei, Chen, and Runan commanderies are Western Chu; from Pengcheng eastward — Donghai, Wu, Guangling — are Eastern Chu; Hengshan, Jiujiang, Jiangnan, Yuzhang, Changsha are Southern Chu.
- *The South.* Hou Han, Shi Cen's *Chu shi song*: "the north wind turns to Chu"; Li Shan's commentary: "*shuo* is the north, *Chu* the south."
- *Surname.* Zuozhuan: in Jin, the diviner Chu Qiu; the steward of Zhao Xiangzi, Chu Long.
- *Sharpness (departing tone).* *Tang Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 瘡據, the departing tone of 粗 — "楚 is sharpness."
- *A tree* growing on Mt. Li.
- *Read 疎 (shū)* — *Leipian*: fanqie 山於. Du Fu's "Seeing off Meng the Twelfth": "the autumn wind, the Chu-bamboo cold; in night-snow the Gong plums are spring-like."
- *Rhymes as 杪 (miǎo), fanqie 弭沼.* Former Han, *Biography of Liu Jiao, Prince Yuan of Chu*: "the Supreme One's four sons — Bo died young; Zhong became king in his stead; this dwelling is Chu.
