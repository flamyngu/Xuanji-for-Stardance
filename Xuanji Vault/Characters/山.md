**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, middle; Mountain radical 山; p.307 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 所閒. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 師閒. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 師姦 — the same sound as 刪 (shān).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A mountain is a giving-forth (宣); it gives forth breath, scattering it to give birth to the myriad things; it has rock and is high." Xu (Xu Kai) says: "it pictures the form of mountain-peaks rising together." *Shiming*: "山 means to produce (產); it is what produces the myriad things." Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "Heaven and Earth being set in place, mountain and marsh exchange breath." Shu, *Yu gong*: "he fixed the high mountains and great streams." Erya, *Shi shan*: "south of the He, Mount Hua; west of the He, Mount Yue; east of the He, Mount Dai; north of the He, Mount Heng; south of the Jiang, Mount Heng (衡) — the *Zhouli* calls them the Guardian-mountains (鎮)"; Zheng's note: "the Guardians are the famous mountains that steady the Earth's power."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Shanhai jing: the mountains are divided into five canons — east, west, south, north, and centre. The south runs from central Shu southwestward to the bounds of the mountains of Wu and Yue. The west runs from Huayin and Bozhong to Kunlun, Jishi, and the rest — today the land beyond Yumen in Longxi and Gansu. The north runs from Huqi and Daxing to Wangwu, Mengmen, and the rest — this is the bounds of the two provinces Ji and Yong of the *Yu gong*. The east runs from Taidai and Guye along the seaboard regions — this is the land of Qingzhou, Qi, and Lu of the *Yu gong*. Seen in the *Du Shanhaijing yu*.
- *Lianshan (連山), the name of an ancient *Yi*.* Zhouli, *Chun guan*: "he has charge of the methods of the Three Changes (三易); the first is called Lianshan (連山)"; note: "like the mountains' giving forth and taking in of breath."
- *Surname.* Descendants of the ancient Lieshan clan (烈山氏). Also Gongshan (公山), a compound surname.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 所旃, sounding like 仙 (xiān). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "deep, deep the southern hills (南山)" — rhyming with 干 above, 干 sounded 堅. Also Confucius, *Qiuling ge*: "with a sigh I turn my thoughts, and behold yonder Mount Tai (泰山); densely it towers in its height, and the Liangfu winds and joins on." A note: 山 is in the 刪 rhyme; anciently the sounds 寒, 刪, and 先 ran together by transformation, so this is more than a mere rhyme-loan.
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie 疏臻, sounding like 甡 (shēn). Ban Gu, *Dong du fu*: "they belch flame and breed wind, gulping the wilds and spouting the hills (歕山)" — rhyming with 振 below. *Zhengzitong*: "this radical runs together with the three radicals 土 (earth), 阜 (mound), and 石 (stone) — see each cross-referenced."

**Textual note.** Erya, *Shi shan*, "...south of the Jiang, Mount Heng — these are the Five Marchmounts (是爲五嶽); Zheng's note: the Guardians are the famous mountains that steady the Earth's power" — a note: "these are the Five Marchmounts" is not the original text of the *Erya*, and "the Guardians are the famous mountains that steady the Earth's power" is the *Zhouli* note, not the *Erya* note; accordingly "these are the Five Marchmounts" is changed to "the *Zhouli* calls them the Guardians (鎮)," to introduce the note-text.
