**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Bow radical 弓; p.357 #31)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 戸田. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 戸千 — sounding like 賢 (xián), i.e. **xián**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The string of a bow." It follows 弓 (bow), with a shape imaging the silk and the string-notch. Yili, *Xiang she li* (District Archery): "the officer holds the bow-grip in his left hand, the string in his right, and so presents the bow."

**Senses.**
- *The half-moon.* *Shiming*: "弦 names the half-moon: its form curved on one side, straight on the other, like a bow with the string strung." Former Han, *Treatise on the Calendar*: "Chunyu Linggu re-examined the Taichu calendar; the new moon, dark moon, half-moons (弦), and full moon were all most precise."
- *A state.* Chunqiu, Xi yr 5: "the men of Chu destroyed Xian; the Viscount of Xian fled to Huang"; commentary: "the state of Xian lay southeast of Dai county in Yiyang."
- *Surname.* Guangyun (citing Fengsu Tong): "descendants of the Viscount of Xian." Zuozhuan: the Zheng merchant Xian Gao (Xi yr 23); Chen Qi of Qi sent Xian Shi to rescue the Fan clan (Ai yr 4); Xian Duo was consulted about the qin (Ai yr 11).
- *A pulse that is taut (a medical term).* Shiji, *Biography of Cang-gong*: "the pulse was long and taut (弦)."
- *Place names.* Xianpu and Xianzhong. Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "due west is Yongzhou; its marsh is Xianpu" (commentary: in Qian county); and north of the Yongzhou hills is the Xianzhong valley in Pugu district.
- *Interchangeable with 絃 (string of an instrument).* Liji, *Wenwang shizi*: "in spring they recite, in summer they play the strings (弦)." Liji, *Yue ji*: "Shun made the five-stringed (五弦) qin, to sing the 'South Wind.'
