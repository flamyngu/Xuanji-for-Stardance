**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls з”і, upper; Grass radical иүё; p.1048 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie иҺ«зҙ…. *Ji Yun*: fanqie и¬Ёи“¬ вҖ” sounding like жҝӣ (mГ©ng).

**Etymology and senses.** Erya, *Shi cao*: "и’ҷ is the wangnГј (зҺӢеҘі)"; note: "another name for the dodder (еҘіиҳҝ)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* the *Shi* tradition: "tangmeng (е”җи’ҷ), a vegetable-name."
- *Also* dameng (еӨ§и’ҷ), a drug-name. Guanzi, *Diyuan*: "where the host of drugs safely grow вҖ” the small xin and the great meng (еӨ§и’ҷ)."
- *Also* the *Yi* subcommentary: "и’ҷ is the name for the faint, dim, dark, and weak." The *Shu, Hong fan* tradition: "и’ҷ means yin-darkness (йҡӮй—Ү)."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 1: "and made Wei deceive (и’ҷ) his late ruler"; note: "to deceive."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 13: "the men of Jin seized Jisun Yiru, and covered (и’ҷ) him with a tent"; note: "to wrap."
- *Also* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Xuan*: "though there be calamity, still I will brave (и’ҷ) death to preserve him"; note: "to risk."
- *Also* Shu, *Yi xun*: "he set out instruction fully for the young scholars (и’ҷеЈ«)"; subcommentary: "it means the young and untaught, a term for the lowly and small."
- *Also* Shijing, *Qin feng*: "the feather-painted shield, well-wrought (и’ҷдјҗжңүиӢ‘)"; tradition: "и’ҷ means feather-trimming (иЁҺзҫҪ)"; Jian: "the figure of mixed feathers was painted on the shield."
- *A county-name.* Shiji, *Biographies of Laozi and Zhuangzi*: "Zhuangzi was a man of Meng (и’ҷ)"; note: "by the *Treatise on Geography*, Meng county belonged to the kingdom of Liang."
- *A mountain-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Meng and the Yu were made tillable (и’ҷзҫҪе…¶и—қ)"; subcommentary: "Mount Meng is southwest of Mengyin county in Mount Tai." Also: "the Cai and the Meng were sacrificed-to and set in order (и”Ўи’ҷж—…е№і)"; note: "Mount Meng is in Qingyi county of Shu commandery."
- *A water-name.* Chuci, *Tian wen*: "issuing from the Tang valley, it lodges at the Meng-shoal (и’ҷжұң)"; note: "at dusk it enters the brink of the Meng water at the far west."
- *A gate-name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 27: "the Duke of Song and the grandees of the feudal lords covenanted outside the Meng gate (и’ҷй–Җ)"; note: "a city-gate of Song."
- *Surname.* *Fengsu tong*: "the lord of Dongmeng took Mount Meng (и’ҷеұұ) for his clan-name; Qin had the general Meng Ao."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie жҜҚзёҪ, sounding like жҮө (mДӣng). Liu Zongyuan's prose: "the leather-bag, vast and dim (и’ҷйҙ»)"; note: "both graphs are in the rising tone."
- *Also* fanqie иҺ«йіі, sounding like еӯҹ (mГЁng). Same as йӣә (mist). The *Hanshu* cites the *Yi* tradition: "there is the shedding mist and fog (и’ҷйңҝ), above and below joining."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie иҺ«жұҹ, sounding like е°Ё (mГЎng).* The *Shi*: "the fox-fur, rough and shaggy (и’ҷиҢё)"; Xu Miao read it as еҺ–. Chen Lin, *Da huang fu*: "the Emperor told me of utmost compliance, and charged me again with the child's ignorance (з«Ҙи’ҷ); the sense merges with Confucius, the principle returns alike to King Wen."

**Textual note.** Where the *Yunbu* reading is glossed "йҹіе°Ё" with the *Shi* line "зӢҗиЈҳи’ҷиҢё" вҖ” the *Shi* here is the *Mao shi*, *Bei feng* "Mao qiu," read against Xu Miao's gloss еҺ–.
