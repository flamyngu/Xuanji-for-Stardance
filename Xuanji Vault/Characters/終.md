**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Silk radical 糸; p.921 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣊂, 𤽘, 𣧩, 𠔾, 𡦿, 𢍐, 𣈩, 𤽬, 夊.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 職戎. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 之戎 — sounding like 螽 (zhōng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "[to wind off] the slack silk (絿絲)."
- *The utmost, the exhausted (極, 窮).* *Yupian.* *Ji Yun*: "one sense, to use up (盡)." Yijing, *Xici*: "the Yi as a book traces beginnings and seeks ends (要終)." Shu, *Zhonghui zhi gao*: "be careful of the end (終), [mindful of] its beginning." Shijing, *Daya*: "lofty and bright, a good end (令終)."
- Liji, *Tan gong*: "of the gentleman one says 終 ('came to his end'); of the small man, 死 ('died')."
- *The year's end (the intercalary point).* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 1: "the former kings' calendar: set the start at the origin, the mid at the middle, return the surplus to the end (終)"; subcommentary: "return the remainder to the year's end — i.e. there place the intercalary month."
- *A cycle.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 9: "twelve years now — this is called one 終 (cycle), one Jupiter-cycle." Erya, *Shi tian*: "the moon in [position] 壬 is called 終."
- *A land measure.* Former Han, *Treatise on Punishments*: "a square *li* is a 井; ten 井 a 通; ten 通 a 成 (ten *li* square); ten 成 a 終."
- *Surname.* Zuozhuan, Ding yr 4: among the seven Yin clans, the 終葵 (Zhongkui). Shiji, *Qin annals*: a 終黎 (Zhongli) clan. Former Han, *Biography of Zhong Jun*: Zhong Jun, styled Ziyun, of Jinan.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 諸仍.* Zhouyi, *Kun Wenyan*: "以終 rhyming with 成."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 諸良.* Chen Lin, *Rhapsody on Rosemary*: "…and there is no beginning that has no end (終)."
