**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Yawn radical 欠; p.566 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 許斤 — sounding like 訢 (xīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to laugh and rejoice (笑喜)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "rewards and bounties are then dispensed, and none but rejoice and are glad (欣說)." Zhou yu: "in serving the spirits and protecting the people, none but rejoice (欣喜)."
- *Also 欣欣, the look of joy.* Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "the lord, joyful (欣欣), is glad and at ease." Tao Qian, *Guiqulai ci*: "the trees, flourishing joyfully (欣欣), turn toward bloom." Also written 訢. Former Han, *Biography of Jia Shan*: "all under Heaven rejoicing (訢訢), about to raise up the way of Yao and Shun."
- *Also written 忻.* Shiji, appraisal to the *Biographies of Guan and Yan*: "to hold the whip for him — that would be my glad longing (忻慕)."
- *Also a name for a beast of great strength.* Erya, *Shishou*: "a hare of utmost strength is the xin (欣); an ox of utmost strength is the xinjia (欣犌)."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* *Guangyun*: "originally the land of Yangqu county under the Han; the Sui set up Xinzhou (欣州), named after Xinkou (欣口)."
- *Also a surname*; see the Qixing tong.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 虛言, sounding like 軒 (xuān). Lu Yun, *poem presented to Director Gu*: "the flower's glory has shone, its lingering light hard to prolong; our meeting shallow, our parting swift, grief succeeds (紹) to joy (欣)."

**On the graph.** *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 俽, 惞.
