**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Evening radical 夕; p.247 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 莫鳳. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蒙弄 — the departing tone of 蒙 (mèng).

**Etymology and senses.** The opposite of waking; the shapes of things seen in sleep. Shu, *Yue ming*: "the king dreamed (夢) that God gave him a good helper." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "then they divined my dream (夢)." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Zhanmeng*: "by sun, moon, and stars he divines the fortune of the six dreams: first the regular dream (正夢), second the dread dream (噩夢), third the brooding dream (思夢), fourth the waking dream (寤夢), fifth the glad dream (喜夢), sixth the fearful dream (懼夢)." Also: "the Grand Diviner has charge of the method of the three dreams: first the induced dream (致夢), second the odd dream (觭夢)"; note: "a strange dream"; "third the xianzhi (咸陟)"; note: "to ascend, to advance — having the dream without thought or care; also written 咸夢." Zhuangzi, *Qiwu lun*: "once Zhuang Zhou dreamed (夢) he was a butterfly, fluttering and content, a butterfly; suddenly he awoke, and there, startled, was Zhou." Zhangzi, *Zhengmeng*: "in dreaming, the form is shut and the breath concentrated within; dreams arise from what is old in the habituated mind — hungry, one dreams of taking; sated, one dreams of giving."
- *Also a marsh-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "the lands of Yun and Meng (雲土夢) were made fit." Also Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "Chu has seven marshes, the first called Yunmeng (雲夢); Yunmeng is nine hundred li square." A note: in the Zuozhuan and Hanshu, 雲夢 (as 夢) has both the level and departing tones.
- *Also a water-name.* The Meng waters (夢水), east of Yichun county in Yuanzhou. Huanyu ji: "of old Zhong Yi wished to site a county here and prayed by night for a dream (夢); it accorded with his prayer, so he named the county Si (思) and the water the Meng waters (夢水)."
- *Also a surname*; see the Tongpu.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 莫紅; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 謨中 — sounding like 蒙 (méng). Pan Yue, *Ai yongshi wen*: "I have met it before my eyes, yet there was no omen; even waking and sleeping I do not dream (夢) of you; so I look back upon the household ways, and long entrust my heart to your person."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 彌登, sounding like 萌 (méng). Shijing, *Qifeng*: "gladly with you I would share a dream (夢)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 諸良, sounding like 章 (zhāng). Chen Lin, *Dahuang fu*: "fearing the milfoil-omen held confusion, I withdrew to compose my thoughts in the orchid chamber; my soul, restless, met with the spirit, and again it diagnosed for me a fair dream (夢)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 暮傍 — the departing tone of 茫 (màng). A Daozang hymn: "the crimson robe marks the great assembly; the start of life is like a long dream (夢); virtue hidden, its traces drawn within, in utmost stillness one is unaware of the present."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "夢 means dim, unclear (不明). It follows 夕 (evening), with 瞢 abbreviated as phonetic."

**Textual notes.** In the Xiaoya citation, 斯夢 should read 我夢.
