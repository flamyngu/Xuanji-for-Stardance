**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1165 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Wuyin jiyun*: fanqie 是爲. *Yupian*: fanqie 是推 — sounding like 垂 (chuí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "who, what (何)." *Yupian*: "not knowing his name." Yijing, *Tong ren*: "out the gate, fellowship — who, then, can blame?" Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "who tends it? the grave youngest daughter." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 1: "who shall say it is not so?" Laozi: "I know not whose (誰) child it is." Zhuangzi, *Tian yun*: "a child, born five months, can speak, and before it is a toddler already names people (誰)"; note: "before childhood it already knows people's surnames and names — asking 'who.'"
- *誰昔, long ago.* Erya, *Shi xun.* Shijing, *Guo feng*: "誰昔然矣"; tradition: "like saying 疇昔 (former times)." Guo Pu: "誰, an opening particle."
- *誰何, to interrogate.* *Zheng Yun.* Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "set out sharp weapons and challenged all comers (誰何)"; Shigu: "to ask who one is." *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "the 大誰卒"; Shigu: "in charge of challenging unusual persons — 'what is your name?'; 大誰 comes from the 'who-goes-there' challenge, used to name the office (大誰長), the 卒 being the soldiers under him."
- *Surname.* *Wanxing tongpu*: Shui Long, an officer of Jiujiang prefecture in the Ming Zhengde era.
- *Interchangeable with 孰 (Dushu tong).* Zhuangzi, *Lie Yukou*: "none waking, none aware — what comparing (孰) is there?" Also with 疇: Shu, *Shun dian*: "疇若予上下草木鳥獸" = 孰若, both the same as 誰.
- *Yun Hui*: also written 譙. Shiji, *Biography of Wei Wan*: "did not reprove (譙呵) Wan"; note: "to upbraid; 譙 sounds 誰, 呵 sounds 何." The Hanshu writes 孰何; Li Qi: "孰, who; 何, to scold"; Shigu: "不誰何 is like saying 'did not so much as question him.'" *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 唯.
