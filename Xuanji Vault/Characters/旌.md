**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҜ, lower; Square radical ж–№; p.483 #19)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еӯҗзӣҲ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie е’ЁзӣҲ вҖ” sounding like зІҫ (jД«ng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "split feathers fixed on a yak-tail at the staff-head, by which the soldiers are urged forward." Erya, *Shitian*: "a yak-tail fixed at the staff-head is called ж—Ң"; note: "the yak-tail set at the pole's top, like today's banner (е№ў), also having streamers." Also: ж—Ңж—Ӯ; subcommentary: "as for ж—Ңж—Ӯ вҖ” though the names of banners differ, ж—Ңж—Ӯ is the general term." Guangya: "the Son of Heaven's ж—Ң is nine ren high, a feudal lord's seven, a grandee's five, an officer's three." *Shiming*: "ж—Ң means brilliance (зІҫ): it has a bright sheen." Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "lonely and high, the banner (е№Іж—Ң)." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Sichang*: "split feathers make the ж—Ң." Also: "the roving-chariot bears the ж—Ң."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhangshe*: "for the curtain-palace he sets up the banner-gate (ж—Ңй–Җ)"; note: "planting banners to mark the gate."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Diguan, Zhangjie*: "on the roads the banner-tally (ж—ҢзҜҖ) is used"; note: "this is the tally that envoys today carry."
- *Also* Liji, *Quli*: "the war-chariot lets its banner stream (з¶Ҹж—Ң), the virtue-chariot binds up its banner (зөҗж—Ң)"; note: "the war-chariot esteems martial might, so the banner is loosed and spread like a flower; virtue's beauty is within, so the banner is wound about the pole."
- *Also* Shu, *Bi ming*: "mark out (ж—Ң) and distinguish the good from the evil"; tradition: "it says one should discern the good and evil of the obstinate people"; subcommentary: "banners serve to mark out the noble and base, hence the tradition takes ж—Ң as 'to mark.'"
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 28: "and to make manifest (ж—Ң) the ruler's merit"; note: "ж—Ң means to display (з« )."
- *Also* Zhou yu: "therefore he gave chariot and robe to signalize (ж—Ң) him"; note: "ж—Ң means to mark out (иЎЁ)."
- *Also* Later Han, *Biography of Hu Guang*: "by virtue to make known (ж—Ң) the worthy"; note: "ж—Ң means to illumine (жҳҺ)."

**Textual notes.** In the Erya, *Shitian* citation, the graph иЁ» is added before ж—„ ("иЁ»ж—„йҰ–жӣ°ж—Ң"); the nineteen graphs on ж—Ңж—Ӯ are likewise Erya text, to be placed after "дәҰжңүж—’"; and е»әж—Ң should read ијүж—Ң (per the Zhouli original).
