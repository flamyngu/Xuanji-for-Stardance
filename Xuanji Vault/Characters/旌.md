**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Square radical 方; p.483 #19)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子盈. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 咨盈 — sounding like 精 (jīng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "split feathers fixed on a yak-tail at the staff-head, by which the soldiers are urged forward." Erya, *Shitian*: "a yak-tail fixed at the staff-head is called 旌"; note: "the yak-tail set at the pole's top, like today's banner (幢), also having streamers." Also: 旌旂; subcommentary: "as for 旌旂 — though the names of banners differ, 旌旂 is the general term." Guangya: "the Son of Heaven's 旌 is nine ren high, a feudal lord's seven, a grandee's five, an officer's three." *Shiming*: "旌 means brilliance (精): it has a bright sheen." Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "lonely and high, the banner (干旌)." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Sichang*: "split feathers make the 旌." Also: "the roving-chariot bears the 旌."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhangshe*: "for the curtain-palace he sets up the banner-gate (旌門)"; note: "planting banners to mark the gate."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Diguan, Zhangjie*: "on the roads the banner-tally (旌節) is used"; note: "this is the tally that envoys today carry."
- *Also* Liji, *Quli*: "the war-chariot lets its banner stream (綏旌), the virtue-chariot binds up its banner (結旌)"; note: "the war-chariot esteems martial might, so the banner is loosed and spread like a flower; virtue's beauty is within, so the banner is wound about the pole."
- *Also* Shu, *Bi ming*: "mark out (旌) and distinguish the good from the evil"; tradition: "it says one should discern the good and evil of the obstinate people"; subcommentary: "banners serve to mark out the noble and base, hence the tradition takes 旌 as 'to mark.'"
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 28: "and to make manifest (旌) the ruler's merit"; note: "旌 means to display (章)."
- *Also* Zhou yu: "therefore he gave chariot and robe to signalize (旌) him"; note: "旌 means to mark out (表)."
- *Also* Later Han, *Biography of Hu Guang*: "by virtue to make known (旌) the worthy"; note: "旌 means to illumine (明)."

**Textual notes.** In the Erya, *Shitian* citation, the graph 註 is added before 旄 ("註旄首曰旌"); the nineteen graphs on 旌旂 are likewise Erya text, to be placed after "亦有旒"; and 建旌 should read 載旌 (per the Zhouli original).
