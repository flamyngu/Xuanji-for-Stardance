**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.615 #12)*

**Ancient graph.** 洤.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 疾緣. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 從緣. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 才緣 — sounding like 全 (quán).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the source of water (水原). It pictures water flowing to form a stream." Yijing, *Meng* image: "from beneath the mountain issues a spring (泉)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shishui*: "the lanquan (濫泉) issues straight up — straight up means welling up. The woquan (沃泉) issues hanging down — hanging down means flowing downward. The guiquan (氿泉) issues from a hole — from a hole means issuing sideways."
- *Also, springs issuing from one source but parting to different ends are called feiquan (肥泉).* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "I think of the Fei Spring (肥泉), and over it long I sigh."
- *Also liquan (醴泉, the sweet-wine spring).* Liji, *Liyun*: "Heaven sends down rich dew, Earth puts forth the sweet spring (醴泉)."
- *Also a spring with radiant brilliance is called rongquan (榮泉).* Former Han, *Suburban-sacrifice hymns*: "feeding on sweet dew, drinking of the glory-spring (榮泉)."
- *Also a waterfall is called liquan (立泉).* Ban Gu, *Zhongnan shan fu*: "the standing-spring (立泉) falls and falls."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* Guangyu ji: "in Zhou times it was the land of the Seven Min; the Sui called it Wenling, the Tang Quanzhou (泉州)."
- *Also 天泉 (Tianquan), a star-name.* Gan's *Xingjing*: "the Celestial Spring (天泉) is ten stars, east of the Turtle; one calls it the Great Sea, governing irrigation and the canals."
- *Also 龍泉 (Longquan), a sword-name; that is, Longyuan (龍淵).* Du Fu's poem: "three feet of steel — I present the Longquan (龍泉)."
- *Also a surname.* The Nan shi has Quan Qi (泉企).
- *Also huoquan (貨泉) is coin (錢).* Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Outer Treasury has charge of the issue and receipt of bu-money"; note: "bu (布) is quan (泉); hoarded it is called 泉, circulating it is called 布, taking its name from water-springs, since its circulation reaches everywhere." Also note to *Diguan, Quanfu*: "泉 is sometimes written 錢."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 疾眷 — the departing tone of 全 (quàn); same sense.
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 才勻, sounding like 秦 (qín). Li You, *Dongguan ming*: "the chambers and doors are ranged within, latticed windows displayed without; this is the Eastern Lodge, a grove and spring (林泉) of books."

**On the graph.** *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 灥; also 𤽄, 𤿁, 𤆁.
