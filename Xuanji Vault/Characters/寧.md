**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.291 #08)*

**Ancient graph.** 寍.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 奴丁. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 囊丁. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 奴經 — the level tone of 佞 (níng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a particle of wishing (願詞). It follows 丂, with 寍 as phonetic."
- *Also, peace (安).* Yijing, *Qian*: "rising as the first of all things, the myriad states are all at peace (寧)." Shijing, *Daya*: "King Wen had renown, and ever sought their peace (寧)." Shu, *Kang gao*: "be liberal, and thereby give the people peace (寧)"; note: "practising lenient government, and so settling the people."
- *Also* Shu, *Hongfan*: "of the Five Blessings, the third is ease and peace (康寧)"; note: "freedom from sickness."
- *Also a married woman's returning to visit her parents is called 寧.* Shijing, *Zhounan*: "going home to comfort (寧) her father and mother."
- *Also 予寧 is to take leave for mourning.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Ai*: "for the death of a parent of an Academician's disciple, the son is granted mourning-leave (寧) for three years."
- *Also 無寧 is "rather" (寧), a particle of wishing.* Zuozhuan, Yin yr 11: "would it not be better (無寧) that this Lord of Xu again maintain his altars?" Also Xiang yr 26: "if one must err, better (寧) to over-reward than to over-punish." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "rather than slay the innocent, better (寧) to err in not following the rule."
- *Also 丁寧 (dingning) is to enjoin earnestly and repeatedly.* Former Han, *Biography of Lang Yi*: "enjoining (丁寧) two and three times." Vulgarly written 叮嚀.
- *Also 丁寧 is a gong (鉦).* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 4: "displayed on the dingning (丁寧)"; the Correct Meaning: "to say 'displayed on the dingning' shows the dingning is an implement." Jin yu: "an attack is furnished with bells and drums, to proclaim the enemy's guilt; battle uses the chunyu and the dingning (丁寧), to alert one's own people — so the dingning is a thing used in war."
- *Also a prefecture-name*; the Qin's Beijun; the Wei set up Huazhou, the Western Wei renamed it Ningzhou (寧州).
- *Also a surname.*
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 乃定, sounding like 佞 (nìng); interchangeable with 甯. Former Han, *Suburban-sacrifice hymns*: "abundant, abundant, returning to the upright, fulfilling our former wish (往甯)"; Yan Shigu's note: "the rhyme is in the level tone; it means, the blessings won being many, we return to the right way, able to attain what we wished in former days."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 乃挺 — the rising tone of 佞 (nǐng). Zhang Zai, *Qiming*: "the king's plans fill the four bounds, all the realm hushed and at peace (寧); the southern marches lay by their beacons, the eastern frontiers lift their alarms."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 女良, sounding like 娘 (niáng). Su Shi, *Stele of Duke Fu of Zheng*: "stately Duke Han, ranged with the Lai minister; twice sent on embassy to Yan, the four quarters thereby at peace (寧)."

**On the graph.** *Yun Hui*: properly written 寧; the classics and histories write 寧; vulgarly written 寜. 寧 follows 丂.
