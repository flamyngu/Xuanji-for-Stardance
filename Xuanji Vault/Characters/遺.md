**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Walk radical 辵; p.1265 #18)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𨗮, 𨖽, 𨗭.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 以追. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 夷隹 — sounding like 夷 (yí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to lose (亡)." Yijing, *Tai*: "he does not abandon the distant (不遐遺)"; note: "his mind being broad and great, he casts nothing away (遺)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "casting me off as a thing left behind (遺)"; note: "forgotten and gone, no more kept in mind." Zhouli, *Qiuguan, Sici*: "the third is [pardon for] forgetfulness (遺忘)."
- *To let slip, fail of (失).* *Zheng Yun.* Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "no merit is lost (遺)." Later Han, *Biography of Huan Rong*: "his counsels left no oversight (遺計)."
- *What is left over (餘).* *Ji Yun.* Liji, *Yueji*: "there is a lingering tone (遺音)"; note: "a sound not exhausted." Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "I follow the legacy-rule (遺則) of Peng Xian"; note: "遺 means what remains."
- *To leave behind, bequeath (留).* Shu, *Dagao*: "King Ning left (遺) me the great treasure-tortoise." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 19: "a survival (遺) of the ancients' love."
- *To make water.* *Zihui bu.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "he made water (小遺) upon the hall." Nanshi, *Biography of Xie Jiqing*: "his urine (小遺) splashed the clerk"; note: "that is what is colloquially called urinating."
- *遺遺, interchangeable with 委蛇 (winding).* *Zhengzitong.* Zhanguo ce: "they went out by the winding gate (遺遺之門)"; note: "the road was meandering." Guanzi, *Shuyan*: "winding and yielding (遺遺), as though there were a course to follow."
- *A surname.* Descendants of Nan Yi, steward of Fei in Lu.

**Departing tone (wèi).** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 以醉 — the departing tone of 遺, i.e. **wèi**. "To present, to give."
- *To bestow.* Note to Zhouli, *Diguan, Yiren*: "to bestow on others by way of gifts (餽遺)." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 1: "let me make a present (遺) of it to him."
- *Yupian*: fanqie 余恚; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 于位 — sounding like 𧸽 (wèi). *Ji Yun*: fanqie 徐醉, sounding like 遂 (suì); fanqie 盈之, sounding like 怡 (yí); fanqie 愈水, sounding like 唯 (wěi); the sense in all the same.

**Further reading.**
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 旬爲; *Dengyun*: fanqie 似爲 — same as 隨 (to follow): "to be humble and place oneself below others." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "none will stoop and yield (下遺)"; note: "遺 sounds 隨.
