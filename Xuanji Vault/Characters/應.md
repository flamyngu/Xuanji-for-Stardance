**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҜ, upper; Heart radical еҝғ; p.405 #21)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie ж–јйҷө, sounding like иҶә (yД«ng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To be fitting, to answer to (з•¶)." It follows еҝғ (heart) with рӨё° as phonetic. Xu (Xu Kai) says: "рӨё° is the graph й·№ (hawk)." Originally written рӨ»®, now written жҮү.

**Senses.**
- *A word of conjecture (ж–ҷеәҰиҫӯ).* In Tang poetry, жҮүй Ҳ (surely must) and зҘҮжҮү (can only) are all of this.
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "his uncle indeed received it (жҮү) and yet bore him hate"; note: "like to receive (еҸ—)."
- *A state-name.* *Kuodi zhi*: "the old city of Ying (жҮүеҹҺ), named from Mount Ying, is in Ye county of Ruzhou."
- *Surname.* It comes from Nandun, descended from King Wu of Zhou. Zuozhuan, Xi yr 24: "Han, Jin, Ying, and Han (йӮ—жҷүжҮүйҹ“) were of the line of Wu (жӯҰд№Ӣз©Ҷ)." The Han had Ying Yao, who withdrew into hiding with the Four Whitebeards вҖ” Yao alone never came forth; his eighth-generation descendant Ying Shao made the collected commentary on the *Hanshu*.
- *Also* interchangeable with иҶә. Shu, *Wu cheng*: "he greatly received (иҶә) the mandate of Heaven"; note: "to answer to (з•¶)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie ж–јиӯү, sounding like иӯҚ (yГ¬ng). *Ji Yun*: "to answer (зӯ”)." *Guangyun*: "things answering to one another (зӣёжҮү)." Yijing, *Xian* hexagram: "the two breaths feel and respond (ж„ҹжҮү), and so join."
- *A music-name.* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Shengshi*, note on the ying-music (жҮүжЁӮ): "the ying is six е°ә five еҜё long, shaped like the zhu, with a mallet joined to its base, struck left and right, to answer (жҮү) the zhu." The *Yue shu*: "the ying-music is like the hawk's answering to things вҖ” its take being small; hence the small drum and small pestle are called the ying, being that whereby the great is answered. The small hand-drum is called the ying-drum (жҮүйј“)." Zhouli, *Chun guan, Xiaoshi*: "they beat the ying-drum (жҮүйј“)"; note: "a hand-drum (йјҷ)."
- *Also* the Son of Heaven's gate is called the Ying Gate (жҮүй–Җ). Shijing, *Daya*: "then he set up the Ying Gate (жҮүй–Җ)"; note: "the main gate."
- *A prefecture-name.* In the land of Yanmen, the Tang set up Yingzhou (жҮүе·һ).

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as йӣҚ (yЕҚng), fanqie ж–је®№.* Yijing, *Meng* hexagram: "the youthful and ignorant seek me; its will responds (жҮү); at the first divination I tell, because of the firmness within." Lu Jia, *Xin yu*: "affairs follow one another by kind, and sounds answer (жҮү) one another by tone."

**On the graph.** The graph жҮү has all along in the classics and histories had two sounds, level and departing; the *Zhengzitong* gives only the departing, which is wrong.
