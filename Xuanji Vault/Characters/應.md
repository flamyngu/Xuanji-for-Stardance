**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.405 #21)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 於陵, sounding like 膺 (yīng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To be fitting, to answer to (當)." It follows 心 (heart) with 𤸰 as phonetic. Xu (Xu Kai) says: "𤸰 is the graph 鷹 (hawk)." Originally written 𤻮, now written 應.

**Senses.**
- *A word of conjecture (料度辭).* In Tang poetry, 應須 (surely must) and 祇應 (can only) are all of this.
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "his uncle indeed received it (應) and yet bore him hate"; note: "like to receive (受)."
- *A state-name.* *Kuodi zhi*: "the old city of Ying (應城), named from Mount Ying, is in Ye county of Ruzhou."
- *Surname.* It comes from Nandun, descended from King Wu of Zhou. Zuozhuan, Xi yr 24: "Han, Jin, Ying, and Han (邗晉應韓) were of the line of Wu (武之穆)." The Han had Ying Yao, who withdrew into hiding with the Four Whitebeards — Yao alone never came forth; his eighth-generation descendant Ying Shao made the collected commentary on the *Hanshu*.
- *Also* interchangeable with 膺. Shu, *Wu cheng*: "he greatly received (膺) the mandate of Heaven"; note: "to answer to (當)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 於證, sounding like 譍 (yìng). *Ji Yun*: "to answer (答)." *Guangyun*: "things answering to one another (相應)." Yijing, *Xian* hexagram: "the two breaths feel and respond (感應), and so join."
- *A music-name.* Zhouli, *Chun guan, Shengshi*, note on the ying-music (應樂): "the ying is six 尺 five 寸 long, shaped like the zhu, with a mallet joined to its base, struck left and right, to answer (應) the zhu." The *Yue shu*: "the ying-music is like the hawk's answering to things — its take being small; hence the small drum and small pestle are called the ying, being that whereby the great is answered. The small hand-drum is called the ying-drum (應鼓)." Zhouli, *Chun guan, Xiaoshi*: "they beat the ying-drum (應鼓)"; note: "a hand-drum (鼙)."
- *Also* the Son of Heaven's gate is called the Ying Gate (應門). Shijing, *Daya*: "then he set up the Ying Gate (應門)"; note: "the main gate."
- *A prefecture-name.* In the land of Yanmen, the Tang set up Yingzhou (應州).

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 雍 (yōng), fanqie 於容.* Yijing, *Meng* hexagram: "the youthful and ignorant seek me; its will responds (應); at the first divination I tell, because of the firmness within." Lu Jia, *Xin yu*: "affairs follow one another by kind, and sounds answer (應) one another by tone."

**On the graph.** The graph 應 has all along in the classics and histories had two sounds, level and departing; the *Zhengzitong* gives only the departing, which is wrong.
