## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【殳字部】頁585第02【唐韻】於身切【集韻】【韻會】於巾切，𠀤音咽。【說文】作樂之盛稱殷。【易·豫卦】先王以作樂崇德，殷薦之上帝。　又凡盛皆曰殷。【書·洛誥】肇稱殷禮，祀于新邑。又【呂𠛬】三后成功，惟殷于民。　又【爾雅·釋言】殷，中也，正也。【書·堯典】日中星鳥，以殷仲春，宵中星虛，以殷仲秋。【傳】殷，正也，以正春秋之氣節。鄭𤣥曰：殷，中也。春分，陽之中。秋分，隂之中。又【禹貢】九江孔殷。【正義曰】言甚得地勢之中也。　又衆也。【詩·鄭風】殷其盈矣。【周禮·天官】陳其殷，置其輔。又【春官·大宗伯】殷見曰同，殷覜曰視。【傳】【註】俱訓衆。　又大也。【禮·曾子問】服除而後殷祭。【疏】殷，大也。大祭謂之殷祭。【莊子·山木篇】翼殷不逝，目大不覩。【註】翼大逝難，目大視希，故不見人。　又當也。【史記·天官書】衡殷中州河、濟之閒。【正義曰】衡，北斗衡也。殷，當也。言斗衡當黃河、濟水之閒地。　又國號。【史記】契始封商，後盤庚遷都殷墟，改號曰殷。【書傳】殷，亳之別名，在河南。　又姓。【史記·殷本紀】其後分封，以國爲姓，有殷氏，北殷氏。又齊人言殷聲如衣。今姓有衣者，殷之胄。見《禮記註疏》。　又【爾雅·釋訓】殷殷，憂也。【詩·邶風】憂心殷殷。【釋文】殷，於巾切，又音隱。　又俗謂周致爲殷勤，別作慇。　又【集韻】倚謹切【正韻】於謹切，𠀤音隱。雷發聲也。【詩·召南】殷其雷，在南山之陽。或从石作磤。　又殷殷，盛貌。【史記·蘇秦傳】輷輷殷殷，若有三軍之衆。【揚雄·羽獵賦】殷殷軫軫。　又【集韻】【韻會】𠀤於靳切。音㒚。【莊子·外物篇】其不殷非天之罪。【註】殷，當也，中也。○按當與中皆去聲讀。又【廣韻】【正韻】烏閑切【集韻】於閑切【韻會】幺閑切，𠀤音黫。赤黑色也。【左傳·成二年】左輪朱殷。【杜註】血色久則殷。殷，音近煙。今人以赤黑爲殷色。【杜甫詩】曾閃朱旗北斗殷。【白居易詩】白珠垂露凝，赤珠滴血殷。俱讀黫yān。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Lance radical 殳; p.585 #02)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 於身. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於巾 — sounding like 咽 (yīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the grandeur of music-making is called 殷." Yijing, *Yu*: "the former kings made music to exalt virtue, and grandly (殷) offered it to God on High."
- *Also, whatever is grand is called 殷.* Shu, *Luo gao*: "they first performed the grand rites (殷禮), sacrificing in the new city." Also *Lü xing*: "the three sovereigns accomplished their work, abundantly (殷) for the people."
- *Also* Erya, *Shiyan*: "殷 means middle (中); upright (正)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "the day at its midpoint, the star being the Bird, fixes (殷) mid-spring; the night at its midpoint, the star being Void, fixes (殷) mid-autumn"; tradition: "殷 means to set right (正): to fix the seasonal nodes of spring and autumn." Zheng Xuan says: "殷 means middle: the spring equinox is the midpoint of yang, the autumn equinox the midpoint of yin."
- *Also* Yu gong: "the Nine Rivers were fully central (孔殷)"; the Correct Meaning: "it says they fully attained the center of the land's contours."
- *Also, many, a throng (衆).* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "the throng (殷) — how it fills the place!" Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "array the main body (殷), set the supports." Also *Chunguan, Da zongbo*: "the mass-audience (殷見) is called tong (同); the mass-inspection (殷覜) is called shi (視)"; the tradition and note both gloss 殷 as "many."
- *Also, great (大).* Liji, *Zengzi wen*: "only after the mourning is laid off comes the great sacrifice (殷祭)"; subcommentary: "殷 means great; the great sacrifice is called 殷祭." Zhuangzi, *Shanmu*: "its wings great (殷), it does not fly off; its eyes large, it does not see"; note: "great wings are hard to fly with, large eyes see little, hence it did not see the man."
- *Also, to face, to correspond to (當).* Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "the Beam faces (殷) the central provinces, between the Yellow River and the Ji"; the Correct Meaning: "衡 is the Beam of the Northern Dipper; 殷 means to face; it says the Dipper's Beam faces the land between the Yellow River and the Ji."
- *Also a dynasty-name.* Shiji: "Xie was first enfeoffed at Shang; later Pangeng moved the capital to the Ruins of Yin (殷墟) and changed the name to Yin (殷)." The Shu-tradition: "Yin is another name for Bo, in Henan."
- *Also a surname.* Shiji, *Annals of Yin*: "afterward they were enfeoffed apart, taking the state-name as surname — there are the Yin (殷氏) and Northern-Yin (北殷氏) clans." Also the men of Qi pronounce 殷 like 衣 (yī); those today surnamed Yi (衣) are descendants of Yin. See the subcommentary to the Liji.
- *Also* Erya, *Shixun*: "殷殷 means grieved (憂)." Shijing, *Beifeng*: "the grieving heart, sorrowful (殷殷)"; Shiwen: "殷, fanqie 於巾, also pronounced 隱 (yǐn)."
- *Also,* the common term for thoroughgoing care is 殷勤 (assiduous); separately written 慇.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 倚謹; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於謹 — sounding like 隱 (yǐn): the sound of thunder breaking forth. Shijing, *Shaonan*: "thundering (殷), the thunder, on the south of the southern hill." Sometimes written 磤 with the stone radical.
- *Also 殷殷, the look of abundance.* Shiji, *Biography of Su Qin*: "rumbling and thundering (輷輷殷殷), as though there were the host of three armies." Yang Xiong, *Yulie fu*: "teeming and crowding (殷殷軫軫)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於靳, sounding like 㒚 (yìn). Zhuangzi, *Waiwu*: "that it does not hit the mark (殷) is no fault of Heaven"; note: "殷 means to correspond (當), to hit the center (中)." A note: 當 and 中 are here both read in the departing tone.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 烏閑; *Ji Yun*: fanqie 於閑; *Yun Hui*: fanqie 幺閑 — sounding like 黫 (yān): a dark-red color. Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 2: "the left wheel was dark with blood (朱殷)"; Du's note: "blood-color, grown old, is 殷; 殷 sounds close to 煙 (yān). Men today take dark-red as the 殷 color." Du Fu's poem: "once it flashed, the vermilion banner, the Dipper dark-red (殷)." Bai Juyi's poem: "white pearls hang, dew congealed; red pearls drip, dark with blood (殷)" — both read 黫 (yān).
