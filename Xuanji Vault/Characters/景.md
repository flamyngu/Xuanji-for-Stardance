**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Sun radical 日; p.496 #30)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居影. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 舉影 — sounding like 警 (jǐng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Light (光)." *Shiwen*: "景 means a bound (境); the place the light reaches has a boundary-limit."

**Senses.**
- *Great (大).* Erya, *Shi gu*. Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "to enlarge our great blessing (景福)"; Jian: "to receive great blessing."
- *Also* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the great way I tread (景行行止)"; Jian: "景 means bright (明)." Also Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "景行"; Zhu Xi's tradition: "the great road (大道)."
- *Also* Boya: "景景 means white (白)." *Pianhai*: "fair, fine (韶)." Also "an image (像); to admire (慕); to look up to (仰)."
- *Also* Shijing, *Yong feng*: "the Jing mountain and the Jing height (景山與京)"; tradition: "the Jing mountain (景山) is a great mountain."
- *A mountain-name.* Shijing, *Shang song*: "the Jing's compass is the He (景員維河)"; Zhu Xi's tradition: "景 is a mountain-name, where Shang had its capital."
- *A robe (衣).* Yili, *Shi hun li*: "the bride mounts by the stool, and the matron adds the jing-robe (景)"; note: "the jing is made like the bright-robe (明衣), put on for travel to ward off dust and keep the robe fresh and bright; 景 too means bright."
- *A star-name.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*: "when the sky is clear, the Jing star (景星) appears; the Jing star is a virtue-star."
- *A wind-name.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes*: "the Jing wind (景風) dwells in the south; 景 means the yang force having run its course."
- *Surname.* Jing Cha, seen in Shiji, *Biography of Qu Yuan*.

**Other reading (yǐng).**
- *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於丙. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 於境 — sounding like 影 (yǐng). The shadow (隂影) of a thing. Shijing, *Bei feng*: "drifting, drifting its shadow (景)"; subcommentary: "drifting, one sees its shadow (影) go and come, indistinct." Zhouli, *Di guan, Da situ*: "with the gnomon-jade to gauge the soil's depth and fix the sun's shadow (日景)"; *Shiwen*: "景 in some texts written 影." *Peixi*: "form-shadow (形景) for 影 — this comes from Zhi Chuan (Ge Hong)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 舉兩.* Xiahou Zhan, *Di yi*: "the joy of the Li folk in the realm of Xia is like the roaming form summoning the kindly shade (惠景)" — rhyming with 響 above.
