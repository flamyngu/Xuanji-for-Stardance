## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈巳集上〉【水字部】頁627第38【唐韻】五佳切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𡧧佳切，𠀤音崖。【玉篇】水際也。【書·微子】若涉大水，其無津涯。　又借作窮盡意。【莊子·養生主】吾生也有涯，而知也無涯。　又朱涯，水名。【前漢·地理志】在鬱陵郡臨塵縣。　又【唐韻】【集韻】魚羈切【韻會】疑羈切，𠀤音𡧧。義同。　又【韻補】叶牛何切，音俄。【柳宗元·道州孔子廟𥓓銘】欽惟聖王，厥道無涯。世有頌辭，益疾其多。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.627 #38)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 五佳. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 𡧧佳 — sounding like 崖 (yá).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "the water's margin (水際)." Shu, *Weizi*: "as one fording a great water with no ford or shore (津涯)."

**Senses.**
- *Borrowed for the sense of limit, end (窮盡).* Zhuangzi, *Yang sheng zhu*: "my life has a limit (涯), but knowing has no limit (涯)."
- *朱涯 (Zhuya), a river-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "in Linchen county of Yuling commandery."
- *Also* *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 魚羈. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 疑羈 — sounding like 𡧧 (yí). Same sense.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 俄 (é), fanqie 牛何.* Liu Zongyuan, *Stele-inscription for the Confucius Temple of Daozhou*: "reverently we think on the sage-king, whose Way is without bound (涯); the ages have their songs of praise, the more pained at their excess."
