**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.375 #02)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 𤰞吉. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 璧吉 — sounding like 畢 (bì).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The dividing-limit (分極)." It follows 八 (eight) and 戈; 戈 also gives the sound. *Zhao Yiguang's gloss*: "弋 is like a marker-sign, and the dividing-limit is like a boundary; hence it follows 八 and 弋."

**Senses.**
- *A word of certainty (定辭).* Shijing, *Qi feng*: "in taking a wife, how does one do it? one must (必) tell one's father and mother."
- *Wholly, only (專).* Yang Xiong, *Tai xuan jing*: "the red stone is not robbed of its red — the resolve (必) of the man of principle"; note: "stone cannot be robbed of its hardness, cinnabar cannot be robbed of its red, like the man of principle's wholeness of resolve (必專)."
- *To insist on, count on (期必).* Lunyu: "the Master cut off four things: he had no surmising, no insisting (必)."
- *To make sure (審).* Later Han, *Biography of Liu Tao*: "those he took as friends had surely (必) to be of one mind."
- *Sure, decisive (果).* Later Han, *Eulogy in the Annals of Emperor Xuan*: "the rule of the filial Xuan: rewards were trusted and punishments sure (必罰)."
- *Biyu (必育), a personal name*, an aide of Suiren. *Qunfu lu*: "Biyu received the tax-customs"; note: "he received the levies, taxes, and corvée, and what was fit to be done with them."
- *Also* *Zihui Bu*: 赤犮必力, a mountain-name, where the He water issues. Seen in the monk Zongle's record.
- *Also* *Gujin zikao*: fanqie 幷列, sounding like 縪 (bì). A cord (組). Zhouli, *Dong guan, Kaogong ji*: "in the jade-worker's task, the Son of Heaven's gui-tablet has a mid-cord (中必)"; note: "it means binding the centre with a cord, to guard against its slipping and falling." A note: by the *Shiwen* of the *Zhouli Kaogong*, 必 is just 組 (a cord), and reading it as 縪 is the vulgar reading; the 䪐 of the bow-brace follows 韋, and the *Zhengwei* would cite this to refute the *Shuowen* — which is wrong-headed; one should take the *Shuowen* as correct. Also a note: the graph 必 does not follow 心; the *Zihui* placed it in the Heart radical, and the *Zhengzitong* followed suit, taking it by resemblance of form, for ease of lookup only.
