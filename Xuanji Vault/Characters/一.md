**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; One radical 一; p.75#01*

**Ancient graph.** 弌.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於悉. *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 益悉 — the entering tone of 漪 (yī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "at the very first beginning, the Way was established in the One; it wrought the division of Heaven and Earth, and transformed and completed the myriad things." *Guangyun*: "the beginning of numbers, the ultimate of things." Yijing, *Xici*: "Heaven is one, Earth two." Laozi, *Daodejing*: "the Way bore the One, the One bore the Two."
- *The same (同).* *Guangyun.* Liji, *Yueji*: "rites, music, punishments, and government — their ultimate is one (一)." Shiji, *Forest of Scholars*: "Master Han drew out the meaning of the Odes and made the Inner and Outer Commentaries in tens of thousands of words; his wording differs much from that current between Qi and Lu, yet their purport is one (一)."
- *Few, single (少).* Yan Yanzhi, *Family Precepts*: "in choosing books, aim at the select few (一) and do not prize the crowded and minute." He Chengtian, *Reply to Yan of Yongjia*: "I would humbly wish you to set aside the many and follow the one (一)."
- *Pure (純).* *Zengyun.* Yijing, *Xici*: "all the movement under Heaven is kept correct by the One (一)." Laozi, *Daodejing*: "Heaven got the One and so was clear; Earth got the One and so was at peace; the spirits got the One and so were numinous; the valley got the One and so was full; the myriad things got the One and so were born; lords and kings got the One and so became the standard of the world."
- *Even, uniform (均).* Tang shu, *Biography of Xue Ping*: "the arms and armour kept whole and sharp, the levies and taxes evenly uniform (均一)."
- *Sincerity (誠).* Zhongyong: "that whereby they are carried out is one (一) — single sincerity."
- *Zhengyi (正一).* Tang shu, *Biography of Sima Chengzhen*: "he received the Zhengyi (Orthodox Unity) method from the recluse Tao, four generations down."
- *一一, one by one.* Hanfeizi, *Neichu*: "a retired scholar of Nanguo asked to play the reed-pipe for King Xuan of Qi. The king delighted in him and fed him along with several hundred men. When King Min came to the throne, he liked to hear them one by one (一一), and the scholar fled." Han Yu's poem: "one by one (一一), whom would they have pity?" Su Shi's poem: "fine phrases like pearls strung one by one (一一)."
- *Star-names.* The Xingjing: "the star Tianyi (天一) is outside the gate of the Purple Tenuity palace; the star Taiyi (太一) is half a degree south of Tianyi."
- *Taiyi (太一), a mountain-name,* that is the Zhongnan range; also called Taiyi (太乙).
- *The Three Ones (三一).* Former Han, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "with the Grand Sacrifice they offered to the Three Ones"; note: "Tianyi, Diyi, and Taiyi; Taiyi is the primal *qi* before Heaven and Earth were divided."
- *尺一, an imperial-edict tablet.* Later Han, *Biography of Chen Fan*: "the foot-and-an-inch (尺一) appointments"; note: "the tablet was a foot and an inch long, for writing edicts."
- *百一, the name of a poem-series,* by Ying Qu of Wei.
- *A surname.* In the Ming, Yi Xuanzong. Also a three-graph surname: the Northern Wei had the Yinalou (一那婁) clan, later changed to Lou.
- *Written 壹, 貳, 叄.* The numerals 一, 二, 三 may be written 壹, 貳, 叄. Daxue: "all alike (壹是) take self-cultivation as the root." Shiji, *Treatise on Rites*: "unifying (總一) all within the seas"; the Former Han, *Biography of Huo Guang*, writes it 總壹. The Liushu gu: "nowadays only in the ledgers of money received and paid out are 壹, 貳, 叄 used, to guard against fraud and alteration."
- *Yunbu*: rhymes as 懿 (yì), fanqie 於利. Zuo Si, *Fu on the Wu Capital*: "*huo*-leaf and betel, cardamom and nutmeg, ginger and its kinds — not of one (一) sort; the river-angelica's tribe, the sea-moss's class."
- *Rhymes as 兮 (xī), fanqie 弦雞.* The Cantong qi: "the white is the essence of metal, the black the base of water; water is the pivot of the Way, and its number is named One (一)."
