**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҜ, middle; Hand radical жүӢ; p.439 #08)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie д»–еӣһ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йҖҡеӣһ вҖ” the level tone of йҖҖ, i.e. **tuД«**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to push (жҺ’)." *Zengyun*: "to shove; to press; one says to put forward." Liji, *Yueling*: "in early spring he ploughs the holy field in person: the Son of Heaven pushes [the plough] thrice (дёүжҺЁ), the Three Dukes five times, the ministers and lords nine times." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 14: "some pulled it, some pushed (жҺЁ) it"; note: "to draw from the front is иј“, to push from behind is жҺЁ."
- *To shift, remove (з§»).* Shijing, *Daya*: "the drought being too sore, it cannot be pushed off (жҺЁ)"; subcommentary: "it cannot be made to remove; жҺЁ is a word for putting far away."
- *To yield to another.* Shiji, *Biography of the Marquis of Huaiyin*: "he took off his robe to clothe me, pushed over (жҺЁ) his food to feed me." Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "he pushes (жҺЁ) his loyal heart into others' breasts."
- *To decline; to shift onto another.*
- *To move on in sequence.* *Tang Yun*: fanqie жҳҢйҢҗ; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie е·қйҢҗ вҖ” sounding like дң… (chuД«). Yijing, *Xici*: "cold and heat push each other on (зӣёжҺЁ), and the year is made."
- *To choose; to commend; to promote.* *Zengyun.* Shu, *Zhouguan*: "promote (жҺЁ) the worthy and yield to the able, and the many offices are harmonious." Liji, *Ruxing*: "from above unpulled, from below unpushed (жҺЁ)"; note: "жҺЁ is like 'to advance'"; subcommentary: "not recommended by those below." Former Han, *Biography of Han Xin*: "poor and of no [good] conduct, he could not be put forward (жҺЁж“Ү) for office"; note: "no good conduct by which to be recommended and chosen."
- *To trace out, infer (е°Ӣз№№).* Former Han, appraisal in the *Biography of Liu Xiang*: "with purpose he traced it to its root (жҺЁжң¬)"; note: "he probed the very root, with deep intent; one says, to press to the bottom." Shiji, *Biographies of the Harsh Officials*: "Luo Bi of Tianshui [used] interrogation (жҺЁжёӣ)"; note: "жҺЁ, fanqie зӣҙиҝҪ, means to press and hold [a suspect] to make a case; жёӣ is in one text жҲҗ.
