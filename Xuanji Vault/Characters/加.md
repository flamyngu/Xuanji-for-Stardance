**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls тГљ, lower; Strength radical тіЏ; p.146 #13)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie тЈцуЅЎ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie т▒ЁуЅЎ Рђћ sounding like т«Х (ji─Ђ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to pile up words upon one another (УфъуЏИтбътіа). It follows тіЏ (strength) and тЈБ (mouth)." Xu [Kai] says: "a meaning-compound." Erya, *Shigu*: "to lay upon, to compound (жЄЇ)"; note: "to multiply upon." *Yupian*: "to increase (уЏі)." Lunyu: "what more would you add (тіа)?"
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to set above (СИі); to override (жЎх)." Lunyu: "I too would not impose (тіа) on others"; note: "to override."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "to apply (Тќй); to place upon (УЉЌ)." Liji, *Guan yi*: "pledged at the guest's place, with the threefold capping (СИЅтіа) growing ever more honored Рђћ the cappings mark his coming of age."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie т▒ЁСйЋ, sounding like тЊЦ (g─Њ). Dongfang Shuo, *Qijian*: "mugwort and wormwood are taken into intimacy upon the bed; the horse-orchid sprawls and spreads, daily gaining ground (ТЌЦтіа). Cast off are the angelica and the asarum Рђћ what can I do that the age knows not the fragrant?"
- *Rhymes as* fanqie т▒ЁС╣І, sounding like тДг (j─Ф). Sanl├╝e: "the soft has its uses, the hard its applications; the weak has its employments, the strong its impositions (тіа)."
