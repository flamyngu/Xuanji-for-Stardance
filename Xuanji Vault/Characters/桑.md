**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.526 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣕐, 𠭌.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 息郞. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蘇郞 — the level tone of 顙 (sǎng), i.e. **sāng**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "[the tree] whose leaves the silkworm eats." Xu [Kai]: "叒 sounds *ruo*; when the sun first rises in the east it climbs the Fusang (搏桑) *ruo*-tree at the Hot-Springs Vale; the mulberry being the holy-leaved tree the silkworm eats, 木 (wood) is added below 叒 to mark it off." The Dianshu: "the mulberry is the essence of the Winnowing-Basket star (箕)." Shijing, *Binfeng*, subcommentary: "'I seek the tender mulberry (柔桑)' — the young mulberry; 'lovely is that maiden-mulberry (女桑)' — the sprout-mulberry; 'in the silkworm month they strip the mulberry (條桑)' — lopping the branches to pick their leaves." Liji, *Yueling*: "in the last month of spring, the Forester is charged not to fell mulberry or cudrania"; note: "to spare the silkworms' food." Shiji, *Biographies of the Money-makers*: "a man with a thousand *mu* of mulberry and hemp in Qi or Lu ranks with a marquis of a thousand households."
- *Mulberry-fire.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Siguan*: "he changes the state's fire to ward off seasonal sickness"; note: "in summer he takes the fire of mulberry and cudrania."
- *檿桑, the mountain mulberry.* Its silk suits zither and lute strings. Shu, *Yugong*: "their baskets [held] *yan*-silk (檿絲)." Also, its wood suits bow-staves: Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*: "the bow-maker: in choosing stave-wood, cudrania is best, the *yan*-mulberry next."
- *Taisang (台桑), a place-name, where Lord Qi was born.* Chuci, *Tianwen*: "how did he win the daughter of the Tushan clan and join with her at Taisang?"
- *Kongsang (空桑), a mountain-name.* Lüshi chunqiu: "Yi Yin was born at Kongsang."
- *Sanglin (桑林), a music-name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 10: "the Duke of Song feasted the Marquis of Lu at Chuqiu, and asked to use the *Sanglin* [dance]."
- *The* sanghu *(桑扈), a bird-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 17: "the nine *hu*-birds are the nine farming-overseers"; note: "the *sanghu* is the 'fat-stealer' [hawfinch]."
- *A surname.* Xingyuan: "descendants of the Qin grandee Zisang; the Han had Sang Hongyang and Sang Chu." Also compound surnames: Sangqiu and Gengsang. (𠭨 is the *zhou*-script form of 桑.)

**Textual notes.** "季夏之月" should read "季春之月"; "厥貢檿絲" should read "厥篚檿絲"; "工人取榦之道" should read "弓人"; the *sanghu* note belongs to Zuozhuan Zhao-17, not Xiang-4.
