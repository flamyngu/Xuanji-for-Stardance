**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, middle; Fire radical 火; p.673 #26)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣟒, 𣚨, 𠘩.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 武扶. *Guangyun*: fanqie 武夫. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 微夫 — sounding like 巫 (wú).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to be gone, lacking (亡)." *Yupian*: "not to have (不有)." Shu, *Shun dian*: "firm yet without cruelty, simple yet without arrogance." *Yi ji*: "encourage the exchange of what one has and has not (有無)."
- *The interval of emptiness.* Erya, *Shi gu*; note: "emptiness all has gaps." Laozi: "the myriad things are born of Being (有); Being is born of Non-being (無)." Zhou Dunyi, *Diagram of the Supreme Ultimate*: "the Limitless (無極), and yet the Supreme Ultimate."
- *= 不 (not).* Liji, *Sannian wen*: "the unchanging (無易) Way"; note: "無 is like 不."
- *A county.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: Huiwu county in Yuesui commandery.
- *Surname.* Zhengzitong: in the Han, Wu Qieming, Wu Neng. *Guangyun*: the Han compound surnames 無庸 and 無鉤, both from Chu.
- *文無, a medicine.* *Gujin zhu*: "at parting they give 文無, also called 當歸 (Angelica)."
- *The Shuowen "odd graph" 无.* *Yupian*: "emptiness (虛无)." In the Zhouyi the graph 無 is everywhere written 无.
- *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 亡. Shijing, *Wei feng*: "何有何亡."
- *Interchangeable with 毋.* Shu, *Wu yi*; Shiji, *House of Lu* writes 毋逸.
- *Interchangeable with 毛.* Hou Han, *Biography of Feng Yan*: "the starving have no (毛) food"; note: "in Yan's collected works 毛 is 無 — still so in colloquial speech." *Pei xi ji*: "in Heshuo they say 無 as 毛." *Tongya*: "in Jiang-Chu and Guangdong they say 無 as 'máo.'"

**Editorial notes.** *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 武 — but per Liji, *Liqi*, "詔侑武方," the note says 武 should be 無, an error of sound (Zheng plainly calls it an error); the *Ji Yun* wrongly merged 無 and 武 as one. The *Ji Yun* also writes it 橆, and the *Yun Hui* derives this from 𣞣 (the old 蕃𣞣 graph, borrowed in seal for "have/have-not," its 林 turned by Li Si into four dots). But per *Shuowen*, 𣠮 (from 亡, 無 phonetic) stands in the 亡 section, while the 橆 of 蕃橆 stands in the 林 section — different in sound and sense, not interchangeable; and since "have/have-not" and "luxuriant (蕃橆)" are opposite in meaning, the borrowing is improper. The *Yupian*, *Ji Yun*, and *Yun Hui* are all mistaken here. — Per the *Dushu tong*, 無 is said to pass for 勿, 莫, 末, 沒, 蔑, 微, 不, 曼, 瞀, etc.; but these are only related in sense or near in sound, truly not one character — the *Dushu tong* errs.
- *Sanskrit.* 南無 is pronounced "nā-mó" (那謨) — 那 like 拏 in the rising tone, 謨 sounding like 摩 — meaning "to take refuge (歸依)."
