**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls е·і, middle; Fire radical зҒ«; p.673 #26)*

**Ancient graphs.** рЈҹ’, рЈҡЁ, р ҳ©.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жӯҰжү¶. *Guangyun*: fanqie жӯҰеӨ«. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еҫ®еӨ« вҖ” sounding like е·« (wГә).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to be gone, lacking (дәЎ)." *Yupian*: "not to have (дёҚжңү)." Shu, *Shun dian*: "firm yet without cruelty, simple yet without arrogance." *Yi ji*: "encourage the exchange of what one has and has not (жңүз„Ў)."
- *The interval of emptiness.* Erya, *Shi gu*; note: "emptiness all has gaps." Laozi: "the myriad things are born of Being (жңү); Being is born of Non-being (з„Ў)." Zhou Dunyi, *Diagram of the Supreme Ultimate*: "the Limitless (з„ЎжҘө), and yet the Supreme Ultimate."
- *= дёҚ (not).* Liji, *Sannian wen*: "the unchanging (з„Ўжҳ“) Way"; note: "з„Ў is like дёҚ."
- *A county.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: Huiwu county in Yuesui commandery.
- *Surname.* Zhengzitong: in the Han, Wu Qieming, Wu Neng. *Guangyun*: the Han compound surnames з„Ўеәё and з„ЎйүӨ, both from Chu.
- *ж–Үз„Ў, a medicine.* *Gujin zhu*: "at parting they give ж–Үз„Ў, also called з•¶жӯё (Angelica)."
- *The Shuowen "odd graph" ж— .* *Yupian*: "emptiness (иҷӣж— )." In the Zhouyi the graph з„Ў is everywhere written ж— .
- *Ji Yun*: sometimes written дәЎ. Shijing, *Wei feng*: "дҪ•жңүдҪ•дәЎ."
- *Interchangeable with жҜӢ.* Shu, *Wu yi*; Shiji, *House of Lu* writes жҜӢйҖё.
- *Interchangeable with жҜӣ.* Hou Han, *Biography of Feng Yan*: "the starving have no (жҜӣ) food"; note: "in Yan's collected works жҜӣ is з„Ў вҖ” still so in colloquial speech." *Pei xi ji*: "in Heshuo they say з„Ў as жҜӣ." *Tongya*: "in Jiang-Chu and Guangdong they say з„Ў as 'mГЎo.'"

**Editorial notes.** *Ji Yun*: sometimes written жӯҰ вҖ” but per Liji, *Liqi*, "и©”дҫ‘жӯҰж–№," the note says жӯҰ should be з„Ў, an error of sound (Zheng plainly calls it an error); the *Ji Yun* wrongly merged з„Ў and жӯҰ as one. The *Ji Yun* also writes it ж©Ҷ, and the *Yun Hui* derives this from рЈһЈ (the old и•ғрЈһЈ graph, borrowed in seal for "have/have-not," its жһ— turned by Li Si into four dots). But per *Shuowen*, рЈ ® (from дәЎ, з„Ў phonetic) stands in the дәЎ section, while the ж©Ҷ of и•ғж©Ҷ stands in the жһ— section вҖ” different in sound and sense, not interchangeable; and since "have/have-not" and "luxuriant (и•ғж©Ҷ)" are opposite in meaning, the borrowing is improper. The *Yupian*, *Ji Yun*, and *Yun Hui* are all mistaken here. вҖ” Per the *Dushu tong*, з„Ў is said to pass for еӢҝ, иҺ«, жң«, жІ’, и”‘, еҫ®, дёҚ, жӣј, зһҖ, etc.; but these are only related in sense or near in sound, truly not one character вҖ” the *Dushu tong* errs.
- *Sanskrit.* еҚ—з„Ў is pronounced "nДҒ-mГі" (йӮЈи¬Ё) вҖ” йӮЈ like жӢҸ in the rising tone, и¬Ё sounding like ж‘© вҖ” meaning "to take refuge (жӯёдҫқ)."
