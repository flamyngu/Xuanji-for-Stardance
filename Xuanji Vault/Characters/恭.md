**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҜ, upper; Heart radical еҝғ; p.385 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie д№қе®№. *Ji Yun*: fanqie еұ…е®№ вҖ” sounding like дҫӣ (gЕҚng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Reverent (иӮ…)." Shu, *Hong fan*: "of the bearing one says, reverent (жҒӯ)." Liji, *Qu li*: "the gentleman, reverent and respectful (жҒӯж•¬), measured and yielding, thereby makes ritual clear"; subcommentary: "in the bearing it is жҒӯ (reverence), in the heart it is ж•¬ (respect); much bearing and little heart is жҒӯ, much heart and little bearing is ж•¬."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Liji, *Yu zao*: "the bearing of the hands, reverent (жҒӯ)"; note: "held high and straight."
- *Also* Lunyu: "mild, good, reverent (жҒӯ), frugal, yielding"; subcommentary: "harmonious and compliant, not crossing вҖ” this is called жҒӯ."
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "morning and night reverent (жҒӯ)"; note: "to serve reverently morning and night is called жҒӯ." *Shiming*: "жҒӯ means to fold the hands (жӢұ); to hold oneself folded; it also means to supply the service of others."
- *Also* Shu, *Tai jia*: "in receiving inferiors, think on reverence (жҖқжҒӯ)." Liji, *Shao yi*: "with guests, the host is reverent (жҒӯ)"; note: "to be free of pride and insolence is жҒӯ."
- *To hold up, carry out reverently (еҘү).* Shu, *Gan shi*: "now I do but reverently carry out (жҒӯиЎҢ) Heaven's punishment"; tradition: "жҒӯ means to carry out."
- *In posthumous titles:* "upright in virtue and fair in bearing, reverent and compliant in serving the high, is called жҒӯ."
- *A prefecture-name.* In the land of Liangzhou, the Tang set up Gongzhou (жҒӯе·һ).
- *Surname.* Descendants of the Reverent Heir-son (жҒӯдё–еӯҗ) of Jin, who took the posthumous title for surname.
- *Also* interchangeable with е…ұ. Shijing, *Daya*: "be diligent and reverent (иҷ”е…ұ) in your place"; note: "the graph жҒӯ anciently ran together with е…ұ." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 27: "Qi was not reverent (е…ұ)"; note: "originally written жҒӯ." Also interchangeable with йҫ”: Shu, *Tai shi*: "reverently carry out (жҒӯиЎҢ) Heaven's punishment," sometimes written йҫ”.
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie еҚҖзҺӢ, sounding like еҢЎ (kuДҒng). *Daozang ge*: "the Grand Void stirs a numinous gathering, and bids me bring the writ to being; the gods of Heaven all rejoice, and one and all bow their heads in reverence (жҒӯ)."

**On the graph.** Originally written рўҷ„; it follows еҝғ (heart) with е…ұ as phonetic; now written жҒӯ.

**Textual notes.** Liji, *Qu li*, "the gentleman, reverent and respectful... thereby makes ritual clear"; the gloss "in the bearing it is жҒӯ, in the heart it is ж•¬" вҖ” checked against the original book, иЁ» (note) is corrected to з–Ҹ (subcommentary). Also Lunyu, "mild, good, reverent, frugal, yielding"; the gloss "harmonious and compliant, not crossing вҖ” this is called жҒӯ" вҖ” checked against the original book, иЁ» (note) is corrected to з–Ҹ (subcommentary).
