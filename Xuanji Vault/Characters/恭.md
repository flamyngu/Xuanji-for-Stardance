**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.385 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 九容. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 居容 — sounding like 供 (gōng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Reverent (肅)." Shu, *Hong fan*: "of the bearing one says, reverent (恭)." Liji, *Qu li*: "the gentleman, reverent and respectful (恭敬), measured and yielding, thereby makes ritual clear"; subcommentary: "in the bearing it is 恭 (reverence), in the heart it is 敬 (respect); much bearing and little heart is 恭, much heart and little bearing is 敬."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Liji, *Yu zao*: "the bearing of the hands, reverent (恭)"; note: "held high and straight."
- *Also* Lunyu: "mild, good, reverent (恭), frugal, yielding"; subcommentary: "harmonious and compliant, not crossing — this is called 恭."
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "morning and night reverent (恭)"; note: "to serve reverently morning and night is called 恭." *Shiming*: "恭 means to fold the hands (拱); to hold oneself folded; it also means to supply the service of others."
- *Also* Shu, *Tai jia*: "in receiving inferiors, think on reverence (思恭)." Liji, *Shao yi*: "with guests, the host is reverent (恭)"; note: "to be free of pride and insolence is 恭."
- *To hold up, carry out reverently (奉).* Shu, *Gan shi*: "now I do but reverently carry out (恭行) Heaven's punishment"; tradition: "恭 means to carry out."
- *In posthumous titles:* "upright in virtue and fair in bearing, reverent and compliant in serving the high, is called 恭."
- *A prefecture-name.* In the land of Liangzhou, the Tang set up Gongzhou (恭州).
- *Surname.* Descendants of the Reverent Heir-son (恭世子) of Jin, who took the posthumous title for surname.
- *Also* interchangeable with 共. Shijing, *Daya*: "be diligent and reverent (虔共) in your place"; note: "the graph 恭 anciently ran together with 共." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 27: "Qi was not reverent (共)"; note: "originally written 恭." Also interchangeable with 龔: Shu, *Tai shi*: "reverently carry out (恭行) Heaven's punishment," sometimes written 龔.
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie 區王, sounding like 匡 (kuāng). *Daozang ge*: "the Grand Void stirs a numinous gathering, and bids me bring the writ to being; the gods of Heaven all rejoice, and one and all bow their heads in reverence (恭)."

**On the graph.** Originally written 𢙄; it follows 心 (heart) with 共 as phonetic; now written 恭.

**Textual notes.** Liji, *Qu li*, "the gentleman, reverent and respectful... thereby makes ritual clear"; the gloss "in the bearing it is 恭, in the heart it is 敬" — checked against the original book, 註 (note) is corrected to 疏 (subcommentary). Also Lunyu, "mild, good, reverent, frugal, yielding"; the gloss "harmonious and compliant, not crossing — this is called 恭" — checked against the original book, 註 (note) is corrected to 疏 (subcommentary).
