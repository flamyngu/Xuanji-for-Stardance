## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈申集上〉【艸字部】頁1058第35【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤儒佳切，音甤。【說文】草木華垂貌。【陸機·文賦】播芳蕤之馥馥。　又冠蕤。【禮·雜記】緇布冠不蕤。【疏】緇布冠，古法不蕤。今特云不蕤者，以後代有蕤，此以凶事故不蕤。　又蕤賔，五月律。【周語】蕤賔。【註】蕤，委蕤，柔貌也。【前漢·律歷志】蕤，繼也。　又萎蕤，藥名。見萎字註。　又旗名，鹵簿中有之。【唐人詩】望見葳蕤舉翠華。【集韻】省作苼。    考證：〔【唐人詩】望見萎蕤舉翠華。〕　謹照原文詩萎蕤改葳蕤。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1058 #35)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 儒佳 — sounding like 甤 (ruí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the look of plants drooping with blossom." Lu Ji, *Wen fu*: "scattering the fragrant blossoms (芳蕤), rich and sweet."
- *Also 冠蕤 (cap-tassels).* Liji, *Zaji*: "the black cloth cap has no tassels (蕤)"; subcommentary: "by ancient rule the black cloth cap had no tassels; that it here expressly says 'no tassels' is because in later ages there were tassels, but for a mourning occasion it has none."
- *Also 蕤賔 (Ruibin), the pitch-pipe of the fifth month.* Zhou yu: "Ruibin (蕤賔)"; note: "蕤 is weirui (委蕤), the look of softness." Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "蕤 means to continue (繼)."
- *Also 萎蕤 (weirui), a medicine-name*; see the note under the graph 萎.
- *Also a banner-name, found in the imperial cortège.* A Tang poet: "I see afar the lush-plumed banners (葳蕤) raise their kingfisher pennon."

**On the graph.** *Ji Yun*: abbreviated as 苼.

**Textual notes.** In the Tang poem, 萎蕤 should read 葳蕤 (per the original).
