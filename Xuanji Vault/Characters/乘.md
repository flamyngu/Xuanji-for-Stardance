**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; Slash radical 丿; p.83 #09)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠅞, 𠓸.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 食陵. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 神陵 — sounding like 繩 (chéng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Guangyun*: "to drive, mount (駕); to climb, ride (登)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "in season he rides (乘) the six dragons to govern the sky."

**Senses.**
- *To avail of (因).* Mengzi: "not so good as availing of (乘) the momentum." Laozi, *Daode jing*: "drifting (乗乗) as if with nowhere to return."
- *To set in order (治).* Shijing, *Bin feng*: "make haste to mend (乘) the roof."
- *To overcome (勝).* *Discourses of Zhou*: "to overcome (乘) men unrighteously is to oppress."
- *To reckon (計).* Zhouli, *Xia guan, Gao ren*: "he reckons (乘) their work, tests their bows and crossbows, and by it lowers or raises their rations, with punishment and reward."
- *Surname.* The Han had Cheng Chang, Marquis of Zhuzao.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 實證. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 石證 — sounding like 剩 (shèng). A chariot (車). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the great war-cars, ten teams (十乘), lead off the march."
- *A pair of things is called a 乘.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 33: "Xian Gao, with a pair of hides (乘韋) before twelve oxen, feasted the army." Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "a pair of wild geese is called a 乘."
- *Whatever is counted by fours is called a 乘.* Liji, *Shao yi*: "a set of four jars of wine (乘壺酒)." Mengzi: "he loosed a set of four arrows (乘矢)."
- *Chengqiu (乗丘), a place-name.* Erya, *Shi di* note: "Chengqiu is shaped like a chariot (車乘)."
- *A plant-name.* Erya, *Shi cao*: "the wangchengche (望乘車)"; note: "it can be made into rope, over a 丈 long."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "乘 means to carry, to record (載); it is named from the matter of recording." Mengzi: "the *Cheng* (乗) of Jin." A note: the *Yunrui* cites the *Jin cheng* into the level tone, which is wrong; today the family-record is called 家乘, the sense running with the *Shi cheng* (history-record).
- *Also* *Chuandeng lu*: "Chan has shallow and deep grades: one the Lesser Vehicle (小乘), one the Greater Vehicle (大乘); the sudden waking to one's own mind's untainted wisdom, this mind being itself Buddha, is called the Supreme Vehicle (最上乘)." The Song monk Qisong, *Yuan jiao*: "the Five Vehicles are all gathered under the Three Treasuries: first the Man-vehicle, second the Heaven-vehicle, third the Hearer-vehicle (聲聞), fourth the Pratyeka-vehicle (緣覺), fifth the Bodhisattva-vehicle. The latter three vehicles lead their followers out of the world; the former two, since desire cannot be cast off at once, meet men's feelings and so regulate them." All in the departing tone.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 諸應, sounding like 證 (zhèng). A surname. *Shuowen*: originally written 椉.
