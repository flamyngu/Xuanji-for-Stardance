**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1187 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 士咸. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 鋤咸 — sounding like 饞 (chán).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to slander, defame (譖)." *Yupian*: "to flatter perniciously (佞)." *Zhengzitong*: "to embellish evil words, ruining the good and harming the able."
- *Slander, calumny.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "I detest slanderous (讒) speech and pernicious conduct." Zhuangzi, *Yufu*: "to delight in speaking of others' faults is called slander (讒)." Xunzi, *Xiushen*: "to injure the good is called slander (讒)." Shuoyuan, *Chen shu*: "he who obscures the good is the slanderer (讒) of the state."
- *A star-name.* Jin shu, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "among the six stars of Juanshe (Curled Tongue), one is called Heaven's Slanderer (天讒), governing shamans and physicians."
- *A cauldron-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 3: "the inscription on the Chan cauldron (讒鼎)"; subcommentary: "the Chan cauldron is the cauldron that loathes slander, the same as the Chong cauldron named in the *Mingtang wei*. One account: 讒 is a place-name — Yu cast the Nine Cauldrons at the land of Ganchan (甘讒), hence 'Chan cauldron.'"
- *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 士懺. *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 士監 — the departing tone of 饞 (chàn); same sense.
- *Rhymes as 崇 (chóng), fanqie 鉏弓.* Chuci, *Jiu tan*: "I breathe in the pure essence and breathe out the murky vapours; crossing a crooked age, I will not court favour; I walk knocking at sincerity and do not fawn — and so am thrust aside and meet with slander (讒)."
- *Rhymes as 嚴 (yán), fanqie 銀炎.* Liang Hong, *Poem on Going to Wu*: "I would mount and whip my horse and gallop far away, sick at my age's ways, which I make my reproach (讒); they vie to raise the crooked and set aside the straight, all putting flattery first, fawning on and on (唌唌)" — 唌 sounds like 延.

**On the graph.** The Sushu zhengwu: "[writing it] with two 免 is wrong." [Variant:] 䜛.
