**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Script radical 文; p.477 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 無分 — sounding like 紋 (wén).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "crossed strokes, a figured pattern (錯畫)." *Yupian*: "pattern, ornament (文章)." *Shiming*: "文 is the gathering of many colours to make brocade; the joining of many graphs to make sense and meaning, like embroidery." Yijing, *Xici*: "things mingle, hence 'pattern (文).'" Zhouli, *Tianguan, Diansi*: "she supplies the silk, floss, braid, and figured (文) goods"; note: "in painting and embroidery, blue with red is 文." Liji, *Yueji*: "the five colours form a pattern (文) and are not confused."
- *A written graph.* Preface to the Shu: "anciently, when Fu Xi ruled the world, he first drew the eight trigrams and made the written tally, to replace the governance of knotted cords, and thence writings (文籍) arose"; subcommentary: "文 means written graphs." Preface to the Shuowen: "patterned on kinds and imaging shapes, hence called 文; afterward form and sound added to each other, and these are called 字 (characters)." Gujin tonglun: "Cangjie made writing; where the form stands it is 文, where the sound is complete it is 字."
- *Ornament.* Yijing, *Qian, Wenyan* subcommentary: "文 means ornament."
- *Pattern and principle.* Yijing, *Kun*: "the pattern (文) lies within"; subcommentary: "penetrating the pattern and its principle." Shiji, *Treatise on Rites*: "to honour the root is called 文 (form), to make use in practice is called 理 (substance); the two joined make the complete form."
- *Cultured (文謀).* Shu, *Yaodian*: "reverent, bright, accomplished (文), and thoughtful, calm and easy"; subcommentary: "in his undertakings there was cultured planning (文謀)."
- *Form (as against root).* Liji, *Liqi*: "the former kings, in setting up rites, had root and form (文); loyalty and good faith are the root of rite, right and reason its form." Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "rite works from without, hence 文"; note: "文 is like 'movement'; rite composes a man's outward bearing."
- *Grace; fair (美, 善).* Liji, *Yueji*: "rite tends to diminish, so to press forward is its grace (文); music tends to overflow, so to draw back is its grace"; note: "文 is like 'fair, good.'"
- *Eloquence.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 23: "I am not the equal of [Zhao] Cui in eloquence (文)"; note: "having literary diction."
- *The letter of the law (文法).* Former Han, *Biographies of the Harsh Officials*: "Sima An's [skill in] the letter of the law"; note: "to harm men by the letter of the law"; and: "their cases were so framed (文致) that they could not be overturned"; note: "their dossiers were neat and tight."
- *A surname.* Former Han, *Biographies of Upright Officials*: "Wen Weng, a man of Shu in Lujiang."
- *A posthumous title.* Shiji, the law of titles: "to weave warp and weft of Heaven and Earth is 文; broad in virtue and learning is 文; diligent in study and fond of inquiry is 文; kind, gracious, and loving the people is 文; pitying the people and gracious in rite is 文; granting the people rank and station is 文."
- *A beast-name.* Shanhaijing: "on Mount Fanggao is a beast shaped like a bee, with a forked tail and reversed tongue, good at calling, named *wenwen* (文文)."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 文運, sounding like 問 (wèn). Lunyu: "the petty man, when he errs, is sure to gloss it over (文)"; Zhu's tradition: "文 is to dress it up, in the departing tone."
- *Fanqie 眉貧, sounding like 珉 (mín) — "to ornament."* Liji, *Yuzao*: "the grandee's tablet uses fish-whisker-mottled figured (文) bamboo"; so read by Liu Changzong.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 微勻.* Cui Yin, *Dazhi*: "...moulding with royal substance, carving with Tang's culture (文); the six directions in concord, house by house turned to benevolence." Zhang Heng, *Xijing fu*: "...matching in will [Lord] Wuji, treading the track of Tian Wen."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 無沿.* Cai Hong, *Chess fu*: "lines drawn to mark the bounds, white ground and red pattern (文); upright and true, square and not round.
