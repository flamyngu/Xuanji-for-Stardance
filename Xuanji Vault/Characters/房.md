**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, middle; Door radical 戶; p.415 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 符方, sounding like 防 (fáng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a chamber at the side (室在旁)."
- *A southeastern lunar mansion.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Heavenly Steeds (天駟) are Fang (房)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in the tenth month the sun is in Fang." *Shangshu yunqishou*: "the so-called 'Fang, the way of the four markers.'"
- *A prefecture.* In the Spring-and-Autumn, 房渚; the Qin made it Fangling commandery; in the Tang Wude era it was renamed Fangzhou.
- *Surname.* Shun enfeoffed Yao's son as Marquis of Fang-yi; his descendant Ziling took the father's fief as the clan-name.
- *A sacrificial stand (俎).* Shijing, *Lu song*: "the platters and the great stand (大房)"; note: "the half-carcass stand, with feet and a base, like a hall's side-chamber (堂房)."
- *A quiver (箭室).* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 12: "Zhuangzi of Zhi, each time he shot, drew the choice arrows and put them in Chuzi's quiver (房)."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 步光; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 蒲光 — sounding like 傍 (páng). *Guangyun*: "阿房 (Epang), the name of a Qin palace."
- *Rhymes as 馮 (féng), fanqie 符風.* *Daozang*, *Song of the Zhongyue Immortal*: "…wheeling and soaring by the Gate-of-Heaven chamber (房)."

**Textual note.** 房，天駟也 should read 天駟，房也.
