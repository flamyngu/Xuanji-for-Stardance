**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, lower; Even radical 齊; p.1531 #06)*

**Ancient graph.** 亝.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 徂兮. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 前西 — sounding like 臍 (qí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "grain and wheat putting forth ears, level on top." Note: Xu Kai: "nothing grows so even (齊) as grain and wheat."
- *To order, make neat (整).* *Yupian.* *Zheng Yun*: "without bias." Xunzi, *Fuguo*: "he must cultivate rite to order (齊) the court, rectify the laws to order the officers, and even the governance to order the people"; note: "齊 means to order."
- *Equal (等).* Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "the sons of great houses and the rich, cock-fighting, racing dogs and horses, hunting and gaming, disorder the common folk (齊民)"; note: Ru Chun: "齊 means equal; having no noble or base, they are the 'levelled people' (齊民)."
- *Grave, solemn (莊, 肅).* *Zheng Yun.* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 2: "though the son be grave (齊) and sage, he does not eat before his father"; note: "齊 means solemn."
- *Upright (正).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "men grave (齊) and sage"; note: "men centred, upright, and penetrating."
- *The middle (中).* *Erya, Shiyan*: "*yin* and *qi* mean 'the middle'"; note: "the Shidi says, 'reaching from Qizhou southward'"; subcommentary: "齊 means middle; the central region is Qizhou (齊州), as one says the Central States." Liezi, *Huangdi*: "the country of Huaxu — none knows how many thousand myriad *li* it lies apart from the central (齊) land"; note: "齊 is 'middle.'"
- *Fine (好).* *Guangyun.*
- *To distinguish (辨).* Yijing, *Xici*: "the small and great rest in the hexagrams (齊)"; note: "齊 is like 'to distinguish.'"
- *Swift (速).* Erya, *Shiyan*: "*ji* and *qi* mean 'vigorous'"; note: "swift." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "in youth quick and ready (徇齊)"; note: "徇 is quick, 齊 is swift."
- *A state-name.* The land King Wu enfeoffed to the Grand Duke, now Qingzhou, Jinan, Wei county, Anle, etc. in Shandong. Also Qianqi (乾齊), a county in Jiuquan; see Later Han, *Treatise on Commanderies*.
- *A surname.* Fengsu tong, preface on clan-names: "the four place-derived clans — from Qi, Lu, Song, Wey — are such." Also Fangqi (放齊), a minister of Yao.
- *A posthumous title.* "Holding the heart and able to be grave," and "by aid and support attaining concord," are both titled 齊.
- *Interchangeable with 臍 (navel).* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 6: "later the lord will bite his navel (齊) [in vain regret]."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 在禮, sounding like 薺 (jì). *Ji Yun*: "齊齊, a look of reverent care." Liji, *Yuzao*: "in the temple, reverent (齊齊)."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 在詣; *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 才詣 — sounding like 劑 (jì). Liji, *Neize*: "for all food-blending (齊), look to the springtime." Note to Zhouli, *Tianguan, Jiangren*: "food has its seasoning-blend (和齊), like the compounding of drugs." Wine made by measured proportion is also 齊: Zhouli, *Tianguan, Jiuzheng*: "the five brews (五齊) and three wines." Also 火齊 (huoqi), a gem-name: "like mica, layered and splitting, yellow-red like gold."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 子計, sounding like 霽 (jì) — "to blend." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Shiyi*: "the blending (齊) of the eight delicacies."
- *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 津私, sounding like 貲 (zī). Lunyu: "gathering up his skirt-hem (齊) he ascended the hall"; Kong Anguo: "the lower edge of the robe is 齊." Liji, *Quli*: "with both hands he lifts the robe a foot off the hem (齊)"; note: "齊 is the lower seam of the skirt."
- *Fanqie 莊皆 — same as 齋 (to fast, purify).* Liji, *Jitong*: "to purify (齊) means to make even (齊): to even what is uneven, and so attain purity."
- *Fanqie 牋西, sounding like 齏 (jī) — same as 齏 (minced pickle).* Note to Zhouli, *Tianguan, Hairen*, on 五齊: "齊 should be 齏"; "the five minces (五齊): sweet-flag root, sliced meat, clam, pork-rib, and deep-water cattail"; subcommentary: "齏 is a general term for minced pickled vegetables and meat."
- *Same as 躋 (to ascend).* Liji, *Yueji*: "the earth's *qi* rises (齊)."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 疾私 — interchangeable with 薺. Liji, *Yuzao*: "in hastening he keeps to [the tune] Caiqi (采齊)"; Zheng's note: "齊 should be the 薺 of 楚薺"; *Shiwen*: fanqie 疾私. (*Zheng Yun*: fanqie 才資, sounding like 疵 (cī), for the same Caiqi.)
- *Same as 剪 (to cut).* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 子淺, sounding like 翦 (jiǎn). *Shuowen*: "to cut off; cutting to make it even (齊), hence 齊 is called 'cutting.'" Yili, *Jixi*: "below, the horse's mane is trimmed (齊)"; note: "齊 means to cut." (Also: 齋 was anciently written simply 齊.)

**Textual notes.** "齊大小者" should read "齊小大者"; "猶速也" should read "謂速也"; "以致其齊也" should read "以致齊者也".
