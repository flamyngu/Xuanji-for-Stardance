## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)

> 《康熙字典》〈子集上〉【一字部】頁75第03     〔古文〕：𠆤【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤當經切，音玎。十幹名。【說文】夏時萬物皆丁實。丁承丙，象人心。【六書正譌】丁，蠆尾也，象形。凡造物必以金木爲丁附著之，因聲借爲丙丁字。【爾雅·釋天】太歲在丁曰彊圉，月在丁曰圉。【禮·月令】仲春之月上丁，命樂正習舞釋菜。又【唐書·禮樂志】仲春，仲秋，釋奠於文宣王，皆以上丁。　又五丁，力士。【蜀記】秦惠王欲伐蜀，造石牛，置金其後。蜀人使五丁力士拖石成道，秦遂伐蜀。【杜甫詩】論功超五丁。　又六丁，神名。【道書】陽官六甲，隂官六丁。謂六甲中丁神也。　又【爾雅·釋詁】丁，當也。【註】相當値。【詩·大雅】寧丁我躬。　又民丁。【唐書·食貨志】租庸調之法，以人丁爲本。又授田十畝，歲輸粟二斛，謂之租丁。　又【莊子·養生主】庖丁解牛。【杜甫詩】畦丁負籠至。【宋史·高宗紀】團敎峒丁槍杖手。【又】罷廉州貢珠，縱蛋丁自便。【何異傳】募山丁，捕首亂者。【唐璘傳】團結漁業茶鹽舟夫蘆丁，悉備燎舟之具。【元史·博爾忽傳】畬丁溪子。【橘錄】金橘高不及尺許，結實繁多。園丁種之，以鬻於市。　又零丁，或作伶仃，失志貌。【晉書·李密傳】零丁孤苦，至於成立。　又彼此相屬曰丁寧。【後漢·郞覬傳】丁寧再三，留神於此。俗作叮嚀。　又丁寧，鉦也。【左傳·宣四年】楚伯棼射王，汰輈及鼔跗，著於丁寧。　又丁東，聲也。凡玉珮鐵馬聲皆曰丁當。當東二音古通。　又丁水。【水經注】泗水又東南流，丁溪水注之。　又【爾雅·釋魚】魚枕謂之丁。【註】枕在魚頭骨中，形似篆書丁字。　又丁子，科斗也。初生如丁有尾。【莊子·天下篇】丁子有尾。　又肉丁，瘡名。【物類相感賦】身上生肉丁，芝蔴花擦之。　又烏丁，茶名。見【本草】。　又吉丁，蟲名。【本草註】甲蟲也。背正綠，有翅，在甲下，出嶺南賓澄諸州，人取帶之，令人喜好相愛。　又【貫休詩】蕨苞玉粉生香壟，菌蔟紅丁出靜槎。【陸游詩】滿貯醇醪漬黃甲，密封小甕餉紅丁。皆詩人象形借用也。　又姓。本姜姓，齊太公子伋爲丁公，因以命氏。　又【逸書諡法】述義不克曰丁。　又【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤中莖切，音朾。【詩·小雅】伐木丁丁。【註】伐木聲相應也。　又【韻會小補】叶都陽切，音當。【韓愈·贈張籍詩】相見不復期，零落甘所丁。嬌兒未絕乳，念之不能忘。【正字通】唐書張弘靖傳，汝輩挽兩石弓，不如識一丁字。按續世說一丁作一个，因篆文个與丁相似。傳寫譌作丁。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; One radical 一; p.75 #03)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠆤.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 當經 — sounding like 玎 (dīng).

**Etymology and senses.** The name of the fourth of the Ten Stems. *Shuowen*: "in summer-time the myriad things are all robust and solid (丁實); 丁 follows 丙, and pictures the human heart." Liushu zhengwei: "丁 is a scorpion's tail (蠆尾), pictographic; in making any object one always uses metal or wood as a nail (丁) to fasten it — by its sound it was borrowed for the graph of the 丙丁 stem." Erya, *Shitian*: "when the Grand Year is at ding (丁) it is called Qiangyu (彊圉); when the moon is at ding it is called Yu (圉)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "on the first ding-day (上丁) of mid-spring, he orders the Music-Master to practise dancing and to make the vegetable-offering." Also Tang shu, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "in mid-spring and mid-autumn, the libation-sacrifice to King Wenxuan [Confucius] is always on the first ding-day (上丁)."
- *Also 五丁 (the Five Stalwarts), strongmen.* Shu ji: "King Hui of Qin, wishing to attack Shu, made stone oxen and placed gold behind them. The men of Shu sent the Five Stalwarts (五丁) to drag the stones and make a road, and Qin then attacked Shu." Du Fu's poem: "in merit he surpasses the Five Stalwarts (五丁)."
- *Also 六丁 (the Six Ding), spirit-names.* The Daoist books: "the yang officers are the Six Jia, the yin officers the Six Ding (六丁)" — the ding-spirits among the Six Jia.
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "丁 means to meet, to befall (當)"; note: "to coincide with." Shijing, *Daya*: "alas, it befalls (丁) my own person."
- *Also 民丁 (the able-bodied).* Tang shu, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "the system of land-tax, labor-service, and cloth-levy took the able-bodied man (人丁) as its basis; also, ten mu of land were granted, and two hu of grain levied yearly — called the tax-poll (租丁)."
- *Also, a man engaged in a trade.* Zhuangzi, *Yangsheng zhu*: "Cook Ding (庖丁) cut up an ox." Du Fu's poem: "the field-hand (畦丁) comes bearing a basket." Song shi, *Annals of Gaozong*: "they grouped and drilled the cave-folk (峒丁) as spearmen." Also: "the pearl-tribute of Lianzhou was abolished, and the pearl-divers (蛋丁) let do as they pleased." *Biography of He Yi*: "he recruited the hill-folk (山丁) to capture the ringleaders of the revolt." *Biography of Tang Lin*: "he banded together the fishers, tea- and salt-men, boatmen, and reed-cutters (蘆丁), all furnished with gear for firing boats." Yuan shi, *Biography of Borhu*: "the swidden-folk (畬丁) and stream-lads." Julu: "the golden kumquat grows scarce a foot high, bearing fruit in plenty; the garden-hand (園丁) plants it to sell in the market."
- *Also 零丁 (lingding, forlorn), sometimes written 伶仃, the look of being downcast.* Jin shu, *Biography of Li Mi*: "forlorn (零丁) and alone in my hardship, until I came of age."
- *Also, mutual enjoining is called 丁寧.* Later Han, *Biography of Lang Yi*: "enjoining (丁寧) two and three times, giving heed to this." Vulgarly written 叮嚀.
- *Also 丁寧 is a gong (鉦).* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 4: "Bo Fen of Chu shot at the king; the arrow grazed the chariot-shaft and the drum-stand, and lodged in the gong (丁寧)."
- *Also 丁東 (dingdong), a sound*; the sound of jade pendants and iron horse-bells is all called 丁當; the two sounds 當 and 東 anciently interchanged.
- *Also 丁水 (the Ding waters).* Shuijing zhu: "the Si waters flow further southeast, and the Dingxi waters (丁溪水) join them."
- *Also* Erya, *Shiyu*: "the fish's pillow-bone is called 丁"; note: "the pillow-bone is within the fish's head, shaped like the seal-script graph 丁."
- *Also 丁子 (dingzi), the tadpole*; newly born it is like a 丁 with a tail. Zhuangzi, *Tianxia*: "the tadpole (丁子) has a tail."
- *Also 肉丁 (rouding), a name for a sore.* Wulei xianggan fu: "if a flesh-boil (肉丁) grows on the body, rub it with sesame flower."
- *Also 烏丁 (wuding), a tea-name*; see the Bencao.
- *Also 吉丁 (jiding), an insect-name.* Note to the Bencao: "a beetle; its back pure green, with wings beneath the carapace; it comes from the prefectures of Bin and Cheng in Lingnan; people take it and wear it, and it makes them loved and cherished."
- *Also* Guanxiu's poem: "fern-buds, jade-powdered, grow on fragrant ridges; mushroom-clusters, red-knobbed (紅丁), spring from the quiet raft." Lu You's poem: "brim-full of mellow ale to steep the yellow crab, tightly sealing the small jar to feast on the red knobs (紅丁)" — all are poets' pictographic borrowings.
- *Also a surname.* Originally of the Jiang clan; Ji, son of the Grand Duke of Qi, was Duke Ding (丁公), and so it became the clan-name.
- *Also* the Yishu *Shifa*: "to expound righteousness but fail to realize it is called 丁."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 中莖, sounding like 朾 (zhēng). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "they fell the trees, chop-chop (丁丁)"; note: "the sounds of wood-felling answering one another."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunhui xiaobu*) as fanqie 都陽, sounding like 當 (dāng). Han Yu, *poem to Zhang Ji*: "we meet, but with no set time again; fading and falling, I take what befalls (丁); my darling child not yet weaned — to think of it I cannot forget."

**Editorial notes.** *Zhengzitong*: "Tang shu, *Biography of Zhang Hongjing*: 'you fellows draw a two-picul bow, but it is no use unless you know a single graph 丁.' A note: the Xu Shishuo writes 'a single 丁' as 'a single 个' (ge); because in seal script 个 and 丁 resemble each other, in copying it was miswritten as 丁."
