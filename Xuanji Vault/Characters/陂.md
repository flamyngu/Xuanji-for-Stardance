**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Mound radical 阜; p.1348 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 彼爲. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 班麋. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 逋眉 — sounding like 𥓓 (bēi).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A slope (阪). One account: a pool (池)." *Yupian*: "a marsh-bank (澤鄣)." *Fengsu tongyi*: "陂 means to multiply (繁); it speaks of gathering water below to multiply the good of the myriad things; today all 陂 are for irrigation." Shu, *Yu gong*: "the nine marshes were already banked (陂)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "do not drain the rivers and marshes, do not bail dry the embanked pools (陂池)"; note: "stored water is called 陂."

**Senses.**
- *A place-name.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 4: "the men of Xu defeated them at Zhanbei (展陂)"; note: "Zhanbei is a place in Xu." Also Zhao yr 13: "he camped at Yubei (魚陂)"; note: "northwest of the city of Jingling county there is the Ganyu embankment (甘魚陂)." Also *Zhengzitong*: "Huangbei (黃陂), a county-name, now in Huangzhou prefecture."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "they urge on the rain-master to sprinkle the roadside (路陂)"; note: "路陂 is the roadside."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: sometimes written 波. Shiji, *Biographies of the Money-makers*: "dwelling by water with a thousand 石 of fish-ponds (魚陂)." Also the *Biography of Guan Fu*: "embanked pools, fields and gardens (陂池田園)"; the *Hanshu* in both places writes 波. Also Former Han, *Biographies of the Thirteen Kings of Jing's line*: "he roamed the Lei pool (雷波)"; note: "波 is read as 陂; Leibei (雷陂) is the name of an embankment."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 蒲麋, sounding like 皮 (pí). 陂池 (pituo), the look of a thing toppling at the side.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 滂禾, sounding like 坡 (pō). *Ji Yun*: "same as 𨸭; 𨸭陀 (potuo), uneven." Erya, *Shi di*: "a slope is called a 阪 (陂者曰阪)." Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "陂 means slanting; in Chen, Chu, Jing, and Yang it is called 陂." *Shiming*: "the side of a hill is called 陂; it speaks of being sloped (陂陁)." *Yupian*: "陂陀 (potuo), shelving and trailing." *Tang yun zheng*: "in the Shu, *Hong fan*, 'without bias, without slant (無偏無陂), follow the king's right' — it was originally written 頗, and Tang Minghuang (Emperor Xuanzong) changed it to 陂." The *Yunbu* says: "anciently the graph 義 was all sounded 俄, and the *Zhouguan* note too sounds it 俄; hence the old-text *Shangshu* originally wrote '無偏無頗遵王之義,' to rhyme with the 俄 sound. Tang Minghuang took the present sound of 義 as 乂, and changed 頗 to 陂 to follow the present sound, and so the old sound was lost."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 逋禾, sounding like 波 (bō). *Boya*: "陂陁 (potuo), declining (衰)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: "one account: a hillside (山坡); sometimes written 岥."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 蒲波, sounding like 婆 (pó). 陂陁 (potuo), uneven.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 彼義. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 兵媚 — sounding like 賁 (bì). *Yupian*: "tilted (傾); aslant (邪)." *Zheng Yun*: "what is not level is called 險 (perilous); what is not upright is called 陂." Yijing, *Tai* hexagram: "no level without a slope (无平不陂), no going without a return." Zhouli, *Chun guan, Diantong*: "the slanting tone (陂聲) is scattered." Shiji, *Hereditary Houses of the Five Lineages*: "of the envoys and travellers passing through Handan, because Pengzu was perilous and crooked (險陂), none would stay at Handan."
