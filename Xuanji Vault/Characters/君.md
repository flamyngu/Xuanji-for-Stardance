**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.177 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠱩, 𠱭.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 舉云. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 拘云 — sounding like 軍 (jūn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Honoured (尊). From 尹 (to govern); because he issues commands, it [also] follows 口 (mouth)." *Baihu Tong*: "君 means 'flock (羣)' — the multitude below turn their hearts to him." Yijing, *Shi*: "the great lord (大君) has a command." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "August Heaven favoured him with its command, that he should possess all within the Four Seas and be lord (君) of the world."

**Senses.**
- *Any holder of land.* Yili, *Zixia zhuan*: "君, the most honoured"; note: "the Son of Heaven, the feudal lords, and the land-holding ministers and grandees are all called 君."
- *A lord's wife.* Shijing, *Yong feng*: "I take her for 君"; tradition: "君國, the 'lesser ruler (小君).'" Lunyu: "the wife of a state-lord — the people call her 君夫人; she calls herself 寡小君 before other states; men of other states also call her 君夫人."
- *Of parents.* Yijing, *Jia ren*: "the household has its stern lords (嚴君) — meaning father and mother." Of forebears: Kong Anguo's Preface to the Shu: "my late lord (先君) Confucius, born at the end of Zhou."
- *An elder of a younger brother.* Du Mu, epitaph for his brother: "君 (he), tabooed name Yi."
- *A concubine of her husband.* Liji, *Nei ze*: "when 君 (the master) has eaten, [she] clears away"; note: "this refers to the concubine of a knight or grandee." Yili, *Sang fu*: "the concubine calls [her husband] 君"; note: "not being his equal, she adds honour to him."
- *A wife of her husband.* Yuefu: "at seventeen I became 君's wife."
- *A husband of his wife: 細君.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "I take it home to give my 'little lady (細君).'"
- *A superior of an inferior.* Shiji, *Biography of Shentu Jia*: "the Emperor said, '君 (sir), say nothing — I favoured him in private.'"
- *A title of nobility.* Shiji, *Lord Shang*: "Qin fiefed him at Shang with fifteen towns, titled Lord Shang (商君)." Of women too: Shiji, *Hereditary Houses of the Consort Kin*: "honoured the Dowager's mother Zang Er as Lady Pingyuan (平原君)."
- *Mutual address.* Shiji, *Biography of Sima Rangju*: "the people's lives all hang on 君 (you)" — 君 being Zhuang Jia. *Biography of Zhang Yi*: a retainer said, "I do not know 君 (you); I know Lord Su."
- *徵君, a summoned recluse.* Hou Han, *Biography of Han Kang*: "the village-head [thought] the summoned-gentleman (徵君) Han would arrive."
- *使君, a commissioned envoy.* Hou Han, *Biography of Kou Xun*: "I dare not coerce the 使君 (commissioner)."
- *Posthumous title.* "To reward and to awe is 君; to be followed into a flock is 君."
- *君子, the term for perfected virtue.* Yijing, *Qian*: "the gentleman (君子), all day vigilant." Lunyu: "is he not a gentleman (君子)?"; note: "君子, the name for perfected virtue."
- *Surname.* Zhengzitong: in the Ming, Jun Zhu.
- *Rhymes as 涓 (juān), fanqie 姑員.* Liu Xiang, *Lives of Women*: "she drew the blame and yielded; King Xuan awoke to it; morning and night honouring the Way, he became a restoring lord (中興君)."
