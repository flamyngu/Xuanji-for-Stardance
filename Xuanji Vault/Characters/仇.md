**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Man radical 人; p.91 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 巨鳩. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 渠尤 — sounding like 求 (qiú).

**Etymology and senses.** "A mate (匹)." Kuang Heng cites the *Shijing*: "the gentleman's good mate (好仇)" — the same as 逑. Erya, *Shigu*: "仇 means to match (合)"; note: "it means a matched pair."
- *Also a hated foe (怨敵).* Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "I will mend my dagger-axe and spear, and share with you a common foe (同仇)." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "Shi Fu said, 'a good mate is called 妃, a hated mate is called 仇.'"
- *Also, an enemy (讐).* Shiji, *Hereditary House of the Marquis of Liu*: "Zhang Liang took vengeance (報仇) for Han."
- *Also* Erya, *Shixun*: "仇仇 and 敖敖 mean haughty (傲)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "he holds me carelessly (仇仇)"; tradition: "like 謷謷 (disdainfully)."
- *Also* *Shiming*: "仇 is a spear-foe (矛讐): what it strikes is laid low, as in chastising an enemy."
- *Also a surname.* The Song grandee Chou Mu (仇牧). Also Zhangchou (章仇), a compound surname; the Sui had Zhangchou Taiyi (章仇太翼).
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 恭於; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 斤於 — the same as 㪺. Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the guests then ladle (仇) with their hands"; note: "to scoop up wine with the hand."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 渠之, sounding like 奇 (qí). Shijing, *Zhounan*: "a good mate (好仇) for lord and prince" — rhyming with 逵 above.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 彊於, sounding like 渠 (qú). Lu Ji, *Ganqiu fu*: "he girds his bright spirit to engage the world, and makes of his very life a match (仇); forgetting the long darkness through a thousand years, men vie for a morning's bloom in a moment's span."
