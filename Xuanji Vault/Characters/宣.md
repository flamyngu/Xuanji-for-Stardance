**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.284 #16)*

**Ancient graph.** 𡩦.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 須緣. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 荀緣. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息緣 — sounding like 瑄 (xuān).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The Son of Heaven's Xuan Chamber (宣室). It follows 宀 (roof) with 𠄢 as phonetic." Xu Xuan: "it follows 回 (to turn) — the wind turning round, that by which yin and yang are diffused (宣)."

**Senses.**
- *Everywhere, all about (徧).* Erya, *Shi yan*. Shijing, *Daya*: "being settled, then it spread abroad (宣)"; note: "順 means at ease; 宣 means everywhere."
- *To penetrate, get through (通).* Zuozhuan, Jia Kui's note. Shijing, *Zhou song*: "penetrating and wise (宣哲) is the man"; note: "宣 means to penetrate, 哲 means wise."
- *To spread, to scatter (布, 散).* Shu, *Gao Yao mo*: "daily displaying the three virtues (宣三德)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in late autumn, gather in the stores of Heaven and Earth, letting nothing be let out (宣出)"; note: "things are all gathered in, with nothing disclosed and let out and scattered (宣露出散)."
- *To summon (召).* *Zengyun*. Bao Ji's poem: "across the screen I first hear the jade voice proclaimed (宣)."
- *Slow, easy (緩).* Erya, *Shi yan*.
- *To exhaust; to make clear; to show (盡, 明, 示).* *Discourses of Zhou*: "one who manages a river breaches it to make it flow; one who manages a people lets them speak out (宣)." Also Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 13: "thus we proclaim (宣) it, to chastise the disunited."
- *Also* *Zhaoshu bielu*: "by Tang precedent, the Secretariat Drafter managed edicts and proclamations, always copying two versions — one the master-copy (底), one the proclamation-copy (宣); kept in the Secretariat for checking, this was called the true proclamation (正宣)."
- *In posthumous titles (諡法):* "good repute, penetrating all round, is called 宣"; one account: "holy goodness heard all round is called 宣."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Kao gong ji*: "in the cartwright's work, half a carpenter's-square is called a 宣."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi qi*: "a jade-disc six inches across is called a 宣"; Guo Pu's note: "it is the xuan-jade (瑄玉) of the Hanshu; 瑄 and 宣 are the same."
- *A prefecture-name.* The Wu Xuancheng commandery; the Tang renamed it Xuanzhou (宣州).
- *Surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "Xuan Bing, a man of the Eastern Han."
- *Hair going white and falling (頭髮皓落).* Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "Xun is white hair (宣髮)"; note: "the hair turning white early." The modern text corruptly writes 寡髮 (sparse hair); *Shiwen*: "寡 was properly 宣."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 荀 (xún), fanqie 相倫.* Xu Gan, *Qidu fu*: "the sun does not shift its shadow, and the dark bounty is spread (宣) abroad; the Quail-Fire flies south, and my lord comes touring." *Ji Yun*: properly written 𡨈.

**Textual note.** "Erya, *Shi xun*: 'to penetrate (通)'" — a note: the Erya has no such text; it is corrected to the Zuozhuan, Jia Kui's note. Jia's note is seen in Shiji, chapter 42.
