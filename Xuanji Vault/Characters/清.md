**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.632 #15)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠗜.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun* and *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 七情. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 親盈 — sounding like 圊, i.e. **qīng**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Limpid" — the look of clarified water. *Shiming*: "清 (clear) is 靑 (blue-green): rid of turbidity, far from filth, its colour like azure." Shijing, *Zheng feng*: "How clear it runs!"

**Senses.**
- *Still, settled, pure.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "be upright—only [then] pure."
- *Of water, brought to order.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the spring's flow is now clear."
- *Of the gaze, bright.* Shijing, *Yong feng*: "your gaze clear and bright"; commentary: "a clear, bright look is 清." Shijing, *Qi feng*: "Ah, how fair! beautiful eyes so clear"; tradition: "above the eye is 名, below the eye is 清."
- *A wine.* Zhouli, *Jiu zheng* (Wine Officer): of the four drinks, the first is "清"; commentary: "清 is the strained/clarified sweet-wine." More broadly, all such drinks are called "清": Zhouli, *Shanfu*: "for the King's offerings of drink, the Six Clarities (六清) are used."
- *A river.* Shanhaijing: from Mt. Taishi the Qing waters issue. *Shuijing*: the Qing waters rise north of Xiuwu county in Henan, in the Black Mountains.
- *A river (Jiang).* Guangyu Ji: the Qing-jiang, south of Linjiang prefecture's seat.
- *A river (He).* Shuijingzhu: the Baigou waters pass northeast of Guangzong county and eastward become the Qing-he.
- *A town.* Chunqiu, Yin yr 4: "in summer Duke [of Lu] met the Duke of Song at Qing"; commentary: "Qing, a Wei town." Shijing, *Zheng feng*: "the men of Qing are at Peng"; *Correct Meaning*: "Qing, a Zheng town."
- *Surname.* In the Song there was the *jinshi* Qing Xian.

**Further readings.**
- *Ji Yun, Yun Hui, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 疾郢, sounding like 靜 (jìng) — "pure, clean."
- *Ji Yun, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 疾正, sounding like 淨 (jìng) — same as 瀞.
- *Ji Yun, Yun Hui, Zheng Yun*: fanqie 七正, sounding like 婧 (jìng) — same as 凊, "cold."
- *Yunbu*: rhymes as fanqie 于羊, sounding like 瑲. Zhang Ji's poem: "…by the bent bank of Huangzi Slope, the pool broad, the air's colour clear."

**Textual note.** In the *Qi feng* citation, 美目淸矣 should read 美目淸兮.
