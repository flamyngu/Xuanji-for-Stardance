**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls дәҘ, upper; Horse radical йҰ¬; p.1434 #11)*

**Ancient graph.** р§Ӯ“.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie зӣҙйӣў. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie йҷізҹҘ вҖ” sounding like жұ  (chГӯ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to run and gallop (иө°еҘ”)." *Guangyun*: "to race (йҰійЁ–); to drive fast (з–ҫй©…)." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 10: "the duke was about to gallop (йҰі) after them."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Yupian*: "the Son of Heaven's road." *Zhengzitong*: "the gallop-road (йҰійҒ“) is the way the Son of Heaven travels, like today's central road."
- *Surname.* *Xingyuan*: "Chi Jiugai of the Ming, a graduate of Renshou county."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie е”җдҪ•, sounding like й§қ (tuГі). Also "to run."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as йҡӢ (suЗ’), fanqie еҫ’иҮҘ.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "not losing his pace (йҰі), he looses the arrow as if to shatter."
- *Rhymes as жҒҘ (chЗҗ), fanqie е°әйҮҢ.* Liu Xiang, *Jiu tan*: "I roam and survey the Four Seas, my will rising and falling, galloping high (й«ҳйҰі); I summon the Nine Gods at the turning pole, and raise the rainbow's hues to beckon and point."
