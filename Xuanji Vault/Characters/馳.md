## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈亥集上〉【馬字部】頁1434第11    〔古文〕：𧂓【廣韻】直離切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】陳知切，𠀤音池。【玉篇】走奔也。【廣韻】馳騖也，疾驅也。【左傳·莊十年】公將馳之。　又【玉篇】天子道也。【正字通】馳道，天子所行路，若今之中道。　又姓。【姓苑】明馳九垓，仁壽縣舉人。　又【集韻】唐何切，音駝。亦走也。　又叶徒臥切，音隋。【詩·小雅】不失其馳，舍矢如破。　又叶尺里切，音恥。【劉向·九歎】周流覽於四海兮，志升降以高馳。徵九神於回極兮，建虹采以招指。　又叶直詈切，音滯。【蔡邕·彈棊賦】於是列象，彫華逞麗。豐腹斂邊，中隱四企。輕利調博，易使騁馳。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, upper; Horse radical 馬; p.1434 #11)*

**Ancient graph.** 𧂓.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 直離. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 陳知 — sounding like 池 (chí).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to run and gallop (走奔)." *Guangyun*: "to race (馳騖); to drive fast (疾驅)." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 10: "the duke was about to gallop (馳) after them."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Yupian*: "the Son of Heaven's road." *Zhengzitong*: "the gallop-road (馳道) is the way the Son of Heaven travels, like today's central road."
- *Surname.* *Xingyuan*: "Chi Jiugai of the Ming, a graduate of Renshou county."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 唐何, sounding like 駝 (tuó). Also "to run."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 隋 (suǒ), fanqie 徒臥.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "not losing his pace (馳), he looses the arrow as if to shatter."
- *Rhymes as 恥 (chǐ), fanqie 尺里.* Liu Xiang, *Jiu tan*: "I roam and survey the Four Seas, my will rising and falling, galloping high (高馳); I summon the Nine Gods at the turning pole, and raise the rainbow's hues to beckon and point."
- *Rhymes as 滯 (zhì), fanqie 直詈.* Cai Yong, *Danqi fu*: "thereupon are arrayed the figures, carved and flowering, flaunting their beauty; full-bellied and trim-edged, the centre sunk and the four corners rising; light and handy, easy to play, easily made to course and gallop (騁馳).
