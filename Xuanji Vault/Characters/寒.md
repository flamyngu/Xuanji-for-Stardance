**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҜ…, upper; Roof radical е®Җ; p.289 #09)*

**Ancient graphs.** рЎ«®, рЎ«ҫ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie иғЎе®ү. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жІіе№І вҖ” sounding like йҹ“ (hГЎn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To freeze (еҮҚ)." Properly written рЎ«ҫ: it shows a person (дәә) under a roof (е®Җ), with mats and grasses (иҢ») heaped to cover him, and ice (д»Ң) below вҖ” д»Ң being water. In clerical script it is abbreviated to еҜ’. *Shiming*: "еҜ’ means to ward off (жҚҚ); жҚҚ means to fend off (ж ј)." *Yupian*: "the winter season (еҶ¬жҷӮ)." Yijing, *Xi ci*: "sun and moon move in their courses, now cold (еҜ’), now hot." Shu, *Hong fan*: "among the manifold signs are warmth (зҮ ) and cold (еҜ’)"; tradition: "by warmth things are grown, by cold things are completed."

**Senses.**
- *еҸёеҜ’, the water-god.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 4: "with a black bull and black millet they offered to Sihan (еҸёеҜ’)"; note: "Sihan is Xuanming, the water-god."
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "when the Fire star appears, the clear wind gives warning of cold (жҲ’еҜ’)." Xun Yue, *Shen jian*: "if below there are cold (еҜ’) people, then above the robes are incomplete."
- *Also, poor and hard-pressed (зӘ®зӘҳ).* Shiji, *Biography of Fan Ju*: "has Fan Shu come to such cold poverty (дёҖеҜ’) as this!" Songshi, *Biography of Chen Tongfu*: "the eccentric and the poor scholars (еҜ’еЈ«) all relied on him."
- *A state-name*, on the Northern Sea, at Hanting east of Pingshou county. Shiji, *Basic Annals of Xia*: "the fief of Han Zhuo, the slanderous son of the Boming clan."
- *Surname.* Han Lang, an Erudite of the Han.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as иіў (xiГЎn), fanqie иғЎз”°.* Chuci, *Tian wen*: "where is it warm in winter? where is it cold (еҜ’) in summer? where is there a forest of stone? what beast can speak?"
- *Rhymes as зңһ (zhД“n), fanqie еҒҙй„°.* Ouyang Xiu, *Reply to Han Zihua*: "the talented sink to low places; let evil depart and good will then unfold вҖ” reaching every house and every door, that the hungry be fed and the cold-clad (иЎЈеҜ’) clothed."
