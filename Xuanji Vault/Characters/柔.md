## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集中〉【木字部】頁519第05【唐韻】耳由切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】而由切，𠀤受平聲。【說文】木曲直也。　又柔者剛之反。【易·說卦】立地之道，曰柔與剛。【書·洪範】沈潛剛克，高明柔克。【老子·道德經】豈非以其柔耶。　又安也。【書·舜典】柔遠能邇。　又服也。【左傳·僖二十八年】我得天，楚伏其罪，我且柔之矣。　又草木新生曰柔。【詩·小雅】薇亦柔止。　又國名。【前漢·地理志】屬琅邪郡。俗作𣐱，非。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.519 #05)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 耳由. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 而由 — the level tone of 受 (róu).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The bending and straightening of wood (木曲直)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* the soft (柔) is the opposite of the hard. Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "the way of Earth set up is called soft and (與) hard." Shu, *Hong fan*: "for the sunken and deep, the hard overcomes; for the high and bright, the soft overcomes (柔克)." Laozi, *Daode jing*: "is it not by its softness (柔)?"
- *To make at peace (安).* Shu, *Shun dian*: "cherish the far and be kind to the near (柔遠能邇)."
- *To make submit (服).* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "I have won Heaven's favour, Chu has bowed to its guilt, and I shall now subdue (柔) it."
- *Newly grown grass and trees are called 柔 (tender).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the osmund too is tender (柔)."
- *A state-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "it belongs to Langya commandery." Commonly written 𣐱, which is wrong.
