**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls е·і, upper; Water radical ж°ҙ; p.623 #04)*

**Ancient graph.** гі….

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie еҠӣжұӮ, sounding like з•ҷ, i.e. **liГә**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The going of water." *Erya, Shi yan*: "жөҒ means 'to extend/reach' (иҰғ)"; subcommentary: "the flowing of water must spread on and reach further." Shijing, *Daya*: "like the flowing of a river."

**Senses.**
- *To select, to seek.* Erya, *Shi gu*: "жөҒ means 'to choose,' 'to seek'." Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "left and right she seeks (жөҒ) it."
- *Downward.* Shijing, *Bin feng*: "in the seventh month the Fire-star descends (жөҒ)."
- *Unchecked, without restraint.* Liji, *Yue ji*: "when music wins out, there is dissoluteness (жөҒ)."
- *A tribute-zone.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "the two-hundred-*li* zone is the 'flowing' zone." Liji, *Wang zhi*: "beyond a thousand *li* are called *cai* and *liu*."
- *To banish.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "with banishment (жөҒ) he tempered the Five Punishments."
- *An unsteady, wandering gaze.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 6: "the Earl of Zheng's gaze wandered (жөҒ) and his step was hurried."
- *To flee.* Zhanguo Ce: "King Xiang of Chu fled and hid (жөҒж·№) at Chengyang"; commentary: "to run and conceal oneself."
- *A spout.* Zhouli, *Kaogong ji*, commentary to the jade-worker: "the 'beak-ladle' is called жөҒ; all such spouts are made as dragon-mouths."
- *A weight of silver.* Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "Zhuti silver, eight *liang* to a жөҒ."
- *A 'stream/school' of thought.* Former Han, *Treatise on Letters*: the Confucian stream, the Daoist stream, the Yin-Yang stream, the Legalist stream, the Mohist stream, the Diplomatist stream, the Eclectic stream, the Agriculturalist stream, the Storyteller stream вҖ” each with its own origin, divided into nine schools.
- *To spread abroad.* Yijing, Modesty hexagram: "the way of Earth alters the full and makes flow toward (жөҒ) the modest."
- *To circulate, revolve.* Liji, *Zhongni yan ju*: "circulating everywhere (е‘ЁжөҒ) with nothing left out."
- *To indulge to excess.* Zhang Xie, *Qi ming*: "tip the wine-jar one morning, and you wallow (жөҒж№Һ) a thousand days"; commentary: "to even one's complexion and balance many and few is called жөҒ."
- *An office: 'Chief of the Flow' (й•·жөҒ).* Yan Zhitui, *Family Instructions*: "the Adjutant for Adjudicating Prisons was called *Chang-liu*."
- *'Yellow Flow,' a wine.* Shijing, *Daya*: "the yellow flow is in the midst."
- *Shuangliu, a county name.* Guangyu Ji: belonging to Chengdu prefecture.

**Rhyming variants.**
- Rhymes as й–ӯ (lЗҳ), fanqie рЈјҠеҰӮ. Lu Yun's poem: "music sounds its grief; words come, and tears flow."
- Rhymes as жәң (liГ№), fanqie еҠӣж•‘. Bo Xuan's poem: "far, far is Jianping; the imperial grace has not yet flowed.
