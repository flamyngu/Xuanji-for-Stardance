> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【氏字部】頁598第01    〔古文〕：𠘽【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤彌鄰切，音泯。【說文】衆萌也。言萌而無識也。【易·師卦】君子以容民畜衆。【書·咸有一德】后非民罔使，民非后罔治。【禮·緇衣】民以君爲心，君以民爲體。　又四民。【穀梁傳·成元年】古者四民：有士民，有商民，有農民，有工民。【註】德能居位曰士，辟土植穀曰農，巧心勞手成器物曰工，通財貨曰商。　又司民，星名。【周禮·秋官·司民】及孟冬祀司民之日，獻其數于王。【註】司民，軒轅角也。【釋文】軒轅十七星，如龍形，有兩角，角有大民、小民。　又官名。【周禮·秋官·司民】掌登萬民之數，自生齒以上，皆書於版。　又民曹，漢官名。【後漢·百官志】民曹尚書主凡吏上書事。【註】《蔡質·漢舊儀》曰：典膳治功作，監池、苑囿、盜賊事。　又民部，今戸部也。【文獻通考】漢置尚書郞四人，其一人主財帛委輸。至魏文帝，置度支尚書寺，專掌軍國支計。吳有戸部，晉有度支，皆主筭也。後周置大司徒卿一人，如《周禮》之制，隋初有度支尚書，則幷後周民部之職。開皇二年，改度支爲民部。永徽初，改民部爲戸部。　又北方有比肩民，見【爾雅·釋地】。南方有裸民，見【呂氏春秋】。又白民國，白身，背有角，乗之壽二千歲。羽民國，其民皆生羽毛。卵民國，其民皆生卵。毛民國，其民皆生毛。又有勞民、搖民、壎民、盈民、臷民、蜮民諸國，𠀤見【山海經】。又鶴民國，人長三寸，日行千里，見【窮神祕苑】。　又姓，見【姓苑】。　又叶鄰知切，音離。【夏禹襄陵操】洪水滔天，下民愁悲。上帝愈咨，三過吾門不入。父子道衰，嗟嗟不欲煩下民。　又叶彌延切，音眠。【楊方·合歡詩】齊彼蛩蛩獸，舉動不相捐。生有同穴好，死成倂棺民。【六書略】民，象俯首力作之形。○按民字之義非一。有總言人者，《詩》天生蒸民，厥初生民。是也。有對君而言者，《書》民惟邦本。是也。有別於在位而言者，《詩》宜民宜人。註：人謂臣，民謂衆庶。是也。有對幽而言者，《論語》務民之義，《左傳》先成民而後致力于神。是也。有對已而言者，《詩》民莫不穀，我獨于罹。是也。有對農而言者，《漢·食貨志》粟甚貴傷民，甚賤傷農。是也。况四民兼士農工商，豈力田始稱民乎。《六書略》之說穿鑿，不可從。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Clan radical 氏; p.598 #01)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠘽.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 彌鄰, sounding like 泯 (mín).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The teeming sprouts (衆萌); it speaks of sprouting without understanding." Yijing, *Shi* hexagram: "the gentleman thereby cherishes the people (民) and nurtures the multitude." Shu, *Xian you yi de*: "the ruler without the people has none to employ, the people without the ruler have none to govern them." Liji, *Zi yi*: "the people take the ruler for their heart, the ruler takes the people for his body."

**Senses.**
- *The four kinds of folk (四民).* Guliang zhuan, Cheng yr 1: "anciently there were four kinds of folk: the scholar-folk, the merchant-folk, the farmer-folk, and the artisan-folk"; note: "able in virtue to hold office is the scholar (士); to open the soil and plant grain is the farmer (農); to make vessels with cunning heart and toiling hand is the artisan (工); to circulate goods is the merchant (商)."
- *Simin (司民), a star-name.* Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Simin*: "and on the day of sacrificing to Simin in early winter, he presents its numbers to the king"; note: "Simin is the horn of Xuanyuan." *Shiwen*: "Xuanyuan, seventeen stars, shaped like a dragon, with two horns; on the horns are the Great Folk and the Lesser Folk (大民, 小民)."
- *An office-name.* Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Simin*: "he has charge of registering the number of the myriad folk; all from the cutting of teeth upward are written on the tablets."
- *Mincao (民曹), a Han office-name.* Later Han, *Treatise on Officialdom*: "the Mincao Master of Writing has charge of all officials' submitting of documents"; note: "Cai Zhi's *Han jiu yi* says: he had charge of the provisioning, the management of works, and the oversight of ponds, parks, and brigand-affairs."
- *Minbu (民部), the present Board of Revenue.* The *Wenxian tongkao*: "the Han set up four Masters of Writing of the Secretariat, one of whom had charge of treasure and transport. By Emperor Wen of Wei the Court of the Master of Revenue-control (度支尚書寺) was set up, with sole charge of the state's military reckonings. Wu had a Board of Revenue (戸部), Jin a Revenue-control (度支), both with charge of accounts. The Later Zhou set up one Grand Minister of Instruction, as in the system of the *Zhouli*; early Sui had the Master of Revenue-control (度支尚書), which joined to it the office of the Later Zhou Minbu. In the second year of Kaihuang, 度支 was renamed 民部; in the early Yonghui era, 民部 was renamed 戸部 (Board of Revenue)."
- *Of distant peoples.* In the north there are the Shoulder-to-shoulder Folk (比肩民), seen in Erya, *Shi di*; in the south the Naked Folk (裸民), seen in the Lüshi chunqiu. Also the White-folk Kingdom (白民國), white of body, with horns on the back, riding which one lives two thousand years; the Feather-folk Kingdom (羽民國), whose people are all born with feathers; the Egg-folk Kingdom (卵民國), whose people are all born of eggs; the Hair-folk Kingdom (毛民國), whose people are all born with hair; and the Toiling Folk (勞民), the Roaming Folk (搖民), the Xun Folk (壎民), the Brimming Folk (盈民), the Zhi Folk (臷民), and the Yu Folk (蜮民), all seen in the Shanhai jing. Also the Crane-folk Kingdom (鶴民國), whose people are three 寸 tall and go a thousand li in a day, seen in the *Qiongshen biyuan*.
- *Surname.* Seen in the *Xingyuan*.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 離 (lí), fanqie 鄰知.* The *Xia Yu Xiangling cao*: "the floodwaters drowned the sky, and the folk below (下民) grieved; the Lord on High was the more distressed; thrice I passed my gate and entered not; the way of father and son grows thin — alas, alas, I would not trouble the folk below (下民)."
- *Rhymes as 眠 (mián), fanqie 彌延.* Yang Fang, *Hehuan shi*: "like those qiongqiong beasts, whose every move forsakes not the other; living, they have the joy of a shared burrow; dead, they make one coffin's folk (倂棺民)."

**On the graph.** The *Liushu lüe*: "民 pictures the form of one who bows the head and labours with might." A note: the sense of the graph 民 is not single. There is the general term for men: the Shijing, "Heaven gives birth to the teeming folk (蒸民)," and "at first it bore the folk (生民)," are this. There is its use over against the ruler: the Shu, "the people (民) are the root of the state," is this. There is its use as distinct from those in office: the Shijing, "fitting for the folk, fitting for the men (宜民宜人)"; note: "men (人) means the ministers, folk (民) means the common throng," is this. There is its use over against the unseen [spirits]: the Lunyu, "to attend to the duty owed the folk (民)," and the Zuozhuan, "first perfect the folk and afterward bend effort to the spirits," are this. There is its use over against oneself: the Shijing, "the folk (民) all have their grain, I alone meet sorrow," is this. There is its use over against the farmer: the *Han, Treatise on Food and Money*, "grain too dear hurts the folk (民), too cheap hurts the farmer (農)," is this. Moreover the four kinds of folk take in scholar, farmer, artisan, and merchant — how should only the tiller of fields be called 民? The *Liushu lüe*'s account is forced, and is not to be followed.
