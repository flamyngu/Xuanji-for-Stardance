## Shuowen Jiezi (說文解字) — original
> 《風部》風：八風也。東方曰明庶風，東南曰清明風，南方曰景風，西南曰涼風，西方曰閶闔風，西北曰不周風，北方曰廣莫風，東北曰融風。風動蟲生。故蟲八日而化。从虫凡聲。凡風之屬皆从風。

## Guangyun (廣韻) — original
> 《廣韻·上平聲·東·風》風：敎也，佚也，告也，聲也，河圖曰風者天地之使元命包曰隂陽怒而爲風。方戎切，七。

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集下〉【風字部】頁1411第01    〔古文〕：𠙊、𠙈、凬、飌、檒【唐韻】方戎切【集韻】方馮切【正韻】方中切，𠀤音楓。風以動萬物也。【莊子·齊物論】大塊噫氣，其名爲風。【河圖】風者，天地之使。【元命包】隂陽怒而爲風。【爾雅·釋天】南風謂之凱風，東風謂之谷風，北風謂之凉風，西風謂之泰風。【禮·樂記】八風從律而不姦。【疏】八方之風也。【史記·律書】東北方條風，立春至。東方明庶風，春分至。東南方淸明風，立夏至。南方景風，夏至至。西南方凉風，立秋至。西方閶闔風，秋分至。西北方不周風，立冬至。北方廣莫風，冬至至。【周禮·春官·保章氏】以十有二風，察天地之和命，乖別之妖祥。【註】十有二辰皆有風吹其律，以知和不。　又【玉篇】散也。【易·繫辭】風以散之。　又趨風，疾如風也。【左傳·成十六年】郤至三遇楚子之卒，見楚子必下免冑而趨風。　又【玉篇】敎也。【書·畢命】彰善癉惡，樹之風聲。又【說命】四海之內，咸仰朕德，時乃風。【註】言天下仰我德，是汝之敎也。【詩·關雎序】風之始也。【箋】風是諸侯政敎也。　又風俗。【禮·樂記】移風易俗，天下皆寧。　又疾名。【左傳·昭元年】風淫末疾。【註】末，四肢也。風爲緩急。　又【廣韻】佚也。【書·費誓】馬牛其風。【傳】馬牛風佚。【疏】僖四年《左傳》云：惟是風馬牛不相及也。賈逵云：風，放也。牝牡相誘謂之風。然則馬牛風佚，因牝牡相逐而遂至放佚遠去也。　又防風，國名。今湖州武康縣。　又地名。寧風，齊地，見【左傳】。右扶風，見【漢書】。　又官名。【前漢·地理志】武帝太初元年，更名主爵都尉爲右扶風。　又鳥名。【詩·秦風】鴥彼晨風。【爾雅·釋鳥】晨風，鸇。【疏】晨風，一名鸇，摯鳥也。郭云：鷂屬。　又【晉書·輿服志】相風中道。【正字通】晉制，車駕出，相風居前。刻烏於竿上，名相風。又【述征記】長安南有臺，高十仞，立相風銅烏，遇風輒動。　又草名。【西京雜記】懷風，苜蓿別名。一名光風。　又姓。黃帝臣風后。又風胡，見【越絕書】。　又【神異經】西方有披髮東走，一名狂，一名顚，一名狷，一名風。　又【廣韻】方鳳切，音諷。【詩·關雎序】詩有六義焉。一曰風，上以風化下，下以風刺上，主文而譎諫，言之者無罪，聞之者足戒，故曰風。【箋】風化。風刺，皆謂譬諭，不直言也。【釋文】下以風之。風，福鳳反。【註】風刺同。　又叶分房切，音方。【楚辭·惜誓】涉丹水而馳騁兮，右大夏之遺風。黃鵠之一舉兮，知山川之紆曲，再舉兮睹天地之圜方。　又叶甫煩切，音蕃。【王粲詩】烈烈冬日，肅肅淒風。潛鱗在淵，歸鴈載軒。　又叶閭承切。【後漢·馮衍顯志賦】摛道德之光輝兮，匡衰世之渺風。褒宋襄于泓谷兮，表季札于延陵。　又叶孚金切，音分。【詩·邶風】絺兮綌兮，淒其以風。我思古人，實獲我心。又【大雅】吉甫作頌，穆如淸風。仲山甫永懷，以慰其心。【楚辭·九章】乗鄂渚而反顧兮，欸秋冬之緒風。步余馬兮山臯，邸余車兮方林。【釋名】兗豫幷冀，橫口含脣言之，讀若分。靑徐，踧口開脣推氣言之，讀若方。風，放也，氣放散也。【𨻰第·毛詩古音考】風古與心林音淫爲韻，孚金切。或曰今太行之西，汾晉之閒讀風如分，猶存古音。【正韻】一東收風，十二侵闕，蓋未詳風古有分音也。【說文】風動蟲生，故蟲八日而化。从虫凡聲。【趙古則曰】凡物露風則生蟲，故風从虫，凡諧聲。    考證：〔【爾雅】西風謂之秦風。〕　謹照原文秦風改泰風。〔【書·費誓】馬牛其風。【疏】賈逵云，風，放也。牝牡相誘謂之風，然則馬牛放佚，因牝牡相逐，而遂至放佚遠去也。〕　謹照原文馬牛放佚改馬牛風佚。〔【陳第·毛詩古音考】風古與心林音淫爲韻，孚金切。【正韻】一東收風，二十侵闕，蓋未詳風古有分音也。〕　謹照原文二十侵改爲十二侵。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, lower; Wind radical 風; p.1411 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠙊, 𠙈, 凬, 飌, 檒.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 方戎. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 方馮. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 方中 — sounding like 楓 (fēng).

**Senses.** "Wind, by which the myriad things are stirred." Zhuangzi, *Qiwu lun*: "the Great Clod belches breath, and its name is wind." *Hetu*: "wind is the envoy of Heaven and Earth." *Yuanmingbao*: "yin and yang in anger make wind."
- *The named winds.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the south wind is 凱風, the east 谷風, the north 凉風, the west 泰風." Liji, *Yue ji*: "the eight winds follow the pitch-pipes and are not perverse." Shiji, *Lü shu*: the eight directional winds keyed to the solar terms — NE 條風 (Beginning of Spring), E 明庶風 (Spring Equinox), SE 淸明風 (Beginning of Summer), S 景風 (Summer Solstice), SW 凉風 (Beginning of Autumn), W 閶闔風 (Autumn Equinox), NW 不周風 (Beginning of Winter), N 廣莫風 (Winter Solstice). Zhouli, *Baozhangshi*: "by the twelve winds he examines the harmony of Heaven and Earth and the portents of discord"; note: "the twelve [hour-]lodges each have a wind blowing their pitch, to know harmony or its lack."
- *To scatter (散).* *Yupian.* Yijing, *Xici*: "wind scatters them."
- *趨風, to hasten (swift as wind).* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 16: Xizhi, thrice meeting the Chu lord's troops, would dismount, doff his helmet, and hasten (趨風).
- *To teach, transform (敎).* *Yupian.* Shu, *Bi ming*: "make plain good and ill, and set up the influence (風) of repute." Shu, *Yue ming*: "all within the Four Seas look up to my virtue — this is your influence (風)"; note: "the world looks up to my virtue: this is your teaching." Preface to *Guan ju*: "the beginning of the Airs (風)"; *Jian*: "風 is the governance and teaching of the lords."
- *Custom (風俗).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "shift the customs and change the manners (移風易俗), and the world is at peace."
- *A disease.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 1: "wind taints the extremities into disease"; note: "末, the four limbs; wind makes them slack or tense."
- *To let loose (佚).* *Guangyun.* Shu, *Fei shi*: "the horses and cattle stray in heat (風)"; tradition: "horses and cattle wander in rut (風佚)." Subcommentary: Zuozhuan, Xi yr 4: "風馬牛不相及"; Jia Kui: "風 means to set loose; male and female enticing each other is 風 — so horses and cattle, drawn by mating, run off astray."
- *防風 (Fangfeng), a state* — today's Wukang county in Huzhou.
- *Place names.* 寧風 (Ningfeng), a Qi place (Zuozhuan); 右扶風 (Youfufeng) (Hanshu).
- *An office.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: in Wudi's Taichu first year, the 主爵都尉 was renamed 右扶風.
- *A bird.* Shijing, *Qin feng*: "swift that 晨風 (falcon)." Erya, *Shi niao*: "晨風 is the 鸇"; subcommentary: "晨風, also called 鸇, a bird of prey; Guo: a kind of sparrowhawk."
- *相風, the wind-vane.* Jinshu, *Treatise on Carriages and Robes*: "the 相風 in the middle of the road"; Zhengzitong: "by the Jin system, when the carriage went out the 相風 went before — a crow carved on a pole, called 相風." *Shuzheng ji*: "south of Chang'an stood a terrace ten *ren* high, set with a bronze 相風 crow that moved whenever wind came."
- *A plant.* *Xijing zaji*: "懷風, another name for clover; also called 光風."
- *Surname.* The Yellow Emperor's minister 風后 (Fenghou). Also 風胡 (Fenghu) (Yuejue shu).
- *Shenyijing*: "in the west is one who runs east with hair unbound, named 狂, 顚, 狷, or 風."

**Departing tone (*fěng*).** *Guangyun*: fanqie 方鳳, sounding like 諷 (fěng). Preface to *Guan ju*: "poetry has six principles; the first is 風 — those above by it transform those below, those below by it remonstrate above; principal in form yet oblique in admonition, the speaker blameless and the hearer warned, hence 風"; *Jian*: "風化 (transforming) and 風刺 (admonishing) both mean to use metaphor, not direct speech."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 方 (fāng), fanqie 分房.* Chuci, *Xi shi*: "…on the right the lingering airs (風) of Great Xia… and beholds the roundness and squareness of Heaven and Earth."
- *Rhymes as 蕃 (fán), fanqie 甫煩.* Wang Can's poem: "fierce the winter sun, keen the chill wind (風); the lurking fish in the deep, the homing geese aloft."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 閭承.* Hou Han, Feng Yan's *Xianzhi fu*: "…to right the faint airs (風) of a declining age… setting forth Ji Zha at Yanling."
- *Rhymes as 分 (fēn), fanqie 孚金.* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "fine cloth and coarse, chill it is with the wind (風); I think of the ancients, who truly won my heart." *Shiming*: "in Yan-Yu-Bing-Ji speech, mouth wide and lips closed, read like 分; in Qing-Xu, mouth pursed and lips parted pushing breath, read like 方; 風, to release — the breath releases and scatters." Chen Di, *Maoshi guyin kao*: "風 anciently rhymed with 心, 林, 淫 — fanqie 孚金; today west of the Taihang, between Fen and Jin, they still read 風 as 分." *Zheng Yun*: "the first 東 rhyme includes 風 while the twelfth 侵 omits it, not realising 風 anciently had the 分 sound."

**On the graph (Shuowen).** "When wind moves, insects are born — hence insects transform in eight days. From 虫 (insect), 凡 as phonetic." Zhao Guze: "all things exposed to wind breed insects, so 風 follows 虫, with 凡 as the phonetic."

**Textual notes.** 西風謂之秦風 should read 泰風; 馬牛放佚 should read 馬牛風佚; 二十侵 should read 十二侵.
