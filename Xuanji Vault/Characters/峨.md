**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҜ…, middle; Mountain radical еұұ; p.311 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie дә”дҪ•. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie зүӣдҪ• вҖ” sounding like иҺӘ (Г©).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "еөҜеіЁ, a mountain high." Lu Ji, *Ballad of the Campaign*: "the towering mountains, dense and craggy-high (еөҜеіЁ)."

**Senses.**
- *еіЁзңү (Emei), a mountain-name*, a hundred li south of Mei county in Jiading prefecture, Sichuan вҖ” also written еі©еұұ; it has three peaks, called Great Emei, Middle Emei, and Lesser Emei. *Record of Famous Mountains*: "two mountains face each other like moth-eyebrows (иӣҫзңү), hence the name." Also Guihua and Taining in Min, Taiping in Yue, and Jia county in Yu all have a Moth-eyebrow Mountain (иӣҫзңүеұұ): the name the same but the place distinct.
- *еі©еі©, a lofty look.* Shijing, *Daya*: "bearing the jade sceptres, towering (еі©еі©)"; note: "those holding the gui and zhang sceptres in aid of the sacrifice, their bearing lofty (еі©еі©)." Also Lu Ji, *Reply to Jia Changyuan*: "fair fame, lofty and high (еі©еі©)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "interchangeable with дҝ„." Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "tilting (дҝ„) his canopied carriage and cap, his robes intermingled."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as е®ң (yГӯ), fanqie йӯҡзҫҲ.* Liu Xiang, *Jiu tan*: "cherishing fragrance and clasping orchid, I wear the river-angelica's perfume; gripping the pepper and the duruo, I cap myself with the drifting clouds, towering high (еі©еі©)."
- *Rhymes as дә” (wЗ”), fanqie йҳ®еҸӨ.* Han Yu, *Yuanhe shengde shi*: "the river-spirits vast and dim, the mountain-gods soaring high (е¶«еіЁ); sated with the fat and the fragrant offerings, they yield blessings and send down good fortune (еҳҸ)." (еҳҸ is sounded еҸӨ, gЗ”.) *Ji Yun*: sometimes written р ©ҷ.
