**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, upper; Gate radical 門; p.1332 #03)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𨳡, 𨳢.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 古閑. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居閑. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居顏 — sounding like 蕑 (jiān).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A chink, a gap (隙)." It follows 門 (gate) and 月 (moon) — an associative compound, and also pictographic. Xu Kai: "the gate is shut at night; shut, yet the moonlight is seen — that is, there is a chink, a gap (𨻶)." Liji, *Yue ji*: "the one moving, the one still — that is the interval (閒) between Heaven and Earth." Zhuangzi, *Shan mu*: "I, Zhou, would dwell between the timbered and the untimbered (材不材之閒)." Also Shiji, *Biography of Guo Jie*: "among the men of Luoyang there were two at feud; the worthies and stalwarts of the town who mediated (居閒) numbered in the tens, but they would not listen"; note: "to stand in the middle and reconcile them by other means." *Discourses of Zhou*: "our former king Buzhu thereby lost his office, and fled and hid himself among the Rong and Di (戎翟之閒)."

**Senses.**
- *To hold, admit (容).* Liji, *Wen wang shizi*: "in all cases when waiting in attendance on the Grand Director of Studies, near or far, leave an interval (閒) of three mats"; note: "閒 is like to hold, to admit." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wen*: "I beg to ask for a private moment (請閒)"; Yan Shigu's note: "閒 means to admit — like saying 'a middle space' now; he begged for the space of a leisure moment, having something to set forth."
- *Spare, simple (𥳑).* *Shiming*: "閒 means simple; the work of affairs being pared down and spared."
- *The creak of carriage-linchpins.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "creak-creak (閒關) goes the carriage-linchpin"; tradition: "閒關 is the sound made by the fitted linchpin."
- *黃閒 (huangjian), a crossbow-name.* Former Han, *Biography of Li Guang*: "he shot with the Great Yellow"; note: "the Yellow-shoulder crossbow." Jin Zhuo: "that is the huangjian; the Great Yellow is the largest of them."
- *A place-name.* Chunqiu, Zhao yr 22: "a grand hunt at Changjian (昌閒)."
- *Personal names.* Shiji, *Annals of Xiang Yu*: "Tian Jian (田閒), younger brother of Tian Jiao, a former general of Qi." Also *Biography of Yue Yi*: "he made Yue Yi's son Jian (閒) Lord of Changguo"; note: "閒, fanqie 紀閒."

**Further readings (xián).**
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 何閒. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 何艱 — sounding like 閑 (xián). *Ji Yun*: "at peace (安)." Shijing, *Zhou nan*, tradition on "the modest, retiring, virtuous maiden": "it means the queen-consort has the virtue of the *Guan ju*, being a good woman secluded and retiring (幽閒), chaste and devoted." Zhu Xi's tradition: "having the virtue of being secluded, retiring, chaste, and still." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 33: "Huang Wuzi said: 'let you, sir, take its elk and deer, to give my mean realm respite (閒) — how would that be?'" Zhouli, *Di guan, Lü shi*: "he managed the gathering of the wilds' hoe-tax grain, house-tax grain, and idle-tax grain (閒粟)"; note: "what is paid by idle people (閒民) without office or duty"; *Shiwen*: "sounded 閑 (xián)." Liji, *Wang zhi*: "the rest is used to pay officers, and made into fallow land (閒田)." Shiji, *Biography of the Lord of Xinling*: "Master Hou then dismissed the others and spoke privately (閒語)"; note: "閒 sounded 閑 — it means to speak quietly." Also *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "calm and easy, refined and leisurely (閒雅), most elegant"; note: Wei Zhao, "閒 is read 閑."
- *A chink, gap (𨻶).* Liji, *Qu li*: "after a little while (少閒), I should like to make a report"; note: "meaning he wishes to wait for a little leisure-space, having something to state."
- *Near (近).* *Guangyun*. Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 16: "by your lordship's grace, I am close (閒) upon donning armour and helmet"; note: "閒 is like near."

**Further readings (jiàn).**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 古莧. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 居莧. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居諫. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居晏 — sounding like 襉 (jiàn). Erya, *Shi gu*: "to take turns, to alternate (代)." Shu, *Li zheng*: "aiding me with the people received, harmonizing our many suits and many cautions — at such times, let none interpose (閒) upon it"; tradition: "閒 means to replace." Shijing, *Zhou song*: "the August One thereby alternates (閒) it." Yili, *Yan li*: "then they sang in alternation (閒歌) the *Yu li*, with the reed-organ following the *You geng*."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi yan*: "a spy (俔)"; note: "in the Zuozhuan called 諜; today's secret agent"; *Shiwen*: "閒 sounded 諫 (jiàn)."
- *Also* Boya: "to slander (䛼)"; *Shiwen*: "𧨚 is the 𧨚 of 𧨚謗 (to defame)."
- *To interpose, come between (厠).* *Guangyun*. Yijing, *Zhun* hexagram, note: "firm in purpose and shared in liking, admitting no other interposing (閒)"; *Correct Meaning*: "閒 means to come between; the fifth responds in the second — that is, firm in its purpose, bent on shared liking, admitting no other man to interpose (閒厠) between them." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 3: "Shi Que said: 'the distant come between (閒) the near, the new between the old'"; *Shiwen*: "閒, the 閒 of 閒厠 (to interpose)."
- *To alternate (迭).* Shu, *Yi ji*: "the reed-organ and the great bell in alternation (閒)"; tradition: "閒 means to alternate"; subcommentary: "to take turns and interpose, the sense of replacing one another — so 閒 is 'to alternate'; blowing the reed-organ and striking the bell, they sound by turns."
- *To space apart, separate (隔).* Former Han, *Biography of King Yuan of Chu*: "some slips dropped out, some bindings came apart (閒編)"; note: "閒, fanqie 古莧 — meaning the old binding rotted through, and on re-ordering it the front and back fell into confusion." *Biography of Wei Xuancheng*: "every other year (閒歲) the collective sacrifice"; note: "閒歲 means separated by one year."
- *To take part in (與).* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 10: "the army of Qi attacked us, and the duke was about to give battle; Cao Gui begged audience, and his fellow-villager said: 'the meat-eaters plan it — why take part (閒) in it?'"; note: "閒 is like to take part."
- *Empty, by stealth (空).* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Gao*: "on foot he followed a by-road (閒道) to the army"; note: "閒 means empty — to go by an empty gap, not openly."
- *To estrange, set apart (離).* *Discourses of Jin*: "moreover, to estrange (閒) a father's love yet to relish his gift"; note: "閒 means to estrange."
- *Far apart (遠).* Huainanzi, *Chu zhen*: "a stump in a ditch — then ugly and fair are far apart (有閒)"; note: "閒 means far."
- *A non-primary colour is called 閒.* Shijing, *Weifeng*, tradition on "green, ah, the jacket": "green is an intermediate colour (閒色)."
- *To recover from illness (瘳).* *Guangyun*. *Zhengzitong*: "sickness is constantly in the body, with no least respite; now the sickness having abated there is a respite (空𨻶), hence recovery from illness is called 閒."
- *To send off (送).*
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "to disparage (非訾) is also called 閒."
- *To work in a rift is called 閒.* Zuozhuan, Ding yr 4: "to sow discord (閒𢙄) in the royal house."
- *To outwit the enemy by stratagem is called 反閒 (the counter-spy).* Sunzi's *Art of War*: "the counter-spy (反閒) is to make use of the enemy's own spies."
- *A place-name.* Zhanguo ce: "to cede Hejian (河閒) to serve Qin"; note: "閒 sounded 諫; a Zhao territory."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 賈限, sounding like 𥳑 (jiǎn). A place-name, citing the Chunqiu, "a grand hunt at Changjian (昌閒)." A note: in Lu Deming's *Yinyi*, 閒 is read as written, with none reading it in the rising tone; it is unknown what the *Ji Yun* relied on.
- *Also* Boya: "閒 means to peer, spy (覗)"; *Shiwen*: "閒, fanqie 孤限; 覗 sounded 司 (sī)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 下瞎, sounding like 𤛉 (xiá). Erya, *Shi gu*: "to alternate (代)" — the reading of Shi Qian.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 巾 (jīn).* Gao Biao's poem: "men have their schemes and counsels, six surprises and five spy-craft (五閒); gathering up these three matters, in planning then inquire and consult."

**Textual notes.** Shu, *Li zheng*, "aiding me with the people I love (愛民)…" — checked against the original, "愛民" should read "受民" (the people received). · Huainanzi, "*Shu zhen*" chapter, note "閒 means far" — checked against the original work, "*Shu zhen* (淑眞)" should read "*Chu zhen* (俶眞)." · Zuozhuan, Ding yr 4, "閒惎 the royal house" — checked against the original, "閒惎" should read "閒𢙄."
