**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls й…ү, upper; Speech radical иЁҖ; p.1156 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie зҲІе‘Ҫ вҖ” sounding like жіі (yЗ’ng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to sing (жӯҢ)." *Yupian*: "to draw out the words (й•·иЁҖ)." *Zengyun*: "to chant, sing, intone, hum." Shu, *Yiji*: "striking the sounding-stone, beating the zither and lute, to chant (и© )"; tradition: "to blend with the sound of chant and song." Preface to the Erya: "setting forth the poets' stirrings and chantings (иҲҲи© )"; subcommentary: "и©  is to prolong the words." Former Han, *Treatise on Letters*: "to chant (и© ) its sound is called singing."
- *A bird's song.* Lu Ji, *Bei zai xing*: "the ear grieves at the season-birds' singing (и© )"; note: "the birds' voices change with the season."
- *Also written е’Ҹ.* Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "to sing and chant (и© ) its sound." Liji, *Yueji*: "they made song and chant (е’Ҹ)."
- *Contracted to ж°ё.* The Shuowen changjian. Shu, *Shundian*: "poetry voices the intent, song prolongs the words (ж°ёиЁҖ).
