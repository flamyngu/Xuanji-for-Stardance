## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈酉集上〉【言字部】頁1156第18【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤爲命切，音泳。【說文】歌也。【玉篇】長言也。【增韻】詠歌謳吟也。【書·益稷】戛擊鳴球，搏拊琴瑟以詠。【傳】以合詠歌之聲也。【爾雅序】敘詩人之興詠。【疏】詠者，永言也。【前漢·藝文志】詠其聲謂之歌。　又鳥鳴亦曰詠。【陸機·悲哉行】耳悲詠時禽。【註】禽聲應時而變也。　又通作咏。【史記·樂書】歌詠其聲也。【禮·樂記】作歌咏。　又【說文長箋】省作永。【書·舜典】詩言志，歌永言。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1156 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 爲命 — sounding like 泳 (yǒng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to sing (歌)." *Yupian*: "to draw out the words (長言)." *Zengyun*: "to chant, sing, intone, hum." Shu, *Yiji*: "striking the sounding-stone, beating the zither and lute, to chant (詠)"; tradition: "to blend with the sound of chant and song." Preface to the Erya: "setting forth the poets' stirrings and chantings (興詠)"; subcommentary: "詠 is to prolong the words." Former Han, *Treatise on Letters*: "to chant (詠) its sound is called singing."
- *A bird's song.* Lu Ji, *Bei zai xing*: "the ear grieves at the season-birds' singing (詠)"; note: "the birds' voices change with the season."
- *Also written 咏.* Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "to sing and chant (詠) its sound." Liji, *Yueji*: "they made song and chant (咏)."
- *Contracted to 永.* The Shuowen changjian. Shu, *Shundian*: "poetry voices the intent, song prolongs the words (永言).
