**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; See radical 見; p.1138 #14)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𥍊, 𥸑, 𥷯.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 古玩 — the departing tone of 官, i.e. **guàn**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to look closely, contemplate (諦視)." *Yun Hui*: "that which is viewed; to display." Yijing, *Guan*: "the great spectacle (觀) is above; yielding and compliant, centred and upright, it shows itself to the world"; Zhu's note: "觀 is to display centred uprightness to men, and be looked up to." Shu, *Yiji*: "I wish to display (觀) the emblems of the ancients"; tradition: "to display the dress-system of the model emblems." Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*: "the Li clan makes the measure; the fine measure once done, it is shown (觀) to the four states"; note: "to display it to the four quarters, that they may copy it." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Xuan*: "displayed (觀) to them treasures"; Yan Shigu: "觀 means to show."
- *容觀, mien and bearing.* Liji, *Yuzao*: "once robed, he practises his bearing (容觀) and jade-chime sounds, and then goes forth."
- *Many (多).* *Erya, Shigu.* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "long and abundant (觀) the reaping"; gloss: "奄 is 'long,' 觀 is 'many'"; one reading 官.
- *A tower-gate (闕).* Erya, *Shigong*: "觀 is called the *que*"; note: "the paired towers of a palace gate"; subcommentary: "beside the Zhi Gate is called 觀, also called 闕." Baihu tong: "above are hung the law-emblems, lofty and great, called the *xiangwei*; since people view (觀) them, it is called 觀." Sanfu huangtu: "Zhou set up two towers (兩觀) to mark the palace gate; ascending them one can view afar, hence 觀." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 5: "the duke, having held the new-moon audience, climbed the watchtower (觀臺) to gaze and record — this was rite"; note: "a house built on a terrace, from which one may view afar."
- *樓觀, a tower-lodge; a Daoist temple.* *Guangyun*, *Yun Hui.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "immortals love to dwell in towers; the Emperor had built at Chang'an the Feilian and Gui lodges (桂觀), at Ganquan the Yiyanshou lodge (觀)." The crown prince's palace had the Jia Lodge (甲觀): Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Cheng*: "Emperor Yuan, while in the crown prince's palace, was born in the painted hall of the Jia Lodge." The Dongguan (東觀) was the Han imperial library: Later Han, *Annals of Emperor An*: "he ordered the Erudites to collate at the Dongguan the texts of the Five Classics"; note: "the southern palace of Luoyang had the Dongguan, also called the Peng Lodge."
- *京觀, a triumph-mound (corpses heaped with earth).* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 12: "Pan Dang said, 'why not raise a war-monument and gather the Jin dead to make a triumph-mound (京觀)?'"
- *壯觀, 奇觀, a grand or wondrous sight.* *Yun Hui*: scenes splendid and affairs marvellous, worth the seeing.
- *Hot.* *Zihui bu.* Note to Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Siguan*: "in present Yan custom, hot water is called 觀."
- *A personal name and a state-name.* Zhushu jinian: "in the tenth year of Emperor Qi, he banished his youngest son Wu Guan (武觀) to Xihe"; note: "Wu Guan is the 'Five Guan' of the Chu yu; the state of Guan is the present Wey county in Dunqiu."
- *A county-name and a river-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Dong commandery had Panguan county, the Jiaodong kingdom had Guanyang county"; Ying Shao: "[Guanyang] is on the north (陽) of the Guan water."
- *A surname.* The Chu yu: "what Chu held precious was Guan Shefu."
- *Interchangeable with 館 (a lodge).* Wenxuan, Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "the divine warders feast in the quiet lodge (館)"; and "let palace and lodge stand empty, unfilled" — the Shiji and Hanshu both write 觀.
- *Interchangeable with 鸛 (the stork).* Zhuangzi, *Yuyan*: "like storks (觀) and sparrows, gnats and gadflies passing before one."

**Level tone (guān).** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 古丸; *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 沽歡 — sounding like 官 (guān). *Boya*: "to look." *Zhengzitong*: "to look afar or upward is 觀, to look near or downward is 臨."
- *To behold.* Yijing, *Guan*: "the first six: a child's looking (童觀)"; Zhuzi: "the hexagram takes 'displaying' as its sense, the lines take 'beholding' as theirs." Shu, *Pangeng*: "I see [you] as one watching (觀) a fire"; tradition: "I see your minds as one sees a fire" (Zheng Kangcheng reads it departing). Guliang zhuan, Yin yr 5: "the duke watched the fishing (觀) at Tang"; tradition: "an ordinary matter is 視, an extraordinary one 觀." Guanyinzi, *Erzhu*: "love is my essence, beholding (觀) my spirit; love is water, beholding is fire; love held and beholding upon it makes Wood; beholding kept and love drawing it in makes Metal."
- *To roam, sightsee.* Mengzi: "what must I cultivate to match the progresses (觀) of the former kings?"
- *To divine, observe omens.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Heavens*: "observe (觀) the completed Huang"; Jin Zhuo: "觀 is to divine; Huang is the chariot-house of the Five Emperors."
- *Like 'to make manifest.'* Former Han, *Biography of Yan An*: "to flaunt [it] (觀) and make the world covet"; Yan Shigu: "to show it forth, making them yearn and covet."
- *Yunhui xiaobu*: fanqie 區願, sounding like 勸 (quàn). Liji, *Ziyi*: "of old God on high *zhou-tian-guan* the virtue of King Wen"; note: "周田觀, in the archaic text is 割申勸"; Lu Deming: "觀, following the note, read as 勸."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 古黃, sounding like 光 (guāng).* Jizhong Zhoushu: "Shi Kuang sang the Wuyi: 'the state is truly at peace, and far men come to behold (觀); right and norm cultivated, in love of music yet no excess.'"
- *Rhymes, fanqie 規倫, sounding like 均 (jūn).* Former Han, Gao Biao's poem: "to bend the Way for accord leaves nothing to behold (觀); the lofty integrity of forebears far may be ever followed."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 居員, sounding like 涓 (juān).* Fu Yi, *Qiji*: "probing the deep, exhausting the kinds, in nothing not widely versed (博觀); bright, rich, and fine, the age honoured his words."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 扃縣, the departing tone of 涓.* Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "...rearing and towering, they form a lodge (觀); I lift my head to gaze high, but the eyes go dim and see nothing."
- *Rhymes (read as 多).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "bream and carp — and so, how many (觀)!"; gloss: "觀 means 'many'"; the Han Shi writes 覩.

**Textual note.** The alleged "叶掌與切, 音煮" reading and Zhu's note are spurious; 掌與切 is the sound of 者, not 觀.
