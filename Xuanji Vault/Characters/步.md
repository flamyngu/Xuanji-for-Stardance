## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【止字部】頁574第15【唐韻】【正韻】薄故切【集韻】【韻會】蒲故切，𠀤音捕。【說文】行也。【書·武成】王朝步自周。【傳】步，行也。《正義》曰：《爾雅·釋宮》云：堂上謂之行，堂下謂之步。彼相對爲名耳，散則可以通，故步爲行也。【楚辭·招䰟】步騎羅些。【註】乗馬爲騎，徒行爲步。　又【小爾雅】跬，一舉足也。倍跬謂之步。【白虎通】人踐三尺法天地人，再舉足步備隂陽也。【周禮·夏官】射人以貍步張三侯。【註】鄭司農云：貍步，謂一舉足爲步，於今爲半步。　又【司馬法】六尺爲步，步百爲畝。【禮·王制】古者以周尺八尺爲步，今以周尺六尺四寸爲步。【正義曰】古者八寸爲尺，周尺八尺爲步，則一步六尺四寸。【史記·秦始皇紀】數以六爲紀，六尺爲步。【註】索隱曰：《管子》《司馬法》皆云六尺爲步，非獨秦制。又王制八尺爲步，今以六尺四寸爲步，步之尺數亦不同。　又輦行曰步。【韻會】世稱輦車曰步輦，謂人荷而行，不駕馬也。　又徐行曰步。【屈原·離騷】步余馬於蘭臯兮。【說苑·建本篇】走者之速，步者之遲。　又促行曰趨，闊行曰步。【莊子·田子方】步亦步，趨亦趨。　又【任昉·述異記】水際謂之步。上虞縣有石駞步，吳中有瓜步，吳江中有魚步、龜步，湘中有靈妃步。按吳楚閒謂浦爲步，語之訛耳。【水經注】贛水逕豫章郡北爲津步，步卽水渚也。【靑箱雜記】嶺南謂村市爲墟，水津爲步。　又【柳宗元·鐵爐步志】江之滸，凡舟可縻而上下者曰步。【韓愈·孔戣墓誌】蕃舶至泊步，有下碇之稅。通作埠。今人呼船儈曰埠頭。埠音如步。　又【爾雅·釋樂】徒擊鼓謂之步。【疏】凡八音備作曰樂。一音獨作不得以樂名也。　又人才特出謂之獨步。【晉書·王坦之傳】江東獨步王文度。　又馬步，謂神爲災害馬者。一曰行神。【周禮·夏官·校人】冬祭馬步。　又人物烖害之神皆曰步。【周禮·夏官·校人疏】𤣥冥之步，人鬼之步是也。又【地官·族師·祭酺註】酺者，爲人物烖害之神。故書酺爲步，蓋步與酺字異而音義同也。　又習馬曰步馬。【左傳·襄二十六年】左師見夫人之步馬者。又牽行也。【禮·曲禮】步路馬必中道。　又行師曰步師。【左傳·僖三十三年】寡君聞吾子將步師出于敝邑。　又行爵曰步爵。【禮·少儀】未步爵，不嘗羞。　又推歷曰步歷。【左傳·文元年疏】日月轉運於天，猶如人之行步，故推歷謂之步歷。【後漢·楊厚傳】就同郡鄭伯山，受河洛書及天文推步之術。【陸機·演連珠】儀天步晷，而修短可量。　又《律歷》，書名，五星爲五步。見【漢制考】。　又運也，國運曰國步，天運曰天步。【詩·小雅】國步蔑資。【又】天步艱難。　又陟大位曰攺步。【周語】攺玉攺步。　又步驟。【後漢·曹褒傳】三五步驟，優劣殊軌。【註】《孝經鉤命決》曰：三皇步，五帝驟，三王馳。宋均註云：步謂德𨺚道用，日月爲步，時事彌順，日月亦驟，勤思不已，日月乃馳，是優劣也。　又姓。【廣韻】晉有步場，食采於步，後因氏焉。孔子弟子有步叔乗，三國吳丞相步騭。又三字姓。【後魏書】有步六孤氏，後改爲陸氏。又西方步鹿根氏，後改爲步氏。又【北齊書】有步大汗氏。　又百步，溪名。【廣輿記】在台州臨海縣，一呼惡溪。　又千步，香名。【任昉·述異記】南海山出千步香，佩之香聞千步。今海嵎有千步草，是其種也。《雜貢籍》曰：南郡貢千步香。　又步光，劒名，見【越絕書】。　又步搖，婦人首飾名。見【採蘭雜志】。【俗書正訛】从𣥂，反止也。从少，非。𣥂字仅在步字中使用，日人将步简化为歩，便利步字的使用。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Stop radical 止; p.574 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 薄故. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 蒲故 — sounding like 捕 (bù).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to walk (行)." Shu, *Wucheng*: "in the morning the king set out (步) from Zhou"; tradition: "步 means to go"; Correct Meaning: "the Erya, *Shigong* says, 'on the hall it is 行, below the hall it is 步' — named in contrast; loosely interchangeable, so 步 is 'to go.'" Chuci, *Zhaohun*: "foot and horse are ranged (步騎)"; note: "to ride is 騎, to go afoot is 步."
- *A pace.* *Xiao Erya*: "a 跬 (half-pace) is one lift of the foot; twice a 跬 is a 步 (pace)." Baihu tong: "a man treads three *chi*, modelling Heaven, Earth, and Man; two lifts of the foot complete the pace (步), modelling yin and yang." Zhouli, *Xiaguan*: "the Archer with the wildcat-pace (貍步) sets up the three targets"; note: Zheng [Zhong] says, "貍步 means one lift of the foot for a pace — in present terms, a half-pace."
- *A measure of length.* The Sima fa: "six *chi* make a pace (步), a hundred paces a *mu*." Liji, *Wangzhi*: "anciently eight Zhou-*chi* made a pace; now six *chi* four *cun*"; Correct Meaning: "anciently eight *cun* were a *chi*; eight Zhou-*chi* to a pace makes one pace six *chi* four *cun*." Shiji, *Annals of Qin Shihuang*: "they reckoned by sixes, six *chi* to a pace"; note: the Suoyin says, "the Guanzi and Sima fa both say six *chi* to a pace — not the Qin system alone."
- *To go by hand-carriage.* *Yun Hui*: "the hand-carriage is called the 步輦, borne by men and not drawn by horses."
- *To walk slowly.* Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "I pace (步) my horse by the orchid bank." Shuoyuan, *Jianben*: "the runner's swiftness, the walker's (步) slowness."
- *A long stride (opposed to 趨, a quick gait).* Zhuangzi, *Tian Zifang*: "when [you] walk I walk (步), when [you] hasten I hasten."
- *A waterside, a landing.* Ren Fang, *Shuyi ji*: "a waterside is called a 步; Shangyu county has Shituo-bu, Wuzhong has Guabu, in the Wu River are Yubu and Guibu, in Xiang is Lingfei-bu; in Wu and Chu, 浦 (creek-mouth) is called 步 — a slip of the tongue." Shuijing zhu: "the Gan water passing north of Yuzhang is a ferry-landing (津步); 步 is just a sandbar." Qingxiang zaji: "in Lingnan a village market is a 墟, a water-ferry a 步." Liu Zongyuan, *Tielu-bu zhi*: "on the river's bank, wherever boats may be moored to go up and down is called a 步." Han Yu, *Tomb inscription for Kong Kui*: "foreign ships coming to anchor at the landing (步) paid an anchorage tax" — also written 埠; a ship-broker is now called a 埠頭 (wharf-head), 埠 sounding like 步.
- *To beat the drum alone.* Erya, *Shiyue*: "to beat the drum alone is called 步"; subcommentary: "when all eight timbres play together it is 'music'; one timbre alone may not be named 'music.'"
- *獨步, peerless ("walking alone").* Jinshu, *Biography of Wang Tanzhi*: "peerless (獨步) in Jiangdong was Wang Wendu."
- *馬步, a horse-bane spirit (or road-god).* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Jiaoren*: "in winter they sacrifice to the Horse-bane (馬步)." Any god of harm to men and beasts is called 步: subcommentary, "the *bu* of Xuanming, the *bu* of the human dead, are such"; the note on the *pu* sacrifice (Diguan, Zushi): "the *pu* is the god of harm to men and beasts; the old text writes 酺 as 步 — they differ in graph but agree in sound and sense."
- *步馬, to exercise or lead a horse.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 26: "the Marshal of the Left saw the lady's horse-walker (步馬者)." Liji, *Quli*: "in walking (步) the ruler's road-horse, keep to the middle of the way."
- *步師, to march troops.* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 33: "my lord has heard that you will march your army (步師) out past our poor town."
- *步爵, to pass round the wine-cups.* Liji, *Shaoyi*: "before the cups have gone round (步爵), one does not taste the dishes."
- *步歷, to compute the calendar.* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 1 subcommentary: "sun and moon turn through the heavens as a man walks, so calendar-reckoning is called 步歷." Later Han, *Biography of Yang Hou*: "...he received the Heluo writings and the art of astronomical reckoning (推步)." Lu Ji, *Yan lianzhu*: "modelling the heavens and pacing the gnomon (步晷), the long and short can be measured." In the *Lüli* (a book-title), the five planets are the "five paces (五步)"; see the Hanzhi kao.
- *Fortune, course.* A state's course is 國步, Heaven's course 天步. Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the state's course (國步) is without resource"; and: "Heaven's course (天步) is hard and toilsome."
- *改步, to ascend the great seat.* The Zhouyu: "change the jade [insignia], change the gait (改步)."
- *步驟, pace and gallop.* Later Han, *Biography of Cao Bao*: "the Three and the Five at walk and gallop (步驟), better and worse on different tracks"; note: the Xiaojing goumingjue says, "the Three August Ones walked, the Five Emperors trotted, the Three Kings galloped"; Song Jun's note: "步 means virtue ample and the Way in use — the sun and moon as it were walking; as affairs grow more pressing, sun and moon trot; toiling without rest, they gallop — such is the gradation of better and worse."
- *A surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Jin had Bu Yang, fiefed at Bu, whose line took it as surname." Confucius's disciple Bushu Cheng; the Wu chancellor Bu Zhi. Three-syllable surnames: the Buliugu clan (later Lu), the western Bulugen clan (later Bu), the Budahan clan.
- *Baibu (百步), a stream-name.* Guangyu ji: "in Linhai county of Taizhou, also called the Foul Stream."
- *Qianbu (千步), an incense-name.* Ren Fang, *Shuyi ji*: "the South-Sea hills yield 'thousand-pace' incense (千步香); worn, its scent carries a thousand paces."
- *步光, a sword-name.* See the Yuejue shu.
- *步搖, a woman's hair-ornament.* See the Cailan zazhi.

**On the graph.** Sushu zhenge: "it follows 𣥂, an inverted 止; to write it from 少 is wrong.
