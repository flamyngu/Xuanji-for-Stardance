**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Grain radical 禾; p.853 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 弋支. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 余支. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 延知 — sounding like 匜 (yí).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Grain leaning and shifting upon one another (禾相倚移)." *Liushu gu*: "to transplant seedlings (移秧); in planting rice, after the seedling, one moves (移) it." Also *Shuowen*: "one account: a grain-name."

**Senses.**
- *To remove, shift (遷).* *Yun Hui*: "today's 遷徙 (to move) borrows 迻, written 移." Shu, *Duo shi*: "I move (移) you to the far distance."
- *To change; to draw out (易, 延).* *Guangyun*. Boya: "to turn (轉)." Shu, *Bi ming*: "the age changes and the customs shift (風移)." Liji, *Da zhuan*: "for a kin-line cut off, the mourning is not extended (移服)"; subcommentary: "what is at the side and reaches over is 移 — meaning it does not draw out to reach it." Also *Yu zao*: "in hurrying fast one would start forward, but hands and feet do not sway (移)"; subcommentary: "移 means to sway and stir."
- *To let slip, lose (遺).* *Guangyun*. Boya: "移 means to slip and lose (脫遺)."
- *Where the bureaus and ducal offices are not under one another's authority, there is the 移-document — of the note, memorial, and like kinds.* Former Han, *Biography of Gongsun Hong*: "Hong then sent in a sick-leave notice (移病) and was dismissed home"; note: "a document declaring illness." Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "thereupon he had his staff draw up a circular (文移)"; note: "a document circulated to the dependent counties."
- *Surname.* *Fengsu tong*: "Yi Liang of the Han, Grand Administrator of Hongnong."
- *A mountain-name.* Shuijing zhu: "the Yuan water rises from Mount Xu and flows into the Xiong stream; the Xiong stream is bordered south by Mount Yi (移山); the mountain was north of the water, but one evening, in wind and rain, by morning it had moved (移) south of the water — hence the name."
- *Interchangeable with 施.* Former Han, *Biography of Wei Wan*: "the sword is a thing men exchange (施易) — has it lasted to now alone?"; Ru Chun's note: "施 read as 移; it means the sword is what men prize, hence often exchanged (移易) and traded."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 以豉, sounding like 異 (yì). Liji, *Jiao te sheng*: "to make the people envious (移民)"; note: "移 speaks of envy (羨)"; subcommentary: "at the za-sacrifice all is rich and abundant, all drunk and sated with food and drink, making men long for it." Also Cao Zhi, *Yaoque fu*: "the sparrow, hearing the hawk's words, was greatly unmoved (不移); its eyes like split peppercorns, it leapt and beat its two wings"; note: "移 means to remove."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 敞𠇍, sounding like 侈 (chǐ). Liji, *Biao ji*: "with robes to make them ample (移之)"; note: "移 read like the 移 of 水汜移; it is like 'broad and great.'"

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 俄 (é), fanqie 牛何.* Chuci, *Qi jian*: "clear and cold it is cut off and destroyed, turbid and murky it grows day by day; the owls and night-jars have made their flocks, and the dark cranes furl their wings and screen off, withdrawing (屏移)."
