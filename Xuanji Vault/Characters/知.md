**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; Arrow radical 矢; p.824 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢇻, 𥎿.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 陟离. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 珍離. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 珍而 — the level tone of 智 (zhī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "an utterance (詞)"; it follows 口 (mouth) and 矢 (arrow). Xu [Kai]: "the swiftness of knowing a principle is like the speed of an arrow."
- *To know, recognize, be aware.* *Yupian*: "to recognize (識); to be aware (覺)." *Zengyun*: "to understand (喩)." Yijing, *Xici*: "the common people use it daily and do not know (知) it." Shu, *Gaoyao mo*: "to know (知) men is wisdom, for then one can give them office."
- *The "knowing-and-seeing" law (見知法).* Shiji, *Biographies of the Harsh Officials*: "Zhao Yu and Zhang Tang deliberated and fixed the various statutes, making the 'seen-and-known' law"; note: "an official who saw and knew [a crime] yet did not impeach it was held guilty of deliberate connivance."
- *To be acquainted (相交).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 4: "Gongsun Ming became acquainted (知) with Shusun in Qi"; note: "to be intimate and know one another." Also Zhao yr 28: "Master Wei said, 'Long ago when Shuxiang went to Zheng, Zong Mie spoke one good word, and Shuxiang took his hand as though an old friend (故知).'" Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "no joy is greater than a new acquaintance (相知)."
- *A mate (匹).* *Erya, Shigu*: "a mate." Shijing, *Kuaifeng*: "I delight in your having no mate (知)"; note: "匹, a mate."
- *Desire (欲).* *Guangyun.* Liji, *Yueji*: "when likes and dislikes have no measure within, and desire (知) lures from without."
- *Like remembering (記憶).* Lunyu: "one's parents' age one cannot but keep in mind (知)."
- *Like to take charge of (主).* Yijing, *Xici*: "Qian governs (知) the great beginning." Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 26: "Gongsun Hui said, 'Zichan is about to take charge of (知) the government.'" Wei Liaoweng, *Dushu zachao*: "the 知 in the official titles of later ages begins here."
- *To recover from illness (愈).* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "in southern Chu, of one recovered from sickness some say 知." Huangdi Suwen: "at the second needling there is recovery (知)"; note: "the ancients took easy urine and a settled belly to be 'recovery.'"
- *A drug-name.* The Rihua zhi: "the *yuzhizi* (預知子) — sewn onto the collar, when poison (蠱) is near, one hears it give a sound."
- *A place-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 27: "the duke's foot-soldiers were defeated at Qiezhi (且知)."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 知義. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 知意 — same as 智 (zhì, "wisdom"). Yijing, *Lin* hexagram: "wise (知) overlooking — the fitness of the great ruler." Xunzi, *Xiushen*: "to affirm the right and deny the wrong is called wisdom (知)."
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan: "Jin had Zhi Ji (知季), that is Xun Shou; he held the fief of Zhi (知) town apart, and so his line became the Zhi clan."
- *Posthumous-title rule:* "to appoint men matching their substance is called 知."
