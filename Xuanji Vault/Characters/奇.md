> 《康熙字典》〈丑集下〉【大字部】頁250第31【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】渠羈切【正韻】渠宜切，𠀤音琦。異也。【莊子·北遊篇】萬物一也。臭腐化爲神奇，神奇復化爲臭腐。【仙經】人有三奇，精，氣，神也。　又祕也。【史記·𨻰平傳】平凡六出奇計，其奇祕世莫得聞。　又姓。　又天神名。【淮南子·地形訓】窮奇廣莫，風之所生也。又四凶之一。【史記·五帝紀】少皡氏有不才子，天下謂之窮奇。【註】窮奇，卽共工氏。　又獸名。【司馬相如·上林賦】窮奇𧰼犀。【註】狀如牛，蝟毛，音如嘷狗，食人。　又江神謂之奇相。【江記】帝女也。卒爲江神。　又與琦通。　又【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤居宜切，音羈。一者，奇也。陽奇而隂偶。【易·繫辭】陽卦奇，亦零數也。【又】歸奇于扐以𧰼閏。　又隻也。【禮·投壷】一算爲奇。　又餘夫也。【韓非子·十過篇】遺有奇人者，使治城郭。　又數奇，不偶也。【史記·李廣傳】大將軍隂受上誡，以爲李廣老數奇，毋令獨當單于。　又奇𢷎，一拜也。【周禮·春官】大祝辨九𢷎，七曰奇𢷎。　又奇車，奇邪不正之車。【禮·曲禮】國君不乗奇車。　又奇衺，詭異也。【周禮·地官】比長有辠，奇衺則相及。　又【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤隱綺切。與倚通。依倚也。【前漢·鄒陽傳】輪囷離奇。　又【字彙補】倚蠏切。同矮，短人也。【後漢·五行志】童謠，見一奇人，言欲上天。　又叶古禾切，音戈。【宋玉·招魂】娭光眇視，目曾波些。被文服纖，麗而不奇些。【說文】从大从可。別作①。俗作竒，非。①字从大从司。    考證：〔【司馬相如·上林賦】窮奇犀𧰼。【註】狀如牛，蝟毛，音如皡狗，食人。〕　謹照原文犀𧰼改𧰼犀。皡改嘷。〔【周禮·地官】奇衺則相及，徙于國中及郊。〕　謹照原文徙於國中及郊則從而授之二句相連，不便單引一句，謹改比長有辠，奇衺則相及。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Great radical 大; p.250 #31)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 渠羈. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 渠宜 — sounding like 琦 (qí). Strange (異).

**Etymology and senses.** Zhuangzi, *Zhi bei you*: "the myriad things are one; the foul and rotten turn into the divine and marvellous (神奇), and the divine and marvellous turn back into the foul and rotten." *Xianjing*: "man has three wonders (三奇): essence, breath, and spirit."

**Senses.**
- *Secret, hidden (祕).* Shiji, *Biography of Chen Ping*: "Ping in all brought forth six marvellous stratagems (奇計), and their marvellous secrets (奇祕) the world could not get to hear."
- *Surname.*
- *A heaven-god's name.* Huainanzi, *Di xing*: "Qiongqi (窮奇) and Guangmo are where the winds are born." Also one of the Four Fiends. Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "the Shaohao clan had a worthless son, whom the world called Qiongqi (窮奇)"; note: "Qiongqi is the Gonggong clan."
- *A beast-name.* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "the qiongqi (窮奇) and the elephant-rhino"; note: "shaped like an ox, with hedgehog-bristles, its voice like a howling dog; it eats men."
- *The river-god is called Qixiang (奇相).* *Jiang ji*: "the Emperor's daughter, who at death became the river-god."
- *Interchangeable with 琦.*

**Further readings (jī).**
- *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居宜, sounding like 羈 (jī). The odd (奇): yang is odd, yin is even. Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the yang trigram is odd (奇)" — also a remainder-number. Also: "the remainder (奇) is returned to the fingers, to image the intercalary month."
- *A single one (隻).* Liji, *Tou hu*: "a single counter is an odd one (奇)."
- *A spare man (餘夫).* Hanfeizi, *Shi guo*: "the spare men (奇人) left over were set to mend the walls."
- *數奇, the unlucky, the unpaired.* Shiji, *Biography of Li Guang*: "the Grand General had secretly received the Emperor's warning that Li Guang was old and his fortune unlucky (數奇), and should not be left to face the Chanyu alone."
- *奇拜, a single obeisance.* Zhouli, *Chun guan*: "the Grand Invoker distinguished the nine obeisances; the seventh is the single obeisance (奇拜)."
- *奇車, an odd, irregular carriage.* Liji, *Qu li*: "the lord of a state does not ride an odd carriage (奇車)."
- *奇衺, freakish and wrong.* Zhouli, *Di guan*: "if a precinct-head commits a crime, the freakish and wrong (奇衺) implicate one another."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 隱綺 — interchangeable with 倚, "to lean on (依倚)." Former Han, *Biography of Zou Yang*: "knotted and curling, gnarled and odd (離奇)."
- *Also* *Zihui Bu*: fanqie 倚蠏 — same as 矮, a short man. Later Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: a children's rhyme: "I saw an odd man (奇人), who said he wished to go up to Heaven."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 戈 (gē), fanqie 古禾.* Song Yu, *Zhao hun*: "with playful glance and narrowed eye, the eyes like layered waves; clad in figured silk and fine gauze, fair but not freakish (奇)." *Shuowen*: "it follows 大 and 可." Otherwise written with 大 over 司; the common form 竒 is wrong.

**Textual notes.** Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*, "the qiongqi, the rhino-elephant (犀𧰼)... its voice like a howling (皡) dog" — checked against the original, "犀𧰼" should read "𧰼犀," and "皡" should read "嘷." · Zhouli, *Di guan*, "the freakish and wrong implicate one another, and are removed within the state and to the suburbs" — a note: the original's two clauses run together and cannot be cited singly; it is corrected to "if a precinct-head commits a crime, the freakish and wrong implicate one another."
