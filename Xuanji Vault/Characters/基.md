## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集中〉【土字部】頁231第12    〔古文〕：𡉝【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤居之切，音朞。【揚子·方言】基，據也。在下，物所依據也。【詩·大雅】止基廼理。【潘岳·藉田賦】結崇基之靈趾。　又田器。【孟子】雖有鎡基。　又門塾之址。【詩·周頌】自堂徂基。　又樂名。【孝經緯】伏犧之樂曰立基。　又山名。【山海經】亶爰山東三百里，曰基山。　又【集韻】渠之切。與朞通。【咸陽靈臺𥓓】承祠基年，𩷙魚複生。又叶古詣切，音計。【劉向·列女頌】以爲世基。叶下避字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.231 #12)*

**Ancient graph.** 𡉝.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 居之, sounding like 朞 (jī).

**Etymology and senses.** Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "基 means a footing (據); being below, it is what a thing rests on." Shijing, *Daya*: "they fixed the footing (基) and so set it in order." Pan Yue, *Jitian fu*: "he knit the sacred footprints of the lofty base (崇基)."

**Senses.**
- *A field-tool.* Mengzi: "though one have a hoe (鎡基)."
- *The site of a gate-school (門塾).* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "from the hall to the threshold-base (基)."
- *A music-name.* The *Xiaojing wei*: "the music of Fuxi was called Liji (立基)."
- *A mountain-name.* Shanhai jing: "three hundred li east of Mount Tanyuan is Mount Ji (基山)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 渠之. Interchangeable with 朞. The *Xianyang Lingtai stele*: "in the base-year (基年) of tending the shrine, the carp came to life again."
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie 古詣, sounding like 計 (jì). Liu Xiang, *Lienü song*: "and made it the age's foundation (世基)" — rhyming with the graph 避 below.
