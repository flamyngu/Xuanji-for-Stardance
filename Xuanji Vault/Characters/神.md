**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Spirit radical 示; p.842 #03)*

**Ancient graph.** 𥛠.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 食鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 乗人 — sounding like 晨 (chén).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a heavenly spirit, that which draws forth the myriad things." Xu [Kai]: "申 is to draw out; Heaven's office is to send down *qi* and stir the myriad things — hence 'draws forth the myriad things.'"
- *The seat of spirit.* *Huangji jingshi*: "the spirit of Heaven roosts in the sun; the spirit of man roosts in the eye."
- *Divine illumination (神明).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "now sage, now spirit-like (神)"; Kong's tradition: "the sage is unobstructed in all; the spirit-like is wondrous beyond bounds." Yijing, *Xici*: "the unfathomable in yin and yang is called the spirit (神)"; Wang Bi says, "the spirit is the utmost of transformation, named for the wonder it works in all things, not to be pinned to form." Mengzi: "sage, and beyond knowing — this is called the spirit (神)."
- *Ghosts and spirits (鬼神).* The yang *hun*-soul is the spirit (神), the yin *po*-soul the ghost (鬼); the *qi* that stretches forth is spirit, that which bends back is ghost.
- *A posthumous title.* Shiji: "one the people are unable to name [for his merit] is titled 神 (the Spirit-like)."
- *A surname.* The Han had Shen Yao, Commandant of Cavalry.
- *Fanqie 升人, sounding like 伸 (shēn).* Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "Shenshu (神荼) stands beside it"; note: "the name of a spirit in the sea; 神 sounds 伸, 荼 sounds 舒."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 時連, sounding like 禪 (chán).* The Jiao Zhongqing poem: "...so I have made an ill resolve — blame no more the ghosts and spirits (神)." Ban Gu, *Reply to a Guest's Jest*: "...his counsel matched the sage-spirits (聖神); Fu Yue of Yin was found through a dream at Fuyan, Lü Wang of Zhou through an omen stirring on the banks of the Wei (濱)"; note: "神 rhymes fanqie 時連, 濱 rhymes fanqie 𤰞眠."

**On the graph.** A note: Gu Yanwu's *Jinshi wenzi ji* says that 神 in old steles is often written 𥛠, with 旦 below; the Liji, *Jiao te sheng*, has "the meaning of communing with the bright dawn (旦明)," and Zheng Kangcheng says "旦 should be 神, a corruption of the seal graph"; the Zhuangzi's "a dawn-dwelling (旦宅) but no [death] of feeling" is likewise read 神 — the old copyists having dropped the upper half, it was corrupted into 旦. This view is quite right: it is not that 旦 may be written for 神.
