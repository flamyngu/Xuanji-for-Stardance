**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Wine radical 酉; p.1280 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 滂佩. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 滂沛 — sounding like 嶏 (pèi).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The colour of wine (酒色)." *Yupian*: "a match (匹); to mate (媲); a counterpart (對); to befit (當); to join (合)." Yijing, *Xi ci*: "broad and great, it matches (配) Heaven and Earth; in change and penetration it matches (配) the four seasons." Shu, *Jun ya*: "answering and lifting up the bright mandate of Wen and Wu, to match (配) the forebears."

**Senses.**
- *Of husband and wife, a mate (配偶).* Yijing, *Meng* hexagram, tradition on "taking a wife is auspicious": "the wife is she who, matched (配) to oneself, completes one's virtue." Shijing, *Daya*: "Heaven set up his mate (配), and the mandate received was now firm."
- *To accompany in offering (侑).* *Zengyun*. Yijing, *Yu* hexagram: "the former kings thereby made music and exalted virtue, offering it in abundance to the High God, to match (配) ancestors and forebears." Liji, *Za ji*: "a man is enshrined with his grandfather, and then matched (配)."
- *Also* Liji, *Li qi*: "when the men of Qi were about to sacrifice at Mount Tai, they first sacrificed at Peilin (配林)"; note: "Peilin is a forest-name."
- *Banishment and penal servitude (流刑隸).* Wang Pu, *Wudai huiyao*: "in the third year of Tianfu of the Jin, Han Yansi, an attendant of the Left Ward, was sentenced to two and a half years' servitude, branded on the face, and banished (配) to Huazhou for transport-labour." Some say: though the Tang had the text of banishment to south of the passes, yet banishment with face-branding must have begun here.
- *Also* *Wuyin jiyun*: fanqie 補妹, sounding like 朏 (fěi). "A match (匹); to join (合)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 坯 (pī), fanqie 鋪枚.* Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "and then he honoured the High God in the Hall of Light, and raised up Guangwu as his consort (配); discerning the quarters and setting the rule straight, the Five Essences led on and came pressing."
