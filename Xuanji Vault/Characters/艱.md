**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, lower; Stopping radical иү®; p.1013 #29)*

**Ancient graphs.** рЎҶ’, еӣҸ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie еҸӨй–‘. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie еұ…й–‘. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еұ…йЎҸ вҖ” sounding like й–’ (jiДҒn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "land hard to till." Erya, *Shigu*: "йҳ» and иүұ mean hard, difficult (йӣЈ)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "if the ruler can feel the hardship (иүұ) of being a ruler, and the minister the hardship of being a minister..." Also *Zhou guan*: "only by being resolute and decisive will there be no later trouble (еҫҢиүұ)."
- *Also* *Shiming*: "иүұ means a root (ж №), like the root of a thing."
- *Also, perilous, treacherous (йҡӘ).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "what man is that? вҖ” his heart is full of guile (еӯ”иүұ)"; Zhu's note: "иүұ means treacherous."
- *Also* Wang Jian, *stele-text for Chu Yuan*: "he left office to observe mourning for his mother (жҜҚиүұ); though the matter sprang from a sense of duty, his feeling was as that of his own kin."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie еұ…зңһ, sounding like е·ҫ (jД«n). Cui Yin, *Dali zhen*: "of old there was Zhongni, who pitied us men; Zihan honored the proper use of punishments, and the men of Wei were freed from distress (иүұ)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie з¶“е…Ҳ, sounding like е … (jiДҒn). Ban Biao, *Beizheng fu*: "I sigh for the Western Earl at Youli, grieving that the 'Darkening of the Light' met with hardship (иүұ); he unfolded the changes of the nine and the six, long pent in the strait, through the passing years."
