**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, middle; Mound radical йҳң; p.1357 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie еҸӨи«§. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еұ…и«§ вҖ” sounding like зҡҶ (jiД“).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the palace steps (йҷӣ)." *Yupian*: "the way of ascending to the hall; a flight of steps (зҙҡ)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "they danced with shields and plumes between the two stairways (е…©йҡҺ)."
- *Also* *Shiming*: "йҡҺ is a ladder (жўҜ), like a ladder having its graded ranks." Liji, *Sang da ji*: "for the soul-recall rite, if there are forests and foothills the forester (иҷһдәә) sets up the steps; if not, the Di-man sets them up"; note: "the йҡҺ is what one mounts to climb to the roof вҖ” a ladder."
- *Also* Yijing, *Xici*: "speech makes the stairway (йҡҺ) to trouble." Shijing, *Daya*: "who begot this stairway to calamity (еҺІйҡҺ)?"
- *Also* Liji, *Shaoyi*: "one may not йҡҺ the host"; note: "йҡҺ means one who advances upward"; subcommentary: "йҡҺ is a grade of rank; a man mounting the steps must go upward, hence йҡҺ is taken as 'advancing upward.'"
- *Also a star-name.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "I beg to set forth the Six Tokens of the Great Steps (жі°йҡҺе…ӯз¬Ұ)"; note: "the Great Steps (жі°йҡҺ) are the Three Terraces (дёүеҸ°)."
- *Also official rank (е®ҳйҡҺ).* Nan shi, *Biography of Zhang Rong*: "Rong takes no notice of rank-grades (йҡҺзҙҡ), and rank-grades may as well take no notice of Rong." Tang shu, *Treatise on Officials*: "the civil grades numbered twenty-eight, the military grades twenty-one."
- *Also зғҸйҡҺ, a plant-name.* Note to Erya, *Shicao*: "the jue (ж¬”) is also called wujie (зғҸйҡҺ)."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* *Yun Hui*: "a separate tribe of the Western Rong, the land of the White-Horse Di; the Wei called it Wuzhou, and the Tang changed it to Jiezhou (йҡҺе·һ)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie еұ…еӨ·, sounding like еҹә (jД«). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "what man is that, dwelling on the river's marge? Without strength, without courage, he is but the stairway to disorder (дәӮйҡҺ)." Also *Daya*: "ah, that clever woman вҖ” she is an owl, a screech-owl. A woman with a long tongue is the stairway to ruin (еҺІд№ӢйҡҺ)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie е …еҘҡ, sounding like зЁҪ (jД«). Ban Gu, *Xidu fu*: "though nimble and swift, agile and bold, yet he stares aghast and cannot climb (йҡҺ); scaling the well-railing, not half-way up, his eyes swim and his mind grows dazed."
