**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Mound radical 阜; p.1357 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 古諧. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居諧 — sounding like 皆 (jiē).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the palace steps (陛)." *Yupian*: "the way of ascending to the hall; a flight of steps (級)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "they danced with shields and plumes between the two stairways (兩階)."
- *Also* *Shiming*: "階 is a ladder (梯), like a ladder having its graded ranks." Liji, *Sang da ji*: "for the soul-recall rite, if there are forests and foothills the forester (虞人) sets up the steps; if not, the Di-man sets them up"; note: "the 階 is what one mounts to climb to the roof — a ladder."
- *Also* Yijing, *Xici*: "speech makes the stairway (階) to trouble." Shijing, *Daya*: "who begot this stairway to calamity (厲階)?"
- *Also* Liji, *Shaoyi*: "one may not 階 the host"; note: "階 means one who advances upward"; subcommentary: "階 is a grade of rank; a man mounting the steps must go upward, hence 階 is taken as 'advancing upward.'"
- *Also a star-name.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "I beg to set forth the Six Tokens of the Great Steps (泰階六符)"; note: "the Great Steps (泰階) are the Three Terraces (三台)."
- *Also official rank (官階).* Nan shi, *Biography of Zhang Rong*: "Rong takes no notice of rank-grades (階級), and rank-grades may as well take no notice of Rong." Tang shu, *Treatise on Officials*: "the civil grades numbered twenty-eight, the military grades twenty-one."
- *Also 烏階, a plant-name.* Note to Erya, *Shicao*: "the jue (欔) is also called wujie (烏階)."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* *Yun Hui*: "a separate tribe of the Western Rong, the land of the White-Horse Di; the Wei called it Wuzhou, and the Tang changed it to Jiezhou (階州)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 居夷, sounding like 基 (jī). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "what man is that, dwelling on the river's marge? Without strength, without courage, he is but the stairway to disorder (亂階)." Also *Daya*: "ah, that clever woman — she is an owl, a screech-owl. A woman with a long tongue is the stairway to ruin (厲之階)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 堅奚, sounding like 稽 (jī). Ban Gu, *Xidu fu*: "though nimble and swift, agile and bold, yet he stares aghast and cannot climb (階); scaling the well-railing, not half-way up, his eyes swim and his mind grows dazed."
