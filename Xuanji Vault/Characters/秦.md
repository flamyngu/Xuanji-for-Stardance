**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҲ, lower; Grain radical зҰҫ; p.851 #30)*

**Ancient graph.** рЈңҲ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*: fanqie еҢ йҡЈ. *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie ж…ҲйҡЈ вҖ” sounding like иһ“ (qГӯn), i.e. **qГӯn**.

**Senses.**
- *A state.* Shuowen: "the state enfeoffed to the descendants of Boyi; the land suited to grain." Shijing, *Qin feng, "Che lin"* commentary: "Qin, a valley name in Longxi, northeast of Mt. Niaoshu in Yongzhou"; subcommentary: "today's Qin-ting, the Qin valley." *Yun Hui*: "the Qin state of the Spring-and-Autumn; the Han set up Tianshui commandery; the Northern Wei renamed it Qinzhou." *Shiming*: "Qin means 'moist' (жҙҘ): its land is fertile and well-watered."
- *The Three Qin.* Shiji, *Annals of Xiang Yu*: he split the Pass-lands in three and made kings of Qin's surrendered generals вҖ” Zhang Han, King of Yong; Sima Xin, the Sai King; Dong Yi, the Di King вҖ” the Three Qin.
- *Great Qin (the Roman East).* Hou Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "Great Qin lies west of the sea, also called the Sea-West Kingdom; its people are tall, upright, and well-formed, resembling the Chinese, hence the name 'Great Qin.'"
- *Surname.* The Qin descend from Zhuanxu; after Ziying was destroyed, collateral lines took Qin as their surname.
- *A kind of grain.* Shuowen, alternate sense: "Qin is also the name of a grain."
- *Rhymes as зүҶ (qiГЎng), fanqie ж…ҲиүҜ.* Yilin: "jade discs, *cong* and *zhang*; bearing tribute, this is the king. Ningyue of the hundred *li* answered the summons to Qi and Qin.
