## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集上〉【口字部】頁172第07【唐韻】直少切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】直笑切，𠀤潮去聲。【說文】評也。【王逸曰】以手曰招，以言曰召。【書·甘誓】大戰于甘，乃召六卿。【詩·齊風】自公召之。【禮·曲禮】父召，無諾唯而起。　又【廣韻】【正韻】實照切【集韻】【韻會】時照切，𠀤同邵。【廣韻】邑名。【詩序】甘棠，美召伯也。【箋】召伯，姬姓，名奭，食邑于召。　又姓。【廣韻】召公之後。【前漢·循吏傳】召信臣，九江壽春人。【氏族博考】春秋召與邵一氏，後分爲二，汝南安陽之族皆从邵。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.172 #07)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 直少. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 直笑 — the departing tone of 潮 (zhào).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to call, to summon (評)." Wang Yi says: "to beckon with the hand is 招; to summon with words is 召." Shu, *Gan shi*: "about to give great battle at Gan, he summoned (召) the six ministers." Shijing, *Qifeng*: "from the duke comes the summons (召)." Liji, *Quli*: "when a father calls (召), one does not answer with a drawled 'yes' (諾) but with a prompt 'yes' (唯) and rises."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 實照; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 時照 — the same as 邵 (shào). *Guangyun*: a town-name. Preface to the *Shijing*: "the *Gantang* praises the Earl of Shao (召伯)"; Jian: "the Earl of Shao was of the Ji clan, named Shi (奭), with a fief-town at Shao (召)." Also a surname; *Guangyun*: "descendants of the Duke of Shao." Former Han, *Biographies of Reasonable Officials*: "Shao Xinchen, a man of Shouchun in Jiujiang." Shizu bokao: "in the Spring and Autumn period 召 and 邵 were one clan, later split into two; the families of Runan and Anyang all follow the form 邵."
