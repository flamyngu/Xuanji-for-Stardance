**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Corpse radical 尸; p.300 #30)*

**Ancient graphs.** 㞐, 㝒.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 九魚. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 斤於 — sounding like 車 (jū).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To dwell, to abide (凥處); it follows 尸 (body), gaining a stool (几) and so resting." It cites the Xiaojing, "Zhongni dwelt (凥)" — 凥 meaning to dwell at leisure; an associative compound thus. The modern script writes 居.

**Senses.**
- *At ease, settled (安).* *Guangyun*. Shu, *Pan geng*: "to fix where they should dwell (居)." Liji, *Wang zhi*: "in all settling of the people, gauge the land to lay out the town, measure the land to settle (居) the people; land, town, and people must be in due proportion to one another."
- *Also* Shu, *Shun dian*: "the five banishments and three dwellings (三居)"; note: "the three dwellings are the Zhou's barbarian-zone, garrison-zone, and frontier-zone."
- *To sit (坐).* Lunyu, *Yang huo*: "sit (居), and I will tell you."
- *To hoard, to store (積, 蓄).* Shu, *Gao Yao mo*: "encouraging exchange of what one has and has not, to transform what is hoarded (化居)"; note: "化 means to exchange — it means to barter what one has stored up (居積)." Also Shiji, *Treatise on the Balanced Standard*: "rich merchants transported grain by the hundredfold, withholding and hoarding (廢居), storing in the towns"; Xu Guang's note: "廢居 is a name for hoarding — withholding some, storing some, meaning to seize the moment for profit."
- *To stop, halt (止).* Liji, *Yue ling*: "if in late autumn the spring ordinances are followed, the troops rise and do not rest (不居)"; subcommentary: "do not cease and rest."
- *A sea-bird is called 爰居.* *Discourses of Lu*: "a yuanju (爰居) settled outside the east gate of Lu."
- *Surname.* The Han Ju Ban, enfeoffed Marquis of Songcheng.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居之, sounding like 基 (jī). A particle. Liji, *Tan gong*: "at the mourning of Gongyi Zhongzi, he passed over the grandson and set up the son; Tan Gong said: 'how is that (何居)? I have not heard of such before'"; note: "a phrase of wonder, like saying 'for what reason.'" One account: 何居 is like saying 'where lies this rightness'; 居 read as written, with no need to change it to 基. *Ji Yun*: interchangeable with 其.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 據 (jù), fanqie 居御.* Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "it is the dove that dwells (居) in it" — rhyming with 御 below. *Tang feng*: "do not be too much at ease, but mind your station (其居)" — rhyming with 瞿 below (瞿 in the departing tone). A note: the *Shuowen* glosses 居 also as "to squat"; the *Changjian* takes 凥 as "to dwell" and 居 as "to squat." The *Yun Hui* and *Zheng Yun* file it in the 御-rhyme, citing the Shijing "居居懷惡 (harbouring ill-will)" — so 居 has a 倨-reading. *Zhengzitong*: "to squat (蹲踞) is interchangeable with 倨; for 'to dwell' and 'to abide' versus 'to squat' and 'arrogant,' it is well to follow the separate usage of classics and histories."

**Textual notes.** "Liji, *Zengzi wen*: sit, and I will tell you" — a note: the *Zengzi wen* has no such words; it belongs to the Lunyu, and is corrected to Lunyu, *Yang huo*. · "Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: rich merchants transported grain by the hundredfold, withholding and hoarding, storing in the towns; Xu Guang's note: 廢居 is a name for hoarding" — a note: Xu Guang's note is seen in the Shiji, not the *Treatise on Food and Money*; it is corrected from Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*, to Shiji, *Treatise on the Balanced Standard*.
