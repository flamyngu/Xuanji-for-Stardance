**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Square radical 方; p.482 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢒃, 𠇜, 𠤒.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 式支. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 商支. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 申支 — sounding like 詩 (shī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the look of a banner; Yue Shi of Qi was styled Ziqi (Master Banner), whence we know 施 is a banner." Note: Xu Kai: "the trailing waver of a banner; one says, to set up, deploy (設)." Shu, *Yiji*: "with the five pigments to display and apply (施) the five colours."
- *戚施, a toad (a hunchback).* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "got this toad (戚施)"; tradition: "戚施, one who cannot look up"; gloss: "one who fawns on others with his looks, hence cannot look up."
- *施施, ambling slowly.* Shijing, *Wangfeng*: "he will come slowly, slowly (施施)"; tradition: "the sense of coming on with difficulty"; gloss: "ambling along, watching for a chance, coming alone to see me." Mengzi: "ambling (施施) he came in from outside"; Zhao Qi: "施施 is like 扁扁, a look of glad delight."
- *To levy, allot (賦).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Neizai*: "she assigns (施) their tasks"; note: "施 is like 'to levy.'"
- *To set down, record (著).* Liji, *Jitong*: "inscribed (施) on the winter-sacrifice vessels and cauldrons"; note: "施 is like 'to record.'"
- *To display [a corpse].* The Jinyu: "the men of Qin killed Ji Rui and exposed him (施)"; note: "施 is to display the corpse."
- *To spread, stretch (張); to use, add (用, 加).* *Yupian*, *Zengyun.*
- *A surname.* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 9: "Shifu (施父)"; note: "a Lu grandee." Liji, *Zaji*: "Confucius said, 'I ate at the Shaoshi (少施) house and was sated'"; note: "the Shaoshi clan, descendants of Shifu."
- *To bestow, give (惠, 與).* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 施智, sounding like 翅 (shì). Yijing, *Qian*: "his virtue is bestowed (施) on all"; and: "the clouds move and the rain falls (施)." Liji, *Quli*: "the highest prize virtue, the next strive in giving (施) and requiting." Zuozhuan, Xi yr 24: "the requiters are wearied, the givers (施) not yet sated"; note: "施 means service rendered." *Zengyun*: "the 施 of 'to deploy' is level tone; the 施 of 'to give' rhymes in both level and departing."
- *To extend, move (移).* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 以豉, sounding like 易 (yì). Shijing, *Zhounan*: "the dolichos spreads, trailing (施) into the valley"; tradition: "施 is to extend, move." Shijing, *Daya*: "extending (施) to sons and grandsons"; gloss: "施 is like 'to pass on, prolong.'" Yili, *Sangfu*: "for a cut-off clan there is no extended (施) mourning"; note: "to reach collaterally is 施."
- *To set aside, relax (rising tone).* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 賞是. "To set aside; to change; interchangeable with 弛." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Shaozai*: "he orders the exemptions (施舍)"; note: "those not assigned labour." Lunyu: "the gentleman does not neglect (施) his kin"; He Yan: "施 is to exchange — not to put another's kin in place of one's own." Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "leading the host he relaxed (施) the punishments"; note: "施 is read 弛, to loosen."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 余支, sounding like 移 (yí) — 㢮 is sometimes written 施. Shiji, *Biography of Wei Wan*: "a sword is what people pass on and exchange (施)"; note: "施 is read 移."
- *Slanting (西斜).* Shiji, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "on the *gengzi* day, [the sun] slanting (施)"; note: "施, fanqie 矢遺"; the Suoyin: "like 'slanting westward.'"

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 詩戈.* Han Gaozu, *Lady Qi's song*: "...though there be stringed arrows, where now can they be used (施)?
