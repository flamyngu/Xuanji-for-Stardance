**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, lower; Deer radical 鹿; p.1510 #31)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠧥, 𠀙, 麗, 𪋘.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 郞計. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 力霽 — sounding like 隷 (lì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to travel in company. It is the deer's nature, when it sees food and hastens, always to go in a herd (旅行)." Also Sima Xiangru, *Daren fu*: "yoking the responding-dragon to the ivory chariot, looping and winding, swaying along (委麗)"; Yan Shigu's note: "the look of walking, advancing, and halting."
- *A pair (偶).* *Yupian.* Yijing, *Dui*: "joined marshes are Dui (麗澤兌)"; note: "麗 is like 'linked.'" Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Jiaoren*: "a pair of horses (麗馬) to one groom"; note: "two horses." Also: "a bundle of silk and a pair of hides (麗皮)"; note: "two hides." Shi shiji: "Taihao first ordained marriage, using a pair of hides (麗皮) as the rite"; the gloss: "麗 means an even pair."
- *Fair, beautiful (好, 美).* *Yupian*, *Guangyun.* Chuci, *Zhaohun*: "robed in patterned, fine, and lovely (麗) silks, yet not outlandish." Also Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "he made virtue his splendour (麗)."
- *A number (數).* *Yupian.* Shijing, *Daya*: "the sons and grandsons of Shang, their number (麗) not [merely] a hundred thousand."
- *To lodge in, strike (著).* *Guangyun.* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 12: "he shot the elk, lodging [the arrow] (麗) in its back-bone"; note: "麗 means to lodge in."
- *To cleave to, attach (附).* *Zheng Yun.* Yijing, *Li*: "Li is 'to cleave' (麗); sun and moon cleave to Heaven, the grains and grasses and trees cleave to the earth." Also Liji, *Wangzhi*: "blame and punishment are attached (麗) to the deed"; note: "麗 means to attach — in blaming or punishing a man each must fit the deed, and one may not borrow from elsewhere one's likes and angers."
- *To tie, fasten (繫).* Liji, *Jiyi*: "on the sacrifice-day the ruler leads the victim, and having entered the temple gate, ties (麗) it to the stone pillar"; note: "麗 is like 'to fasten.'"
- *Splendid, figured (華綺).* *Yupian*, *Zheng Yun.* Shu, *Biming*: "a decayed custom of luxury and finery (奢麗), the same in current through myriad ages." Han Shi waizhuan: "Yuan Xian said to Zigong, 'to hide benevolence and right while flaunting fine robes and furs (麗) — that I cannot bear to do.'"
- *To set forth, apply (施).* *Yupian.* Shu, *Duofang*: "unable to open up the people's order (麗)"; tradition: "unable to open up the governance set forth among the people; 麗 means to set forth — it speaks of their benightedness."
- *Bright, luminous.* *Zheng Yun.*
- *Place-names.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 13: "the Jin army crossed the Jing, reached Houli (侯麗), and turned back"; note: "Houli, a Qin place." Also Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Huali (華麗) county in Lelang commandery."
- *Same as 欐, a roof-ridge.* Liezi, *Liming*: "where they dwell, the ridge-beams (麗) join."
- *麗譙, a high tower.* Zhuangzi, *Xu Wugui*: "let my lord by no means marshal cranelike ranks before his high tower (麗譙)." Note to Former Han, *Biography of Chen Sheng*: "a tower is also called 譙, hence a fair (麗) tower is called 麗譙."
- *梁麗, a siege-beam (a vehicle-name).* Zhuangzi, *Qiushui*: "a 梁麗 can batter a city wall but cannot stop up a hole — they are different tools."
- *魚麗 (fish-array), a battle-formation.* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 5: "Gao Qumi with the central army attended the duke in a fish-array (魚麗) formation."
- *A small boat.* *Zheng Yun.*
- *A surname (see the Xingyuan); also a compound surname.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 17: "Duke Li of Jin disported at the house of Jiangli (匠麗)."

**Further readings.**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 呂支; *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 鄰知; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 鄰溪 — sounding like 離 (lí). *Shiming*: "麗 is 離 (apart): one eye looks to Heaven, one to earth, the sight clear and divided (離), each seeing differently."
- *Gaoli (高麗), a state-name.* Weizhi: "Gaogouli (高句麗) lies east of Liaodong." The Former Han History writes 高句驪.
- *A mountain-name.* Shiji, *Biography of Qing Bu*: "Bu was formerly a convict of Li Mountain (麗山)." Sometimes written 驪.
- *Same as 鸝 (oriole).* Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "the orioles (麗黃) cry *ying-ying*"; note: "𪁐 and 麗 are anciently interchangeable."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 憐題, sounding like 黎 (lí); same sense.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 里弟, sounding like 禮 (lǐ). Xiao Gai says: Pengli (彭蠡), a lake-name, anciently written 彭麗.
- *Fanqie 力智, sounding like 詈 (lì) — "beautiful."*
- *Leipian*: fanqie 山宜, sounding like 釃 (shī) — "to split (㭊)."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 郞狄, sounding like 歷 (lì) — a county-name. It follows 丽; the vulgar form from two 日 is wrong.

**On the graph.** Liushu zhenge: "丽 is the old 麗 graph, a shape of mutual attachment, borrowed for 伉麗 (a married pair); the vulgar make a separate graph 儷."

**Textual notes.** The Chuci *Zhaohun* line should read 被文服纖麗 (adding 服). In the Zuozhuan Huan-5 citation, 陣 should read 陳.
