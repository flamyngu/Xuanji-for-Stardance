**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Grain radical 禾; p.340 #01)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠫺.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*: fanqie 奴顚. *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 寧顚. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 寧田 — the level tone of 撚 (nián).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "originally written 秊; the ripening of grain." It follows 禾 (grain) with 千 as phonetic.
- *A harvest, a year of plenty.* Chunqiu, Huan yr 3: "a year of plenty (有年)"; Guliang tradition: "when the five grains all ripen, it is 'a year of plenty.'" Xuan yr 16: "a great year of plenty (大有年)"; Guliang: "when the five grains greatly ripen, it is 'a great year of plenty.'"
- *A year (歲).* Erya, *Shitian*: "Xia called it 歲, Shang 祀, Zhou 年, Tang and Yu 載"; note: "歲 takes Jupiter's passing one station, 祀 the four seasons' one round, 年 the grain's one ripening, 載 things ending and beginning anew"; subcommentary: "年 is the name of the grain's ripening; each year it ripens once, hence used as the name for the year." Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "to fix the *sui* and *nian* in order to arrange affairs"; note: "the count by mid-points is *sui*, the count by new moons is *nian*"; subcommentary: "within one year there are twenty-four breaths; the nodal breath comes first, the mid-breath after; the nodal breath is also called the new-moon breath; when the mid-breaths complete a round it is a *sui*, when the new-moon breaths complete a round it is a *nian*." Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 3: "the divined span was seven hundred years (年)."
- *Age, years of life (齒).* Shiming: "年 is 'to advance' (進): advancing forward." Liji, *Wangzhi*: "in the way the Three Kings cared for the aged, all reckoned by age (引年)"; note: "引年 is comparing ages." Zuozhuan, Ding yr 4: "King Wu had eight younger brothers by the same mother; the Duke of Zhou was Grand Steward, Kangshu Minister of Crime, Danji Minister of Works, and the other five uncles held no office — was it then a matter of age (年)?"; note: "rank went by virtue, not age by seniority."
- *A surname.* The Wanxing tongpu: "in the Yongle era there was Nian Dang (年當), a man of Huaiyuan, who rose to be President of the Board of Revenue."
- *Rhymes as 紉 (rén), fanqie 禰因.* Former Han, *Postface*: "the feng and shan sacrifices, the suburban rites — he raised in rank the hundred spirits; he harmonized the pitch-pipes and reformed the calendar, to enjoy this everlasting span (永年)." Cui Yin, *Inscription on Socks*: "long may you tread in great blessing, even to a hundred million years (年); the august spirits having favoured you, blessings and emoluments come thronging."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 乃定, sounding like 佞 (nìng). A personal name. Gongyang zhuan, Xiang yr 30: "Nìngfū (年夫)"; the Shiwen: "年 sounds like 佞; the other two traditions write 佞夫." *Ji Yun*: also written 䄭.

**Textual notes.** "又叶禰因切，音民" — but fanqie 禰因 is not the sound of 民; checked against the phonetic gloss, 民 should read 紉.
