**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Arrive radical 至; p.1001 #11)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𦤳, 𦤴, 𦤵, 𡊏.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 脂利. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 支義 — sounding like 摯 (zhì).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A flying bird coming down from on high to the ground; it follows 一, 一 being like the ground; pictographic — not going up and away, but coming down below." *Yupian*: "to come (來)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "as a river just at its cresting (至), so that nothing is not increased." Liji, *Yue ji*: "things arrive (至) and the knowing knows, and thereafter liking and loathing take form"; note: "至 means to come."

**Senses.**
- *To reach, to get through from here to there (達).* *Yupian*. Shu, *Wu yi*: "from morning until (至) the sun's decline at midday." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "I march westward, and reach (至) the wild of Jiao."
- *Utmost, extreme (極).* Yijing, *Kun* hexagram: "utmost (至), the primal power of Kun!"; note: "至 means utmost in the extreme." Also *Xi ci*: "the Yijing — is it not utmost (至)!" Zhuangzi, *Xiaoyao you*: "hence it is said: the utmost man (至人) has no self"; note: "the man of utmost extremity."
- *Good, excellent (善).* Liji, *Fang ji*: "by this to keep the people in bounds, yet among wives there are still some who fall short (不至)." Zhouli, *Kao gong ji*: "the bow-maker covers it, and the horn is good (角至)"; note: "至 is like good."
- *Great (大).*
- *The solstice.* Yijing, *Fu* hexagram: "the former kings, at the solstice (至日), closed the passes — merchants and travellers did not move, and the ruler did not inspect the regions"; note: "the winter solstice is the return of the yin, the summer solstice the return of the yang." *Zhengzitong*: "the summer solstice is called 日長至 (the day's-length-extreme): on that day the day-clepsydra marks fifty-nine, the night forty-one; before this the day-clepsydra was still fifty-eight. The day's length here reaches its extreme, hence 日長至 — 至 taking the sense of utmost extremity. The *Lüshi chunqiu*, twelve records: 'in mid-summer, the day reaches its longest (日長至)' is this. The winter solstice is also called 日長至: on that day the day-clepsydra marks forty-one, the night fifty-nine; past this the day-clepsydra is forty-two. The day's lengthening here begins, hence also 日長至 — 至 taking the sense of coming-to-arrive. Liji, *Jiao te sheng*: 'the suburban sacrifice welcomes the coming (至) of the lengthening day' is this. Yet the *Lüshi chunqiu* at mid-winter again says 日短至 (the day's-shortness-extreme). Huang Zhen: 'the vulgar mostly mistake the winter solstice for the length-extreme (長至), not knowing it is the shortness-extreme (短至).' By this account, 短至 should be the winter solstice; that it is also called 日長至 is because the yang here first lengthens — the sense of upholding the yang and curbing the yin."
- *至掌 (zhizhang), an insect-name.* Erya, *Shi chong*: "the leech-rou, zhizhang (至掌)." Kangcangzi, *Chen dao*: "the utmost man (至人) forgets the passions."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 徒結, sounding like 咥 (dié). 單至, a flighty, hasty look. Liezi, *Li ming*: "Mochi and Danzhi (單至)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 窒 (zhì), fanqie 職日.* Shijing, *Bin feng*: "the stork cries on the ant-hill, the wife sighs in her room; she sprinkles and sweeps, stops the chinks, and my march is at last arrived (至)."
- *Rhymes as 支 (zhī), fanqie 章移.* Liang Wudi, *Imitating the Ancients*: "the appointed day long past without return, home-land remote of voice and gleam; voice and gleam vainly knot delay, and half-asleep I wake as if he had come (至)" — rhyming with 期 and 絲 above.
