**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Cave radical 穴; p.863 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 苦紅. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 枯公 — sounding like 崆 (kōng).

**Etymology and senses.** Empty, void (虛). Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "when the Red Emperor enacts his virtue, the Celestial Prison [stars] are called 空 (empty)."
- *Also, great (大).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in that great (空) valley"; tradition: "great."
- *Also, exhausted, used up (盡).* Erya, *Shigu*: "空 means exhausted." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the shuttle and the loom-beam are emptied (空)."
- *Also 太空 (the great void), the sky.*
- *Also place-names.* Erya, *Shidi*: "the land bearing the Dipper and Pole to the north is Kongtong (空桐)." Zuozhuan, Ai yr 20: "the Duke of Song rambled at Kongze (空澤)"; note: "Kongze, a place in Song." Shiji, *Annals of Yin*, note: "Yi Yin was born at Kongsang (空桑)." Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Jingzhao has twelve counties, the third called Chuansikong (船司空)"; note: "a county name; originally the office in charge of boats, later made a county." Also *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "in the fifth year of Yuanding he progressed to Yong, crossed Long, and climbed Kongtong (空同)"; note: "Kongtong, a mountain name, also written 崆峒." Shanhai jing: "two hundred li north of White Horse Mountain is Mount Kongsang (空桑), whence the Kongsang waters issue." Kuodi zhi: "the place where Zhengzai bore Confucius, in the land of Kongsang, is now called Kongdou (空竇), on Mount Nüling twenty li south of Qufu county." Wei tudi ji: "ninety li northeast of Dai city is Konghou city (空侯城)."
- *Also an office-name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "Earl Yu was made Minister of Works (司空)." Also *Zhou guan*: "the Minister of Works governs the lands of the realm, settles the four classes of people, and times the use of the land's resources."
- *Also a name of obeisance.* Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "the Grand Invocator distinguishes nine obeisances, the third called 空首 (the empty-head bow)"; subcommentary: "first the two hands are joined to the ground, then the head is brought down to the hands — this is the 空首."
- *Also a musical-instrument name.* Fengsu tong: "the konghou (箜篌) is also called kanhou (坎侯); some say 空侯, taking it from being hollow within (空中)." Note to the *Chuci*: "kongsang (空桑) is the name of a zither."
- *Also a name for a prison.* Subcommentary to the Liji: "the jail (囹圄) was called 司空 under the Wei."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Han had two compound surnames, Kongtong (空桐) and Kongxiang (空相)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 苦動; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 康董 — sounding like 孔 (kǒng): a hole (穴), an aperture (竅), a cavity (竉); interchangeable with 孔. Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the armorer inspects the drilled holes (空)." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "the Blind Man had Shun dig a well, and Shun made a hidden hole (匿空) to emerge at the side." *Biography of Dayuan*: "Zhang Qian bored an opening (鑿空)"; note: "the Western Regions originally had no roads; now he bored a passage and opened them up." Hanfeizi, *Yu Lao*: "the apertures (空竅) are the doors and windows of the spirit." Yunhui xiaobu: "the men of Qin called an earth-cave a 土空 (earth-hollow)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 苦貢 — sounding like 控 (kòng): destitute, brought low (窮). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "[the ruler] ought not to impoverish (空) our host"; note: "he ought not let petty men reduce the people to destitution."
- *Also, lacking, a gap (缺).* Yangzi, *Fayan*: "the *Jiu gao* chapter is for a moment defective (空)."
- *Also, empty (虛).* Lunyu: "[Yan] Hui is near it — yet he is often empty (空) of means"; note: "空 is like being empty within."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 枯江. Xu Gan, *Sishi shi*: "the happy meeting has no set time; my inmost heart is broken and grieved. I take no joy in food and drink; gnawing, gnawing, I am ever hungry and empty (空)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 枯良. Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "lesser East and greater East, the shuttle and loom-beam emptied (空); tangled are the kudzu sandals, fit to tread the frost."

**Textual notes.** In the *Treatise on Geography* citation, 木土船 should read 本主船 (per the original).
