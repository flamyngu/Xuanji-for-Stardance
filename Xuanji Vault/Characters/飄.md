**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, lower; Wind radical 風; p.1414 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 𤰞遙 — sounding like 標 (biāo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "a whirlwind."
- *A whirling, swift wind.* Shijing, *Kuaifeng*: "no, the wind whirls (飄)"; Shiwen: "fanqie 符遙, also 必遙." Former Han, *Biography of Kuai Tong*: "whirling it comes (飄), the wind rising"; note: "fanqie 必遙; it means a swift wind."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 符霄. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 毗霄 — sounding like 瓢 (piáo); same sense. Erya, *Shitian*: "a turning wind is a 飄"; Shiwen: "飄 sounds like 瓢." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the whirlwind (飄) blows and blows"; Shiwen: "飄, fanqie 避遙."
- *Tang Yun*: fanqie 撫招. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 紕招 — sounding like 嫖 (piāo); 飄颻, fluttering aloft. Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "the Son of Heaven was greatly pleased, soaring (飄飄) as with a spirit that mounts the clouds."
- *To blow.* *Yun Hui.* Cao Zhi's poem: "a startling wind drives (飄) the white sun." Tao Qian, *Returning Home*: "the wind flutters and flutters (飄飄), blowing my robe."
- *To fall.* Zhuangzi, *Dasheng*: "even the resentful-hearted bear no grudge against a falling (飄) tile"; note: "to fall"; the Yunyi: "飄, fanqie 匹遙."
- *Used interchangeably with 漂.* *Yun Hui.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Yun*: "blown free (漂然), all of them having integrity, knowing the bounds of taking office and withdrawing."
- *Used interchangeably with 縹.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "soaring and soaring (縹縹) with the will to mount the clouds."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 匹妙, sounding like 剽 (piào). Cao Zhi, *Fu on Being Stirred by the Season*: "I break the Ruohua bloom to veil the sun, hoping the crimson light may shine long; I would lodge my body on the flying tumbleweed, and ride the yang wind to drift far away (遠飄)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen* writes it 𩙞; the Liushu gu also writes it 𩙞.
