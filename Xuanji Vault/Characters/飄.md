**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, lower; Wind radical йўЁ; p.1414 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie рӨ°һйҒҷ вҖ” sounding like жЁҷ (biДҒo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "a whirlwind."
- *A whirling, swift wind.* Shijing, *Kuaifeng*: "no, the wind whirls (йЈ„)"; Shiwen: "fanqie з¬ҰйҒҷ, also еҝ…йҒҷ." Former Han, *Biography of Kuai Tong*: "whirling it comes (йЈ„), the wind rising"; note: "fanqie еҝ…йҒҷ; it means a swift wind."
- *Guangyun*: fanqie з¬Ұйң„. *Ji Yun*: fanqie жҜ—йң„ вҖ” sounding like з“ў (piГЎo); same sense. Erya, *Shitian*: "a turning wind is a йЈ„"; Shiwen: "йЈ„ sounds like з“ў." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the whirlwind (йЈ„) blows and blows"; Shiwen: "йЈ„, fanqie йҒҝйҒҷ."
- *Tang Yun*: fanqie ж’«жӢӣ. *Ji Yun*: fanqie зҙ•жӢӣ вҖ” sounding like е«– (piДҒo); йЈ„йў», fluttering aloft. Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "the Son of Heaven was greatly pleased, soaring (йЈ„йЈ„) as with a spirit that mounts the clouds."
- *To blow.* *Yun Hui.* Cao Zhi's poem: "a startling wind drives (йЈ„) the white sun." Tao Qian, *Returning Home*: "the wind flutters and flutters (йЈ„йЈ„), blowing my robe."
- *To fall.* Zhuangzi, *Dasheng*: "even the resentful-hearted bear no grudge against a falling (йЈ„) tile"; note: "to fall"; the Yunyi: "йЈ„, fanqie еҢ№йҒҷ."
- *Used interchangeably with жјӮ.* *Yun Hui.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Yun*: "blown free (жјӮз„¶), all of them having integrity, knowing the bounds of taking office and withdrawing."
- *Used interchangeably with зё№.* Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "soaring and soaring (зё№зё№) with the will to mount the clouds."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie еҢ№еҰҷ, sounding like еүҪ (piГ o). Cao Zhi, *Fu on Being Stirred by the Season*: "I break the Ruohua bloom to veil the sun, hoping the crimson light may shine long; I would lodge my body on the flying tumbleweed, and ride the yang wind to drift far away (йҒ йЈ„)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen* writes it р©ҷһ; the Liushu gu also writes it р©ҷһ.
