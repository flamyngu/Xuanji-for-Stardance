**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Square radical 方; p.481 #03)*

**Ancient graph.** 𤙗.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 府良. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 分房 — sounding like 芳 (fāng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "boats joined side by side. It pictures two boats with their prows tied together, abbreviated. Sometimes written 汸 with the water radical." Shijing, *Zhounan*: "the river is long, it cannot be rafted (方)"; tradition: "方 is a raft (泭)"; Shiwen: "a small raft is 泭." Erya, *Shishui*: "a grandee crosses by a twin-boat (方舟)"; note: "two boats joined." Shiji, *Biography of Li Yiji*: "joining boats (方船) they went downstream"; note: "it means boats side by side."
- *Also* Yijing, *Kun*: "the second six: straight, square (方), and great"; note: "the body of Earth is calm and still — that is its squareness (方)." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the round answers to the compass, the square (方) to the carpenter's square." Huainanzi, *Tianwen*: "the way of Heaven is called round, the way of Earth square (方); the square governs the dark, the round governs the bright."
- *Also* Yijing, *Guan*: "the gentleman surveys the regions (方), observes the people, and sets up teaching"; subcommentary: "to survey the myriad regions." Shijing, *Daya*: "he beholds the four quarters (四方)." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Zhongzai*: "to distinguish the directions and fix the positions"; note: "to mark off the four directions"; Shiwen: "observing the sun's shadow to distinguish east, west, south, north — the four directions — so as to set them apart." Liji, *Neize*: "teach him the numbers and the names of the directions (方名)"; note: "方名, such as east and west."
- *Also* Yijing, *Weiji*: "the gentleman carefully distinguishes things and settles them in their places (方)"; subcommentary: "each in its own place, all at rest in their station." Shijing, *Daya*: "the cynosure (方) of the myriad states, the king of the common folk"; Jian: "方 is like 'to face toward' (嚮)"; subcommentary: "wherever 方 is spoken of, it means dwelling elsewhere and people turning toward it in longing, hence '方 is like 嚮.'"
- *Also, the Way (道).* Yijing, *Heng*: "the gentleman takes his stand and does not change his way (方)"; note: "方 is like 道." Liji, *Yue ji*: "music prevails and the people turn toward the Way (方)"; note: "方 is like 道."
- *Also* Yijing, *Fu*: "the sovereign does not inspect the regions (方)"; note: "方 is like 'affairs' (事)"; subcommentary: "he does not inspect his regional affairs."
- *Also, an art (術); a method (法).* Yijing, *Xici*: "by their methods (方) things gather in kinds"; subcommentary: "方 means methods, arts, and the conduct of one's nature." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 29: "the officials cultivate their craft (方)"; note: "方 is method and art."
- *Also, to defy (放).* Shu, *Yao dian*: "he defies (方) orders and ruins his kind"; Shiwen: "方 means to defy (放)."
- *Also, to possess it (有之).* Shijing, *Shaonan*: "the magpie has a nest, the dove possesses (方) it"; tradition: "方 means to possess it."
- *Also, now (今).* Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "now (方), what shall be the term?"; Jian: "now, at what time shall be the return-date?" Zhuangzi, *Tiandi*: "he is now about (方且) to root himself in his body yet differ in form"; note: "wherever 方且 is said, it means now about to do something."
- *Also the grain just sprouting but not yet eared.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "now in the husk (方), now in the ear"; Jian: "方 is the husk (房): it means when the sheath first forms and has not yet closed."
- *Also, to set side by side (倂).* Yili, *Xiangshe li*: "do not bring the feet together (方足)"; note: "方 is like 倂 (side by side)."
- *Also, jutting out at the side.* Yili, *Da she li*: "left and right are 方"; note: "方 is jutting out at the side."
- *Also, a tablet (板).* Yili, *Pin li*: "if fewer than a hundred graphs, write on a tablet (方)"; note: "方 is a tablet." Liji, *Zhongyong*: "set forth on tablets and slips (方策)"; note: "方 is a tablet (板), 策 a bamboo slip."
- *Also, a fixed rule (常).* Liji, *Tan'gong*: "to left and right they wait and tend without fixed rule (無方)"; note: "方 is like 常."
- *Also, a pattern (文).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "when varied into a pattern (方) it is called tone"; note: "方 is like 文章 (a figured pattern)."
- *Also, the fitness of righteousness.* Zuozhuan, Yin yr 3: "I have heard that one who loves his son teaches him by the right way (義方)." Also Min yr 2: "assigning the fit task and employing the able (授方任能)"; note: "方 is the fitness of all affairs."
- *Also, to compare (比方).* Lunyu: "Zigong compares men (方人)"; He Yan's note: "comparing men one with another."
- *Also* Boya: "方 means great (大); upright (正)."
- *Also a sacrifice-name.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "with the soil-sacrifice and the fang (方)"; tradition: "welcoming the airs of the four quarters at the suburb."
- *Also a place-name.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "they invaded Hao and Fang (方)"; note: "Hao and Fang are both northern place-names."
- *Also a surname.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "Fang Shu (方叔) comes to his post"; tradition: "Fang Shu was a minister."
- *Also office-names.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Sima*: the Zhifang masters (職方氏), Tufang (土方氏), Huaifang (懷方氏), Hefang (合方氏), Xunfang (訓方氏), Xingfang (形方氏). Former Han, *Biography of Zhu Yun*, note: "the Shangfang (尚方) is a subordinate office of the Privy Treasury, making vessels and objects for imperial use."
- *Also a medical prescription (醫方).* Shiji, *Biography of Bian Que*: "he then took all his secret prescription-books (禁方書) and gave them all to Bian Que." Former Han, *Treatise on the Suburban Sacrifices*: "Shaojun was formerly the prescription-master (主方) of a retainer of the Marquis of Shenze"; note: "he was the marquis's household master of prescriptions and drugs."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 符方, sounding like 房 (fáng). 方與 (Fangyu), a county-name. Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Gao*: "the Lord of Pei attacked Huling and Fangyu (方與)"; note: "pronounced 房預 (fángyù); it belonged to Shanyang commandery."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 蒲光, sounding like 旁 (páng); 彷 is sometimes written 方. Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "wandering (方皇) in the western quiet"; note: "方皇 is like 彷徨 (to roam)."
- *Also* fanqie 文紡; 蝄 is sometimes written 方. Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Fangxiangshi*: "he drives off the fangliang (方良)"; note: "方良 is 罔兩 (wangliang); the goblins of wood and stone are the kui and the wangliang." Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "he dashes out the brains of the fangliang (方良)"; note: "方良 is a spirit of marsh and meadow." A note: the *Shuowen* writes it 蝄蜽.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 甫兩, sounding like 倣 (fǎng): to imitate (效).
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 膚容. Daozang, *Song of the Left Madam*: "leaping up on the cloud-bright shafts, drifting to behold the rosy heights of the void; the misty curtained-carriages dance crosswise and athwart, the purple canopies mark the numinous realm (靈方)."
