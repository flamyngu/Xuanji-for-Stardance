## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈卯集上〉【心字部】頁385第09【唐韻】【集韻】胡艮切【韻會】含艮切【正韻】下艮切，𠀤音拫。【說文】怨也。从心艮聲。一曰怨之極也。【蜀志·劉巴傳】巴不得反使，遂遠適交阯。先主深以爲恨。　又悔恨也。【史記·李廣傳】王朔謂李廣曰：將軍自念豈嘗有所恨乎。廣曰：羌降者八百餘人，吾詐而盡殺之，至今大恨。　又叶胡甸切，音現。【歐陽修·祭范仲淹文】自公云亡，謗不待辨。始屈終伸，公其無恨。【正字通】恨與憾聲義微別。憾意淺，恨意深，憾音輕，恨音重。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.385 #09)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 胡艮. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 含艮. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 下艮 — sounding like 拫 (hèn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To resent (怨)." It follows 心 (heart) with 艮 as phonetic. One account: the extreme of resentment. Shuzhi, *Biography of Liu Ba*: "Ba could not get a return commission, and so went far off to Jiaozhi; the First Ruler deeply regretted (恨) it."

**Senses.**
- *Remorse, regret (悔恨).* Shiji, *Biography of Li Guang*: "Wang Shuo said to Li Guang: 'does the general, reflecting, perhaps have anything he regrets (恨)?' Guang said: 'over eight hundred Qiang had surrendered, and I tricked and slaughtered them all — to this day I greatly regret (大恨) it.'"

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 現 (xiàn), fanqie 胡甸.* Ouyang Xiu, *Sacrificial address for Fan Zhongyan*: "since you, sir, passed away, slander awaited no refuting; first bent, at last unfolded — you, sir, have no regret (無恨)." *Zhengzitong*: "恨 and 憾 differ slightly in sound and sense: 憾 is the shallower meaning, 恨 the deeper; 憾 the lighter sound, 恨 the heavier."
