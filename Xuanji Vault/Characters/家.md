**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.286 #17)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠖔, 𡩅, 𡧚.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 古牙. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居牙 — sounding like 加 (jiā).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a dwelling (家居)." The Erya: "between door and window is the 扆 (screen-space); within it is the 家 (home)." Shijing, *Zhounan*: "fit for her chamber and home (室家)"; note: "家 means all within one door."
- *A husband (as a wife names him).* Mengzi: "when a girl is born, [her parents] wish her to have a home (家) [a husband]."
- *夫家, a man-and-household (one man's hundred-*mu* allotment).* Zhouli, *Diguan*: "on best land a household (家) of seven, on middling land six, on poor land five"; note: "having a husband and wife, then it is a 家."
- *A grandee's fief; 家臣, a house-vassal.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 29: "the grandees are all rich; the rule will pass to the [great] houses (家)."
- *天家, the Heavenly House (the Son of Heaven).* Cai Yong, *Duduan*: "the Son of Heaven has no 'outside'; he takes the world as his house (家)."
- *To settle in a place.* Shiji, *Biography of Lu Jia*: "with the good fields of Haozhi, he went and made his home (家) there."
- *A 'school' of authorship.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he exalted the Six Classics and dismissed the Hundred Schools (百家)." The Grand Historian's Preface: "to complete the words of one school (一家)."
- *家人, a hexagram-name.*
- *A surname.* The Han had Jia Xian, magistrate of Ju; the Song had Jia Xuanweng.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 古胡, sounding like 姑 (gū). Shijing, *Binfeng*: "I had not yet a chamber and home (家)" (rhyming with 据, 荼). *Xiaoya*: "restore my state and home (家)" (rhyming with 居, 樗).
- *Same as 姑; 大家 (da-gu), an honorific for a woman.* The Han Ban Zhao, wife of Cao Shishu, was called Da-gu; she was [Ban] Chao's sister.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 古俄, sounding like 歌 (gē).* The old *"Pheasant Flies at Dawn"* air: "I alone, what is my lot? — I have no home (家); the time draws to dusk, and what can be done?" Kong Zang, *Smartweed fu*: "if not for virtue and right, I would not make a home (家); idle and without care, what better than the birds and beasts?"
- *Rhymes, fanqie 古暮, sounding like 固 (gù).* Jiao's Yilin: "the three-footed lone bird, bright spirit as its driver; overseeing faults and punishing evil, it harms its own house (家)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "it follows 宀 (roof), with 豭 contracted as phonetic." Zhou Bowen: "a pen where pigs dwell is a 家, hence it follows 宀 and 豕 (pig); later men borrowed it for the 家 of 'home.'" A note: the Liushu gu derives it from 宀 over 㐺 (men gathered under a roof), 乑 being the old 族 graph corrupted into 豕, and calls the Shuowen's "豭 contracted" meaningless.

**Textual notes.** The Shuowen entry should read 家居也, and the Erya "戸牖之閒謂之扆"; "襄二十六年" should read "襄二十九年".
