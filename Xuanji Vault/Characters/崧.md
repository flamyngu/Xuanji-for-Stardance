## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈寅集中〉【山字部】頁315第03【廣韻】息弓切【集韻】【韻會】思融切【正韻】息中切，𠀤音松。【爾雅·釋山】山大而高，崧。【疏】崧，竦也。亦高稱也。【詩·大雅】崧高維嶽。【傳】嶽，四嶽也。【疏】堯時止有四嶽而已，不主中嶽。又云：崧，高貌。此則山高大者自名崧，不主中嶽而言。今之中嶽名崧，或取此以立名也。【韓愈·送侯參謀詩】三月崧少步，躑躅紅千層。【註】謂嵩山少室也。【說文】嵩，中嶽嵩山也。【註】亦从松。【通雅】孫愐，徐鉉合崧嵩爲一字，戴侗非之，泥。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, middle; Mountain radical 山; p.315 #03)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 息弓. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 思融. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息中 — sounding like 松 (sōng).

**Etymology and senses.** Erya, *Shi shan*: "a mountain great and high is 崧"; subcommentary: "崧 means to rise up (竦); it is also a term for height." Shijing, *Daya*: "lofty (崧) and high are the Marchmounts"; tradition: "the Marchmounts are the Four Marchmounts"; subcommentary: "in Yao's time there were only the Four Marchmounts, with no Central Marchmount presiding. It further says: 崧 means a high look — so a mountain high and great is of itself named 崧, not said with reference to the Central Marchmount. Today's Central Marchmount is named 崧 (Song), perhaps taking its name from this." Han Yu, *Sending off Adjutant Hou*: "in the third month, strolling at Song and Shao, the azaleas a thousand layers of red"; note: "this means Mount Song's Shaoshi peak." *Shuowen*: "嵩 is the Central Marchmount, Mount Song"; note: "also written with 松." *Tongya*: "Sun Mian and Xu Xuan merged 崧 and 嵩 into one graph; Dai Tong faulted this — pedantically."
