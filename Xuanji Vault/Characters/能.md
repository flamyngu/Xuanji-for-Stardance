**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Flesh radical 肉; p.981 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 奴登 — sounding like 儜 (néng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "of the bear kind, with feet like a deer's. The neng-beast (能獸) is firm within, hence the name; the worthy and able, the strong and sturdy, are called nengjie (能傑)." Xu [Kai] says: "firm within means the bones and joints are solid."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "good, skilled (善)." *Zengyun*: "equal to a task (勝任)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "you alone are unboastful, and none in the world can contend with you in ability (能)."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "to be tractable and practiced (順習)." Shijing, *Daya*: "soothing the far and conciliating (能) the near."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 奴來; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 囊來 — sounding like 㾍 (nái): a three-legged soft-shelled turtle. Erya, *Shiyu*: "a turtle with three legs is the neng (能)"; note: "the Shanhai jing: Mount Cong has many three-legged turtles; today the pond on Mount Jun in Yangxian county also has them."
- *Also interchangeable with 台 (tái).* 三能 (the Three Steps), a star-name. Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "below the Bowl are six stars, paired two by two, named the Three Steps (三能)"; note: "written 三台."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: fanqie 乃帶, sounding like 柰 (nài): a surname. The Tang had Nai Yanshou (能延壽) and Nai Yuanhao (能元皓); the Song had Nai Di (能迪).
- *Also interchangeable with 耐 (nài, to endure).* Former Han, *Biography of Chao Cuo*: "the Hu and Mo by nature endure (能) cold, the Yang and Yue by nature endure heat"; note: "能 is read 耐."
- *Rhymes as* 尼 (ní). Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "abundantly I have within me this inner beauty, and add to it cultivated talent (能)" — rhyming with 佩 below, 佩 pronounced 皮 (pí).
- *Rhymes as* 寧 (níng). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "each presents his ability (能)" — rhyming with 湛 above, 湛 pronounced 沈 (chén).
- *Rhymes as* 袽 (rú). Liu Zongyuan, *Peiwei fu*: "passing the nine bends I rush straight on, and surely break the shaft and lose my way; following the great road yet swerving in my track, again I seek to arrive and cannot (能)."
