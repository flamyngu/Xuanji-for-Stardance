## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集中〉【木字部】頁528第01    〔古文〕：𣹷、渿【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】呂張切【正韻】龍張切，𠀤音良。【說文】水橋也。【禮·月令】孟冬謹關梁。【詩·大雅】造舟爲梁。【爾雅·釋地】梁莫大于溴梁。　又石絕水爲梁。【詩·衞風】在彼淇梁。【禮·王制】獺祭魚，然後虞人入澤梁。　又魚梁，水堰也。堰水爲關空，承之以笱，以捕魚。梁之曲者曰罶。【詩·齊風】敝笱在梁。【小雅】胡逝我梁。　又【爾雅·釋地】隄謂之梁。又屋脊柱曰棟，負棟曰梁。【爾雅·釋宮】楣謂之梁。　又冠梁，冠上橫脊也。【漢大官令】冠兩梁。　又陸梁。【揚雄·甘泉賦】帶干將而秉玉戚兮，飛蒙茸而走陸梁。【註】陸梁，亂走貌。　又跳梁。【莊子·逍遙遊】貍狌東西跳梁，中于機辟。　又彊梁。【金人銘】彊梁者不得其死。【後漢·禮儀志】神名，能食鬼。　又大梁，西方之宿。【爾雅·釋天】大梁，昴也。　又州名。【書·禹貢】華陽黑水惟梁州。　又國名。周平王封少子康于夏陽，是謂梁伯。　又大梁，地名。【史記】魏惠王徙治大梁。　又山名。【詩·大雅】奕奕梁山。　又呂梁，水名。【莊子·達生篇】孔子觀于呂梁。　又都梁，香草名，澤蘭也。【荆州記】都梁山下生蘭草，因以爲名。　又姓。【廣韻】出安定、天水、河南三望。周梁鱣，漢梁鴻。複姓，梁丘、梁由。    考證：〔【詩·小雅】敝笱在梁。【又】胡逝我梁。〕　謹照原文小雅改齊風。又字改小雅。〔【爾雅·釋天】大梁，昴星。〕　謹照原文昴星改昴也。〔【晉語】魏惠王徙都大梁。〕　謹按晉語無徒都大梁之文，今照史記魏世家將晉語改爲史記。都字改爲治字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.528 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𣹷, 渿.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 呂張. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 龍張 — sounding like 良 (liáng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A water-bridge (水橋)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in early winter, watch the barriers and bridges (關梁)." Shijing, *Daya*: "he joined boats to make a bridge (梁)." Erya, *Shi di*: "of bridges none is greater than the Xu bridge (溴梁)."

**Senses.**
- *Stones set across water make a 梁 (a weir-causeway).* Shijing, *Wei feng*: "on that weir of the Qi (淇梁)." Liji, *Wang zhi*: "when the otter sacrifices fish, then the forester enters the marsh-weirs (澤梁)."
- *A fish-weir (魚梁), a water-dam.* Water is dammed to make a barrier-gap, and a basket-trap (笱) is set in it to catch fish; the curved part of the weir is called the 罶. Shijing, *Qi feng*: "the worn trap is at the weir (梁)." *Xiaoya*: "why do you go to my weir (梁)?"
- *Also* Erya, *Shi di*: "a dyke is called a 梁." Also the ridge-pillar of a roof is the 棟, and what bears the 棟 is the 梁 (beam). Erya, *Shi gong*: "the lintel is called the 梁."
- *The cap-ridge (冠梁), the crosswise ridge on top of a cap.* The Han Court-Provisioner's rule: "the cap has two ridges (兩梁)."
- *Luliang (陸梁).* Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "girt with the Ganjiang sword and grasping the jade axe, flying in their shaggy throng they run wild (陸梁)"; note: "陸梁 is the look of running in disorder."
- *Tiaoliang (跳梁, to leap about).* Zhuangzi, *Xiaoyao you*: "the wildcat leaps about (跳梁) east and west, and falls upon the snare."
- *Qiangliang (彊梁, the overbearing).* The *Jinren ming* (Inscription on the Metal Man): "the overbearing (彊梁) do not die a natural death." Later Han, *Treatise on Ceremonial*: "a god's name, able to eat ghosts."
- *Daliang (大梁), a lodge of the west.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "大梁 is the Mao lodge."
- *A province-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "south of Hua and the Black Water is Liangzhou (梁州)."
- *A state-name.* King Ping of Zhou enfeoffed his younger son Kang at Xiayang, who is called the Earl of Liang (梁伯).
- *Daliang (大梁), a place-name.* The *Shiji*: "King Hui of Wei moved his seat to Daliang."
- *A mountain-name.* Shijing, *Daya*: "lofty is Mount Liang (梁山)."
- *Luliang (呂梁), a water-name.* Zhuangzi, *Da sheng*: "Confucius gazed at the Lüliang (呂梁)."
- *Duliang (都梁), a fragrant-herb name, the marsh-orchid (澤蘭).* The *Jingzhou ji*: "beneath Mount Duliang grows the orchid-herb, whence it took its name."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "it comes from the three seats of Anding, Tianshui, and Henan." Zhou had Liang Zhan; the Han, Liang Hong. Compound surnames: Liangqiu (梁丘) and Liangyou (梁由).

**Textual notes.** Shijing, "the worn trap is at the weir; why do you go to my weir" — checked against the original, *Xiaoya* (小雅) is corrected to *Qi feng* (齊風) for the first line, and the second "又" to *Xiaoya* (小雅). Also Erya, *Shi tian*, "大梁 is the Mao star (昴星)" — checked against the original, 昴星 is corrected to 昴也. Also the *Discourses of Jin*, "King Hui of Wei moved his capital to Daliang" — a note: the *Discourses of Jin* has no such text; following the *Shiji, Hereditary House of Wei*, *Discourses of Jin* is corrected to *Shiji*, and 都 (capital) to 治 (seat).
