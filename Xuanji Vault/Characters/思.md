**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.381 #04)*

**Ancient graphs.** 恖, 𠂺, 𡴓, 𢍄.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 息兹. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 新兹. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 息移 — sounding like 司 (sī).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Penetrating insight (睿)." Shu, *Hong fan*: "thinking yields insight (思曰睿)." *Liushu Zongyao*: "to keep in mind (念), to deliberate (慮); to unravel a principle is 思."

**Senses.**
- *To wish, desire (願).* Shijing, *Daya*: "would there were these many officers!"; gloss: "願, to wish"; *Correct Meaning*: "what the mind thinks is necessarily what the feelings wish."
- *A final particle.* Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "it cannot be swum across — 思"; *Daya*: "may the spirits draw near — 思."
- *An initial particle.* Shijing, *Daya*: "grave and reverent was Tai Ren"; *Lu song*: "joyfully [by] the Pan waters."
- *Posthumous title.* "To plan and ponder without fault is 思."
- *A prefecture.* The Qianzhong land of Chu; the Tang set up Sizhou, named for the Siqiong River.
- *Surname.* Taken from the posthumous title; in the Ming there was Si Zhidao.

**Further readings.**
- *Guangyun, Ji Yun, Leipian, Yun Hui*: fanqie 相吏, sounding like 四 (sì). Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "storing the essence, bending the thought (思)."
- *Grief (悲).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "[like] a rat I grieve and weep blood"; note: "思 in the departing tone; 鼠思 means grieving with sorrow — that is, grief." Shu, *Yao dian*: "reverent, intelligent, accomplished and thoughtful (思)"; note: "virtue pure and complete is called 思"; phonetic gloss: fanqie 息嗣, or read in the usual sound.
- *Rhymes as 須 (xū), fanqie 相居.* Xu Gan, *Chamber Thoughts*: "though my body is far away, how could I leave my lord even a moment? Having been close, I'll not turn cold; I think my lord now thinks of me too (思)."
- *Rhymes as 腮 (sāi), fanqie 桑才* — the look of a heavy beard. Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 2: the wall-builders of Song sang, "bearded, oh bearded (于思于思)! having dropped your armour, back you come."
- *To think of (念).* Yijing, *Xian*: "restless the going and coming; friends follow your thought (思)." Shijing, *Bei feng*: "none goes, none comes; far, far my longing (思)."

**On the graph (Shuowen).** It follows 心 (heart) with 囟 as phonetic. 囟 is the hollow of the crown-bone (fontanelle); from the crown to the heart, [the channels run] like silk threads strung together unbroken.
