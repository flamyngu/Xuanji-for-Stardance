**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls й…ү, upper; Speech radical иЁҖ; p.1175 #20)*

**Ancient graph.** рҘЎқ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie иӢҰе…ј вҖ” the level tone of жӯү (qiДҒn).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Respectful (ж•¬)." *Yupian*: "yielding (и®“)." *Zengyun*: "to render reverence (иҮҙжҒӯ); not to be self-satisfied."

**Senses.**
- *A hexagram-name.* Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "Modesty is penetrating; the gentleman has an end (и¬ҷдәЁ)"; *Shiwen*: "lowering and withdrawing is its sense, bending oneself below others." Zhu Xi's *Original Meaning*: "the sense of having yet not dwelling on it." Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "the gentleman takes modest withdrawal (и¬ҷйҖҖ) as ritual."
- *Surname.* Seen in the *Zihui*.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: sometimes written е—ӣ. Former Han, *Treatise on Arts and Letters*: "the modesty-upon-modesty (е—ӣе—ӣ) of the *Yi*"; Yan Shigu's note: "same as и¬ҷ." Also Sima Xiangru, *Feng shan shu*: "Your Majesty in modesty (е—ӣи®“) does not set it forth"; the *Shiji* writes и¬ҷи®“.
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie иғЎе…ј. Same as е«Ң (suspicion). Xunzi, *Zhongni*: "trusted, yet not resting in suspicion (и¬ҷ)"; note: "it means, having won trust from above, not to rest in a place of suspicion, lest men suspect him of wielding favour and terror."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie иӢҰз°ҹ вҖ” the rising tone of жӯү (qiЗҺn). The look of calm and quiet. Liji, *Da xue*: "this is what is called self-contentment (иҮӘи¬ҷ)"; note: "и¬ҷ is read as ж…Ҡ; ж…Ҡ speaks of satisfaction (еҺӯ)." Zhu Xi's tradition: "и¬ҷ means glad, sufficient (еҝ«, и¶і)." *Yun Hui*: "и¬ҷ and ж…Ҡ are interchangeable."
- *Also* *Zihui*: fanqie иӢҰеҠ«, sounding like жҖҜ (qiГЁ). In the *Da xue* the sense is the same; this is Zhu Xi's reading. *Liushu tong*: also written р§«Ё.
