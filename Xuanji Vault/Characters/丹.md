**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; Dot radical 丶; p.80 #16)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠁿, 㣋, 𠕑.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 都寒. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 多寒. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 都艱 — sounding like 單 (dān). Red cinnabar (赤色丹砂).

**Etymology and senses.** Shu, *Yu gong*: "whetstones, grindstones, arrowhead-stone, and cinnabar (丹)." Shanhai jing: "丹 is chiefly red; the black and white are also of the cinnabar kind." Tao Hongjing: "it is vermilion (朱砂)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* the Daoists call the smelting of metals and stones in the cauldron the outer elixir (外丹), and the breathing-out of the old and drawing-in of the new the inner elixir (內丹). *Huangting jing*: "the nine-times-cycled eight-gem elixir (八瓊丹)"; note: "the eight are: cinnabar, realgar, azurite, sulphur, mica, rock-salt, niter, and orpiment."
- *Also* *Bowu zhi*: "when the harmonious breath responds, the hills bring forth black cinnabar (黑丹); the benevolent ruler thrives, the people lengthen their lives, and the realm is at peace."
- *To coat a thing in vermilion is 丹.* Yang Xiong, *Jie chao*: "he reddens (朱丹) his hubs."
- *A fine complexion is called 渥丹 (rich vermilion).* Shijing, *Qin feng*: "his face like rich vermilion (渥丹)."
- *A sincere heart without falsehood is called 丹.* Xie Tiao's poem: "holding the cinnabar-stone heart (丹石心), how could I shed plain-silk tears?"
- *Surname.* The Han Dan Yu, the Song Dan Shan, the Ming Dan Zhong.
- *Danyang (丹陽), a commandery-name.* Emperor Wu of Han renamed Zhang commandery as Danyang commandery; Emperor Wu of Jin split off the two commanderies of Xuancheng and Piling. Also a prefecture-name: originally the land of the Red Di, the Yuan Wei set up Fenzhou, later renamed Danzhou (丹州). Also Cui Bao, *Gujin zhu*: "the Danjiao (丹徼) — the southern frontier, its colour red, hence called the cinnabar frontier, the southern extremity."
- *丹丹 (Dandan), a state-name.* Seen in the Nanshi.
- *竊丹 (qiedan), a bird-name*, one of the Nine Phoenixes.
- *牡丹 (the peony), a flower-name.* *Bencao*: "also called the rat's-aunt (鼠姑)." Also 木丹 (mudan), another name for the gardenia-flower; 紫丹 (zidan), another name for the gromwell.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 顚 (diān), fanqie 都懸.* Lu Ji, *Luofu ge*: "the south cliff fills the gauze tent, the north islet brims with curtained carriages; the clear stream holds the duckweed's gleam, the high bank is clad in flowery vermilion (丹)." *Shuowen*: "丹 is the red stone of Yue in Ba. The outer part images the cinnabar-well, the inner the form of cinnabar." The graphs 靑, 彤, 雘, and the like follow this.
