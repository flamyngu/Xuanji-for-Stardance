## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈巳集上〉【水字部】頁611第21【唐韻】所加切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】師加切，𠀤音紗。【說文】水散石也。从水从少，水少沙見。楚東有沙水。【水經注】逢澤陂東北流爲新溝，又東北注梁爲沙水。　又【山海經】盧其之山，無草木，多沙石，沙水出焉，南流注于涔水。　又潁水之別名。【爾雅·釋水】潁爲沙。【註】謂大水溢出別爲小水之名。　又疏土也。【易·需卦】需于沙。【正義】沙，水旁之地。【詩·大雅】鳧鷖在沙。　又州名，在西徼外，取沙角山爲名，卽三秦記鳴沙山也。【廣輿記】沙州鳴沙山，峰勢危峻，沙如乾糖。天氣淸朗則沙鳴，聞數里外。　又流沙，西境極遠之地。【書·禹貢】西被于流沙。　又丘名。【爾雅·釋丘】邐迤曰沙丘。【疏】謂丘形斜行，連接而長者。　又長沙，郡名，屬楚。【廣輿記】今湖廣長沙府有長沙縣。　又長沙，星名。在軫旁，主壽命。見【史記·天官書】。　又水中有沙者曰沙衍。【𥠇天子傳】天子乃遂東征，南絕沙衍。　又沙汰，詳汰字註。　又【謝察微·算經】十塵爲沙，十沙爲纖。　又木名。【范成大·桂海草木記】沙木與杉同類，尤高大，葉尖成叢，穗少與杉異。　又豆名。【崔豹·古今注】貍豆，一名貍沙，一名獵沙。虎豆，一名虎沙。馬豆，一名馬沙。　又吹沙，魚名。【爾雅·釋魚】鯊鮀。【郭璞註】今吹沙也。【陸璣註】魚狹而小，常張口吹沙，故曰吹沙。　又小而甘美之稱。【韓彥直·橘錄】沙橘取其細而甘美，或曰種之沙州之上，故其味特珍。然邦人稱物之小而甘美者必曰沙，如沙瓜，沙蜜，沙糖之類，特方言耳。　又沙門，謂勤行也。【佛說四十二章經】辭親出家，識心達本，解無爲法，名曰沙門。　又姓。宋勇將有沙世堅。　又借爲紗縠字。【周禮·天官】內司服素沙。【註】素沙，今之白縳也。以白縳爲裏，使之章顯。今世有紗縠者，名出于此。　又【集韻】蘇和切【正韻】桑何切，𠀤音蓑。亭名，在元城。【春秋·定七年】齊侯衞侯盟于沙。【註】今陽平元城縣東南有沙亭。　又與犧通。【詩·魯頌】犧尊將將。【鄭註】素何反。【毛傳】有沙飾也。　又酒名。【儀禮·大射儀兩壷獻酒註】獻讀爲沙。沙酒濁，特泲之，必摩沙者也。　又【集韻】山宜切，音釃。亦水傍也。　又【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤所稼切，音嗄。聲嘶也。【周禮·天官】內饔鳥皫色而沙鳴貍。【註】沙音所嫁反，或蘇他反。【疏】沙，嘶也。○按沙本有蘇何、山宜二切，不必又入歌支叶韻。今刪去。    考證：〔【周禮·天官】內司服素沙。【註】素沙，今之白縛也。以白縛爲裏。〕　謹照原晝兩縛字𠀤改縳。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.611 #21)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 所加. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 師加 — sounding like 紗 (shā).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Stone scattered by water (水散石); it follows 水 (water) and 少 (few) — when the water is little, the sand (沙) shows. East of Chu there is the Sha river." Shuijing zhu: "the Fengze marsh flows northeast and becomes the New Ditch, then flows northeast into the Liang, becoming the Sha river (沙水)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Shanhai jing: "Mount Luqi has no grass or trees but much sand and stone; the Sha river (沙水) issues there and flows south into the Cen water."
- *Another name for the Ying river.* Erya, *Shi shui*: "the Ying becomes the Sha (沙)"; note: "the name for where a great water overflows and parts into a lesser water."
- *Loose earth (疏土).* Yijing, *Xu* hexagram: "waiting on the sand (沙)"; *Correct Meaning*: "沙 is the land beside the water." Shijing, *Daya*: "the ducks and gulls on the sand (沙)."
- *A prefecture-name*, beyond the western frontier, named from Shajiao Mountain — the Mingsha Mountain of the *Sanqin ji*. *Guangyu ji*: "the Mingsha Mountain of Shazhou, its peaks steep and sheer, the sand like dry sugar; when the weather is clear, the sand rings (鳴), heard several li off."
- *流沙 (the Moving Sands), a far western land.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "westward it reached to the Moving Sands (流沙)."
- *A hill-name.* Erya, *Shi qiu*: "winding and trailing is a sand-hill (沙丘)"; subcommentary: "a hill aslant in form, joined on and long."
- *Changsha (長沙), a commandery-name*, belonging to Chu. *Guangyu ji*: "now Changsha prefecture in Huguang has Changsha county."
- *Changsha (長沙), a star-name*, by the Zhen lodge, governing lifespan. Seen in Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*.
- *Water with sand in it is called 沙衍.* *Mu tianzi zhuan*: "the Son of Heaven then marched east and crossed the sandy shallows (沙衍) to the south."
- *沙汰 (to sift out).* See in full under the graph 汰.
- *Also* Xie Chawei, *Suan jing*: "ten motes are a sand (沙), ten sands a fibre."
- *A tree-name.* Fan Chengda, *Guihai cao mu ji*: "the sha-tree (沙木) is of the same kind as the fir, but taller and greater; its leaves pointed and clustered, its tassels fewer — differing from the fir."
- *Bean-names.* Cui Bao, *Gujin zhu*: "the racoon-bean is also called the racoon-sand (貍沙) or hunting-sand; the tiger-bean the tiger-sand (虎沙); the horse-bean the horse-sand (馬沙)."
- *吹沙 (the sand-blower), a fish-name.* Erya, *Shi yu*: "the sha-tuo (鯊鮀)"; Guo Pu's note: "today's sand-blower." Lu Ji's note: "a narrow, small fish that ever gapes and blows sand, hence called sand-blower (吹沙)."
- *A term for the small and sweet.* Han Yanzhi, *Ju lu*: "the sand-orange (沙橘) is so called for being small and sweet; some say it is grown on the sandbanks (沙州), hence its flavour especially fine. But the folk call whatever is small and sweet 沙 — sand-melon (沙瓜), sand-honey (沙蜜), sand-sugar (沙糖) — merely a dialect word."
- *沙門 (the śramaṇa), meaning diligent practice.* The *Sutra in Forty-two Sections*: "to leave one's kin and go forth from the home, to know the mind and reach the root, and grasp the law of non-action, is called śramaṇa (沙門)."
- *Surname.* The Song had the bold general Sha Shijian.
- *Borrowed for the graph 紗縠 (gauze).* Zhouli, *Tian guan*: "the plain gauze (素沙)"; note: "素沙 is today's white-spun silk; with white-spun as lining, it is made to show bright; the gauze (紗縠) of today takes its name from this."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 蘇和. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 桑何 — sounding like 蓑 (suō). A pavilion-name, at Yuancheng. Chunqiu, Ding yr 7: "the Marquis of Qi and the Marquis of Wei covenanted at Sha (沙)"; note: "now southeast of Yuancheng county in Yangping there is the Sha pavilion (沙亭)."
- *Interchangeable with 犧.* Shijing, *Lu song*: "the xi-goblet, stately (犧尊將將)"; Zheng's note: "fanqie 素何"; Mao's tradition: "it has sand-ornament (沙飾)."
- *A wine-name.* Yili, *Da she yi*, note on the two wine-jars: "獻 is read as 沙; sha-wine is turbid, and is specially strained, surely by rubbing-and-sifting (摩沙)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 山宜, sounding like 釃 (shī). Also "the waterside."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 所稼, sounding like 嗄 (shà). A hoarse voice (聲嘶). Zhouli, *Tian guan*: "the Inner Cook, a bird of pale colour and hoarse cry (沙鳴)"; note: "沙, fanqie 所嫁, or fanqie 蘇他"; subcommentary: "沙 means hoarse." A note: 沙 already has the fanqie 蘇何 and 山宜; it need not also enter the 歌 and 支 rhymes — these are here struck out.

**Textual note.** Zhouli, *Tian guan*, "the plain gauze (素沙)... with white-spun (白縛) as lining" — checked against the original, both 縛 are corrected to 縳 (white-spun silk).
