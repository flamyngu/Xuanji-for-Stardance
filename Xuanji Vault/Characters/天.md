**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Great radical 大; p.248 #04)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠕹, 𠀡, 𠀘, 𦴞, 兲.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 他前. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 他年 — the level tone of 腆 (tiān).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The crown of the head (顚); the utmost height, on high; it follows 一 (one) over 大 (great)." *Baihu tong*: "to settle, to quell (鎭) — dwelling on high and ordering what is below, the queller of things." Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "Qian is Heaven (天)." Liji, *Li yun*: "Heaven holds the yang, and hangs out the sun and stars." Xunzi: "Heaven has no solid form; all the utter void above the earth is Heaven." Shao Yong: "outside the self-so there is no separate Heaven." *Bequeathed Writings of Master Cheng*: "the blue-blue of Heaven — is that the form of Heaven? Looking down, it is again just so." *Zhengmeng of Master Zhang*: "Heaven turns to the left, and what is within it follows along; lagging a little, it then runs to the right." *Classified Sayings of Master Zhu*: "the *Li sao* has the theory of Nine Heavens, and various schools have wrongly explained that there are nine heavens. As I see it, it is only nine layers. For Heaven, in its revolving, has many layers — the inner layers softer, the outer growing harder; one supposes that by the ninth layer it becomes like a hard shell, and there it turns the tighter." A note: the form of Heaven is like the white of an egg. Examine the egg-white closely, and at its dense, blended core there are indeed seven layers; the eighth, a white membrane, is somewhat hard; and the last, ninth, becomes a hard shell. One may see the marvel of Master Zhu's grasp of the workings of creation. Today's Western calendrical account holds that Heaven, layer by layer, turns more slowly outward — and this is why the Seven Regulators in their retrograde wheeling have their slow and quick.

**Senses.**
- *A star-name.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "Tian is Gendi (根氐)." *Discourses of Zhou*: "when the Heaven-root (天根) appears, the waters dry up."
- *An ancient imperial style.* Getian shi (葛天氏), seen in the *Shuye ji*.
- *The name of a god.* Shanhai jing: "Xingtian (形天) contended with the Lord for godhead; the Lord cut off his head, and he made his nipples his eyes and his navel his mouth, and grasping shield and axe he danced." 形 is also written 𠛬. Tao Qian's poem: "Xingtian (𠛬天) danced with shield and axe, his fierce will ever there." (To take it as a beast's name is wrong.)
- *A place-name.* *Treatise on the Land of Shu*: "behind the Qiongbo mountains of Shu, the four wilds have no clear day — it is called the Leaking Sky (漏天)." Du Fu's poem: "the land near the Leaking Sky, rain the whole year."
- *A mountain-name.* *Jiuzhou yaoji*: "the old Wucheng of Liangzhou has the Tian Mountains (天山), where the Yellow Emperor received the golden-liquid divine elixir." One account places it in Yizhou; note: "the Tian Mountain is the Qilian range."
- *Tian, the name of a music.* The Junitian Broad Music (鈞天廣樂), seen in Shiji, *Hereditary House of Zhao*.
- *An office-name.* *Tang liudian*: "of the inner halls, only the Library Hall, for its grandeur, is called the Wooden Sky (木天)." Today the Hanlin Academy is called the Mutian office.
- *景天 (jingtian), a plant-name.* Tao Hongjing: "set in a pot and placed on the roof, it wards off fire-disaster."
- *The shaving punishment (髡𠛬).* Yijing, *Kui*, image: "that man is shaven (天) and his nose cut off."
- *Surname.* Tian Gao, magistrate of Changshe under the Han, seen in the *Xingyuan*.

**On the graph.** A note: in the 先-rhyme, anciently it ran together with 眞 and 文, so the graph 天 is everywhere given fanqie 鐵因; examining the classics and histories it is so throughout — only in the Yijing, "the six positions formed in their seasons, in season mounting the six dragons to drive Heaven (御天)," does it run with the 庚 and 靑 rhymes. The *Zhengzitong* holds that nothing is more exalted than Heaven, and below Heaven nothing more so than the lord and father, so the graph's sound must never be presumptuously changed to rhyme — a view quite upright and grand. 𠀑 is the graph for 天 devised by Empress Wu, resembling the seal-script 天.
