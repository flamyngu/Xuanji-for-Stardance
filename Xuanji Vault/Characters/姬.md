## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集下〉【女字部】頁260第23    〔古文〕：㚦【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】𠀤居之切，音基。【說文】黃帝居姬水，以姬爲氏，周人嗣其姓。【左傳·昭二十八年】武王克商，光有天下，兄弟之國者，十有五人。姬姓之國者，四十人。　又【廣韻】與之切【集韻】盈之切，𠀤音怡。婦人美稱。一曰王妻別名。一曰衆妾總稱。【師古曰】姬本周姓，其女貴於列國之女，所以婦人美號皆稱姬。【劉安世曰】政和中，大臣不學，以郡主爲宗姬。姬，周姓，漢初取爲𡣕嬙之號已可笑，今乃以𡣕嬙名其女，尤非。　又九魚切。同居。【列子·黃帝篇】姬將告女。【註】姬作居。    考證：〔【左傳·昭二十七年】武王克商，光有天下。〕　謹照原文二十七年改二十八年。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Woman radical 女; p.260 #23)*

**Ancient graph.** 㚦.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 居之, sounding like 基 (jī).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The Yellow Emperor dwelt by the Ji river (姬水) and took 姬 as his clan-name; the men of Zhou inherited it as their surname." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 28: "when King Wu conquered Shang and gloriously possessed the realm, the states of his brothers numbered fifteen, and the states of the Ji surname (姬姓) numbered forty."

**Further readings.**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 與之. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 盈之 — sounding like 怡 (yí). A fair title for a woman. One account: another name for a king's wife. One account: a collective term for the host of concubines. Yan Shigu: "姬 was originally the Zhou surname; its women ranked above the women of the other states, so all honorific titles for women came to be called 姬." Liu Anshi: "in the Zhenghe era the great ministers, being unlearned, made commandery-princesses into clan-Ji (宗姬). 姬 is the Zhou surname; that the early Han took it as a title for palace-women (𡣕嬙) was already laughable, and now to name their daughters with 𡣕嬙 is more wrong still."
- *Also* fanqie 九魚 — same as 居 (jū). Liezi, *Huang di*: "I (姬) shall tell you"; note: "姬 stands for 居."

**Textual note.** Zuozhuan citation given as "Zhao yr 27, 'when King Wu conquered Shang and gloriously possessed the realm'" — checked against the original, "year 27" should read "year 28."
