**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, upper; Field radical 田; p.763 #18)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢄖.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 羊吏. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 以智 — the departing tone of 移 (yì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to divide"; it follows 廾 (two hands) and 𢌿, where 𢌿 means "to hand over." *Boya*: "異 is to divide."
- *To divide, live apart.* Shiji, *Biography of Shang Yang*: "households with two or more grown sons who did not divide and live apart (分異) had their levy doubled."
- *To be different.* Shu, *Lü ao*: "the king then made his virtue manifest, reaching even to states of other (異) surnames." Liji, *Ruxing*: "those who agree he does not merely side with, those who differ (異) he does not condemn"; subcommentary: "though that man be estranged and different (異) from himself, if what he does is good he will not disparage him."
- *Strange (怪).* Shiming: "異 is what differs from the ordinary." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 26: "the duke found something strange (異) in it"; note: "異 is like 'strange.'" Shiji, *Biographies of Qu Yuan and Jia Yi*: "transformed into a strange thing (異物) — what is there to dread?"
- *Extraordinary (奇).* Zhouli, *Diguan, Zhiren*: "[the market-clerk] manages the wares of the completed market — goods, persons, oxen and horses, weapons, and rarities (珍異)"; note: "珍異 are the foods of the four seasons." Shiji, *Biographies of the Disciples of Confucius*: "of those who received instruction and mastered it in person there were seventy-seven, all men of extraordinary (異) ability."
- *To go against (違).*
- *A surname.* In the Tang, Yi Mouxun (異牟尋) submitted to the Tang and was invested as King of Nanzhao; the Baishui Man still bear this surname.
- *The name of a plant.* Erya, *Shicao*: "連 (lian) is *yiqiao* (異翹)."
- *wumingyi (無名異), a drug-name,* used chiefly for metal-wounds and fractures.
- *Yunbu*: rhymes as 怡 (yí), fanqie 延知. Shijing, *Beifeng*: "truly fair, and rare (異)" — rhyming with 貽 below.
- *Rhymes as 逸 (yì), fanqie 弋質.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "it only makes for estrangement (異)"; Zhu Xi's note: "fanqie 逸織." An anonymous *Song of Joy in Virtue*: "what is seen is wondrous and strange (異)" — rhyming with "sweet, fine wine and food."

**Textual notes.** In Zhouli, *Diguan, Zhiren*, "賞成市之貨賄" — checked against the original, 賞 should read 掌.
