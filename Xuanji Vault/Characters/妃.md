**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, lower; Woman radical 女; p.255 #22)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 芳非. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 芳微 — sounding like 霏 (fēi).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a mate, consort (匹)." Zongyao: "a woman who is a man's own companion. In antiquity, among the honored consorts, she who ranked next after the queen was called 妃." Liji, *Tan'gong*: "Shun was buried in the wilds of Cangwu, and his three consorts (三妃), it seems, did not follow him there."
- *Also, the principal wife of a crown prince is likewise called 妃.*
- *Also 天妃 (the Heavenly Consort), a water-deity.* Sima Guang says: "water is of the yin kind, so its spirit ought to be female."
- *Also 湘妃 (the Xiang Consort), a bamboo-name.* Its mottling is like tear-stains; that from Gula is the finest, that from within Mount Taoxu next. See the Zhupu.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 盈之, sounding like 怡 (yí); the same as 姬: a general term for the host of concubines.
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 滂佩, sounding like 配 (pèi). Liji, *Quli*: "the Son of Heaven's consort (妃) is called the queen (后)"; note: "妃 means a match (配)." Preface to the *Weifeng* ode "Mang": "she lost her mate (妃耦)." Former Han, *Annals of Empress Lü*: "Gaozu's consort (妃) in his lowly days." The same as 配. Sometimes written 𡝞; otherwise written 婜.
- *Also interchangeable with 婓*; see the note under the graph 婓.
