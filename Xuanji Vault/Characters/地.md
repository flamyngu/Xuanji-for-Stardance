## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集中〉【土字部】頁224第06    〔古文〕：埊、𨻐、埅、𠏂、嶳【廣韻】徒四切【集韻】大計切【韻會】徒二切【正韻】徒利切，𠀤音弟。【說文】元氣初分，重濁隂爲地，萬物所𨻰列也。【白虎通】地者，易也。言養萬物懷任交易變化也。【釋名】地，底也，其體底下，載萬物也。【易·說卦傳】坤爲地。【內經】岐伯曰：地爲人之下，太虛之中。黃帝曰：馮乎。曰：大氣舉之。【周禮·地官】土訓掌道地圖，以詔地事，道地慝，以辨地物，而原其生，以詔地求。【博物志】地以名山爲輔佐，石爲之骨，川爲之脈，艸木爲之毛，土爲之肉。　又第也，但也。【前漢·丙吉傳】西曹地忍之。　又叶徒何切，音沱。【屈原·橘頌】閉目自愼，終不失過兮。秉德無私，參天地兮。【揚雄·羽獵賦】鳥不及飛，獸不得過，軍驚師駭，刮野埽地。○按吳棫收地入箇韻，音隋，則過可如字讀，沱隋亦平去閒耳。本作坔。    考證：〔【內經】岐伯曰，地爲人之下，太虛之中。黃帝曰，何憑。曰，大氣舉之。〕　謹照原文何憑改馮乎。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.224 #06)*

**Ancient graphs.** 埊, 𨻐, 埅, 𠏂, 嶳.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 徒四. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 大計. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 徒二. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒利 — sounding like 弟 (dì).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "When the primal force first divided, the heavy and turbid yin became Earth (地), where the myriad things are set out in array." *Baihu tong*: "地 means change (易) — it nurtures the myriad things, bearing and conceiving them through interchange and transformation." *Shiming*: "地 means the bottom (底); its body is low beneath, and it bears the myriad things." Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "Kun is Earth (地)." *Neijing*: "Qibo said: 'Earth is below man, in the midst of the Great Void.' The Yellow Emperor said: 'does it lean on anything?' He said: 'the great breath holds it up.'" Zhouli, *Di guan*: "the Tuxun officer managed the telling of land-maps (地圖), to report on land-affairs; the telling of land-evils, to distinguish land-products and trace their growth, and to report on land-demands." *Bowu zhi*: "Earth has the famed mountains for its aides; rocks are its bones, rivers its veins, grass and trees its hair, soil its flesh."

**Senses.**
- *Only, merely (第, 但).* Former Han, *Biography of Bing Ji*: "the Western Bureau should merely (地) bear with it."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 沱 (tuó), fanqie 徒何.* Qu Yuan, *Ode to the Orange*: "with closed eyes you keep yourself wary, and in the end commit no fault (過); holding to virtue without self-interest, you stand third with Heaven and Earth (天地)." Yang Xiong, *Yu lie fu*: "the birds had no time to fly, the beasts could not escape (過); the host startled and the army shaken, they scoured the wilds and swept the ground (地)." A note: Wu Yu placed 地 in the 箇-rhyme, sounding it 隋 (suí), so that 過 may be read as written; 沱 and 隋 too hover between the level and departing tones. Properly written 坔.

**Textual note.** *Neijing*, "the Yellow Emperor said: 'on what does it rest (何憑)?'" — checked against the original, "何憑" should read "馮乎" ("does it lean on anything?").
