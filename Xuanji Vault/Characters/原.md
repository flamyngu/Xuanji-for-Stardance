**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Cliff radical 厂; p.162 #03)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠪥, 𨘡, 𠪰.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 愚袁, sounding like 元 (yuán).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "High and level ground is called 原, what men climb up to." Erya, *Shi di*: "a great wild is called 平, a broad level is called 原." Zhouli, *Di guan, Da situ*: "to distinguish the names and kinds of mountains, forests, marshes, dykes, lowlands, plateaus (原), and bottoms." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in the first month of summer, order the Wilds-Warden to go out over the fields and plains (田原) and, for the Son of Heaven, to comfort the farmers."

**Senses.**
- *Source, root (本).* Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "farming, fishing, trade, and merchandise — these four are the source (原) of clothing and food." (Former Han,) *Biography of Dong Zhongshu*: "the greatness of the Way takes its source (原) from Heaven." (Former Han,) *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "you roam the source (游原)"; note: Meng Kang, "原 means root."
- *Again, a second time (再).* Erya, *Shi yan*: "原 means again"; subcommentary: "to repeat, a second time." Yijing, *Bi* hexagram: "divine again (原筮): primal, ever-constant"; Zhu Xi's tradition: "one must divine a second time, examining oneself for the virtue of primal goodness, lasting length, and upright firmness." Liji, *Wen wang shizi*: "he charged the steward, saying, 'let there be no second serving (原)'"; note: "末 means not — the remnant of what has been eaten may not be served again." Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "they made the Pei palace the secondary shrine (原廟)"; note: Yan Shigu, "原 means again — the proper shrine being already set up, a further one was set up."
- *To trace back, infer (推原).* *Yun Hui*. Yijing, *Xi ci*: "to trace the beginning (原始) and seek the end." Former Han, *Biography of Xue Xuan*: "to fix guilt by tracing the heart (原心)"; note: Yan Shigu, "原 means to seek out the root." Guanzi, *Jie*: "to go out in spring and inspect (原) the farm-work that is not properly rooted is called touring"; note: "原 means to examine."
- *To pardon a crime is called 原.* Jinshu, *Biography of Pan Yue*: "it happened that an edict pardoned (原) him."
- *Interchangeable with 源 (a spring-source).* Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "like damming a river at its source (川原) to make a stagnant pool"; note: Yan Shigu, "原 means the root of the water-spring."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: Confucius's disciple Yuan Xian (原憲). Former Han, *Biography of Zhao Guanghan*: "the great clans of Yingchuan, the Yuan and the Chu (原褚)"; note: "Yuan and Chu are two surnames."
- *A prefecture-name.* *Yun Hui*: "the Han Gaoping county; under the Wei, Zhenzhou; renamed Yuanzhou (原州)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes (per the Yunbu), fanqie 虞雲.* Shiji, *Postface*: "King Huai died abroad, and Lan blamed Qu Yuan (屈原); fond of flattery and trusting slander, Chu was swallowed by Qin."
