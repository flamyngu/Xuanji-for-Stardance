**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.203 #21)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𨲠, 差.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 咨邪 — sounding like 罝 (jiē).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "to sigh, to lament (嗟歎)." *Guangyun*: "to sigh (咨)." *Ji Yun*: "one says, to grieve and regret (痛惜)." Erya, *Shigu*: "嗟 means to sigh (𨲠)." *Shiming*: "嗟 means to aid (佐): words do not suffice to exhaust the meaning, so one utters this sound to aid oneself." Shu, *Gan shi*: "the king said, 'Ah (嗟), you men of the six offices, I solemnly charge you.'" Shijing, *Zhounan*: "alas (嗟), I long for him." Also *Qifeng*: "ah, alas (猗嗟), how splendid!"; subcommentary: "猗 is the heart's disquiet within, 嗟 is the mouth's choked murmur — both are sounds of grieved sighing." Liji, *Tan'gong*: "'Hey (嗟), come and eat!'" *Guangyun*: the same as 𧨁. *Ji Yun*: originally written 𧪰, also written 𧪘.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 遭哥, sounding like 䦈 (jiē). Yijing, *Li*: "if he does not drum the jar and sing, then comes the sigh (嗟) of great age"; the Yinyi: "Wang Su reads it fanqie 遭哥." Ruan Ji, *Yonghuai shi*: "Lord Li grieved at the eastern gate, Master Su felt the Three Rivers close in; seeking benevolence and gaining it, why again sigh and lament (咨嗟)?"
- *Also* *Yunhui bu*: fanqie 子夜, sounding like 借 (jiè); the same as 唶. Sancang gu: "咄嗟 (in a trice) means easily done, as if to say within a single breath." The graph was originally written 唶. Of old there was a Duojie ge (咄唶歌). The Shishuo xinyu: "Shi Chong made bean-gruel and had it ready in a trice (咄嗟)."
