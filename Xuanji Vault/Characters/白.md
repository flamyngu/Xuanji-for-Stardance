**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; White radical 白; p.785 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𦣺, 𤼽.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 旁陌. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 薄陌 — sounding like 帛 (bái).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The colour of the west (西方色); when the yin holds sway, things take a white colour. It follows 入 joined to 二, 二 being the yin-number." *Shiming*: "白 means to open (啓), like the colour of water at the opening of the year." Erya, *Shi tian*: "autumn is the white storing (白藏)"; subcommentary: "autumn's breath being temperate, the colour is white, and there is gathering and storing." Zhouli, *Kao gong ji*: "in the work of painting and embroidery, the west is called white." Shu, *Yu gong*: "Jizhou — its soil is white loam; Qingzhou — its soil is white and rich." Also Liji, *Tan gong*: "the men of Yin prized white (白)."

**Senses.**
- *Plain; pure (素, 潔).* *Zengyun*. Yijing, *Bi* hexagram: "plain adornment, no fault (白賁)"; note: "its substance plain, not troubling with ornament." Also *Shuo gua*: "Xun is white (白)"; subcommentary: "the wind blows away dust, hence pure-white."
- *Bright (明).* Liji, *Zengzi wen*: "the light (白) of the household-head's place"; note: "the northwest corner, where the door gets light." Xunzi, *Zheng ming*: "if the teaching does not take, then make the way bright (白道) and rest in obscurity"; note: "白道 means the bright way." Former Han, *Biography of Gu Yong*: "to set aside the manifest crimes (白罪)"; note: "crimes plain and clear, all set aside."
- *白屋 (a thatched house), a house roofed with thatch.* Former Han, *Biography of Xiao Wangzhi*: "fearing it is not the Duke of Zhou's intent, who as chancellor to King Cheng came to the thatched houses (白屋)."
- *白衣, those who do the runners' work in offices.* Former Han, *Biography of the Two Gongs*: "I heard it from a commoner (白衣) — beware, sir, do not speak."
- *白徒, like 白身 (a man without rank).* Guanzi, *Cheng ma*: "thirty plain men (白徒) attend two carts."
- *白丁 (a commoner).* Beishi, *Biography of Li Min*: "Emperor Xuan of Zhou said to Princess Leping: 'what office has Min?' She answered: 'a mere commoner (白丁).'"
- *白民.* Weishu, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "Emperor Zhuang promulgated the grain-contribution rule: a commoner (白民) contributing five hundred dan was allowed an entry-rank accordingly."
- *白著.* Tang shu, *Biography of Liu Yan*: "what is taken crosswise beyond the tax is called baizhuo (白著)." *Chunming tuichao lu*: "the world calls deep drunkenness 白著, meaning that after harshness a man surely stumbles, dead-drunk like one drowned in drink."
- *Also* Liji, *Yu zao*: "the lord wears the fox-white fur (狐白裘)"; Chen's note: "made of the white fur from the fox's belly."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi qi*: "white metal (白金) is called silver."
- *Also* Tang shu, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "in late Sui they circulated the five-zhu white coins (白錢)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Punishments*: "the convicts are the white-grinders (白粲)"; note: "set to pick rice till it is pure white — a three-year punishment."
- *Also* *Gujin zhu*: "the white brush (白筆), the old ear-tucked brush, to show the gentleman is furnished with both civil and martial."
- *Also* *Zixue yuanyuan*: "the flying-white script (飛白書), made by Cai Yong on seeing a whitewash-brush."
- *A star-name.* Boya: "Taibai (太白) is called the Long Geng."
- *A banner-name.* Liji, *Ming tang wei*: "the Great White (大白) of Yin."
- *A penalty-cup name.* *Shuoyuan*: "Marquis Wen of Wei, drinking with his grandees, made Gongsheng Buren master of the toasts, saying: 'whoever does not drain his cup, fine him with the Great White (大白).'"
- *A wine-name.* Liji, *Nei ze*: "the wine, clear and white (淸白)"; note: "the white is the affair-wine, the aged wine; both white in colour, hence named by white."
- *Also* rice is called white (白), millet black. Zhouli, *Tian guan, Bian ren*: "their contents: the fried wheat, the hemp, the white, and the black."
- *A horse-name.* Shijing, *Qin feng*: "there is a horse white-browed (白顚)"; subcommentary: "with white hair on the forehead — today's star-browed horse."
- *A fierce-beast name.* *Jizhong Zhoushu*: "the Yiqu offered the zibai (茲白)"; note: "the zibai, also called the bo (駁), can eat tigers and leopards."
- *An insect-name.* Erya, *Shi chong*: "the silverfish, white-fish (白魚)"; note: "the worm in clothes and books." Also Da Dai li: "the white birds (白鳥) are the gnats and mosquitoes."
- *A plant-name.* Former Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "Shanshan has much white grass (白草)."
- *三白 (the three whites), the first-month snow.* A northwestern farm-proverb: "for fit wheat, look for the three whites (三白)."
- *五白 (the five whites), the five gaming-pieces.* Song Yu, *Zhao hun*: "making the owl and seizing the lead, calling 'five white (五白)!'"
- *In Sanskrit, one year is one white (一白).* *Chuandeng lu*: "I have dwelt in the forest, already nine whites (九白)."
- *A mountain-name.* Later Han, *Biography of Geng Gong*: "Dou Gu earlier struck the White Mountain (白山), his merit foremost in the three armies"; note: "winter and summer it has snow, hence White Mountain." Jinshi, *Treatise on Rites*: "the officials said: Mount Changbai (長白山) is in the land where the dynasty arose; by rite it should be honoured."
- *A water-name.* Sang Qin, *Shuijing*: "the Bai water (白水) issues west of Zhaoyang county."
- *A prefecture-name.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Geography*: "in the fourth year of Wude, Baizhou (白州) was set up, named from the Bobai Brook."
- *Beyond the seas there is the White-folk Kingdom (白民國).* Seen in the Shanhai jing.
- *白狄 (the White Di), a separate name of the Di.* Seen in Chunqiu, Cheng yr 9.
- *Of the six kinds of Rong, one is the Old White (老白).* Seen in the *Fengsu tong*.
- *Surname.* Descendants of the Yellow Emperor. The Zuozhuan: the Qin grandee Bai Yibing. Also compound surnames: Shiji, *Basic Annals of Qin*: "the Baiming (白冥) clan, of the Qin lineage." *Qianfu lun*: "the Jibai (吉白) clan, descendants of the Shen surname." Also Baiyangti (白楊提), a three-graph surname of the northern marches.
- *In posthumous titles (諡法):* "upright and recovered within and without is called 白."
- *To tell, to report (告語).* *Yupian*. *Zhengzitong*: "to report upward to a superior is 稟白; for peers to relate matters and set forth meaning is also 白." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Gao*: "the Emperor had Zhou Chang pick out the stout men of Zhao fit to be generals, and four were presented (白見)." Later Han, *Biography of Zhong Hao*: "Zhong Jin often reported (白) Li Ying's words to Hao."
- *Also* Tang shu, *Biography of the Eunuchs*: "in Xuanzong's time, the circuits yearly presented castrated boys, called the private-whites (私白)." A note: in the *Shuowen* the entering tone has a 白 radical, and the departing-tone 自 radical also lists a 白 graph; the one in the 自 radical is read fanqie 疾二, "this too is the graph 自" — the abbreviated 自, the breath of speech coming from the nose to aid the mouth. So the 白 of "to tell" is read 自, and the 白 of "the western colour" is read 帛 — sound and sense distinct; Xu divided them into two radicals. The *Yupian* merged them into one, which is here followed.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 步化, sounding like 杷 (pà). Also the colour of the west.
- *Also* fanqie 博陌 — same as 伯 (eldest). "Senior, eldest (長)." One account: a noble title. Also a surname. *Yinsou*: "there is a Bailuan (白鸞) clan"; note: "that is the graph 伯."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 薄 (bó), fanqie 旁各.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "splendid those blossoms, some yellow, some white (白); I see this man, riding his four piebalds."
- *Rhymes as 弼 (bì), fanqie 房密.* Su Shi, *Cold-food rain poem*: "in the dark it is stolen and borne away, at midnight there is real strength; how unlike a sick youth, who from sickness rises with hair already white (白)." 𦣹 is the old graph for 自, fanqie 疾二.

**Textual note.** Liji, *Ming tang wei*, "the Great White (太白) of Yin" — checked against the original, "太白" should read "大白".
