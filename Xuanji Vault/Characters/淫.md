**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.631 #09)*

**Ancient graph.** 㸒.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 余箴. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 夷針 — sounding like 霪 (yín).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "浸淫 (to seep and spread), following the grain (理)." Xu [Kai] says: "to soak and steep, following its veins and grain." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the good dike-builder lets the water seep (淫) into it"; note: "it means letting silt seep and ooze (淫液) so as to thicken it."
- *Also, to give free rein (放).* Liji, *Aigong wen*: "unrestrained in conduct (淫德) and never wearying."
- *Also, greedy, to excess (貪).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the sound runs to excess (淫), reaching the shang note."
- *Also, to overflow (溢); to exceed (過).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "be not excessive (淫) in pleasure."
- *Also, extreme (甚).* Liezi, *Huangdi*: "the Yellow Emperor said, 'my faults are excessive (淫) indeed.'"
- *Also, great (大).* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "there is great (淫) majesty."
- *Also, to usurp, to presume (僭).* Wu yu: "he seized kingship over the Eastern Sea and made his presumptuous (淫) name heard by the Son of Heaven."
- *Also, long-lasting (久).* Jin yu: "stuck fast and long delayed (滯淫)"; note: "滯 is to be held up, 淫 is long-protracted."
- *Also, depraved (邪).* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "his will is corrupt (淫) and he loves the perverse."
- *Also* Liji, *Quli*: "do not let the gaze rove (淫視)"; subcommentary: "it means the eyes drifting and shifting."
- *Also* Xiao Erya: "when man and woman couple without ritual propriety, it is called 淫 (lewdness)."
- *Also, when the Year-star strays from its station it is likewise called 淫.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 28: "the Year-star was in Xingji, yet strayed (淫) into Xuanxiao."
- *Also 淫淫, the look of going far away.* Yang Xiong, *Yulie fu*: "streaming away and crowding on (淫淫與與)"; note: "the look of coming and going."
- *Also a water-name.* Huainanzi, *Lanming*: "Nüwa heaped reed-ash to stem the flood-waters (淫水)"; note: "water welling up on level ground is 淫."
- *Also a spring-name.* Wang Zinian, *Shiyi ji*: "south of Rinan there is the shore of the Yin Spring (淫泉)."
- *Also a tree-name.* Cailan zazhi: "the country of Xundun has the yin-tree (淫樹)."
- *Also a fish-name.* Huainanzi, *Shuoshan*: "when Hu Ba played the zither, the yin-fish (淫魚) came forth to listen."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 以贍, sounding like 豔 (yàn). In eastern Ba there is the Yinyu Mound (淫預堆). An old song: "Yinyu (淫預), big as a horse." Now written 灩澦.
- *Also read as 涅 (niè).* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the Hu masters, in scouring silk, steep (淫) it with clam-ash"; note: "淫 should be read 涅."
- *Also read as 瑤 (yáo).* Shanhai jing: "there are the Yin waters (淫水), clear and rippling"; note: "淫 is pronounced 遙, the same as 瑤."

**Textual notes.** The gloss "星記" should be 歲星 (the Year-star); and in "歲在星記" the 記 should read 紀 (per the original). Also, in the Kaogong ji citation, 㡛人 should read 㡛氏 (per the original).
