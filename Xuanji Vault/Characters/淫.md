**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls е·і, upper; Water radical ж°ҙ; p.631 #09)*

**Ancient graph.** гё’.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie дҪҷз®ҙ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еӨ·йҮқ вҖ” sounding like йңӘ (yГӯn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "жөёж·« (to seep and spread), following the grain (зҗҶ)." Xu [Kai] says: "to soak and steep, following its veins and grain." Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the good dike-builder lets the water seep (ж·«) into it"; note: "it means letting silt seep and ooze (ж·«ж¶І) so as to thicken it."
- *Also, to give free rein (ж”ҫ).* Liji, *Aigong wen*: "unrestrained in conduct (ж·«еҫ·) and never wearying."
- *Also, greedy, to excess (иІӘ).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the sound runs to excess (ж·«), reaching the shang note."
- *Also, to overflow (жәў); to exceed (йҒҺ).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "be not excessive (ж·«) in pleasure."
- *Also, extreme (з”ҡ).* Liezi, *Huangdi*: "the Yellow Emperor said, 'my faults are excessive (ж·«) indeed.'"
- *Also, great (еӨ§).* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "there is great (ж·«) majesty."
- *Also, to usurp, to presume (еғӯ).* Wu yu: "he seized kingship over the Eastern Sea and made his presumptuous (ж·«) name heard by the Son of Heaven."
- *Also, long-lasting (д№…).* Jin yu: "stuck fast and long delayed (ж»Ҝж·«)"; note: "ж»Ҝ is to be held up, ж·« is long-protracted."
- *Also, depraved (йӮӘ).* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "his will is corrupt (ж·«) and he loves the perverse."
- *Also* Liji, *Quli*: "do not let the gaze rove (ж·«иҰ–)"; subcommentary: "it means the eyes drifting and shifting."
- *Also* Xiao Erya: "when man and woman couple without ritual propriety, it is called ж·« (lewdness)."
- *Also, when the Year-star strays from its station it is likewise called ж·«.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 28: "the Year-star was in Xingji, yet strayed (ж·«) into Xuanxiao."
- *Also ж·«ж·«, the look of going far away.* Yang Xiong, *Yulie fu*: "streaming away and crowding on (ж·«ж·«иҲҮиҲҮ)"; note: "the look of coming and going."
- *Also a water-name.* Huainanzi, *Lanming*: "NГјwa heaped reed-ash to stem the flood-waters (ж·«ж°ҙ)"; note: "water welling up on level ground is ж·«."
- *Also a spring-name.* Wang Zinian, *Shiyi ji*: "south of Rinan there is the shore of the Yin Spring (ж·«жіү)."
- *Also a tree-name.* Cailan zazhi: "the country of Xundun has the yin-tree (ж·«жЁ№)."
- *Also a fish-name.* Huainanzi, *Shuoshan*: "when Hu Ba played the zither, the yin-fish (ж·«йӯҡ) came forth to listen."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie д»ҘиҙҚ, sounding like иұ” (yГ n). In eastern Ba there is the Yinyu Mound (ж·«й җе Ҷ). An old song: "Yinyu (ж·«й җ), big as a horse." Now written зҒ©жҫҰ.
- *Also read as ж¶… (niГЁ).* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the Hu masters, in scouring silk, steep (ж·«) it with clam-ash"; note: "ж·« should be read ж¶…."
- *Also read as з‘Ө (yГЎo).* Shanhai jing: "there are the Yin waters (ж·«ж°ҙ), clear and rippling"; note: "ж·« is pronounced йҒҷ, the same as з‘Ө."

**Textual notes.** The gloss "жҳҹиЁҳ" should be жӯІжҳҹ (the Year-star); and in "жӯІеңЁжҳҹиЁҳ" the иЁҳ should read зҙҖ (per the original). Also, in the Kaogong ji citation, гЎӣдәә should read гЎӣж°Ҹ (per the original).
