> 《康熙字典》〈未集下〉【臣字部】頁999第13    〔古文〕：𢘑【唐韻】植鄰切【集韻】【韻會】丞眞切，𠀤音辰。事人之稱。【說文】臣，牽也，事君也。象屈服之形。【白虎通】臣者，纏也，勵志自堅固也。【廣韻】伏也。仕於公曰臣，任於家曰僕。【易·序卦】有父子，然後有君臣，有君臣，然後有上下。【詩·小雅】率土之濱，莫非王臣。　又【前漢·王陵傳】陳平謝曰：主臣。【註】文穎曰：惶恐之辭，猶今言死罪。晉灼曰：主，擊也。臣，服也。言其擊服。惶恐之辭。【通雅】發語敬謝之辭，猶主在上，臣在下，自然敬恐也。　又姓。【奇姓通】唐臣悅，著平陳紀。　又【韻補】叶音禪。【道藏歌】躋景西那東，肆覲善因緣。常融無地官，皆是聖皇臣。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Minister radical 臣; p.999 #13)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢘑.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 植鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 丞眞 — sounding like 辰 (chén). A term for one who serves another.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "臣 means to be led, drawn (牽), to serve the lord; it images a form of submission." *Baihu tong*: "臣 means to be bound (纏), spurring the will to make oneself firm." *Guangyun*: "to bow down (伏); serving in public office is 臣, serving in a household is 僕 (a servant)." Yijing, *Xu gua*: "there being father and son, thereafter there is lord and minister (君臣); there being lord and minister, thereafter there is high and low." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "to the very margins of the land, none is not the king's minister (王臣)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Former Han, *Biography of Wang Ling*: "Chen Ping demurred, saying '主臣'"; note: Wen Ying, "a phrase of dread, like saying 'a capital offence' now"; Jin Zhuo, "主 means to strike, 臣 means to submit — meaning to be struck into submission; a phrase of dread." *Tongya*: "an opening phrase of respectful apology — as the master (主) is above and the minister (臣) below, so there is naturally respect and awe."
- *Surname.* *Qixing tong*: "Chen Yue of the Tang, who wrote the *Annals of the Pacification of Chen*."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 禪 (chán).* *Daozang ge*: "rising in radiance west of Na and east, freely they audience the good karmic bonds; ever blending, without earth-officers, all are the sage-sovereign's ministers (臣)."
