**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, lower; Minister radical иҮЈ; p.999 #13)*

**Ancient graph.** рўҳ‘.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жӨҚй„°. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie дёһзңһ вҖ” sounding like иҫ° (chГ©n). A term for one who serves another.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "иҮЈ means to be led, drawn (зүҪ), to serve the lord; it images a form of submission." *Baihu tong*: "иҮЈ means to be bound (зәҸ), spurring the will to make oneself firm." *Guangyun*: "to bow down (дјҸ); serving in public office is иҮЈ, serving in a household is еғ• (a servant)." Yijing, *Xu gua*: "there being father and son, thereafter there is lord and minister (еҗӣиҮЈ); there being lord and minister, thereafter there is high and low." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "to the very margins of the land, none is not the king's minister (зҺӢиҮЈ)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Former Han, *Biography of Wang Ling*: "Chen Ping demurred, saying 'дё»иҮЈ'"; note: Wen Ying, "a phrase of dread, like saying 'a capital offence' now"; Jin Zhuo, "дё» means to strike, иҮЈ means to submit вҖ” meaning to be struck into submission; a phrase of dread." *Tongya*: "an opening phrase of respectful apology вҖ” as the master (дё») is above and the minister (иҮЈ) below, so there is naturally respect and awe."
- *Surname.* *Qixing tong*: "Chen Yue of the Tang, who wrote the *Annals of the Pacification of Chen*."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as зҰӘ (chГЎn).* *Daozang ge*: "rising in radiance west of Na and east, freely they audience the good karmic bonds; ever blending, without earth-officers, all are the sage-sovereign's ministers (иҮЈ)."
