**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Step radical 彳; p.365 #32)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢔎, 𨓹.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 于兩. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 雨兩. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 羽枉 — the rising tone of 王 (wǎng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To go (之)." *Yupian*: "to proceed (行); to depart (去)." Yijing, *Lü* hexagram: "plain in his treading, he goes (往) without fault." Shu, *Shun dian*: "the Emperor said: 'Yes — you, go (往)!'" Liji, *Qu li*: "rites esteem reciprocity (往來); to go and not be met is not propriety; to be met and not to go is also not propriety."

**Senses.**
- *Of old; the past (古往, 昔).* *Yupian*; *Guangyun*. Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the Yijing makes plain the past (往) and examines the future." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "consulting the ancient past (往古), making it fit for the present."
- *To touch briefly on past matters is 往往.* Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "reaching the elders, all here and there (往往) named the places of the Yellow Emperor, Yao, and Shun; their customs and teachings were indeed distinct."
- *In general, to send a thing to a person is 往.* Cao Zhi, *Letter to Yang Xiu*: "now I send (往) one set of the rhapsodies and prose I wrote when small, for us to revise together." Wang Xizhi's note: "now I send (往) a length of silk-cloth, a single bolt, to show my regard."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 於放, the departing tone of 王 (wàng). To turn and look toward (歸嚮). Shiji, *Eulogy in the Hereditary House of Confucius*: "though I cannot reach it, my heart turns longingly toward (鄕往) it."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 勇 (yǒng), fanqie 尹竦.* Dongfang Shuo, *Qi jian*: "strike the gong, and the gong answers; pluck the jue, and the jue stirs; the tiger roars, and the valley-wind rises; the dragon comes, and the brocade clouds go (往)."
