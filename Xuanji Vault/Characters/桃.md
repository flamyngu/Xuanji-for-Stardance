**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Tree radical 木; p.525 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒刀 — sounding like 陶 (táo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a fruit." Erya, *Shimu*: "the mao (旄) is the winter peach (冬桃); the si (榹) is the mountain peach (山桃)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-spring the peach (桃) first blossoms." *Neize*: "of the peach it is said, 'gall (膽) it'"; subcommentary: "the peach has much fuzz; one wipes and dresses it green and smooth like a gall-bladder." Also 桃諸; Wang Su says: "諸 is a pickle (菹) — what we now call preserved peach (藏桃)."
- *Also* Dianshu: "the peach is the essence of the five trees, an immortal wood." Liji, *Tan'gong*: "when a ruler attends a minister's funeral, the shaman-invocator bears peach-wood and reeds (桃茢) and grasps a dagger-axe." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 4: "a peach-wood bow and thorn arrows (桃弧棘矢), to ward off calamity." Later Han, *Treatise on Ceremonial*: "they made peach-wood seals (桃印) and set them at the doors to stay noxious vapors."
- *Also 含桃 is the cherry (櫻桃).* Erya: "it is called the xie (楔)."
- *Also 桃氏 (the Tao masters), the metal-working artisans.* Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji*: "the Tao masters (桃氏) make blades."
- *Also 胡桃 (the walnut).* Mingwu zhi: "it is called the Qiang peach (羌桃)."
- *Also the yaoyi (銚芅) is called the yangtao (羊桃).* Erya, *Shicao*: "the changchu (萇楚) is the yaoyi (銚芅)."
- *Also 桃枝, a bamboo-name.* Erya, *Shicao*: "the taozhi (桃枝) bamboo has a joint every four inches."
- *Also* Bencao: the zhuling fungus (豬苓) is called diwutao (地烏桃).
- *Also 桃蟲, the wren (鷦), also called the qiaofu (巧婦, "deft-wife").* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "truly that little wren (桃蟲) — yet flapping up, it is a great bird."
- *Also 桃林 (Taolin), a place-name, east of Mount Hua.* Shu, *Wu cheng*: "he turned the oxen loose in the wilds of Taolin (桃林)." Also 老桃 (Laotao), a place in Song; Zuozhuan, Yin yr 10: "the duke met the Marquis of Qi and the Earl of Zheng at Laotao (老桃)."
- *Also a water-name.* Shanhai jing: "from Mount Leyou the Tao waters (桃水) issue."
- *Also a surname.* Xingyuan: "Tao Ying of the Warring States, Tao Bao of the Jin."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 他彫, sounding like 祧 (tiāo): a long handle, by which to lift things from within a vessel.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 上與, sounding like 墅 (shù): a vessel for ladling things out.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 直紹, sounding like 趙 (zhào); the same as 㸠. Boya: "a board (板)."

**Textual notes.** On "Erya: written 楔" — since 含桃 is also named 楔, this is no graphic interchange; corrected to read "Erya: it is called the xie (楔)."
