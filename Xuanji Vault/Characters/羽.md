**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, middle; Feather radical зҫҪ; p.955 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie зҺӢзҹ© вҖ” sounding like зҰ№ (yЗ”).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the long feathers of a bird." *Guangyun*: "a bird's wing." Yijing, *Jian*: "its feathers (зҫҪ) may be used for ceremonial display." Shu, *Yu gong*: "teeth, hides, feathers (зҫҪ), and hair"; tradition: "зҫҪ is bird-feathers." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Paoren*: "in winter serve fresh fish and feathered game (зҫҪ)"; note: "зҫҪ here is the wild goose."
- *Also* Diguan, *Situ*: "suited to feathered creatures (зҫҪзү©)"; note: "of the pheasant kind." Liji, *Yue ling*: "its creatures are the feathered (зҫҪ)"; note: "it images things stirred by the wind like rustling leaves вҖ” the flying-bird kind."
- *Also one of the five notes.* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Da shi*: "all are patterned with the five notes: gong, shang, jue, zhi, yu (зҫҪ)."
- *Also* Da sile: "in all music, the Yuanzhong is the gong, Huangzhong the jue, Dacu the zhi, Guxian the yu (зҫҪ)"; note: "of the five notes generated from the gong, the muddier is the jue, the clearer the zhi and yu." Liji, *Yue ling*: "its note is yu (зҫҪ)"; note: "the yu's number is forty-eight; belonging to Water, it is taken as the image of the clearest of things."
- *Also* Liji, *Yue ji*: "gong is the ruler, shang the minister, jue the people, zhi affairs, yu (зҫҪ) things." Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "зҫҪ means a canopy (е®Ү): things gathered and stored, with a canopy over them."
- *Also what dancers hold.* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "they danced with shields and plumes (зҫҪ) between the two stairways"; tradition: "зҫҪ is a screen-fan, held by the dancers." Zhouli, *Diguan, Wushi*: "he teaches the plume-dance (зҫҪиҲһ)"; note: "the зҫҪ is made of split white feathers, shaped like a fu-banner." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 5: "for the first time six rows of plumes (е…ӯзҫҪ) were presented. The duke asked the multitude about the number of plumes, and Zhongzhong replied, 'the Son of Heaven uses eight, the feudal lords six, the grandees four.'"
- *Also a mountain-name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "he banished Gun to Mount Yu (зҫҪеұұ)"; tradition: "Mount Yu, on the eastern margin, lies in the sea." Also *Yu gong*: "Meng and Yu (зҫҪ) were made fit for sowing"; subcommentary: "Mount Yu is south of Zhuqi county in the Eastern Sea commandery." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*, note: "Mount Yu is within the bounds of Linyi county in Yizhou."
- *Also a star-name.* Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "to its south are many stars called the Yulin Heavenly Army (зҫҪжһ—еӨ©и»Қ)"; note: "the Yulin (зҫҪжһ—) is thirty-five stars, clustered three by three, south of the Ramparts вҖ” the Heavenly Army."
- *Also an office-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Officials*: "the Qimen and the Yulin (зҫҪжһ—)"; note: Yan Shigu says: "the Yulin is likewise a guards' office; it says they are swift as feathers and many as a forest. One account: зҫҪ means they are the king's wings (зҫҪзҝј)."
- *Also a surname.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 30: "Yu Jie (зҫҪй Ў) fled to Jin."
- *Also* Shanhai jing: "the Land of the Feathered Folk (зҫҪж°‘еңӢ), whose people have long necks and grow feathers (зҫҪ) on their bodies."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie зҺӢйҒҮ, sounding like йӣ© (yГә); same sense.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie еҫҢдә”, sounding like жҲё (hГ№): to slacken, to ease (з·©). Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji, Shiren*: "dividing its length into five, they feather (зҫҪ) one part of it."
