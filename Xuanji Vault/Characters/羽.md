**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Feather radical 羽; p.955 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 王矩 — sounding like 禹 (yǔ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the long feathers of a bird." *Guangyun*: "a bird's wing." Yijing, *Jian*: "its feathers (羽) may be used for ceremonial display." Shu, *Yu gong*: "teeth, hides, feathers (羽), and hair"; tradition: "羽 is bird-feathers." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Paoren*: "in winter serve fresh fish and feathered game (羽)"; note: "羽 here is the wild goose."
- *Also* Diguan, *Situ*: "suited to feathered creatures (羽物)"; note: "of the pheasant kind." Liji, *Yue ling*: "its creatures are the feathered (羽)"; note: "it images things stirred by the wind like rustling leaves — the flying-bird kind."
- *Also one of the five notes.* Zhouli, *Chunguan, Da shi*: "all are patterned with the five notes: gong, shang, jue, zhi, yu (羽)."
- *Also* Da sile: "in all music, the Yuanzhong is the gong, Huangzhong the jue, Dacu the zhi, Guxian the yu (羽)"; note: "of the five notes generated from the gong, the muddier is the jue, the clearer the zhi and yu." Liji, *Yue ling*: "its note is yu (羽)"; note: "the yu's number is forty-eight; belonging to Water, it is taken as the image of the clearest of things."
- *Also* Liji, *Yue ji*: "gong is the ruler, shang the minister, jue the people, zhi affairs, yu (羽) things." Former Han, *Treatise on Pitch and Calendar*: "羽 means a canopy (宇): things gathered and stored, with a canopy over them."
- *Also what dancers hold.* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "they danced with shields and plumes (羽) between the two stairways"; tradition: "羽 is a screen-fan, held by the dancers." Zhouli, *Diguan, Wushi*: "he teaches the plume-dance (羽舞)"; note: "the 羽 is made of split white feathers, shaped like a fu-banner." Zuozhuan, Yin yr 5: "for the first time six rows of plumes (六羽) were presented. The duke asked the multitude about the number of plumes, and Zhongzhong replied, 'the Son of Heaven uses eight, the feudal lords six, the grandees four.'"
- *Also a mountain-name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "he banished Gun to Mount Yu (羽山)"; tradition: "Mount Yu, on the eastern margin, lies in the sea." Also *Yu gong*: "Meng and Yu (羽) were made fit for sowing"; subcommentary: "Mount Yu is south of Zhuqi county in the Eastern Sea commandery." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*, note: "Mount Yu is within the bounds of Linyi county in Yizhou."
- *Also a star-name.* Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "to its south are many stars called the Yulin Heavenly Army (羽林天軍)"; note: "the Yulin (羽林) is thirty-five stars, clustered three by three, south of the Ramparts — the Heavenly Army."
- *Also an office-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Officials*: "the Qimen and the Yulin (羽林)"; note: Yan Shigu says: "the Yulin is likewise a guards' office; it says they are swift as feathers and many as a forest. One account: 羽 means they are the king's wings (羽翼)."
- *Also a surname.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 30: "Yu Jie (羽頡) fled to Jin."
- *Also* Shanhai jing: "the Land of the Feathered Folk (羽民國), whose people have long necks and grow feathers (羽) on their bodies."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 王遇, sounding like 雩 (yú); same sense.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie 後五, sounding like 戸 (hù): to slacken, to ease (緩). Zhouli, *Dongguan, Kaogong ji, Shiren*: "dividing its length into five, they feather (羽) one part of it."
