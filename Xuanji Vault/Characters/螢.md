**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls з”і, middle; Insect radical иҷ«; p.1093 #45)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жҲёжүғ. *Yun Hui*: fanqie дә’жүғ вҖ” sounding like зҶ’ (yГӯng).

**Etymology and senses.** The name of the firefly (зҒ«иҹІ). Liji, *Yue ling*: "the rotting grass becomes fireflies (иһў)." Erya, *Shichong*: "the firefly (иһўзҒ«) is the jizhao (еҚізӮӨ)"; note: "it flies by night, with a fire beneath its belly." Gujin zhu: "the firefly is also called the night-glower (иҖҖеӨң), the sky-light (жҷҜеӨ©), the glimmering (зҶ зҮҝ), the cinnabar-fair (дё№иүҜ), the will-o'-the-wisp (зҮҗ), the cinnabar-bird (дё№йіҘ), the night-shiner (еӨңе…ү), and the night-candle (е®өзҮӯ)." Piya: "the firefly has no stomach yet is bred." Jin shu, *Biography of Che Yin*: "his house being poor, he could not always get oil; in summer he would fill a silk bag with several dozen fireflies (иһўзҒ«) to light his reading."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie дәҺе№і, sounding like жҰ® (rГіng); same sense; sometimes written р§“Ң.
