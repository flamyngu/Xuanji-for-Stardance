> 《康熙字典》〈丑集中〉【土字部】頁224第31【唐韻】居勻切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】規倫切，𠀤音鈞。【說文】平也。【詩·小推】大夫不均。【周禮·地官·大司徒】以土均之法，均齊天下之政。【春官】軍禮有五二日大均之禮，恤衆也。　又調也。【詩·小雅】六轡旣均。　又徧也。【易·說卦】坤爲均。【莊子·寓言篇】萬物皆種也。以不同形相禪，始卒循環，莫得其倫，是謂天均。　又天子設四代之學，曰成均，見【禮·文王世子註】。　又造瓦之具，旋轉者也。董仲舒曰：泥之在均，惟甄者之所爲。　又樂器。【禮·樂記】樂所以立均。【尚書疏】堂上之樂，皆受笙均。堂下之樂，皆受磬均。【後漢·律歷志】冬夏至，𨻰八音，聽五均。【註】均，長七尺，繫以絲，以節樂音。　又均服，戎服也。【左傳·僖五年】均服振振。亦作袀。　又地名。均，古麇國，在襄陽。【唐書·中宗紀】嗣聖元年，太后遷帝房州。又遷于均。　又【集韻】王問切【正韻】禹慍切，𠀤音韻。【說文先訓】古無韻字，均卽韻也。　又與專切，音沿。【史記·夏本紀】均河海，通淮泗。【鄭元曰】均讀沿。　又叶古頑切，音鰥。【韓愈·孟郊失子詩】問天生下人，薄厚胡不均。夫曰天地人，由來不相關。　又通作鈞。【書·泰誓】厥罪惟鈞。　又或作旬。【禮·內則】旬而見。【周禮·地官】均人公旬用三日。【註】均古通旬。別作𡉲。    考證：〔【周禮·地官·大司徒】以土均之法，鈞齊天下之政。〕　謹照原文鈞改均。〔又地名。均，古麋國，在襄陽。〕　謹按春秋左傳有麇國無麋國，麋改麇。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Earth radical 土; p.224 #31)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 居勻. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 規倫 — sounding like 鈞 (jūn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "level, even (平)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the grandees are not impartial (均)." Zhouli, *Diguan, Da situ*: "by the method of land-equalizing (土均) he evens (均) the governance of the realm." Chunguan: "the military rites are five; the second is the rite of the Great Levy (大均), to relieve the host."
- *To tune, attune (調).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the six reins are well-tuned (均)."
- *All, throughout (徧).* Yijing, *Shuogua*: "Kun is the even (均)." Zhuangzi, *Yuyan*: "the myriad things are all seeds; in unlike forms they hand on one to another; beginning and end a circling round, none can grasp its order — this is called the Heavenly Equality (天均)."
- *成均, the school of the four dynasties.* See the note to Liji, *Wen wang shizi*.
- *A potter's wheel.* Dong Zhongshu: "the clay on the wheel (均) is what the potter makes of it."
- *A tuning-standard.* Liji, *Yueji*: "music is that by which the pitch-standard (均) is set." Shu subcommentary: "the hall-music all takes its pitch from the reed-organ standard (笙均); the courtyard-music from the chime-stone standard (磬均)." Later Han, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes*: "at the solstices, the eight timbres are set out and the five standards (五均) heard"; note: "the *jun* is seven *chi* long, strung with silk, to regulate the music's tones."
- *均服, war-dress (also 袀).* Zuozhuan, Xi yr 5: "in war-dress (均服), thronging."
- *A place-name.* Jun, the ancient state of Qun, at Xiangyang. Tangshu, *Annals of Zhongzong*: "in the first year of Sisheng, the Empress Dowager removed the Emperor to Fangzhou, then to Jun."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 王問; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 禹慍 — sounding like 韻 (yùn). The Shuowen xianxun: "anciently there was no graph 韻; 均 was just 韻 (rhyme)."
- *Fanqie 與專, sounding like 沿 (yán).* Shiji, *Annals of Xia*: "following (均) the rivers and sea, he opened the Huai and Si"; Zheng Xuan: "均 is read 沿."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 古頑, sounding like 鰥 (guān).* Han Yu, *Poem on Meng Jiao's losing his son*: "I ask Heaven, in begetting men below, why thin and thick are not made even (均)."

**On the graph.** Interchangeable with 鈞: Shu, *Taishi*: "their guilt is equal (鈞)." Sometimes written 旬: Liji, *Neize*: "after a ten-day round (旬) the child is presented"; Zhouli, *Diguan*: "on public labour the ten-day round (旬) uses three days"; note: "均 anciently is interchangeable with 旬." Also written 𡉲.

**Textual notes.** "鈞齊天下之政" should read "均齊"; "古麋國" should read "古麇國".
