**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, upper; Jade radical 玉; p.733 #04)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 良止. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 兩耳. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 良以 — sounding like 里 (lǐ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To work jade (治玉)." Xu (Kai): "of the grain and veining (脈理) of things, jade is the finest, hence it follows 玉." Huainanzi, *Lan ming*: "in the time of Jie of Xia, the heaped metal lost its edge, and the jade-discs were piled without grain (無理)"; note: "from too much vexatious use, they were all blunted and unpatterned."

**Senses.**
- *To govern (治).* *Shuowen*, Xu's note: "the working of jade and the governing of the people are both called 理." Shu, *Zhou guan*: "to expound the Way and order the state, harmonizing and regulating (理) yin and yang." Former Han, *Biography of the Upright Officials*: "governance even and litigation well-ordered (訟理)."
- *Upright, correct (正).* *Yupian*. Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 2: "the former kings bounded and ordered (疆理) the realm"; note: "理 means to set straight."
- *The Way; principle (道, 義理).* *Yupian*; *Guangyun*. Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the Yijing being plain, the principles (理) of the world are grasped." Shiji, *Biography of the Lord of Pingyuan*: "he said to Gongsun Long: 'sir, do not contend again with Kong Zigao over matters; that man's reasoning (理) outdoes his words, and your words outdo your reasoning; when words outdo reasoning, in the end one is sure to be confounded.'" *Huangji jingshi*: "the numbers of the world issue from principle (理); to violate principle is to fall into mere technique (術); worldly men, by numbers, fall into technique, and so lose hold of principle."
- *Nature (性).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the Heavenly principle (天理) is destroyed"; note: "理 is like nature."
- *Order, articulation (條理).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "looking down, he examines the patterns of the earth (地理)"; subcommentary: "the earth has its mountains, rivers, uplands, and lowlands, each with its order (條理), hence called 理." Also *Shuo gua*: "in concord with the Way and its virtue, and ordered (理) in righteousness." Liji, *Zhongyong*: "patterned and ordered, close and discerning (文理密察)"; Zhu Xi's note: "理 means order, articulation."
- *Also* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the bearing (理) issues without, and the people all comply"; note: "理 is the advance and stay of one's outward mien."
- *Pattern, line (文).* *Yupian*. Former Han, *Biography of Zhou Bo*: "vertical lines (縱理) running into the mouth." Tang shu, *Annals of Taizong*: "if the inner heart is not upright, then the veins and lines (脈理) are all aslant."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "between skin and flesh is the cou-li (湊理), so called for its having grain and veining (脈理)." Liji, *Nei ze*: "in slicing it thin, one must cut across its grain (理)." Du Fu's poem: "the skin-grain (肌理) fine and smooth, the flesh and bone even."
- *To divide (分).* Liji, *Yue ji*: "music is what penetrates the orderings of kind (倫理)"; note: "理 means to divide"; *Shiwen*: "分, fanqie 扶問."
- *To rely on (賴).* Mengzi: "greatly unprovided (不理) for by mouth."
- *To set in order, attend to (料理).* *Guangyun*. Jinshu, *Biography of Huan Chong*: "Chong said to Huizhi: 'you have been long in the office; you ought to set things in order (料理).'"
- *An office of justice.* *Yun Hui*: "the prison-managing officer is called 理." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in the first month of autumn, the Justice-officer (理) is charged to look to the wounded, examine the injured, and inspect the maimed"; note: "理 is the prison-managing officer." Shiji, *Biography of the Upright Officials*: "Li Li was the Justice-officer (理) of Duke Wen of Jin."
- *A go-between, matchmaker (媒).* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "I loose my girdle-pendant to plight my words, and bid Jian Xiu be my matchmaker (理)"; note: "to send the ancient worthy Jian Xiu as go-between (媒理)." The Five Officials: "to make Jian Xiu the matchmaker, to convey the words and reasons (辭理)."
- *Surname.* *Wuyin jiyun*: "Gao Yao was Grand Justice (大理), and his descendants took the office as their clan-name; the Yin had Li Zheng (理徵)."
- *A paper-name.* *Bowu zhi*: "in the Southern Sea they make paper from sea-moss; its grain (理) runs aslant, hence called side-grain paper (側理)."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "Dali (大理), the name of an ancient Dian barbarian state. From the Tang it first had dealings with China, passing through the four surnames Meng, Zhao, Yang, and Duan, who all usurped the title of emperor. By the Yuan it first submitted to China, styled a Commandery-General; and by the Ming it fell, and was made Dali prefecture, belonging to Yunnan."
- *Interchangeable with 李.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 13: "the charge of the travelling envoy (行理)"; note: "an emissary." *Discourses of Zhou*: "the travelling envoy (行理) met him with due ceremony"; Jia Kui's note: "the lesser messenger"; Kong Chao's note also writes 李. Former Han, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "the left horn of the Cavalry-Officer is called 理"; the Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*, writes 李.

**Textual note.** *Discourses of Zhou*, "the travelling envoy met him with due ceremony," with the Zhouli, *Di guan, Xiao xing ren*, Kong Chao's note "also writes 李" — a note: the Zhouli's *Xiao xing ren* note has no text of Kong Chao writing 李; Kong Chao annotated the *Guoyu*, and the three words "小行人" are likewise from Jia Kui's *Guoyu* note. Following the citation in the subcommentary to Zuozhuan, Xi yr 30, of the *Discourses of Zhou* note, it is corrected to: "Jia Kui's note, 'the lesser messenger'; Kong Chao's note also writes 李."
