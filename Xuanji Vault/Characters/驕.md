## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈亥集上〉【馬字部】頁1445第25【唐韻】舉喬切【集韻】【韻會】居妖切，𠀤音嬌。【說文】馬高六尺爲驕。【玉篇】壯貌。　又野馬也。　又馬𩧙逸不受控制也。　又【正字通】恣也，自矜也。【書·周官】位不期驕。【詩·小雅】驕人好好，勞人草草。　又或作喬。【禮·樂記】齊音敖辟喬志。【註】喬音驕。　又【集韻】嬌廟切，音撟。驕驁，馬行貌。又縱恣也。【史記·司馬相如傳】低卬夭蟜据以驕驁兮。【註】椐，直項也。驕驁，縱恣也。驕音居召反。　又【五音集韻】居夭切，音矯。僨驕不可禁之勢。【莊子·在宥篇】僨驕而不可係者，其惟人心乎。【音義】驕如字。又居表反。　又【集韻】虛嬌切【正韻】吁驕切，𠀤音囂。與獢同。短喙犬也。【詩·秦風】載獫歇驕。【釋文】驕，本又作獢。許喬反。　又古通虞韻。【前漢·敘傳】漢興柔遠，與爾剖符。皆恃其岨，乍臣乍驕。    考證：〔【史記·司馬相如傳】夭蟜椐以驕驁兮。〕　謹照原文夭蟜上增低卬二字。椐改据。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, upper; Horse radical 馬; p.1445 #25)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 舉喬. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 居妖 — sounding like 嬌 (jiāo).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A horse six feet tall is a 驕." *Yupian*: "a sturdy look (壯貌)."

**Senses.**
- *A wild horse.*
- *A horse bolting unruly and beyond control.*
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "to give free rein (恣); to vaunt oneself (自矜)." Shu, *Zhou guan*: "rank does not invite arrogance (驕)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the proud man (驕人) is gay and glad, the toiling man worn and weary."
- *Also* sometimes written 喬. Liji, *Yue ji*: "the music of Qi is arrogant, perverse, and proud in spirit (喬志)"; note: "喬 is sounded 驕."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 嬌廟, sounding like 撟 (jiǎo). 驕驁, the gait of a horse; also "to give loose rein (縱恣)." Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "stooping and rearing, bending and prancing in proud career (驕驁)"; note: "椐 means stiff-necked; 驕驁 means to give loose rein. 驕, fanqie 居召."
- *Also* *Wuyin jiyun*: fanqie 居夭, sounding like 矯 (jiǎo). The force of a thing rearing up that cannot be checked (僨驕). Zhuangzi, *Zai you*: "rearing up beyond restraint (僨驕) — is it only the human heart?"; *Yinyi*: "驕 read as written; also fanqie 居表."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 虛嬌. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 吁驕 — sounding like 囂 (xiāo) — same as 獢, a short-snouted dog. Shijing, *Qin feng*: "carrying the long-snouted and the short-snouted hounds (歇驕)"; *Shiwen*: "驕 is also written 獢, fanqie 許喬."
- *Also* anciently it runs together with the 虞-rhyme. Former Han, *Postface*: "the Han, arising, dealt softly with the far ones, and shared the tally with you; all, trusting in their fastnesses, were now subjects, now proud (驕)."

**Textual note.** Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*, "bending and prancing in proud career (夭蟜椐以驕驁兮)" — checked against the original, the two words 低卬 are added before 夭蟜, and 椐 should read 据.
