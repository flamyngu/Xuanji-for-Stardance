**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls дё‘, upper; Mouth radical еҸЈ; p.188 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie дё•йЈ®. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie дё•йҢҰ вҖ” the rising tone of еҢ№ (pЗҗn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the many, the multitude (иЎҶеә¶)." *Guangyun*: "a kind, a class (йЎһ)." Yijing, *Qian*: "the kinds of things (е“Ғзү©) flow into form"; subcommentary: "things of every class spread abroad and take form."
- *Also* Shu, *Shun dian*: "the five relations (дә”е“Ғ) were not docile"; subcommentary: "е“Ғ means rank-orders; within a single household, the gradations of high and low вҖ” namely father, mother, elder brother, younger brother, and son."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "an item, an article (зү©д»¶) is called е“Ғ." Shu, *Yu gong*: "their tribute was metal of three sorts (дёүе“Ғ)"; subcommentary: "Zheng Xuan held the three sorts of metal to be copper of three colors." Yijing, *Xun*: "in the hunt he takes three sorts (дёүе“Ғ)"; note: "the first for the sacrificial dried meats, the second for guests, the third to fill the ruler's kitchen." Liji, *Liqi*: "the offerings are not made fine by many dishes (еӨҡе“Ғ)." Also *Shaoyi*: "in asking about the dishes (е“Ғе‘і), one says, 'do you often eat at So-and-so's?'"; subcommentary: "е“Ғе‘і are the viands." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Shanfu*: "he tastes each of the dishes (е“Ғеҳ—йЈҹ)"; note: "е“Ғ means tasting each thing вҖ” the way of serving the honored."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: a standard, a rank (е“Ғж ј). Liji, *Tan'gong*: "to grade and regulate this (е“ҒзҜҖ) вҖ” this is what is called ritual"; subcommentary: "е“Ғ is a graded rank, зҜҖ is to restrain and decide."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to make even, to order (йҪҠ)." Zhou yu: "he orders (е“Ғ) his hundred baskets."
- *Also, the same (еҗҢ).* Former Han, *Biography of Li Xun*: "a hundred li make one е“Ғ"; note: Meng Kang says: "е“Ғ means the same; it says that within a hundred li the measures are uniform."
- *Also* *Yupian*: official rank (е®ҳе“Ғ). Zhou yu: "the outer officials did not exceed nine ranks (д№қе“Ғ)"; note: "the nine ministers."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "a model (ејҸ); a rule (жі•)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "two mouths give rise to dispute; three mouths can then weigh and judge (е“ҒйҮҸ)."
- *Also an office-name.* *Zhengzitong*: "in the Tang, eunuchs were called rank-officers (е“Ғе®ҳ)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: a surname; from He's Xingyuan. *Zhengzitong*: "the Ming had Pin Yan (е“Ғеө’)."
- *Also a place-name.* Zuozhuan, Wen yr 16: "the Viscount of Chu, riding the post-carriage, joined his forces at Linpin (иҮЁе“Ғ)." Former Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "the king of Ronglu governed at Beipin city (рӨ°һе“ҒеҹҺ)."

**Textual notes.** In the Yu gong subcommentary, й„ӯдә‘ should read й„ӯе…ғ (Zheng Xuan); and the Zuozhuan citation should be Wen yr 16 (not 15), with й©ӣ corrected to йҰ№.
