**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1064 #22)*

**Ancient graph.** 匨.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 昨郞. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徂郞 — sounding like 鑶 (cáng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to hide (匿)." Yijing, *Qian, Wenyan*: "the hidden dragon does not act; the yang energy lies concealed (藏)."
- *Also, to store up (蓄).* Yijing, *Xici*: "the gentleman keeps his implement stored (藏) on his person, awaiting the time to act."
- *Also fanqie 兹郞, sounding like 臧 (zāng): a plant-name.* Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "in the low wet ground there grow zanglang (藏莨), reeds and rushes"; note: "zanglang is a grass among which oxen and horses are foddered."
- *Also fanqie 才浪, sounding like 臓 (zàng).* Liji, *Yue ling*: "carefully cover the stores (藏)." Jin yu: "when Duke Wen went into exile, the page Tou Xu, keeper of the stores (守藏者), did not follow him."
- *Also interchangeable with 臓 (the viscera).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Ji yi*: "verify it by the movements of the nine viscera (九藏)"; note: "the proper viscera are five, and there are also the stomach, bladder, large intestine, and small intestine"; subcommentary: "the five proper viscera are the heart, liver, spleen, lungs, and kidneys — where the qi is stored." Baihu tong: "man has five viscera (五藏) and six bowels — modeled on what? On the Five Phases and the Six Coordinates." *Shuowen*: in the Hanshu, 臧 is used interchangeably.

**Textual notes.** In the Jin yu citation "豎豆須守藏者也," 豆 should read 頭 (per the original). In the Zhouli citation, the source "Yishi (醫師)" should read "Ji yi (疾醫)."
