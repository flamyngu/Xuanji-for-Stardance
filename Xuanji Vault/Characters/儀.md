**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.118 #21)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 魚羈. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 疑羈 — sounding like 宜 (yí).

**Etymology and senses.** The Two Norms (兩儀) are Heaven and Earth. Also the Three Norms (三儀) are Heaven, Earth, and Man.

**Senses.**
- *Bearing, deportment (容).* Shijing, *Cao feng*: "his bearing (儀) does not err."
- *Dignified deportment (威儀).* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "the dignity of his bearing (威儀), rich and full, is not to be measured."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Di guan, Baoshi*: "he teaches the sons of state by the six deportments (六儀): first at sacrifice, second with guests, third at court, fourth in mourning-rites, fifth in the host, sixth the bearing for chariot and horse."
- *Also* *Chun guan, Dianming*: "he is charged with the five ranks (五儀) of the feudal lords"; note: "duke, marquis, earl, viscount, and baron."
- *Also* *Qiu guan, Siyi*: "he has charge of the rites of guiding and assisting guests in the nine ceremonies (九儀), to instruct in deportment, speech, and the forms of salute and yielding."
- *Also* the head of the Tang Board of Rites was called the Grand Deportment (大儀).
- *Also* *Shiming*: "儀 means fitting (宜); to attain the fitness of a matter."
- *Youyi (由儀), a sheng-pipe ode.* Its *Preface*: "Youyi means that in the birth of the myriad things, each attains its fitness."
- *An image; a model (象, 法).* Shijing, *Daya*: "he took for his model (儀刑) King Wen."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi gu*: "a mate (匹)"; subcommentary: "the Shijing, *Yong feng*: 'truly she is my mate (我儀).'"
- *Also* *Discourses of Zhou*: "Danzhu leaned his body to mate (儀) with her"; the *Tongya*: "to pair; it means coupling and joining."
- *To gauge (度).* Shijing, *Daya*: "I gauge and plan it (儀圖)."
- *Also* Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "to come (來); between the Huai and the Ying it is called 儀."
- *Yili (儀栗), a Zhou town-name*; seen in Zuozhuan, Ding yr 7.
- *Surname.* Qin had the grandee Yi Chu; the Han, Yi Changru.
- *Also* used for 擬 (to liken). Former Han, *Biographies of the Imperial Affines*: "all set their hearts on (心儀) General Huo's daughter"; note: "心儀 is just 心擬 (to fix the heart upon)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 俄 (é), fanqie 牛何.* Yang Xiong, *Tai xuan jing*: "the yang-breath spreads broadcast, neither biased nor aslant; things have no strife or suit, but each follows its own measure (儀)." Originally written 𠌾.

**Textual notes.** Where the text reads "Youyi, a sheng-pipe ode; the *subcommentary*: 'Youyi means...'" — a note: this is the ode's *Preface*, so 疏 (subcommentary) is corrected to 序 (Preface). Also "*Discourses of Zhou*: Danzhu, defiling his body (坋身), mated with her" — checked against the original, 坋身 is corrected to 馮身 (leaned his body).
