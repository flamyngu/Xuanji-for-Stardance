**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.286 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 何蓋. *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 下蓋. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 合蓋 — the departing tone of 孩 (hài).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To injure (傷). It follows 宀 (roof) and 口 (mouth) — speech (言) starts from the household; with 丯 as phonetic." Xu (Kai): "calamity ever starts from the household and arises from the slight and minute, hence 害 follows 宀."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "benefit (利) is the opposite of 害." Also "to harm (殘); calamity (禍)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "ghosts and spirits harm (害) the full and bless the humble." *Xi ci*: "by decrease one keeps far from harm (害), by increase one raises up benefit." Also *Discourses of Zhou*: "the former kings did not devote themselves to war, but toiled in pity for the people's hardships and removed their harms (害)."
- *To hinder, obstruct (妨).* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 6: "meaning that the three seasons were not hindered (害), and the people were in concord and the harvest abundant."
- *要害 (the vital points).* Zhanguo ce: "in Qin's orders, rewards and punishments, terrain and strategic advantage (利害), the realm is not its equal." Shiji, *Basic Annals of the First Emperor*: "in the north he took the strategic (要害) commanderies."
- *To dread, be jealous of (忌).* Shiji, *Hereditary House of Yan*: "King Zhao of Yan sent Yue Yi to pact with Zhao and Chu to attack Qi; the feudal lords, fearing (害) King Min of Qi's arrogance and violence, all agreed"; note: "害 is like to be anxious about it." Also *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "the Grand Master Shangguan, of equal rank, vied for favour, and in his heart envied (害) his ability."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie 何割, sounding like 曷 (hé). "What (何)." Erya, *Shi yan*: "why not (盍)"; note: "盍 means 'why not.'" Or written 害, interchangeable with 曷 and 盍. Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "what to wash, and what not (害澣害否)"; note: "害 means what." Shu, *Da gao*: "why does the king not (害不) go against the divination?"; note: "害 stands for 曷."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 係 (xì), fanqie 古詣.* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "I would speak my longing for you — may no flaw or harm (害) befall" — rhyming with 逝 above. Also Jia Yi, *Hanyun fu*: "the fields and furrows wither and lose their moisture, and the broken stones gather and do harm (害)" — rhyming with 涕 below. Former Han, *Eulogy on Sui, the two Xiahous, Jing, Yi, and Li*: "doubting the precarious, neither lacking nor flawed, they crossed the crowd and crossed the age; shallowly it made for regret and blame, deeply it wrought solid harm (害)." Commonly written 害. The graph 害 has 丯 as phonetic; the common form follows 丰.
