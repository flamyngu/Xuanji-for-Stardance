**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҜ…, upper; Roof radical е®Җ; p.286 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie дҪ•и“Ӣ. *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie дёӢи“Ӣ. *Yun Hui*: fanqie еҗҲи“Ӣ вҖ” the departing tone of еӯ© (hГ i).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To injure (еӮ·). It follows е®Җ (roof) and еҸЈ (mouth) вҖ” speech (иЁҖ) starts from the household; with дёҜ as phonetic." Xu (Kai): "calamity ever starts from the household and arises from the slight and minute, hence е®і follows е®Җ."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "benefit (еҲ©) is the opposite of е®і." Also "to harm (ж®ҳ); calamity (зҰҚ)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "ghosts and spirits harm (е®і) the full and bless the humble." *Xi ci*: "by decrease one keeps far from harm (е®і), by increase one raises up benefit." Also *Discourses of Zhou*: "the former kings did not devote themselves to war, but toiled in pity for the people's hardships and removed their harms (е®і)."
- *To hinder, obstruct (еҰЁ).* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 6: "meaning that the three seasons were not hindered (е®і), and the people were in concord and the harvest abundant."
- *иҰҒе®і (the vital points).* Zhanguo ce: "in Qin's orders, rewards and punishments, terrain and strategic advantage (еҲ©е®і), the realm is not its equal." Shiji, *Basic Annals of the First Emperor*: "in the north he took the strategic (иҰҒе®і) commanderies."
- *To dread, be jealous of (еҝҢ).* Shiji, *Hereditary House of Yan*: "King Zhao of Yan sent Yue Yi to pact with Zhao and Chu to attack Qi; the feudal lords, fearing (е®і) King Min of Qi's arrogance and violence, all agreed"; note: "е®і is like to be anxious about it." Also *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "the Grand Master Shangguan, of equal rank, vied for favour, and in his heart envied (е®і) his ability."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie дҪ•еүІ, sounding like жӣ· (hГ©). "What (дҪ•)." Erya, *Shi yan*: "why not (зӣҚ)"; note: "зӣҚ means 'why not.'" Or written е®і, interchangeable with жӣ· and зӣҚ. Shijing, *Zhou nan*: "what to wash, and what not (е®іжҫЈе®іеҗҰ)"; note: "е®і means what." Shu, *Da gao*: "why does the king not (е®ідёҚ) go against the divination?"; note: "е®і stands for жӣ·."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as дҝӮ (xГ¬), fanqie еҸӨи©Ј.* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "I would speak my longing for you вҖ” may no flaw or harm (е®і) befall" вҖ” rhyming with йҖқ above. Also Jia Yi, *Hanyun fu*: "the fields and furrows wither and lose their moisture, and the broken stones gather and do harm (е®і)" вҖ” rhyming with ж¶• below. Former Han, *Eulogy on Sui, the two Xiahous, Jing, Yi, and Li*: "doubting the precarious, neither lacking nor flawed, they crossed the crowd and crossed the age; shallowly it made for regret and blame, deeply it wrought solid harm (е®і)." Commonly written е®і. The graph е®і has дёҜ as phonetic; the common form follows дё°.
