**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, middle; Fire radical 火; p.667 #13)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 于廉. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 疑廉. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 移廉 — sounding like 鹽 (yán).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the upward light of fire." *Yupian*: "hot (熱); to burn (焚)." Shu, *Yin zheng*: "when fire blazes (炎) on Kun ridge, jade and stone burn alike." Also *Hongfan*: "Fire means blazing upward (炎上)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shixun*: "爞爞 and 炎炎 mean scorching (熏)." Shijing, *Daya*: "blazing, blazing (赫赫炎炎)."
- *Also* Wu yu: "the days grow long and burning (炎炎)"; note: "the look of advancing."
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: "blazing (熾)."
- *Also* Liji, *Yue ling*: "its god is the Flame Emperor (炎帝)"; note: "this is the sovereign of the red essence, the Flame Emperor Datíng."
- *Also* Lüshi chunqiu: "the south is called the Blazing Heaven (炎天), the northeast the Blazing Wind (炎風)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie 于凡 — the level tone of 槏; same sense.
- *Also* *Leipian*: fanqie 徒甘, sounding like 談 (tán): fine eloquence (美辨). Zhuangzi, *Qiwu lun*: "great words are grand (炎炎)"; note: "the look of fine abundance."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 以贍, sounding like 豔 (yàn). Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "catching the last radiance and utmost blaze (絕炎) of sun and moon, to unfold his colored duties and ordered affairs"; note: "beholding the surpassing use of the last light of sun and moon, to unfold one's office."
- *Also interchangeable with 焰 (flame).* Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "what men shun, its breath blazes (炎) and draws it on." Cai Yong, *Shihui*: "fearing lest the smoke and flame (煙炎) bring ruin and quenching."
- *Also* Liezi, *Tangwen*: "south of Chu is the land of the Yan folk (炎人之國)"; note: "炎 in the departing tone."

**On the graph.** *Ji Yun*: properly written 爓; also the same as 燄, 𤒦. A note: in the Shuowen, Yupian, and Leipian, 炎 forms its own radical-section.

**Textual notes.** The source 殷湯篇 should read 湯問篇.
