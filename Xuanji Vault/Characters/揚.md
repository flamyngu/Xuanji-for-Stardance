**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, middle; Hand radical 手; p.442 #08)*

**Ancient graphs.** 敭, 𠭲.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 與章. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 余章. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 移章 — sounding like 陽 (yáng).

**Etymology and senses.** "To fly up, to lift (飛舉)." Shijing, *Wangfeng*: "the surging (揚) water cannot float a bundle of firewood"; tradition: "揚 means surging up (激揚)"; subcommentary: "it means the water rushing and leaping up — the sense of swift waves and current." Also *Binfeng*: "to prune the far-reaching, up-springing shoots (遠揚)"; subcommentary: "it means the long shoots that spring up."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "to set forth (發); to make manifest (顯)." *Guangyun*: "to raise up (舉)." Yijing, *Guai*: "proclaimed (揚) in the king's court"; subcommentary: "to set forth and decide matters in the king's court." Shu, *Yao dian*: "make manifest those of bright virtue, and raise up (揚) the humble and obscure." Liji, *Wenwang shizi*: "some are advanced (揚) for their speech"; subcommentary: "those able in speech and reply are also raised to use."
- *Also, to extol, to proclaim (稱說).* Liji, *Jitong*: "the inscription is that by which one names oneself so as to extol (揚) the excellence of one's ancestors and make it manifest to later ages." Former Han, *Biography of Ji Bu*: "were I to go about extolling (游揚) you, making your name known throughout the world, would that not be fine?"
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "𢋐 and 揚 mean to continue (續)."
- *Also, to winnow off the chaff (簸).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in the south is the Winnowing-fan, but it cannot winnow (簸揚)."
- *Also the region above and below the brow is called 揚.* Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "broad of brow (揚) and fair of skin"; tradition: "揚 is the breadth above the brow." Also: "your clear brow (淸揚)"; subcommentary: "揚 is a fair name for the brow-region; having named the brow 揚, one accordingly calls both above and below the brow 揚."
- *Also a battle-axe (鉞).* Shijing, *Daya*: "shields and dagger-axes, battle-axes great and small (戚揚)."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* Shu, *Yu gong*: "between the Huai and the sea lies Yangzhou (揚州)"; subcommentary: "the air south of the Yangtze is restless and vigorous, its nature light and buoyant (輕揚), hence Yangzhou; also it is said the region has much water, and the waves rise up (揚)."
- *Also a surname.* Yang Xiong's *Postface*: "the Yang are a separate clan. King Xuan of Zhou's son Shangfu was enfeoffed as Marquis of Yang (揚侯), and so it became the clan-name." Also the Song had Yang Biju (揚避舉), the Ming Yang Guangxiu (揚光休). Interchangeable with 颺.
