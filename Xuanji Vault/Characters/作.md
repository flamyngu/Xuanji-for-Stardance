**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.99 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** 胙, 𢓓.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 則洛. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 卽各 — the entering tone of 臧 (zāng), i.e. **zuò**.

**Etymology and senses.** "To rise up, arise." Yijing, *Qian*: "the sage arises (作) and the myriad things behold him."
- *To rouse.* Shu, *Kanggao*: "rouse the people to renewal (作新民)."
- *To make, create.* Liji, *Yueji*: "the maker (作者) is called a sage." Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "when Ding is at the zenith, he builds (作) the Chu palace."
- *To do.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "when mine is worn, I will make (作) it anew."
- *To begin.* Shijing, *Lusong*: "ah, the horses start forth (作)."
- *坐作, sitting and rising [drill].* Zhouli, *Xiaguan*: "the Grand Minister of War teaches the measures of sitting, rising (坐作), advance and retreat."
- *將作, Director of Works (a Qin office).* Former Han, *Table of Officials*: "rank two-thousand *shi*, in charge of palaces and chambers."
- *Like 斮 (to cut, dress).* Liji, *Neize*: "of fish one says 'dress it' (作之)"; note: "meaning to scrape off its scales."
- *汨作, the title of a lost chapter of the Shu.*
- *A surname.* The Han had Zuo Xian, governor of Zhuo commandery.
- *Same as 詛 (to curse).* "To resent and revile." Shijing, *Daya*: "now cursing (作), now imprecating (祝)."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子賀, sounding like 佐 (zuò). Later Han, *Biography of Lian Fan*: "Lian Shudu — why so late in coming? He bans no [night] fires, so the people work (作) by night; of old we had no jacket, now we have five pairs of breeches." Han Yu's poem: "...when you go, ask of Fang Bridge; Fang Bridge is built (作) just so" — in the modern Fang pronunciation 作 reads 佐. Colloquially 做 is used.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 總古, sounding like 阻 (zǔ).* Han Yu, *Stele for the Confucius temple at Chuzhou*: "this temple-school was made (作) by the Marquis of Ye; at first it was low and mean, and the god had no fit hall."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 子悉, sounding like 卽 (jí).* The *Beating-the-Earth Song*: "at sunrise I rise to work (作), at sunset I rest." Chen Lin, *Kenan*: "the Great King raised his dwelling, a hundred walls all rising (作)."

**Textual notes.** In Yijing *Qian*, 萬物睹 should read 萬物覩. The "定之方中" citation is from *Yongfeng* (鄘風), not *Beifeng* (邶風).
