**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еӯҗ, middle; Person radical дәә; p.99 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** иғҷ, рў““.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie еүҮжҙӣ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еҚҪеҗ„ вҖ” the entering tone of иҮ§ (zДҒng), i.e. **zuГІ**.

**Etymology and senses.** "To rise up, arise." Yijing, *Qian*: "the sage arises (дҪң) and the myriad things behold him."
- *To rouse.* Shu, *Kanggao*: "rouse the people to renewal (дҪңж–°ж°‘)."
- *To make, create.* Liji, *Yueji*: "the maker (дҪңиҖ…) is called a sage." Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "when Ding is at the zenith, he builds (дҪң) the Chu palace."
- *To do.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "when mine is worn, I will make (дҪң) it anew."
- *To begin.* Shijing, *Lusong*: "ah, the horses start forth (дҪң)."
- *еқҗдҪң, sitting and rising [drill].* Zhouli, *Xiaguan*: "the Grand Minister of War teaches the measures of sitting, rising (еқҗдҪң), advance and retreat."
- *е°ҮдҪң, Director of Works (a Qin office).* Former Han, *Table of Officials*: "rank two-thousand *shi*, in charge of palaces and chambers."
- *Like ж–® (to cut, dress).* Liji, *Neize*: "of fish one says 'dress it' (дҪңд№Ӣ)"; note: "meaning to scrape off its scales."
- *жұЁдҪң, the title of a lost chapter of the Shu.*
- *A surname.* The Han had Zuo Xian, governor of Zhuo commandery.
- *Same as и©ӣ (to curse).* "To resent and revile." Shijing, *Daya*: "now cursing (дҪң), now imprecating (зҘқ)."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie еӯҗиіҖ, sounding like дҪҗ (zuГІ). Later Han, *Biography of Lian Fan*: "Lian Shudu вҖ” why so late in coming? He bans no [night] fires, so the people work (дҪң) by night; of old we had no jacket, now we have five pairs of breeches." Han Yu's poem: "...when you go, ask of Fang Bridge; Fang Bridge is built (дҪң) just so" вҖ” in the modern Fang pronunciation дҪң reads дҪҗ. Colloquially еҒҡ is used.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie зёҪеҸӨ, sounding like йҳ» (zЗ”).* Han Yu, *Stele for the Confucius temple at Chuzhou*: "this temple-school was made (дҪң) by the Marquis of Ye; at first it was low and mean, and the god had no fit hall."
- *Rhymes, fanqie еӯҗжӮү, sounding like еҚҪ (jГӯ).* The *Beating-the-Earth Song*: "at sunrise I rise to work (дҪң), at sunset I rest." Chen Lin, *Kenan*: "the Great King raised his dwelling, a hundred walls all rising (дҪң)."

**Textual notes.** In Yijing *Qian*, иҗ¬зү©зқ№ should read иҗ¬зү©иҰ©. The "е®ҡд№Ӣж–№дёӯ" citation is from *Yongfeng* (й„ҳйўЁ), not *Beifeng* (йӮ¶йўЁ).
