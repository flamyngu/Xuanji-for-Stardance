**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1157 #15)*

**Ancient graph.** 𧥳.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 書之. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 申之 — the level tone of 始, i.e. **shī**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "intent, aspiration (志)." *Shiming*: "之 (to go toward) — that toward which the intent goes." Shu, *Shun dian*: "poetry voices the intent (詩言志)"; tradition: "what the heart goes toward is called intent; the heart having a direction, it must take form in words — hence 'poetry voices intent.'" Preface to *Guan ju*: "in the heart it is intent, uttered in words it is poetry (詩)." Former Han, *Treatise on Letters*: "to chant one's words is called poetry."
- *六詩, the six modes of poetry.* Zhouli, *Da shi*; subcommentary: "in the Shijing only Feng, Ya, Song are genre-names; among them are Bi, Fu, Xing — together the six 詩." Zheng Xuan, *Liuyi lun*: "詩 is the sound of stringed song and chanted admonition." Zhu Xi: "Feng, Ya, Song are names of the divisions of song-music; Fu, Bi, Xing are the means by which their forms are composed."
- *A song-stanza (樂章).* Xunzi, *Quan xue*: "詩 is where the centred sound comes to rest"; note: "詩 means the song-stanza, regulating the sounds to the centred and there stopping, not letting them grow dissolute."
- *To bear, to hold (承, 持).* *Leipian.* Liji, *Nei ze*: "when a ruler's heir is three days old, a knight is divined to carry him… in court dress he bears (詩) him"; note: "詩 means to bear"; subcommentary, *Shi hanshenwu*: "詩 is to hold — holding in the hands, the sense of reverent bearing, supporting from below and carrying in the arms."
- *Surname.* Hou Han, *Account of the Southern Man*: Shi Suo, a man of Zhuyuan in Jiaozhi.
- *Read as 誅 (zhū).* *Zihui bu.* Xunzi, *Wang zhi*: "to order the statutes and examine 詩商"; note: "詩商 should be 誅賞 (punishment and reward)." The *Yue lun* writes 審誅賞.
