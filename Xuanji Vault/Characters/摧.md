**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, middle; Hand radical 手; p.450 #23)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 昨回 — sounding like 漼 (cuī); read like 罪 in the level tone.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to push, press (擠); one says to shove (挏); one says to break (折)."
- *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徂回, sounding like 嗺 (cuī); same sense.
- *To thwart, dishearten (沮).* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "the folk of the house all in turn rebuff (摧) me"; gloss: "摧 is the language of gibe and reproach."
- *To crush; to subdue (挫, 抑).* *Zengyun.* Shiji, *Biography of Ji Bu*: "able to bend (摧) the hard into the soft."
- *To arrive (至).* *Erya, Shigu*: "摧 and 詹 mean 'to arrive.'" Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "摧詹 is Chu speech." Shijing, *Daya*: "the forefathers do arrive (摧)"; tradition: "to arrive"; subcommentary: "where do the spirits of the forefathers arrive?"
- *To perish, be ended (滅).* Shijing, *Daya*, Zhu's tradition: "the sacrifices to the forefathers will from here be ended (滅)."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 遵綏, the level tone of 醉 — "to retreat." Yijing, *Jin*: "advancing, retreating (摧如)" — so read by Zheng Kangcheng.
- *Fanqie 催內, sounding like 啐 (cuì) — "to lessen."*
- *Same as 莝 (to chop fodder).* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 寸臥, sounding like 剉 (cuò). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the team-horses in the stall, chop fodder (摧) for them and grain them"; gloss: "摧 is the modern graph 莝.
