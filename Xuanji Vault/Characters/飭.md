**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, lower; Food radical йЈҹ; p.1417 #15)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie жҒҘеҠӣ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie и“„еҠӣ вҖ” sounding like ж•• (chГ¬).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To make firm (иҮҙе …)." *Yupian*: "a careful look (и¬№иІҢ)." Liji, *Yue ji*: "with a second beginning they make plain the advance, and with a repeated finale they order the retreat (йЈӯжӯё)"; note: "the drum is beaten again to make clear the advance, and the gong struck again to order the retreat."

**Senses.**
- *To put in order, repair (дҝ®жІ»).* Yijing, *Za gua*: "Decay (и ұ) then means setting in order (йЈӯ)."
- *Diligent (еӢӨ).* Zhouli, *Tian guan, Da zai*: "the hundred craftsmen diligently transform (йЈӯеҢ–) the eight materials"; subcommentary: "йЈӯ means diligent." Also *Dong guan, Kaogong ji*: "to apply diligent strength to grow the land's wealth is called the farmer."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to set in trim and ready (ж•ҙеӮҷ)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the war-chariots are now made ready (йЈӯ)"; tradition: "йЈӯ means to set right." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-winter, order the affairs of death (йЈӯжӯ»дәӢ)"; note: "to charge and warn the six armies, and rouse their resolve to die." Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "he ordered himself (йЈӯиә¬) and kept the fast."
- *Also* same as ж••. To command (е‘Ҫд»Ө). *Discourses of Qi*: "he charged (йЈӯ) their sons and brothers, telling one another of affairs." Former Han, *Biography of Zhang Chang*: "he plainly charged (йЈӯ) the senior officers and the assistant-wardens." *Ji Yun*: sometimes written йЈҫ.

**Textual notes.** Zhouli, *Tian guan, Da zai*, "the hundred craftsmen diligently transform the eight materials"; the gloss "йЈӯ means diligent" вҖ” checked against the original, иЁ» (note) is corrected to з–Ҹ (subcommentary). Also Shijing, *Xiaoya*, "my chariots are now made ready (жҲ‘и»Ҡж—ЈйЈӯ)" вҖ” checked against the original, жҲ‘и»Ҡ is corrected to жҲҺи»Ҡ, and иЁ» (note) to еӮі (tradition).
