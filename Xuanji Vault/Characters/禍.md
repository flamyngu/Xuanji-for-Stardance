**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Spirit radical 示; p.844 #31)*

**Ancient graphs.** 旤, 𥚍, 𣨷, 𥙯.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 胡果. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 戸果. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 合果 — the rising tone of 和 (huò).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "harm; the spirits withholding blessing." *Shiming*: "ruin (毀); it means destruction." *Zengyun*: "calamity (殃); disaster (災)."
- *Misfortune, calamity.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "two men walk together — who has made this calamity (禍)?" Liji, *Biaoji*: "the gentleman is careful, and so avoids misfortune (禍)."
- *Also written 旤.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Five Phases*: "[a portent among] the six domestic animals is called 旤, meaning it is manifest."
- *Also written 祻.* Jin shu, *Annals of Emperor Wen*: "calamity (祻) springs alike from the trigger."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 後五, the rising tone of 戸 (hù).* Feng Yan, *Fu on Manifesting My Aim*: "of old the three sovereigns were pure and unmixed; yet each latter age ran into utter ruin (禍); I mourn Jie of Xia at Nanchao, and weep for Zhou of Yin at Muye" — 野 here sounds like 豎.
- *Rhymes in the 紙 rhyme, fanqie 虎委.* Xunzi, *Chengxiang*: "the calamity (禍) of the age — to hate worthy men: Zixu was slain, Baili exiled."
- *Rhymes in the 支 rhyme, fanqie 許規.* Xunzi, *Chengxiang*: "weigh the ministers' faults, reverse their doings; honour the ruler and secure the state, esteem the worthy and the right (義); but to shut out remonstrance and gloss over wrong, being foolish yet siding with those above — the state must come to ruin (禍)" — 義 here rhymes as 宜.
