**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, middle; Fire radical 火; p.680 #10)*

**Ancient graph.** 焈.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 許其. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 虛其. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 虛宜 — sounding like 僖 (xī).

**Etymology and senses.** Erya, *Shigu*: "緝熙 means light (光)." Shijing, *Daya*: "ah, continuously bright (緝熙), reverent and at rest."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "熙 means to rise, to flourish (興)." Shijing, *Zhousong*: "now it is wholly resplendent (純熙)"; Zhu's tradition: "also means light."
- *Also* Zhou yu: "熙 means broad (廣)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "all the works are made to flourish (熙)." *Shun dian*: "is there one able to exert his services and magnify (熙) the Emperor's undertakings?" Shiji, *Hereditary House of Wu*: "singing the Daya, Ji Zha said, 'how broad, how genial (熙熙)!'"
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "harmonious, genial (和)." Laozi, *Daode jing*: "the multitude are merry and content (熙熙)."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "to dry, to warm (燥)."
- *Also a personal name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 29: "the house of Shaohao had four uncles: Chong, Gai, Xiu, and Xi (熙) — they indeed mastered metal, wood, and water."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: a prefecture-name; land of the Tubo; the Song set up Xizhou (熙州).
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: the same as 禧. Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "the blessed (熙) affair was fully accomplished"; note: Yan Shigu says: "an affair of blessing and felicity (福熙)."
- *Also interchangeable with 嬉 (to sport).* Song Yu, *Dengtuzi haose fu*: "setting out from Xianyang, sporting (熙) at Handan"; note: "熙 means to play (戲)."
- *Also* Huainanzi, *Xiuwu*: "the pole-sporter (木熙) is not merely nimble and strong"; note: "the muxi (木熙) is today's runner-up-the-high-pole and rope-climber; it says he grips the great boughs, dances among the spreading branches, leaping and bounding, winding slow and easy at will."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 盈之, sounding like 飴 (yí). Yangzi, *Fangyan*: "鬱熙 means to grow tall (長)"; note: "it means sturdy and large."
- *Also a personal name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 8: "Prince Xi (熙) of Zheng."

**Editorial notes.** A note: the Shiwen gives 熙 as fanqie 許其, and Xu [Miao] gives the sound 怡 (yí); both readings may be used. The Liushu gu: separately written 𤇴. A note: in the Zihui and Zhengzitong the sound and sense of 熙 are given in full under the ten-stroke entry 熈; but examining the Shuowen, Yupian, and Guangyun, all write 熙, not 熈 — so 熈 must be the vulgar form, and the sound and sense are here set out in full.
