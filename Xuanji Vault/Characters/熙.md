**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls е·і, middle; Fire radical зҒ«; p.680 #10)*

**Ancient graph.** з„Ҳ.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie иЁұе…¶. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иҷӣе…¶. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie иҷӣе®ң вҖ” sounding like еғ– (xД«).

**Etymology and senses.** Erya, *Shigu*: "з·қзҶҷ means light (е…ү)." Shijing, *Daya*: "ah, continuously bright (з·қзҶҷ), reverent and at rest."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "зҶҷ means to rise, to flourish (иҲҲ)." Shijing, *Zhousong*: "now it is wholly resplendent (зҙ”зҶҷ)"; Zhu's tradition: "also means light."
- *Also* Zhou yu: "зҶҷ means broad (е»Ј)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "all the works are made to flourish (зҶҷ)." *Shun dian*: "is there one able to exert his services and magnify (зҶҷ) the Emperor's undertakings?" Shiji, *Hereditary House of Wu*: "singing the Daya, Ji Zha said, 'how broad, how genial (зҶҷзҶҷ)!'"
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "harmonious, genial (е’Ң)." Laozi, *Daode jing*: "the multitude are merry and content (зҶҷзҶҷ)."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "to dry, to warm (зҮҘ)."
- *Also a personal name.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 29: "the house of Shaohao had four uncles: Chong, Gai, Xiu, and Xi (зҶҷ) вҖ” they indeed mastered metal, wood, and water."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: a prefecture-name; land of the Tubo; the Song set up Xizhou (зҶҷе·һ).
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: the same as зҰ§. Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "the blessed (зҶҷ) affair was fully accomplished"; note: Yan Shigu says: "an affair of blessing and felicity (зҰҸзҶҷ)."
- *Also interchangeable with е¬ү (to sport).* Song Yu, *Dengtuzi haose fu*: "setting out from Xianyang, sporting (зҶҷ) at Handan"; note: "зҶҷ means to play (жҲІ)."
- *Also* Huainanzi, *Xiuwu*: "the pole-sporter (жңЁзҶҷ) is not merely nimble and strong"; note: "the muxi (жңЁзҶҷ) is today's runner-up-the-high-pole and rope-climber; it says he grips the great boughs, dances among the spreading branches, leaping and bounding, winding slow and easy at will."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie зӣҲд№Ӣ, sounding like йЈҙ (yГӯ). Yangzi, *Fangyan*: "й¬ұзҶҷ means to grow tall (й•·)"; note: "it means sturdy and large."
- *Also a personal name.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 8: "Prince Xi (зҶҷ) of Zheng."

**Editorial notes.** A note: the Shiwen gives зҶҷ as fanqie иЁұе…¶, and Xu [Miao] gives the sound жҖЎ (yГӯ); both readings may be used. The Liushu gu: separately written рӨҮҙ. A note: in the Zihui and Zhengzitong the sound and sense of зҶҷ are given in full under the ten-stroke entry зҶҲ; but examining the Shuowen, Yupian, and Guangyun, all write зҶҷ, not зҶҲ вҖ” so зҶҲ must be the vulgar form, and the sound and sense are here set out in full.
