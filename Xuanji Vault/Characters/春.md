**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Sun radical 日; p.493 #06)*

**Ancient graphs.** 萅, 𦸬, 旾, 𣈤.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 昌脣. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 樞倫 — the level tone of 蠢, i.e. **chūn**.

**Etymology and senses.** *Erya, Shitian*: "spring is the Green Yang (靑陽)"; note: "the air clear, warm, and yang." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Zongbo* subcommentary: "spring is what brings forth and gives life to the myriad things." Gongyang zhuan, Yin yr 1: "what is spring? — the year's beginning"; note: "spring is the start of Heaven and Earth's opening, the head of nurture and life, whence the model-emblems issue; when at dusk the Dipper points east, it is spring." Shiji, *Treatise on the Heavens*: "the eastern [phase] Wood governs spring." Former Han, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes and Calendar*: "the yang *qi* stirs things; in season it is spring; 春 is 蠢 (to stir): things stir into life and so move on."
- *A surname.* He's *Xingyuan*: "descendants of Lord Chunshen, Huang Xie."
- *A wine-name.* Tang guoshi bu: "among wines are the Fushui-chun of Ying, the Ruoxia-chun of Wucheng, the Shangku-chun of Xingyang, the Shidong-chun of Fuping, the Shao-chun of Jiannan."
- *A flower-name.* Huamu kao: "a kind of poppy is named the Lichun (麗春)."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 尺尹, sounding like 蠢 (chǔn). Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*, Carpenter: "stretching the hide-target and perching the bull's-eye, then they stir (春) to the work"; note: "春 is read 蠢; 蠢 is to rouse, to come forth.
