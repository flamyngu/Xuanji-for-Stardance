## Shuowen Jiezi (說文解字) — original
> 《𨸏部》陰：闇也。水之南、山之北也。从𨸏侌聲。

## Guangyun (廣韻) — original
> 《廣韻·下平聲·侵·音》陰：隂陽也。《說文》作隂闇也，水之南山之北也，又姓出武威風俗通云管修自齊適楚爲隂大夫其後氏焉。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Mound radical 阜; p.1353 #06)*

**Ancient graphs.** 霒, 侌, 𤽎, 𨽙, 𠊺, 瘖.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於今. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於禽 — sounding like 音 (yīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "dark (闇)." *Shiming*: "陰 is 蔭 (shade): the *qi* lies within, deep and shaded." *Yupian*: "dim and formless, deep and hard to fathom, is called 陰." Yijing, *Kun*: "though the yin (陰) has beauty, it holds it in, to serve the king's work, not daring to claim the completion — such is the Way of Earth, of the wife, of the minister." Liji, *Yueling*: "the hundred officers keep their business quiet and forgo punishment, to settle what the late yin (晏陰) brings to completion."
- *The inner (women's) rites (陰禮).* Zhouli, *Tianguan, Neizai*: "by the inner rites she teaches the Six Palaces"; note: "陰禮, the rites of women." The Nei xiaochen: "in charge of the king's private affairs and private orders (陰事陰令)." Diguan, *Da situ*: "by the [marriage] rites (陰禮) he teaches affection, and the people do not resent"; note: "陰禮, the rites of man and woman."
- *Overcast.* Shijing, *Beifeng*: "gloomy and overcast (陰)"; and: "now overcast, now raining (以陰以雨)."
- *The north (shady) side of a hill.* *Shuowen.* Shu, *Yugong*: "south to Huayin (華陰)."
- *The south side of a river.* *Shuowen.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Fenyin (汾陰) county in Hedong"; note: "Mount Jie lies to its south."
- *A shadow (影).* *Yupian.* Jinshu, *Biography of Tao Kan*: "the Great Yu grudged an inch of time (寸陰); we should grudge a fraction (分陰)."
- *The back of a stele.* *Zhengzitong.* Yang Xiu deciphered the eight graphs on the back (陰) of the Cao E stele.
- *陰陰, shadowing.* Former Han, *Sacrificial Hymns*: "the spirit arrives, blessing shadowing all (陰陰)"; note: Yan Shigu says, "the shade spreading and covering all below."
- *Silent, secret (默).* *Yupian.* Zhanguo ce: "the dealings of Qi and Qin secretly (陰) combined."
- *The carriage-front cover.* Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "the front-rail (陰) and traces with white-metal links"; tradition: "陰 is the covering of the carriage-front (軓)." *Shiming*: "陰 is 蔭: set crosswise at the carriage-front to screen [the front-board]."
- *Place-names.* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 9: "crossed at Yinban (陰阪) and invaded Zheng." Zhao yr 19: "the Chu Director of Works Chi removed [the people of] Yin to Xiayin (下陰)." Zhao yr 22: "encamped at Yin (陰)." Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yin (陰) county in Nanyang"; note: "the Xiayin of the Zuozhuan." The Han also had two Yinshan (陰山) counties, in Xihe and in Guiyang.
- *A mountain-name.* Shiji, *Annals of Qin Shihuang*: "from Yuzhong along the River eastward, [the land] joined to the Yin Mountains (陰山)"; note: Xu Guang says, "north of Wuyuan."
- *A surname.* *Guangyun*: "Guan Xiu went from Qi to Chu and became grandee of Yin, whose line took it as surname." A note: in the Shiji, Chu Shaosun's *Biography of the Tortoise and Milfoil*, "Yin Jing saved him and they fled together"; the Suoyin: "陰 is the surname, 兢 the name" — so the Yin surname existed already in the Shang. Also Zuozhuan, Xi yr 15: "Yin Yisheng of Jin met the Earl of Qin"; note: "Yisheng was fiefed at Yin." The Zhanguo ce has Yin Jian and Yin Ji; and Zhao yr 24 has Yin Buning — Yin too is likely a surname.
- *A man's member.* *Zhengzitong.* Shiji, *Biography of Lü Buwei*: "she privately sought out the well-endowed (大陰) Lao Ai as a retainer."
- *The crane.* The Yi Zhoushu: "on the altar-ground they spread a red canopy with *yin*-plumes (陰羽)"; note: "陰 is the crane." (*Yupian*: "now written 隂"; *Wuyin jiyun*: "vulgarly 𨹉"; *Zihui*: "vulgarly 阥.")
- *Same as 闇, the mourning-hut.* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 烏含, sounding like 菴 (ān). Lunyu: "Gaozong in the mourning-hut (諒陰) spoke not for three years."

**Departing tone (yìn).** *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 於禁, sounding like 蔭 (yìn).
- *To bury, inter (瘞藏).* *Ji Yun.* Liji, *Jiyi*: "flesh and bone perish below, buried (陰) as the soil of the wilds"; note: "陰 is read as the 廕 of 依廕." Shijing, *Daya*: "I have come to shelter (陰) you, yet you turn and rage at me"; gloss: "to cover and shade." Yunhui xiaobu: "蔭 is interchangeable with 陰."
- *Sounding like 飮 (yǐn).* *Zhengzitong*: the old medical books have the malady 淡陰, vulgarly written 淡飮 (phlegm-rheum).

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 於容, sounding like 雍 (yōng).* Shijing, *Binfeng*: "in the days of the second [month] they hew the ice clinking, in the days of the third they store it in the ice-house (凌陰)"; gloss: "凌陰 is the ice-house." Yangzi's *Taixuan jing*: "the sun flies and hangs its shade (陰), and the myriad things teem warm."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 於虔, sounding like 煙 (yān).* The Huangting jing: "above is the soul-spirit, below the Gate-Source; the left is the Lesser Yang, the right the Grand Yin (太陰)."
- *Originally written 殷 — a pale-black colour; also written 陰.* *Yunhui xiaobu.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*, "my horse is a *yin*" tradition: "pale-black mixed with white is the *yin*-grey (駰); 陰 is a pale-black colour."

**Textual notes.** "齊秦之處陰合" should read "齊秦之交陰合"; "揜軌" should read "揜軓"; "赤奕陰羽" should read "赤帟陰羽".
