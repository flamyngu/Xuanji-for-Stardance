**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Child radical 子; p.277 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 㜽, 𢀈, 𢀉, 𡐫, 𣕓.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 卽里. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 祖似 — sounding like 梓 (zǐ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "In the eleventh month the yang force stirs and the myriad things teem and grow (滋); it is taken as the name." Xu Kai: "at midnight of the eleventh month the yang force begins; man bears the yang, hence it is taken as the name."

**Senses.**
- *To breed; an heir (息, 嗣).* *Guangyun*; *Zengyun*. Yijing, *Xu gua*: "there being male and female, thereafter there is husband and wife; there being husband and wife, thereafter there is father and son (父子)." *Baihu tong*: "the king, fathering Heaven and mothering Earth, is called the Son of Heaven (天子); the Son of Heaven's son is called the principal son (元子)." Shu, *Gu ming*: "to reverently protect the principal son (元子) Zhao." Also Yili, *Sang fu*: "the feudal lord's son is styled prince (公子)." Also, in general the principal eldest son is the tomb-son (冢子), that is the lineage-heir (宗子); the principal wife's next son, or a son of the host of concubines, is the cadet-son (別子), also the branch-son (支子). Liji, *Qu li*: "the branch-son does not sacrifice; if he sacrifices, he must report to the lineage-heir."
- *A general respectful term for a man.* Yan Shigu: "子 is a fair title for a man, hence whoever has perfected virtue is called a gentleman (君子)." Wang Su: "子 is a general title for one having virtue and rank."
- *From the secondary wives downward, the self-designation is 婢子 (handmaid)*, seen in Liji, *Qu li*. Also a minister's wife is called the inner one (內子). Yili, *You si che*, note: "the inner one does not present the dish."
- *Also* Liji, *Tan gong*: "a brother's son is as one's own son (猶子)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Biography of Yan Zhu*, note: "to have one's son go and become a son-in-law in the wife's family is called a pledged son (贅子)."
- *A ruler's loving care of the people is 子.*
- *A branch-name.* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year in 子 is called Kundun (困敦)." Former Han, *Treatise on the Pitch-pipes and Calendar*: "teeming and sprouting (孳萌) in 子."
- *Also* Liji, *Wang zhi*: "duke, marquis, earl, viscount (子), and baron — five ranks in all"; subcommentary: "the viscount (子) upholds favour and proclaims virtue."
- *左庶子 (Left Junior) and 中庶子 (Inner Junior), official titles.*
- *A state-name.* *Kuodi zhi*: "Zicheng (子城) is in Shencheng county of Weizhou."
- *Changzi (長子), a county-name.* The fief of Xin Jia, a Zhou historian; later a Zhao town, belonging to Shangdang.
- *Surname.* Shiji, *Basic Annals of Yin*: "Xie's mother swallowed a swallow's egg (鳦子) and bore him, hence the Zi clan (子氏)." Also compound surnames: in the Zuozhuan, the Zheng grandee Zirenshi, the Lu grandees Zifushi and Zijiashi.
- *子細 (carefully, in detail), like to distinguish.* Beishi, *Biography of Yuan Sili*: "in governing one should raise the great outlines — what need to be too detailed (太子細)?" *Zhengzitong*: "子 read like 薺 (jì); in regional speech, to distinguish. Commonly written 仔細."
- *Also* in the departing tone, fanqie 才四. Zhongyong: "treating the common people as children (子庶民)" — the reading of Xu Miao.
- *Interchangeable with 慈.* Liji, *Yue ji*: "the heart of ease, uprightness, kindness, and sincerity (子諒) springs up freely"; the *Han shi waizhuan* writes 子諒 as 慈良.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 走 (zǒu), fanqie 濟口.* Former Han, Ban Gu, *Postface*: "the blessing of the kings and princes, fortune reaching to grandsons and sons (孫子); the ducal lineage spreading wide, its branches and leaves luxuriant." (茂 sounded 某, mǒu.)
- *Rhymes as 則 (zé), fanqie 子德.* Shijing, *Bin feng*: "having taken my young (子), do not wreck my nest." Yang Shen, *Guyin congmu*: "the same as Zhu Xi's tradition."

**Textual notes.** "A woman too is called 子. Liji, *Qu li*: the lady styles herself 婢子" — a note: styling oneself 婢子 is the designation from the secondary wives downward, not of a lady; a lady styles herself 小童, not 婢子. It is corrected to: "from the secondary wives downward, the self-designation is 婢子, seen in Liji, *Qu li*." · "Erya, *Shi sui*: the Grand Year in 子 is called Kundun" — checked against the original, "*Shi sui*" should read "*Shi tian*."
