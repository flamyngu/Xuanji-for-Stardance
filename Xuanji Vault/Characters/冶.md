> 《康熙字典》〈子集下〉【冫字部】頁131第31【唐韻】羊者切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】以者切，𠀤音野。【說文】消也。从仌，台聲。【三蒼】冶，銷也。遭熱卽流，遇冷卽合。與冰同志，故字从冰。【正韻】鎔也，銷也，鑄也。【韻會】爐鑄謂之冶。引尸子，蚩尤造九冶。又鑄匠曰冶。【前漢·董仲舒傳】金之在鎔，惟冶者之所鑄。又妖冶。【說文】女態。【易·繫辭】冶容誨淫。【正韻】裝飾也。又姓。【廣韻】左傳，衞大夫冶厪。又【玉篇】公冶，複姓。又江南有梅根冶。【宋書·百官志】江南有梅根及冶塘二冶。【庾信·枯樹賦】南陵以梅根作冶。【孟浩然詩】火熾梅根冶。○按此皆晉時冶官所居。楊愼謂冶卽野字，非。　又【韻補】叶演女切，音與。【齊·武帝·估客樂詩】昔經樊鄧役，阻潮梅根冶。深懷悵往事，意滿辭不敘。又叶鄔果切，音婐。容也。【謝惠連詩】酈生無文章，西施整妖冶。胡爲空耿介，悲哉君志瑣。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Ice radical 冫; p.131 #31)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 羊者. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 以者 — sounding like 野 (yě).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to melt (消). It follows 仌 (ice), with 台 as phonetic." Sancang: "冶 means to smelt (銷): meeting heat it flows, meeting cold it congeals — of one will with ice, hence the graph follows 冰." *Zheng Yun*: "to fuse (鎔); to smelt (銷); to cast (鑄)." *Yun Hui*: "furnace-casting is called 冶." It cites the Shizi: "Chiyou made the Nine Forges (九冶)." Also a foundryman is called 冶. Former Han, *Biography of Dong Zhongshu*: "metal in the crucible is cast just as the smelter (冶者) shapes it."
- *Also 妖冶 (seductive).* *Shuowen*: "a woman's alluring manner." Yijing, *Xici*: "a seductive appearance (冶容) invites lewdness." *Zheng Yun*: "to deck out, adorn (裝飾)."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "in the Zuozhuan, the Wey grandee Ye Jin (冶厪)." Also *Yupian*: Gongye (公冶), a compound surname.
- *Also* south of the Yangtze there is the Meigen foundry (梅根冶). Song shu, *Treatise on Officials*: "south of the Yangtze are the two foundries Meigen and Yetang (冶塘)." Yu Xin, *Kushu fu*: "at Nanling they made a foundry (冶) at Meigen." Meng Haoran's poem: "the fire blazes at the Meigen foundry (梅根冶)." A note: these were all where the foundry-officials dwelt in Jin times. Yang Shen's claim that 冶 is simply the graph 野 is wrong.
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 演女, sounding like 與 (yǔ). Emperor Wu of Qi, *Guke yue*: "once I served in the Fan-Deng campaign, held up by the tide at the Meigen foundry (梅根冶); deeply I cherish, rueful, those bygone things — my heart is full, but words do not unfold them."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 鄔果, sounding like 婐 (wǒ): an alluring appearance (容). Xie Huilian's poem: "Master Li had no literary art, Xi Shi dressed in seductive charm (妖冶); why be vainly upright and aloof? — alas, that your aims are so petty."
