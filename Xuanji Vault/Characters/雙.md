## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集中〉【隹字部】頁1368第34【唐韻】所江切【集韻】【韻會】疎江切，𠀤音䉶。【說文】隹二枚也。从雔，又持之。【後漢·王喬傳】有雙鳧，從東南飛來。　又【玉篇】兩也。【儀禮·聘禮】凡獻執一雙。　又【玉篇】偶也。【詩·齊風】冠綏雙止。【箋】五人爲奇，而襄公往從而雙之。　又獸名。【山海經】南海之外，赤水之西，流沙之東，有三靑獸相幷，名雙雙。【註】郭璞曰：言體合爲一也。公羊傳所云雙雙而俱至者，蓋謂此也。吳任臣云：獸經曰：文文善呼，雙雙善行。駢志云：雙雙合體，蛩蛩假足。麟書曰：雙雙俱來，孟極是覆。　又鳥名。【公羊傳·宣五年】其諸爲其雙雙而俱至者與。【疏】舊說云：雙雙之鳥，一身二首尾，有雌雄，隨便而偶，常不離散，故以喩焉。○按公羊傳疏與山海經註異。　又【正字通】溫庭筠詩：招客先開四十雙。王弇州註云：雙，五畝也。四十雙，二百畝也。或曰唐書南詔傳：官給田，四雙爲二百畝。又雲南雜志：俗耕田，三人使二牛，前牽，中壓，後驅，犂一曰爲一雙。又陶九成輟耕錄，謂一雙爲四畝，佛地以二畝爲一雙，蓋各從其方俗而名之，非有成數也。　又姓。【正字通】南北朝孝子雙泰貞。　又【集韻】朔降切，音淙。偶也。　又叶所終反。【謝惠連·七夕詩】弄杼不成藻，聳轡騖前蹤。昔離秋已兩，今聚夕無雙。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical 隹; p.1368 #34)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 所江. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 疎江 — sounding like 䉶 (shuāng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "two birds; it follows 雔 (a pair of birds), with 又 (a hand) holding them." Later Han, *Biography of Wang Qiao*: "there were a pair of ducks (雙鳧) flying in from the southeast."
- *A pair, two (兩).* *Yupian.* Yili, *Pinli*: "in every offering one holds a pair (一雙)."
- *Even, matched (偶).* *Yupian.* Shijing, *Qifeng*: "cap-tassels in pairs (雙)"; gloss: "five men is an odd number, and Duke Xiang went and made them up to pairs."
- *A beast-name.* Shanhaijing: "beyond the South Sea, west of the Red Water, east of the Flowing Sands, are three green beasts joined together, named *shuangshuang* (雙雙)"; note: Guo Pu says, "their bodies are joined as one; the Gongyang zhuan's 'coming in pairs' likely means this." Wu Renchen: "the Beast-Classic says, 'the *wenwen* is good at calling, the *shuangshuang* good at going'; the Pianzhi: '*shuangshuang* joined in body, *qiongqiong* lending feet'; the Linshu: '*shuangshuang* come together, the *mengji* covering them.'"
- *A bird-name.* Gongyang zhuan, Xuan yr 5: "is it not for their coming in pairs (雙雙) together?"; subcommentary: "the *shuangshuang* bird has one body with two heads and tails, male and female, pairing as suits them, never parting — hence the figure." (A note: the Gongyang subcommentary differs from the Shanhaijing note.)
- *A land-measure.* *Zhengzitong*: Wen Tingyun's poem, "to welcome a guest I first open forty *shuang* (雙)"; Wang Shizhen's note, "a *shuang* is five *mu*; forty *shuang* is two hundred *mu*." Some cite the Tangshu, *Account of Nanzhao*, "four *shuang* making two hundred *mu*"; or the Yunnan zaji, "one day's plough is one *shuang*"; or Tao Jiucheng's Chuogeng lu, "a *shuang* is four *mu*; in Buddhist lands two *mu* make a *shuang*" — each named by local custom, with no fixed figure.
- *A surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "the Northern-and-Southern dynasties' filial son Shuang Taizhen."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 朔降, sounding like 淙 (zhuàng) — "a pair."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 所終.* Xie Huilian, *Qixi poem*: "...at last parting two autumns are gone, now meeting, the evening has no equal (無雙).
