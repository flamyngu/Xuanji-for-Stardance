**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical йҡ№; p.1368 #34)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie жүҖжұҹ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie з–Һжұҹ вҖ” sounding like дү¶ (shuДҒng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "two birds; it follows йӣ” (a pair of birds), with еҸҲ (a hand) holding them." Later Han, *Biography of Wang Qiao*: "there were a pair of ducks (йӣҷйі§) flying in from the southeast."
- *A pair, two (е…©).* *Yupian.* Yili, *Pinli*: "in every offering one holds a pair (дёҖйӣҷ)."
- *Even, matched (еҒ¶).* *Yupian.* Shijing, *Qifeng*: "cap-tassels in pairs (йӣҷ)"; gloss: "five men is an odd number, and Duke Xiang went and made them up to pairs."
- *A beast-name.* Shanhaijing: "beyond the South Sea, west of the Red Water, east of the Flowing Sands, are three green beasts joined together, named *shuangshuang* (йӣҷйӣҷ)"; note: Guo Pu says, "their bodies are joined as one; the Gongyang zhuan's 'coming in pairs' likely means this." Wu Renchen: "the Beast-Classic says, 'the *wenwen* is good at calling, the *shuangshuang* good at going'; the Pianzhi: '*shuangshuang* joined in body, *qiongqiong* lending feet'; the Linshu: '*shuangshuang* come together, the *mengji* covering them.'"
- *A bird-name.* Gongyang zhuan, Xuan yr 5: "is it not for their coming in pairs (йӣҷйӣҷ) together?"; subcommentary: "the *shuangshuang* bird has one body with two heads and tails, male and female, pairing as suits them, never parting вҖ” hence the figure." (A note: the Gongyang subcommentary differs from the Shanhaijing note.)
- *A land-measure.* *Zhengzitong*: Wen Tingyun's poem, "to welcome a guest I first open forty *shuang* (йӣҷ)"; Wang Shizhen's note, "a *shuang* is five *mu*; forty *shuang* is two hundred *mu*." Some cite the Tangshu, *Account of Nanzhao*, "four *shuang* making two hundred *mu*"; or the Yunnan zaji, "one day's plough is one *shuang*"; or Tao Jiucheng's Chuogeng lu, "a *shuang* is four *mu*; in Buddhist lands two *mu* make a *shuang*" вҖ” each named by local custom, with no fixed figure.
- *A surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "the Northern-and-Southern dynasties' filial son Shuang Taizhen."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie жң”йҷҚ, sounding like ж·ҷ (zhuГ ng) вҖ” "a pair."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie жүҖзөӮ.* Xie Huilian, *Qixi poem*: "...at last parting two autumns are gone, now meeting, the evening has no equal (з„Ўйӣҷ).
