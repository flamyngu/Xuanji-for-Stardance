## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈未集中〉【羽字部】頁957第36【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤七醉切，音綷。【說文】靑羽雀也。出鬰林。【爾雅·釋鳥】翠鷸。【註】似燕，紺色。【疏】李巡曰：鷸一名曰翠，其羽可以爲飾。樊光云：靑羽。出交州。【前漢·賈山傳】飾以翡翠。【註】雄曰翡，雌曰翠。又【南越王趙陀傳】翠鳥幹。【博物志】翡身通黑，惟胸前背上翼後有赤毛。翠身通靑黃，惟六翩上毛長寸餘靑，其飛則羽鳴翠翡翠翡然，因以爲名。　又【禮·內則】舒雁翠。【註】舒鴈，鵝也。翠，尾肉也。　又【爾雅·釋山】未及上翠微。【疏】謂未及頂上，在旁陂陀之處，名翠微。一說山氣靑縹色，故曰翠微。　又姓。【急就篇註】翠氏，楚景萃之後也。避入關三遷，懷土逃匿，改姓爲翠。    考證：〔又姓。【急就篇註】翠氏，楚晨之後也。〕　謹照原文楚晨改楚景萃。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Feather radical 羽; p.957 #36)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 七醉 — sounding like 綷 (cuì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a green-feathered finch; it comes from Yulin." Erya, *Shiniao*: "the cui (翠) is the yu (鷸)"; note: "like a swallow, of a dark-blue color"; subcommentary: "Li Xun says: the yu is also called 翠, and its feathers may serve for ornament; Fan Guang says: green-feathered, from Jiaozhou." Former Han, *Biography of Jia Shan*: "adorned with kingfisher-plumes (翡翠)"; note: "the male is fei (翡), the female cui (翠)." Also the *Biography of Zhao Tuo, King of Nanyue*: "the trunks of kingfishers (翠鳥)." Bowu zhi: "the fei is wholly black in body, with red feathers only on the breast, upper back, and behind the wings; the cui is wholly green-yellow, with green feathers over an inch long only on the six quills; in flight its wings cry 'cui-fei, cui-fei,' and so it is named."
- *Also* Liji, *Neize*: "the rump-flesh (翠) of the tame goose"; note: "the tame goose (舒鴈) is the goose; 翠 is the flesh of the tail."
- *Also* Erya, *Shishan*: "not yet up to the cuiwei (翠微)"; subcommentary: "it means the place not yet at the summit, on the sloping flanks, called 翠微; one account: the mountain's vapor is a pale blue-green, hence 翠微."
- *Also a surname.* Note to the Jijiu pian: "the Cui (翠) clan are descendants of Jing Cui (景萃) of Chu; fleeing into the Pass they moved three times, and longing for their homeland they hid, changing their surname to Cui (翠)."

**Textual notes.** In the Jijiu pian note, 楚晨 should read 楚景萃 (Jing Cui of Chu, per the original).
