## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈申集上〉【艸字部】頁1023第16【唐韻】康土切【集韻】【韻會】孔五切，𠀤音䇢。【說文】大苦，苓也。【詩·唐風】采苦采苦，首陽之下。【傳】苦，苦菜也。陸璣云：生田及山澤中，得霜甜脃而美。【謝靈運·山居賦】二箭殊葉，四苦齊味。【自註】四苦：靑苦、白苦、紫苦、黃苦。　又味也。【書·洪範】炎上作苦。【詩·邶風】誰謂荼苦，其甘如薺。　又【集韻】勤也。【孟子】必先苦其心志。　又患也。【前漢·賈誼傳】非徒病腫，又苦𨂂盭。　又【類篇】急也。　又【博雅】悵也。　又【揚子·方言】快也。楚曰苦，秦曰了。【郭璞曰】苦而爲快，猶以臭爲香，治爲亂，反覆用之也。　又去聲。【正韻】苦故切，音庫。困也。【西溪叢語】今人不善乗船謂之苦船，北人謂之苦車。　又姓。大夫苦成，見《國語》。又【通志·氏族略】漢有會稽太守苦灼。　又果五切，音古。惡也。【周禮·冬官考工記】辨其苦良。【註】謂分別縑白之麤細。【齊語】辨其功苦。【註】功，牢也。苦，脃也。【史記·五帝紀】河濵器皆不苦窳。【註】苦，麤也。　又音怙。地名。【史記·老莊列傳】老子者，楚苦縣厲鄕曲仁里人也。◎按苦良，苦窳，功苦之苦，或作楛，或作沽，亦通盬，義同。《直音》作苦。苦字从艸作。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1023 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 康土. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 孔五 — sounding like 䇢 (kǔ).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "大苦 (great bitter) is the *ling* [liquorice]." Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "I gather sowthistle, gather sowthistle (苦), below Shouyang"; tradition: "苦 is the bitter herb (苦菜)." Lu Ji says: "it grows in fields and on hills and marshes; after frost it turns sweet, crisp, and good." Xie Lingyun, *Shanju fu*: "two bamboos of differing leaf, four 'bitters' of like savour"; his own note: "the four bitters — green-bitter, white-bitter, purple-bitter, yellow-bitter."
- *A taste.* Shu, *Hongfan*: "[fire,] blazing upward, makes the bitter (苦)." Shijing, *Beifeng*: "who says the sowthistle is bitter (苦)? — it is sweet as shepherd's-purse."
- *To toil (勤).* *Ji Yun.* Mengzi: "[Heaven] must first make bitter (苦) his heart and will."
- *To suffer, be distressed by (患).* Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "not only sick with swelling, but also afflicted (苦) by [a foot] sprained and twisted."
- *Urgent, pressing (急).* *Leipian.*
- *Downcast, rueful (悵).* *Boya.*
- *Pleased, glad.* Yangzi's *Fangyan*: "glad"; in Chu they say 苦, in Qin 了. Guo Pu says: "'bitter' meaning 'glad' is like 'stink' for 'fragrant' or 'order' for 'disorder' — words used in reverse."
- *Departing tone.* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 苦故, sounding like 庫 (kù) — "distressed, hard-pressed." Xixi congyu: "people today who are poor sailors call it 'boat-sick (苦船)'; northerners say 'cart-sick (苦車).'"
- *A surname.* The grandee Kucheng; see the Guoyu. Also Tongzhi, *Monograph on Clans*: "the Han had Ku Zhuo, governor of Kuaiji."
- *Fanqie 果五, sounding like 古 (gǔ) — "bad, of poor make."* Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*: "to tell its poor (苦) from its good"; note: "to distinguish the coarse from the fine." The Qiyu: "to tell its sound (功) from its flimsy (苦)"; note: "功 is sturdy, 苦 is brittle." Shiji, *Annals of the Five Emperors*: "the wares from the banks of the River were none of them shoddy (苦窳)"; note: "苦 means coarse."
- *Sounding like 怙 (hù), a place-name.* Shiji, *Biographies of Laozi and Zhuangzi*: "Laozi was a man of Quren hamlet, Li village, Ku county (苦縣) in Chu."

**On the graph.** A note: in 苦良, 苦窳, and 功苦, the 苦 is sometimes written 楛 or 沽, and is also interchangeable with 盬, the sense being the same. The Zhiyin writes 苦.
