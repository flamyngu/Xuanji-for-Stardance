## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集中〉【音字部】頁1396第25【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤於今切，音隂。【說文】聲也。生於心，有節於外，謂之音。宮商角徵羽，聲。絲竹金石匏土革木，音也。从言，含一。【書·舜典】八音克諧。【禮·樂記】變成方謂之音。【疏】方謂文章，聲旣變轉和合，次序成就文章謂之音。音則今之歌曲也。【周禮·春官·大師】以六律爲之音。【疏】以大師吹律爲聲，又使其人作聲而合之，聽人聲與律呂之聲合，謂之爲音。【詩序】情發於聲，聲成文，謂之音。【疏】此言聲成文謂之音，則聲與音別。樂記註：雜比曰音，單出曰聲。記又曰：審聲以知音，審音以知樂，則聲音樂三者不同，以聲變乃成音，音和乃成樂，故別爲三名。對文則別，散則可以通。季札見歌《秦》曰：此之謂夏聲。公羊傳曰：十一而稅頌聲作，聲卽音也。下云治世之音，音卽樂也。是聲與音樂各得相通也。　又【易·中孚】翰音登于天。【禮·曲禮】雞曰翰音。　又姓。見【姓苑】。　又【正韻】於禁切。與䕃通。【左傳·文十七年】鹿死不擇音。【杜預註】音，所茠䕃之處。古字借用。○按註借用，是古字聲同，皆相假借，且釋文作於鳴反，𠀤不作去聲讀，正韻非。    考證：〔【詩序】聲成文，謂之音。【疏】季扎見歌秦曰，此之謂夏聲。〕　謹照原文季扎改季札。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Sound radical 音; p.1396 #25)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 於今 — sounding like 隂 (yīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "sound (聲). What arises in the heart and is articulated outwardly is called 音 (tone). Gong, shang, jue, zhi, and yu are the notes (聲); silk, bamboo, metal, stone, gourd, clay, hide, and wood are their tones (音). The graph follows 言 (speech), enclosing 一 (one)." Shu, *Shun dian*: "the eight tones (八音) are brought into harmony." Liji, *Yue ji*: "when varied into patterns it is called 音"; subcommentary: "方 means a wrought pattern; when sounds, once varied and turned, blend harmoniously and in due order complete a pattern, this is called 音; 音 is what we now call song-melody." Zhouli, *Chunguan, Da shi*: "by the six pitch-pipes their tones (音) are made"; subcommentary: "the Grand Master blows the pitch-pipes to give the sound, then has the men produce sound to match it; when the human voice is heard to accord with the pipes, this is called 音." Preface to the *Shijing*: "feeling issues as sound, and sound formed into pattern is called 音"; subcommentary: "this says that sound formed into pattern is 音, so sound (聲) and tone (音) are distinct. The note to the *Yue ji*: 'mixed and ranked together is 音; issuing singly is 聲.' The *Ji ji* also says: 'examine sound to know tone, examine tone to know music (樂)' — so sound, tone, and music are three different things: sound varied becomes tone, tone harmonized becomes music; hence the three separate names. Set against one another they differ; used loosely they may be interchanged. Ji Zha, hearing the *Qin* airs sung, said: 'this is what is called the Xia sound (夏聲).' The Gongyang zhuan says: 'when the tithe of one in eleven was levied, the sound of praise-odes (頌聲) arose' — here 聲 is just 音. Below it says 'the tone (音) of a well-ordered age' — here 音 is just 樂. Thus sound, tone, and music can each be interchanged."
- *Also* Yijing, *Zhong fu*: "the fowl's cry (翰音) mounts to heaven." Liji, *Quli*: "the cock is called 翰音 (the lofty-voiced)."
- *Also a surname*; see the Xingyuan.
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於禁; interchangeable with 䕃 (yìn, "shade"). Zuozhuan, Wen yr 17: "a dying deer does not choose its shade (音)"; Du Yu's note: "音 is the place where one takes shade and rest; the old graph was borrowed." A note: the borrowing in the gloss is because the old graphs sounded alike and were mutually loaned; moreover the Shiwen gives fanqie 於鳴 and does not read it in the departing tone — the *Zheng Yun* reading is mistaken.

**Textual notes.** In the Preface-subcommentary citation, 季扎 should read 季札 (Ji Zha).
