## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈卯集上〉【心字部】頁388第37【唐韻】府眉切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】逋眉切，𠀤音𤰞。【說文】痛也。从心非聲。有聲無淚曰悲。【詩·豳風】女心傷悲。【毛傳】春女悲，秋士悲。感其物化也。【鄭箋】春女感陽氣而思男，秋士感隂氣而思女，是其物化，所以悲也。一曰心非爲悲。心之所以非則悲矣。【淮南子·原道訓】憂悲多恚，病乃成積。　又叶補皆切，擺平聲。【楚辭·九辯】春秋逴逴而日高兮，然惆悵而自悲。四時遞來而卒歲兮，隂陽不可與儷偕。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.388 #37)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 府眉. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 逋眉 — sounding like 𤰞 (bēi).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Pain (痛)." It follows 心 with 非 as phonetic. "Sound without tears is 悲." Shijing, *Bin feng*: "the maiden's heart is wounded and grieving (傷悲)"; Mao tradition: "in spring the maiden grieves, in autumn the man grieves — moved by the turning of things"; Zheng *Jian*: "in spring the maiden, stirred by the *yang qi*, longs for a man; in autumn the man, stirred by the *yin qi*, longs for a woman — this is the turning of things, and the cause of grief." One saying: "心非 (the heart's denial) makes 悲: what the heart holds wrong (非), it grieves." Huainanzi, *Yuandao xun*: "sorrow, grief, and much rage — illness then builds up."

**Rhyming variant.** *Rhymes as 擺 (bǎi), fanqie 補皆.* Chuci, *Jiu bian*: "spring and autumn recede afar as the days mount; then, disconsolate, I grieve of myself (悲); the four seasons come in turn and the year ends — *yin* and *yang* will not keep pace together."
