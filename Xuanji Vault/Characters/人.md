**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.91 #01)*

**Ancient graph.** 𠔽.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 如鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 而鄰 — sounding like 仁 (rén).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the noblest of the natures of Heaven and Earth." *Shiming*: "人 is 仁 (benevolence); benevolence gives life to things." Liji, *Liyun*: "Man is the virtue of Heaven and Earth, the intercourse of yin and yang, the meeting of ghosts and spirits, the finest *qi* of the Five Phases."
- *The One Man (一人), the ruler.* Shu, *Lüxing*: "when the One Man has blessing, the myriad people depend on it." Also 予一人 (I, the One Man), the Son of Heaven's term for himself. *Tang gao*: "Ah, you multitudes of the myriad regions, hear clearly the proclamation of me, the One Man."
- *The two persons (二人), father and mother.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "till the dawn breaks I sleep not, longing for the two persons."
- *A place name.* Zuoren and Zhongren, two towns of the Di state.
- *An office-title element.* Zhouli has the kitchener (庖人), the cook (亨人), the drink-master (漿人), the ice-keeper (凌人), and the like.
- *The maple-man (楓人), formed from an old maple.* See the *Chaoye qianzai*. Also the rush-man (蒲人) and mugwort-man (艾人); see the *Suishi ji*.
- *A surname.* The Ming had Ren Jie. Also Zuoren and Wenren, both compound surnames.

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 如延, sounding like 然 (rán).* Liu Xiang, *Lienü song*: "she read his face and begged pardon for the fault, and Duke Huan praised her; thereafter she governed the inner [palace] and was set up as his lady (夫人)."
