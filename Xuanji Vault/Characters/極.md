> 《康熙字典》〈辰集中〉【木字部】頁542第02【唐韻】渠力切【集韻】【韻會】竭憶切【正韻】竭戟切，𠀤禁入聲。【說文】棟也。【徐曰】極者屋脊之棟，今人謂高及甚爲極，義出於此。　又天地未分以前曰太極。【易·繫辭】易有太極，是生兩儀。【註】無稱之稱，不可得而名也。　又皇極，大中也。【書·洪範】皇建其有極。【疏】人君爲民之主，大自立其有中之道。　又北辰曰北極，老人星曰南極。【書·舜典·正義】引《王蕃·渾天說》曰：北極出地三十六度，南極入地三十六度，而嵩高正當天之中極。　又三極，三才也。【易·繫辭】三極之道。【疏】謂天、地、人三才，至極之道也。　又【爾雅·釋地】東至于泰遠，西至于邠國，南至于濮鈆，北至于祝栗，謂之四極。　又五極，五常之中正也。【書·呂𠛬】屬于五極，咸中有慶。　又六極，窮極惡事也。【書·洪範】威用六極：一曰凶短折，二曰疾，三曰憂，四曰貧，五曰惡，六曰弱。又四方上下，亦謂之六極。【莊子·天運篇】天有六極。　又八極。【韻會】四極，方隅之極也。【淮南子·本經訓】紀綱八極，經緯六合。　又至也。【詩·周頌】立我烝民，莫非爾極。　又取止也。【詩·大雅】匪疚匪棘，王國來極。　又盡也。【易·繫辭】極其數，遂定天下之象。　又放也。【儀禮·大射儀】贊設決，朱極三。【註】極，放也。以朱韋爲之，所以韜指利放弦也。　又【爾雅·釋天】月在癸曰極。　又國名。【春秋·隱二年】無駭帥師入極。　又疲也。【世說】顧和謁王導，導小極對之疲睡。　又【正韻】訖逆切，音戟。與亟同。【荀子·箴賦】反覆甚極。　又【集韻】訖力切，音棘。殊也。　又【唐韻】渠綺切，音技。【淮南子·精神訓】賤之而弗憎，貴之而弗喜，隨其天資，而安之不極。　又【廣韻】渠記切，音曁。【楚辭·天問】厥萌在初，何所意焉。璜臺十成，何所極焉。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.542 #02)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 渠力. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 竭憶. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 竭戟 — the entering tone of 禁 (jí).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A ridgepole (棟)." Xu (Kai): "the 極 is the ridgepole of a roof-spine; today men call the high and the utmost 極 — the sense issues from this."

**Senses.**
- *Before Heaven and Earth divided is called the Supreme Ultimate (太極).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the Yijing has the Supreme Ultimate (太極), which gives birth to the Two Modes"; note: "the name of the nameless, that cannot be named."
- *The August Ultimate, the great mean (皇極, 大中).* Shu, *Hong fan*: "the sovereign establishes his ultimate (有極)"; subcommentary: "the ruler is the people's master, who greatly sets up of himself the Way of holding the mean."
- *The Pole-star is called the North Pole (北極); the Old Man star the South Pole (南極).* Shu, *Shun dian*, *Correct Meaning*, citing Wang Fan's *Theory of the Armillary Heaven*: "the North Pole rises thirty-six degrees above the earth, the South Pole sinks thirty-six degrees below it, and Mount Song stands just at the central pole of Heaven."
- *The Three Ultimates (三極), the Three Powers.* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the Way of the Three Ultimates (三極)"; subcommentary: "the Way of utmost extremity in the Three Powers — Heaven, Earth, and man."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi di*: "east to Taiyuan, west to the state of Bin, south to Puqian, north to Zhuli — these are called the Four Ultimates (四極)."
- *The Five Ultimates (五極), the right mean of the Five Constants.* Shu, *Lü xing*: "joined to the Five Ultimates (五極), all in the mean and with blessing."
- *The Six Ultimates (六極), the extremes of evil things.* Shu, *Hong fan*: "by awe employ the Six Ultimates (六極): first, ill-fated early death; second, sickness; third, sorrow; fourth, poverty; fifth, wickedness; sixth, weakness." Also the four directions, above and below, are called the Six Ultimates. Zhuangzi, *Tian yun*: "Heaven has Six Ultimates (六極)."
- *The Eight Ultimates (八極).* *Yun Hui*: "the Four Ultimates are the extremes of the directions and corners." Huainanzi, *Ben jing*: "they ruled the Eight Ultimates (八極) and ordered the Six Confines."
- *To reach (至).* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "you settled us, the teeming people, and nothing is not your reaching (極)."
- *To take as the goal, the stopping-point (取止).* Shijing, *Daya*: "neither vexing nor pressing, the royal domain comes to its goal (來極)."
- *To exhaust (盡).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "exhausting (極) its numbers, one then fixes the images of the world."
- *To loose, let go (放).* Yili, *Da she*: "the assistant sets the thumb-ring and the three vermilion finger-guards (朱極)"; note: "極 means to loose — made of vermilion leather, to sheathe the fingers for easy loosing of the string."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the moon in 癸 is called 極."
- *A state-name.* Chunqiu, Yin yr 2: "Wuhai led the army and entered Ji (極)."
- *Weary, spent (疲).* *Shishuo*: "Gu He visited Wang Dao; Dao, a little spent (極), dozed wearily before him."
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 訖逆, sounding like 戟 (jǐ) — same as 亟 (urgent). Xunzi, *Zhen fu*: "turning back and forth most pressingly (甚極)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 訖力, sounding like 棘 (jí). "Distinct, exceptional (殊)."
- *Also* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 渠綺, sounding like 技 (jì). Huainanzi, *Jing shen*: "debased yet not loathing it, honoured yet not rejoicing in it, following one's Heaven-given lot and resting in it without strain (不極)."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 渠記, sounding like 曁 (jì). Chuci, *Tian wen*: "their sprouting was at the start — what was intended therein? the jasper terrace of ten storeys — where was its limit (極)?"
