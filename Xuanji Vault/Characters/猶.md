**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, lower; Dog radical 犬; p.715 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 以周. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 夷周 — sounding like 由 (yóu).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "of the macaque kind. It follows 犬 (dog), with 酋 as phonetic." *Ji Yun*: "it dwells in the mountains; hearing a human voice, it climbs a tree in advance, and only when there is no one does it come down. The world calls indecision 猶豫 (hesitation)." Sometimes written 𧳫. Erya, *Shishou*: "the you (猶) is like a muntjac, good at climbing trees"; note: "vigorous in going up trees." Liji, *Quli*: "ritual is the means of letting the people resolve doubt and settle their wavering (猶與)"; subcommentary: "猶 and 與 are two beasts, both much given to hesitation in advancing and retreating; men full of doubt resemble them, hence the term 猶與." Huainanzi, *Bing lüe*: "strike them while they waver (猶猶), override them while they dither (與與)."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "in Longxi a puppy is called 猶."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to resemble, to be like (似)." Shijing, *Shaonan*: "truly my lot is not like theirs (不猶)"; tradition: "猶 means 'like' (若)." Also *Xiaoya*: "good men, gentlemen, whose virtue is unmatched (不猶)." Liji, *Sangfu*: "a brother's son is like a son (猶子)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: "a particle of 'may you.'" Shijing, *Weifeng*: "be careful, oh! you may (猶) come back — do not stay"; tradition: "猶 means 'may' (可)."
- *Also properly written 猷.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "able to make great his plan (猶)"; tradition: "猶 means a way (道)"; Jian: "猶 means a plan (謀), a military plan"; subcommentary: "able to magnify the way of his strategy." Also: "the counsels (謀猶) go awry"; Jian: "the way of conducting government." Also *Daya*: "the king's plan (猶) is truly filling the realm"; Jian: "猶 means a plan."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "still, yet (尚)." Liji, *Tan'gong*: "Zhongzi too still (猶) follows the way of the ancients."
- *Also* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "the long hills and lofty peaks, truly mapped (猶), and the gathered rivers"; Jian: "猶 means to map (圖); the small hills and high peaks were all duly sacrificed to in order, according to a map of the mountains and rivers."
- *Also 夷猶 (yiyou, to linger).* Liji, *Tan'gong*: "the gentleman is, as it were, unhurried (猶猶)"; note: "midway between fast and slow." Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "the lord does not set forth — he lingers (夷猶)."
- *Also* Guanzi, *Diyuan*: "the lowest soil is called the 'five-you' (五猶); the form of the five-you is like dung."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Qiuyou county (厹猶縣), belonging to Linhuai commandery."
- *Also a surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "the Song had You Daoming (猶道明)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 餘招, sounding like 遙 (yáo). *Ji Yun*: "properly written 䚻: to sing unaccompanied (徒歌)." Sometimes written 謠.
- *Also interchangeable with 搖.* Liji, *Tan'gong*: "chanting, then swaying (猶); swaying, then dancing"; note: "猶 should be 搖, meaning the body sways; among the men of Qin, 猶 and 搖 sounded alike." Shiwen: "猶, following the note, is written 搖, pronounced 遙."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 居祐, sounding like 究 (jiù). The Yinyi to Erya, *Shishou*: "fanqie 弋又."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 余救, sounding like 柚 (yòu). The Yinyi to Erya, *Shishou*: "fanqie 羊救."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 余久, sounding like 酉 (yǒu). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "let us be good to one another, and not flout (猶) one another" — 好 rhyming as fanqie 許厚.

**Textual notes.** The source of the "be careful, oh" citation, 衞風, should read 魏風.
