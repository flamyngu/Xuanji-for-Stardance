**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, upper; Rice radical 米; p.910 #10)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子盈. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 咨盈 — sounding like 晶 (jīng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To select, sift (擇)." *Guangyun*: "refined (熟); fine (細); single, undivided (專一)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "be refined (精), be single." Yijing, *Xi ci*: "to refine (精) the sense to the point of the divine, and so bring it to use."

**Senses.**
- *Fine, exact (密).* Gongyang zhuan, Zhuang yr 10: "the coarse is called 侵 (a raid), the fine (精) is called 伐 (a campaign)"; note: "精 is like 精密, fine and exact. In a 侵 one reproaches the unsubmissive and pushes troops into the territory; in a 伐 one strikes the deeper, with somewhat more precise intent."
- *Numinous; the true vital force (靈, 眞氣).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "the refined force (精氣) makes things"; subcommentary: "the numinous force of yin and yang, dense and gathering, becomes the myriad things." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 7: "Zichan said: 'when the things used are refined and many (精多), then the soul is strong, and so there is keen spirit (精爽) reaching to the divine and bright.'" Also (Zuozhuan,) Zhuang yr 25, subcommentary on the eclipse: "the sun is the yang-essence (陽精), the moon the yin-essence (隂精)." Also Xiang yr 28, subcommentary on "spring without water": "the Five Stars are the essences (精) of the Five Phases — the wood-essence is the Year-star (Jupiter), the fire-essence is Yinghuo (Mars), the earth-essence is the Quelling-star (Saturn), the metal-essence is Taibai (Venus), the water-essence is the Chen-star (Mercury)." Laozi, *Dao de jing*: "within it there is essence (精), and that essence is very true." Zhuangzi, *De chong fu*: "you wear out your essence (精)."
- *Correct; good; fine (正, 善, 好).* *Guangyun*. Liji, *Jing jie*: "pure, still, fine and subtle (精微) — that is the teaching of the Yijing."
- *Bright (明).* Former Han, *Biography of Jing Fang*: "the dark mist does not clear (不精)"; note: "精 means the sunlight being clear and bright."
- *Hulled, polished (鑿).* Lunyu: "he did not tire of finely-hulled (精) grain." Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "I pound fine (精) jade-dust for my provender"; note: "精 means hulled."
- *Skilful, ingenious (巧).* *Yun Hui Xiaobu*.
- *Of any thing, its pure and utmost is called 精.* *Zengyun*.
- *Anciently jade was called 精.* *Discourses of Chu*: "one pure (純) and two essences (精)."
- *地精 and 黃精, plant-names.* Boya: "地精 is ginseng (人葠). 黃精 is also called the immortal's leftover grain (仙人餘糧)."
- *精衞, a bird-name.* Shanhai jing: "on Mount Fajiu there is a bird named Jingwei (精衞)."
- *Sifted rice is called 精.* Zhuangzi, *Ren jian shi*: "to ply the divining-stalks and scatter the rice (精)"; note: "sifted rice is called 精."
- *精絕, a state-name.* Shuijing zhu: "the southern He flows further east past the state of Jingjue (精絕)." Former Han, *Account of the Western Regions*: "the city of Jingjue is over 8,820 li from Chang'an."
- *精廬, 精舍, a study-lodge.* Former Han, *Discussion in the Forest of Scholars*: "study-lodges (精廬) were briefly built"; note: "a 精廬 is a hall for lecturing and reading." Later Han, *Biography of Li Chong*: "Chong set up a study-lodge (精舍) and lectured."
- *Also* *Guangdong xinyu*: "the chieftain of the Yao is titled the jing-fu (精夫)."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: "same as 睛 (the eyeball)."
- *A bird-name, interchangeable with 鶄.* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "the jiao-jing (交精) with whirling eyes"; note: "the jiao-jing is like a wild duck but high-legged, with a feathered crest; it wards off fire-disaster."
- *Same as 菁.* Erya, *Shi cao*, note on 茢薽: "also called the heaven-creeper, 天蔓精."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 將 (jiāng), fanqie 咨良.* Zuo Si, *Wu du fu*: "the Weaving-Maid lends her radiance there, the Wings and Axletree lodge their essence (精)"; note: "精, fanqie 咨良, rhymes with 疆 above."

**Further reading.**
- *Guangyun*: fanqie 子姓. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 子正 — sounding like 婧 (jìng). "Strong (强)."
