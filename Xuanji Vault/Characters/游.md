**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 巳, upper; Water radical 水; p.635 #14)*

**Ancient graph.** 汓.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 以周. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 夷周 — sounding like 猷 (yóu).

**Etymology and senses.** A river-name. Shuijing zhu: "the Huai water branches off at Huaipu county, the northern branch being the You water (游水)."
- *To float along, swim.* Erya, *Shishui*: "going down with the current is called 遡游 (downstream)." Shijing, *Qinfeng*: "downstream (遡游) I follow her."
- *A detached palace.* Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Gatekeepers, four to each gate of the king's palace, and the same for the park-lodges (囿游)"; note: "游 is a detached palace."
- *A precinct.* Guanzi, *Shouxian*: "he divides the administration into ten precincts (十游), each precinct having its head."
- *The earth's driftings.* Shangshu kaolingyao: "the earth has its four driftings (四游), ever moving though men know it not."
- *To roam, take pleasure.* Liji, *Shaoyi*: "the gentleman leans on virtue and roams (游) in the arts."
- *Idle, at leisure (閒曠).* Liji, *Wangzhi*: "let there be no idle (游) folk."
- *A look of ease.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "be heedful in your ease (優游)."
- *The look of spreading boughs.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "in the marsh is the spreading *long*-plant (游龍)"; tradition: "龍 is the *hong* [knotweed]"; gloss: "游 is like 'unrestrained' — the knotweed flings out its boughs and leaves in the marsh."
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 徐由, sounding like 囚 (qiú); same sense.
- *A banner-streamer.* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 力求, sounding like 留 (liú); originally written 斿, also 旒. Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "the girdle-pendants, the banner-streamers (游) and breast-straps."
- *Jiuyou (九游), a star.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Heavens*: "the Nine Streamers (九游), nine stars, lie southwest of the Jade Well."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 延知, sounding like 移 (yí).* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "trailing the rainbow banner, waving the cloud-flag; before, the leather-canopied cars, behind, the road-train (道游)."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 衣虛, sounding like 於 (yū).* Wan Zhen, *Nanzhou yiwu zhi* encomium: "the folk of Hepu, used to the water, swim (游) well; looking up at the tiered pools, like apes gazing up at a tree-trunk."

**Textual note.** "少依于德" should read "士依於德," and 于 should be 於.
