**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical 隹; p.1369 #16)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 呂支. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 鄰知 — sounding like 驪 (lí).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The yellow oriole (黃倉庚); when it sings, the silkworms hatch." It follows 隹 with 离 as phonetic. *Yupian*: also written 鸝. *Guangyun*: "today 鸝 is used for the oriole and 離 is borrowed for 'to part (離別).'" *Ji Yun*: also written 鵹.

**Senses.**
- *A trigram / hexagram.* Yijing, *Li*: the Tuan says, "離 is clinging (麗)." *Yupian*: "離, brightness (明)."
- *To scatter, to part (散).* *Guangyun*: "near is 離, far is 別." Yang Xiong, *Fangyan*: "to divide; in Qin-Jin, 離." Yijing, *Qian*: "advancing and retreating without constancy, yet not leaving (離) the group." Shijing, *Wang feng*: "there is a woman cast off (仳離)."
- *To meet, encounter (遇).* *Fangyan*: "a net is called 離." Yijing, *Xiaoguo*: "the flying bird is caught (離) by it." Former Han, *Yang Xiong*, note to *Fan Lisao*: Ying Shao, "離 is like 遭 (to befall)."
- *To pass through (歷).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "not passing (離) within"; subcommentary, "離, to pass through."
- *Two, paired (兩).* Liji, *Qu li*: "離坐離立 (sit by twos, stand by twos)"; note: "離, two; two clinging together is 離."
- *To divide, to judge (判).* Liji, *Xue ji*: "in the first year they observe his punctuating of texts (離經) and discerning of aims"; note: "離經, breaking off the phrases." Zhouli, *Xingfangshi*: "let there be no crookedly-cut-off (華離) land."
- *To arrange in sequence.* Liji, *Mingtang wei*: "Shu's arrayed (離) chime-stones"; note: "離, sequencing the suspended stones."
- *To pass, to skim (過, 獵).* Yili, *Da she li*: "中離維綱"; note: "離 is like 過, 獵."
- *To set out, display (陳).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 1: "設衞離服"; note: "離, to arrange/display."
- *Kinship term.* Erya, *Shi qin*: a man calls his sister's son 出, and 出's son a 離孫 ("parted grandchild").
- *離離, the look of not attending closely to affairs.* Xunzi, *Fei xiang*.
- *Luxuriant, overgrown.* Erya, *Shi gu*: "覭髳茀離"; note: "the tangled lushness of plants and trees; 茀離 = 彌離, luxuriant."
- *陸離, jagged and teeming / scattered.* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "long my pendants, glittering and varied (陸離)"; Xu Shen: "a fair appearance"; Yan Shigu: "scattered."
- *流離, those who have lost their place.* Former Han, *Jiao si ge*: "闢流離."
- *林離 / 淋離, spreading, profuse.* Sima Xiangru, *Daren fu*: "麗以林離"; Yang Xiong, *Yulie fu*: "淋離廓落."
- *長離, a numinous bird (the southern Vermilion Bird).* Sima Xiangru, *Dalin fu*: "before, the Changli; behind, the Yuhuang"; note (Fu Qian): "all gods' names"; Shigu: "Changli, the numinous bird." (The Han *Treatise on Rites and Music* writes 長麗, glossed as a star name.) Zhang Heng, *Sixuan fu*: "before, the Changli brushes its plumes"; note: "Changli, the Vermilion-Bird god of the south."
- *大琴 (the great zither) is called 離.* Erya, *Shi yue*; subcommentary: "its tones much-varied, the sound flowing (流離)."
- *璧流離, coloured glass / lapis.* Former Han, *Western Regions*: "Jibin produces 璧流離"; Shigu, citing the *Weilüe*: "Daqin yields ten kinds of 流離 — red, white, black, yellow, blue, green, pale-blue, dark-blue, crimson, purple."
- *A horse's name (纖離).* Li Si, *Memorial against Expelling Guests*: "you ride the Xianli steed."
- *A bird's name.* Shijing, *Bei feng*: "the young of the 流離"; tradition: "流離, a bird." (Erya, *Shi niao* note writes 留離.)
- *A plant name.* Qu Yuan, *Li sao*: "I wore river-eupatorium (江離) and angelica"; note: "離 = 蘼蕪 (a fragrant herb)." Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "蘖離朱楊"; note: "離, the mountain pear." *Piya*: the *Hanshi* says, "the peony is the 'parting plant' (離草); about to part, one gives peonies; also called 可離."
- *A tree's name.* Shiji, *House of Confucius*, note citing the *Huanglan*: "trees in the graveyard — oak, white-elm, 雒, and 離."
- *A river's name.* Former Han, *Annals of Wudi*: "issuing from the Li waters below Lingling."
- *A place name.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 15: "met Wu at Zhongli"; note: "Zhongli, a Chu town in Huainan county."
- *A state name.* Former Han, *Western Regions*: "Dongli, a great state." *Shiyi ji*: "the state of Nili came to court."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: Mencius's disciple Li Lou.

**Further readings.**
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 抽知, sounding like 痴 (chī) — properly written 螭 (see the 螭 entry under the insect radical).
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 輦尒, sounding like 邐 (lǐ) — 離跂, the look of baring the arms.
- *離靡, connected unbroken.* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "離靡廣衍"; 離 read fanqie 力爾.
- *Guangyun, Ji Yun, Yun Hui*: fanqie 力智, sounding like 荔 (lì). *Guangyun*: "to go away (去)." Shu, *Yin zheng*: "the officials forsook (離) their posts"; *Shiwen*: "離 usual sound, also fanqie 力智." Liji, *Qu li*: "the parrot can speak, yet leaves not (離) the birds."
- *Same as 荔.* Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "答遝離支"; note (Jin Zhuo): "離支 (lychee), big as a hen's egg, rough-skinned; peeled, the flesh like egg, the centre yellow, sweet with little sourness"; Shigu: read fanqie 力智.
- *Guangyun, Ji Yun, Yun Hui*: fanqie 郞計, sounding like 麗 (lì). Liji, *Yue ling*: "宿離不貸"; note: "離 read like 儷 (to pair); 宿儷 means the asterisms paired — the astronomers Fengxiangshi and Baozhangshi pair off the lodges, watching closely without error"; *Shiwen*: "離 fanqie 呂計, to pair."
- *Rhymes as 黎 (lí).* Zhuo Wenjun, *White-Hair Lament*: "…white-haired, never to part (離)."
- *Rhymes as 羅 (luó), fanqie 良何.* Han Yu, *Tomb inscription for Adjutant Pei*: "branches divided, the clan dispersed (離), each became a great house" (家 sounding 歌).

**Textual notes.** The *Qu li* note should read "兩相麗謂之離"; the Yang Xiong rhapsody should be the *Yulie fu*, not *Jiaolie fu*; and the Sima Xiangru passage should be the *Dalin fu*, not *Shanglin fu*.
