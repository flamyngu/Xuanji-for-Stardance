**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls з”і, middle; Tiger radical иҷҚ; p.1074 #24)*

**Ancient graphs.** р ҲҢ, еҗі.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie йҒҮдҝұ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie е…ғдҝұ вҖ” sounding like ж„ҡ (yГә).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the zouyu (йЁ¶иҷһ): a white tiger with black markings, its tail longer than its body, a benevolent beast that eats only the flesh of creatures already dead." Shijing, *Zhounan*: "ah, alas, the zouyu (йЁ¶иҷһ)!"
- *Also, to anticipate, to reckon (еәҰ).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "be watchful and wary when there seems no cause for concern (з„Ўиҷһ)." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 17: "in matters of the border, carefully guard your own and provide against the unforeseen (дёҚиҷһ)."
- *Also, to set at rest (е®ү).* Note to Yili, *Shi yu li*: "when an officer has buried his father or mother, he welcomes the soul back, and at noon sacrifices to it in the funeral chamber to set it at rest (е®үд№Ӣ)."
- *Also, to err, to misgive (иӘӨ).* Shijing, *Lusong*: "without disloyalty, without misgiving (иҷһ), God watches over you"; subcommentary: "it says all the world turns to Zhou, with no divided heart, no doubt or error."
- *Also, a matter requiring readiness (еӮҷ).* Jin yu: "Duke Wen of Wey had the threat (иҷһ) of Xing and the Di."
- *Also, joy (жЁӮ).* Mengzi: "the people of a hegemon are merry and content (й©©иҷһ)"; Zhao Qi's note: "the hegemon does good and cares for the people, his kindness plainly seen and easily known, so the people are glad and rejoice (й©©иҷһ) in it."
- *Also* Boya: "to help (еҠ©); to look toward (жңӣ); to choose (ж“Ү)."
- *Also* *Yupian*: "to possess (жңү); to control (е°Ҳ)."
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: "to ponder (ж…®); to gauge (жё¬)."
- *Also an office-name.* Yijing, *Zhun*: "going after deer without a forester (иҷһ)"; note: "it means the forester-official (иҷһе®ҳ)." Zhouli, *Tianguan, Da zai*: "the foresters and weights-officers (иҷһиЎЎ) prepare the materials of hill and marsh"; subcommentary: "those who have charge of hills and marshes are called иҷһ (foresters)."
- *Also a state-name.* Shijing, *Daya*: "Yu and Rui (иҷһиҠ®) brought their dispute to settlement." Zuozhuan note: "the state of Yu (иҷһ) was in Daiyang county of Hedong." Also a county-name. Jin shu, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yu county (иҷһзёЈ) belonged to the principality of Liang."
- *Also a surname.* Qianfu lun: "the Emperor Shun's surname was Yu (иҷһ)." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 3: "Jibo, Zhibing, Yusui (иҷһйҒӮ), Boxi"; note: "the four men were all descendants of Shun." Tongzhi, *Shizu lГјe*: "Yu enfeoffed Shangjun's son at Yucheng (иҷһеҹҺ) as a feudal lord, and his descendants took the state-name as their clan."
- *Also иҷһж·ө (Yuyuan), a place-name.* Huainanzi, *Tianwen*: "when the sun reaches Yuyuan (иҷһж·ө), this is the 'high-pounding' hour."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie е…ғе…·, sounding like йҒҮ (yГ№). Yang Xiong, *Changyang fu*: "upholding the glory of the Supreme Honored One, following the measures of Wen and Wu, restoring the hunting-grounds of the Three Kings and returning to the parks (иҷһ) of the Five Emperors."
- *Also the same as г•Ұ.* Shiji, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "neither boastful (иҷһ) nor proud"; the Suoyin reads it и©ұ.
- *Also interchangeable with еҗҫ.* Wuqiu Shouwang (еҗҫдёҳеЈҪзҺӢ) вҖ” the Shuijing zhu writes Yuqiu Shouwang (иҷһдёҳеЈҪзҺӢ). Wang Yinglin, *Shi kao*: "Zouyu (й„’иҷһ) is sometimes written Zouwu (йЁ¶еҗҫ); see Liu Fang's Shiyi shu." The Zhiyin: vulgarly written иҷһ.

**Textual notes.** In the Yang Xiong, *Changyang fu* citation, ж—Ҙ should read з”° (per the original).
