**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, lower; Grain radical 禾; p.859 #24)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 子昔. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 資息 — sounding like 跡 (jī).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To gather, heap up (聚)." *Zengyun*: "to pile up (累); to heap and stack (堆𤴁)." Yijing, *Da you*: "the great cart bears the load, what is heaped within (積中) does not fail." Also *Sheng* hexagram: "by heaping the small (積小) it grows high and great." Shijing, *Zhou song*: "they heaped it up (積之), grain upon grain." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in mid-autumn, the officers are charged to urge the people to heap up much store (積聚)." Former Han, *Treatise on Food and Money*: "stores laid up (積貯) are the great lifeline of the realm."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Yili, *Shi guan li*: "the leather cap and the plain pleated skirt (素積)"; Zheng's note: "積 is like to pleat (辟) — using plain silk for the skirt, gathered and pleated at the waist."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 則歷, sounding like 績 (jì). Also "to gather."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 子智. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 資四 — sounding like 恣 (zì). To gather; to store up (儲蓄). Shijing, *Daya*: "then they stored (積), then they granaried." Zhouli, *Tian guan, Xiao zai*: "he managed the pen-provisions and the stockpiles (委積)"; note: "委積 means the penned beasts, grain, firewood, and fodder supplied for guests' road-use." Also *Da situ*: "he had the wilds repair the roads and lay up stores (委積)"; note: "the little is 委, the much is 積, both to supply guests."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 子 (zǐ), fanqie 恣此.* Shijing, *Zhou song*: "full and solid are its stores (積), myriads and millions and more."
