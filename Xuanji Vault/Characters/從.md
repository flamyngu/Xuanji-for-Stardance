**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Step radical 彳; p.368 #18)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 疾容. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 牆容 — the level tone of 俗 (cóng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** Originally written 从. "To hearken to one another (相聽)." Shu, *Yi ji*: "do not comply to my face (面從)." *Yue ming*: "when the ruler complies with (從) remonstrance, he is a sage."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to draw near, attach to (就)." Yijing, *Qian* hexagram: "the clouds follow (從) the dragon, the wind follows the tiger." Liji, *Qu li*: "in taking counsel of an elder, one must bear stool and staff and follow (從) him."
- *Also* Erya, *Shi gu*: "from (自)." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "from (從) whom, pray, comes it?"; Jian: "it says: he who slanders me — from whom does this talk arise?" Jinshu, *Annals of Emperor Ming*: "I have not heard of a man coming from (從) beside the sun."
- *Surname.* *Guangyun*: "the Han had General Cong (從公)." *He shi xingyuan*: "now people of Dongguan."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 七恭 — the level tone of 促 (cōng). *Guangyun*: "at ease (從容)." *Zheng Yun*: "從容, a look of ease and leisure." Shu, *Jun Chen*: "at ease (從容) and harmonious." Liji, *Zhongyong*: "at ease (從容) he hits the Mean."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 書容, sounding like 舂 (chōng). 從容, the sense of long duration. Liji, *Xue ji*: "wait for its lingering (從容), and only then draw out its full sound."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 將容, sounding like 蹤 (zōng). East-west is called 衡 (crosswise), north-south is called 從 (lengthwise). Shijing, *Qi feng*: "crosswise and lengthwise (衡從) his furrows." Shiji, *Biography of Su Qin*: "if the Lengthwise (從, the vertical alliance) joins, then Chu is king; if the Crosswise (衡) is formed, then Qin is emperor."
- *Also* interchangeable with 蹤 (a trace). Shiji, *Biography of Nie Zheng*: "he mutilated himself to cut off the trail (從)." Former Han, *Biography of Zhang Tang*: "where did the trail (從迹) start?"
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 祖動, sounding like 總 (zǒng). A look of being very high. *Yun Hui*: "the hair-knot high." Liji, *Tan gong*: "do not be lofty and stiff (從從) in your manner."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 鋤江 — the level tone of 浞 (chuáng). The sense the same.
- *Also* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 慈用. *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 才用 — the departing tone of 俗 (cóng). *Shuowen*: originally written 𨑢. "To go in train, to follow after (隨行)." Shijing, *Qi feng*: "his train (從) like a cloud." Lunyu: "those who follow (從) me — is it You?"
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: to attend the Son of Heaven is called fa-cong (法從, the lawful train) or shi-cong (侍從, the attendant train). Shu, *Jiong ming*: "his attendants and grooms and train (僕從)." Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "the Brilliant Companion Zhao, whenever the Emperor went to Ganquan, always rode in the lawful train (法從)"; note: "Yan Shigu says: by the lawful rule it should be sounded 從; one account: 從 is the imperial state-carriage (法駕)." Later Han, *Treatise on Officialdom*: "the Feathered-Forest Gentlemen have charge of the night-guard and attendant train (侍從)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie 似用, sounding like 頌 (sòng). Of the same clan (同宗). Erya, *Shi qin*: "a father's eldest-uncle and younger-uncle are the cong-grandfather, the grand-uncle (從祖, 祖父); a father's eldest-aunt and younger-aunt are the cong-grandmother, the grand-aunt." *Shiming*: "the cong-grandparents (從祖父母) — it means parting from one's own near grandfather and going down a side line; it also means being named by following (隨從) one's own grandfather."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 子用 — same as 縱 (to let loose). Liji, *Qu li*: "desires may not be let loose (從)." Lunyu: "loosed out (從), it is pure and unbroken."

**Textual note.** Shijing, *Qi feng*, "crosswise and lengthwise his furrows (從衡其畝)" — checked against the original, 從衡 is corrected to 衡從.
