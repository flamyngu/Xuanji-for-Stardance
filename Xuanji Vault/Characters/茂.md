**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 申, upper; Grass radical 艸; p.1025 #20)*

**Ancient graph.** 楙.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 莫𠊱 — sounding like 懋 (mào).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "grass abundant and flourishing." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "like the luxuriance (茂) of pine and cypress." Yijing, *Wuwang*: "the former kings thereby flourishingly (茂) matched the seasons and nurtured the myriad things"; note: "茂 means abundant (盛)."
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "sowing the fine yellow grain (黃茂)"; note: "茂 means fine (美)." Former Han, *Biography of Liu Pi, King of Wu*: "in season he inquired after the men of excellent talent (茂才)"; note: "men of fine ability."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "茂 means to encourage (勉)." Zhou yu: "the former kings, toward the people, earnestly (茂) rectified their virtue and enriched their nature."
- *Also* Baihu tong: "five men make a 茂."
- *Also* Erya, *Shitian*: "when the Grand Year is in xu (戌) it is called Yanmao (閹茂)."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Maoling (茂陵) in Youfufeng"; note: "originally Mao-town (茂鄕) of Huaili."
- *Also a surname.* *Zhengzitong*: "the Han had the Juyang magistrate Mao Zhen (茂眞)."
- *Also interchangeable with 懋.* Former Han, *Biography of Dong Zhongshu*: "the Shu says, 'be diligent (茂)! be diligent!'"
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: pronounced 畝 (mǔ). Former Han, *Ban Gu's Postface*: "branch and leaf great and luxuriant (碩茂)"; Yan Shigu's note: "茂, fanqie 莫口."
- *Also* the *Shi benyin*: "'how fine (茂) you are,' read as 耄 (mào). Anciently 茂 and 卯 sounded alike, hence the Shiji, *Lüshu* says: '丣 means 茂 (flourishing).'"
- *Also* the *Maoshi guyin kao*: pronounced 每 (měi). Emperor Wu of Wei, *Bu chu xia men xing*: "the trees grow in thickets, the hundred grasses lush and rife (茂); the autumn wind sighs and rustles, the great waves surge up."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 滿補, sounding like 母 (mǔ). Yilin: "in the sixth month of summer, the branches and leaves grow rife (茂); the luan-bird takes shelter, and the Earl of Shao escapes the heat."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 莫故, sounding like 暮 (mù). Bai Juyi's poem: "there is a tree named cherry, that, well-rooted, early grows lush (茂); its leaves dense, it alone catches the sun; its flowers thick, it most receives the dew."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: in the He rhyme, pronounced 末 (mò). King Wu of Zhou, *Bi ming* (the Brush-inscription): "the bristle-hairs, thick and fine (茂茂); sunk in water, one may yet get free; sunk in writing, there is no escaping alive." The Zhiyin: written 茂.

**On the graph.** The graph 茂 is written with the grass radical 艸.
