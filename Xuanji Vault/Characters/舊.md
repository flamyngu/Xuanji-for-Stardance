**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Mortar radical 臼; p.1006 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 巨救. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 巨又 — sounding like 柩 (jiù).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "the *chijiu* (鴟舊) is the *jiuliu* [owl]." Xu [Kai]: "that is the eared owl."
- *Old, former (故).* *Guangyun.* *Zengyun*: "a term opposed to 'new.'" Shijing, *Binfeng*: "when new, how very fair — and when old (舊), what then?" Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "the men chanted, 'the meadowland lush and green — let go the old (舊) and plan the new.'" Gongyang zhuan, Zhuang yr 29: "what is 'renewing the Yan stables'? — repairing the old (舊)."
- *Long [in time] (久).* Shijing, *Daya*: "ah, young man, I tell you of old (舊) [ways]"; gloss: "舊 means long-standing."
- *Of former times; a venerable elder (老宿).* *Yun Hui.*
- *A surname.* The Han had Jiu Qiang, governor of Shangdang.
- *Same as 柩 (coffin).* Jinshu, *Biography of Cai Gui*: "the coffin (舊) of King Ling of Yan"; 舊 is anciently used interchangeably with 柩.
- *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 巨九, sounding like 臼 (jiù); same sense.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 許尤, sounding like 休 (xiū) — same as 鵂 (the owl).

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 巨己, sounding like 技 (jì).* Shijing, *Daya*: "it is not that God is untimely — it is that Yin used not the old [ways] (舊)" (時 sounds 氏). Also: "the men of today, do they not still have the old (舊)?" (rhyming above with 里).
- *Rhymes, fanqie 巨寄, sounding like 忌 (jì).* Wei Xuancheng's poem: "...he looked on me fallen and hurt, and restored my rank to its old [place] (舊)." Guanzi, *Mumin*: "if one reveres not the ancestral and the old (祖舊), then filial and brotherly duty is not complete." A note: by the Tang yun zheng, 舊 sounds 忌, so 忌 is also a proper sound, not merely a rhyming one.
- *Rhymes, fanqie 去九, sounding like 糗 (qiǔ).* Former Han, *Author's Postface*: "...the withered poplar puts forth flowers — how can it be as of old (舊)?
