**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, middle; Halberd radical 戈; p.412 #05)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢦬.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 古拜. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 居拜 — sounding like 介 (jiè).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to be on guard, to warn (警)." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "be watchful and wary (警戒) when there is no apparent cause."
- *Also, to admonish, to instruct (諭).* Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "admonish (戒) them with the promise of blessing."
- *Also, to inform, to notify (告).* Yili, *Shi guan li*: "the host notifies (戒) the guests"; note: "to inform." *Pin li*: "he notifies (戒) the chief assistant likewise"; note: "like a command."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "to be careful (愼); to make ready (具)." Also: to provide against (備). Yijing, *Cui*: "guard (戒) against the unforeseen"; note: "to provide against the unforeseen."
- *Also* Yijing, *Xici*: "the sage thereby purifies and warns himself (齊戒)"; note: "to cleanse the heart is 齊, to guard against trouble is 戒"; Zhu Xi's *Benyi*: "limpid and wholly single is called 齊, solemnly watchful and wary is called 戒."
- *Also, to keep guard (守).* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Zhanggu*: "by night, the three watch-drums to call the guard (號戒)"; note: "it means beating the drum on the night-rounds to keep watch." Also Sima fa: "the drum sounded three rolls at midnight is called the dawn-watch (晨戒)."
- *Also interchangeable with 誡.* Yijing, *Xici*: "a small chastening and a great warning (誡)." Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "the front cart overturns, the rear cart takes warning (誡)."
- *Also the same as 界 (boundary).* Shiji, *Tianguan shu*: "a comet at the River's bound (河戒)." Also Tang shu, *Treatise on Astronomy*: "the Yangtze and the Yellow River make the two bounds, north and south (兩戒)."
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as fanqie 居吏, sounding like 記 (jì). Liutao: "if the general is not ever on guard (戒), then the three armies lose their readiness."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 紀力, sounding like 亟 (jí). Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "are we not daily on guard (戒)? the Xianyun press hard."

**Textual notes.** In the Yijing, *Xici* citation, 齋 should read 齊; and in the Sima fa citation, 發戒 should read 晨戒.
