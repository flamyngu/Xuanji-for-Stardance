**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Sun radical 日; p.491 #12)*

**Ancient graph.** 朙.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 武兵. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 眉兵 — sounding like 鳴 (míng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to illumine (照)." Yijing, *Xici*: "sun and moon push each other, and brightness (明) is born"; also, "of hanging images displaying brightness, none is greater than sun and moon"; subcommentary: "sun and moon at the centre shine over all, no dark unlit, hence 明." Shiji, *Lishu*: "sun and moon being complete, hence brightness; 明 means 'eldest, the beginning (孟).'"
- Yijing, *Qian*: "the Great Brightness (大明) ends and begins"; subcommentary: "大明 comprehends the beginning and end of the myriad things." Also *Qian*: "the world is patterned and bright (文明)"; subcommentary: "having pattern and brightness."
- Shu, *Yao dian*: "reverent, bright (明), accomplished, thoughtful, calm"; subcommentary: "to shine over the four quarters is 明." *Shun dian*: "demote the dark and promote the bright (明)"; tradition: "advance the bright [i.e. worthy]." *Tai jia*: "looking afar is 明"; subcommentary: "to scrutinise right and wrong." *Hong fan*: "seeing is called 明"; tradition: "must be clear and discerning."
- Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the sacrificial service most complete (孔明)"; *Jian*: "明 is like 備 (well-prepared)." *Daya*: "bright and discerning (明明) here below"; tradition: "明明, discerning"; Erya subcommentary: "明明 means very bright."
- Liji, *Tan gong*: "called 'spirit-vessels (明器),' treating [the dead] as spiritually numinous." *Li yun*: "thus the ruler is one whom [men] honour (明)"; subcommentary: "明 is like 尊." *Yue ji*: "the maker is called sage, the transmitter is called 明"; subcommentary: "明, one who discerns and argues right and wrong." Han Feizi, *Nan san*: "to know the subtle is called 明." *Guangyun*: "luminous (昭); penetrating (通)."
- *A star.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in the east is 啓明"; tradition: "the morning star at dawn is called 啓明 (Opener of Brightness)." Also *Xiaoya*: "from daybreak (明發) sleepless"; subcommentary: "as the sky is about to brighten, light stirs forth." Zhengzitong: "厥明 and 質明 both mean the same as 昧爽 (daybreak)."
- *Surname.* *Xingshi jijiu pian*: the Ming clan — Ming Pu of Pingyuan; the Jin Xun Xi's retainer Ming Yu.
- *= 盟 (covenant).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "not to be sworn with (明)"; *Jian*: "明 should be 盟."
- *= 孟.* Zhouli, *Zhifangshi* note: "望諸, the 明都"; *Shiwen*: "明都 — the *Yu gong* writes 孟豬; now read per the Shu."
- Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "葭明 in Guanghan commandery"; note: Shigu, "明 sounds 萌 (méng)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 謨郞 (máng).* Shu, *Yi ji*: "the head is bright (明) indeed, the limbs are good indeed, all affairs are at ease indeed." Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "…the night gleaming bright, and now it dawns (明)."
- *Rhymes, fanqie 彌延 (mián).* *Daozang* hymn: "…the *yin* essence shines (明) no more."
