> 《康熙字典》〈亥集上〉【骨字部】頁1451第06    〔古文〕：𩪆【唐韻】【正韻】他禮切【集韻】【韻會】土禮切，𠀤涕上聲。【說文】總十二屬也。【釋名】體，第也。骨肉毛血表裏大小相次第也。【廣韻】四支也。【禮·中庸】動乎四體。　又【易·文言】君子體仁，足以長人。【疏】體包仁道。　又【書·畢命】辭尚體要。【註】辭以理實爲要。　又【詩·衞風】爾卜爾筮，體無咎言。【傳】體，兆卦之體。　又【詩·大雅】方苞方體，維葉泥泥。【箋】體，成形也。　又【周禮·天官】體國經野。【註】體，猶分也。　又【周禮·天官·內饔】辨體名肉物。【註】體名，脊脅臂臑之屬。　又【禮·文王世子】外朝以官體異姓也。【註】體，猶連結也。　又【禮·學記】就賢體遠。【註】體，猶親也。　又【禮·中庸】體物而不可遺。【註】猶生也。　又【禮·中庸】體羣臣也。【註】猶接納也。　又【左傳·昭二十年】聲亦如味，一氣二體。【疏】樂之動身體者，唯有舞耳。舞者有文武二體。【廣韻】俗作軆。【集韻】作躰。【增韻】俗作体，非。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 亥, upper; Bone radical 骨; p.1451 #06)*

**Ancient graph.** 𩪆.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 他禮. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 土禮 — the rising tone of 涕 (tǐ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The sum of the twelve members (總十二屬)." *Shiming*: "體 means a sequence (第); bone, flesh, hair, and blood, outer and inner, great and small, follow one another in order." *Guangyun*: "the four limbs (四支)." Liji, *Zhongyong*: "it stirs in the four limbs (四體)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Yijing, *Wen yan*: "the gentleman embodies benevolence (體仁), enough to be the head of men"; subcommentary: "his body embraces the way of benevolence."
- *Also* Shu, *Bi ming*: "in speech, value the essential substance (體要)"; note: "speech takes principle and substance as its essential."
- *Also* Shijing, *Wei feng*: "you divined by shell and by stalk, and the body of the augury (體) had no ill word"; tradition: "體 is the body of the augured hexagram."
- *Also* Shijing, *Daya*: "now in bud, now formed (方苞方體), the leaves soft and lush"; Jian: "體 means to take complete form."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Tian guan*: "he laid out the state and ordered the fields (體國經野)"; note: "體 is like to divide."
- *Also* Zhouli, *Tian guan, Nei yong*: "he distinguished the parts and named the meats (體名肉物)"; note: "the part-names (體名) are such as the spine, the ribs, the foreleg, and the hock."
- *Also* Liji, *Wen wang shizi*: "at the outer court they joined (體) the offices to those of other surnames"; note: "體 is like to link and bind."
- *Also* Liji, *Xue ji*: "draw near the worthy and embrace the distant (就賢體遠)"; note: "體 is like to make intimate."
- *Also* Liji, *Zhongyong*: "it embodies things and cannot be left out (體物而不可遺)"; note: "like to give life."
- *Also* Liji, *Zhongyong*: "to take to heart the host of ministers (體羣臣)"; note: "like to receive and admit."
- *Also* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 20: "sound too is like taste — one breath, two members (一氣二體)"; subcommentary: "what stirs the body in music is dance alone; and dance has two members, the civil and the martial." *Guangyun*: commonly written 軆. *Ji Yun*: written 躰. *Zengyun*: commonly written 体, which is wrong.
