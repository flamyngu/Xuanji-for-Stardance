**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Short-thread radical 幺; p.342 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*: fanqie 於虯. *Yun Hui*: fanqie 幺虯. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 於尤 — sounding like 呦 (yōu).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "hidden, secluded (幽隱)." Yijing, *Lü*: "the recluse (幽人), steadfast and fortunate"; subcommentary: "the hidden man, keeping the Way, is steadfast and fortunate." Liji, *Ruxing*: "[the scholar] dwells in seclusion (幽) and is not dissolute"; subcommentary: "though the gentleman lives hidden, he ever keeps himself ordered and unswerving." Later Han, edict of Emperor Zhang, Zhanghe 1: "his light shines into the six dark places (六幽)"; note: "the hidden recesses of the six directions."
- *Minute (微).* *Erya, Shigu*; subcommentary: "幽 is deep and minute." Shiji, *Treatise on Music*: "reaching the utmost depth (幽) yet not obscured."
- *Deep and far (深遠).* *Yupian.* Yijing, *Xici*: "there is no far or near, dark (幽) or deep [it does not reach]"; subcommentary: "even to the recesses dark and deep, all is told." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "deep and far (幽幽) is the southern hill"; note: "幽幽 is deep and far."
- *Dark, not bright (不明, 闇).* *Yupian*, *Zheng Yun.* Shu, *Shundian*: "demote and promote the dark (幽) and the bright"; note: "to put down the dark, raise up the bright." Liji, *Tangong*: "[at burial] one looks toward the unseen (幽), seeking the dead in the way of ghosts and spirits"; note: "ghosts and spirits dwell in the dark."
- *To imprison (囚).* *Zheng Yun.* Shiji, the Grand Historian's Preface: "imprisoned (幽) in fetters." Yang Yun, *Letter to Sun Huizong*: "my person confined (幽) at the northern tower."
- *A prefecture-name.* Shu, *Shundian*: "he founded the twelve provinces"; tradition: "after Yu's flood-works, Shun split Jizhou into Youzhou (幽州) and Bingzhou." Erya, *Shidi*: "Yan is Youzhou"; subcommentary: "Yan's air is deep and essential, its nature swift and fierce, hence 幽; 幽 means essential."
- *A place-name.* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 16: "they swore a joint covenant at You (幽)"; note: "You, a Song place."
- *A state-name.* Shanhaijing: "in the great wilds is the country of Siyou (思幽), where the thinking-men take no wives and the thinking-women no husbands"; note: "its people conceive by thought-stirring and *qi*-union alone, bearing children without mating."
- *A surname.* *Guangyun*: "found in the Xingyuan."
- *Interchangeable with 黝 (jet-black).* *Ji Yun*: "黝 is sometimes written 幽." Liji, *Yuzao*: "at the first command, dark-red knee-cover and black (幽) crossbar; at the second, red knee-cover and black crossbar"; note: "幽 is read as the 黝 of 黝黑 (jet-black)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 於交, sounding like 窔 (yào).* The Daozang hymn: "...the six breaths turn through the deep dark (重幽); my bounds — how can they be exhausted? — they make you in the end not wither."
- *Rhymes (a black colour).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the marsh-mulberry is lovely, its leaves dark (幽); now I have seen the gentleman, his good fame holds firm"; tradition: "幽, a black colour."

**Textual note.** "望及諸幽" should read "望反諸幽".
