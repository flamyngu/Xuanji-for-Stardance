**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, middle; Knight radical 士; p.242 #38)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 鉏里 — sounding like 仕 (shì).

**Etymology and senses.** Of the four classes of people, the *shi* (gentleman) stands first. Shijing, *Daya*: "praised and pre-eminent, these gentlemen (士)." Liji, *Wangzhi*: "the districts are ordered to assess their distinguished men (秀士) and send them up to the Minister of Education — these are the 'select men' (選士). The Minister assesses the finest of the select men and sends them up to the schools — these are the 'eminent men' (俊士). Those sent up to the Minister are exempt from district service; those sent up to the schools are exempt from the Minister — these are the 'finished men' (造士). The Grand Director of Music assesses the finest of the finished men and sends them up to the Minister of War — these are the 'advanced men' (進士)."
- *A general name for officers.* Shu, *Lizheng*: "the many regular good officers (吉士)." Liji, *Wangzhi*: "the Son of Heaven's primary officers (元士); the feudal lords' upper, middle, and lower officers."
- *A judicial officer.* Kong Anguo: "士 is the judge (理官), who seeks the rights and wrongs of a case." Shu, *Shundian*: "the Emperor said, 'Gao Yao, you shall be the Judge (士).'" Zuozhuan, Xi yr 28: "Shi Rong served as the Grand Judge (大士)."
- *A stripped nobleman (公士).* By Han institution, one who had held the rank of duke or marquis and was stripped of it was called a 公士.
- *Tripod-lifting men of might (鼎士).* Former Han, *Biography of Zou Yang*; note: "those able to lift a cauldron."
- *Heaven-men (天士).* Former Han, *Biography of Li Xun*; note: "those who can know the Way of Heaven."
- *A wine-officer (酒士).* Former Han, *Biography of Li Ye*: "Wang Mang made Ye a wine-officer"; note: "at that time the government sold wine, hence the post."
- *Attendant gentlemen.* Zizhi tongjian: "Tang's Du Ruhui and the other eighteen scholars (學士) were said at the time to have 'ascended to Yingzhou.'"
- *Soldiers (士卒).* Zuozhuan, note on the 'Qiujia': "one war-chariot has three armoured men (甲士) and seventy-two foot-soldiers."
- *A general term for a man.* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "thronging, their men (士)."
- *A lady of gentlemanly conduct (女士).* Shijing, *Daya*: "[Heaven] bestows on us lady-gentlemen (女士)."
- *Affairs (事).* Guanzi, *Mumin*: "there is the gentleman's constant (士經)"; note: "士 means affairs, 經 the constant."
- *A Buddhist epithet.* In Buddhist writings a disciple diligent in Buddha-practice is called a 德士 (virtue-gentleman) or 無上士 (unsurpassed gentleman).
- *A god-image (木居士).* Colloquially a sculpted idol is called a 木居士 (wood-dwelling gentleman). Han Yu's poem: "by chance it was inscribed 'Wood-dwelling Gentleman,' and at once endless people came seeking blessings."
- *A state name.* Shanhaijing: "in the great wilds of the west is a country named Shushi (淑士)."
- *A gentlemen's district (士鄉).* Later Han, *Biography of Zheng Xuan*: "anciently Qi established gentlemen's districts"; note: "Guan Zhong, minister to Duke Huan, divided the state into twenty-one districts — six of artisans and merchants, fifteen of gentlemen."
- *A county name.* Yongshi (勇士) county, in Tianshui commandery; see Later Han, *Account of the Western Qiang*.
- *A surname.* The descendants of Shi Wei, a scion of Tao-Tang [Yao], became the Shi clan; see the *Tongpu*. Also the compound surname Shisun: the Han had Shisun Rui, a man of Fufeng.
- *Interchangeable with 事 (to serve).* Shu, *Luogao*: "they came to render service (士) at Zhou"; note: "all came to take up the labour." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "no more service (士) of holding the gag"; note: "henceforth they need not march in ranks with the gag in mouth."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 主矩, sounding like 雨 (yǔ).* Shijing, *Daya*: "blazing and bright, the king commands his high officers (卿士)" — rhyming below with 父, which here sounds 甫 (fǔ).

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "士 means affairs; numbers begin at one and end at ten; it follows 一 (one) and 十 (ten)." *Ji Yun*: "originally written 𣎶." It is also interchangeable with 仕 (to serve in office).
