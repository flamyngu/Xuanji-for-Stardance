**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Inch radical 寸; p.295 #01)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𠧡, 𠧢, 𡵏.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 職緣. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 朱遄. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 朱緣 — sounding like 磚 (zhuān).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A six-inch tablet (六寸簿); it follows 寸 (inch) with 叀 as phonetic." Xu (Kai): "簿 is a record-tablet." *Guangyun*: "single (壹); sincere (誠)." *Zengyun*: "pure and earnest (純篤)." Yijing, *Xi ci*: "Qian, in its stillness, is concentrated (專)." Mengzi: "if one does not concentrate (專) the heart and apply the will, one will not get it."

**Senses.**
- *Alone, sole (獨).* Shu, *Yue ming*: "do not let Aheng monopolize (專) the fair fame of Shang alone." Also Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 12: "Zige replied: 'these four states alone (專) suffice to fear.'"
- *To arrogate; to be self-willed (擅, 自是).* Zhongyong: "base, yet fond of acting on one's own (自專)." Liji, *Fang ji*: "while the parents live, gifts do not reach to carriage and horse, to show one dares not act alone (專)." Also Zuozhuan, Huan yr 15: "Zhai Zhong was high-handed (專), and the Earl of Zheng was troubled by it."
- *Surname.* The Wu man Zhuan Zhu (專諸).
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 徒官, sounding like 團 (tuán). "To gather (聚)." Zhouli, *Di guan, Da situ*: "its people are clustered (專) and grow tall."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 珍 (zhēn), fanqie 陟鄰.* *Poem written for the wife of Jiao Zhongqing*: "serving and obeying the husband's mother, in coming and going I dared take nothing on myself (專); by day and night I toiled at work and rest, forlorn and bound in bitter toil." *Yun Hui*: "interchangeable with 顓 and 剸." *Zhengzitong*: "專 and 叀 are interchangeable."

**Textual note.** "Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 3: 'these four states alone suffice to fear,' adding 'how much more with Chu'" — a note: "how much more with Chu" leaves the sense incomplete, and the year is wrong; it is corrected to Zhao yr 12, "Zige replied: 'these four states alone suffice to fear.'"
