**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, lower; Walk radical 辵; p.1258 #04)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢓶.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 他紅. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 他東 — the level tone of 統 (tōng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to reach through (達)." *Zheng Yun*: "to penetrate (徹)." Yijing, *Xici*: "he first made the eight trigrams, to penetrate (通) the powers of the spirits." Liji, *Xueji*: "to know by category and reach through (通達)."
- *To prosper, go smoothly (亨, 順).* Liji, *Ruxing*: "he reaches upward (通) and is not hemmed in"; note: "in office he reaches up to the lord, not hemmed in by any lack of virtue." Yijing, *Jie* hexagram: "he does not leave the courtyard gate — he knows the open (通) and the blocked."
- *Free-flowing (暢).* Erya: "the four seasons in harmony make 通正"; note: "通 is level and free-flowing."
- *To total (總).* Liji, *Wangzhi*: "by the aggregate (通) of thirty years they regulated the state's expenditure"; note: "totalling the revenue of thirty years, so that there be ten years' surplus."
- *To open up (開).* Former Han, *Biography of He Wu*: "he opened (通) the office of the Three Dukes"; note: "he established it anew."
- *To set forth (陳).* Former Han, *Biography of Xiahou Sheng*: "the master set forth (通) upright words"; note: "he expounded them."
- *Friendly intercourse.* People's friendly dealings are called 通. Former Han, *Biography of Ji Bu*: "have no dealings (通) with any who is not a worthy elder." Also *Biography of Lu Jia*: "they split the tally and exchanged (通) envoys."
- *Book-titles.* The Baihu tong, by Ban Gu; the Fengsu tong, by Ying Shao.
- *A complete copy.* A document whole from head to tail is one 通 (copy). Later Han, *Biography of Cui Shi*: "he ought to write out one copy (一通)."
- *Of unmixed colour.* A thing of pure colour is called 通. Zhouli, *Chunguan, Sichang*: "plain (通) silk makes the *zhan* banner"; note: "通帛, silk with no other ornament."
- *The through-drum (通鼓).* Zhouli, *Diguan, Guren*: "with the metal bell to pass on the drumming (通鼓)"; subcommentary: "the Master of Horse shakes the bell, and from the general down all beat their drums, hence 'through-drum.'"
- *A land-measure.* Former Han, *Treatise on Punishments*: "a *li* square is a *jing*; ten *jing* make a 通."
- *A prefecture-name.* *Guangyun.*
- *Horse-dung.* *Yun Hui*: horse-dung is called 通. Later Han, *Biography of Dai Jiu*: "they smoked him with horse-dung (馬通)."
- *Illicit intercourse.* Zuozhuan, Du Yu's note: illicit intercourse is called 通.
- *Rhymes as 湯 (tāng), fanqie 他郞.* Dongfang Shuo, *Seven Remonstrances*: "my body laid low by sickness, day by day I grieve; my feelings sunk and pressed down, never lifted; among the throng there is none with whom to discuss the Way — I grieve that my spirit finds no thoroughfare (通)."

**Textual notes.** In Erya, "四時通政" — the original reads "四時和爲通正"; the graphs 和爲 cannot be deleted, corrected to "四時和爲通正"; and as Erya has no *Shisui* chapter, that citation is dropped. In the Baihu tong, "班彪著" — 班彪 should read 班固. In "後漢崔實傳：政論一通" — should read "宜寫一通." In "前漢地理志：方里爲井，井十爲通" — this comes from the *Treatise on Punishments*, not the *Treatise on Geography*. In "後漢戴就傳：傅以馬通" — corrected to "以馬通薰之."
