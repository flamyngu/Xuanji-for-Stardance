**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Roof radical 宀; p.291 #04)*

**Ancient graphs.** 宲, 𡾍.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Guangyun*: fanqie 神質. *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 食質 — sounding like 失 (shí).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "實 means rich, full (富); it follows 宀 (roof) and 貫; 貫 is goods and cowries (貨貝)." *Guangyun*: "sincere (誠); full (滿)." *Zengyun*: "to fill (充); the opposite of empty." Yijing, *Original Meaning*: "Qian, being one, is solid (實); Kun, being two, is empty." Mengzi: "to be filled full (充實) is called beauty; filled full and with radiance is called greatness." Song, Cheng Yi: "when the heart has a master it is solid (實); being solid, outer troubles cannot enter."

**Senses.**
- *Blossom and fruit (華實).* *Discourses of Jin*: "blossom (華), then, is flourishing; not knowing the fruit (實) — I beg to attend to the fruit." Shiji, *Biography of Shang Yang*: "fair-seeming words are blossom (華); words that hit home are fruit (實)."
- *Of a thing coming to fruit (物成實).* Erya, *Shi cao*: "the gua-luo, the kualou" — 實 being the seed (子). Liji, *Yue ling*: "in late spring, they then pray for the wheat's ripening (祈實)"; note: "it means, while it is in ear, to seek its ripening."
- *Goods, sundry things (品物).* Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 22: "the courtyard filled (庭實) with a hundred kinds"; note: "what fills the courtyard, set out in a hundred sorts — meaning the goods complete." Also Xiang yr 31: "if they bring it in, it is the lord's treasury-store (府實)."
- *軍實 (military stores).* Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 24: "at the Qi sacrifice to the soil, they reviewed the army stores (軍實)"; Du's note: "sacrificing to the soil, they took occasion to count over the weapons."
- *Also* Yili, *Te sheng kui shi*: "they fill the dou and bian dishes (實豆籩)"; note: "it means taking up the bian and dou and filling them." Zhouli, *Chun guan, Xiao zongbo*, note: "the dou's contents are filled in jars, the gui's contents are filled in baskets."
- *To count, tally fully (具數).* Shiji, *Annals of the First Emperor*: "he had the black-headed people themselves register their fields (自實田)"; note: "it means ordering the people themselves to declare the true acreage."
- *Also* *Tang liudian*: "in all wards there is the hand-register law (手實法): at year's end, the people's ages and the breadth of their lands are listed to make the village-account."
- *To verify (驗).* Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "he had each verify (實) the rank of the two-thousand-bushel officials, down to the yellow-cord ranks."
- *Deeds, facts (事跡).* Shiji, *Biography of Zhuang Zhou*: "all are empty words, with no facts (事實)." *Biography of Han Fei*: "on the contrary he promotes the frivolous and parasitic, setting them above men of solid merit (功實)."
- *To befit, correspond (當).* Shu, *Lü xing*: "examine and verify (實) their crimes"; note: "make them correspond with the law."
- *To be (是).* Shijing, *Daya*: "truly walls (實墉) and truly moats (實壑)."
- *Surname.*
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: fanqie 脂利, sounding like 至 (zhì) — same as 至. Liji, *Za ji*: "in condolence to the principal heir one says: 'your humble grandee, an outside private connection of my son, has died unsalaried, and has sent me, So-and-so, hither (實)'"; note: "it means, on account of the condolence, to come hither." *Zengyun*: "實 is also written 寔." *Yun Hui*: "實 and 寔 are divided into two."

**Textual notes.** Erya, *Shi cao*, "the gua-luo's leaf, its fruit much kualou" — checked against the original, the three words 屮, 其, 多 are dropped, and Xing Bing's subcommentary is added after 括樓. · Liji, *Yue ling*, "in late spring, for the people they pray for the wheat" — checked against the original, it is corrected to "they then pray for the wheat's ripening (乃爲麥祈實)." · Zuozhuan, Xiang yr 24, "the Marquis of Qi sacrificed to the soil and reviewed the army stores; note: meaning the armour and weapons" — a note: the Zuozhuan has no words 侯祭, and the note has no text of armour and weapons; it is corrected to "齊社, 蒐軍實. Du's note: sacrificing to the soil, they took occasion to count over the weapons." · Yili, *Te sheng kui shi*, "they fill the bian and dou (實籩豆)" — checked against the original, "籩豆" should read "豆籩."
