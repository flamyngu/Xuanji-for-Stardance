## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈丑集上〉【口字部】頁192第12    〔古文〕：啺、𣉺、𡃯【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤徒郎切，音堂。【說文】大言也。从口庚聲。【莊子·天下篇】荒唐之言。　又【史記·司馬相如·上林賦】瑉玉旁唐。【註】郭璞云：旁唐，盤薄。　又國名。【玉篇】堯稱唐者蕩蕩，道德至大之貌。【書·五子之歌】惟彼陶唐，有此冀方。【疏】韋昭云：陶，唐皆國名，猶湯稱殷商也。○按書傳皆言堯以唐侯升爲天子，不言封於陶唐。陶唐二字，或共爲地名，未必如昭言也。又【詩·唐風疏】唐者，成王母弟叔虞所封其地，帝堯夏禹所都之墟，漢曰太原郡。　又漢縣名。【前漢·地理志】中山國唐縣。【註】故堯國也，唐水在西。【詩·唐風疏】皇甫謐曰：堯始封於唐，今中山唐縣是也。後徙晉陽，及爲天子，都平陽，於詩爲唐國，則唐國爲平陽也。　又魯地。【春秋·隱二年】公及戎盟于唐。【註】高平方輿縣北有武唐亭。又【戰國策】左濟右天唐。【註】謂高唐。【前漢·地理志】平原郡高唐。【註】桑欽言，漯水所出也。又【武帝紀】南巡狩至於盛唐。【註】韋昭曰：在南郡。又【地理志】會稽郡錢唐。【註】武林水所出。又【後漢·光武紀】進屠唐子鄉。【註】唐子鄉有唐子山，在今唐州湖陽縣西南。【廣韻】唐州，楚地。戰國時屬晉，後入於韓，秦屬南陽郡，後魏爲淮州，隋爲顯州，貞觀攺爲唐州，因唐城山爲名。　又姓。【廣韻】唐堯之後，子孫氏焉。【史記·秦本紀】孝文王立，尊唐八子爲唐太后。又【屈原傳】楚有宋玉，唐勒，景差之徒。又唐山，複姓。【前漢·禮樂志】高祖唐山夫人。　又【詩·𨻰風】中唐有甓。【傳】中，中庭也。唐，堂途也。【疏】爾雅釋宮云：廟中路謂之唐，堂途謂之𨻰。李巡曰：唐，廟中路名。孫炎引詩中唐有甓。堂途，堂下至門之徑也。然則唐之與𨻰，廟庭之異名耳，其實一也。故云：唐，堂途也。【周語】陂唐汚庳，以鍾其美。【註】唐，俗本作塘。【說文】無塘字。【莊子·徐無鬼】其求唐子也，而未始出域，有遺類矣。【註】唐子者，堂途給使令之人，猶周禮云門子耳。　又草名。【詩·鄘風】爰采唐矣。【傳】唐，蒙菜名。【爾雅·釋草】唐蒙，女蘿。女蘿，菟絲。　又弓名。【周禮·夏官·司弓矢】唐弓，大弓，以授學射者。　又叶徒紅切，音同。【歐陽修·楊諫議銘】震官太尉，四世以公。於陵正直，僕射於唐。    考證：〔【武帝紀】南巡狩至於盛唐。【註】韋昭曰：南郡。〕　謹照原文南郡上增在字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 丑, upper; Mouth radical 口; p.192 #12)*

**Ancient graphs.** 啺, 𣉺, 𡃯.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 徒郎 — sounding like 堂 (táng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "big talk (大言). It follows 口 (mouth) with 庚 as phonetic." Zhuangzi, *Tianxia*: "wild and extravagant (荒唐) words."
- *Also* Shiji, Sima Xiangru, *Shanglin fu*: "minerals and jade spread broad (旁唐)"; note: Guo Pu says: "旁唐 is sprawling and vast (盤薄)."
- *Also a state-name.* *Yupian*: "Yao is called Tang (唐), 'vast' (蕩蕩) — the look of virtue at its greatest." Shu, *Wuzi zhi ge*: "that house of Taotang (陶唐) held this land of Ji"; subcommentary: "Wei Zhao says: Tao and Tang are both state-names, as the Tang [founder] was called Yin-Shang." A note: the Shu-traditions all say Yao rose from Marquis of Tang to Son of Heaven, and do not say he was enfeoffed at Taotang; the two graphs Taotang may together be a place-name — not necessarily as Zhao says.
- *Also* subcommentary to Shijing, *Tangfeng*: "Tang (唐) was the land enfeoffed to King Cheng's younger brother Shuyu, the old capital-site of Emperor Yao and Yu of Xia; in Han times it was Taiyuan commandery."
- *Also a Han county-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Tang county (唐縣) in the principality of Zhongshan"; note: "the old state of Yao; the Tang River (唐水) is to its west." Subcommentary to *Tangfeng*: "Huangfu Mi says: Yao was first enfeoffed at Tang, which is the present Tang county of Zhongshan; later he moved to Jinyang, and when he became Son of Heaven he capitalled at Pingyang; in the Shijing the 'state of Tang' is then Pingyang."
- *Also a place in Lu.* Chunqiu, Yin yr 2: "the duke covenanted with the Rong at Tang (唐)"; note: "north of Fangyu county in Gaoping is the Wutang pavilion (武唐亭)."
- *Also* Zhanguo ce: "the Ji on the left, Tiantang (天唐) on the right"; note: "it means Gaotang (高唐)."
- *Also* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Gaotang (高唐) in Pingyuan commandery"; note: "Sang Qin says the Ta River issues from there."
- *Also* *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "on his southern tour he reached Shengtang (盛唐)"; note: Wei Zhao says: "in Nan commandery."
- *Also* the *Treatise on Geography*: "Qiantang (錢唐) in Kuaiji commandery"; note: "where the Wulin waters issue."
- *Also* Later Han, *Annals of Guangwu*: "he advanced and slaughtered at Tangzi-town (唐子鄉)"; note: "Tangzi-town has Mount Tangzi, southwest of the present Huyang county in Tangzhou." *Guangyun*: "Tangzhou (唐州) is Chu land; in the Warring States it belonged to Jin, later passed to Han; the Qin made it part of Nanyang commandery; the Later Wei made it Huaizhou, the Sui Xianzhou; in the Zhenguan era it was renamed Tangzhou, named after Mount Tangcheng (唐城山)."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "descendants of Tang Yao, his posterity taking it as the clan-name." Shiji, *Annals of Qin*: "when King Xiaowen ascended, he honored the Tang Eight-Concubine (唐八子) as the Tang Dowager." Also the *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "Chu had Song Yu, Tang Le (唐勒), Jing Cha and their like." Also Tangshan (唐山), a compound surname; Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "Gaozu's Lady Tangshan (唐山夫人)."
- *Also* Shijing, *Chenfeng*: "in the courtyard-path (中唐) are tiles"; tradition: "中 is the central courtyard; 唐 is the hall-path." Subcommentary: "Erya, *Shigong* says: the path in the temple is called 唐, the hall-path 陳. Li Xun says: 唐 is the name of the temple's inner path. Sun Yan cites the Shi, 'in the 中唐 are tiles.' The hall-path is the way from below the hall to the gate. Thus 唐 and 陳 are but different names for the temple court, in truth one and the same — hence '唐 is the hall-path.'" Zhou yu: "embankments and dikes (陂唐), pools and lowlands, to gather their bounty"; note: "唐 is in vulgar editions written 塘; the Shuowen has no graph 塘." Zhuangzi, *Xu Wugui*: "in seeking the tangzi (唐子), without ever leaving the precinct, there is yet a remnant kind"; note: "the tangzi is the hall-path attendant who runs errands, like the 'gate-sons' (門子) of the Zhouli."
- *Also a plant-name.* Shijing, *Yongfeng*: "there I gather the tang (唐)"; tradition: "唐 is the meng vegetable (蒙菜)." Erya, *Shicao*: "tangmeng (唐蒙) is the nüluo (女蘿, dodder); nüluo is the tusi (菟絲)."
- *Also a bow-name.* Zhouli, *Xiaguan, Sigongshi*: "the Tang bow (唐弓) and the Great bow are given to those learning archery."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 徒紅, sounding like 同 (tóng). Ouyang Xiu, *Yang jianyi ming*: "in office Zhen was Grand Commandant, for four generations they served as ducal lords; upright at Yuling, Vice Director under the Tang (唐)."

**Textual notes.** In the *Annals of Emperor Wu* citation, before 南郡 the graph 在 is added (per the original — "in Nan commandery").
