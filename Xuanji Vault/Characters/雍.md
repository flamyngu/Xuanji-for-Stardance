**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жҲҢ, middle; Short-tailed-bird radical йҡ№; p.1366 #14)*

**Ancient graph.** йӮ•.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie ж–је®№, sounding like е»ұ (yЕҚng).

**Etymology (Yupian).** *Yupian*: "harmonious (е’Ң)." Shu, *Yao dian*: "the black-haired folk, ah, were transformed and made concordant (жҷӮйӣҚ)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Erya, *Shi tian*: "the Grand Year standing in жҲҠ is called Zhuyong (и‘—йӣҚ)."
- *A water-name.* *Shuijing*: "water on all four sides is called йӣҚ."
- *A county-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on Geography*: "Yongnu county (йӣҚеҘҙзёЈ) in Yuyang commandery."
- *Also* Former Han, *Biography of Prince Jing of Zhongshan*: "the Master of the Yong Gate (йӣҚй–Җеӯҗ) chanted faintly"; note: "Zhang Yan says: a worthy of Qi who dwelt at the Yong Gate, taking it for his style. Su Lin says: a man of the Six-States time, named Zhou."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: a surname. Yong Jiu (йӣҚзіҫ), son-in-law of Zhai Zhong; seen in Zuozhuan, Huan yr 15.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: used interchangeably with йӣқ. Ban Gu, *Liang du fu*: "and so flows the moat of the Hall of Learning (иҫҹйӣҚ)." A note: the *Liji, Wang zhi* writes иҫҹйӣқ.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie е§”еӢҮ, sounding like еЈ… (yЗ’ng). Zhouli, *Qiu guan, Sikou*: "the Yong officers (йӣҚж°Ҹ)"; note: "it means those who embank and dam to stop water"; *Shiwen*: "йӣҚ, fanqie ж–јеӢҮ."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: "to aid (зҘҗ)." Yang Xiong, *Ganquan fu*: "the gods aid (йӣҚ) his weal"; note: "Jin Zhuo says: йӣҚ means to aid; Yan Shigu says: йӣҚ means to gather; йӣҚ is read as ж“Ғ."
- *Also* *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie ж–јз”Ё, sounding like гҷІ (yГІng). *Guangyun*: one of the Nine Provinces. йӣҚ means to enclose (ж“Ғ): the Xiao on the east, the Han on the west, the marches on the south, Juyong on the north вҖ” enclosed and screened by the four ranges. Shu, *Yu gong*: "the Black Water and the western He make Yongzhou (йӣҚе·һ)"; *Shiwen*: "йӣҚ, fanqie ж–јз”Ё." A note: the *Erya, Shi di* writes йӣқ.
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: a state-name. Zuozhuan, Xi yr 24: "Gao, Yong, Cao, and Teng (йғңйӣҚжӣ№ж»•)"; note: "the state of Yong (йӣҚеңӢ), in Shanyang county of Henei"; *Shiwen*: "йӣҚ, fanqie ж–јз”Ё."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: a surname. *Yun Hui*: "descendants of Yongbo (йӣҚдјҜ), son of King Wen."
