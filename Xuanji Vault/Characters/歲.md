## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集下〉【止字部】頁577第02    〔古文〕：𢧁、亗【唐韻】相銳切【集韻】須銳切，𠀤音帨。【釋名】歲，越也，越故限也。【白虎通】歲者，遂也。【易·繫辭】寒暑相推而歲成。【書·洪範】五紀，一曰歲。【傳】所以紀四時。【又】王省惟歲。【傳】王所省職，兼總羣吏，如歲兼四時。　又星名。【爾雅·釋天】唐虞曰載，夏曰歲，商曰祀，周曰年。【郭註】歲，取歲星行一次也。【疏】按《律歷志》分二十八宿爲十二次，歲星十二歲而周天，是年行一次也。【周禮·春官】馮相氏掌十有二歲。【又】保章氏以十有二歲之相，觀天下之妖祥。【疏】此太歲在地，與天上歲星相應而行。歲星右行於天，一歲移一辰，十二歲一小周，千七百二十八年一大周。太歲左行於地，一與歲星跳辰，年數同。歲星爲陽人之所見，太歲爲隂人所不覩，故舉歲星以表太歲。歲星與日同次之月，一年之中惟於一辰之上爲法。若元年甲子朔旦冬至，日月五星俱赴於牽牛之初，是歲星與日同次之月。十一月斗建子，子有太歲，至後年，歲星移向子上，十二月日月會於𤣥枵。十二月斗建丑，丑有太歲。推此已後皆然。又歲星木會在東方，爲靑龍之象，天之貴神福德之星，所在之國必昌。又【史記·天官書】歲星，一曰攝提，曰重華，曰應星，曰紀星，營室爲淸廟，歲星廟也。【孝經·鉤命決】歲星守心年穀豐。【左傳·昭三十二年】史墨曰：越得歲而吳伐之，必受其凶。又【岳珂·桯史】今星家以太歲爲凶星。【王充·論衡】抵太歲凶，負太歲亦凶。抵太歲名曰歲下，負太歲名曰歲破。　又年穀之成曰歲。【左傳·哀十六年】國人望君，如望歲焉。【杜註】歲，年穀也。【前漢·武帝詔】爲歲事曲加禮。　又周制有歲計、歲會。【周禮·春官】職歲。【註】主歲計者。【又】歲終，則令百官各正其治，受其會。三歲則大計羣吏之治，而誅賞之。【又】司會以參互攷日成，以月要攷月成，以歲會攷歲成。　又【史記·天官書】臘之明日曰初歲。【四民月令】亦曰小歲。又始歲曰獻歲。【楚辭·招魂】獻歲發春。【註】獻，進也。歲始來進，春氣奮揚也。又【東京夢華錄】除夕夜，士庶之家圍爐團坐，達旦不寐，謂之守歲。又【風土記】除夜祭先，竣事，長幼聚飮，祝頌而散，謂之分歲。又【蘇軾·饋歲詩序】蜀中値歲晚閒遺，謂之饋歲。酒食相邀爲別歲。　又萬歲，山名。在桂陽。【水經注】萬歲山生靈壽木，溪下卽千秋水。水側居民號萬歲村。又水名。【伏琛·三齊略記】曲城、齊城東有萬歲水，水北有萬歲亭。又湖名。【廣輿記】萬歲湖，在建昌府南豐縣。又宮名。【三輔黃圖】汾陽有萬歲宮。又木名。【爾雅·釋木疏】杻一名檍，今宮園種之，名萬歲木，取名於億萬也。　又【集韻】相絕切，音雪。義同歱。【曹植·平原公主誄】城闕之詩，以日喩歲。况我愛子，神光長滅。歲亦讀雪。　又【集韻】蘇臥切，音䐝。䮑歲，穀名。【說文】从步戌聲。律歷書名五行爲五步。一說从步者，躔度之行，可推步也。从戌者，木星之精，生於亥，自亥至戌而周天。戌與歲亦諧聲，別作嵗、歳，𠀤非。    考證：〔【周禮·春官】馮相氏疏十有二歲。小周，千七百二十八年爲大周。〕　謹照原文爲字改一字。〔三歲則大計羣吏之屬，而誅賞之。〕　謹照周禮原文屬改治。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, lower; Stop radical 止; p.577 #02)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢧁, 亗.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 相銳. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 須銳 — sounding like 帨 (suì).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shiming*: "歲 is 越 (to pass over): it passes the old bound." Baihu tong: "歲 is 遂 (to fulfil)." Yijing, *Xici*: "cold and heat push each other on, and the year (歲) is made." Shu, *Hongfan*: "of the five reckonings, the first is the year (歲)"; tradition: "by which the four seasons are reckoned"; and: "the king's surveying is like the year (歲) — gathering all the officers, as the year gathers the four seasons."
- *A star-name.* Erya, *Shitian*: "[the year was] called 載 under Tang and Yu, 歲 under Xia, 祀 under Shang, 年 under Zhou"; Guo's note: "歲 takes its name from Jupiter (歲星) moving through one station." Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "the Fengxiang officer keeps the twelve years (十二歲)"; and: "the Baozhang officer, by the aspects of the twelve years, observes the omens of the world"; subcommentary: "the Grand Year (太歲) is below in earth, moving in correspondence with Jupiter above; Jupiter runs rightward, shifting one mark a year, a small round in twelve years, a great round in 1,728 years; the Grand Year runs leftward, jumping marks with Jupiter, the count of years the same; Jupiter is the yang, seen by men, the Grand Year the yin, unseen — hence Jupiter marks the Grand Year; Jupiter, where Wood gathers in the east, is the Azure Dragon, Heaven's noble spirit, star of blessing and virtue, and the land it stands over is sure to flourish."
- *Other names of Jupiter.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Heavens*: "Jupiter (歲星) is also called Sheti, Chonghua, Yingxing, Jixing; the Encampment is the Pure Temple, the temple of Jupiter." Xiaojing goumingjue: "when Jupiter guards [the asterism] Heart, the year's harvest is rich." Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 32: "Shi Mo said, 'Yue has gained the year-star and Wu attacks it — Wu must suffer the calamity.'" Yue Ke, *Tingshi*: "astrologers now hold the Grand Year (太歲) to be a baleful star." Wang Chong, *Lunheng*: "to face the Grand Year is ill, to turn one's back on it is ill too; facing it is 'under the year (歲下),' turning the back 'year-break (歲破).'"
- *The year's harvest.* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 16: "the people look to their lord as they look for the harvest (歲)"; Du's note: "歲 is the year's grain." Former Han, edict of Emperor Wu: "for the harvest's (歲) sake add ceremony in full."
- *歲計, 歲會, the Zhou year-audits.* Zhouli, *Chunguan*: "the Zhisui officer"; note: "in charge of the year-account"; and: "at year's end he has the hundred officers each set right their administration and receive their reckoning; every three years he greatly audits the officers' conduct, punishing and rewarding"; and: "the Sikuai, by cross-checking, audits the daily totals, by the monthly summary the monthly, by the year-reckoning the yearly."
- *初歲, 小歲, the New Year.* Shiji, *Treatise on the Heavens*: "the day after the La festival is the First of the Year (初歲)"; the Simin yueling: "also called the Little Year (小歲)." The year's beginning is 獻歲: Chuci, *Zhaohun*: "the year offered up (獻歲), spring breaks forth"; note: "the year comes on anew, the spring *qi* rising." The Dongjing menghua lu: "on New Year's Eve households sit circled round the brazier till dawn — called 'keeping the year (守歲).'" The Fengtu ji: "they sacrifice to the ancestors, then young and old drink and give blessing-toasts — called 'parting the year (分歲).'" Su Shi, preface to the Year-Gift poem: "in Shu, at year's end, they exchange gifts — 'gifting the year (饋歲)'; inviting one another to wine is 'parting the year (別歲).'"
- *萬歲, a mountain-name (in Guiyang).* Shuijing zhu: "Wansui Mountain grows the *lingshou* tree; below is the Qianqiu water; the folk by the water are called Wansui village." Also a river: Fu Chen, *Sanqi lüeji*: "east of Qucheng is the Wansui water, north of it the Wansui pavilion." Also a lake: Guangyu ji: "Wansui Lake, in Nanfeng county of Jianchang." Also a palace: Sanfu huangtu: "at Fenyang is the Wansui Palace." Also a tree: Erya, *Shimu* subcommentary: "the *niu* is also called *yi*; planted in palace gardens, named the Myriad-Years tree (萬歲木), from 'a hundred million.'"
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 相絕, sounding like 雪 (xuě). Cao Zhi, *Dirge for the Princess of Pingyuan*: "the poem of the city-towers likens a day to a year (歲); how much more my dear child, her spirit-light forever quenched" — 歲 here read 雪.
- *Ji Yun*: fanqie 蘇臥, sounding like 䐝 (suò) — 䮑歲, a grain-name.

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "it follows 步 (pace) with 戌 as phonetic." The calendar-books name the Five Phases the "five paces (五步)." One says: it follows 步 because the planet's coursing through the degrees can be paced out (推步); it follows 戌 because Jupiter's essence is born in 亥, and from 亥 to 戌 it circles the heavens. The variant forms 嵗 and 歳 are both wrong.

**Textual notes.** "千七百二十八年爲大周" should read "一千七百二十八年"; "大計羣吏之屬" should read "大計羣吏之治".
