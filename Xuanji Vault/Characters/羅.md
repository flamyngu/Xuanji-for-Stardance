**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, middle; Net radical зҪ‘; p.950 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie йӯҜдҪ•. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie иүҜдҪ•. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жң—дҪ• вҖ” sounding like иҳҝ (luГі).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To net birds with a silk snare (д»ҘзөІзҪҹйіҘ); anciently the Mang clan first made the зҫ…." Erya, *Shi qi*: "a bird-net is called зҫ…"; note: "it means to enmesh (зҫ…зөЎ) them." Shijing, *Wang feng*: "the pheasant caught in the net (зҫ…)." Zhouli, *Xia guan, Luo shi*: "the Luoshi managed the netting of crows and birds."

**Senses.**
- *Silk gauze (еёӣ).* *Leipian*. *Shiming*: "зҫ…ж–Ү is open-worked gauze (з–Һзҫ…)." Zhanguo ce: "the lower palace muddles gauze and fine silk, trailing damask and crГӘpe."
- *To array, set in rows (еҲ—).* *Guangya*. Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "arrayed (зҫ…), they grow below the hall"; note: "growing ranged in rows."
- *A state-name.* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 12: "the men of Luo (зҫ…) wished to attack them"; note: "Luo, a state of the Xiong surname, in the western mountains of Yicheng county, later removed."
- *A river-name.* Shiji, *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "then he cast himself into the Miluo (жұЁзҫ…)"; note: "the Mi water is in Luo, hence Miluo."
- *ж–°зҫ… (Silla), an Eastern Yi state-name.* Tang shu, *Account of the Eastern Yi*: "Silla is the offspring of Byeonhan, dwelling in the Han Lelang land."
- *Surname.* *Xingshi jijiu pian*: "the Luo clan, descendants of Zhuanxu, enfeoffed at Luo, now Fangzhou; their offspring took it as their clan."
- *зҫ…зҫ… (luoluo), a beast-name.* Shanhai jing: "the Northern Sea has a beast shaped like a tiger, named the luoluo (зҫ…зҫ…)." *Pianya*: "a blue tiger is called luoluo."
- *иҸҙзҫ… (anluo), a fruit-name.* *Bencao*: "of the pear kind, yellow in colour, like a goose-pear."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie й„°зҹҘ, sounding like йӣў (lГӯ). Same sense.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie йғһдҪҗ, sounding like еӣү (luГІ). йӮҸ is sometimes abbreviated to зҫ…: "to patrol (е·Ў)."
