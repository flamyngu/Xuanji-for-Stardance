**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, middle; Net radical 网; p.950 #12)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 魯何. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 良何. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 朗何 — sounding like 蘿 (luó).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To net birds with a silk snare (以絲罟鳥); anciently the Mang clan first made the 羅." Erya, *Shi qi*: "a bird-net is called 羅"; note: "it means to enmesh (羅絡) them." Shijing, *Wang feng*: "the pheasant caught in the net (羅)." Zhouli, *Xia guan, Luo shi*: "the Luoshi managed the netting of crows and birds."

**Senses.**
- *Silk gauze (帛).* *Leipian*. *Shiming*: "羅文 is open-worked gauze (疎羅)." Zhanguo ce: "the lower palace muddles gauze and fine silk, trailing damask and crêpe."
- *To array, set in rows (列).* *Guangya*. Chuci, *Jiu ge*: "arrayed (羅), they grow below the hall"; note: "growing ranged in rows."
- *A state-name.* Zuozhuan, Huan yr 12: "the men of Luo (羅) wished to attack them"; note: "Luo, a state of the Xiong surname, in the western mountains of Yicheng county, later removed."
- *A river-name.* Shiji, *Biography of Qu Yuan*: "then he cast himself into the Miluo (汨羅)"; note: "the Mi water is in Luo, hence Miluo."
- *新羅 (Silla), an Eastern Yi state-name.* Tang shu, *Account of the Eastern Yi*: "Silla is the offspring of Byeonhan, dwelling in the Han Lelang land."
- *Surname.* *Xingshi jijiu pian*: "the Luo clan, descendants of Zhuanxu, enfeoffed at Luo, now Fangzhou; their offspring took it as their clan."
- *羅羅 (luoluo), a beast-name.* Shanhai jing: "the Northern Sea has a beast shaped like a tiger, named the luoluo (羅羅)." *Pianya*: "a blue tiger is called luoluo."
- *菴羅 (anluo), a fruit-name.* *Bencao*: "of the pear kind, yellow in colour, like a goose-pear."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 鄰知, sounding like 離 (lí). Same sense.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 郞佐, sounding like 囉 (luò). 邏 is sometimes abbreviated to 羅: "to patrol (巡)."
