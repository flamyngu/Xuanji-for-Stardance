> 《康熙字典》〈卯集上〉【心字部】頁375第01【唐韻】息林切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】思林切。【說文】人心，土藏，在身之中。象形。博士說以爲火藏。【徐曰】心爲大火，然則心屬火也。【玉篇】【廣韻】𠀤訓火藏。　又【荀子·解蔽篇】心者，形之君也，而神明之主也。【禮·大學疏】總包萬慮謂之心。　又【釋名】心，纖也。所識纖微無不貫也。　又本也。【易·復卦】復其見天地之心乎。【註】天地以本爲心者也。【正義曰】言天地寂然不動，是以本爲心者也。【禮·禮器】如松柏之有心也。【註】得氣之本也。【孔疏】得氣之本，故巡四時，柯葉無凋攺也，心謂本也。　又中也。心在身之中。【詩序】情動于中。【正義曰】中謂中心。凡言中央曰心。【禮·少儀】牛羊之肺，離而不提心。【註】不提心，謂不絕中央也。【古歌】日出當心，謂日中也。【邵雍淸夜吟】月到天心處，言月當天中也。　又東方五度，宿名。【史記·天官書】心爲明堂。　又【禮·明堂位】夏后氏祭心。【註】氣主盛也。又【月令】季夏祭先心。【註】五藏之次，心次肺，至此則心爲尊也。　又去聲。【吳棫·韻補】息吝切。【外紀】禹曰：堯舜之民，皆以堯舜之心爲心。下心字去聲。　又叶思眞切，音新。【前漢·安世房中歌】我定歷數，人告其心。敕身齊戒，施敎申申。　又叶先容切，音松。【詩·大雅】吉甫作頌，𥡆如淸風。仲山甫永懷，以慰其心。【前漢·禮樂志】流星隕，感惟風，籋歸雲，撫懷心。　又叶思征切，音騂。【揚子·太𤣥經】勤于心否貞。　又叶桑鳩切，音修。【荀子·解蔽篇】鳳凰秋秋，其翼若干，其聲若簫。有鳳有凰，樂帝之心。簫叶疏鳩切。　又叶思敬切，音性。【王微觀海詩】善卽誰爲御，我來無別心。聊復寓兹興，兹興將何詠。【說文》長箋】借華心形，故惢字从心，就今文言也。若精蘊同文諸書，各以意闡古文，與今文稍遠，槪不泛引。【類篇】偏旁作忄。亦作㣺。◎按《字彙》《正字通》心俱音辛，誤。辛在眞韻，齊齒音也。心在侵韻，閉口音也。如心字去聲，音近信，然不得竟以信字音之者，蓋信字爲眞韻內辛字之去聲，乃齊齒音也。若侵韻內心字之去聲，乃閉口音，有音而無字矣。字有不可下直音者，此類是也。蓋齊齒之辛，商之商也，閉口之心，商之羽也。每一音中，具有五音，不可無別。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, upper; Heart radical 心; p.375 #01)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 息林 (sī-lín). *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, and *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 思林 — i.e. **xīn**.

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "The human heart; the viscus of the Earth-phase, lying at the centre of the body. A pictograph." The Academicians (*boshi*) held it instead to be the viscus of the Fire-phase. **Xu Kai** comments: the Heart is the Great Fire (the asterism Antares), so the heart belongs to Fire. The *Yupian* and *Guangyun* both gloss it as "the Fire viscus."

**Senses.**
- *The seat of mind and spirit.* Xunzi, "Dispelling Blindness": "The heart is the ruler of the body and the master of spiritual intelligence." Subcommentary to the *Great Learning* (Liji): "That which gathers in all thought is called the heart."
- *Fine, all-penetrating (a gloss by sound).* *Shiming*: "心 means 'fine' (纖 xiān); whatever it apprehends, however minute, it threads through completely."
- *Root, origin (本).* Yijing, Return hexagram: "In Return do we not see the heart of Heaven and Earth?" Commentary: "Heaven and Earth take the root as their heart." *Correct Meaning*: "Heaven and Earth are silent and unmoving, thus they take the root as their heart." Liji, *Liqi*: "like the heartwood (pith) of pine and cypress"; commentary: "it holds the root of the *qi*"; Kong Yingda: "holding the root of the *qi*, through the four seasons its boughs and leaves never wither or alter — 'heart' here means the root."
- *Centre, middle (中).* The heart sits at the body's centre. Mao Preface to the *Shijing*: "feeling stirs within (中)"; *Correct Meaning*: "中 means the centre-heart; in general the central point is called the 'heart.'" Liji, *Shaoyi*: "the lungs of ox and sheep are cut but not severed at the heart"; commentary: "not severing the heart means not cutting through the centre." An ancient song: "the sun stands at the heart" = noon. Shao Yong, *Clear-Night Chant*: "the moon reaches Heaven's heart" = the moon at the zenith.
- *A lunar mansion.* The eastern mansion spanning five degrees, named Heart (心宿, around Antares). Shiji, *Treatise on the Celestial Offices*: "Heart is the Bright Hall (明堂)."
- *In sacrifice.* Liji, *Mingtang Wei*: "the house of Xia sacrificed the heart [first]"; commentary: "because its *qi* is dominant." *Yueling*: "in late summer the sacrifice offers the heart first"; commentary: "in the order of the five viscera the heart follows the lungs, but here the heart is honoured foremost."

**Tonal and rhyming variants.**
- *Departing tone.* Wu Yu, *Yunbu*: fanqie 息吝. *Waiji*: Yu said, "The people of Yao and Shun all took Yao and Shun's heart for their own heart" — the second 心 is read in the departing tone.
- *Rhymes as 新 (xīn), fanqie 思真.* Former Han, *Anshi Palace Songs*: "I fixed the calendar's count; the people declared their heart. I disciplined myself, fasted and purified, and spread instruction on and on."
- *Rhymes as 松 (sōng), fanqie 先容.* Shijing, *Daya*: "Jifu makes this hymn, mild as a clear breeze; Zhong Shanfu cherishes it always, to comfort his heart." Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "the shooting star falls… treading the homing clouds, it soothes the cherished heart."
- *Rhymes as 騂 (xīng), fanqie 思征.* Yang Xiong, *Taixuanjing*: "diligent of heart, though thwarted, yet upright."
- *Rhymes as 修 (xiū), fanqie 桑鳩.* Xunzi: "The phoenixes dance and dance, their wings like shields, their cry like panpipes; male phoenix and female, they gladden the Thearch's heart" (簫 rhyming as fanqie 疏鳩).
- *Rhymes as 性 (xìng), fanqie 思敬.* Wang Wei (of Liu-Song), *Gazing on the Sea*: "If it be goodness, then who shall drive? I come here with no divided heart; for now I lodge in this delight — and this delight, how shall I sing it?"

**Editorial notes.** The *Shuowen Changjian* says the graph borrows the shape of a flower's pistil ("heart"), so 惢 takes 心 as its element, speaking in the modern script; works like the *Jingyun* and *Tongwen*, which expound the archaic script by conjecture and stray from the modern forms, are not cited here. *Leipian*: as a side-radical it is written 忄, also 㣺. — A note: the *Zihui* and *Zhengzitong* both give 心 the reading 辛 (xīn), which is wrong. 辛 belongs to the Zhen rhyme (an "even-teeth" sound), whereas 心 belongs to the Qin rhyme (a "closed-mouth" sound). The departing-tone of 心 is near 信 (xìn), yet cannot simply be glossed by 信, since 信 is the departing tone of 辛 in the Zhen rhyme and is an even-teeth sound — the departing 心 of the Qin rhyme is a closed-mouth sound with a pronunciation but no written graph. Some characters admit no direct homophone-gloss; this is one. The even-teeth 辛 is the "shang-of-shang"; the closed-mouth 心 is the "yu-of-shang." Within every single note all five notes are present, and the distinction must not be lost.
