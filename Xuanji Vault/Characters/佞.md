## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈子集中〉【人字部】頁99第11【唐韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤乃定切，音甯。才也。故自稱不才曰不佞。【晉語】夷吾不佞。　又巧諂捷給也。【論語】焉用佞。【博物志】堯時有指佞草，佞人入朝則屈而指之。　又【韻補】叶奴經切，音寧。【夏侯湛·抵疑】猗靡容悅，出入崎傾。逐巧點姸，嘔喁辯佞。【說文】巧讇，高材也。从女信省。【徐鉉曰】女子之信，近於佞也。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, middle; Person radical 人; p.99 #11)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 乃定, sounding like 甯 (nìng).

**Etymology and senses.** "Talent (才)" — hence to call oneself untalented is 不佞 ("without glibness"). *Discourses of Jin*: "I, Yiwu, am untalented (不佞)."

**Senses.**
- *Artfully fawning, quick-tongued (巧諂捷給).* Lunyu: "what use is glibness (佞)?" *Bowu zhi*: "in Yao's time there was the finger-the-flatterer plant (指佞草); when a glib man (佞人) entered court, it bent and pointed at him."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 寧 (níng), fanqie 奴經.* Xiahou Zhan, *Di yi*: "yielding and pleasing, in and out by crooked ways; chasing cleverness and tricking out charm, mumbling and murmuring their glib flattery (辯佞)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "artful flattery (巧讇), high talent. It follows 女 (woman) with an abbreviated 信 (trust)." Xu Xuan: "a woman's 'trust' comes near to glibness (佞)."
