## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈戌集中〉【雨字部】頁1376第29【唐韻】所莊切【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】師莊切，𠀤音驦。【玉篇】露凝也。【釋名】其氣慘毒，物皆喪也。【大戴禮】陽氣勝，則散爲雨露。隂氣勝，則凝爲霜雪。【易·坤卦】履霜堅冰至。【詩·秦風】白露爲霜。　又【字彙補】國名。【西域記】屈霜國。　又姓。見【姓苑】。　又【正字通】歷年曰霜。【李白詩】陛下之壽三千霜。　又【集韻】【類篇】𠀤色壯切，音孀。隕霜，殺物也。或作灀。【潘岳·馬汧督誄】馬生爰發，在險彌亮。精貫白日，猛烈秋霜。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, middle; Rain radical 雨; p.1376 #29)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 所莊. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 師莊 — sounding like 驦 (shuāng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Yupian*: "congealed dew (露凝)." *Shiming*: "its breath is cruel and harsh, and all things perish." Da Dai li: "when the yang force prevails, it scatters as rain and dew; when the yin force prevails, it congeals as frost and snow." Yijing, *Kun* hexagram: "treading the frost (霜), the hard ice comes." Shijing, *Qin feng*: "the white dew becomes frost (霜)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Zihui Bu*: "a state-name." *Record of the Western Regions*: "the state of Qushuang (屈霜國)."
- *Surname.* Seen in the *Xingyuan*.
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "a passing year is called 霜." Li Bai's poem: "Your Majesty's years, three thousand frosts (霜)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Leipian*: fanqie 色壯, sounding like 孀 (shuàng). 隕霜, frost falling and killing things; also written 灀. Pan Yue, *Eulogy for the Commandant of Maqian*: "Ma sprang up and set forth, in danger the more brilliant; his essence pierced the bright sun, fierce as autumn frost (秋霜)."
