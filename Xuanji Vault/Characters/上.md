**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, upper; One radical 一; p.76 #07)*

**Ancient graphs.** 丄, 𠄞.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 時亮, sounding like 尙 (shàng).

**Etymology and senses.** The 上 of "on top," the counterpart-term to "below"; to exalt (崇); to honour (尊). Yijing, *Wen yan*: "what is rooted in Heaven draws near to the above (上)."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "the sovereign (君)." 太上 (the Most High) is the term of utmost honour. Cai Yong, *Du duan*: "上 is where the honoured seat is; one says only 上, not daring to speak the honoured title."
- *上日 (the first day).* Shu, *Shun dian*: "in the first month, on the chief day (上日)"; note: "Kong says: 上日 is the new-moon day; Ye says: a day of the first ten-day period; Zeng says: such as the chief 戊, the chief 辛, the chief 丁."
- *Surname.* The Han had Shang Xiong; the Ming, Shang Guan and Shang Zhi. Also Shangguan (上官), a compound surname.
- *Also* *Tang Yun*: fanqie 時掌. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 是掌 — the rising tone of 商 (shàng). "To climb, to rise (登, 升); to go from below upward." Yijing, *Xu* hexagram: "the clouds rise (上) to the sky." Liji, *Qu li*: "ascending the steps, feet brought together, step by step one goes up (上)."
- *To advance, present (進).* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "Shuo presented (上) three thousand memorial-tablets."
- *Also* interchangeable with 尙. Shijing, *Wei feng*: "above all (上) be careful in this!" Former Han, *Biography of Jia Yi*: "to honour (上) kin, to honour age, to honour the worthy, to honour the noble." Also the *Biography of Kuang Heng*: "he who governs the world examines what he sets above (上)."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 常 (cháng), fanqie 辰羊.* Chuci, *Jiu huai*: "I look on the abyss, so vast; I gaze on the wood, sunk in dimness; I trim my broidered robe, and riding the rainbow go up to the south (南上)."
- *Rhymes as 愼 (shèn), fanqie 時刃.* Wang Wei, *Poem viewing the sea*: "if the root shines unobscured, then at the tip the principle is known to gleam; of a sudden, mounting the whirling, twisting force, I leap and soar to the cliff-top (崖上)."
- *Rhymes as 審 (shěn), fanqie 矢忍.* Guo Pu, *Wandering immortals*: "I lift my head and gaze on the Grand Pure, where the morning clouds cast no added shadow; though I would muse on rising and transforming, the Dragon-ford is not easily gone up (上)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: "上 means high; an indicative graph; fanqie 時掌." A note: the graph has both a moving (動) and a still (靜) sound. All the rhyme-books take the rising tone, fanqie 是掌, as the 上 of "rising up," belonging to the moving; and the departing tone, fanqie 時亮, as the 上 of "what is on top of a thing," belonging to the still. Now, looking closely, the *Shuowen*'s rising-tone graph 上, "high," speaks with reference to a thing — so the 上 of "on top of a thing" too should be in the rising tone. Yet it is right to divide the moving and still sounds as the rhyme-books do. Hereafter, the like.

**Textual note.** Chuci, *Jiu huai*, "...riding the lightning (騎電), of a sudden going up (忽上)" — checked against the original, 騎電 is corrected to 騎霓 (riding the rainbow), and 忽上 to 南上 (up to the south).
