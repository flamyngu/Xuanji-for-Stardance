**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, upper; Inch radical 寸; p.294 #16)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢪇.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 卽良. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 資良 — sounding like 漿 (jiāng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "properly the graph for 'general (將帥)'; one says a word of [something] gradually [coming]." Su Lin: "將 is a word of 'just beginning.'" Yijing, *Xici*: "thus the gentleman is about to (將) act, about to set forth."
- *To harbour [intent].* Gongyang zhuan, Zhuang yr 32: "against ruler or kin one must harbour no [treasonous] intent (將); to harbour it is to be put to death"; Yan Shigu's note: "將 is to have the intent of it."
- *Or rather (抑然之辭).* Chuci, *Buju*: "were it better to grub up weeds and till by main force, or rather (將) to court the great and make a name?"
- *And; about to (且).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "now (將) at peace, now at ease."
- *To nourish, tend (養).* *Guangyun.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "no leisure to tend (將) my father."
- *To aid (助).* Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "to mend faults and further (將) the good."
- *To escort, send off (送).* Shijing, *Zhaonan*: "a hundred chariots escort (將) her." Beifeng: "this girl goes to her home, far do they escort (將) her."
- *Great (大).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "and it is very great (將)." Shangsong: "I received the mandate, vast and great (將)."
- *To receive; to bear; to carry out.* Shijing, *Shangsong*: "the offering borne (將) by Tang's descendant." Shu, *Yinzheng*: "now I, with you the host, reverently carry out (奉將) Heaven's punishment"; note: "將 means to carry out."
- *To bestow; to hold; to go along with.* *Zengyun.* *Zheng Yun*: "to support." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "do not push (將) the great cart." Zuozhuan, Zhuang yr 21: "the Earl of Zheng attended (將) the king, entering by the Yu Gate."
- *To advance (進).* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "by the day attaining, by the month advancing (將)."
- *To follow, attend (從, 隨).* Former Han, *Sacrificial Hymns*: "the nine Yi submit and attend (將)."
- *Vigorous (壯).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "few of us are now in our prime (將)"; note: "壯."
- *Long (長).* Chuci, *Jiubian*: "I fear my span will not be long (將)"; note: "長."
- *The side (側).* Shijing, *Daya*: "on the side (將) of the Wei"; note: "側."
- *To go away, depart.* Xunzi, *Fulun*: "the season is all but gone (將)"; note: "the affairs of the time are past and not to be recovered."
- *A surname.* The Later Zhao had Jiang Rong, governor of Changshan.
- *干將, an ancient swordsmith.* Zhang Yi: "the name of King Han's swordsmith; now a sword is called a *ganjiang*."

**Level tone (qiāng).** *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 千羊; *Yun Hui*: fanqie 七羊 — sounding like 鏘 (qiāng). "To beg; a word of wish and entreaty."
- *To entreat.* Shijing, *Weifeng*: "pray (將), sir, be not angry." Xiaoya: "I beg you, sir (將伯), help me."
- *A chiming sound.* Shijing, *Zhengfeng*: "the girdle-jades chiming (將將)."
- *A look of stern majesty.* Shijing, *Daya*: "the great gate stands stately (將將)."
- *A clinking ensemble.* Shijing, *Zhousong*: "the chimes and pipes ringing together (將將)."

**Departing tone (jiàng).** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 卽諒; *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子諒 — sounding like 醬 (jiàng). *Guangyun*: "a general, commander." *Zengyun*: "to lead, command." Note to the Liji: Fang says, "ability enough to command (將) things and master them is the 'general (將)'; wisdom enough to lead things and go before them is the 'marshal (帥).'"

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 卽兩, sounding like 獎 (jiǎng).* Shijing, *Daya*: "Heaven will not sustain (將) me" (rhyming below with 往).
