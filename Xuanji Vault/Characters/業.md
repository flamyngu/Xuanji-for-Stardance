**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, middle; Wood radical 木; p.541 #05)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𤑽, 𤎸.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 魚怯. *Ji Yun*: fanqie 逆怯 — sounding like 鄴 (yè).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "業 is a great board, used to adorn the hanging of bells and drums." Shijing, *Daya*: "the bell-stand boards and the studs (虡業維樅)"; subcommentary: "the upright timbers for hanging bells and chime-stones — the upright are called 虡, the cross-pieces 栒; on the 栒 a great board is set as ornament, which is the 業. The board is carved with notches and teeth like a saw, hence called 業."

**Senses.**
- *Achievement, accomplishment (功業).* Yijing, *Xi ci*: "to have abundance is called great achievement (大業)."
- *Undertaking, work (事業).* Yijing, *Kun* hexagram: "flowing into the four limbs, manifest in undertakings (事業)."
- *Foundation, patrimony (基業).* Mengzi: "to found an enterprise (創業) and hand down the line."
- *Studies (學業).* Liji, *Qu li*: "what is practised must have its lesson (業)."
- *A hereditary calling (世業).* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 1: "Zichan said: 'Taitai could carry on (業) his office.'"
- *Also* Erya, *Shi xun*: "業業 means peril, anxiety (危)." Shu, *Gao Yao mo*: "fearful and anxious (兢兢業業), in a day or two ten thousand crises."
- *Stout, robust (壯).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the four stallions, sturdy (業業)." Also, whatever one works at and manages is called 業; and a thing already begun but not yet finished is also called 業. Mengzi: "there was a sandal in the making (業履) on the windowsill."
- *Already, by now (已然).* Former Han, *Biography of Liu Bi, King of Wu*: "Gaozu summoned Bi and read his face, then regretted that he had already (業) made the appointment."
- *A craft, trade (藝業).* Shiji, *Biography of the Money-makers*: "farming is a clumsy trade (拙業), selling drinks a petty trade (小業)."
- *Jianye (建業), a place-name.* Wuzhi: "Quan renamed Moling as Jianye (建業)."
- *Surname.* The *Xingyuan* has a Ye clan (業氏).
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 逆及, sounding like 岌 (jí). Also "robust."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 玉盍. Also "perilous."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes as 逆 (nì), fanqie 宜戟.* Heguanzi, *Tai hong*: "two governings and four attainments, with pauses between for rest; returning in season and parting with the breath, to complete the myriad works (萬業)."
- *Rhymes as 虐 (nüè), fanqie 逆約.* Former Han, *Treatise on Arts and Letters, summary*: "Fuxi drew the trigrams, and writing arose after; Yu, Xia, Shang, Zhou — Confucius compiled their works (業)."

**On the graph.** A note: the *Shuowen* takes it as following 莘 and 巾 — 巾 imaging the board, not following 木 — and files it under the Xin radical (莘部); it is now wrongly placed here.
