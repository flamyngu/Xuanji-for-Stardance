**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 卯, lower; Axe radical 斤; p.480 #10)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢀝.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 息鄰. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 斯人. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 斯鄰 — sounding like 辛 (xīn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to take wood (i.e. to fell firewood)."
- *New, fresh; the first (初).* *Boya*: "the first." Yijing, *Daxu*: "daily he renews (新) his virtue." Shu, *Yin zheng*: "all join in renewal (新)." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "her new (新) [marriage] is very fair." Liji, *Yueling*: "in the first month of autumn the farmers bring in the grain; the Son of Heaven tastes the new (新) crop, first offering it at the ancestral shrine."
- *also.* Tang shu, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "on New Year's morning the assembled officers offer toasts of a thousand autumns and ten thousand years; the rescript calls it the felicity of treading the new (履新)."
- *A surname.* The Jinyu: "Xin Muzi (新穆子)."
- *A compound surname.* Shiji, *Biography of Lu Zhonglian*: "Xinyuan Yan (新垣衍)"; note: the Suoyin: "Xinyuan is the surname, Yan the name; he was a general of Liang."
- *A prefecture-name.* Under the Han it belonged to Hepu commandery; the Liang set up Xinzhou (新州).
- *A new field (新田).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "I go to gather the bitter-herb in that new field (新田)"; Zhu Xi's tradition: "a field in its first year is 葘, in its second a 'new field' (新田)."
- *Yunbu*: rhymes, fanqie 蘇前. A Daozang hymn: "ending a kalpa, beginning a kalpa again, ever more I feel the numinous face renewed (新); the Daolin flourishes in the heavenly capital, shedding light below on all the other immortals."
