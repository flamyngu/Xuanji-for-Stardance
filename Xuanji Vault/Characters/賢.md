**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, middle; Shell radical 貝; p.1209 #28)*

**Ancient graphs.** 臤, 贒.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 戸田. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 戸千 — sounding like 弦 (xián).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "rich in talent (多才)." *Yupian*: "of good conduct." Yijing, *Ding*: "great prosperity, to nourish the sage and worthy (賢)." Xici: "what may endure is the worthy man's (賢人) virtue; what may grow great is the worthy man's work." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "in the wilds no worthy (賢) is left." Xian you yi de: "in appointing officers, only the worthy and able (賢材)."
- *Finer, better (善).* Liji, *Neize*: "if rich, then provide two victims and offer the better (賢) of them to the head of the lineage"; note: "賢 is like 'good.'"
- *To surpass, win (勝).* Liji, *Touhu*: "So-and-so beats (賢) So-and-so by so many pairs"; note: "to win is 賢."
- *Fanqie 下見, sounding like 現 (xiàn).* Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*, Wheelwright: "divide the hub's length in five, take one off to make the 賢"; note: "賢 is the large bore of the hub."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 下珍.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in service I alone am put to toil (賢)" (rhyming above with 臣). Former Han, *Author's Postface*: "having risen to rank, with emolument and gifts to nourish the worthy (賢), yet in cloth quilt and plain fare, by thrift he ordered himself.
