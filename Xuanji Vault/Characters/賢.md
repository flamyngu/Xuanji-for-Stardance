**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls й…ү, middle; Shell radical иІқ; p.1209 #28)*

**Ancient graphs.** иҮӨ, иҙ’.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жҲёз”°. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie жҲёеҚғ вҖ” sounding like ејҰ (xiГЎn).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "rich in talent (еӨҡжүҚ)." *Yupian*: "of good conduct." Yijing, *Ding*: "great prosperity, to nourish the sage and worthy (иіў)." Xici: "what may endure is the worthy man's (иіўдәә) virtue; what may grow great is the worthy man's work." Shu, *Da Yu mo*: "in the wilds no worthy (иіў) is left." Xian you yi de: "in appointing officers, only the worthy and able (иіўжқҗ)."
- *Finer, better (е–„).* Liji, *Neize*: "if rich, then provide two victims and offer the better (иіў) of them to the head of the lineage"; note: "иіў is like 'good.'"
- *To surpass, win (еӢқ).* Liji, *Touhu*: "So-and-so beats (иіў) So-and-so by so many pairs"; note: "to win is иіў."
- *Fanqie дёӢиҰӢ, sounding like зҸҫ (xiГ n).* Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*, Wheelwright: "divide the hub's length in five, take one off to make the иіў"; note: "иіў is the large bore of the hub."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie дёӢзҸҚ.* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "in service I alone am put to toil (иіў)" (rhyming above with иҮЈ). Former Han, *Author's Postface*: "having risen to rank, with emolument and gifts to nourish the worthy (иіў), yet in cloth quilt and plain fare, by thrift he ordered himself.
