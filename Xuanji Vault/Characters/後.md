**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Step radical 彳; p.366 #18)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𨒥, 𢔏.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 胡口. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 很口 — sounding like 厚 (hòu).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "to be late (遲); following 彳, 幺, and 夂 [a man with foot trailing] is 'behind.'" Xu Kai says: "幺 is as if tripping and hobbling him." *Yupian*: "before and after." *Guangyun*: "earlier and later." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "neither before me nor after (後) me."
- *Posterity, heir.* Liji, *Aigong wen*: "the son is the parent's continuation (後)." Shu, *Cai Zhong zhi ming*: "hand down a law to your posterity (後)." Zuozhuan, Huan yr 2: "will not Zangsun Da have posterity (後) in Lu?"
- *Also a surname.* *Ji Yun.*
- *然後 (and then), a particle.* *Yun Hui.*

**Departing tone.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 胡遘; *Ji Yun*: fanqie 下遘; *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 胡茂 — the departing tone of 厚. *Zengyun*: "to put oneself behind (後) others, not daring to go first; the 後 of 'this first and that after.'"
- *To put behind.* Laozi, *Daodejing*: "he who puts himself behind (後), others put before." Lunyu: "in serving the lord, attend to the duty and put the pay last (後)."
- *先後, those who go before and behind.* Shijing, *Daya*: "I have those who go before and behind (先後)"; tradition: "to guide before and after is 先後." *Guangya*: "娣姒 (brothers' wives) are 先後." Former Han, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "the Spirit Lord was a woman of Changling who died in childbed and appeared as a spirit to her sister-in-law Wanruo (先後)"; note: "brothers' wives call each other 先後, anciently 娣; in Guanzhong they are now called 先後, in Wu and Chu 妯娌."

**Rhyming variant.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 後五, the rising tone of 胡.* Yang Xiong, *Encomium on the Portrait of Zhao Chongguo*: "...martial and mighty, he too continued his line (後).
