**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, middle; River radical 巛; p.324 #17)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 鋤交 — sounding like 鄛 (cháo).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a bird's perch in a tree is 巢 (a nest); in a hole it is 窠 (a burrow). It follows 木 (tree) and is pictographic." Shijing, *Shaonan*: "the magpie has a nest (巢), the dove dwells in it." Liji, *Liyun*: "the former kings had no palaces; in winter they dwelt in dug burrows, in summer in nests of piled boughs (檜巢)." Gushi kao: "Xu You in summer would dwell in a nest (巢), hence he was styled the Nest Father (巢父)."
- *Also a state-name.* Preface to the *Zhou shu*: "the Earl of Chao (巢伯) came to court"; tradition: "a feudal lord of the Yin."
- *Also a lake-name.* Kuodi zhi: "Chao county in Luzhou has Lake Chao (巢湖)."
- *Also a musical-instrument name.* Erya, *Shiyue*: "the large mouth-organ is called the chao (巢)"; subcommentary: "巢 means high; it speaks of its high sound."
- *Also a chariot-name.* Zuozhuan, Cheng yr 16: "the Viscount of Chu mounted the watch-tower chariot (巢車) to survey the army of Jin"; note: "the 巢車 has a lookout-tower built atop the chariot."
- *Also a surname.* *Guangyun*: "descendants of Youchaoshi (有巢氏)." Shiji, *Annals of the Three Sovereigns*: "Youchaoshi (有巢氏), the title of one who held the world."
- *Also a vegetable-name.* Lu Guimeng's poem-preface: "the vegetables of Shu have two chao (巢): the large chao (大巢) is the unfruiting kind of pea, the small chao (小巢) grows in the rice-paddies; one calls it the wild silkworm-bean."
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 士稍, sounding like 㑿 (chào): a plank-gallery, a scaffold (棧閣).
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 莊交, sounding like 𥊌 (zhuā): the large mouth-organ.
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: fanqie 子小; *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 子了 — sounding like 勦 (jiǎo): a state-name.
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 牀魚, sounding like 鋤 (chú). Cui Yin, *Fandu fu*: "at the dawn of the great Han, the land of Yong was their seat; in the age of peace at the capital, the mynah came to nest (巢)."
- *Rhymes as* fanqie 鋤尤, sounding like 愁 (chóu). Song Yu, *Gaotang fu*: "the ospreys and orioles, the zhengming and the Chu-doves; the cuckoos and the homing 'longing-wife' birds, the hanging-fowl in their high nests (高巢)."

**On the graph.** *Shuowen*: this is the original graph of 巢; above 木 (tree) it is written with 𦥑.
