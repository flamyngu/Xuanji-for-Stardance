**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Flesh radical 肉; p.992 #28)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 甫無. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 風無. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 方無 — sounding like 跗 (fū).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "in the Zhou-script written 臚." *Yupian*: "skin (皮)." *Guangyun*: "the skin (皮膚)." *Shiming*: "膚 means to spread (布): spread over the surface." Shijing, *Weifeng*: "skin (膚) like congealed lard." Yijing, *Shihe*: "the second six bites through the soft flesh (膚)"; note: "膚 is a soft, tender thing."
- *Also, pork is called 膚.* Yili, *Pin li*: "the pork (膚), fresh fish, and fresh dried meat"; note: "膚 is pork."
- *Also, sliced meat is called 膚.* Liji, *Neize*: "dried-meat broth, hare-sauce, and sliced venison (麋膚)"; note: "膚 is sliced meat."
- *Also* Boya: "fine, beautiful (美)." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "the duke's grandson, great and fair (碩膚)"; tradition: "膚 means fair."
- *Also* Boya: "to transmit (傳)"; also "to part, to separate (離)"; also "to flay (剝)."
- *Also, great (大).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "to achieve great merit (膚公)"; tradition: "膚 means great."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: 膚淺 (superficial), a metaphor for being at the skin, not deep.
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: four finger-breadths is a 膚. Gongyang zhuan, Xi yr 31: "a 膚 and an inch, and the clouds joined"; note: "the hand turned on its side is a 膚."
- *Also 膚受.* Lunyu: "slander that soaks in like through the skin (膚受之愬)." Shu, *Pangeng*: "now you clamor, raising up trust in dangerous and superficial talk (險膚)"; tradition: "raising up trust in dangerous, false, skin-deep words."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Suburban Sacrifices*: "in all, four sacrifices at Fushi (膚施)"; note: "Fushi is a county of Shang commandery."
- *Also a personal name.* Former Han, *Table of Ancient and Modern Persons*: "Yang Fu (陽膚)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 凌如, sounding like 臚 (lú); same sense.
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as 浮 (fú). Yilin: "Master Fan, of marvelous talent, was disgraced and his flesh (膚) wounded; later he was chancellor of Qin, enfeoffed as the Marquis of Ying."
