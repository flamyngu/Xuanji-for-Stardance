**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls жңӘ, lower; Flesh radical иӮү; p.992 #28)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie з”«з„Ў. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie йўЁз„Ў. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie ж–№з„Ў вҖ” sounding like и·— (fЕ«).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "in the Zhou-script written иҮҡ." *Yupian*: "skin (зҡ®)." *Guangyun*: "the skin (зҡ®иҶҡ)." *Shiming*: "иҶҡ means to spread (еёғ): spread over the surface." Shijing, *Weifeng*: "skin (иҶҡ) like congealed lard." Yijing, *Shihe*: "the second six bites through the soft flesh (иҶҡ)"; note: "иҶҡ is a soft, tender thing."
- *Also, pork is called иҶҡ.* Yili, *Pin li*: "the pork (иҶҡ), fresh fish, and fresh dried meat"; note: "иҶҡ is pork."
- *Also, sliced meat is called иҶҡ.* Liji, *Neize*: "dried-meat broth, hare-sauce, and sliced venison (йәӢиҶҡ)"; note: "иҶҡ is sliced meat."
- *Also* Boya: "fine, beautiful (зҫҺ)." Shijing, *Binfeng*: "the duke's grandson, great and fair (зў©иҶҡ)"; tradition: "иҶҡ means fair."
- *Also* Boya: "to transmit (еӮі)"; also "to part, to separate (йӣў)"; also "to flay (еүқ)."
- *Also, great (еӨ§).* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "to achieve great merit (иҶҡе…¬)"; tradition: "иҶҡ means great."
- *Also* *Yun Hui*: иҶҡж·ә (superficial), a metaphor for being at the skin, not deep.
- *Also* *Zheng Yun*: four finger-breadths is a иҶҡ. Gongyang zhuan, Xi yr 31: "a иҶҡ and an inch, and the clouds joined"; note: "the hand turned on its side is a иҶҡ."
- *Also иҶҡеҸ—.* Lunyu: "slander that soaks in like through the skin (иҶҡеҸ—д№Ӣж„¬)." Shu, *Pangeng*: "now you clamor, raising up trust in dangerous and superficial talk (йҡӘиҶҡ)"; tradition: "raising up trust in dangerous, false, skin-deep words."
- *Also a place-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Suburban Sacrifices*: "in all, four sacrifices at Fushi (иҶҡж–Ҫ)"; note: "Fushi is a county of Shang commandery."
- *Also a personal name.* Former Han, *Table of Ancient and Modern Persons*: "Yang Fu (йҷҪиҶҡ)."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie еҮҢеҰӮ, sounding like иҮҡ (lГә); same sense.
- *Rhymes* (per the *Yunbu*) as жө® (fГә). Yilin: "Master Fan, of marvelous talent, was disgraced and his flesh (иҶҡ) wounded; later he was chancellor of Qin, enfeoffed as the Marquis of Ying."
