**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 寅, lower; Step radical 彳; p.372 #04)*

**Ancient graph.** 𢾰.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 許歸. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 吁韋 — sounding like 揮 (huī).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "a slanting leg-binding (衺幅)."
- *Also* Erya, *Shigu*: "good (善)"; subcommentary: "徽 means fine and good." Shu, *Shun dian*: "carefully made beautiful (徽) the five teachings." Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "the gentleman has a fine plan (徽猷)." Also: "Tai Si carried on her fair fame (徽音)."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: the fret-studs of a zither are called 徽. Former Han, *Biography of Yang Xiong*: "strung high and tight at the frets (徽)"; note: "the zither's fret-marks (琴徽), which mark where to press and stop." Xi Kang, *Qin fu*: "its frets (徽) of jade from Mount Zhong"; note: "the frets are made of jade."
- *Also* *Shuowen*: "a thrice-twisted cord (三糾繩)." *Yupian*: "徽 is a great rope (大索)." Yijing, *Kan*: "bound with cords (徽纆)." Yang Xiong, *Jie chao*: "his ribs broken and his hip-bones smashed, he escaped the binding-ropes (徽索)."
- *Also 徽嫿, the look of galloping.* Ma Rong, *Guangcheng song*: "racing and darting (徽嫿霍奕), they gallop off this way and rush apart that."
- *Also a prefecture-name.* Song shi, *Treatise on Geography*: "in the third year of Xuanhe, Shezhou was renamed Huizhou (徽州)."
- *Also interchangeable with 幑: an emblem (幟).* Liji, *Da zhuan*: "the sage, facing south to govern the world, must change the first day of the year and distinguish the emblems and titles (徽號)"; note: "徽號 are of the banner kind." Yang Xiong, *Yulie fu*: "the emblem-chariots (徽車), light and martial"; note: "the 徽車 is a chariot bearing an emblem-flag."
- *Also interchangeable with 褘.* Zhang Heng, *Sixuan fu*: "she lets flutter the gaily-figured robe-sash (袿徽)"; note: "the Erya says, a woman's 徽 is called the 褵; Guo Pu says, it is today's scented sash (香纓)." A note: in Erya, *Shiqi* the graph is properly 褘.

**Textual notes.** The source title 校獵賦 should read 羽獵賦.
