**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 未, lower; Mortar radical 臼; p.1004 #13)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𢌱, 𦦲, 𠔔.

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*: fanqie 弋諸. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 弋渚. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 演女 — sounding like 予 (yǔ).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "A faction, a party (黨與)." *Zhanguo ce*: "so the lord, joining Qi with mighty Chu (與)"; note: "與 means a party." Guanzi, *Ba guan*: "when petitions are granted above, then factions (黨與) form below."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Guangyun*: "good (善)." Liji, *Li yun*: "the feudal lords treat one another (相與) by ritual."
- *Also* *Zengyun*: "and, together with (及)." Yijing, *Shuo gua*: "thus the way of Heaven set up is called yin and (與) yang; the way of Earth set up is called soft and (與) hard; the way of Man set up is called benevolence and (與) right."
- *To grant; to side with (許, 從).* Lunyu: "I am with (與) Dian." Guanzi, *Xingshi jie*: "the spirits aid him, Heaven and Earth side with (與) him."
- *To wait for (待).* Lunyu: "the years do not wait for (與) me."
- *Also* *Boya*: "like, as (如)." Former Han, *Biography of Han Xin*: "let the great king reckon for himself — in valour, fierceness, kindness, and strength, how does he compare with (孰與) King Xiang?"; note: "Yan Shigu says: 與 means as." Sima Xiangru, *Zixu fu*: "the King of Chu's hunt — how does it compare with (孰與) mine?"
- *To give, bestow (施予).* Liji, *Qu li*: "one who gives (與) to a man does not ask what he wants." Zhouli, *Chun guan, Da bu*: "by state affairs he makes the eight charges of the tortoise: first 'campaign,' second 'image,' third 'give' (與)"; note: "與 means to give a man things."
- *To help (助).* *Zhanguo ce*: "I shall press deep into the Wu host; whether I strike a man down or seize a man by the hair, it is to help (與) the bold-hearted"; note: "與 is like to help."
- *A kind, a sort (類).* *Discourses of Zhou*: "in establishing ritual, the finished one is the banquet-feast (飫), which makes plain the great forms only, with little of the lesser sort (曲與)"; note: "與 means a sort; the lesser observances are few in number."
- *With, by means of (以).* Shijing, *Zhao nan*: "this lady going home does not take me with her (不我與)"; Zhu Xi's note: "與 is like 以 (with); it means taking oneself along to go together."
- *Harmonious (和).* *Zhanguo ce*: "if within there is no disorder unreconciled (不與) and without the foe cannot be withstood"; note: "𡨥 is like disorder, 與 is like harmony."
- *To use (用).* Shijing, *Tang feng*: "the words men make, take care not to heed (無與)"; tradition: "無與 means not to use."
- *To count, reckon (數).* Liji, *Qu li*: "for the living, count from the coming day (生與來日)"; note: "與 is like to count."
- *A particle (語辭).* Liji, *Biao ji*: "the gentleman, rather than have the blame of a broken promise (諾責), had better bear a present grudge."
- *容與 (rongyu), a look of ease and leisure.* Zhuangzi, *Renjian shi*: "following on what stirs a man, he seeks to please (容與) his heart"; note: "to seek to let himself go at ease, and so indulge his extravagant heart." Shiji, *Biography of Sima Xiangru*: "the King of Chu then slackened pace, wheeling and soaring at leisure (容與)"; note: "the *Suoyin* says: it means self-content."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: 大與 (Dayu), an office-name, the officer in charge of ranks and emoluments.
- *Buyu (不與), a state-name.* Shanhai jing: "there is the state of Buyu, of the Lie surname, who eat millet."
- *Surname.*
- *Also* *Guangyun*: fanqie 羊洳. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 羊茹 — sounding like 豫 (yù). "To take part in (參與)." *Zheng Yun*: "to be concerned in (干)." *Zhongyong*: "the simplicity of common men and women may yet take part (與) in the knowing of it." Zhouli, *Dong guan, Kaogong ji*: "the state has six functions, and the hundred craftsmen hold (與) one of them."
- *A county-name.* Shiji, *Hereditary House of Chancellor Cao*: "Can, following as a palace-attendant, was to strike Huling and Fangyu (方與)"; note: "the *Suoyin* says: by the *Treatise on Geography*, both counties belong to Shanyang. The *Correct Meaning*: 與 is sounded 預."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: in doubt, undecided (疑慮未決); used for 豫. Former Han, *Biography of the King of Changyi*: "Yang Chang, still wavering (猶與), made no decision." Also the *Biography of Chen Tang*: "the soldiers wavered (猶與)"; used for 豫.
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 羊諸. *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 雲俱 — sounding like 余 (yú). A particle. Lunyu: "is this not the root of benevolence (與)?" Liji, *Tan gong*: "Zengzi said: 'no, surely (微與); at his slighting one may leave, at his apology one may take the food'"; subcommentary: "微 means no; 與 is a particle."
- *Also* Shijing, *Xiaoya*: "my millet lush and full (與與), my grain thick and trim"; Jian: "與與 and 翼翼 are the look of luxuriance."
- *Also* Lunyu: "easy and composed (與與如也)"; note: "the look of a fitting and measured bearing."
- *To stretch out, expand (舒).* Former Han, *Treatise on Rites and Music*: "the Red Brightness in full growth, spreading open (旉與) the myriad things"; note: "Yan Shigu says: 敷與 means to open out; 與 is fanqie 弋於."
- *A personal name.* Shu, *Shun dian*: "Chui bowed and knocked his head, yielding to Shuqiang and Boyu (伯與)"; tradition: "Shuqiang and Boyu, two ministers' names"; *Shiwen*: "與 is sounded 餘."
- *A mountain-name; same as 輿.* Shanhai jing: "Mount Dunyu (敦與之山)"; note: "by the *Mingsheng zhi* it is written Mount Dunyu (敦輿山)." Also: "at the head of Mount Ku is Mount Xiuyu (休與之山)"; note: "與 is sometimes written 輿."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: fanqie 倚亥, sounding like 欸 (ǎi). Same as 与. A note: by the *Shuowen*, 与 is glossed "to bestow, to give (賜予)," one 勺 making 与; while 與 is glossed "a faction (黨與)." Today vulgar usage runs the graph 與 together with 与.
