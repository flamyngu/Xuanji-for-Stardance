## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈子集下〉【冫字部】頁131第24    〔古文〕：𣲝【唐韻】筆陵切【集韻】【韻會】悲陵切，𠀤逼平聲。【說文】本作仌。【徐曰】今文作冰。【韓詩】說冰者，窮谷隂氣所聚，不洩，則結而爲伏隂。【禮·月令】孟冬水始冰，仲冬冰益壯，季冬冰方盛。水澤腹堅，命取冰，冰以入。【周禮·天官】淩人共冰。秋刷冰室，冬藏春啓，夏頒冰。又【爾雅·釋器】冰，脂也。【註】莊子云：肌膚若冰雪。冰雪，脂膏也。【疏】脂膏一名冰脂。又矢筩蓋曰冰。【左傳·昭二十五年】公徒釋甲執冰而踞。【註】冰，櫝丸蓋。【疏】盛弓者也。或云：櫝丸是箭筩，其蓋可以取飮。又【集韻】【正韻】𠀤魚陵切，音凝。同凝。【正韻】古文冰作仌，凝作冰。後人以冰代仌，以凝代冰。又【集韻】讀去聲，逋孕切。【唐書·韋思謙傳】涕泗冰須。【註】謂涕著須而凝也。【李商隱詩】碧玉冰寒漿。又【韻補】叶筆良切，音近浜。【𨻰琳·大荒賦】心慇懃以伊感兮，愵永思以增傷。悵太息而攬涕，乃揮雹而淚冰。    考證：〔【韓詩外傳】冰者，窮谷隂氣所聚，不洩，則結爲伏隂。〕　謹按韓詩外傳無此語，查初學記引作韓詩。謹照初學記原文省外傳二字。冰字上增說字。結字下增而字改。〔【正韻】古文冰作仌，凝作冰。後人以冰代仌，以凝伐冰。〕　謹按文義伐冰改代冰。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 子, lower; Ice radical 冫; p.131 #24)*

**Ancient graph.** 𣲝.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 筆陵. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 悲陵 — the level tone of 逼 (bīng).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "Properly written 仌." Xu (Xuan): "the modern script writes 冰." *Han shi*: "speaking of ice — where the yin breath of deep valleys gathers and does not vent, it congeals and becomes the hidden yin (伏隂)." Liji, *Yue ling*: "in early winter the waters first ice over (冰), in mid-winter the ice grows stouter, in late winter the ice is at its fullest; when the waters and marshes are frozen solid to the core, the order is given to cut ice (取冰), and the ice is stored away." Zhouli, *Tian guan*: "the Ice-man supplied the ice (冰): in autumn he scoured the ice-house, in winter stored it, in spring opened it, and in summer doled out the ice."

**Senses.**
- *Also* Erya, *Shi qi*: "冰 means tallow (脂)"; note: "Zhuangzi says: 'skin like ice and snow' — ice and snow being fat and grease (脂膏)"; subcommentary: "fat and grease are also called ice-tallow (冰脂)."
- *The cap of an arrow-quiver is called 冰.* Zuozhuan, Zhao yr 25: "the duke's followers laid off their armour and, holding the quiver-cap (冰), squatted down"; note: "冰 is the case-cap"; subcommentary: "what holds the bow. Some say: the case is the arrow-quiver, and its cap can be used to dip and drink."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 魚陵, sounding like 凝 (níng) — same as 凝. *Zheng Yun*: "in the old script 冰 was written 仌, and 凝 was written 冰; later men used 冰 for 仌, and 凝 for 冰."
- *Also* *Ji Yun*: read in the departing tone, fanqie 逋孕. Tang shu, *Biography of Wei Siqian*: "tears freezing on the beard (冰須)"; note: "it means the tears clinging to the beard and congealing." Li Shangyin's poem: "the jasper freezes (冰) the cold draught."

**Rhyming variants.**
- *Rhymes, fanqie 筆良, a sound near 浜 (bāng).* Chen Lin, *Da huang fu*: "my heart earnest and stirred with feeling, anxious in long thought and the more grieved; sighing in dismay I wipe my tears, then brushing off the hail, my tears freeze (冰)."

**Textual notes.** The *Han shi waizhuan* citation "ice — where the yin breath of deep valleys gathers and does not vent, congeals to become the hidden yin" — a note: the *Han shi waizhuan* has no such passage; checking the *Chuxue ji*, it cites it as the *Han shi*. Following the *Chuxue ji* original, the two words "外傳" are dropped, the word "說" is added before 冰, and the word "而" added after 結. · *Zheng Yun*, "later men used 冰 for 仌, and 凝 for (伐) 冰" — by the sense, "伐冰" should read "代冰."
