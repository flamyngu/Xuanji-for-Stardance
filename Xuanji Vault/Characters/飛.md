**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 戌, lower; Fly radical 飛; p.1415 #14)*

**Ancient graphs.** 𩙱, 𦐭.

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 甫微. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*: fanqie 匪微 — sounding like 非 (fēi).

**Senses.** *Yupian*: "a bird soaring (翥)." *Guangyun*: "to fly and wheel (飛翔)." Yijing, *Qian*: "the flying dragon is in the heavens." Shijing, *Bei feng*: "the swallows in their flight (于飛)."
- *An office.* Former Han, *Annals of Xuandi*: when the Western Qiang rebelled, [he] recruited the 佽飛 archer-troops.
- *飛廬, the upper deck-cabin.* *Shiming*: "the high cabin on a boat is the 飛廬; being on top, it is called '飛' (flying)."
- *六飛, the Six Flyers (the imperial horses).* Former Han, *Biography of Yuan Ang*: "galloping the Six Flyers, racing down unfathomable hills"; note: "the six horses' swiftness like flying." Also written 騛.
- *桑飛, another name for the wren (鷦鷯).*
- *飛廉 (Feilian), a divine beast.* *Sanfu Huangtu*: "it can summon wind; body like a deer, head like a sparrow, with horns, a snake's tail, markings like a leopard." Guo Pu: "Feilian is the dragon-sparrow"; the world took Feilian for the Wind Earl's name, but it is really a beast.
- *Bencao*: "漏蘆 (a herb) is also called 飛廉."
- *Anciently interchangeable with 蜚.* *Guangyun.* Shiji, *Qin annals*: "蜚廉 was a good runner."
- *Borrowed for 非.* The Han stele *Eulogy for Magistrate Cai*: "飛 (were it not) Tao-Tang, who could be thus?" *Shuowen* Xu note: "the upper side 飞 images a bird's neck."

**Textual note.** 其郭能 should read 其孰能.
