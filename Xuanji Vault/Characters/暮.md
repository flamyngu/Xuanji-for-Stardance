## Kangxi Dictionary (康熙字典)
**Original entry (verbatim):**

> 《康熙字典》〈辰集上〉【日字部】頁499第21【廣韻】【集韻】【韻會】【正韻】𠀤莫故切，音慕。本作莫。【說文】莫，日且冥也。从日，在茻中。【註】平野中望日將落，如在草茻中也。【史記·伍子胥傳】吾日暮塗遠。【屈原·離騷】恐美人之遲暮。○按經典本皆作莫。今或相承用暮字。

**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 辰, upper; Sun radical 日; p.499 #21)*

**Pronunciation.** *Guangyun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun* all: fanqie 莫故 — sounding like 慕 (mù). Originally written 莫.

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "莫 — the sun about to grow dark." It follows 日 (sun) set amid 茻 (thicket); note: "in the open plain one watches the sun about to set, as though amid the grassy thicket." Shiji, *Biography of Wu Zixu*: "my day grows late (暮) and the road is long." Qu Yuan, *Lisao*: "I fear the fair one's growing late (遲暮)."

**Editorial notes.** A note: in the classics it is everywhere written 莫; nowadays the graph 暮 is sometimes used by convention.
