**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 午, middle; Dish radical 皿; p.794 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 氏征. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 時征 — sounding like 成 (chéng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "millet and grain set in a vessel for sacrifice." Shu, *Taishi*: "the sacrificial victims and grain-offerings (粢盛)"; tradition: "the millet and grain are 粢; in the vessel they are 盛." Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Field-Master leads his subordinates to plough and weed the king's holy field, to supply the grain-offerings (齍盛)"; note: "meaning millet, grain, rice, and sorghum, such as may fill the *fu* and *gui* vessels."
- *A vessel.* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 13: "one vessel (盛) of fine wine"; note: "one vessel." Liji, *Sang da ji*: "he eats gruel from a bowl (盛) without washing"; note: "meaning the cups and bowls of today."
- *To hold, receive (受).* *Guangyun.* Shijing, *Zhaonan*: "wherewith to hold (盛) it — round basket and square." Gujin zhu: "城 (city wall) is 盛: that which holds and receives the people and their goods."
- *To complete (成).* Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*: "white plaster (白盛)"; note: "盛 here means to complete — with shell-lime one whitewashes the walls, to finish and adorn the palace."
- *盛服, full dress, solemn attire.* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 2: "Xuanzi, in full dress (盛服), was about to go to court"; note: "盛 sounds 成; some texts write 成."
- *A dyke.* Erya, *Shishan*: "a mountain shaped like a dyke is 盛"; subcommentary: "盛 is read as in 粢盛; oblong and lofty, like grain heaped in a vessel."
- *A mountain-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "the sun-deity is worshipped at Sheng Mountain (盛山)"; note: "in Buye county of Donglai."
- *A state-name.* Gongyang zhuan, Yin yr 5: "in autumn the Wey army entered Cheng (盛)"; note: "盛 sounds 成." The Zuozhuan writes 郕.

**Departing tone (shèng).** *Tang Yun*: fanqie 丞政. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie 時正 — the departing tone of 成, i.e. **shèng**.
- *Many; flourishing.* *Boya*: "many." *Guangyun*: "growing." *Zengyun*: "great; luxuriant." Yijing, *Xici*: "daily renewal is called flourishing virtue (盛德)." Liji, *Yueling*: "the life-breath is at its full (盛), the yang *qi* pours forth." Zhongyong: "officials in abundance (官盛) to serve one's commissions." Shiji, *Biographies of Upright Officials*: "the age praised it as splendid (盛美)." Lüshi chunqiu: "when the trees flourish (盛), the flying birds return to them."
- *The utmost.* Zhuangzi, *Dechongfu*: "the level is water's stillness at its perfection (盛)."
- *Like 'fine, admirable.'* Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "extolling (盛) the consummate beauty of the house of Xia, and reverencing the spirits."
- *To hold a thing.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "a pot is that which holds (盛)"; Yan Shigu's note: "rhyming, sounded in the departing tone."
- *Place-names.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "on his southern progress he reached Shengtang (盛唐)." Weishu, *Annals of Emperor Shenyuan*: "the Wei first ancestor moved to Shengle (盛樂) in Dingxiang."
- *A surname.* Later Han, *Account of the Western Qiang*: "Sheng Bao, governor of Beihai — his forebears were surnamed Shi (奭), and to avoid Emperor Yuan's taboo changed it to Sheng." Some say the clan descends from the state of Sheng in the time of King Mu of Zhou. Mu tianzi zhuan: "[Sheng Ji was] of the Ji surname, daughter of Shengbo; the Son of Heaven took her, eldest of the Ji line, and so named her Lady of the House of Sheng (盛門)"; note: "Sheng is a state-name; Sheng Ji was of the king's own surname.
