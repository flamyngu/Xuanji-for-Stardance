**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls еҚҲ, middle; Dish radical зҡҝ; p.794 #20)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*: fanqie ж°ҸеҫҒ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жҷӮеҫҒ вҖ” sounding like жҲҗ (chГ©ng).

**Etymology and senses.** *Shuowen*: "millet and grain set in a vessel for sacrifice." Shu, *Taishi*: "the sacrificial victims and grain-offerings (зІўзӣӣ)"; tradition: "the millet and grain are зІў; in the vessel they are зӣӣ." Zhouli, *Tianguan*: "the Field-Master leads his subordinates to plough and weed the king's holy field, to supply the grain-offerings (йҪҚзӣӣ)"; note: "meaning millet, grain, rice, and sorghum, such as may fill the *fu* and *gui* vessels."
- *A vessel.* Zuozhuan, Ai yr 13: "one vessel (зӣӣ) of fine wine"; note: "one vessel." Liji, *Sang da ji*: "he eats gruel from a bowl (зӣӣ) without washing"; note: "meaning the cups and bowls of today."
- *To hold, receive (еҸ—).* *Guangyun.* Shijing, *Zhaonan*: "wherewith to hold (зӣӣ) it вҖ” round basket and square." Gujin zhu: "еҹҺ (city wall) is зӣӣ: that which holds and receives the people and their goods."
- *To complete (жҲҗ).* Zhouli, *Dongguan kaogong ji*: "white plaster (зҷҪзӣӣ)"; note: "зӣӣ here means to complete вҖ” with shell-lime one whitewashes the walls, to finish and adorn the palace."
- *зӣӣжңҚ, full dress, solemn attire.* Zuozhuan, Xuan yr 2: "Xuanzi, in full dress (зӣӣжңҚ), was about to go to court"; note: "зӣӣ sounds жҲҗ; some texts write жҲҗ."
- *A dyke.* Erya, *Shishan*: "a mountain shaped like a dyke is зӣӣ"; subcommentary: "зӣӣ is read as in зІўзӣӣ; oblong and lofty, like grain heaped in a vessel."
- *A mountain-name.* Former Han, *Treatise on the Feng and Shan*: "the sun-deity is worshipped at Sheng Mountain (зӣӣеұұ)"; note: "in Buye county of Donglai."
- *A state-name.* Gongyang zhuan, Yin yr 5: "in autumn the Wey army entered Cheng (зӣӣ)"; note: "зӣӣ sounds жҲҗ." The Zuozhuan writes йғ•.

**Departing tone (shГЁng).** *Tang Yun*: fanqie дёһж”ҝ. *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui*, *Zheng Yun*: fanqie жҷӮжӯЈ вҖ” the departing tone of жҲҗ, i.e. **shГЁng**.
- *Many; flourishing.* *Boya*: "many." *Guangyun*: "growing." *Zengyun*: "great; luxuriant." Yijing, *Xici*: "daily renewal is called flourishing virtue (зӣӣеҫ·)." Liji, *Yueling*: "the life-breath is at its full (зӣӣ), the yang *qi* pours forth." Zhongyong: "officials in abundance (е®ҳзӣӣ) to serve one's commissions." Shiji, *Biographies of Upright Officials*: "the age praised it as splendid (зӣӣзҫҺ)." LГјshi chunqiu: "when the trees flourish (зӣӣ), the flying birds return to them."
- *The utmost.* Zhuangzi, *Dechongfu*: "the level is water's stillness at its perfection (зӣӣ)."
- *Like 'fine, admirable.'* Zhang Heng, *Dongjing fu*: "extolling (зӣӣ) the consummate beauty of the house of Xia, and reverencing the spirits."
- *To hold a thing.* Former Han, *Biography of Dongfang Shuo*: "a pot is that which holds (зӣӣ)"; Yan Shigu's note: "rhyming, sounded in the departing tone."
- *Place-names.* Former Han, *Annals of Emperor Wu*: "on his southern progress he reached Shengtang (зӣӣе”җ)." Weishu, *Annals of Emperor Shenyuan*: "the Wei first ancestor moved to Shengle (зӣӣжЁӮ) in Dingxiang."
- *A surname.* Later Han, *Account of the Western Qiang*: "Sheng Bao, governor of Beihai вҖ” his forebears were surnamed Shi (еҘӯ), and to avoid Emperor Yuan's taboo changed it to Sheng." Some say the clan descends from the state of Sheng in the time of King Mu of Zhou. Mu tianzi zhuan: "[Sheng Ji was] of the Ji surname, daughter of Shengbo; the Son of Heaven took her, eldest of the Ji line, and so named her Lady of the House of Sheng (зӣӣй–Җ)"; note: "Sheng is a state-name; Sheng Ji was of the king's own surname.
