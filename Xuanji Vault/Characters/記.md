**Full English translation:**

*(Kangxi: rolls 酉, upper; Speech radical 言; p.1149 #02)*

**Pronunciation.** *Tang Yun*, *Ji Yun*, *Yun Hui* all: fanqie 居吏, sounding like 冀 (jì).

**Etymology (Shuowen).** "To set down item by item (疏)." Xu (Xu Kai) says: "it means to record them one by one, each apart." *Boya*: "to note (識)." *Shiming*: "to chronicle (紀)." *Yupian*: "to record (錄)." *Guangyun*: "to mark down (志)." Shu, *Yi ji*: "they flog to make it remembered (記)"; tradition: "to make him note and remember his fault." Liji, *Wang zhi*: "the Grand Scribe has charge of ritual and holds the tablet-records (簡記)"; note: "when the state has a ritual matter, he beforehand holds the tablets and slips, recording the rites that ought to be performed." Tang shu, *Biography of Yu Zhining*: "on the left was the scribe who recorded words, on the right the officer who recorded affairs." Guanyinzi, *Wu jian*: "coming again to a former haunt, the memory (記憶) is vivid."

**Senses.**
- *Also* *Boya*: "a writing (書)." Former Han, *Biography of Xiao Wangzhi*: "the expectant-appointee Zheng Peng submitted a memorandum (奏記) to Xiao Wangzhi"; note: "記 is a writing; the submitting of memoranda began with Peng." *Wenxin diaolong*: "in the Later Han there first were memoranda submitted to ducal offices; 記 means to set forth one's will, advancing one's own intent."
- *Also* *Zhengzitong*: "an official document (文符)." Later Han, *Biography of Zhong Liyi*: "young, he was a commandery postal inspector; at the time a county post-chief had taken wine and gifts from someone, and the prefecture sent down a writ (記) to investigate the case; Yi sealed and returned the writ, and the Grand Administrator thought him very worthy."
- *An office-name.* Later Han, *Treatise on Officialdom*: "the Recording-Office Clerk (記室令史) has charge of memorials, dispatches, and correspondence."
- *Also* interchangeable with 其. *Zihui Bu*: "a particle." Shijing, *Wang feng*: "that (其) man yonder"; Jian: "其 is sometimes written 記, or written 己; in reading the sounds are alike."
- *Also* rhymes, fanqie 苟起, sounding like 几 (jǐ). Cui Yan, *Shu chu fu*: "I gaze toward Gaomi and press my journey, and reaching the plain gate I make my halt; I behold the loftiness of You and Xia, and hear the chapters and records (篇記) of the great plans." One account: it was originally written 紀; 紀 rhymes with 止, and was miswritten 記.
